# Workout Splits Explained: Find the Best Training Split for You

How should you divide your training throughout the week? Full body every session? Body part splits? Push/pull/legs?

This guide breaks down the most popular workout splits and helps you choose the right one.

## What Is a Workout Split?

A workout split is how you organize muscle groups across your training week.

**Examples:**
- Full body: All muscles each session
- Upper/Lower: Upper body one day, lower the next
- Push/Pull/Legs: Muscles grouped by function

## Key Factors in Choosing a Split

### Training Frequency

How often you train each muscle per week.

**Research suggests:** 2-3× per week per muscle group is optimal for hypertrophy.

### Recovery

Your ability to recover between sessions affects what split works.

### Schedule

How many days can you realistically train?

### Goals

- Strength: Frequency on main lifts matters
- Hypertrophy: Volume and frequency both matter
- General fitness: Flexibility in approach

### Experience

Beginners often do better with simpler splits.

## The Main Splits

### Full Body (3 days/week)

**Structure:**
- Day 1: Full body
- Day 2: Rest
- Day 3: Full body
- Day 4: Rest
- Day 5: Full body
- Days 6-7: Rest

**Example session:**
- Squat: 3 × 8
- Bench Press: 3 × 8
- Barbell Row: 3 × 8
- Overhead Press: 2 × 10
- Romanian Deadlift: 3 × 10
- Core work

**Pros:**
- High frequency (3× per muscle)
- Only 3 gym days required
- Great for beginners
- Good for strength

**Cons:**
- Longer individual sessions
- Limited volume per muscle per session
- Can be fatiguing

**Best for:**
- Beginners
- Busy schedules (only 3 days)
- Strength-focused training
- Those who can only train 3 days

### Upper/Lower (4 days/week)

**Structure:**
- Monday: Upper
- Tuesday: Lower
- Wednesday: Rest
- Thursday: Upper
- Friday: Lower
- Weekend: Rest

**Example Upper:**
- Bench Press: 4 × 6
- Barbell Row: 4 × 6
- Overhead Press: 3 × 8
- Pull-ups: 3 × 8
- Lateral Raises: 3 × 12
- Tricep/Bicep work

**Example Lower:**
- Squat: 4 × 6
- Romanian Deadlift: 3 × 8
- Leg Press: 3 × 10
- Leg Curl: 3 × 10
- Calf Raises: 4 × 12
- Core work

**Pros:**
- Good frequency (2× per muscle)
- Balanced volume
- Manageable session length
- Good for most goals

**Cons:**
- Requires 4 days
- Upper days can get long

**Best for:**
- Intermediate lifters
- Most general goals
- Those with 4 days available
- Balanced development

### Push/Pull/Legs (6 days/week)

**Structure:**
- Monday: Push
- Tuesday: Pull
- Wednesday: Legs
- Thursday: Push
- Friday: Pull
- Saturday: Legs
- Sunday: Rest

**Example Push:**
- Bench Press: 4 × 6-8
- Incline Dumbbell Press: 3 × 10
- Overhead Press: 3 × 8
- Lateral Raises: 4 × 12
- Tricep Pushdowns: 3 × 12
- Overhead Tricep Extension: 2 × 12

**Example Pull:**
- Deadlift (or Barbell Row): 4 × 5
- Pull-ups: 4 × 8
- Cable Row: 3 × 10
- Face Pulls: 3 × 15
- Bicep Curls: 3 × 10
- Hammer Curls: 2 × 12

**Example Legs:**
- Squat: 4 × 6
- Romanian Deadlift: 3 × 10
- Leg Press: 3 × 12
- Leg Curl: 3 × 12
- Walking Lunges: 2 × 10 per leg
- Calf Raises: 4 × 15

**Pros:**
- High frequency (2× per muscle)
- High volume possible
- Muscles grouped logically
- Popular for hypertrophy

**Cons:**
- Requires 6 days
- High commitment
- Can be hard to recover from

**Best for:**
- Intermediate to advanced
- Hypertrophy focus
- Those with time and recovery capacity
- Bodybuilding

### Push/Pull/Legs (3 days/week)

**Structure:**
- Monday: Push
- Wednesday: Pull
- Friday: Legs

Same workouts as above, but only 1× per week per muscle.

**Pros:**
- Only 3 days
- Simple organization

**Cons:**
- Low frequency (1× per muscle)
- Not optimal for most goals

**Best for:** Those who can only train 3 days but prefer body part focus.

### Bro Split (5-6 days/week)

**Structure:**
- Monday: Chest
- Tuesday: Back
- Wednesday: Shoulders
- Thursday: Legs
- Friday: Arms
- Weekend: Rest (or weak point day)

**Example Chest Day:**
- Bench Press: 4 × 8
- Incline Dumbbell Press: 4 × 10
- Cable Flyes: 3 × 12
- Dips: 3 × 12
- Pec Deck: 3 × 15

**Pros:**
- High volume per muscle per session
- Focus on individual muscles
- Popular in bodybuilding culture

**Cons:**
- Low frequency (1× per muscle per week)
- Many skip leg day
- Not optimal based on research

**Best for:**
- Advanced bodybuilders
- Those who enjoy high-volume single-muscle days
- Not recommended for most people

### Arnold Split (6 days/week)

**Structure:**
- Day 1: Chest + Back
- Day 2: Shoulders + Arms
- Day 3: Legs
- Day 4: Chest + Back
- Day 5: Shoulders + Arms
- Day 6: Legs
- Day 7: Rest

**Pros:**
- High frequency (2× per muscle)
- Antagonist training (push/pull same day)
- Used by Arnold Schwarzenegger

**Cons:**
- 6 days required
- Long sessions
- High volume

**Best for:** Advanced lifters with high recovery capacity.

## Comparison Table

| Split | Days/Week | Frequency | Best For |
|-------|-----------|-----------|----------|
| Full Body | 3 | 3×/muscle | Beginners, strength |
| Upper/Lower | 4 | 2×/muscle | Most people |
| PPL (6 day) | 6 | 2×/muscle | Hypertrophy, advanced |
| PPL (3 day) | 3 | 1×/muscle | Limited time |
| Bro Split | 5-6 | 1×/muscle | Advanced bodybuilders |
| Arnold | 6 | 2×/muscle | Advanced, high volume |

## Choosing Your Split

### If You Can Train 3 Days

**Best choice:** Full Body

Train all muscles each session. Highest frequency possible with limited days.

### If You Can Train 4 Days

**Best choice:** Upper/Lower

Excellent balance of frequency, volume, and recovery.

### If You Can Train 5 Days

**Options:**
- Upper/Lower/Upper/Lower/Full Body
- Push/Pull/Legs/Upper/Lower
- Full Body 5× (lower volume per session)

### If You Can Train 6 Days

**Best choice:** Push/Pull/Legs (2× through)

High frequency and volume. Requires good recovery.

## Split Progression Over Time

### Beginner (0-12 months)

Start with **Full Body 3×/week**
- Learn movements
- Build base strength
- Establish habit

### Intermediate (1-2 years)

Progress to **Upper/Lower 4×/week**
- More volume per session
- Still good frequency
- More exercise variety

### Advanced (2+ years)

Consider **Push/Pull/Legs 6×/week** or specialized splits
- Higher volume
- More isolation work
- Based on weak points

## Common Mistakes

### Overcomplicating Early

Beginners don't need 6-day splits. Simple works.

### Wrong Split for Schedule

Choosing PPL when you can only train 4 days consistently wastes a day.

### Skipping Legs

Every split should include legs. Period.

### Too Much Volume Too Soon

High-volume splits require built-up work capacity.

### Ignoring Recovery

More isn't always better. Match split to recovery ability.

## Making Any Split Work

### Progressive Overload

Regardless of split, you must progress over time.

### Consistency

The best split is one you'll actually follow.

### Recovery

Sleep, nutrition, and rest days matter more than split selection.

### Individual Response

Some people thrive on high frequency; others prefer more recovery. Experiment.

## Conclusion

There's no universally "best" split. The right split depends on:
- How many days you can train
- Your training experience
- Your goals
- Your recovery capacity

**General recommendations:**
- **Beginners:** Full Body 3×/week
- **Intermediate:** Upper/Lower 4×/week
- **Advanced/Hypertrophy:** Push/Pull/Legs 6×/week

Choose a split you can stick with consistently. Execute it well. Progress over time. That matters more than the "perfect" split.
