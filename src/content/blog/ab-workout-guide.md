# Ab Workout: The Complete Guide to Core Training

Everyone wants visible abs, but few train their core correctly. Endless crunches won't give you a six-pack—and more importantly, won't build a truly strong, functional core.

This guide covers how to train your abs for both aesthetics and performance.

## Core Anatomy

Your "core" is more than just abs:

### Rectus Abdominis
- The "six-pack" muscle
- Runs from ribs to pelvis
- Flexes the spine (crunching motion)

### Obliques (Internal and External)
- Side of your waist
- Rotate and side-bend the spine
- Create the "V" lines

### Transverse Abdominis
- Deepest ab muscle
- Wraps around like a corset
- Stabilizes spine, compresses abdomen

### Erector Spinae
- Lower back muscles
- Extend the spine
- Critical for posture and stability

### Hip Flexors (Psoas)
- Not technically core, but involved in many ab exercises
- Often take over when abs are weak

## Types of Core Exercises

### Anti-Extension
Resist arching the back:
- Planks
- Dead bugs
- Ab wheel rollouts

### Anti-Rotation
Resist twisting:
- Pallof press
- Single-arm exercises
- Bird dogs

### Anti-Lateral Flexion
Resist side bending:
- Side planks
- Suitcase carries

### Flexion
Traditional ab work:
- Crunches
- Leg raises
- Sit-ups

### Rotation
Controlled twisting:
- Russian twists
- Cable woodchops

**Key insight:** A complete core routine includes all types, with emphasis on anti-movement exercises for stability.

## Best Ab Exercises

### Planks and Anti-Extension

**Standard Plank:**
1. Forearms and toes on ground
2. Body in straight line
3. Squeeze glutes, brace abs
4. Don't let hips sag or pike up
5. Hold 30-60 seconds

**RKC Plank:**
Harder version:
1. Same position
2. Actively squeeze everything—glutes, quads, fists
3. Pull elbows toward toes (don't actually move)
4. Hold 10-20 seconds (very intense)

**Dead Bugs:**
1. Lie on back, arms up, knees bent 90 degrees
2. Press lower back into floor
3. Lower opposite arm and leg
4. Return, switch sides
5. 3 sets of 10 per side

**Ab Wheel Rollout:**
1. Kneel with ab wheel
2. Roll forward, keeping abs tight
3. Go only as far as you can control
4. Roll back to start
5. 3 sets of 8-12 reps

### Anti-Rotation

**Pallof Press:**
1. Cable or band at chest height
2. Hold handle at chest, facing sideways to anchor
3. Press hands straight out, resist rotation
4. Hold 2-3 seconds, return
5. 3 sets of 10 per side

**Bird Dogs:**
1. Hands and knees position
2. Extend opposite arm and leg
3. Keep hips level—don't rotate
4. Hold 2-3 seconds, return
5. 3 sets of 10 per side

### Side Planks and Anti-Lateral Flexion

**Side Plank:**
1. Forearm and side of foot on ground
2. Body in straight line
3. Don't let hips sag
4. Hold 30-45 seconds per side

**Copenhagen Plank:**
Harder, with adductor work:
1. Side plank with top leg on bench
2. Lift bottom leg to meet it
3. Hold position
4. 3 sets of 20-30 seconds per side

**Suitcase Carry:**
1. Heavy dumbbell in one hand
2. Walk without leaning to that side
3. 3 sets of 40 steps per side

### Flexion Exercises

**Crunches:**
1. Lie on back, knees bent
2. Curl shoulders off floor
3. Lower with control
4. 3 sets of 15-20 reps

**Reverse Crunches:**
Better for lower abs:
1. Lie on back, legs up
2. Curl pelvis off floor toward chest
3. Lower with control
4. 3 sets of 12-15 reps

**Hanging Leg Raises:**
Advanced:
1. Hang from pull-up bar
2. Raise legs to parallel (or higher)
3. Lower with control—no swinging
4. 3 sets of 10-15 reps

**Cable Crunches:**
1. Kneel facing cable, rope behind head
2. Crunch down, bringing elbows to knees
3. Feel abs contract
4. 3 sets of 12-15 reps

### Rotation Exercises

**Russian Twists:**
1. Sit with knees bent, lean back slightly
2. Rotate torso side to side
3. Optional: hold weight
4. 3 sets of 20 total (10 per side)

**Cable Woodchops:**
1. Cable at high position
2. Pull diagonally across body to opposite hip
3. Rotate through core
4. 3 sets of 12 per side

**Bicycle Crunches:**
1. Lie on back, hands behind head
2. Bring opposite elbow to knee
3. Alternate sides
4. 3 sets of 20 total

## Sample Ab Workouts

### Quick Core (10 minutes)

1. Dead bugs: 10 per side
2. Plank: 45 seconds
3. Side plank: 30 seconds each side
4. Reverse crunches: 15 reps

### Complete Core Session

1. Dead bugs: 3 × 10 per side
2. Pallof press: 3 × 10 per side
3. Ab wheel rollouts: 3 × 10
4. Side planks: 3 × 30 sec per side
5. Hanging leg raises: 3 × 10
6. Russian twists: 2 × 20

### Stability Focus

1. RKC plank: 3 × 15 seconds
2. Dead bugs: 3 × 10 per side
3. Pallof press: 3 × 10 per side
4. Bird dogs: 3 × 10 per side
5. Side planks: 3 × 30 sec per side

### Six-Pack Focus

1. Hanging leg raises: 4 × 12
2. Cable crunches: 3 × 15
3. Reverse crunches: 3 × 15
4. Planks: 3 × 45 sec
5. Bicycle crunches: 3 × 20

### Home Workout

1. Dead bugs: 3 × 10 per side
2. Plank: 3 × 45 sec
3. Side planks: 3 × 30 sec per side
4. Reverse crunches: 3 × 15
5. Bird dogs: 3 × 10 per side
6. Bicycle crunches: 3 × 20

## How to Actually See Your Abs

Here's the truth: visible abs are more about body fat than ab workouts.

### Body Fat Matters Most
- Men need roughly 10-14% body fat for visible abs
- Women need roughly 16-20% body fat
- No amount of ab work reveals abs hidden under fat

### Nutrition Is Key
- Caloric deficit for fat loss
- Adequate protein to preserve muscle
- Consistency over time

### Training for Definition
- Build the abs with progressive overload
- Include anti-movement exercises
- Train abs 2-4 times per week

### Full Picture
- Reduce body fat (nutrition + overall training)
- Build ab muscle (direct ab training)
- Both are necessary for defined abs

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Only Doing Crunches
Crunches only train flexion. Include anti-movement exercises for complete core development.

### Using Momentum
Swinging through reps (especially leg raises) means your abs aren't doing the work. Control every rep.

### Holding Breath
Breathe! Exhale on exertion, maintain intra-abdominal pressure without breath-holding.

### Neglecting Lower Back
Your erector spinae are part of your core. Include exercises like bird dogs and Superman holds.

### Training Abs Every Day
Abs need recovery like any muscle. 3-4 sessions per week is plenty.

### Thinking Ab Work Burns Belly Fat
You can't spot-reduce fat. Ab exercises build muscle; diet burns fat.

## Programming Tips

### Frequency
- 3-4 ab sessions per week
- Can be at end of other workouts
- Short, focused sessions work best

### Volume
- 6-12 sets per session
- Mix exercise types
- Quality over quantity

### Progression
- Add time to planks
- Add reps to dynamic exercises
- Add weight when bodyweight is easy
- Progress to harder variations

### When to Train Abs
- End of workout (so fatigue doesn't hurt main lifts)
- Or as a separate session
- Not before heavy squats/deadlifts

## For Specific Goals

### For Lower Back Pain
- Prioritize anti-movement exercises
- Dead bugs, bird dogs, planks
- Build stability before power
- Avoid exercises that hurt

### For Athletes
- Emphasize anti-rotation and stability
- Include carries and throws
- Transfer to sport-specific movements
- Build power, not just endurance

### For Aesthetics
- Train all portions of the abs
- Progressive overload (add weight/reps)
- Don't neglect obliques
- Diet is 80% of the battle

### For Posture
- Strengthen entire core, including back
- Anti-extension and anti-rotation focus
- Address tight hip flexors
- Build endurance (longer holds)

## Conclusion

Effective ab training goes beyond crunches. Build a complete core with anti-movement exercises (planks, dead bugs, Pallof press), flexion work (leg raises, crunches), and rotation (woodchops).

Train abs 3-4 times per week with progressive overload. But remember: visible abs require low body fat, which comes from nutrition and overall training—not endless sit-ups.

Focus on function first, aesthetics second. A strong, stable core improves everything from lifting performance to daily life, whether your six-pack is visible or not.
