# Tricep Exercises: Build Bigger, Stronger Arms

Want bigger arms? Most people focus on biceps, but triceps make up about two-thirds of your upper arm mass. Strong triceps also improve your bench press, overhead press, and any pushing movement.

This guide covers the best exercises for complete tricep development.

## Tricep Anatomy

The triceps brachii has three heads (hence "tri"):

### Long Head
- Largest of the three heads
- Attaches to the shoulder blade
- Only head that crosses the shoulder joint
- Best trained with overhead movements

### Lateral Head
- On the outside of the arm
- Creates the "horseshoe" shape
- Most visible from the side
- Trained well with pushdowns

### Medial Head
- Underneath the other two heads
- Provides thickness
- Works in all tricep exercises
- Often undertrained

**Key insight:** For complete triceps, you need exercises that target all three heads—especially the long head, which requires overhead positioning.

## Best Tricep Exercises

### Compound Movements

These build overall tricep strength:

**Close-Grip Bench Press:**
1. Lie on bench, grip bar shoulder-width or narrower
2. Keep elbows tucked close to body
3. Lower bar to lower chest
4. Press back up
5. 3-4 sets of 8-10 reps

**Dips:**
1. Support yourself on parallel bars
2. Keep torso upright (more upright = more tricep)
3. Lower until upper arms are parallel
4. Press back up to lockout
5. 3 sets of 8-12 reps

**Diamond Push-Ups:**
1. Hands together under chest, forming diamond
2. Lower chest toward hands
3. Press back up
4. 3 sets of max reps

### Cable/Pulley Exercises

**Tricep Pushdowns (Rope):**
1. Stand facing cable machine, rope attachment
2. Elbows pinned to sides
3. Push down and spread rope at bottom
4. Control return
5. 3 sets of 12-15 reps

**Tricep Pushdowns (Straight Bar):**
1. Same as rope but with bar attachment
2. Overhand or underhand grip
3. Focus on lockout
4. 3 sets of 12-15 reps

**Overhead Cable Extension:**
Long head emphasis:
1. Face away from cable, rope behind head
2. Extend arms forward and up
3. Control return behind head
4. 3 sets of 12-15 reps

**Single-Arm Pushdown:**
1. Use single handle attachment
2. One arm at a time
3. Great for fixing imbalances
4. 3 sets of 12 per arm

### Dumbbell Exercises

**Overhead Tricep Extension:**
Long head focus:
1. Hold one dumbbell with both hands
2. Extend overhead
3. Lower behind head, elbows pointing up
4. Extend back up
5. 3 sets of 10-12 reps

**Lying Tricep Extension (Skull Crushers):**
1. Lie on bench, dumbbells or EZ bar
2. Arms extended above chest
3. Bend elbows, lowering weight toward forehead
4. Extend back up
5. 3 sets of 10-12 reps

**Tricep Kickbacks:**
1. Bent over, upper arm parallel to ground
2. Extend forearm back until arm is straight
3. Squeeze at full extension
4. Lower with control
5. 3 sets of 12-15 per arm

**Single-Arm Overhead Extension:**
1. Hold dumbbell in one hand
2. Extend overhead
3. Lower behind head
4. Extend back up
5. 3 sets of 10-12 per arm

### Bodyweight Exercises

**Bench Dips:**
1. Hands on bench behind you
2. Feet on floor (or elevated for harder)
3. Lower by bending elbows
4. Press back up
5. 3 sets of 12-15 reps

**Close-Grip Push-Ups:**
1. Hands closer than shoulder-width
2. Elbows stay close to body
3. Lower and press
4. 3 sets of max reps

**Bodyweight Tricep Extension:**
1. Hands on bar or elevated surface
2. Body at angle, arms extended
3. Bend elbows, lowering head toward hands
4. Extend back out
5. 3 sets of 10-15 reps

## Sample Tricep Workouts

### Quick Pump (10 minutes)

1. Rope pushdowns: 3 × 15
2. Overhead dumbbell extension: 3 × 12
3. Diamond push-ups: 2 × max

### Complete Tricep Session

1. Close-grip bench press: 4 × 8
2. Overhead cable extension: 3 × 12
3. Rope pushdowns: 3 × 15
4. Skull crushers: 3 × 10
5. Kickbacks: 2 × 12 per arm

### Long Head Focus

1. Overhead dumbbell extension: 4 × 10
2. Overhead cable extension: 3 × 12
3. Incline dumbbell tricep extension: 3 × 12
4. Rope pushdowns: 3 × 15

### Home Workout

1. Diamond push-ups: 4 × max
2. Bench dips: 3 × 15
3. Overhead dumbbell extension: 3 × 12
4. Close-grip push-ups: 3 × max
5. Tricep kickbacks: 3 × 12

### Strength Focus

1. Close-grip bench press: 5 × 5
2. Weighted dips: 4 × 6
3. Skull crushers: 3 × 8
4. Pushdowns: 3 × 10

## Form Tips

### For Pushdowns
- Keep elbows pinned to sides
- Don't lean forward excessively
- Full extension at bottom
- Control the return—don't let weight pull arms up

### For Overhead Extensions
- Keep elbows pointing forward/up
- Don't let elbows flare out
- Lower until you feel stretch in triceps
- Control the movement throughout

### For Skull Crushers
- Angle arms slightly back (not perfectly vertical)
- Lower to forehead or just behind head
- Keep elbows relatively fixed
- Don't let them flare out

### For Dips
- Stay upright for tricep focus (lean forward for chest)
- Full lockout at top
- Don't go too deep if shoulders hurt
- Control the descent

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Swinging and Using Momentum
Especially on pushdowns—keep body still and let triceps do the work.

### Flaring Elbows
Elbows pointing outward shifts work away from triceps. Keep them in.

### Not Training Long Head
Pushdowns alone won't fully develop triceps. Include overhead movements.

### Partial Reps
Full extension and full stretch. Don't cut range of motion short.

### Too Much Weight
Tricep isolation requires moderate weight with strict form. Ego lifting doesn't work here.

### Neglecting Compound Work
Isolation is great, but close-grip pressing and dips build overall strength.

## Programming Tips

### Frequency
- 2-3 tricep sessions per week
- Or include tricep work after chest/shoulder days
- Triceps recover fairly quickly

### Volume
- 8-12 direct sets per week for most people
- Compound pressing also trains triceps
- More isn't always better—quality over quantity

### Exercise Selection
Include at least:
- One overhead movement (long head)
- One pushdown variation
- One compound movement (dips or close-grip press)

### Rep Ranges
- Compounds: 6-10 reps
- Isolation: 10-15 reps
- Mix ranges for complete development

## Elbow-Friendly Modifications

If tricep work bothers your elbows:

- **Warm up thoroughly:** 5-10 minutes of light work first
- **Use higher reps:** Lower weight, more reps = less joint stress
- **Avoid extreme stretch:** Don't go too deep on skull crushers
- **Try different angles:** Some positions feel better than others
- **Use cables:** Often smoother than free weights
- **Strengthen forearms:** Sometimes the real issue

## Stretching for Triceps

### Overhead Tricep Stretch
1. Raise one arm, bend elbow
2. Hand reaches down back
3. Use other hand to push elbow
4. Hold 30 seconds per side

### Cross-Body Tricep Stretch
1. Reach arm across chest
2. Use other hand to pull at elbow
3. Rotate to feel different parts
4. Hold 30 seconds per side

## Conclusion

Strong triceps improve pushing strength and arm size. The key is hitting all three heads—especially the often-neglected long head with overhead movements.

Include a mix of compound pressing (close-grip bench, dips), overhead extensions (for long head), and pushdowns (for lateral head). Use proper form, control the weight, and progress over time.

Train triceps 2-3 times per week, and you'll see noticeable improvements in both strength and size.
