Tight hips are almost universal in modern life. Hours of sitting shortens the hip flexors, weakens the glutes, and creates stiffness that affects everything from walking to squatting to simply feeling comfortable in your own body.

The good news: hip tightness responds well to consistent stretching. A few minutes daily can make a dramatic difference.

Here are the best stretches for tight hips, plus a routine you can do anywhere.

## Why Your Hips Are Tight

Understanding the problem helps you fix it:

**Prolonged sitting:** When you sit, your hip flexors stay shortened for hours. Over time, they adapt to this position.

**Weak glutes:** Sitting also makes your glutes inactive. When glutes don't do their job, hip flexors work overtime.

**Limited movement variety:** Modern life involves a narrow range of hip positions compared to what our bodies evolved for.

**Stress:** Tension accumulates in the hips for many people (along with neck and shoulders).

The solution: regularly take your hips through their full range of motion with targeted stretches.

## The Best Hip Stretches

### 1. 90/90 Stretch

One of the most effective hip openers, targeting both internal and external rotation.

**How to do it:**
1. Sit on the floor with one leg in front (knee bent 90°, outer thigh on floor)
2. Other leg to the side (knee bent 90°, inner thigh on floor)
3. Both knees at roughly 90-degree angles
4. Sit tall and lean slightly forward over the front leg
5. Hold 30-60 seconds, then switch sides

**Feel it in:** Front hip external rotators, back hip internal rotators

**Tip:** If you can't get flat, sit on a yoga block or cushion.

### 2. Half-Kneeling Hip Flexor Stretch

The essential stretch for tight hip flexors from sitting.

**How to do it:**
1. Kneel on one knee, other foot in front (90° angle at both knees)
2. Tuck your pelvis under (posterior pelvic tilt)—this is crucial
3. While maintaining the tuck, shift weight slightly forward
4. You should feel stretch in the front of the hip of the kneeling leg
5. Hold 30-60 seconds each side

**Feel it in:** Hip flexors (psoas, rectus femoris) of the back leg

**Tip:** The pelvic tuck is essential. Without it, you'll just arch your lower back instead of stretching the hip flexor.

### 3. Pigeon Pose

Deep stretch for the external rotators and glutes.

**How to do it:**
1. From hands and knees, bring one knee forward and angle it toward your wrist
2. Extend the other leg straight behind you
3. Lower your hips toward the floor
4. Keep hips square (both pointing forward)
5. Stay upright or fold forward over the front leg for a deeper stretch
6. Hold 60-90 seconds each side

**Feel it in:** Outer hip and glute of the front leg

**Tip:** If this is too intense, do figure-4 stretch lying on your back instead.

### 4. Figure-4 Stretch (Supine Pigeon)

A gentler alternative to pigeon pose, good for beginners.

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on your back, knees bent, feet flat on floor
2. Cross one ankle over the opposite knee
3. Reach through and pull the bottom thigh toward your chest
4. Keep the top foot flexed
5. Hold 30-60 seconds each side

**Feel it in:** Outer hip and glute of the crossed leg

### 5. Frog Stretch

Opens the inner thighs (adductors), which also contribute to hip tightness.

**How to do it:**
1. Start on hands and knees
2. Widen your knees as far as comfortable
3. Turn feet out so inner edges are on the floor
4. Slowly shift hips back toward your heels
5. Keep spine neutral—don't round or arch excessively
6. Hold 60-90 seconds

**Feel it in:** Inner thighs (adductors), groin

**Tip:** Go slowly and don't force depth. This stretch can be intense.

### 6. Couch Stretch

Intense hip flexor and quad stretch, great for undoing sitting damage.

**How to do it:**
1. Kneel facing away from a wall (or couch)
2. Place one knee on the floor close to the wall, shin vertical against it
3. Other foot in front in a lunge position
4. Tuck your pelvis under
5. Gradually bring your torso more upright
6. Hold 60-90 seconds each side

**Feel it in:** Hip flexors and quadriceps of the back leg

**Tip:** This is intense. Start with shorter holds and work up gradually.

### 7. World's Greatest Stretch

A dynamic stretch that hits multiple areas including hips.

**How to do it:**
1. Step into a lunge, placing same-side hand on the floor
2. Keep back leg straight
3. Rotate opposite arm toward the ceiling, opening your chest
4. Hold 5-10 seconds
5. Bring elbow down toward the floor inside your front foot
6. Hold 5-10 seconds
7. Return to standing and repeat on the other side
8. Do 5-6 per side

**Feel it in:** Hip flexors, hamstrings, inner thighs, thoracic spine

### 8. Butterfly Stretch

Opens the inner hips and groin.

**How to do it:**
1. Sit on the floor
2. Bring soles of your feet together, knees falling out to the sides
3. Hold your feet and sit tall
4. Gently press knees toward the floor with your elbows
5. For a deeper stretch, hinge forward at the hips with a flat back
6. Hold 30-60 seconds

**Feel it in:** Inner thighs, groin, hip adductors

### 9. Supine Twist

Rotational stretch that releases hip and lower back tension.

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on your back, arms out to the sides
2. Bring both knees toward your chest
3. Lower both knees to one side, stacking them
4. Keep both shoulders on the floor
5. Turn head opposite to knees if comfortable
6. Hold 30-60 seconds each side

**Feel it in:** Outer hips, lower back, obliques

### 10. Hip Circles (Dynamic)

Movement-based stretch that takes hips through their range.

**How to do it:**
1. Stand on one leg (hold something for balance)
2. Lift the other knee up
3. Make large circles with the knee—forward, out, back, down
4. Do 10 circles in each direction
5. Switch legs

**Feel it in:** All around the hip, lubricating the joint

## The 10-Minute Hip Opening Routine

Do this routine daily for significant improvements in hip mobility:

**Dynamic warm-up (1 minute):**
- Hip circles: 10 each direction, each leg

**Deep stretches (8 minutes):**
- 90/90 stretch: 45 seconds each side
- Half-kneeling hip flexor stretch: 45 seconds each side
- Pigeon pose (or figure-4): 60 seconds each side
- Frog stretch: 60 seconds
- Butterfly stretch: 45 seconds

**Movement finish (1 minute):**
- World's greatest stretch: 5 each side

## Quick Relief (5-Minute Version)

When time is limited:
- Half-kneeling hip flexor stretch: 30 seconds each side
- Figure-4 stretch: 30 seconds each side
- 90/90 stretch: 30 seconds each side
- Hip circles: 10 each direction, each leg

## At Your Desk (2 Minutes)

For breaks during the workday:
- Seated figure-4 stretch: Cross ankle over knee, lean forward. 30 seconds each side.
- Standing hip flexor stretch: Step back into a lunge, tuck pelvis, squeeze back glute. 30 seconds each side.

## Tips for Better Results

### Frequency Beats Duration

Five minutes daily produces better results than 30 minutes once a week. Consistency is key.

### Breathe Deeply

Deep breathing helps muscles relax into stretches. Exhale as you move deeper into each position.

### Don't Force It

Stretching should feel like tension, not pain. Aggressive stretching can cause injury and trigger protective muscle tightening.

### Be Patient

Flexibility improves over weeks and months, not days. Significant changes in hip mobility typically take 4-8 weeks of consistent work.

### Address the Root Cause

Stretching helps, but also:
- Take movement breaks from sitting every 30-60 minutes
- Strengthen your glutes
- Vary your positions throughout the day

## When Hip Tightness Is More Than Tightness

See a professional if you experience:
- Sharp or stabbing pain
- Pain that radiates down your leg
- Clicking, catching, or locking in the hip
- Pain that doesn't improve with stretching
- Significant limitation in daily activities

These could indicate issues beyond simple muscle tightness that need proper evaluation.

## The Bottom Line

Tight hips are fixable with consistent attention:

1. **Stretch daily** (5-10 minutes minimum)
2. **Target all areas:** Hip flexors, external rotators, internal rotators, adductors
3. **Move throughout the day**, not just during dedicated stretching
4. **Be consistent**—results take weeks
5. **Address lifestyle factors** like prolonged sitting

Your hips are designed to be mobile. They've just adapted to too much sitting. With regular stretching, you can restore that mobility and move better in everything you do.

Start today. Your hips will thank you.
