# Best Exercises for Knees: Strengthen and Protect Your Joints

Strong muscles around your knees protect the joint, reduce pain, and keep you moving well. Whether you're preventing problems or recovering from injury, these exercises build the strength your knees need.

## How to Protect Your Knees

Your knees are supported by surrounding muscles:

**Quadriceps** – Front of thigh, extends the knee
**Hamstrings** – Back of thigh, flexes the knee
**Glutes** – Control hip position, which affects knee alignment
**Calves** – Support ankle, affecting knee mechanics

Weakness in any of these increases knee stress. Strengthening them takes the load off the joint.

## Best Exercises for Knee Strength

### 1. Straight Leg Raise

**Quad strengthening without bending the knee.**

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back, one knee bent, one leg straight
2. Tighten quad of straight leg
3. Raise leg to height of bent knee
4. Hold briefly at top
5. Lower with control

**Sets/reps:** 3 sets × 15 each leg

**Why it works:** Strengthens quads with zero knee joint stress. Great for pain or post-injury.

### 2. Terminal Knee Extension (TKE)

**Targets the VMO (inner quad).**

**How to do it:**
1. Loop band behind knee, attach to anchor
2. Stand facing anchor, slight knee bend
3. Straighten knee against band resistance
4. Squeeze quad at full extension
5. Return with control

**Sets/reps:** 3 sets × 15 each leg

**Why it works:** Isolates the VMO, which stabilizes the kneecap. Essential for knee rehab.

### 3. Glute Bridge

**Strengthens glutes to reduce knee stress.**

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back, knees bent, feet flat
2. Drive through heels, lift hips
3. Squeeze glutes at top
4. Don't hyperextend spine
5. Lower with control

**Sets/reps:** 3 sets × 15 reps

**Why it works:** Strong glutes control hip rotation, reducing inward knee collapse.

### 4. Clamshell

**Hip stability for knee alignment.**

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on side, knees bent, feet together
2. Open top knee toward ceiling
3. Keep feet touching, don't rotate hips
4. Lower with control

**Sets/reps:** 3 sets × 15 each side

**Why it works:** Strengthens glute medius, which prevents knee valgus (inward collapse).

### 5. Step-Up

**Functional single-leg strength.**

**How to do it:**
1. Stand facing step or box
2. Place one foot fully on step
3. Drive through heel to stand up
4. Don't push off back foot
5. Step down with control

**Sets/reps:** 3 sets × 10-12 each leg

**Why it works:** Single-leg strength in functional pattern. Control is key.

**Start low:** Use 4-6 inch step, increase height as strength improves.

### 6. Wall Sit

**Isometric quad strength.**

**How to do it:**
1. Back against wall
2. Slide down until thighs parallel to floor
3. Keep knees at 90° (not past toes)
4. Hold position

**Sets/reps:** 3 sets × 30-60 seconds

**Why it works:** Builds quad endurance without joint movement. Low impact.

### 7. Partial Squat

**Controlled knee bending.**

**How to do it:**
1. Feet shoulder-width apart
2. Squat down 45-90° (pain-free range)
3. Keep knees over toes
4. Push through heels to stand

**Sets/reps:** 3 sets × 12-15 reps

**Why it works:** Strengthens quads and glutes through safe range. Progress depth over time.

### 8. Leg Curl (Lying or Seated)

**Hamstring strengthening.**

**How to do it:**
1. Machine set up properly
2. Curl heels toward glutes
3. Squeeze hamstrings at top
4. Lower with control

**Sets/reps:** 3 sets × 12-15 reps

**Why it works:** Strong hamstrings balance the quads and stabilize the knee.

**No machine?** Do Nordic curl negatives or slider leg curls.

### 9. Calf Raise

**Lower leg strength for knee stability.**

**How to do it:**
1. Stand on edge of step, heels hanging off
2. Rise onto toes as high as possible
3. Lower below step level (full stretch)
4. Control throughout

**Sets/reps:** 3 sets × 15-20 reps

**Why it works:** Strong calves support the ankle-knee chain.

### 10. Monster Walks

**Glute activation with knee control.**

**How to do it:**
1. Band around legs (above knees or ankles)
2. Quarter squat position
3. Walk forward and sideways
4. Keep knees pushed out against band

**Sets/reps:** 2 sets × 20 steps each direction

**Why it works:** Activates glutes while training knee alignment under tension.

## Complete Knee Strengthening Routine

### Workout A: Knee-Focused (20 minutes)

**Warm-Up (3 minutes)**
- Walk in place: 1 minute
- Bodyweight squats (shallow): 10 reps
- Leg swings: 10 each leg

**Main Workout**

1. **Straight Leg Raises**: 3 × 15 each leg
2. **Terminal Knee Extensions**: 3 × 15 each leg
3. **Wall Sit**: 3 × 30-45 seconds
4. **Clamshells**: 3 × 15 each side
5. **Glute Bridges**: 3 × 15

**Cool-Down (2 minutes)**
- Quad stretch: 30 seconds each
- Hamstring stretch: 30 seconds each

### Workout B: Functional Strength (25 minutes)

**Warm-Up (3 minutes)**
- Same as above

**Main Workout**

1. **Partial Squats**: 3 × 12
2. **Step-Ups** (low step): 3 × 10 each leg
3. **Glute Bridges**: 3 × 12
4. **Leg Curls** (or slider curls): 3 × 12
5. **Monster Walks**: 2 × 20 steps each direction
6. **Calf Raises**: 3 × 15

**Cool-Down (2 minutes)**
- Stretching

## Exercises for Specific Knee Issues

### Runner's Knee (Patellofemoral Pain)
**Priority exercises:**
- Terminal knee extensions (VMO focus)
- Clamshells and glute work
- Straight leg raises
- Step-downs with control

### Knee Arthritis
**Priority exercises:**
- Low-impact strengthening
- Straight leg raises
- Water exercises
- Partial range squats (pain-free)
- Cycling

### After ACL Surgery
**Priority exercises:**
- Follow your PT's protocol
- Straight leg raises early
- Progress to step-ups, squats
- Hamstring strengthening crucial

### General Knee Pain
**Priority exercises:**
- Start with isometrics (straight leg raises, wall sits)
- Add partial range exercises
- Progress to full range as tolerated
- Always strengthening, not stretching, fixes most knee pain

## Exercises to Modify or Avoid

### Deep Squats (If Painful)
- Reduce depth to pain-free range
- Use box to control depth
- Progress gradually

### Lunges (If Painful)
- Use shorter stride
- Don't let knee go past toes
- Try reverse lunges instead

### Leg Extensions (Full Range)
- Can stress kneecap
- Use partial range (last 30° of extension)
- Or substitute straight leg raises

### High-Impact Activities
- Running and jumping stress knees
- Build strength first
- Progress impact gradually

## Tips for Knee Health

### Strengthen Before Stretching
- Most knee pain comes from weakness
- Build muscle around the joint
- Flexibility without strength creates instability

### Control is Key
- Slow, controlled movements
- No bouncing or jerking
- Quality over quantity

### Don't Train Through Sharp Pain
- Discomfort may be acceptable
- Sharp or worsening pain means stop
- Some soreness next day is normal

### Balance Quads and Hamstrings
- Don't neglect hamstrings
- Include leg curls, RDLs, hip hinges
- Balance prevents problems

### Don't Forget Glutes
- Weak glutes = poor knee alignment
- Include bridges, clamshells, hip work
- Often the missing link in knee rehab

## When to See a Professional

See a doctor or physical therapist if:
- Pain doesn't improve with rest
- Swelling, redness, or warmth
- Knee gives way or locks
- Pain worsens with exercise
- Injury from trauma

## The Bottom Line

Strong muscles protect your knees better than anything else. Focus on quadriceps (especially VMO), hamstrings, glutes, and calves. Start with low-impact exercises like straight leg raises and wall sits, then progress to functional movements like step-ups and squats.

Control your movements. Progress gradually. Address weakness in your hips—knee problems often start there.

Consistent strengthening will reduce pain, improve function, and keep your knees healthy for years. The exercises are simple; doing them regularly is what works.
