      </p>

      <h2>The Golden Rule</h2>

      <p>
        <strong>Be considerate of others.</strong> Almost every gym etiquette rule comes down to this. You're sharing space with other people who are also trying to get their workout done.
      </p>

      <h2>Equipment Etiquette</h2>

      <h3>Rerack Your Weights</h3>
      <p>
        This is the #1 rule. When you're done with dumbbells, barbells, or plates, put them back where they belong. Nobody wants to hunt for equipment or unload someone else's bar.
      </p>
      <ul>
        <li>Dumbbells go back on the rack, in order</li>
        <li>Plates go back on the tree or rack</li>
        <li>Barbells should be stripped (no plates left on)</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>Wipe Down Equipment</h3>
      <p>
        After using benches, machines, or mats, wipe them down with the provided spray and towels. Nobody wants to lie in your sweat.
      </p>

      <h3>Don't Hog Equipment</h3>
      <p>
        During busy times, be mindful of how long you're using popular equipment:
      </p>
      <ul>
        <li>Limit rest times on machines others are waiting for</li>
        <li>Don't do 10 exercises at the squat rack when there's a line</li>
        <li>If you're resting between sets, offer to let someone "work in" (alternate sets)</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>Don't Block the Dumbbell Rack</h3>
      <p>
        Grab your dumbbells and step back. Don't stand directly in front of the rack doing your curls—you're blocking everyone else's access.
      </p>

      <h3>Ask Before Using Equipment Someone's Near</h3>
      <p>
        If someone is standing near a machine or has a towel/water bottle on it, ask "Are you using this?" Don't assume equipment is free just because no one is actively on it.
      </p>

      <h2>Space and Awareness</h2>

      <h3>Don't Walk in Front of the Mirror During Someone's Set</h3>
      <p>
        People use mirrors to check their form. Walking between them and the mirror during a lift is distracting and inconsiderate. Wait for them to finish or go around.
      </p>

      <h3>Give People Space</h3>
      <p>
        Don't set up your exercise right next to someone when there's plenty of room elsewhere. People need space to move, especially during dynamic exercises.
      </p>

      <h3>Be Aware of Your Surroundings</h3>
      <p>
        Look before you walk through the weight floor. People are lifting heavy objects—don't walk into someone's deadlift path.
      </p>

      <h2>Noise and Distraction</h2>

      <h3>Keep Phone Calls Outside</h3>
      <p>
        Don't take phone calls on the gym floor. Step outside or to a lobby area. Nobody wants to hear your conversation while they're working out.
      </p>

      <h3>Use Headphones</h3>
      <p>
        If you want to listen to music or podcasts, use headphones. Don't play audio from your phone speaker.
      </p>

      <h3>Minimal Grunting</h3>
      <p>
        Some noise during heavy lifts is normal and acceptable. Excessive screaming or grunting on every rep is obnoxious. Find a balance.
      </p>

      <h3>Don't Drop Weights Unnecessarily</h3>
      <p>
        Controlled lowering is part of the exercise. Dropping dumbbells or slamming weights when it's not necessary is loud, damages equipment, and looks attention-seeking. Exception: Olympic lifts and some heavy barbell work where dropping is safer.
      </p>

      <h2>Social Etiquette</h2>

      <h3>Don't Give Unsolicited Advice</h3>
      <p>
        Unless someone is about to seriously injure themselves, don't correct other people's form or offer training advice they didn't ask for. It's condescending.
      </p>

      <h3>Don't Stare</h3>
      <p>
        People are self-conscious at the gym. Don't stare at others, regardless of what they look like or what they're doing.
      </p>

      <h3>It's OK to Ask for Help</h3>
      <p>
        Staff are there to help. Most regular gym-goers are friendly if you politely ask how to use a machine or if they're done with equipment.
      </p>

      <h3>Respect the Headphones</h3>
      <p>
        Someone wearing headphones usually doesn't want to chat. If you need to communicate, a quick gesture or tap to get attention is fine, but don't expect a long conversation.
      </p>

      <h2>Locker Room Etiquette</h2>

      <h3>Don't Hog Space</h3>
      <p>
        Keep your belongings contained to your area. Don't spread across the entire bench.
      </p>

      <h3>Respect Privacy</h3>
      <p>
        Eyes forward. No staring, no commenting on others' bodies.
      </p>

      <h3>Clean Up After Yourself</h3>
      <p>
        Don't leave towels, trash, or toiletries lying around. Use the provided bins and containers.
      </p>

      <h2>What NOT to Worry About</h2>

      <p>
        Some things feel awkward but are totally fine:
      </p>

        <li><strong>Not knowing how to use a machine:</strong> Ask staff or look it up.</li>
        <li><strong>Sweating a lot:</strong> It's a gym. People sweat. Just wipe equipment.</li>
        <li><strong>Taking time between sets:</strong> Reasonable rest is fine.</li>
        <li><strong>Looking in the mirror:</strong> That's what it's for.</li>

        It happens. Your options:
      </p>

      <ul>

        <li><strong>Work around it:</strong> Choose different equipment or timing</li>
        <li><strong>Report to staff:</strong> For serious or repeated issues</li>
        <li><strong>Let it go:</strong> Minor annoyances aren't worth the stress</li>
      </ul>

      <p>

        <li>Wipe down equipment after use</li>
        <li>Let people work in during busy times</li>
        <li>Use headphones for personal audio</li>
        <li>Give others space</li>
        <li>Ask before using occupied-looking equipment</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>Don't:</h3>
      <ul>
        <li>Leave weights on the floor or bars</li>
        <li>Hog equipment during peak hours</li>
        <li>Stand in front of the dumbbell rack</li>
        <li>Take calls on the gym floor</li>
        <li>Give unsolicited advice</li>
        <li>Drop weights unnecessarily</li>
      </ul>

      <h2>The Bottom Line</h2>
