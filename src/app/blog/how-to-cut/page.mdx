# How to Cut: The Complete Guide to Losing Fat While Keeping Muscle

You've built the muscle—now it's time to reveal it. Cutting is the art of losing fat while preserving your hard-earned muscle. Done right, you'll get leaner while staying strong. Done wrong, you'll lose muscle along with the fat and end up skinny-fat. Here's how to cut properly.

## What Is Cutting?

Cutting is a fat loss phase where you eat in a calorie deficit while training to maintain muscle mass. The goal: lose fat, keep muscle, reveal the physique you've built.

### Cutting vs. Just Losing Weight

**Regular weight loss:** Lose weight, some muscle, some fat
**Cutting:** Lose primarily fat, preserve muscle

The difference is:
- Higher protein intake
- Continued resistance training
- Moderate (not extreme) deficit
- Patience

## Setting Up Your Cut

### Step 1: Find Maintenance Calories

Same as bulking—you need to know your starting point.

**Methods:**
1. Track food and weight for 2 weeks
2. If weight stable = maintenance found
3. Use TDEE calculator as estimate

**Quick estimates:**
- Sedentary: BW × 14-15
- Moderately active: BW × 15-16
- Very active: BW × 17-18

### Step 2: Create a Deficit

**Conservative (recommended): 300-500 calorie deficit**
- Rate: ~0.5-1 lb loss per week
- Muscle preservation: Excellent
- Sustainable: Yes

**Moderate: 500-750 calorie deficit**
- Rate: ~1-1.5 lbs loss per week
- Muscle preservation: Good
- Sustainable: Moderate

**Aggressive: 750-1000+ calorie deficit**
- Rate: 1.5-2+ lbs loss per week
- Muscle preservation: Risky
- Sustainable: Difficult

**Recommendation:** Start with 300-500 deficit. You can always increase later.

### Step 3: Set Macros

**Protein: INCREASE from bulk (1-1.2g per pound)**
- Preserves muscle during deficit
- Keeps you full
- Higher than bulking phase
- Most important macro

**Fat: 0.3-0.4g per pound bodyweight**
- Hormone health (don't go too low)
- Essential fatty acids
- Keeps you satiated

**Carbs: Fill remaining calories**
- Lower than bulking (deficit comes mostly from carbs)
- Still important for training
- Time around workouts

### Sample Cutting Macros (180 lb person, 2,200 cal)

| Macro | Grams | Calories |
|-------|-------|----------|
| Protein | 200g | 800 cal |
| Fat | 60g | 540 cal |
| Carbs | 215g | 860 cal |
| **Total** | — | **2,200 cal** |

## Cutting Nutrition Strategies

### Maximize Satiety

**High-volume, low-calorie foods:**
- Vegetables (unlimited basically)
- Leafy greens
- Berries
- Watermelon
- Egg whites
- Lean meats
- Plain Greek yogurt

**High protein at every meal:**
- Keeps you full longest
- 30-50g per meal
- Preserves muscle

**Fiber:**
- 25-35g daily
- Slows digestion
- Improves fullness

### Sample Cutting Day

**Breakfast (450 cal):**
- 4 egg whites + 1 whole egg
- 2 slices Ezekiel bread
- Large serving spinach
- Salsa

**Lunch (500 cal):**
- 8 oz chicken breast
- Large salad (lettuce, tomato, cucumber)
- 1/2 cup rice
- Light dressing

**Pre-Workout Snack (200 cal):**
- Greek yogurt (0% fat)
- Berries

**Post-Workout (400 cal):**
- Protein shake (40g whey)
- Banana
- Rice cake

**Dinner (500 cal):**
- 8 oz white fish or shrimp
- Large serving roasted vegetables
- Small sweet potato

**Evening (200 cal):**
- Cottage cheese
- Cucumber slices
- Celery

**Total: ~2,250 cal, ~200g protein**

### Foods to Minimize

**Calorie-dense, easy to overeat:**
- Oils and butter (use sparingly)
- Nuts (easy to overeat)
- Full-fat dairy
- Fried foods
- Alcohol
- Sugary drinks
- Processed snacks

**Not "bad"—just hard to fit in a deficit.**

### Meal Timing Tips

**Around workouts:**
- Some carbs pre-workout (energy)
- Protein + carbs post-workout (recovery)

**General:**
- Don't skip meals (leads to overeating later)
- Larger meals when hunger is highest (varies by person)
- Some people prefer IF; others don't—personal preference

## Training During a Cut

### The Golden Rule

**Maintain training intensity.**

Your training stimulus tells your body to keep muscle. If you reduce intensity or stop lifting, your body has no reason to preserve muscle.

### Training Modifications

**What to KEEP:**
- Heavy compound lifts
- Training frequency
- Effort level (RPE)

**What you might REDUCE:**
- Total volume (slightly)
- Number of sets
- Accessory work

**Why reduce volume?**
- Recovery is impaired in a deficit
- Can't recover from as much
- Quality over quantity

### Sample Cutting Adjustments

**Bulking Program:**
- Squat: 4 × 6
- Leg Press: 4 × 10
- Lunges: 3 × 10
- Leg Extension: 3 × 12
- Leg Curl: 4 × 10

**Cutting Program:**
- Squat: 4 × 6 (keep heavy)
- Leg Press: 3 × 10 (reduce 1 set)
- Lunges: 2 × 10 (reduce 1 set)
- Leg Extension: 2 × 12 (reduce 1 set)
- Leg Curl: 3 × 10 (reduce 1 set)

**Same exercises, same intensity, slightly less volume.**

### Cardio During a Cut

**Role of cardio:**
- Increases calorie expenditure
- Creates larger deficit without eating less
- Optional but helpful

**Types:**
- LISS (Low Intensity Steady State): Walking, easy cycling
- HIIT: Sprint intervals, circuit training
- MISS (Moderate Intensity): Jogging, elliptical

**Recommendations:**
- Start with minimal cardio
- Add as needed to maintain deficit
- Don't overdo it (interferes with recovery)
- 2-4 sessions per week is usually plenty

**Cardio Progression:**
- Week 1-4: 2 × 20 min LISS
- Week 5-8: 3 × 25 min LISS
- Week 9-12: 3-4 × 30 min LISS or add HIIT

## Tracking Progress

### What to Track

**Daily:**
- Weight (same time, same conditions)
- Food intake

**Weekly:**
- Average weight (more reliable than daily)
- Strength on key lifts

**Biweekly/Monthly:**
- Progress photos
- Measurements (waist, etc.)

### Interpreting the Scale

**Weight fluctuates due to:**
- Water retention
- Food volume
- Sodium intake
- Carb intake
- Hormones
- Sleep
- Stress

**Use weekly averages:**
- Weigh daily, average each week
- Compare weekly averages
- Look at 2-4 week trends

### Rate of Loss Guidelines

| Starting Body Fat | Weekly Loss Target |
|-------------------|-------------------|
| 20%+ | 1-1.5 lbs |
| 15-20% | 0.75-1 lb |
| 12-15% | 0.5-0.75 lb |
| 10-12% | 0.25-0.5 lb |

**Leaner = slower rate needed to preserve muscle.**

### When to Adjust

**If weight stalling (2+ weeks):**
1. First check: Are you tracking accurately?
2. Reduce calories by 100-150
3. Or add 1-2 cardio sessions
4. Re-evaluate in 2 weeks

**If losing too fast:**
- Add 100-200 calories
- May be losing muscle

**If strength dropping significantly:**
- Deficit may be too aggressive
- Add calories
- Reduce cardio
- Prioritize sleep

## Common Cutting Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Cutting Too Aggressively

**The Problem:** 1000+ calorie deficit, crash dieting
**The Result:** Muscle loss, metabolic adaptation, binges
**The Fix:** Moderate 300-500 calorie deficit

### Mistake 2: Dropping Weights

**The Problem:** "Light weight, high reps to tone"
**The Result:** Loss of strength and muscle
**The Fix:** Keep weights heavy, reduce volume slightly

### Mistake 3: Too Much Cardio

**The Problem:** 2 hours cardio daily, running on empty
**The Result:** Burnout, muscle loss, metabolic issues
**The Fix:** Minimum effective dose of cardio

### Mistake 4: Not Enough Protein

**The Problem:** Same protein as bulking or less
**The Result:** Increased muscle loss
**The Fix:** INCREASE protein during cut (1-1.2g/lb)

### Mistake 5: Weekend Blowouts

**The Problem:** Perfect Monday-Friday, overeat weekends
**The Result:** No weekly deficit, no fat loss
**The Fix:** Account for social events, stay consistent

### Mistake 6: Cutting Too Long

**The Problem:** Dieting for 6+ months straight
**The Result:** Metabolic adaptation, mental burnout
**The Fix:** 8-16 weeks, then maintenance break

## Diet Breaks and Refeeds

### Diet Breaks

**What:** 1-2 weeks at maintenance calories
**When:** Every 8-12 weeks of dieting
**Why:** Reduce metabolic adaptation, mental reset

**How:**
- Increase calories to maintenance
- Primarily add carbs
- Keep training
- Not a free-for-all

### Refeeds

**What:** 1-2 high carb days per week
**When:** During extended cuts, especially when lean
**Why:** Replenish glycogen, boost leptin, improve training

**How:**
- Increase carbs 50-100%
- Keep fat low
- Slight surplus or maintenance
- Time on hard training days

## How Long Should You Cut?

### Duration Guidelines

**Typical cut:** 8-16 weeks
**Maximum before break:** 12-16 weeks

**End your cut when:**
- Reached goal body fat (~10-12% for visible abs)
- Strength significantly declining
- Mentally burned out
- Reached 12-16 week mark
- Life circumstances require maintenance

### After the Cut

**Don't immediately bulk:**
1. Reverse diet to maintenance (add 100-200 cal/week)
2. Maintain for 2-4 weeks minimum
3. Allow body to stabilize
4. Then decide: cut more, maintain, or bulk

## Supplements for Cutting

### Actually Helpful

**Caffeine:**
- Appetite suppression
- Energy for training
- Slight metabolic boost

**Protein Powder:**
- Helps hit protein targets
- Low calorie, high protein

**Creatine:**
- Continue taking (don't stop)
- Preserves strength and muscle

### Skip These

- Fat burners (marginal effect, usually just caffeine)
- CLA
- L-Carnitine (maybe slight effect)
- "Shredding" supplements

## The Mental Game

### Expect Hunger

You're in a deficit—some hunger is normal. Strategies:
- High volume foods
- Stay busy
- Drink water/zero-cal drinks
- Schedule meals strategically

### Expect Fatigue

Less energy in = less energy out. Manage with:
- Prioritize sleep
- Caffeine (strategically)
- Time intense sessions when energy highest

### Expect Strength Plateau/Decline

Especially as you get leaner:
- Don't panic
- Maintain as much as possible
- It comes back when you eat more

### Stay Consistent, Not Perfect

One bad day doesn't ruin a cut. One bad week might slow things. Consistency over perfection.

## The Bottom Line

Cutting is a controlled process: moderate deficit, high protein, continued training, patience.

**The formula:**
1. Create 300-500 calorie deficit
2. Eat 1-1.2g protein per pound
3. Keep lifting heavy (reduce volume slightly)
4. Add cardio as needed
5. Track progress, adjust accordingly
6. Take breaks every 8-12 weeks

The muscle you built doesn't disappear overnight. Protect it with protein and training, and let the fat slowly melt away. When you're done, you'll reveal the physique that was hiding underneath.

Start your deficit. Keep training hard. The lean look is coming.
