# Shoulder Pain Exercises: Relieve Pain and Restore Function

Shoulder pain affects nearly 70% of people at some point in their lives. Whether it's from sleeping wrong, desk work, or overuse, the good news is that most shoulder pain responds well to targeted exercises.

**Key insight:** The shoulder is the most mobile joint in your body, which makes it prone to problems. Building strength and control in the right muscles usually resolves pain better than rest alone.

---

## Understanding Shoulder Pain

### Common Causes

| Condition | Typical Signs |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Rotator cuff issues** | Pain with overhead reaching, weakness |
| **Impingement** | Pain when lifting arm, pinching sensation |
| **Frozen shoulder** | Severe stiffness, limited motion |
| **Bursitis** | Pain on outside of shoulder, worse lying on it |
| **Instability** | Feeling of looseness, apprehension |
| **Referred from neck** | Travels down arm, neck movement triggers it |

### The Real Problem

Most shoulder pain isn't from a single injury—it develops from:
- Weak rotator cuff muscles
- Poor scapular (shoulder blade) control
- Muscle imbalances (chest tight, back weak)
- Forward head and rounded shoulder posture

**The solution:** Address weakness and imbalance, not just symptoms.

---

## Phase 1: Pain Relief

### Pendulum Exercises

Start here when shoulder is very painful.

**How to do it:**
1. Bend at waist, supporting yourself with good arm on table
2. Let painful arm hang completely relaxed
3. Gently swing arm in small circles (clockwise, then counterclockwise)
4. Swing forward and back
5. Swing side to side
6. Keep arm completely passive—body creates movement
7. Perform 1-2 minutes each direction, 3-4 times daily

### Passive Range of Motion

Use good arm to move painful arm.

**Supine Flexion:**
1. Lie on back
2. Hold wrist of painful arm with good hand
3. Lift both arms overhead
4. Good arm does the work
5. Hold at end range 5-10 seconds
6. Lower slowly
7. Perform 10-15 repetitions

**Supine External Rotation:**
1. Lie on back, elbow at side, bent 90°
2. Hold wrist of painful arm
3. Rotate forearm outward (toward floor)
4. Good arm provides the movement
5. Hold 5-10 seconds at comfortable end range
6. Perform 10-15 repetitions

### Cross-Body Stretch

Stretches posterior shoulder capsule.

**How to do it:**
1. Bring arm across body at shoulder height
2. Use other hand to gently push arm closer to chest
3. Feel stretch in back of shoulder
4. Hold 30 seconds
5. Perform 3-4 times

**Caution:** Skip if this reproduces your pain.

## Phase 2: Restore Mobility

### Sleeper Stretch

Targets internal rotation—often limited with impingement.

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on painful side
2. Arm straight out in front, elbow bent 90°
3. Use other hand to push forearm toward floor
4. Keep shoulder blade flat on surface
5. Hold 30 seconds
6. Perform 3-4 repetitions

### Doorway Pec Stretch

Tight pecs pull shoulders forward.

**How to do it:**
1. Stand in doorway
2. Place forearm on door frame, elbow at shoulder height
3. Step through doorway until stretch felt in chest
4. Hold 30 seconds
5. Repeat with arm higher and lower for different fibers
6. Perform 3 positions, each side

### Thoracic Extension

Poor upper back mobility limits shoulder movement.

**How to do it:**
1. Sit in chair, hands behind head
2. Arch upper back over chair back
3. Don't extend lower back—isolate thoracic
4. Hold 5 seconds
5. Perform 10-15 repetitions

**With foam roller:**
1. Lie on foam roller across upper back
2. Support head with hands
3. Extend over roller
4. Move roller to different segments
5. Perform 10 extensions at each position

## Phase 3: Rotator Cuff Strengthening

### Side-Lying External Rotation

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on non-painful side
2. Elbow of top arm bent 90°, resting on hip
3. Rotate forearm toward ceiling
4. Keep elbow pinned to side
5. Lower slowly
6. Perform 15-20 repetitions, 2-3 sets

**Progression:** Add light dumbbell (1-3 lbs to start).

### Prone Y-T-W Raises

Targets rotator cuff and lower trapezius.

**How to do it:**
1. Lie face down on floor or bench
2. **Y:** Raise arms at 45° angle, thumbs up
3. **T:** Raise arms straight out to sides, thumbs up
4. **W:** Elbows bent 90°, squeeze shoulder blades, rotate forearms up
5. Hold each position 3-5 seconds
6. Perform 10-15 repetitions of each

### Standing External Rotation with Band

**How to do it:**
1. Attach band at elbow height
2. Stand sideways to anchor
3. Elbow bent 90°, pinned to side
4. Rotate forearm away from body against band
5. Control return
6. Perform 15-20 repetitions, 2-3 sets each side

### Internal Rotation with Band

**How to do it:**
1. Attach band at elbow height
2. Stand sideways to anchor (band on working side)
3. Elbow bent 90°, pinned to side
4. Rotate forearm across body against band
5. Control return
6. Perform 15-20 repetitions, 2-3 sets each side

---

## Phase 4: Scapular Strengthening

Your shoulder blade must move properly for pain-free arm movement.

### Wall Slides

**How to do it:**
1. Stand with back against wall
2. Arms in "goal post" position (elbows 90°, backs of hands on wall)
3. Slide arms up wall, keeping contact
4. Only go as high as you can maintain contact
5. Slide back down
6. Perform 15-20 repetitions, 2-3 sets

### Scapular Wall Push-Ups

**How to do it:**
1. Stand facing wall, hands at shoulder height
2. Let shoulder blades come together (protract)
3. Push hard through hands, spreading shoulder blades apart
4. Feel serratus anterior working around ribs
5. Perform 15-20 repetitions, 2-3 sets

**Progression:** Progress to regular push-up position with "plus" at top.

### Face Pulls

Essential for shoulder health.

**How to do it:**
1. Attach band at face height
2. Grip band with both hands
3. Pull toward face, elbows high
4. At end, rotate hands outward (external rotation)
5. Squeeze shoulder blades
6. Control return
7. Perform 15-20 repetitions, 2-3 sets

### Rows (Band or Dumbbell)

**How to do it:**
1. Hinge at hips, back flat
2. Pull weight toward lower ribs
3. Lead with elbow, squeeze shoulder blade back
4. Lower with control
5. Perform 12-15 repetitions, 2-3 sets

---

## Phase 5: Functional Strengthening

### Band Pull-Aparts

**How to do it:**
1. Hold band at shoulder height, arms straight
2. Pull band apart by squeezing shoulder blades
3. Control return to start
4. Perform 20-30 repetitions, 2-3 sets

### Overhead Press (When Ready)

**How to do it:**
1. Start only when pain-free with arm overhead
2. Begin with light weight
3. Press straight overhead
4. Keep core tight, don't arch back
5. Lower with control
6. Perform 10-12 repetitions, 2-3 sets

### Push-Up Progressions

**How to do it:**
1. Start with wall push-ups if needed
2. Progress to incline, then floor
3. Add "plus" at top (extra push, spreading shoulder blades)
4. Keep elbows at 45° (not flared out)
5. Perform 10-15 repetitions, 2-3 sets

## Sample Programs

### Acute Pain Program (Daily)

| Exercise | Sets × Reps |
|----------|-------------|
| Pendulums | 2 min each direction |
| Passive flexion | 15 reps |
| Passive external rotation | 15 reps |
| Ice | 15-20 min |

### Early Rehabilitation (Daily)

| Exercise | Sets × Reps |
|----------|-------------|
| Cross-body stretch | 3 × 30 sec |
| Doorway pec stretch | 3 positions × 30 sec |
| Side-lying external rotation | 2 × 15 |
| Scapular wall push-ups | 2 × 15 |
| Prone Y-T-W | 2 × 10 each |

### Maintenance Program (3x/week)

| Exercise | Sets × Reps |
|----------|-------------|
| Thoracic mobility | 2 × 10 |
| Face pulls | 3 × 15 |
| External rotation with band | 3 × 15 |
| Rows | 3 × 12 |
| Wall slides | 2 × 15 |
| Push-up plus | 2 × 12 |

## Exercise Tips by Condition

### Rotator Cuff Tendinopathy
- Focus on eccentric strengthening (slow lowering)
- Avoid painful arcs of motion initially
- Isometrics help with pain relief

### Impingement
- Prioritize posterior shoulder stretching
- Strengthen external rotators and lower traps
- Improve thoracic extension
- Avoid overhead work until pain-free

### Frozen Shoulder
- Heat before exercise
- Gentle stretching (don't force)
- Pendulums and passive ROM essential
- Recovery takes months—be patient

### Post-Surgical
- Follow surgeon's protocol strictly
- Don't rush strengthening
- Passive → active-assisted → active progression

---

## Red Flags (See Doctor)

- Severe pain following trauma
- Visible deformity
- Complete inability to raise arm
- Significant weakness without pain
- Numbness or tingling in arm
- Symptoms lasting more than 6-8 weeks without improvement
- Night pain that doesn't improve with position changes

---

## Prevention

### Daily Habits
- Take breaks from desk work every 30-60 minutes
- Vary sleeping positions
- Maintain good posture
- Include upper back exercises in workouts

### Training Principles
- Balance pushing and pulling exercises (most people need more pulling)
- Warm up shoulders before intense upper body work
- Don't ignore rotator cuff—include it regularly
- Progress gradually

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Most shoulder pain is treatable with exercise** — Surgery is rarely needed
2. **Rotator cuff strengthening is essential** — These small muscles control the joint
3. **Don't forget the shoulder blade** — Scapular control matters as much as rotator cuff
4. **Address the whole picture** — Posture, thoracic mobility, and muscle balance all contribute
5. **Be patient** — Shoulder rehab often takes 6-12 weeks for significant improvement
6. **Prevention is key** — Regular maintenance exercises keep shoulders healthy

Your shoulder pain is likely very fixable with consistent, targeted exercise. Start with pain relief, build to strengthening, and maintain what you've gained.
