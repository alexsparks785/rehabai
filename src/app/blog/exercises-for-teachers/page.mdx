# Exercises for Teachers: Stay Strong Through the School Year

Teaching is physically demanding in ways that surprise people. Hours on your feet, bending to help students, carrying books and supplies, and the mental exhaustion that manifests as physical tension—it all adds up. Strategic exercise helps you finish the year as strong as you started.

**Key insight:** Teachers need both the endurance for long days on their feet AND the ability to recover from the unique stresses of the job. Training should address both.

---

## Physical Demands of Teaching

### What the Job Requires

- **Prolonged standing:** 6-8+ hours on hard floors
- **Bending and squatting:** Student desks, demonstrations
- **Voice projection:** Sustained vocal effort
- **Walking:** Circulating through classrooms
- **Carrying:** Books, supplies, student work
- **Emotional regulation:** High-stress situations

### Common Teacher Health Issues

| Issue | Cause | Prevention Focus |
|-------|-------|------------------|
| Lower back pain | Standing, bending, poor chairs | Core strength, hip mobility |
| Leg and foot fatigue | Long hours standing | Leg strength, footwear, stretching |
| Neck/shoulder tension | Stress, computer work, writing | Mobility, stress management |
| Voice strain | Sustained projection | Voice care, posture |
| Headaches | Tension, fluorescent lights, dehydration | Neck exercises, hydration |
| Plantar fasciitis | Hard floors, poor footwear | Calf stretching, arch support |

## Quick In-School Exercises

### During Prep Period (5 minutes)

**Standing Calf Raises:**
- Rise onto balls of feet 20 times
- Helps circulation

**Hip Flexor Stretch:**
- Lunge position against desk
- 30 seconds each side

**Shoulder Rolls:**
- 10 forward, 10 backward

**Neck Stretches:**
- Ear to shoulder, 20 seconds each
- Gentle rotation

**Desk Push-Ups:**
- Hands on desk, 10-15 reps
- Quick upper body work

### Between Classes (1-2 minutes)

**Calf Raises:** 10-15 reps

**Shoulder Shrugs:** 10 reps

**Chin Tucks:** 5 reps

**Deep Breath:** 3 slow breaths

### Lunch Break (10-15 minutes)

**Walk:** 5 minutes outside if possible

**Stretching:**
- Hip flexors: 30 seconds each
- Hamstrings: 30 seconds each
- Chest: 30 seconds
- Neck: all directions

**Seated Exercises:**
- Glute squeezes: 20 reps
- Ankle circles: 10 each direction
- Spinal twist: 20 seconds each side

---

## Before School Routine (10 minutes)

Start the day prepared:

### Warm-Up (3 minutes)

- March in place: 1 minute
- Arm circles: 30 seconds
- Hip circles: 30 seconds

### Activation (4 minutes)

**Glute Bridges:**
- 15 reps to wake up glutes

**Cat-Cow:**
- 10 cycles for spine mobility

**Bird Dog:**
- 8 each side for core activation

### Stretching (3 minutes)

- Hip flexor stretch: 30 seconds each
- Calf stretch: 30 seconds each
- Shoulder rolls: 10 each direction

---

## After School Recovery (15-20 minutes)

Undo the day's stress:

### Lower Body Focus

**Calf Stretches:**
1. Wall stretch, straight leg: 45 seconds each
2. Wall stretch, bent knee: 45 seconds each

**Hip Flexor Stretch:**
1. Kneeling position
2. 60 seconds each side
3. Add side reach for more stretch

**Quad Stretch:**
1. Standing or lying
2. 30 seconds each side

**Foot Rolling:**
1. Golf ball or tennis ball under foot
2. Roll for 60 seconds each foot

### Upper Body and Back

**Doorway Chest Stretch:**
1. Arm on doorframe
2. 45 seconds each side

**Thread the Needle:**
1. On all fours, reach under and through
2. 8 reps each side

**Child's Pose:**
1. Kneel, sit back, reach forward
2. Hold 60 seconds

**Upper Trap Stretch:**
1. Ear to shoulder, gentle pressure
2. 30 seconds each side

### Relaxation

**Legs Up the Wall:**
1. Lie on back, legs up wall
2. Stay 5 minutes
3. Great for leg recovery

## Voice Care Exercises

Your voice is a tool—protect it.

### Posture for Voice

Good posture supports better breathing and projection.

**Standing Alignment:**
- Feet hip-width apart
- Weight evenly distributed
- Shoulders back and relaxed
- Head balanced (not forward)

### Breathing Exercises

**Diaphragmatic Breathing:**
1. Place hand on belly
2. Breathe in—belly rises
3. Breathe out—belly falls
4. Practice 5 minutes daily

### Vocal Warm-Up (Before Teaching)

**Humming:**
- Start low, slide to high pitch
- 1-2 minutes

**Lip Trills:**
- "Brrrr" sound while exhaling
- 30 seconds

### Voice Recovery

**Steam Inhalation:**
- Hot shower or bowl of hot water
- Hydrates vocal cords

**Hydration:**
- Sip water throughout day
- Avoid clearing throat (irritating)

## Stress Relief Exercises

### Quick Stress Busters (1-2 minutes)

**Box Breathing:**
1. Inhale 4 counts
2. Hold 4 counts
3. Exhale 4 counts
4. Hold 4 counts
5. Repeat 4 cycles

**Shoulder Drops:**
1. Shrug shoulders to ears
2. Hold 5 seconds
3. Drop and relax
4. Repeat 5 times

**Progressive Relaxation:**
1. Tense fists for 5 seconds
2. Release
3. Work through body parts

### Longer Stress Relief (10 minutes)

**Yoga Sequence:**
- Child's pose: 1 minute
- Cat-cow: 10 cycles
- Downward dog: 30 seconds
- Forward fold: 30 seconds
- Mountain pose with breathing: 1 minute

## Weekly Exercise Plan

### Minimum (Stay Functional)

- **Daily:** Pre-school warm-up (5 min)
- **Daily:** Post-school stretching (10 min)
- **2x/week:** Strengthening (20 min)

### Optimal (Thrive)

- **Daily:** Pre-school routine (10 min)
- **Daily:** Lunch movement break
- **Daily:** After-school recovery (15-20 min)
- **3-4x/week:** Full workout (strength + cardio)
- **1x/week:** Longer activity (walk, yoga, sport)

## Classroom Ergonomics

### Standing

- Wear supportive shoes (change them out)
- Consider anti-fatigue mat at desk
- Shift weight and move frequently
- Don't lock knees

### Sitting (When You Can)

- Use a proper chair when doing desk work
- Feet flat on floor
- Back supported
- Screen at eye level

### Working with Students

- Squat or kneel to student level (instead of bending)
- Use a rolling stool when possible
- Take turns circulating vs. staying at front

---

## End-of-Year vs. Start-of-Year

### September (Fresh Start)

- Build habits early
- Morning routine every day
- Higher energy for exercise

### December/January (Mid-Year Fatigue)

- Maintain basics even when exhausted
- Focus on recovery stretches
- Don't abandon exercise—modify intensity

### May/June (Finish Line)

- Extra recovery needed
- Gentle movement over intense exercise
- Plan for summer rebuilding

---

## Common Mistakes

### Exercise Errors

- **Too much too fast:** Especially after breaks
- **Skipping stretching:** Makes stiffness worse
- **Weekend warrior:** Can't make up for weekday neglect
- **Ignoring pain:** It will escalate

### Work Habit Errors

- **Standing still too long:** Keep moving
- **Wearing bad shoes:** Worth the investment
- **Skipping lunch break:** You need the movement
- **Neglecting hydration:** Affects everything

---

## The Bottom Line

Teaching demands both physical endurance and emotional resilience. Regular exercise—even in small doses—helps with both. Start with what you can sustain, and build from there.

**Non-negotiables:**
1. Move throughout the day (don't stand still)
2. Stretch after school (undo the day's damage)
3. Strengthen core 2-3x/week (protect your back)
4. Care for your feet (supportive shoes, stretching)
5. Manage stress with breath and movement

You can't pour from an empty cup. Take care of yourself so you can take care of your students.
