# How to Fix Forward Head Posture: Exercises for Tech Neck

Forward head posture—often called "tech neck" or "text neck"—is epidemic in our screen-focused world. Your head weighs 10-12 pounds, and for every inch it sits forward, the strain on your neck doubles. Here's how to fix it.

## What Is Forward Head Posture?

Stand sideways in front of a mirror. Your ear should align with your shoulder. If your ear is in front of your shoulder, you have forward head posture.

**Signs:**
- Head juts forward of shoulders
- Chin pokes out
- Upper back rounds
- Neck pain and tension
- Headaches at base of skull
- Tight upper traps

## What Causes Forward Head Posture?

It's a muscle imbalance:

**Tight muscles:**
- Suboccipitals (base of skull)
- Sternocleidomastoid (front of neck)
- Upper trapezius
- Pectorals (chest)
- Levator scapulae

**Weak muscles:**
- Deep neck flexors (front of neck, deep)
- Lower trapezius
- Rhomboids
- Middle back muscles

**The cause:** Looking down at phones, forward at computers, and general desk life.

## The Fix: Strengthen Deep Flexors, Stretch Tight Muscles

The key insight: the muscles at the FRONT of your neck (deep neck flexors) are weak, not the back. Most people try stretching the back of the neck—but you need to strengthen the front.

## Exercises to Fix Forward Head Posture

### 1. Chin Tuck

**The single most important exercise.**

**How to do it:**
1. Sit or stand tall
2. Pull chin straight back—like making a double chin
3. Don't look down—keep eyes level
4. Hold 5-10 seconds
5. Release and repeat

**Sets/reps:** 15-20 reps, multiple times daily

**Key:** Imagine someone pushing your forehead straight back. The movement is horizontal, not down.

**Why it works:** Strengthens deep neck flexors, retrains head position.

### 2. Chin Tuck Against Wall

**Added feedback for proper form.**

**How to do it:**
1. Stand with back against wall
2. Try to touch back of head to wall
3. Perform chin tuck while maintaining contact
4. Hold 5-10 seconds

**Sets/reps:** 10-15 reps

**Progression:** Can't touch head to wall? That shows how much you need this.

### 3. Prone Chin Tuck (Y-Position)

**Strengthens in gravity-resisted position.**

**How to do it:**
1. Lie face down, forehead on rolled towel
2. Arms in Y position overhead
3. Tuck chin (pull back)
4. Lift head 1-2 inches off towel
5. Hold 5 seconds
6. Lower and repeat

**Sets/reps:** 2-3 sets × 10-12 reps

### 4. Deep Neck Flexor Activation

**Isometric strengthening.**

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back, knees bent
2. Tuck chin toward chest (small movement)
3. Gently press back of head into floor
4. Hold 5-10 seconds
5. Don't lift head—just press

**Sets/reps:** 3 sets × 10 reps

**Key:** This is subtle. You're activating deep muscles, not surface muscles.

## Stretches for Forward Head Posture

### 1. Suboccipital Release

**Targets base of skull tension.**

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back
2. Place two tennis balls in a sock, tied off
3. Position balls at base of skull (occipital ridge)
4. Rest head weight on balls
5. Nod head gently yes/no
6. Hold 1-2 minutes

**Alternative:** Use fingers to apply pressure at base of skull while tilting head back slightly.

### 2. SCM Stretch

**Stretches front/side of neck.**

**How to do it:**
1. Turn head 45° to one side
2. Tilt head back slightly, looking up
3. Feel stretch in front of neck on opposite side
4. Hold 30 seconds each side

### 3. Upper Trap Stretch

**Releases chronically tight upper traps.**

**How to do it:**
1. Sit, hold chair with one hand
2. Tilt ear toward opposite shoulder
3. Use other hand for gentle pressure
4. Hold 30 seconds each side

### 4. Levator Scapulae Stretch

**Common source of neck pain.**

**How to do it:**
1. Turn head 45° to one side
2. Look down toward armpit
3. Use hand on head for gentle pressure
4. Feel stretch from neck to shoulder blade
5. Hold 30 seconds each side

### 5. Chest Stretch

**Opens front of body to allow head back.**

**How to do it:**
1. Doorway stretch: forearm on frame, step through
2. Hold 30 seconds each position (low, mid, high)

**Why:** Tight chest pulls shoulders forward, which pulls head forward.

## Upper Back Strengthening

Forward head posture is connected to weak upper back. Include these:

### Face Pulls
- Cable or band at face height
- Pull toward face, elbows high
- External rotate at end
- 3 sets × 15-20 reps

### Band Pull-Aparts
- Hold band at shoulder width
- Pull apart to chest
- Squeeze shoulder blades
- 3 sets × 15-20 reps

### Prone Y-T-W Raises
- Face down, lift arms in Y, T, and W positions
- Thumbs up, squeeze shoulder blades
- 2 sets × 10 of each

### Wall Angels
- Back against wall
- Slide arms up and down
- Keep contact with wall
- 2 sets × 10-12 reps

## Complete Forward Head Posture Routine

### Daily Quick Fix (8 minutes)

**Do 2-3 times daily:**

**Strengthening (4 minutes):**
- Chin tucks: 15 reps
- Wall chin tucks: 10 reps
- Band pull-aparts: 15 reps

**Stretching (4 minutes):**
- Upper trap stretch: 30 sec each side
- Levator stretch: 30 sec each side
- Chest stretch: 30 sec each position

### Full Correction Session (20 minutes)

**Strengthening (10 minutes):**
1. Chin tucks: 3 × 15
2. Deep neck flexor activation: 3 × 10
3. Prone chin tuck: 2 × 12
4. Face pulls: 3 × 15
5. Band pull-aparts: 2 × 15
6. Prone Y-T-W: 2 × 8 of each

**Stretching (8 minutes):**
1. Suboccipital release: 2 minutes
2. SCM stretch: 30 sec each side
3. Upper trap stretch: 45 sec each side
4. Levator stretch: 45 sec each side
5. Chest stretch: 30 sec × 3 positions

**Posture Practice (2 minutes):**
- Wall posture check
- Practice maintaining head position
- Walk around with awareness

## Posture Fixes Throughout Your Day

### At Your Computer
- Screen at eye level (use monitor stand or laptop stand)
- Sit back in chair
- Keyboard close to body
- Take breaks every 30 minutes

### On Your Phone
- Bring phone UP to eye level
- Don't crane neck down
- Limit phone time
- Use voice-to-text

### While Driving
- Adjust headrest to touch back of head
- Sit tall with head back
- Use mirrors to check posture

### Sleeping
- Pillow height: keeps neck neutral
- Side sleeper: pillow fills space between ear and shoulder
- Back sleeper: smaller pillow, don't stack pillows
- Avoid stomach sleeping

### Standing
- Ears over shoulders
- Think "tall" through crown of head
- Light chin tuck

## Movement Breaks

**Every 30-60 minutes:**
1. Stand up
2. Chin tucks: 5 reps
3. Shoulder rolls: 10 reps
4. Look around the room (move your neck)
5. Quick chest stretch

## How Long Until Improvement?

**Week 1-2:** Increased awareness, temporary improvements

**Week 3-4:** Exercises feel easier, holding position is easier

**Month 2:** Noticeable resting posture change

**Month 3+:** New position becomes more natural

**The key:** Daily consistency. Years of forward head don't reverse in days.

## Common Mistakes

### Stretching the Back of Neck Only
- Deep neck flexors (front) are what's weak
- Chin tucks are strengthening, not stretching

### Forcing Head Back
- Creates tension, not lasting change
- Chin tuck is subtle, not aggressive

### Only Exercising
- Must also change daily habits
- Screen position matters as much as exercises

### Inconsistency
- Random efforts don't produce change
- Daily practice is essential

## The Bottom Line

Forward head posture is fixed primarily by strengthening the deep neck flexors with chin tucks, and by addressing the upper back weakness that allows the head to drift forward.

Do chin tucks multiple times daily. Stretch tight upper traps and chest muscles. Fix your screen ergonomics. Practice awareness throughout the day.

Your neck pain will decrease. Your headaches may improve. And you'll look better and more confident standing tall.
