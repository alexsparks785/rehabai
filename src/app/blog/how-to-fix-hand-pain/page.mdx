# How to Fix Hand Pain: Exercises for Computer Users and More

Your hands do an incredible amount of work—typing thousands of keystrokes, clicking, scrolling, gripping, and manipulating objects all day long. When they start to hurt, everything becomes harder. Here's how to identify what's causing your hand pain and fix it.

## Common Causes of Hand Pain

### Repetitive Strain Conditions

**Carpal Tunnel Syndrome**
- Compression of median nerve at the wrist
- Numbness/tingling in thumb, index, middle finger
- Worse at night
- Weakness in grip

**De Quervain's Tenosynovitis (Texting Thumb)**
- Inflammation of thumb tendons at the wrist
- Pain at base of thumb
- Worse with gripping or thumb movement

**Trigger Finger**
- Tendon catches and releases with a snap
- Finger may lock in bent position
- Stiffness, especially in morning

**Mouse Hand / Computer Hand**
- General term for pain from mouse and keyboard use
- Usually affects the dominant hand
- May involve multiple structures

### Other Causes

**Arthritis**
- Joint pain, stiffness, swelling
- Worse in morning or after rest
- May affect multiple joints

**Tendinitis**
- Inflammation of tendons
- Pain with specific movements
- May have warmth or swelling

**Overuse/Muscle Fatigue**
- Aching after prolonged use
- Improves with rest
- No specific injury

## Identifying Your Pain Pattern

### Pain in the Palm/Fingers (Median Nerve Area)
- Likely carpal tunnel if numbness in thumb, index, middle finger
- May include weakness in grip

### Pain at Base of Thumb
- Likely de Quervain's if worse with thumb movement
- Could be thumb CMC arthritis if at the joint

### Pain in Finger Joints
- Could be arthritis
- Trigger finger if catching/locking

### Pain on Top of Hand/Wrist
- May be extensor tendinitis
- Common with keyboard use

### General Hand Fatigue/Aching
- Often overuse
- May respond to rest and ergonomics

## Exercises for Hand Pain

### General Hand Stretches and Mobility

#### 1. Finger Spread
**How to do it:**
1. Spread all fingers apart as wide as possible
2. Hold for 5 seconds
3. Relax and repeat
4. Do 10 reps

#### 2. Fist to Spread
**How to do it:**
1. Make a tight fist
2. Open hand and spread fingers wide
3. Repeat 10-15 times
4. Move slowly and deliberately

#### 3. Tendon Glides
**Why:** Keeps tendons moving smoothly through their tunnels.

**How to do it:**
1. Start with fingers straight
2. Bend fingers at knuckles only (tabletop position)
3. Make a hook fist (bend middle and end joints only)
4. Make a full fist
5. Return to straight
6. Cycle through 10 times

#### 4. Wrist Circles
**How to do it:**
1. Hold your arm out, make a loose fist
2. Circle your wrist slowly
3. 10 circles clockwise, 10 counterclockwise
4. Do both hands

### Wrist Stretches

#### 5. Wrist Flexor Stretch
**How to do it:**
1. Extend arm in front of you, palm up
2. Use other hand to gently pull fingers down toward floor
3. Keep elbow straight
4. Hold 20-30 seconds
5. Do both hands

#### 6. Wrist Extensor Stretch
**How to do it:**
1. Extend arm in front of you, palm down
2. Use other hand to gently push fingers down
3. Keep elbow straight
4. Hold 20-30 seconds
5. Do both hands

#### 7. Prayer Stretch
**How to do it:**
1. Press palms together in front of chest (prayer position)
2. Lower hands while keeping palms pressed together
3. Stop when you feel a stretch in wrists/forearms
4. Hold 20-30 seconds

#### 8. Reverse Prayer Stretch
**How to do it:**
1. Press backs of hands together, fingers pointing down
2. Raise hands while keeping them pressed together
3. Feel stretch on top of wrists
4. Hold 20-30 seconds

### Exercises for Carpal Tunnel

#### 9. Median Nerve Glide
**How to do it:**
1. Start with arm at side, wrist neutral, fist closed
2. Open hand, extend fingers
3. Extend wrist back (fingers up)
4. Extend elbow
5. Rotate arm so palm faces up
6. Tilt head away from that arm
7. Reverse the sequence
8. Do 10-15 reps, smooth and slow

#### 10. Carpal Tunnel Stretch
**How to do it:**
1. Extend arm, palm facing out (like signaling "stop")
2. Use other hand to pull fingers back gently
3. Hold 15-20 seconds
4. Do 3-4 times per hand

### Exercises for De Quervain's (Thumb Pain)

#### 11. Thumb Stretch
**How to do it:**
1. Make a fist with thumb inside fingers
2. Gently bend wrist toward pinky side
3. Feel stretch at base of thumb
4. Hold 15-20 seconds
5. Do 3-4 reps

#### 12. Thumb Opposition Exercise
**How to do it:**
1. Touch thumb to each fingertip in sequence
2. Make the O-shape firm but not straining
3. Go slowly and deliberately
4. Do 10 full sequences

#### 13. Thumb Extension
**How to do it:**
1. Place hand flat on table, palm down
2. Lift just your thumb up toward ceiling
3. Hold 5 seconds
4. Lower slowly
5. Do 15-20 reps

### Strengthening Exercises

#### 14. Grip Strengthening
**How to do it:**
1. Squeeze a soft stress ball or therapy putty
2. Hold for 5 seconds
3. Release
4. Do 15-20 reps
5. Progress to firmer resistance

**Note:** Only do this if it doesn't increase pain.

#### 15. Finger Extension with Rubber Band
**How to do it:**
1. Place rubber band around all fingertips
2. Spread fingers apart against resistance
3. Hold briefly
4. Release
5. Do 15-20 reps

#### 16. Pinch Strengthening
**How to do it:**
1. Pinch therapy putty between thumb and each finger
2. Hold 5 seconds
3. Do 10 pinches per finger

#### 17. Wrist Curls
**How to do it:**
1. Rest forearm on table, wrist hanging off edge
2. Hold light weight (1-2 lbs)
3. Curl wrist up, then lower slowly
4. Do 15-20 reps, palm up and palm down

### Self-Massage

#### 18. Forearm Massage
**How to do it:**
1. Use your thumb to massage the forearm muscles
2. Work from elbow toward wrist
3. Find tender spots and hold pressure
4. Spend 2-3 minutes per arm

#### 19. Palm Massage
**How to do it:**
1. Use your thumb to press into the palm
2. Work in circles around the entire palm
3. Focus on the fleshy area at the base of thumb
4. 1-2 minutes per hand

#### 20. Finger Pulls
**How to do it:**
1. Grasp each finger at the base
2. Gently pull and twist as you slide to the tip
3. Do each finger 3-4 times
4. Provides gentle decompression

## Ergonomic Fixes

### Keyboard
- Keep wrists in neutral position (not bent up or down)
- Elbows at about 90 degrees
- Consider a split keyboard or ergonomic keyboard
- Don't rest wrists on hard edges while typing

### Mouse
- Keep mouse close to keyboard
- Use whole arm to move mouse, not just wrist
- Consider a vertical mouse or trackball
- Alternate hands if possible

### Overall Setup
- Screen at eye level
- Shoulders relaxed
- Forearms supported or floating (not resting on hard edge)
- Take breaks every 20-30 minutes

### Grip Habits
- Don't grip tools/mouse/pen too tightly
- Relax hands when not actively using them
- Use tools with larger, padded grips

## Your Daily Routine

### Morning (3 minutes)
- Wrist circles: 10 each direction
- Tendon glides: 10 reps
- Prayer stretch: 20 seconds
- Fist to spread: 10 reps

### Every Hour at Computer
- Shake out hands
- Quick stretches (30 seconds)
- Check posture and grip tension

### Evening (5 minutes)
- Full stretching routine
- Self-massage: forearms and palms
- Nerve glides if carpal tunnel symptoms
- Strengthening exercises (if appropriate)

## When to See a Doctor

**Seek evaluation if:**
- Pain is severe or worsening
- Numbness or tingling is persistent
- You have significant weakness
- Fingers lock or catch
- Swelling or visible deformity
- Pain wakes you at night
- Symptoms don't improve with self-care after 2-3 weeks

### Treatment Options
- Physical or occupational therapy
- Splinting (especially at night for carpal tunnel)
- Anti-inflammatory medications
- Corticosteroid injections
- Surgery (for severe, unresponsive cases)

## Prevention Strategies

### Take Breaks
- Every 20-30 minutes, pause and stretch
- Every hour, get up and move around
- Microbreaks prevent cumulative damage

### Vary Activities
- Alternate between tasks
- Use both hands when possible
- Don't do the same motion for hours

### Strengthen and Stretch
- Regular hand exercises maintain health
- Prevention is easier than treatment

### Manage Workload
- Pace yourself
- Don't power through pain
- Address problems early

## The Bottom Line

Hand pain from computer work and repetitive activities is common but usually manageable. The keys are:

1. **Identify the pattern** — Where and when does it hurt?
2. **Modify the cause** — Fix ergonomics, take breaks, change habits
3. **Stretch and mobilize** — Keep tendons gliding and muscles supple
4. **Strengthen appropriately** — Once pain improves, build resilience

Don't ignore hand pain. Your hands are irreplaceable tools. Small changes in habits and consistent stretching can prevent minor discomfort from becoming a chronic problem.

If symptoms persist despite self-care, get professional help. Conditions like carpal tunnel syndrome are much easier to treat early than after years of progression.

Your hands have carried you this far. Take care of them.
