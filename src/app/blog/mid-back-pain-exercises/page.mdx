Mid back pain—the area between your neck and lower back—is often overlooked but increasingly common in our screen-dominated world. The thoracic spine (your mid back) is designed for stability and rotation, but modern life often leaves it stiff, stuck, and painful.

Here's how to fix it.

## Understanding Your Mid Back

### The Thoracic Spine

Your mid back consists of 12 vertebrae (T1-T12) that connect to your ribs. This region is:

- **More stable** than your neck or lower back (ribs add structure)
- **Designed for rotation** (turning your torso)
- **Prone to stiffness** from prolonged positions
- **Connected to breathing** (ribs attach here)

### Why Mid Back Pain Develops

**Postural stress:**
- Hunched shoulders from desk work
- Forward head position
- Rounded upper back (kyphosis)
- Hours of looking at screens

**Muscle imbalances:**
- Weak upper back muscles (rhomboids, mid traps)
- Tight chest muscles
- Overworked neck and shoulder muscles

**Stiffness:**
- Lack of thoracic mobility
- Sitting in one position too long
- Not enough rotation in daily life

**Other causes:**
- Rib dysfunction
- Facet joint irritation
- Muscle strain from sudden movements
- Stress and tension

## Immediate Relief Techniques

### Foam Roller Extension

The single best exercise for thoracic stiffness.

1. Place foam roller perpendicular to spine at mid back
2. Support head with hands
3. Lean back over roller, extending spine
4. Hold 5 seconds, then move roller to next segment
5. Work from lower to upper thoracic spine
6. Spend extra time on stiff areas

### Seated Rotation Stretch

1. Sit in a chair, feet flat
2. Cross arms over chest
3. Rotate torso to one side
4. Hold 20-30 seconds
5. Rotate to other side
6. Repeat 2-3 times each side

### Thread the Needle

1. Start on hands and knees
2. Reach one arm under your body, following with shoulder
3. Let torso rotate, lowering shoulder toward floor
4. Hold 20-30 seconds
5. Return and switch sides

### Cat-Cow with Thoracic Focus

1. On hands and knees
2. Focus the movement on your mid back
3. Round upper back toward ceiling (cat)
4. Then drop chest toward floor, opening front of spine (cow)
5. Move slowly, 10-15 repetitions

### Tennis Ball Release

1. Lie on back with tennis ball or lacrosse ball
2. Place ball beside spine (not directly on spine)
3. Position in tight areas of mid back
4. Relax onto the ball for 30-60 seconds
5. Move to different spots as needed

## Thoracic Mobility Exercises

### Open Book

Excellent for rotation.

1. Lie on side, knees bent 90°
2. Arms extended in front, hands together
3. Keeping bottom arm still, rotate top arm to opposite side
4. Follow with eyes and let torso rotate
5. Return and repeat
6. 10 repetitions each side

### Quadruped Thoracic Rotation

1. On hands and knees
2. Place one hand behind head
3. Rotate that elbow toward opposite knee
4. Then rotate up toward ceiling
5. Follow with eyes
6. 10 repetitions each side

### Wall Rotation

1. Stand sideways to wall, arm's length away
2. Place hand on wall at shoulder height
3. Rotate torso away from wall
4. Keep hips facing forward
5. Hold 20-30 seconds each side

### Prone Thoracic Extension

1. Lie face down
2. Place hands under forehead or beside shoulders
3. Gently lift chest off floor, focusing on mid back extension
4. Hold 5 seconds
5. Lower and repeat 10 times

### Deep Breathing Extension

1. Sit or stand tall
2. Clasp hands behind head
3. Inhale deeply, lifting chest and elbows up
4. Feel thoracic extension
5. Exhale, returning to neutral
6. 10 breaths

## Strengthening Exercises

Weak upper back muscles allow poor posture. Strengthen them.

### Y-T-W-L Raises

**Y Raise:**
1. Lie face down (or bent over)
2. Raise arms overhead in Y shape, thumbs up
3. Squeeze between shoulder blades
4. Lower and repeat
5. 2 sets of 10

**T Raise:**
1. Same position
2. Raise arms out to sides (T shape)
3. Squeeze shoulder blades together
4. 2 sets of 10

**W Raise:**
1. Raise arms to W shape (elbows bent, hands up)
2. Focus on pulling shoulder blades down and together
3. 2 sets of 10

**L Raise:**
1. Arms at sides, elbows bent 90°
2. Rotate forearms up toward ceiling
3. Squeeze shoulder blades
4. 2 sets of 10

### Face Pulls

1. Attach band at face height
2. Pull band toward face, elbows high
3. Squeeze shoulder blades together
4. Slowly return
5. 3 sets of 15

### Rows

Any rowing motion strengthens mid back.

**Seated row with band:**
1. Sit on floor, legs extended
2. Band around feet
3. Pull band toward ribs
4. Squeeze shoulder blades
5. 3 sets of 12

**Bent-over row:**
1. Hinge at hips, back flat
2. Pull weights to ribs
3. Squeeze shoulder blades at top
4. 3 sets of 10

### Prone Snow Angels

1. Lie face down, arms at sides
2. Lift arms off floor
3. Sweep arms overhead (like making snow angel)
4. Return to sides
5. Keep arms lifted throughout
6. 2 sets of 10

### Wall Angels

1. Stand with back against wall
2. Arms in goalpost position (elbows at 90°)
3. Keep back, head, arms touching wall
4. Slide arms up overhead
5. Slide back down
6. 2 sets of 10

## Stretching Tight Areas

### Chest Stretch (Doorway)

Tight chest pulls shoulders forward, stressing mid back.

1. Stand in doorway
2. Place forearms on door frame
3. Step through doorway
4. Feel stretch across chest
5. Try different arm heights (low, middle, high)
6. Hold 30 seconds each position

### Lat Stretch

1. Hold onto something stable at waist height
2. Step back and hinge at hips
3. Let body hang, stretching lats and spine
4. Hold 30 seconds

### Upper Trap Stretch

1. Sit or stand
2. Tilt ear toward shoulder
3. For more stretch, gently pull head with hand
4. Hold 30 seconds each side

### Levator Scapulae Stretch

1. Turn head 45° to one side
2. Tilt that ear toward your armpit
3. Place hand on back of head for gentle pressure
4. Hold 30 seconds each side

## Posture Correction

Poor posture is the root cause of most mid back pain.

### Awareness Checkpoints

Throughout the day, check:
- Shoulders back and down (not rounded forward)
- Chin tucked (not jutting forward)
- Chest open
- Weight evenly distributed

### Chin Tucks

Corrects forward head posture.

1. Sit or stand tall
2. Pull chin straight back (make a double chin)
3. Hold 5 seconds
4. Release
5. Repeat 10 times, several times daily

### Shoulder Blade Squeezes

1. Sit or stand with arms at sides
2. Squeeze shoulder blades together
3. Hold 5 seconds
4. Release
5. 10-15 repetitions, several times daily

### Wall Posture Check

1. Stand with heels, buttocks, shoulder blades, and head against wall
2. This is approximately ideal posture
3. Step away and try to maintain
4. Practice several times daily

## Workstation Setup

If you sit at a desk, setup matters.

**Monitor:**
- Top of screen at eye level
- Arm's length away
- Directly in front (not to the side)

**Chair:**
- Feet flat on floor
- Knees at 90°
- Lumbar support for lower back curve
- Armrests at elbow height

**Keyboard and mouse:**
- Elbows at 90°
- Wrists neutral (not bent)
- Close to body

**Breaks:**
- Stand every 30-45 minutes
- Move and stretch briefly
- Change positions frequently

## Daily Routine for Mid Back Health

### Morning (5 minutes)

- Cat-cow: 10 reps
- Thread the needle: 5 each side
- Chin tucks: 10 reps
- Shoulder blade squeezes: 10 reps

### Midday (3 minutes)

- Seated rotation: 30 seconds each side
- Doorway chest stretch: 30 seconds
- Shoulder blade squeezes: 10 reps
- Standing back extension: 5 reps

### Evening (10 minutes)

- Foam roller thoracic extension: 2 minutes
- Open book: 10 each side
- Y-T-W raises: 1 set each
- Full stretching routine

## The 4-Week Program

### Week 1: Mobility Focus

**Daily:**
- Cat-cow: 10 reps
- Thread the needle: 5 each side
- Foam roller extension: 2 minutes

**3 times:**
- Open book: 2×10 each side
- Quadruped rotation: 2×10 each side
- Chin tucks: 2×10

### Week 2: Add Stretching

**Daily:**
- Week 1 mobility exercises

**3 times:**
- Doorway chest stretch: 2×30 seconds
- Lat stretch: 2×30 seconds
- Upper trap stretch: 2×30 seconds each
- Y-T-W raises: 1×10 each

### Week 3: Add Strength

**Daily:**
- Mobility routine (5 minutes)

**3 times:**
- Y-T-W-L raises: 2×10 each
- Face pulls: 3×12
- Rows: 3×12
- Wall angels: 2×10

### Week 4: Full Program

**Daily:**
- Morning routine (5 minutes)
- Midday stretch (3 minutes)

**3 times:**
- Full mobility routine
- Strength exercises
- Evening stretching

## When to Seek Help

See a healthcare provider if:

- Pain persists despite 4-6 weeks of exercises
- Pain radiates around ribs or into chest
- You have numbness or tingling
- Pain is severe or worsening
- You have difficulty breathing
- Pain wakes you at night
- You have unexplained weight loss or fever

### Red Flags

Seek immediate care for:
- Severe chest pain (could be cardiac)
- Sudden severe thoracic pain (especially if trauma-related)
- Loss of bladder or bowel control
- Progressive weakness in legs

## The Bottom Line

Mid back pain usually results from stiffness and postural stress—both very fixable problems. The thoracic spine needs:

1. **Mobility** (rotation and extension)
2. **Strength** (upper back muscles)
3. **Flexibility** (chest and lats)
4. **Posture correction** (all day, every day)

Consistent attention to these areas typically resolves mid back pain within 4-6 weeks. Your mid back wasn't designed to sit hunched over a screen—give it the movement it needs, and it will feel better.
