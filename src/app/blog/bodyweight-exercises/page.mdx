# Bodyweight Exercises: Complete Training Without Equipment

You don't need a gym to get strong. Bodyweight training has built powerful physiques for centuries—from ancient athletes to modern military personnel. With just your body and gravity, you can build impressive strength, muscle, and fitness.

**Key insight:** Bodyweight exercises aren't just for beginners. Progressions from basic to advanced can challenge anyone, and skills like muscle-ups and pistol squats require years of dedicated training.

## Push Exercises

### Push-Up

The foundation of upper body pushing.

**How to do it:**
1. Hands shoulder-width, fingers forward
2. Body in straight line from head to heels
3. Lower chest toward ground
4. Elbows at 45° angle (not flared)
5. Push back up
6. Perform 10-20 reps

**Progressions:**
- Wall push-up
- Incline push-up
- Knee push-up
- Full push-up
- Diamond push-up
- Archer push-up
- One-arm push-up

### Dips

**How to do it:**
1. Support yourself on parallel bars or chairs
2. Lower until upper arms parallel to ground
3. Keep elbows pointing back
4. Push back up
5. Perform 8-15 reps

**Easier:** Bench dips
**Harder:** Ring dips, weighted dips

### Pike Push-Up

Vertical pushing without handstand.

**How to do it:**
1. Start in downward dog position
2. Hands shoulder-width apart
3. Lower head toward ground between hands
4. Push back up
5. Perform 8-12 reps

**Progression:** Elevate feet for more vertical angle.

### Handstand Push-Up (Advanced)

**How to do it:**
1. Kick up into handstand against wall
2. Lower head toward ground
3. Push back up
4. Perform 5-10 reps

**Build up with:** Pike push-ups → elevated pike → wall handstand holds → negatives → full HSPU

## Pull Exercises

### Inverted Row

Horizontal pulling without equipment.

**How to do it:**
1. Hang under sturdy table or bar
2. Body straight, heels on ground
3. Pull chest to bar/table edge
4. Lower with control
5. Perform 10-15 reps

**Progression:** Lower bar = harder; bent knees = easier

### Pull-Up

The king of upper body pulling.

**How to do it:**
1. Hang from bar, hands outside shoulders
2. Pull chest toward bar
3. Lead with chest, not chin
4. Lower with control
5. Perform 5-12 reps

**Progressions:**
- Dead hangs
- Scapular pulls
- Negative pull-ups
- Band-assisted
- Full pull-ups
- Weighted
- Muscle-ups

### Chin-Up

**How to do it:**
1. Hang from bar, hands shoulder-width, palms facing you
2. Pull chin over bar
3. Squeeze biceps at top
4. Lower with control
5. Perform 6-12 reps

### Australian Pull-Up

**How to do it:**
1. Set bar at waist height
2. Hang underneath, body straight
3. Pull chest to bar
4. Lower with control
5. Perform 10-15 reps

## Squat Exercises

### Bodyweight Squat

**How to do it:**
1. Feet shoulder-width or slightly wider
2. Push hips back and down
3. Keep chest up, knees over toes
4. Go until thighs parallel or below
5. Drive through heels to stand
6. Perform 15-25 reps

### Split Squat

**How to do it:**
1. Staggered stance, front foot flat
2. Lower back knee toward ground
3. Keep torso upright
4. Push through front heel
5. Perform 10-15 each leg

### Bulgarian Split Squat

**How to do it:**
1. Rear foot elevated on bench
2. Lower until front thigh parallel
3. Keep torso upright
4. Drive through front heel
5. Perform 8-12 each leg

### Pistol Squat (Advanced)

**How to do it:**
1. Stand on one leg
2. Extend other leg in front
3. Squat all the way down
4. Stand back up
5. Perform 3-8 each leg

**Progressions:**
- Assisted (holding support)
- Box pistol
- Counterbalance (weight in front)
- Negative only
- Full pistol

### Shrimp Squat (Advanced)

**How to do it:**
1. Stand on one leg
2. Grab other foot behind you
3. Squat down until back knee touches ground
4. Stand back up
5. Perform 5-8 each leg

## Hinge Exercises

### Single-Leg Romanian Deadlift

**How to do it:**
1. Stand on one leg
2. Hinge at hip, reaching other leg back
3. Keep back flat
4. Touch ground or go to hamstring stretch
5. Stand back up
6. Perform 8-12 each leg

### Good Morning

**How to do it:**
1. Stand with hands behind head
2. Push hips back, torso folds forward
3. Keep back flat
4. Go until hamstring stretch
5. Squeeze glutes to stand
6. Perform 12-15 reps

### Glute Bridge

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back, knees bent
2. Squeeze glutes, lift hips
3. Create straight line from shoulders to knees
4. Hold 2-3 seconds
5. Lower slowly
6. Perform 15-20 reps

### Single-Leg Glute Bridge

**How to do it:**
1. Bridge position, extend one leg
2. Bridge up on single leg
3. Keep hips level
4. Perform 10-15 each leg

## Core Exercises

### Plank

**How to do it:**
1. Forearms and toes on ground
2. Body straight from head to heels
3. Squeeze glutes, brace core
4. Don't let hips sag or pike
5. Hold 30-60 seconds

**Variations:** Side plank, plank with reach, body saw

### Hollow Body Hold

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back
2. Press lower back into floor
3. Lift shoulders and legs off ground
4. Arms by ears or at sides
5. Hold 20-60 seconds

### Dead Bug

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back, arms up, knees at 90°
2. Press lower back into floor
3. Lower opposite arm and leg
4. Keep back flat
5. Perform 10-12 each side

### Leg Raises

**How to do it:**
1. Hang from bar or lie on back
2. Keep legs straight
3. Raise legs to horizontal (or higher)
4. Lower with control
5. Perform 10-15 reps

### L-Sit

**How to do it:**
1. Support yourself on parallettes or chairs
2. Lift legs straight in front, parallel to ground
3. Hold as long as possible
4. Build to 30+ seconds

## Progression Principles

### How to Progress Without Weights

1. **Increase reps** — Up to about 20 for strength
2. **Add sets** — More volume
3. **Slow the tempo** — 3-4 seconds down
4. **Reduce leverage** — Elevate feet, change angle
5. **Single-limb** — Bilateral to unilateral
6. **Add pause** — Pause at bottom
7. **Increase range** — Deficit push-ups, deep squats
8. **Progress to harder variation** — Push-up to archer to one-arm

### When to Progress

Move to next progression when you can do:
- 3 sets of 12-15 reps with good form
- Or specific skill prerequisites met

## Key Takeaways

1. **Progressions are endless** — From wall push-up to one-arm push-up takes years
2. **Train all patterns** — Push, pull, squat, hinge, core
3. **Quality over quantity** — Perfect form before adding difficulty
4. **Pull-ups are king** — Master them for upper body development
5. **Single-leg work builds balance** — Essential for athleticism
6. **Consistency wins** — Regular training beats occasional intense sessions

**Minimum effective training:**
- 3 sessions per week
- One push, one pull, one lower body exercise
- 3 sets each
- Total: 20-30 minutes

Bodyweight training is portable, free, and infinitely scalable. Start where you are, progress consistently, and you'll build impressive strength without ever touching a weight.
