# Exercises for Nurses: Stay Strong Through Long Shifts

Nursing is one of the most physically demanding professions. Twelve-hour shifts on your feet, lifting and repositioning patients, and bending repeatedly takes a toll. Strategic exercise builds the strength and endurance to protect your body while caring for others.

**Key insight:** Nurses have some of the highest rates of occupational back injury. Core and hip strength, combined with proper lifting mechanics, can dramatically reduce this risk.

---

## Physical Demands of Nursing

### What the Job Requires

- **Prolonged standing:** 8-12+ hour shifts on hard floors
- **Patient handling:** Lifting, transferring, repositioning
- **Bending and reaching:** Charting, medication administration
- **Walking:** Miles per shift across large facilities
- **Emotional regulation:** High-stress, often traumatic situations

### Common Nursing Injuries

| Injury | Cause | Prevention Focus |
|--------|-------|------------------|
| Lower back pain | Patient handling, bending | Core strength, lifting mechanics |
| Neck/shoulder pain | Charting posture, reaching | Shoulder mobility, posture exercises |
| Knee pain | Prolonged standing | Leg strength, supportive footwear |
| Plantar fasciitis | Hours on hard floors | Calf stretching, arch support |
| Varicose veins | Standing without movement | Compression, calf exercises |

## Core Strengthening

Your core protects your back during patient handling.

### Plank

**How to do it:**
1. Forearms on floor
2. Body in straight line
3. Hold 30-60 seconds
4. 3 sets

### Bird Dog

**How to do it:**
1. On all fours
2. Extend opposite arm and leg
3. Keep back flat, don't rotate
4. 3 sets of 10 each side

### Dead Bug

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back, arms up, knees at 90°
2. Lower opposite arm and leg
3. Keep lower back pressed into floor
4. 3 sets of 10 each side

### Glute Bridges

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back, knees bent, feet flat
2. Drive through heels, lift hips
3. Squeeze glutes at top
4. 3 sets of 15

### Side Plank

**How to do it:**
1. Forearm on floor, feet stacked
2. Lift hips, maintain straight line
3. Hold 30 seconds each side
4. 3 sets

## Lower Back Protection

### Cat-Cow

**How to do it:**
1. On all fours
2. Arch back (cow), then round back (cat)
3. Move slowly through full range
4. 10-15 cycles

### Hip Hinge Practice

**Why it matters:** The proper way to bend and lift.

**How to do it:**
1. Stand with feet hip-width apart
2. Push hips back, letting torso come forward
3. Keep back flat, slight knee bend
4. Return by driving hips forward
5. Practice 10-15 times before shifts

### Child's Pose

**How to do it:**
1. Kneel, sit back on heels
2. Fold forward, arms extended or at sides
3. Let back relax
4. Hold 30-60 seconds

### Knee-to-Chest Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back
2. Pull one knee toward chest
3. Hold 30 seconds each side
4. Then pull both knees together

---

## Leg Strength and Endurance

### Squats

**How to do it:**
1. Feet shoulder-width apart
2. Squat as if sitting back into chair
3. Keep weight in heels, chest up
4. 3 sets of 15

### Step-Ups

**How to do it:**
1. Step onto sturdy step or chair
2. Drive through top leg
3. Control descent
4. 3 sets of 10 each leg

### Lunges

**How to do it:**
1. Step forward into lunge
2. Lower until front thigh parallel
3. Push back to start
4. 3 sets of 10 each leg

### Calf Raises

**How to do it:**
1. Rise onto balls of feet
2. Hold 2 seconds
3. Lower slowly
4. 3 sets of 20

**Tip:** Do these at the nursing station during quiet moments.

### Wall Sits

**How to do it:**
1. Back against wall, thighs parallel to floor
2. Hold 30-60 seconds
3. 3 sets

---

## Shoulder and Neck Care

### Shoulder Rolls

**How to do it:**
1. Roll shoulders forward 10 times
2. Roll shoulders backward 10 times
3. Do every few hours

### Chin Tucks

**How to do it:**
1. Pull chin straight back (make double chin)
2. Hold 5-10 seconds
3. 10 reps, several times daily

### Upper Trap Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Tilt ear toward shoulder
2. Gently pull head with same-side hand
3. Hold 30 seconds each side

### Doorway Chest Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Arm on doorframe at 90°
2. Step through doorway
3. Feel stretch across chest
4. Hold 30 seconds each side

### Thread the Needle

**How to do it:**
1. On all fours
2. Reach one arm under body, rotating torso
3. Return and reach toward ceiling
4. 8-10 each side

## Quick On-Shift Exercises

Do these during breaks or quiet moments.

### Standing Stretches (2 minutes)

- Hip flexor stretch against wall: 20 seconds each
- Standing quad stretch: 20 seconds each
- Calf stretch: 20 seconds each
- Shoulder rolls: 10 each direction
- Neck stretches: 15 seconds each side

### Chair Exercises (2 minutes)

- Seated marching: 30 seconds
- Seated leg extensions: 10 each leg
- Ankle circles: 10 each direction
- Seated spinal twist: 15 seconds each side
- Shoulder blade squeezes: 10 reps

### Between-Patient Reset (30 seconds)

- 3 deep breaths
- Shoulder rolls
- Chin tuck
- Quick hip flexor stretch

---

## Pre-Shift Warm-Up (5 minutes)

Before your shift begins:

1. **Walking or marching:** 1 minute
2. **Leg swings:** 10 each direction
3. **Hip circles:** 10 each direction
4. **Cat-cow:** 8-10 cycles
5. **Hip hinge practice:** 10 reps
6. **Squats:** 10 reps
7. **Shoulder rolls:** 10 each direction

---

## Post-Shift Recovery (10 minutes)

After your shift ends:

### Lower Body

- Hip flexor stretch: 30 seconds each
- Quad stretch: 30 seconds each
- Hamstring stretch: 30 seconds each
- Calf stretch: 30 seconds each

### Back

- Cat-cow: 10 cycles
- Child's pose: 60 seconds
- Knee-to-chest: 30 seconds each, then both

### Neck and Shoulders

- Upper trap stretch: 30 seconds each
- Chin tucks: 10 reps
- Doorway chest stretch: 30 seconds each

## Weekly Workout Schedule

### Minimum (2x per week, 20 minutes)

**Workout A:**
- Glute bridges: 3x15
- Bird dog: 3x10 each
- Squats: 3x15
- Plank: 3x30 sec
- Hip flexor stretch: 30 sec each

**Workout B:**
- Dead bug: 3x10 each
- Lunges: 3x10 each
- Calf raises: 3x20
- Side plank: 3x20 sec each
- Back stretches: 5 min

### Optimal (3-4x per week, 30 minutes)

**Day 1 - Core + Back:**
- Bird dog: 3x12 each
- Dead bug: 3x12 each
- Plank: 3x45 sec
- Glute bridges: 3x15
- Cat-cow and child's pose: 5 min

**Day 2 - Legs:**
- Squats: 3x15
- Lunges: 3x10 each
- Step-ups: 3x10 each
- Calf raises: 3x20
- Wall sits: 3x30 sec
- Lower body stretches: 5 min

**Day 3 - Full Body:**
- Glute bridges: 2x15
- Squats: 2x12
- Bird dog: 2x10 each
- Push-ups: 2x10
- Planks: 2x30 sec
- Full stretch routine: 10 min

## Lifting and Patient Handling

### Safe Lifting Principles

1. **Get close to the patient/load**
2. **Wide base of support** (feet apart)
3. **Bend at hips and knees** (not back)
4. **Brace your core** before lifting
5. **Use your legs** to power the lift
6. **Don't twist** while lifting—pivot feet instead
7. **Ask for help** with heavy patients

### Building Lifting Strength

**Goblet Squats:**
- Mimic the squat-to-lift motion
- 3 sets of 12

**Romanian Deadlifts:**
- Build hip hinge strength
- 3 sets of 10

**Farmer's Carries:**
- Build grip and core for carrying
- 3 sets of 40 yards

## Managing Night Shift

### Before Night Shift

- Light exercise in the afternoon
- Don't exhaust yourself before work

### During Night Shift

- More frequent micro-movements
- Brief walks when possible
- Stay hydrated

### After Night Shift

- Gentle stretching only (body needs sleep)
- Save workouts for awake hours
- Prioritize sleep over exercise

---

## Common Mistakes

### Training Errors

- **Skipping core work:** It's the foundation of back protection
- **Not stretching after shifts:** Recovery matters
- **Overdoing it on days off:** Fatigued body needs rest
- **Ignoring warning signs:** Small pains become big injuries

### On-Shift Errors

- **Lifting alone when help is available**
- **Twisting while lifting**
- **Bending from the back**
- **Standing still too long** (keep moving)
- **Skipping breaks** (your body needs them)

---

## Equipment Recommendations

### Footwear

- **Quality nursing shoes** with arch support
- **Replace every 6-12 months** (they wear out)
- **Compression socks** for long shifts
- **Consider custom orthotics** if foot pain persists

### Support Items

- **Lumbar roll** for chairs and documentation
- **Ergonomic keyboard/mouse** for charting
- **Mat at standing stations** when possible

---

## The Bottom Line

Nursing demands physical resilience. A consistent exercise routine—even just 20 minutes twice a week—can prevent the injuries that sideline careers and reduce the daily aches that make shifts miserable.

**Priority areas:**
1. Core strength (protects your back)
2. Hip hinge mechanics (safe lifting)
3. Leg strength and endurance (standing all day)
4. Daily stretching (recovery between shifts)
5. Shoulder and neck care (charting posture)

Take care of yourself so you can keep taking care of others.
