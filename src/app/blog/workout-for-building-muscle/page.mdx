# Workout for Building Muscle: The Complete Hypertrophy Guide

Building muscle isn't complicated, but it does require understanding a few key principles. This guide covers what actually matters for hypertrophy—and what you can ignore.

## The Three Requirements for Muscle Growth

### 1. Mechanical Tension

Your muscles need to work against resistance. This is the primary driver of growth.

- Lift weights that challenge you
- Use full range of motion
- Control the movement (don't swing)

### 2. Progressive Overload

You must do more over time. Your body only builds muscle when forced to adapt.

- Add weight gradually
- Add reps before adding weight
- Track your progress

### 3. Recovery

Muscle grows during rest, not during training.

- Sleep 7-9 hours
- Eat enough protein
- Allow 48-72 hours between training the same muscle

## Optimal Training Variables

### Rep Ranges

Muscle grows across various rep ranges, but some are more practical:

**6-12 reps:** The hypertrophy sweet spot
- Heavy enough to create tension
- Light enough for sufficient volume
- Best balance of growth and fatigue

**3-5 reps:** Strength focus
- Builds muscle but less efficiently
- Good for main compound lifts
- Higher injury risk

**12-20 reps:** Metabolic stress focus
- Good for isolation exercises
- Useful for joint-friendly training
- Complements heavier work

### Sets Per Muscle Per Week

Research suggests optimal volume is:

**Beginners:** 10-12 sets per muscle group per week
**Intermediate:** 12-18 sets per muscle group per week
**Advanced:** 18-25+ sets per muscle group per week

Start lower and add volume as you adapt.

### Training Frequency

Each muscle should be trained 2-3 times per week for optimal growth.

**Full body:** 3 days per week
**Upper/Lower:** 4 days per week
**Push/Pull/Legs:** 6 days per week

Higher frequency spreads volume across more sessions, improving quality.

## Exercise Selection

### Compound Movements (Priority)

These train multiple muscles and should form your foundation:

**Horizontal Push:** Bench press, dumbbell press, push-ups
**Horizontal Pull:** Rows, cable rows, inverted rows
**Vertical Push:** Overhead press, dumbbell shoulder press
**Vertical Pull:** Pull-ups, lat pulldowns, chin-ups
**Hip Hinge:** Deadlifts, Romanian deadlifts, hip thrusts
**Squat Pattern:** Squats, leg press, lunges

### Isolation Movements (Support)

Target specific muscles after compounds:

**Arms:** Bicep curls, tricep extensions, hammer curls
**Shoulders:** Lateral raises, rear delt flys, face pulls
**Chest:** Flys, cable crossovers
**Back:** Straight-arm pulldowns, pullovers
**Legs:** Leg curls, leg extensions, calf raises

### Exercise Order

1. Most demanding compound movements first
2. Secondary compounds next
3. Isolation exercises last
4. Weakest/priority muscles earlier when fresh

## Beginner Program (3 Days/Week)

Full body training, three times per week with at least one rest day between sessions.

### Day A

1. **Squat**: 3 sets × 8-10 reps
2. **Bench Press**: 3 sets × 8-10 reps
3. **Barbell Row**: 3 sets × 8-10 reps
4. **Romanian Deadlift**: 3 sets × 10-12 reps
5. **Overhead Press**: 2 sets × 10-12 reps
6. **Face Pulls**: 2 sets × 15 reps

### Day B

1. **Deadlift**: 3 sets × 6-8 reps
2. **Incline Dumbbell Press**: 3 sets × 10-12 reps
3. **Pull-ups or Lat Pulldown**: 3 sets × 8-10 reps
4. **Leg Press**: 3 sets × 10-12 reps
5. **Dumbbell Lateral Raise**: 2 sets × 12-15 reps
6. **Bicep Curls**: 2 sets × 10-12 reps

Alternate A/B/A one week, B/A/B the next.

## Intermediate Program (4 Days/Week)

Upper/Lower split allows more volume per muscle group.

### Upper A (Monday)

1. **Bench Press**: 4 sets × 6-8 reps
2. **Barbell Row**: 4 sets × 6-8 reps
3. **Overhead Press**: 3 sets × 8-10 reps
4. **Lat Pulldown**: 3 sets × 10-12 reps
5. **Dumbbell Curl**: 3 sets × 10-12 reps
6. **Tricep Pushdown**: 3 sets × 10-12 reps
7. **Face Pulls**: 2 sets × 15 reps

### Lower A (Tuesday)

1. **Squat**: 4 sets × 6-8 reps
2. **Romanian Deadlift**: 3 sets × 8-10 reps
3. **Leg Press**: 3 sets × 10-12 reps
4. **Leg Curl**: 3 sets × 10-12 reps
5. **Calf Raises**: 4 sets × 12-15 reps
6. **Planks**: 3 sets × 30-45 seconds

### Upper B (Thursday)

1. **Incline Dumbbell Press**: 4 sets × 8-10 reps
2. **Pull-ups or Chin-ups**: 4 sets × 6-10 reps
3. **Cable Row**: 3 sets × 10-12 reps
4. **Dumbbell Shoulder Press**: 3 sets × 10-12 reps
5. **Lateral Raises**: 3 sets × 12-15 reps
6. **Hammer Curls**: 2 sets × 10-12 reps
7. **Overhead Tricep Extension**: 2 sets × 10-12 reps

### Lower B (Friday)

1. **Deadlift**: 4 sets × 5-6 reps
2. **Bulgarian Split Squat**: 3 sets × 8-10 each leg
3. **Hip Thrust**: 3 sets × 10-12 reps
4. **Leg Extension**: 3 sets × 12-15 reps
5. **Seated Calf Raise**: 4 sets × 12-15 reps
6. **Cable Crunch**: 3 sets × 12-15 reps

## Progressive Overload Strategies

### Double Progression

The simplest approach:

1. Pick a rep range (e.g., 8-12)
2. Start at the bottom (8 reps)
3. Add reps each session until you hit the top (12 reps)
4. Add weight, drop back to 8 reps
5. Repeat

### Adding Weight

- Upper body: Add 2.5-5 lbs when ready
- Lower body: Add 5-10 lbs when ready
- Don't rush—consistent small increases add up

### Other Progression Methods

- Add a set
- Slow down the tempo
- Reduce rest periods
- Improve range of motion
- Better form with same weight counts

## Technique Tips for Growth

### Mind-Muscle Connection

Focus on the target muscle:
- Feel it stretch at the bottom
- Feel it contract at the top
- Don't just move weight from A to B

### Tempo

Control the movement:
- 2-3 seconds lowering (eccentric)
- Brief pause at bottom
- 1-2 seconds lifting (concentric)
- Brief squeeze at top

### Full Range of Motion

Partial reps build partial muscles:
- Stretch the muscle fully at bottom
- Contract fully at top
- Don't ego lift with shortened range

### Avoid Momentum

If you're swinging weight, it's too heavy:
- Control every rep
- Pause if needed to reset
- Reduce weight to maintain form

## Recovery for Muscle Growth

### Sleep

Growth hormone releases during deep sleep:
- Aim for 7-9 hours
- Consistent sleep schedule helps
- Quality matters as much as quantity

### Nutrition

**Protein:** 0.7-1g per pound of body weight daily
- Spread across 4-6 meals
- Include protein at every meal
- Complete sources (meat, dairy, eggs) or combined plant sources

**Calories:** Slight surplus for optimal growth
- 200-500 calories above maintenance
- Too much surplus = excess fat gain
- Track and adjust based on results

**Carbs:** Fuel your training
- Eat enough to train hard
- Around workouts is ideal timing

### Rest Days

Muscles grow during rest:
- 48-72 hours between training same muscle
- Active recovery (walking, light movement) is fine
- Don't skip rest days

## Common Muscle-Building Mistakes

### Not Eating Enough

You can't build something from nothing:
- Track calories for a few weeks
- Most people underestimate intake
- Small surplus, not massive bulk

### Program Hopping

Results take time:
- Stick with a program for 8-12 weeks minimum
- Progress the weights before changing programs
- Consistency beats novelty

### Ignoring Weak Points

Address lagging muscles:
- Train them first when fresh
- Add extra volume
- Use exercises that target them better

### All Intensity, No Volume

Going to failure every set isn't optimal:
- Leave 1-3 reps in reserve on most sets
- Save failure for last set of an exercise
- Volume drives growth more than intensity

### Skipping Legs

Half your muscle is below the waist:
- Leg training boosts anabolic hormones
- Full body development looks better
- Functional strength requires legs

## Tracking Progress

### What to Track

- Exercises, weights, sets, reps
- Body weight (weekly average)
- Progress photos (monthly)
- Measurements if desired

### Signs of Progress

- Weight on the bar increasing
- More reps at same weight
- Better muscle definition
- Clothes fitting differently
- Progress photos showing change

### Realistic Expectations

**Beginners:** 1-2 lbs muscle per month
**Intermediate:** 0.5-1 lb muscle per month
**Advanced:** 0.25-0.5 lb muscle per month

Scale weight includes water, glycogen, and fat—don't obsess over daily changes.

## The Bottom Line

Building muscle requires:
- Training each muscle 2-3x per week
- 10-20 hard sets per muscle per week
- Progressive overload over time
- Adequate protein and calories
- Sufficient sleep and recovery

Pick a program that fits your schedule, progressively overload the exercises, eat enough to grow, sleep enough to recover, and stay consistent for months and years.

Muscle building is simple—just not easy. Do the basics well, stay patient, and results will come.
