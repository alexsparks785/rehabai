# Hamstring Exercises: Strengthen and Stretch for Better Performance

Your hamstrings are workhorses—powering every step you take, protecting your knees, and supporting your lower back. Yet they're often neglected until something goes wrong. Strong, flexible hamstrings are essential for athletic performance and injury prevention.

**Key insight:** Hamstrings need both strength AND length. Tight hamstrings are often weak hamstrings—strengthening through full range of motion builds both.

---

## Understanding Your Hamstrings

### Anatomy

| Muscle | Location | Function |
|--------|----------|----------|
| **Biceps femoris** | Outer thigh | Knee flexion, hip extension, external rotation |
| **Semimembranosus** | Inner thigh (deep) | Knee flexion, hip extension, internal rotation |
| **Semitendinosus** | Inner thigh (superficial) | Knee flexion, hip extension, internal rotation |

### Why Hamstrings Matter

- **Injury prevention:** Strong hamstrings protect ACL and reduce strains
- **Running speed:** Primary hip extensor for sprinting
- **Knee health:** Balance quad dominance
- **Lower back:** Support pelvis position
- **Athletic performance:** Power for jumping, cutting, accelerating

### Common Problems

- Hamstring strains (especially in sprinting sports)
- Chronic tightness
- Weakness relative to quadriceps
- Contributing to low back pain

## Hamstring Strengthening

### Romanian Deadlift (RDL)

The gold standard hamstring exercise.

**How to do it:**
1. Hold weight in front of thighs
2. Push hips back (hip hinge)
3. Keep back flat, slight knee bend
4. Lower until you feel hamstring stretch
5. Squeeze glutes and hamstrings to stand
6. Perform 10-12 reps, 3-4 sets

**Key:** Feel the stretch in your hamstrings at the bottom—that's your range.

### Nordic Curl

Most effective exercise for hamstring injury prevention.

**How to do it:**
1. Kneel with ankles anchored (partner, bar, or couch)
2. Keep body straight from knees to head
3. Slowly lower toward ground (5+ seconds)
4. Control as far as possible
5. Catch yourself with hands, push back up
6. Perform 4-8 reps, 3 sets

**Research shows:** Up to 51% reduction in hamstring injuries with Nordic training.

### Glute-Ham Raise

**How to do it:**
1. Position on GHD machine (or improvise)
2. Knees on pad, feet anchored
3. Lower torso toward ground
4. Use hamstrings to pull back up
5. Perform 8-12 reps, 3 sets

### Lying Leg Curl

**How to do it:**
1. Lie face down on leg curl machine
2. Pad above heels
3. Curl heels toward buttocks
4. Squeeze at top
5. Lower slowly (3-4 seconds)
6. Perform 12-15 reps, 3 sets

### Seated Leg Curl

**How to do it:**
1. Sit in leg curl machine
2. Pad behind ankles
3. Curl heels under seat
4. Control the return
5. Perform 12-15 reps, 3 sets

### Single-Leg RDL

**How to do it:**
1. Stand on one leg
2. Hinge at hip, reaching other leg back
3. Keep back flat
4. Lower until hamstring stretch on standing leg
5. Squeeze to return
6. Perform 8-10 each leg, 3 sets

### Swiss Ball Hamstring Curl

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back, heels on stability ball
2. Lift hips into bridge position
3. Curl ball toward buttocks
4. Extend back out while keeping hips up
5. Perform 12-15 reps, 3 sets

### Slider Leg Curl

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back, heels on sliders (or towel on hard floor)
2. Bridge hips up
3. Slide heels toward buttocks
4. Slide back out, maintaining bridge
5. Perform 10-12 reps, 3 sets

## Hamstring Stretches

### Standing Hamstring Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Place heel on low surface (chair, step)
2. Keep both legs straight
3. Hinge at hips, reaching toward foot
4. Keep back flat—don't round
5. Hold 30-60 seconds each leg

### Supine Hamstring Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back
2. Lift one leg toward ceiling
3. Use hands or strap behind thigh
4. Keep knee straight
5. Pull leg toward you gently
6. Hold 30-60 seconds each leg

### Doorway Hamstring Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Lie in doorway
2. Extend one leg up the door frame
3. Other leg through doorway, flat on floor
4. Scoot closer for more stretch
5. Hold 60-90 seconds each leg

### Seated Forward Fold

**How to do it:**
1. Sit with legs extended
2. Reach toward toes
3. Keep back as flat as possible
4. Go until stretch is felt
5. Hold 30-60 seconds

### Standing Pike Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Stand with feet together
2. Fold forward, reaching for floor
3. Keep knees straight (or slightly bent)
4. Let head hang
5. Hold 30-60 seconds

### PNF Hamstring Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Supine position, leg lifted with strap
2. Push leg into strap (contract hamstring) for 5-10 seconds
3. Relax and stretch deeper
4. Repeat 3-4 times
5. Produces faster flexibility gains

## Dynamic Hamstring Exercises

### Leg Swings

**How to do it:**
1. Stand sideways to support
2. Swing leg forward and back
3. Let momentum increase range
4. Keep torso upright
5. Perform 15-20 swings each leg

### Walking RDL

**How to do it:**
1. Stand on one leg
2. Hinge forward while reaching other leg back
3. Touch ground if possible
4. Step through to other leg
5. Continue walking pattern
6. Perform 10-12 total

### Inchworms

**How to do it:**
1. Stand, fold forward, touch floor
2. Walk hands out to plank
3. Walk feet toward hands
4. Stand up
5. Perform 8-10 reps

### Frankenstein Walks

**How to do it:**
1. Walk forward with straight legs
2. Kick leg up toward opposite hand
3. Alternate legs
4. Perform 10-12 each leg

## Hamstring Injury Rehabilitation

### Acute Phase (Days 1-5)

- RICE protocol
- Gentle pain-free movement
- Isometric contractions only
- No stretching

### Early Rehab (Week 1-2)

| Exercise | Sets × Reps |
|----------|-------------|
| Isometric holds (various angles) | 5 × 10 sec |
| Prone heel digs | 3 × 10 |
| Gentle walking | 10-15 minutes |

### Progressive Loading (Week 3-6)

| Exercise | Sets × Reps |
|----------|-------------|
| Bridge march | 3 × 10 each |
| Slider curls (double-leg) | 3 × 10 |
| RDL (light) | 3 × 12 |
| Nordic (partial range) | 3 × 5 |

### Return to Sport (Week 6+)

| Exercise | Sets × Reps |
|----------|-------------|
| Nordic curls (full) | 3 × 8 |
| Single-leg RDL | 3 × 8 each |
| Sport-specific drills | Progressive |
| Running progression | Gradual return |

## Common Mistakes

### 1. Only Stretching

**Problem:** Tight hamstrings are often weak hamstrings
**Fix:** Prioritize strengthening; flexibility often follows

### 2. Rounding Back During RDL

**Problem:** Shifts load to back, reduces hamstring work
**Fix:** Keep back flat, push hips back

### 3. Neglecting Eccentric Work

**Problem:** Missing key injury prevention stimulus
**Fix:** Include Nordic curls; emphasize slow lowering

### 4. Quad Dominance

**Problem:** Hamstrings underdeveloped relative to quads
**Fix:** Balance quad and hamstring training volume

### 5. Static Stretching Before Activity

**Problem:** May reduce power output
**Fix:** Dynamic stretching before, static after

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Nordic curls prevent injuries** — Research strongly supports this
2. **RDL is foundational** — Best overall hamstring builder
3. **Eccentric strength matters** — Slow lowering protects against strains
4. **Strengthen through length** — Build flexibility while building strength
5. **Balance with quads** — Aim for hamstring:quad ratio of 60-80%
6. **Dynamic before, static after** — Match stretch type to timing

**Weekly minimum:**
- 2 hamstring strengthening sessions
- Include Nordic curls or equivalent eccentric
- Daily dynamic mobility
- Post-workout static stretching

Strong, flexible hamstrings are your foundation for speed, power, and injury-free movement. Train them intentionally, and they'll serve you well.
