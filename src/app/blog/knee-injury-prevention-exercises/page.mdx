# Knee Injury Prevention: Exercises to Protect Your Knees

Knee injuries can be devastating—from ACL tears that require surgery to chronic conditions that limit your activities for years. Yet many knee injuries are preventable with the right training. Whether you're an athlete, weekend warrior, or just want to protect your knees as you age, these exercises can dramatically reduce your risk.

## Understanding Knee Injuries

### Common Knee Injuries

**ACL (Anterior Cruciate Ligament):**
- Often non-contact, during cutting or landing
- More common in females (2-8x higher risk)
- Frequently requires surgery
- 6-12 month recovery

**MCL (Medial Collateral Ligament):**
- Usually from direct blow or force to outer knee
- Often heals without surgery
- Common in contact sports

**Meniscus:**
- Twisting injuries
- Degenerative tears with age
- May or may not require surgery

**Patellofemoral Pain (Runner's Knee):**
- Pain around/behind kneecap
- Often from overuse, weakness, or poor mechanics
- Responds well to targeted exercise

### Risk Factors for Knee Injury

**Controllable:**
- Weak quadriceps and hamstrings
- Weak hip muscles (especially gluteus medius)
- Poor landing mechanics
- Muscle imbalances
- Inadequate warm-up
- Poor flexibility

**Less Controllable:**
- Female sex (ACL risk)
- Previous knee injury
- Joint laxity
- Anatomical factors

## The Keys to Knee Protection

### 1. Hip Strength

Weak hips allow the knee to collapse inward (valgus)—the position where most non-contact ACL injuries occur.

### 2. Quad and Hamstring Strength

Strong muscles absorb force and stabilize the joint.

### 3. Balance and Proprioception

Your body needs to know where your knee is in space to protect it.

### 4. Landing and Deceleration Mechanics

How you land from jumps and stop running matters enormously.

### 5. Flexibility

Adequate mobility reduces compensatory stress on the knee.

## Hip Strengthening Exercises

### Clamshell

**The fundamental hip abductor exercise:**

1. Lie on side, knees bent 45°
2. Keep feet together
3. Lift top knee toward ceiling
4. Keep pelvis stable—don't roll back
5. Lower with control

*Reps: 15-20 per side, 3 sets*

*Progress: Add resistance band around knees*

### Lateral Band Walk

1. Place band around ankles (or above knees for easier)
2. Athletic stance, slight knee bend
3. Step sideways, maintaining tension
4. Keep toes pointed forward

*Reps: 15-20 steps each direction, 3 sets*

### Single-Leg Romanian Deadlift

1. Stand on one leg
2. Hip hinge, extending other leg back
3. Keep hips level
4. Return to standing

*Reps: 8-10 per leg, 3 sets*

### Side-Lying Hip Raise

1. Lie on side, bottom knee bent
2. Lift hips off floor
3. Hold at top
4. Lower with control

*Reps: 12-15 per side, 3 sets*

### Fire Hydrant

1. On all fours
2. Lift knee out to side
3. Keep hips square
4. Lower with control

*Reps: 15 per side, 3 sets*

## Quadriceps Strengthening

### Terminal Knee Extension

1. Place band around fixed point at knee height
2. Place band behind your knee
3. Stand facing anchor, knee slightly bent
4. Straighten knee against band resistance

*Reps: 15-20 per leg, 3 sets*

### Wall Sit

1. Back against wall
2. Slide down until thighs parallel to floor
3. Hold
4. Keep knees over ankles

*Hold: 30-60 seconds, 3 sets*

### Step-Down (Eccentric Focus)

1. Stand on step, one foot near edge
2. Slowly lower the other foot toward floor (3-4 seconds)
3. Tap floor lightly
4. Return to start

*Reps: 10-12 per leg, 3 sets*

### Single-Leg Squat to Box

1. Stand on one leg in front of a box/chair
2. Slowly sit back and down
3. Tap the box
4. Stand back up

*Reps: 8-10 per leg, 3 sets*

## Hamstring Strengthening

### Nordic Curl

**The gold standard for hamstring injury prevention:**

1. Kneel on pad, have partner hold ankles (or anchor them)
2. Keeping body straight, slowly lower toward floor
3. Control the descent as long as possible
4. Push up with hands and repeat

*Reps: 5-8, 3 sets (build up slowly)*

### Glute-Ham Bridge

1. Lie on back, heels on elevated surface (ball or slider)
2. Bridge up, then curl heels toward buttocks
3. Extend legs back out
4. Lower with control

*Reps: 10-12, 3 sets*

### Romanian Deadlift

1. Stand with weights in front of thighs
2. Hip hinge, pushing hips back
3. Keep slight knee bend
4. Feel stretch in hamstrings
5. Squeeze glutes to return

*Reps: 10-12, 3 sets*

## Balance and Proprioception

### Single-Leg Stand Progressions

**Level 1:** Single-leg stand on firm surface, eyes open (30 seconds)
**Level 2:** Eyes closed (30 seconds)
**Level 3:** On unstable surface (BOSU, pillow) (30 seconds)
**Level 4:** Eyes closed on unstable surface (30 seconds)
**Level 5:** Add ball catches while balancing

*Duration: Progress through levels as able*

### Single-Leg Mini Squats

1. Stand on one leg
2. Perform shallow squats (quarter depth)
3. Keep knee tracking over second toe
4. Maintain balance throughout

*Reps: 15-20 per leg, 3 sets*

### Star Excursion Balance

1. Stand on one leg
2. Reach other foot to touch the ground in different directions:
   - Forward
   - 45° forward-left
   - Side
   - 45° backward
   - Behind
3. Return to start each time

*Reps: 3-5 in each direction per leg*

## Landing Mechanics Training

### Box Jump with Focus on Landing

1. Jump onto a low box (12-18 inches)
2. Land softly with knees bent
3. Knees should track over toes (not collapsing inward)
4. Step down and repeat

*Reps: 8-10, 3 sets*

### Drop Landing

1. Stand on low box
2. Step off (don't jump)
3. Land with soft knees, athletic position
4. Stick the landing for 2 seconds

*Focus: Knees tracking over toes, quiet landing*
*Reps: 10, 3 sets*

### Depth Jump to Stick

1. Step off box
2. Upon landing, immediately jump up
3. Land and hold (stick)
4. Focus on knee alignment

*Reps: 6-8, 3 sets*

### Single-Leg Landing

1. Hop forward on one leg
2. Land softly on the same leg
3. Stick the landing
4. Check knee position

*Reps: 8-10 per leg, 3 sets*

### Cutting and Deceleration Drills

**Forward to Stop:**
1. Jog forward
2. Plant and stop sharply
3. Athletic position, knees bent
4. Focus on controlled deceleration

**Cut and Plant:**
1. Jog at 45° angle
2. Plant outside foot and cut
3. Keep knee over foot (no inward collapse)
4. Accelerate in new direction

*Reps: 10 each drill*

## FIFA 11+ Warm-Up Protocol

This evidence-based warm-up reduces knee injuries by up to 50% in soccer players and is useful for all sports.

### Part 1: Running (8 minutes)

1. Straight running
2. Hip out (running with external rotation)
3. Hip in (running with internal rotation)
4. Circling partner
5. Jumping with shoulder contact
6. Quick forward-back runs

### Part 2: Strength/Balance (10 minutes)

1. Plank variations
2. Side plank variations
3. Nordic hamstring curls
4. Single-leg balance
5. Squats
6. Lunges

### Part 3: Running Exercises (2 minutes)

1. Running at 75-80% speed
2. Bounding
3. Plant and cut

## Complete Knee Prevention Program

### Daily (5 minutes)

1. Single-leg balance: 30 seconds per leg
2. Clamshells: 15 per side
3. Terminal knee extension: 15 per side

### 3x Per Week (20-25 minutes)

**Warm-Up:**
1. General cardio: 5 minutes
2. Leg swings: 10 per direction per leg
3. Bodyweight squats: 15 reps

**Strength:**
1. Nordic curls: 3x6-8 (or 3x10 glute-ham bridge)
2. Single-leg RDL: 3x8-10 per side
3. Lateral band walks: 3x15 each direction
4. Step-downs: 3x10 per leg
5. Wall sit: 3x30 seconds

**Balance/Plyometrics:**
1. Single-leg mini squats: 3x15 per side
2. Box jumps (landing focus): 3x8
3. Star excursion balance: 3 reps each direction per leg

### Sport-Specific Days

Add cutting drills, sport-specific movements, and practice landing mechanics during practice sessions.

## Special Considerations

### For Female Athletes

Women have higher ACL injury rates due to anatomical, hormonal, and neuromuscular factors. Extra emphasis on:
- Hamstring strengthening (reduce quad dominance)
- Hip strengthening (prevent knee valgus)
- Landing mechanics (avoid "stiff" landings)
- Neuromuscular training programs like FIFA 11+

### For Runners

- Gradual mileage increases (10% rule)
- Hip and glute strengthening is critical
- Address any limb strength asymmetries
- Proper footwear and running mechanics
- Don't run through knee pain

### For Older Adults

- Balance training is essential
- Gradual strengthening progression
- Focus on functional movements
- Consider lower-impact activities
- Address any arthritis proactively

### After Previous Injury

- Complete full rehabilitation
- Address any remaining weaknesses
- Consider bracing for return to sport
- Gradual return to cutting/pivoting
- Work with physical therapist

## Warning Signs to Watch

See a healthcare provider if:

- Knee swelling after activity
- Knee "giving way" or feeling unstable
- Pain that limits activity
- Clicking or catching with pain
- Warmth or redness
- Pain that doesn't improve with rest

## Key Takeaways

1. **Hip strength is crucial**—weak hips cause knee collapse (valgus)
2. **Hamstrings protect the ACL**—prioritize Nordic curls and glute-ham exercises
3. **Landing mechanics matter**—soft landings with knees over toes
4. **Balance training is essential**—progress from simple to challenging
5. **Warm-up properly**—FIFA 11+ reduces injuries by up to 50%
6. **Address asymmetries**—imbalances increase injury risk
7. **Prevention programs work**—but only if you do them consistently

Knee injuries can be devastating, but most are preventable. Invest 20-30 minutes, three times per week, in a targeted prevention program, and you'll dramatically reduce your risk while improving your athletic performance.
