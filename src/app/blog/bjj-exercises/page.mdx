# BJJ Exercises: Build Strength and Mobility for Jiu-Jitsu

Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu demands a unique combination of strength, flexibility, and endurance. You need vice-grip hands, hips that move freely in every direction, and the conditioning to stay dangerous in the final minute of a roll. Strategic training off the mats makes you more effective on them.

**Key insight:** BJJ is about leverage, not brute strength—but strength applied to good technique multiplies its effectiveness. Train to enhance your jiu-jitsu, not replace it.

---

## Physical Demands of BJJ

### What BJJ Requires

- **Grip strength:** Controlling opponents, maintaining position
- **Hip mobility:** Guard play, escapes, scrambles
- **Pulling strength:** Collar drags, arm drags, sweeps
- **Bridging power:** Escaping mount, reversals
- **Rotational core strength:** Finishing submissions, scrambles
- **Isometric endurance:** Holding positions under pressure

### Common BJJ Injuries

| Injury | Cause | Prevention Focus |
|--------|-------|------------------|
| Neck injuries | Stacking, guillotines | Neck strengthening, tapping early |
| Shoulder injuries | Kimuras, americanas, posting | Rotator cuff strength, mobility |
| Knee injuries | Heel hooks, twisting | Leg strength, controlled training |
| Lower back pain | Guard play, compression | Core strength, hip mobility |
| Finger/hand injuries | Gi grips | Grip training, tape, recovery |

## Grip Strength

The foundation of gi jiu-jitsu.

### Gi Pull-Ups

**How to do it:**
1. Drape gi over pull-up bar
2. Grip gi like collar grips
3. Perform pull-ups
4. 3-5 sets to near failure

**Alternative:** Towel pull-ups

### Farmer's Carries

**How to do it:**
1. Hold heavy weights at sides
2. Walk with control
3. 40-60 yards, 3-4 sets

**Why:** Builds grip endurance and core stability.

### Dead Hangs

**How to do it:**
1. Hang from bar
2. Hold as long as possible
3. 3-5 sets

**Variations:** One-arm hangs, towel hangs, gi hangs

### Plate Pinches

**How to do it:**
1. Pinch two weight plates together (smooth sides out)
2. Hold 20-30 seconds
3. 3-5 sets

### Rice Bucket Training

**How to do it:**
1. Fill bucket with rice
2. Open/close hands in rice
3. Rotate wrists, grab and release
4. 2-3 minutes daily

**Benefits:** Grip endurance, forearm recovery

## Hip Mobility

Essential for guard play and escapes.

### 90/90 Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Sit with front leg at 90°, back leg at 90°
2. Lean forward over front shin
3. Hold 45-60 seconds each side

### Frog Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. On all fours, widen knees
2. Rock hips back
3. Hold 60-90 seconds

### Deep Squat Hold

**How to do it:**
1. Squat as deep as possible
2. Keep heels down
3. Hold 60-90 seconds

### Pigeon Pose

**How to do it:**
1. Front shin across body
2. Back leg extended
3. Fold forward
4. 60 seconds each side

### Hip CARs (Controlled Articular Rotations)

**How to do it:**
1. On all fours
2. Lift knee, make large circles
3. Maximum controlled range
4. 5 each direction, each hip

### Butterfly Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Sit, soles of feet together
2. Gently press knees toward floor
3. Hold 45-60 seconds

### Seated Straddle

**How to do it:**
1. Sit with legs wide
2. Walk hands forward
3. Hold 45-60 seconds

## Pulling Strength

BJJ is a pulling sport.

### Pull-Ups

**How to do it:**
1. Dead hang, pull chin over bar
2. Lower with control
3. 3-5 sets of max reps

**Variations:** Chin-ups, wide grip, commando grip

### Bent-Over Rows

**How to do it:**
1. Hinge at hips, flat back
2. Pull weights to torso
3. 3 sets of 10-12

### Single-Arm Dumbbell Rows

**How to do it:**
1. One hand on bench
2. Row dumbbell to hip
3. 3 sets of 10 each arm

### Cable/Band Face Pulls

**How to do it:**
1. Pull to face, separate hands
2. Squeeze shoulder blades
3. 3 sets of 15

### Inverted Rows

**How to do it:**
1. Hang under bar, body straight
2. Pull chest to bar
3. 3 sets of 12-15

## Bridging and Hip Extension

For escapes and sweeps.

### Glute Bridges

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back, knees bent
2. Drive hips up explosively
3. Squeeze glutes at top
4. 3 sets of 15

**Progression:** Single-leg bridges, weighted bridges

### Hip Escapes (Shrimping)

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back
2. Bridge and turn onto shoulder
3. Push hip away, bring knee through
4. 3 sets of 10 each side

### Barbell Hip Thrusts

**How to do it:**
1. Upper back on bench
2. Barbell across hips
3. Drive hips up forcefully
4. 3 sets of 10-12

### Kettlebell Swings

**How to do it:**
1. Hinge and swing kettlebell
2. Drive with hips, not arms
3. 3 sets of 15-20

## Core Training for BJJ

### Rotational Core

**Russian Twists:**
1. Seated, feet elevated
2. Rotate side to side
3. 3 sets of 20

**Cable/Band Woodchops:**
1. Rotate diagonally across body
2. 3 sets of 10 each side

**Medicine Ball Throws:**
1. Rotational throws into wall
2. 3 sets of 10 each side

### Anti-Rotation

**Pallof Press:**
1. Resist rotation while pressing out
2. 3 sets of 10 each side

**Bird Dog:**
1. Opposite arm/leg extension
2. Keep back flat
3. 3 sets of 10 each side

### Stability

**Plank:**
- 60 seconds, 3 sets

**Side Plank:**
- 45 seconds each side, 3 sets

**Dead Bug:**
1. Lower opposite limbs
2. Keep lower back down
3. 3 sets of 10 each side

### Wrestling-Style Core

**Gut Wrench Simulation:**
1. Lie on stability ball belly-down
2. Rotate side to side under control
3. 30-60 seconds

## Neck Strengthening

Critical for avoiding injuries and escaping bad positions.

### Neck Bridges (Begin Carefully)

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back, knees bent
2. Bridge onto top of head
3. Build up gradually
4. Start with 10-15 seconds

### Manual Resistance

**How to do it:**
1. Use hands to resist neck movement
2. Push in each direction
3. 10 seconds each, 3 sets

### Neck Harness

**If available:**
1. Controlled flexion/extension
2. Start very light
3. Build progressively

### Shrugs

**How to do it:**
1. Heavy weights, shrug to ears
2. Hold briefly
3. 3 sets of 15

## Conditioning

### Grappling-Specific Intervals

**Protocol:**
- 5-minute rounds with 1-minute rest
- Match your competition format
- 3-6 rounds

### Rowing Machine

**Why:** Similar muscles to pulling in BJJ

**Protocol:**
- 500m sprints with 90 sec rest
- 4-6 rounds

### Assault Bike/Airdyne

**Protocol:**
- 30 seconds all-out, 30 seconds easy
- 8-12 rounds

### Burpees

**How to do it:**
1. Chest to floor
2. Up and jump
3. 3 sets of 15 or timed rounds

### Jump Rope

**Protocol:**
- 3-minute rounds, 1-minute rest
- 3-5 rounds

## Sample Training Programs

### 2x Per Week (Supplement to BJJ)

**Day 1 - Pull + Core:**
- Pull-ups (or gi pull-ups): 4 sets
- Rows: 3x10
- Face pulls: 3x15
- Pallof press: 3x10 each
- Dead bug: 3x10 each
- Grip work: 10 min

**Day 2 - Lower + Mobility:**
- Goblet squats: 3x12
- Glute bridges: 3x15
- Single-leg RDL: 3x10 each
- Russian twists: 3x20
- Full hip mobility: 15 min
- Neck work: 10 min

### 3x Per Week (More Development)

**Day 1 - Pull Strength:**
- Pull-ups: 4x max
- Bent-over rows: 4x10
- Single-arm rows: 3x10 each
- Face pulls: 3x15
- Grip circuit: 15 min

**Day 2 - Lower Body + Core:**
- Squats: 3x10
- Hip thrusts: 3x12
- Bulgarian split squats: 3x8 each
- Kettlebell swings: 3x15
- Core circuit: 15 min
- Neck work: 10 min

**Day 3 - Conditioning + Mobility:**
- Rowing intervals: 5x500m
- Burpees: 3x15
- Full hip mobility: 20 min
- Grip maintenance
- Stretching: 10 min

## Position-Specific Training

### For Guard Players

**Prioritize:**
- Hip mobility (90/90, frog, pigeon)
- Pulling strength (rows, pull-ups)
- Core rotation (Russian twists, throws)
- Hamstring flexibility

### For Top Players/Passers

**Prioritize:**
- Hip drive (hip thrusts, kettlebell swings)
- Pushing strength (push-ups, pressing)
- Core stability (planks, Pallof)
- Grip endurance (carries, hangs)

### For Wrestlers/Takedown Focus

**Prioritize:**
- Hip strength (squats, deadlifts)
- Neck strength (bridges, manual resistance)
- Explosive power (cleans, jumps)
- Conditioning (intervals, sprints)

---

## Recovery and Injury Prevention

### Shoulder Health

**Rotator Cuff Work:**
- External rotation: 3x15
- Y-T-W raises: 10 each
- Face pulls: 3x15

### Finger/Hand Care

- Ice after heavy gi sessions
- Tape weak fingers before training
- Grip training for resilience
- Rice bucket for recovery

### General Recovery

- Sleep: 7-9 hours minimum
- Mobility work daily
- Light movement on rest days
- Listen to your body—tap early in training

---

## The Bottom Line

Off-mat training for BJJ should enhance your jiu-jitsu, not compete with it. Focus on grip strength, hip mobility, pulling power, and the conditioning to stay dangerous when others fade.

**Priority areas:**
1. Grip strength (gi pull-ups, farmer's carries)
2. Hip mobility (90/90, frog stretch, CARs)
3. Pulling strength (rows, pull-ups)
4. Core stability and rotation
5. Neck strength (often neglected)

Train smart off the mats, and dominate on them.
