# Exercises After Sitting Too Long: Undo the Damage

We've all been there—hours pass at a desk, and when you finally stand up, your body feels like it aged 20 years. Stiff hips, tight back, rounded shoulders, and glutes that forgot how to work. The good news: targeted exercises can quickly reverse the effects of prolonged sitting.

## What Sitting Does to Your Body

### The Sitting Shutdown

**Hip flexors shorten:**
- Constantly in a shortened position
- Pull on your lower back
- Tight even when you stand up

**Glutes turn off:**
- No demand for activation
- "Gluteal amnesia"
- Other muscles compensate

**Back rounds:**
- Spine flexes forward
- Discs compress unevenly
- Muscles either overstretch or tighten

**Shoulders roll forward:**
- Chest muscles shorten
- Upper back overstretches
- Neck strains forward

**Circulation decreases:**
- Blood pools in legs
- Metabolism slows
- Joints stiffen without movement

### The Longer You Sit, the Worse It Gets

- 30 minutes: Minor stiffness
- 2 hours: Significant tightness, circulation affected
- 4+ hours: Metabolic changes, muscle inhibition
- 8+ hours: Chronic adaptations begin

## The Immediate Undo Routine (5 minutes)

Do this every time you've been sitting for more than an hour.

### 1. Hip Flexor Stretch (45 seconds per side)

**The most important stretch after sitting:**

1. Step one foot forward into a split stance
2. Back knee can lower to ground (half-kneeling) or stay up
3. Tuck your pelvis (posterior tilt)—this is key
4. Shift forward slightly until you feel the stretch
5. Keep torso upright

*Hold: 45 seconds per side*

### 2. Standing Cat-Cow (30 seconds)

1. Stand with hands on thighs
2. Round your back, tuck chin (cat)
3. Arch your back, look up (cow)
4. Move through full range

*Reps: 10 cycles*

### 3. Glute Squeezes (30 seconds)

1. Stand tall
2. Squeeze your glutes as hard as possible
3. Hold for 5 seconds
4. Release and repeat

*Reps: 6 squeezes (wakes up your glutes)*

### 4. Shoulder Rolls and Chest Opener (30 seconds)

1. Roll shoulders backward 10 times
2. Clasp hands behind back
3. Lift and squeeze shoulder blades
4. Hold 10 seconds

### 5. Neck Resets (30 seconds)

1. Chin tucks: 5 reps
2. Neck rotations: 5 each side
3. Ear to shoulder: 10 seconds each side

### 6. Standing Hip Circles (30 seconds)

1. Hands on hips
2. Circle hips 10 times each direction
3. Large, smooth circles

## The Extended Undo Routine (15 minutes)

After a long day of sitting, do this complete routine.

### Phase 1: Release (5 minutes)

**Hip Flexor Release:**
1. Lie face down
2. Place a lacrosse ball just inside your hip bone
3. Let body weight sink into the ball
4. Breathe and relax for 60 seconds per side

**Glute/Piriformis Release:**
1. Sit on a lacrosse ball or foam roller
2. Cross one ankle over opposite knee
3. Roll around the glute
4. 60 seconds per side

**Upper Back Release:**
1. Foam roller under upper back
2. Roll from mid-back to upper back
3. Pause on tight spots
4. 2 minutes total

### Phase 2: Stretch (5 minutes)

**Couch Stretch (Hip Flexor + Quad):**
1. Kneel facing away from a wall or couch
2. Place back knee on ground, shin up against surface
3. Step front foot forward into a lunge
4. Tuck pelvis and stand tall

*Hold: 60-90 seconds per side*

**Pigeon Pose:**
1. One leg bent in front, other straight behind
2. Lower hips toward floor
3. Stretch through front hip glute

*Hold: 60 seconds per side*

**Doorway Chest Stretch:**
1. Forearm on doorframe
2. Step through
3. Feel stretch across chest

*Hold: 30 seconds per side, two positions (elbow high and low)*

**Child's Pose:**
1. Kneel, sit back on heels
2. Reach arms forward
3. Let spine decompress

*Hold: 60 seconds*

### Phase 3: Activate (5 minutes)

**Glute Bridge:**
1. Lie on back, knees bent
2. Squeeze glutes FIRST
3. Lift hips
4. Hold 3 seconds at top
5. Lower with control

*Reps: 15*

**Bird Dog:**
1. On all fours
2. Extend opposite arm and leg
3. Hold 3 seconds
4. Return and switch

*Reps: 10 per side*

**Wall Angels:**
1. Back against wall
2. Arms in W position touching wall
3. Slide arms up toward Y
4. Maintain wall contact

*Reps: 12*

**Bodyweight Squats:**
1. Full range of motion
2. Chest up
3. Activates legs and glutes

*Reps: 15*

## Movement Breaks During the Day

### Every 30-45 Minutes

Set a timer and do one of these:

**Option A: Hip Reset (1 minute)**
- Standing hip flexor stretch: 20 seconds per side
- Glute squeezes: 10 reps
- Hip circles: 10 each direction

**Option B: Upper Body Reset (1 minute)**
- Shoulder rolls: 15 each direction
- Chest stretch: 15 seconds per side
- Chin tucks: 10 reps

**Option C: Full Body Reset (2 minutes)**
- Walk to get water or use restroom
- 5 bodyweight squats
- Hip flexor stretch: 15 seconds per side
- Shoulder circles: 10 each direction

### Every 2 Hours

Take a longer break:
- Walk for 3-5 minutes
- Do the 5-minute immediate undo routine
- Change your position (sit to stand or vice versa)

## Strengthening to Combat Sitting

### Glute Strengthening

Sitting "turns off" your glutes. Wake them up:

**Clamshell:**
1. Side-lying, knees bent
2. Lift top knee, keep feet together
3. Don't roll back

*Reps: 20 per side, 3 sets*

**Single-Leg Glute Bridge:**
1. One leg extended
2. Bridge on the grounded leg
3. Keep hips level

*Reps: 12 per side, 3 sets*

**Hip Thrust:**
1. Upper back on bench/couch
2. Feet flat on floor
3. Drive through heels to lift hips
4. Squeeze glutes hard at top

*Reps: 15, 3 sets*

### Core Strengthening

Sitting weakens your core. Rebuild it:

**Dead Bug:**
1. On back, arms up, knees 90°
2. Lower opposite arm and leg
3. Keep back pressed down

*Reps: 10 per side, 3 sets*

**Plank:**
1. Forearms and toes
2. Body in straight line
3. Squeeze glutes, brace core

*Hold: 30-60 seconds, 3 sets*

### Upper Back Strengthening

Counter the forward hunch:

**Face Pulls (with band):**
1. Band at face height
2. Pull to face, elbows high
3. Squeeze shoulder blades

*Reps: 15-20, 3 sets*

**Y-T-W Raises:**
1. Face down on bench or standing bent over
2. Raise arms in Y, T, W positions
3. Squeeze shoulder blades

*Reps: 10 each position, 2 sets*

## The Post-Work De-Sitting Protocol

Do this complete routine after a full day of sitting:

### Immediately After Work (5 minutes)

1. Walk for 5-10 minutes (even just around your home/office)
2. Standing hip flexor stretch: 45 seconds per side
3. Glute squeezes: 15 reps
4. Shoulder rolls and chest opener

### Before Dinner (10-15 minutes)

**Release:**
- Foam roll: upper back, glutes (5 minutes)

**Stretch:**
- Couch stretch: 90 seconds per side
- Pigeon: 60 seconds per side
- Child's pose: 60 seconds
- Chest stretch: 30 seconds per side

**Activate:**
- Glute bridge: 15 reps
- Bird dog: 10 per side
- Bodyweight squats: 15 reps

### Evening Activity

- Walk after dinner
- Avoid sitting for extended periods again
- If watching TV, do gentle stretches on the floor

## Standing Desk Users

Even standing all day has issues. Alternate:

**Ideal ratio:** 30-45 minutes sitting, 30-45 minutes standing

**Standing too long causes:**
- Foot and leg fatigue
- Lower back compression
- Different (but still problematic) posture issues

**For standing:**
- Use anti-fatigue mat
- Shift weight, move around
- Don't lock knees
- Take sitting breaks

## Permanent Fixes

### Change Your Environment

- Standing desk or desk converter
- Timer/app for movement reminders
- Walking meetings
- Stand while on phone

### Build Better Defaults

- Park farther away
- Take stairs
- Walk to colleagues instead of emailing
- Active commute when possible

### Regular Exercise

- Counteract sitting with activity
- Focus on glute and core strengthening
- Regular stretching routine
- Move more, period

## Signs You're Sitting Too Much

- Stiff getting up from chairs
- Lower back pain that improves with walking
- Tight hip flexors that won't loosen
- Flat or "dead" glutes
- Rounded shoulders that feel normal
- Neck and upper back tension

## Key Takeaways

1. **Hip flexor stretch is #1**—shortened hip flexors cause most sitting problems
2. **Wake up your glutes**—they turn off when you sit; squeeze and activate them
3. **Move every 30-45 minutes**—even brief movement breaks help enormously
4. **The couch stretch is your friend**—best hip flexor stretch for sitters
5. **Strengthen what sitting weakens**—glutes, core, upper back
6. **Standing isn't the complete answer**—alternate sitting and standing
7. **Undo the damage daily**—consistent mobility work beats occasional sessions

Sitting may be unavoidable for many of us, but its negative effects don't have to be. With regular movement breaks and targeted exercises, you can keep your body mobile and functional despite a desk job.
