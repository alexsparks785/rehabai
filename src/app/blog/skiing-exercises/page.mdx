# Skiing Exercises: Prepare Your Legs for the Slopes

Nothing ruins a ski trip faster than burning quads on day one. Skiing demands sustained leg strength, hip stability, and core control that most daily activities don't prepare you for. Pre-season training makes the difference between struggling and shredding.

**Key insight:** Skiing is primarily an eccentric exercise—your muscles work while lengthening to control your descent. Training eccentrically prepares your legs for the unique demands of the sport.

---

## Physical Demands of Skiing

### What Skiing Requires

- **Quad endurance:** Sustained isometric and eccentric contractions
- **Hip stability:** Single-leg balance in dynamic conditions
- **Core strength:** Maintaining posture and absorbing terrain
- **Ankle mobility:** Forward lean in boots
- **Rotational control:** Initiating and controlling turns
- **Cardiovascular fitness:** Altitude and repeated effort

### Common Skiing Injuries

| Injury | Cause | Prevention Focus |
|--------|-------|------------------|
| ACL tears | Twisting falls, poor landing | Hip strength, landing mechanics |
| MCL sprains | Catching an edge, falls | Lateral leg strength |
| Knee pain | Quad fatigue, poor alignment | Eccentric quad training, hip strength |
| Lower back pain | Sustained flexed position | Core endurance, hip mobility |
| Shoulder injuries | Falls, pole plants | Upper body strength |

## Leg Strength Exercises

### Wall Sits

**Why it matters:** Mimics the sustained quad contraction of skiing.

**How to do it:**
1. Back against wall, slide into squat
2. Thighs parallel to ground
3. Hold 30-60 seconds
4. 3-5 sets

**Progression:** Single-leg wall sits, holds longer than 60 seconds

### Goblet Squats

**How to do it:**
1. Hold weight at chest
2. Squat to full depth
3. Keep chest up, knees tracking over toes
4. 3 sets of 12-15

### Bulgarian Split Squats

**Why it matters:** Single-leg strength for uneven terrain and turns.

**How to do it:**
1. Rear foot elevated on bench
2. Lower until front thigh parallel
3. Drive up through front leg
4. 3 sets of 10 each leg

### Eccentric Squats (Slow Lowering)

**Why it matters:** Trains the exact muscle action used in skiing.

**How to do it:**
1. Lower into squat over 4-5 seconds
2. Stand up at normal speed
3. 3 sets of 10

### Lateral Lunges

**How to do it:**
1. Step wide to one side
2. Bend stepping leg, keep other straight
3. Push back to center
4. 3 sets of 10 each side

### Step-Downs (Eccentric Focus)

**How to do it:**
1. Stand on step or box
2. Slowly lower one foot to ground (4 seconds)
3. Touch floor lightly, return
4. 3 sets of 10 each leg

## Hip Stability Exercises

### Clamshells

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on side, knees bent 45°
2. Keep feet together, lift top knee
3. Don't let hips roll back
4. 3 sets of 15-20 each side

### Lateral Band Walks

**How to do it:**
1. Band above knees
2. Athletic stance, slight squat
3. Step sideways, maintaining tension
4. 15-20 steps each direction

### Single-Leg Romanian Deadlift

**How to do it:**
1. Stand on one leg
2. Hinge at hip, reach toward ground
3. Keep back flat
4. 3 sets of 10 each leg

### Side-Lying Hip Abduction

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on side, legs straight
2. Lift top leg toward ceiling
3. Keep toes forward
4. 3 sets of 15 each side

### Fire Hydrants

**How to do it:**
1. On all fours
2. Lift knee out to side
3. Keep back flat
4. 3 sets of 15 each side

## Core Training for Skiing

### Plank

**How to do it:**
1. Forearms on ground
2. Body in straight line
3. Hold 30-60 seconds
4. 3 sets

### Side Plank

**How to do it:**
1. Forearm on ground, feet stacked
2. Lift hips, maintain straight line
3. Hold 30 seconds each side
4. 3 sets

### Dead Bug

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back, arms up, knees at 90°
2. Lower opposite arm and leg
3. Keep lower back pressed down
4. 3 sets of 10 each side

### Russian Twists

**How to do it:**
1. Sit with knees bent, feet elevated
2. Rotate side to side with control
3. 3 sets of 20 total

### Pallof Press

**How to do it:**
1. Stand perpendicular to band
2. Press arms out, resist rotation
3. 3 sets of 10 each side

## Balance and Proprioception

### Single-Leg Balance

**How to do it:**
1. Stand on one leg
2. Hold 30-60 seconds
3. Progress to eyes closed
4. 3 sets each leg

### BOSU Ball Squats

**How to do it:**
1. Stand on BOSU (dome up or down)
2. Perform squats while maintaining balance
3. 3 sets of 12

### Single-Leg Balance with Movement

**How to do it:**
1. Stand on one leg
2. Reach opposite leg in different directions
3. Maintain balance throughout
4. 30 seconds each leg

### Balance Board Training

**How to do it:**
1. Stand on wobble board
2. Maintain balance
3. Progress to squatting movements
4. 3-5 minutes

## Plyometric Exercises

### Box Jumps

**How to do it:**
1. Jump onto box, land softly
2. Stand fully, step down
3. 3 sets of 8

### Lateral Hops

**How to do it:**
1. Stand on one leg
2. Hop sideways to other leg
3. Stick landing
4. 3 sets of 8 each direction

### Squat Jumps

**How to do it:**
1. Squat down
2. Explode up
3. Land softly, absorb into next squat
4. 3 sets of 10

### Skater Bounds

**How to do it:**
1. Bound laterally from one leg to the other
2. Swing opposite arm forward
3. Land and hold briefly
4. 3 sets of 10 each side

---

## Flexibility and Mobility

### Hip Flexor Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Half-kneeling position
2. Tuck pelvis, shift forward
3. Hold 30-45 seconds each side

### Quad Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Standing or lying, pull heel to glute
2. Keep knees together
3. Hold 30 seconds each side

### Calf Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Wall stretch position
2. Straight leg for gastrocnemius
3. Bent knee for soleus
4. Hold 30 seconds each

### 90/90 Hip Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Sit with front leg at 90°, back leg at 90°
2. Lean forward over front shin
3. 45 seconds each side

### Ankle Mobility (Knee Over Toe)

**How to do it:**
1. Kneeling lunge position
2. Push knee forward over toes
3. Keep heel down
4. Hold 30 seconds each side

## Sample Pre-Season Programs

### 8 Weeks Out (Foundation)

**3x per week:**

**Day 1:**
- Goblet squats: 3x12
- Lateral lunges: 3x10 each
- Clamshells: 3x15 each
- Plank: 3x30 sec
- Hip mobility: 10 min

**Day 2:**
- Wall sits: 3x45 sec
- Bulgarian split squat: 3x8 each
- Side-lying hip abduction: 3x15 each
- Dead bug: 3x10 each
- Calf raises: 3x15

**Day 3:**
- Single-leg RDL: 3x10 each
- Step-downs: 3x10 each
- Lateral band walks: 3x15 each way
- Side plank: 3x20 sec each
- Balance work: 10 min

### 4 Weeks Out (Intensity)

**3-4x per week:**

**Day 1 - Strength:**
- Goblet squats: 3x10 (heavier)
- Bulgarian split squat: 3x10 each
- Eccentric squats: 3x10
- Single-leg RDL: 3x10 each
- Core circuit: 15 min

**Day 2 - Power:**
- Box jumps: 3x8
- Lateral hops: 3x8 each
- Skater bounds: 3x10 each
- Squat jumps: 3x10
- Wall sits: 3x60 sec

**Day 3 - Stability:**
- Single-leg balance: 3x45 sec each
- BOSU squats: 3x12
- Clamshells: 3x20 each
- Pallof press: 3x10 each
- Full mobility routine

**Day 4 (optional) - Endurance:**
- Cycling or stair climbing: 30-45 min
- Wall sits: 5x30 sec
- Light stretching

### Week Before Trip

- Reduce intensity, maintain movement
- Focus on mobility and light activation
- No heavy strength work 3-4 days before
- Stay hydrated, sleep well

## On-Mountain Tips

### Daily Warm-Up

Before first run:
- Leg swings: 10 each direction
- Walking lunges: 10 steps
- Squats: 10 reps
- Gentle quad/hip flexor stretch

### During the Day

- Take breaks before exhaustion sets in
- Stay hydrated (altitude increases fluid loss)
- Stretch hip flexors during lunch
- Know when to stop—fatigue causes injuries

### After Skiing

- Light walking to cool down
- Stretch quads, hip flexors, calves
- Foam roll if available
- Hydrate and refuel

## Common Mistakes

### Training Errors

- **Starting too late:** Begin 6-8 weeks before season
- **Neglecting single-leg work:** Skiing is essentially single-leg
- **Skipping eccentric training:** This is what skiing demands
- **Ignoring hip stability:** Weak hips cause knee problems
- **All strength, no endurance:** Skiing requires sustained effort

### On the Mountain

- **Skiing when fatigued:** Most injuries happen late in the day
- **Ignoring altitude effects:** Slower pacing at elevation
- **Skipping warm-up:** Cold muscles perform poorly
- **Pushing beyond ability:** Terrain too advanced for fitness level

---

## The Bottom Line

Pre-season ski conditioning pays dividends from your first run. Strong, fatigue-resistant legs let you ski more runs, handle variable terrain, and significantly reduce injury risk.

**Priority exercises:**
1. Wall sits (ski-specific endurance)
2. Eccentric squats and step-downs
3. Single-leg strength (Bulgarian split squats, single-leg RDL)
4. Hip stability (clamshells, lateral band walks)
5. Balance work

Train your legs to last, and the mountain becomes pure enjoyment.
