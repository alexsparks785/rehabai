# CrossFit Exercises: Foundational Movements for Functional Fitness

CrossFit combines weightlifting, gymnastics, and cardio into constantly varied, high-intensity functional movements. The key to success—and staying injury-free—is mastering the foundational movements before adding speed or load.

**Key insight:** CrossFit's methodology is sound when movements are scaled appropriately. Problems arise when athletes sacrifice form for faster times. Mechanics first, then consistency, then intensity.

---

## The 9 Foundational Movements

CrossFit identifies 9 fundamental movements that form the basis of nearly every workout.

### Squat Movements

#### 1. Air Squat

The foundation of all squatting.

**How to do it:**
1. Feet shoulder-width apart, toes slightly out
2. Initiate by pushing hips back and down
3. Keep weight in heels, knees tracking over toes
4. Descend until hip crease is below knee (below parallel)
5. Chest stays up, back flat
6. Drive through heels to stand

**Common faults:**
- Knees caving in
- Heels rising
- Forward lean/chest dropping
- Not hitting depth

#### 2. Front Squat

Squat with barbell in front rack position.

**How to do it:**
1. Bar rests on shoulders, elbows high
2. Fingertips under bar (or crossed arms)
3. Squat with same mechanics as air squat
4. Elbows stay high throughout
5. Drive up, keeping torso vertical

**Key:** If elbows drop, the bar rolls forward

#### 3. Overhead Squat

The most demanding squat variation.

**How to do it:**
1. Bar overhead, arms locked out
2. Wide grip (snatch grip)
3. Active shoulders—push up into bar
4. Squat to full depth
5. Bar stays over mid-foot throughout

**Tip:** Requires excellent shoulder and thoracic mobility. Practice with PVC pipe first.

### Press Movements

#### 4. Shoulder Press (Strict Press)

Pure upper body strength.

**How to do it:**
1. Bar in front rack position
2. Feet hip-width apart
3. Press bar straight up
4. Move head back slightly as bar passes face
5. Lock out overhead, ears in front of arms
6. Lower with control

**Key:** No leg drive—strict upper body only

#### 5. Push Press

Uses legs to drive heavier loads overhead.

**How to do it:**
1. Start like shoulder press
2. Dip by bending knees slightly (2-3 inches)
3. Drive aggressively through legs
4. Use momentum to press bar overhead
5. Lock out and stabilize

**Tip:** Dip is shallow and quick—not a squat

#### 6. Push Jerk

Maximum load overhead using full leg drive and receiving position.

**How to do it:**
1. Start like push press
2. Dip and drive explosively
3. As bar rises, push yourself under it
4. Receive in partial squat position, arms locked
5. Stand to full extension

**Key:** You drop under the bar—you don't press it the whole way

---

### Deadlift/Hip Hinge Movements

#### 7. Deadlift

Picking weight up from the ground.

**How to do it:**
1. Feet hip-width apart, bar over mid-foot
2. Grip bar just outside legs
3. Shoulders over or slightly in front of bar
4. Flat back, chest up
5. Drive through legs, then extend hips
6. Stand tall, shoulders back
7. Lower by pushing hips back first

**Common faults:**
- Rounding lower back
- Bar drifting away from body
- Hips rising faster than shoulders

#### 8. Sumo Deadlift High Pull

Combines hip extension with pulling motion.

**How to do it:**
1. Wide stance, toes out
2. Grip bar with narrow grip (hands inside legs)
3. Deadlift the bar up explosively
4. As hips extend, pull bar to chin
5. Elbows high and outside
6. Lower in reverse order

**Key:** Power comes from hips, not arms

#### 9. Medicine Ball Clean

Teaches the clean motion with a manageable load.

**How to do it:**
1. Feet shoulder-width, ball between feet
2. Squat down, grip ball on sides
3. Stand explosively, shrugging shoulders
4. Pull elbows under ball quickly
5. Receive ball at chest in partial squat
6. Stand to full extension

## Olympic Lifting Movements

### Clean

Getting the bar from ground to shoulders.

**Power Clean (receive in partial squat):**
1. Start like deadlift
2. First pull: bar to above knees
3. Second pull: explosive hip extension
4. Shrug, pull elbows high and outside
5. Rotate elbows under, catch bar on shoulders
6. Stand up

**Full Clean:** Same, but receive in full squat

### Snatch

Ground to overhead in one movement.

**Power Snatch:**
1. Wide grip on bar
2. Same pull mechanics as clean
3. At full extension, pull under bar
4. Receive overhead with locked arms
5. Stand up

**Key:** Most technically demanding movement. Get coaching.

### Clean and Jerk

Clean followed by jerk overhead.

**How to do it:**
1. Clean bar to shoulders
2. Reset stance
3. Dip, drive, and jerk overhead
4. Receive in split or squat position
5. Stand to full lockout

## Gymnastics Movements

### Pull-Up

Strict pull-up is the foundation.

**How to do it:**
1. Dead hang, hands shoulder-width
2. Pull until chin clears bar
3. Lower with control
4. Full extension at bottom

**Kipping Pull-Up:**
- Uses hip drive to generate momentum
- Only after mastering strict pull-ups
- "Kip" swing creates power to pull

### Push-Up

**How to do it:**
1. Hands under shoulders
2. Body in straight line
3. Lower until chest touches floor
4. Press back up, full arm extension

### Handstand Push-Up

**Progression:**
1. Pike push-ups
2. Box pike push-ups
3. Wall walks
4. Wall handstand holds
5. Handstand push-ups (wall supported)
6. Strict, then kipping

### Ring/Bar Muscle-Up

**Prerequisites:**
- 10+ strict pull-ups
- 20+ strict dips
- False grip strength

**Not a beginner movement.** Build to it over months.

### Box Jump

**How to do it:**
1. Stand facing box
2. Dip and swing arms
3. Jump, land softly on box
4. Stand fully, then step down
5. Step down—don't jump down

**Key:** Height appropriate for your ability. Step down to protect joints.

## Conditioning Movements

### Burpee

**How to do it:**
1. From standing, drop to ground
2. Chest and thighs touch floor
3. Push up, jump feet to hands
4. Jump with clap overhead
5. Full hip extension at top

### Double Unders

Jump rope passing twice per jump.

**Progression:**
1. Master single unders (consistent rhythm)
2. Practice double under attempts
3. Mix singles and doubles
4. String doubles together

### Rowing (Erg)

**Technique:**
1. **Catch:** Arms straight, shins vertical, lean slightly forward
2. **Drive:** Legs first, then lean back, then pull arms
3. **Finish:** Lean back slightly, handle to lower ribs
4. **Recovery:** Arms away, body rocks forward, slide forward

### Wall Ball

**How to do it:**
1. Hold ball at chest, face wall
2. Squat to full depth
3. Stand explosively, throw ball to target
4. Catch ball, immediately squat into next rep

## Scaling and Progression

### Scaling Philosophy

Every movement can be scaled to any fitness level. The stimulus matters more than the specific movement.

### Common Scaling Options

| Movement | Scaling Options |
|----------|----------------|
| Pull-up | Ring rows, banded pull-ups, jumping pull-ups |
| Handstand push-up | Pike push-ups, box pike, DB press |
| Muscle-up | Pull-ups + dips separately, transitions |
| Snatch | Power snatch, hang snatch, muscle snatch, PVC |
| Clean | Hang clean, power clean, muscle clean |
| Pistol (single-leg squat) | Air squats, box pistols, assisted pistols |
| Double unders | Single unders (2:1 or 3:1 ratio) |

### Progression Guidelines

**Month 1-3:**
- Focus on the 9 foundational movements
- Use PVC or empty barbell
- Prioritize mechanics over speed

**Month 3-6:**
- Add light load to barbell movements
- Develop strict gymnastics strength
- Learn Olympic lift positions

**Month 6-12:**
- Progressive loading
- Add kipping movements (after strict strength)
- Introduce more complex workouts

**Year 2+:**
- Sport-specific skill work
- Competition-level movements
- Advanced gymnastics

## Sample Beginner Workouts

### Workout 1: Cindy (Scaled)

As many rounds as possible in 20 minutes:
- 5 ring rows (or 3 pull-ups)
- 10 push-ups (knees if needed)
- 15 air squats

### Workout 2: Helen (Scaled)

3 rounds for time:
- 400m run (or row)
- 15 kettlebell swings (light)
- 12 pull-ups (scaled)

### Workout 3: Strength Focus

**Skill:** Front squat technique (15 min)

**Strength:**
- Front squat: 5x5 (build weight)

**Conditioning:**
- 3 rounds:
  - 10 goblet squats
  - 10 push-ups
  - 200m run

## Injury Prevention

### Red Flags

- Pain during movement (not just fatigue)
- Inability to maintain form
- Coach pressure to go heavier/faster than comfortable
- Ego lifting (competing with others at expense of form)

### Prevention Strategies

- **Scale appropriately:** Check your ego
- **Prioritize mobility:** Warm up thoroughly
- **Learn from qualified coaches:** Not YouTube alone
- **Progress gradually:** Months, not weeks
- **Listen to your body:** Rest when needed

### Common CrossFit Injuries

| Injury | Usual Cause | Prevention |
|--------|-------------|------------|
| Shoulder impingement | Kipping without strict strength | Build strict strength first |
| Lower back strain | Poor deadlift/clean mechanics | Perfect form before adding weight |
| Knee pain | Squat form breakdown | Ensure proper tracking and depth |
| Wrist pain | Front rack position | Mobility work, gradual loading |
| Rhabdomyolysis | Extreme volume, not scaled | Scale appropriately, build gradually |

## The Bottom Line

CrossFit's movements are functional and effective when performed correctly and scaled appropriately. The methodology of constantly varied functional movements at high intensity works—but only if the foundation is solid.

**Non-negotiables:**
1. Learn mechanics before adding intensity
2. Scale every workout to YOUR level
3. Find qualified coaching
4. Progress gradually over months
5. Prioritize strict strength before kipping movements

The best CrossFitters aren't the ones who go the fastest—they're the ones who move the best and stay healthy year after year.
