# Exercises During Pregnancy: Safe Workouts by Trimester

Exercise during pregnancy benefits both you and your baby—when done safely. This guide covers appropriate exercises for each trimester, important modifications, warning signs, and how to maintain fitness throughout your pregnancy.

## Benefits of Prenatal Exercise

**For you:**
- Reduced back pain
- Better sleep
- Improved mood and energy
- Easier labor and delivery
- Faster postpartum recovery
- Reduced risk of gestational diabetes
- Less excessive weight gain
- Decreased constipation and bloating

**For baby:**
- Healthy birth weight
- Better stress tolerance during delivery
- Potentially improved brain development

## Before You Start

### Get Clearance

**Talk to your healthcare provider, especially if you have:**
- Heart or lung conditions
- Preeclampsia or high blood pressure
- Placenta previa
- Cervical insufficiency
- Multiple pregnancy (twins+) at risk for preterm labor
- Persistent bleeding
- Severe anemia

### General Guidelines

**Recommended:**
- 150 minutes of moderate exercise per week
- Can break into 30 minutes, 5 days
- Or shorter sessions more frequently

**Intensity:**
- "Talk test": You should be able to carry on a conversation
- RPE (Rate of Perceived Exertion): 12-14 on 20-point scale
- Not breathless or exhausted

## First Trimester (Weeks 1-12)

### What to Expect

**Physical changes:**
- Fatigue (often intense)
- Nausea
- Breast tenderness
- Minimal visible changes

**Exercise implications:**
- Energy may be low
- Listen to your body
- Morning sickness may affect when you can exercise
- Still generally safe to do most exercises

### Best Exercises

**Cardiovascular:**
- Walking (excellent throughout pregnancy)
- Swimming (great for nausea and fatigue)
- Stationary cycling
- Elliptical
- Low-impact aerobics

**Strength Training:**
- Continue your regular routine with modifications
- Focus on form over weight
- All major muscle groups

**Flexibility:**
- Prenatal yoga
- Gentle stretching
- Avoid deep twisting

### First Trimester Workout

**Warm-Up (5 min):**
- March in place
- Arm circles
- Hip circles
- Gentle stretches

**Cardio (15-20 min):**
- Brisk walking, swimming, or cycling
- Moderate intensity

**Strength (15 min):**
- Squats: 2 × 12
- Wall push-ups: 2 × 12
- Seated rows (band): 2 × 12
- Side-lying leg lifts: 2 × 12 each
- Bird dog: 2 × 8 each side

**Cool-Down (5 min):**
- Gentle stretching
- Deep breathing

### Things to Modify/Avoid

**Avoid:**
- Contact sports
- Activities with fall risk
- Hot yoga or exercising in heat
- Scuba diving
- High altitude (>6000 ft) if not acclimated

**Modify:**
- High-intensity workouts → moderate intensity
- Heavy lifting → lighter weights, more reps

## Second Trimester (Weeks 13-26)

### What to Expect

**Physical changes:**
- Belly growing (balance shifts)
- More energy (often)
- Round ligament pain possible
- Diastasis recti may begin

**Exercise implications:**
- Often the best trimester for exercise
- May need to modify as belly grows
- Avoid lying flat on back after ~20 weeks

### Best Exercises

**Cardiovascular:**
- Walking
- Swimming (even better now—supports belly)
- Stationary cycling (upright preferred)
- Prenatal dance classes

**Strength Training:**
- Modified positions (incline vs. flat)
- Side-lying vs. supine
- Focus on postural muscles

**Core and Pelvic Floor:**
- Kegels
- Diaphragmatic breathing
- Modified core work (no crunches)

### Second Trimester Workout

**Warm-Up (5 min):**
- Walking in place
- Arm swings
- Hip circles
- Cat-cow stretches

**Strength Circuit (20 min):**

**Lower Body:**
- Squats: 3 × 12 (wider stance as needed)
- Lateral band walks: 3 × 10 each direction
- Step-ups: 3 × 10 each leg
- Glute bridges: 3 × 12

**Upper Body:**
- Incline push-ups: 3 × 10
- Seated rows: 3 × 12
- Shoulder press (seated): 3 × 10
- Bicep curls: 2 × 12

**Core/Posture:**
- Bird dog: 3 × 8 each side
- Pallof press: 3 × 10 each side
- Side-lying leg lifts: 2 × 12 each
- Kegels: 10 reps, hold 5 seconds each

**Cardio (15 min):**
- Walking or swimming

**Cool-Down (5 min):**
- Side-lying stretches
- Cat-cow
- Hip flexor stretch (modified)
- Deep breathing

### Important Modifications

**No longer safe:**
- Lying flat on back (after ~20 weeks)
- Traditional crunches or sit-ups
- Heavy lifting
- Jumping/high impact (for most)

**Modify to:**
- Incline positions instead of flat
- Side-lying exercises
- Standing or seated positions
- Lower impact options

## Third Trimester (Weeks 27-40)

### What to Expect

**Physical changes:**
- Large belly affects balance
- Breathlessness easier
- Joint laxity (relaxin hormone)
- Fatigue returns
- Braxton Hicks contractions

**Exercise implications:**
- Shorter, gentler sessions
- More modifications needed
- Focus on preparation for labor
- Listen closely to your body

### Best Exercises

**Cardiovascular:**
- Walking (still excellent)
- Swimming (feels amazing)
- Prenatal water aerobics
- Stationary cycling (if comfortable)

**Strength/Function:**
- Squats (prep for labor positions)
- Pelvic floor exercises
- Upper back strengthening
- Hip mobility

**Preparation:**
- Birth position practice
- Relaxation techniques
- Breathing exercises

### Third Trimester Workout

**Warm-Up (5 min):**
- Slow walking
- Gentle arm movements
- Hip circles
- Ankle circles

**Movement (15-20 min):**

**Standing:**
- Supported squats (holding onto something): 2 × 10
- Calf raises: 2 × 15
- Side leg lifts (holding support): 2 × 10 each

**Seated:**
- Seated arm exercises with light weights: 2 × 12
- Seated rows: 2 × 12
- Seated cat-cow

**Side-Lying:**
- Side-lying leg lifts: 2 × 10 each
- Clamshells: 2 × 10 each
- Side-lying hip circles

**On Hands and Knees:**
- Cat-cow: 10 reps
- Bird dog: 2 × 6 each (shortened range)
- Hip circles: 10 each direction

**Pelvic Floor:**
- Kegels: 10 × 5-second holds
- Quick flicks: 10 reps
- Perineal massage prep (separate practice)

**Cool-Down (10 min):**
- Child's pose (modified, knees wide)
- Side-lying stretches
- Deep breathing/relaxation

### Labor Preparation Exercises

**Squats:**
- Builds leg strength for pushing
- Opens pelvis
- Practice supported and unsupported

**Pelvic Tilts:**
- On hands and knees
- Helps baby position
- Relieves back pain

**Hip Openers:**
- Modified pigeon pose
- Butterfly stretch
- Deep squat holds (supported)

**Breathing Practice:**
- Diaphragmatic breathing
- Relaxation breathing for contractions
- Different patterns for different labor stages

## Pelvic Floor Exercises

**Critical throughout pregnancy:**

### Kegel Exercises

**How to do them:**
- Imagine stopping urine flow (don't actually do this regularly)
- Lift and squeeze pelvic floor muscles
- Hold 5-10 seconds
- Fully release
- 10-15 reps, 3 times daily

**Progression:**
- First trimester: 5-second holds
- Second trimester: 8-second holds
- Third trimester: 10-second holds, plus quick flicks

### Why They Matter

- Supports growing baby weight
- Prevents/reduces incontinence
- Prepares for pushing during labor
- Speeds postpartum recovery

## Exercise to Avoid

**Throughout pregnancy:**
- Contact sports (soccer, basketball, hockey)
- Activities with fall risk (skiing, horseback riding, gymnastics)
- Scuba diving
- Hot yoga/exercising in excessive heat
- Exercises lying flat on back (after 20 weeks)
- Traditional ab exercises (crunches, sit-ups, planks late pregnancy)
- Heavy lifting
- Bouncy movements/high impact (for most)
- Breath holding (Valsalva maneuver)

**Stop exercising and call your provider if:**
- Vaginal bleeding
- Dizziness or feeling faint
- Shortness of breath before exercise
- Chest pain
- Headache
- Muscle weakness
- Calf pain or swelling
- Regular, painful contractions
- Fluid leaking from vagina

## Diastasis Recti Prevention

**What it is:** Separation of abdominal muscles

### Exercises to Avoid
- Crunches and sit-ups
- Traditional planks
- Exercises that cause belly "coning" or "doming"
- Twisting movements with load

### Safe Core Exercises
- Diaphragmatic breathing
- Bird dog (modified)
- Pallof press
- Side-lying core work
- Standing core exercises
- Kegels (connected to core)

### Technique Tips
- Exhale on exertion
- Engage core gently before movement
- Stop if you see doming along midline

## Sample Weekly Schedule

### First Trimester
- Mon: Strength + 15 min walk
- Tue: Swimming 30 min
- Wed: Rest or prenatal yoga
- Thu: Strength + 15 min walk
- Fri: Walking 30 min
- Sat: Prenatal yoga or light activity
- Sun: Rest

### Second Trimester
- Mon: Full body strength 25 min
- Tue: Swimming or walking 30 min
- Wed: Prenatal yoga
- Thu: Strength circuit 20 min
- Fri: Walking or swimming 30 min
- Sat: Light activity/stretching
- Sun: Rest

### Third Trimester
- Mon: Gentle strength 15-20 min
- Tue: Swimming or water aerobics
- Wed: Prenatal yoga (gentle)
- Thu: Walking 20-30 min + stretching
- Fri: Gentle movement + pelvic floor
- Sat: Light walking
- Sun: Rest and relaxation practice

## Postpartum Preview

**After baby arrives:**
- Wait for provider clearance (usually 6 weeks, longer for C-section)
- Start with walking and pelvic floor exercises
- Gradually rebuild core strength
- Progress slowly
- Check for diastasis recti before returning to intense exercise

## Key Takeaways

**Safe throughout pregnancy:**
- Walking
- Swimming
- Prenatal yoga
- Stationary cycling
- Low-impact aerobics
- Modified strength training

**Modify as you progress:**
- Position changes (no flat on back)
- Lower intensity
- Wider stances
- More support
- Shorter sessions

**Most important exercises:**
- Walking (easiest, safest, always appropriate)
- Squats (leg strength, labor prep)
- Kegels (pelvic floor health)
- Upper back exercises (posture support)
- Hip mobility (birth preparation)

**Listen to your body:**
- Fatigue means rest
- Pain means stop
- Talk test for intensity
- When in doubt, ask your provider

Exercise during pregnancy is a gift to yourself and your baby. Stay active, stay safe, and enjoy this special time.
