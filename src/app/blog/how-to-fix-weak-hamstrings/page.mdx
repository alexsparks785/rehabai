# How to Fix Weak Hamstrings: Exercises for Stronger Legs

Weak hamstrings are one of the most common muscle imbalances—and one of the most dangerous. They're a leading cause of hamstring strains, contribute to knee problems, and limit athletic performance. If your quads dominate your leg training, it's time to show your hamstrings some attention.

## Why Hamstrings Matter

Your hamstrings (biceps femoris, semimembranosus, semitendinosus) do critical jobs:

- **Knee flexion:** Bending your knee
- **Hip extension:** Driving your body forward (with glutes)
- **Deceleration:** Slowing you down during running and cutting
- **Knee stability:** Protecting your ACL

When hamstrings are weak relative to your quads, injury risk skyrockets—especially during sprinting or sudden movements.

## Signs of Weak Hamstrings

- **Quad dominance:** Quads burn during squats while hamstrings feel nothing
- **Hamstring strains:** History of pulls or strains
- **Knee pain:** Weak hamstrings can't counterbalance quad pull on the kneecap
- **Lower back pain:** Hamstrings help stabilize the pelvis
- **Can't touch toes comfortably:** May be weak or tight (or both)
- **Poor sprinting power:** Hamstrings drive the running stride
- **Imbalanced leg development:** Quads visibly larger than hamstrings

## Self-Assessment

### Quad-to-Hamstring Strength Ratio
Ideally, hamstring strength should be 60-80% of quad strength. Most people with desk jobs are far below this.

### Single-Leg RDL Test
1. Stand on one leg
2. Hinge forward, reaching opposite hand toward floor
3. Keep back flat, standing leg slightly bent

**If you:**
- Lose balance easily
- Feel minimal hamstring engagement
- Can't control the movement

...your hamstrings need work.

### Nordic Curl Test
1. Kneel on a cushion
2. Anchor your feet under something sturdy
3. Slowly lower yourself toward the floor

**If you:**
- Can't control the descent at all
- Fall immediately

...your hamstrings are significantly weak.

## Why Are Your Hamstrings Weak?

### Sitting All Day
Sitting keeps hamstrings in a lengthened, inactive position. Your brain "forgets" how to use them.

### Quad-Dominant Training
Most gym exercises (leg press, leg extension, squats for many people) emphasize quads over hamstrings.

### Lack of Posterior Chain Work
If you're not doing deadlifts, RDLs, and hamstring curls, you're probably neglecting hamstrings.

### Running Without Strengthening
Running alone doesn't build strong hamstrings—in fact, weak hamstrings are highly injury-prone when running.

## Hamstring Strengthening Exercises

### Foundational Exercises

#### 1. Romanian Deadlift (RDL)
**The gold standard for hamstring strength.**

**How to do it:**
1. Hold dumbbells or barbell in front of thighs
2. Push your hips BACK (not down)
3. Keep slight bend in knees (fixed throughout)
4. Lower weight along your legs until you feel hamstring stretch
5. Drive hips forward to stand
6. 3 sets of 10-12 reps

**Cues:**
- "Push your butt to the wall behind you"
- "Slide the weight down your legs"
- Keep back flat throughout

#### 2. Glute-Ham Raise (or Nordic Curl)
**The best eccentric hamstring exercise.**

**Nordic Curl (more accessible):**
1. Kneel with feet anchored (under couch, with partner holding)
2. Cross arms on chest
3. Slowly lower your body toward the floor
4. Use hands to catch yourself if needed
5. Push back up to start
6. 3 sets of 5-10 reps

**Key:** The lowering phase (eccentric) is what builds hamstring strength and prevents strains.

#### 3. Lying Hamstring Curl
**How to do it:**
1. Machine curl or band around ankles
2. Curl heels toward your butt
3. Squeeze at the top
4. Lower slowly
5. 3 sets of 12-15 reps

**Can also do seated or standing variations.**

#### 4. Good Mornings
**How to do it:**
1. Bar on upper back (like a squat)
2. Push hips back, lowering torso
3. Keep back flat, slight knee bend
4. Feel stretch in hamstrings
5. Drive hips forward to stand
6. 3 sets of 10-12 reps

**Start light—this exercise humbles people.**

#### 5. Hip Thrust (Hamstring Emphasis)
**How to do it:**
1. Back against bench, feet slightly further out than normal
2. Drive through heels to lift hips
3. The further your feet, the more hamstring vs. glute
4. 3 sets of 12-15 reps

### Intermediate/Advanced Exercises

#### 6. Single-Leg RDL
**How to do it:**
1. Stand on one leg
2. Hinge forward, sending free leg behind
3. Keep hips square
4. Feel stretch in standing leg's hamstring
5. Return to standing
6. 3 sets of 8-10 per leg

**Excellent for balance and unilateral strength.**

#### 7. Slider Leg Curl
**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back, heels on sliders (or towels on smooth floor)
2. Lift hips off floor
3. Curl heels toward your butt while maintaining hip height
4. Extend legs back out with control
5. 3 sets of 10-12 reps

**Progress to single-leg version.**

#### 8. Stiff-Leg Deadlift
**How to do it:**
1. Similar to RDL but with completely straight legs
2. Greater hamstring stretch
3. Lighter weight than regular RDL
4. 3 sets of 10-12 reps

#### 9. Cable Pull-Throughs
**How to do it:**
1. Face away from cable, rope between legs
2. Hinge at hips, letting cable pull you back
3. Drive hips forward to stand
4. Squeeze glutes and hamstrings at top
5. 3 sets of 12-15 reps

**Great for learning the hip hinge pattern.**

#### 10. Deficit RDL
**How to do it:**
1. Stand on a plate or small platform
2. Perform RDL with increased range of motion
3. Greater stretch at bottom
4. 3 sets of 8-10 reps

### Eccentric-Focused Exercises

Eccentric strength (lengthening under load) is critical for preventing hamstring strains.

#### 11. Eccentric-Only Leg Curl
**How to do it:**
1. Curl weight up with both legs
2. Lower slowly with ONE leg (5 seconds down)
3. 3 sets of 6-8 per leg

#### 12. Long-Lever Nordic Curl
**How to do it:**
1. Nordic curl but with arms extended overhead
2. Longer lever = harder
3. Progress to this from regular Nordics

## Programming for Hamstring Strength

### Beginner (Weeks 1-4)
**2x per week:**
- RDL: 3x10
- Lying leg curl: 3x12
- Glute bridge with hamstring focus: 3x15

### Intermediate (Weeks 5-8)
**2x per week:**
- RDL: 3x8-10 (heavier)
- Nordic curls: 3x5-8
- Single-leg RDL: 3x8 per leg
- Slider leg curl: 3x10

### Advanced (Weeks 9+)
**2-3x per week:**
- Deficit RDL: 4x8
- Nordic curls: 4x8-10
- Single-leg leg curl (eccentric focus): 3x8 per leg
- Good mornings: 3x10
- Single-leg RDL: 3x8 per leg

## Integrating with Other Training

### With Quad Training
Balance your leg training. For every quad-dominant exercise (squat, leg press, leg extension), include a hamstring exercise.

### With Glute Training
Hamstrings and glutes work together. Hip thrusts, RDLs, and deadlifts train both.

### With Running/Sprinting
If you run, hamstring strength is non-negotiable. Include Nordic curls and RDLs to prevent strains.

## Don't Forget Flexibility

Strong hamstrings should also be mobile. Include:

### Hamstring Stretch (Lying)
1. Lie on back
2. Lift one leg straight up
3. Use hands or strap to pull gently
4. Hold 30-45 seconds per side

### Standing Forward Fold
1. Stand with feet hip-width apart
2. Fold forward from hips
3. Let head hang
4. Hold 30-60 seconds

### 90-90 Stretch
Targets hamstrings and hip rotation together.

## Common Mistakes

**Starting too heavy:** RDLs and good mornings require proper form before loading.

**Rounding the back:** Maintain a flat back during hip hinges.

**Not going through full range:** Partial reps limit hamstring development.

**Neglecting eccentric work:** Nordics and slow negatives are essential for injury prevention.

**Training hamstrings only after quads:** Fatigued hamstrings don't get trained properly.

**Ignoring single-leg work:** Imbalances between legs are common; single-leg exercises reveal and fix them.

## When to Seek Help

**See a professional if:**
- You've had a hamstring strain and it's not healing
- Pain during hamstring exercises
- Significant strength difference between legs
- Persistent tightness despite stretching
- Pain that radiates or includes numbness

## Timeline for Results

- **Week 1-2:** Learning movements, establishing baseline
- **Week 3-4:** Starting to feel hamstrings engage properly
- **Week 6-8:** Noticeable strength gains
- **3+ months:** Significant development, better quad-hamstring balance

## The Bottom Line

Weak hamstrings are common, dangerous, and fixable. The keys are:

1. **Hip hinge movements** (RDL, good morning) for length and strength
2. **Knee flexion exercises** (leg curls) for isolation
3. **Eccentric work** (Nordic curls) for injury prevention
4. **Consistency** (2-3x per week, every week)

If you only do one hamstring exercise, make it the Nordic curl—it's the single most effective exercise for preventing hamstring strains. Add RDLs for overall posterior chain development.

Your hamstrings have been neglected long enough. Give them the attention they deserve, and they'll protect your knees, power your stride, and balance out your physique.

Strong hamstrings aren't optional—they're essential.
