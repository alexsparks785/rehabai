# Exercises for Tech Neck: Fix Phone and Computer Posture

Tech neck—the forward head posture from constantly looking down at phones and screens—has become epidemic. For every inch your head moves forward, it adds 10 pounds of effective weight your neck must support. Here's how to reverse it.

## The Tech Neck Problem

### What's Happening

**The posture:**
- Head juts forward
- Chin pokes out
- Upper back rounds
- Shoulders roll forward
- Chest muscles shorten

**The load:**
- Neutral head: ~10-12 lbs on spine
- 15° tilt: ~27 lbs
- 30° tilt: ~40 lbs
- 45° tilt: ~49 lbs
- 60° tilt (common phone angle): ~60 lbs

### Symptoms

- Neck pain and stiffness
- Headaches (tension type)
- Upper back pain
- Shoulder tightness
- Reduced neck mobility
- Muscle fatigue

## The Foundation Exercise: Chin Tucks

### The Single Most Important Exercise

**How to do it:**
- Sit or stand tall
- Pull chin straight back (make a double chin)
- Keep eyes level—don't look up or down
- Hold 5 seconds
- Relax
- Repeat 10 times

**Why it works:**
- Retrains proper head position
- Strengthens deep neck flexors
- Stretches tight posterior neck muscles
- Can do anywhere, anytime

**Frequency:**
- Every hour while at computer
- After phone use
- 10 reps, 5-6 times daily

### Chin Tuck Progressions

**Against Wall**
- Back and head against wall
- Chin tuck, pressing head into wall
- Hold 10 seconds
- 10 reps

**With Resistance**
- Fingers on chin
- Gentle resistance as you tuck
- Hold 5 seconds
- 10 reps

**Lying Down**
- Lie on back, no pillow
- Chin tuck, pressing head into floor
- Hold 10 seconds
- 10 reps

## Stretching Protocol

### Neck Stretches

**Upper Trapezius Stretch**
- Sit tall, hold chair seat with right hand
- Tilt left ear toward left shoulder
- Optional: Gentle pressure with left hand
- Hold 30-45 seconds each side

**Levator Scapulae Stretch**
- Turn head 45° to one side
- Look down toward armpit
- Gentle pressure on back of head
- Hold 30-45 seconds each side

**Suboccipital Stretch**
- Tuck chin
- Nod forward (small movement)
- Feel stretch at skull base
- Hold 30 seconds

**SCM Stretch (Sternocleidomastoid)**
- Turn head to one side
- Look up slightly
- Feel stretch on front/side of neck
- Hold 30 seconds each side

### Chest and Shoulder Stretches

**Doorway Pec Stretch**
- Forearm on door frame
- Step through, turn body away
- 30-45 seconds each side
- Vary arm height for different fibers

**Corner Stretch**
- Face corner, forearms on walls
- Lean forward
- Feel chest open
- 30-45 seconds

**Chest Opener**
- Clasp hands behind back
- Squeeze shoulder blades
- Lift hands away from body
- Hold 30 seconds

## Strengthening Exercises

### Deep Neck Flexors

**Chin Tuck and Hold**
- Tuck chin
- Hold 10-15 seconds
- Rest
- 10 reps

**Chin Tuck with Lift**
- Lie on back
- Chin tuck
- Lift head 1 inch off floor
- Hold 5 seconds
- 10 reps

### Upper Back Strengthening

**Prone Y-T-W**
- Lie face down (on floor or bed edge)
- Y: Lift arms at angle, thumbs up
- T: Lift arms to sides
- W: Elbows bent, squeeze back
- 8-10 reps each position

**Band Pull-Aparts**
- Band at chest height
- Pull apart, squeeze shoulder blades
- 15-20 reps
- Do daily

**Face Pulls**
- Band at face height
- Pull to face, elbows high
- Squeeze shoulder blades
- 15 reps

**Rows**
- Any row variation
- Focus on squeezing shoulder blades
- 12-15 reps

**Wall Angels**
- Back against wall
- Arms in W position touching wall
- Slide to Y and back
- Everything stays in contact
- 10 slow reps

### Shoulder External Rotation

**Band External Rotation**
- Elbow at side, bent 90°
- Rotate forearm outward against band
- 15 reps each arm

## Daily Protocol

### Morning (3 minutes)

- Chin tucks: 10 reps
- Neck stretches: 20 sec each direction
- Shoulder rolls: 10 each direction
- Chest opener: 20 seconds

### Every Hour at Screen

- Chin tucks: 5 reps
- Shoulder blade squeezes: 5 reps
- Look away from screen
- Quick posture reset

### End of Day (8-10 minutes)

**Stretching (5 min):**
- Upper trap stretch: 45 sec each
- Levator scap stretch: 45 sec each
- Chest stretch: 45 sec each
- Suboccipital release: 45 sec

**Strengthening (5 min):**
- Chin tucks with hold: 10 × 10 sec
- Prone Y-T-W: 8 each position
- Band pull-aparts: 15 reps
- Wall angels: 8 reps

### Weekly (2-3 times)

**Full strengthening routine:**
- Deep neck flexor exercises
- All upper back exercises
- External rotation
- Core work (supports posture)

## Device Modifications

### Phone Use

**Raise the phone:**
- Hold at eye level
- Yes, it feels weird at first
- Or prop on something when sitting

**Use voice:**
- Voice-to-text
- Voice assistants
- Reduces looking down

**Limit duration:**
- Set timers
- Breaks every 15-20 minutes
- Reduce overall phone time

### Computer Setup

**Monitor position:**
- Top of screen at eye level
- 20-26 inches from face
- Directly in front (not to side)

**Laptop modification:**
- External keyboard
- Laptop riser or stand
- Or use external monitor

**Chair and desk:**
- Supportive chair back
- Sit back, not perched forward
- Elbows at 90° for keyboard

## Progress Timeline

**Week 1:**
- Increased awareness
- May feel worse initially (using new muscles)
- Building habit

**Week 2-4:**
- Reduced end-of-day pain
- Chin tucks feeling more natural
- Improved awareness of posture breaks

**Month 2-3:**
- Visible posture improvement
- Less daily discomfort
- Better neck mobility

**Month 3+:**
- New baseline posture
- Maintenance mode
- Occasional tune-ups needed

## Quick Posture Checks

### The Wall Test

- Stand with back to wall
- Heels, butt, shoulder blades, head touch wall
- If head doesn't easily touch = forward head posture
- Practice chin tucks against wall

### Photo Test

- Side profile photo
- Ear should align over shoulder
- Shoulder over hip
- Check monthly for progress

## Key Takeaways

**The essential three:**
1. Chin tucks (10 reps, 5+ times daily)
2. Chest stretching (multiple times daily)
3. Upper back strengthening (daily to 3x weekly)

**Posture habits:**
- Hourly movement breaks
- Raise devices to eye level
- Proper workstation setup
- Posture awareness throughout day

**Most important:**
- Chin tucks are foundational—do them constantly
- Strengthen back muscles (they're weak)
- Stretch chest and front neck (they're tight)
- Change your device habits, not just exercise

Tech neck developed from thousands of hours of poor positioning. It won't reverse overnight, but consistent work over 2-3 months can make dramatic improvements. Start with chin tucks today—they cost nothing and work anywhere.
