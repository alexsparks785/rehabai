# Exercises for QL Pain: Release Your Quadratus Lumborum

The quadratus lumborum (QL) is a deep muscle on each side of your lower back that often causes stubborn, one-sided pain. When tight or overworked, it creates that deep ache that's hard to stretch. Here's how to release and strengthen this troublesome muscle.

## Understanding the QL

### What It Does

**Location:** Deep in lower back, connects pelvis to lower ribs and spine

**Functions:**
- Side bending
- Hiking hip up
- Stabilizing pelvis during walking
- Extending lower back
- Assists breathing

### Why It Gets Tight

**Common causes:**
- Sitting with poor posture
- Leg length discrepancy
- Hip weakness (QL compensates)
- Carrying bags on one side
- Sleeping in twisted position
- Repetitive bending

### Symptoms of QL Problem

- Deep, aching lower back pain (often one-sided)
- Pain when bending to the opposite side
- Pain when coughing or sneezing
- Stiffness getting out of chair
- Pain referring to hip or groin
- Difficulty standing straight

## QL Release Techniques

### Ball Release

**Lying Ball Release:**
- Lie on back
- Place tennis or lacrosse ball under lower back, outside of spine
- Let body weight sink onto ball
- Roll slowly to find tender spots
- Hold on tight spots 30-60 seconds
- 2-3 minutes each side

**Side-Lying Ball Release:**
- Lie on side
- Ball under QL area (between pelvis and ribs)
- Roll gently, find trigger points
- Hold 30-60 seconds on each spot

### Foam Roller Release

- Side-lying on foam roller
- Roller under lower back/side
- Roll slowly from pelvis to ribs
- 60-90 seconds each side

## QL Stretches

### Best Stretches for QL

**Side-Lying QL Stretch**
- Lie on side (tight QL side up)
- Bottom leg bent for stability
- Top leg extended straight back
- Reach top arm overhead
- Feel stretch along top side
- Hold 45-60 seconds

**Standing Side Bend**
- Stand feet hip-width
- Raise one arm overhead
- Bend to opposite side
- Reach arm further
- Hold 30-45 seconds each side

**Seated QL Stretch**
- Sit on floor, legs wide
- Reach one arm overhead
- Side bend toward opposite leg
- Hold 45 seconds each side

**Child's Pose Variation**
- Start in child's pose
- Walk hands to one side
- Feel stretch along opposite side
- Hold 45 seconds each side

**90/90 QL Stretch**
- Sit with legs in 90/90 position
- Rotate torso toward front leg
- Side bend toward front leg
- Hold 30-45 seconds each side

### Thread the Needle with Side Emphasis

- On hands and knees
- Thread arm under body
- As you reach, sink hip away from reaching arm
- Feel QL stretch
- Hold 30 seconds each side

## Strengthening Exercises

### Why Strengthening Matters

Weak QL = overworked QL = tight QL

Strengthen to reduce compensatory tightness

### QL Strengthening

**Side Plank**
- Lie on side, support on forearm
- Lift hips off ground
- Keep body straight
- Hold 20-30 seconds each side
- Progress to longer holds

**Side Plank with Hip Dip**
- In side plank position
- Lower hip toward floor
- Lift back up
- 10 reps each side

**Suitcase Carry**
- Hold weight in one hand
- Walk without leaning
- QL works to keep you upright
- 30-40 steps each side

**Side Bend with Weight**
- Stand, weight in one hand
- Bend away from weight
- Return to standing
- 12 reps each side

### Supporting Muscles

**Hip Strengthening:**
- Clamshells: 15 each side
- Side-lying leg lifts: 15 each side
- Monster walks: 10 steps each direction

**Core Stability:**
- Dead bug: 10 each side
- Bird dog: 10 each side
- Pallof press: 10 each side

## Daily Routine

### Morning (5 minutes)

- Cat-cow: 8 reps
- Side-lying stretch: 30 sec each
- Standing side bends: 5 each side
- Hip circles: 10 each direction

### Throughout Day

- Change positions frequently
- Don't sit for more than 30-60 minutes
- Quick side bends when standing

### Evening (10 minutes)

**Release:**
- Ball release: 2 min each side

**Stretch:**
- Side-lying QL stretch: 45 sec each
- Child's pose with side reach: 30 sec each
- Seated QL stretch: 30 sec each

**Strengthen (3x/week):**
- Side plank: 20 sec each
- Suitcase carry: 30 steps each
- Clamshells: 12 each

## Addressing Root Causes

### Posture Fixes

**Sitting:**
- Sit evenly on both sit bones
- Don't lean to one side
- Support lower back
- Take breaks

**Standing:**
- Weight even on both feet
- Don't shift to one hip
- Avoid locking knees

### Sleeping Position

- Pillow between knees (side sleeper)
- Avoid twisted positions
- Supportive mattress

### Movement Patterns

- Carry bags on both sides (alternate)
- Even weight distribution
- Lift with proper form

## When to Seek Help

**See professional if:**
- Pain persists after 2-3 weeks of self-treatment
- Numbness or tingling
- Pain radiating down leg
- Weakness
- Pain following injury

## Key Takeaways

**The QL release formula:**
- Release: Ball work on tight spots
- Stretch: Side-lying and side bends
- Strengthen: Side planks, carries, hip work

**Most important exercises:**
- Ball release (finding and releasing trigger points)
- Side-lying QL stretch
- Side plank (builds QL endurance)
- Hip strengthening (reduces QL compensation)

**Daily priorities:**
- Don't sit too long
- Release tight spots
- Stretch both sides
- Strengthen supporting muscles

Your QL is working overtime when other muscles aren't doing their job. Address the tightness and weakness together for lasting relief.
