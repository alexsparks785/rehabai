# Workout for Golfers: Build Power, Flexibility, and Injury Resistance

Golf demands more athleticism than most people realize. A powerful, consistent swing requires rotational mobility, core stability, hip flexibility, and full-body coordination. The right training program improves your game and keeps you playing pain-free for years.

## What Golf Demands From Your Body

### Rotational Power
The golf swing is a rotational movement—generating force from the ground up through hips, core, and arms.

### Flexibility
Limited mobility in hips, thoracic spine, or shoulders restricts your backswing and follow-through.

### Core Stability
Your core transfers power from lower body to upper body and protects your spine during rotation.

### Single-Leg Balance
Weight shifts throughout the swing require stability and control on each leg.

### Endurance
Walking 18 holes (4-5 miles) while maintaining focus and swing mechanics.

## Common Golf Injuries

Training properly helps prevent:

- **Low back pain** (most common)
- **Golfer's elbow** (inside of elbow)
- **Shoulder injuries** (rotator cuff)
- **Hip pain** (from repetitive rotation)
- **Wrist injuries** (from impact)

## Key Exercises for Golfers

### Rotational Mobility

**Thoracic Spine Rotation**
1. On hands and knees
2. Place one hand behind head
3. Rotate elbow toward ceiling
4. Return and repeat
5. 10 each side

*Why it helps:* More thoracic rotation means less stress on your lower back during the swing.

**Open Books**
1. Lie on side, knees bent
2. Top arm extended forward
3. Rotate top arm open, looking back
4. Return slowly
5. 10 each side

**Seated Trunk Rotation**
1. Sit on bench, club across shoulders
2. Rotate fully to each side
3. Keep hips still
4. 10 each direction

### Hip Mobility

**90/90 Hip Stretch**
1. Front leg bent 90° in front
2. Back leg bent 90° to side
3. Sit tall, shift weight
4. 30 seconds, then switch

*Why it helps:* Golf requires both hip internal and external rotation.

**Hip Flexor Stretch with Rotation**
1. Half-kneeling position
2. Tuck tailbone under
3. Rotate toward front leg
4. 30 seconds each side

**Deep Squat Hold**
1. Squat as deep as comfortable
2. Use club for balance if needed
3. Shift weight side to side
4. Hold 30-60 seconds

### Core Stability

**Pallof Press**
1. Cable or band at chest height
2. Stand sideways to anchor
3. Press hands out, resist rotation
4. Hold 3 seconds, return
5. 10 reps each side

*Why it helps:* Anti-rotation strength protects your spine and improves power transfer.

**Bird Dogs**
1. On hands and knees
2. Extend opposite arm and leg
3. Keep hips level
4. Hold 3 seconds
5. 8 each side

**Dead Bugs**
1. On back, arms up, knees bent 90°
2. Lower opposite arm and leg
3. Keep lower back pressed to floor
4. 10 each side

**Plank with Shoulder Tap**
1. High plank position
2. Tap opposite shoulder with hand
3. Resist rotation
4. 10 each side

### Rotational Power

**Medicine Ball Rotational Throws**
1. Stand sideways to wall
2. Rotate hips and throw ball
3. Catch and repeat
4. 10 each side

*Why it helps:* Develops explosive rotational power for distance.

**Cable Woodchops**
1. High to low diagonal pattern
2. Rotate through hips and core
3. Control the return
4. 12 each side

**Rotational Slams**
1. Medicine ball overhead
2. Rotate and slam to ground beside hip
3. Powerful, controlled movement
4. 8 each side

### Lower Body Strength

**Goblet Squats**
1. Hold weight at chest
2. Squat deep with good form
3. Drive through heels
4. 3 sets × 12 reps

**Single-Leg Romanian Deadlift**
1. Stand on one leg
2. Hinge at hip, reach opposite hand toward ground
3. Keep back flat
4. 3 sets × 8 each side

*Why it helps:* Single-leg strength improves stability and power through the swing.

**Lateral Lunges**
1. Step wide to one side
2. Sit back into hip
3. Push back to center
4. 10 each side

### Upper Body Strength

**Rows (Cable or Dumbbell)**
1. Pull toward ribs, squeeze shoulder blades
2. Control return
3. 3 sets × 12 reps

**Face Pulls**
1. Pull band toward face, elbows high
2. External rotation at end
3. 3 sets × 15 reps

**Push-Ups**
1. Full range of motion
2. Core engaged
3. 3 sets × 10-15 reps

## Complete Golf Workout

### Pre-Round Warm-Up (10 minutes)

Do this before every round:

**Dynamic Movement (3 minutes)**
- Arm circles: 15 each direction
- Hip circles: 10 each direction
- Trunk rotations: 15 each side
- Leg swings: 10 each leg (front/back and side/side)

**Golf-Specific Activation (4 minutes)**
- Bodyweight squats: 10 reps
- Reverse lunges with rotation: 5 each side
- Push-ups: 10 reps
- Practice swings with increasing intensity: 10 swings

**Dynamic Stretches (3 minutes)**
- Hip flexor stretch: 30 seconds each
- Shoulder stretches: 30 seconds each
- Standing thoracic rotation: 10 each side

### Full Training Session (45 minutes)

Do 2-3 times per week during golf season.

**Warm-Up (5 minutes)**
- Light cardio
- Dynamic stretches

**Mobility Circuit (8 minutes)**
- Thoracic spine rotation: 10 each side
- 90/90 hip stretch: 30 seconds each position
- Hip flexor stretch with rotation: 30 seconds each
- Open books: 8 each side

**Power Circuit (10 minutes)**
- Medicine ball rotational throws: 8 each side
- Jump squats: 10 reps
- Rotational slams: 8 each side
- Explosive step-ups: 8 each leg

**Strength Circuit – 2 rounds (15 minutes)**
1. Goblet squats: 12 reps
2. Single-leg Romanian deadlifts: 8 each side
3. Push-ups: 12 reps
4. Cable rows: 12 reps
5. Pallof press: 10 each side
6. Bird dogs: 8 each side

**Cool-Down (7 minutes)**
- Hip flexor stretch: 1 minute each side
- Piriformis stretch: 1 minute each side
- Chest stretch: 1 minute
- Neck stretches: 30 seconds each direction

## Off-Season vs. In-Season Training

### Off-Season (Build Foundation)

**Focus:**
- Build strength and muscle
- Improve mobility limitations
- Develop power
- Higher volume, 3-4 sessions per week

**Sample Week:**
- Monday: Full body strength
- Wednesday: Power and mobility
- Friday: Full body strength
- Saturday: Flexibility and core

### In-Season (Maintain and Perform)

**Focus:**
- Maintain strength
- Prioritize recovery
- Keep mobility work
- Lower volume, 2 sessions per week

**Sample Week:**
- Monday: Quick full body (30 min)
- Thursday: Mobility and power (30 min)
- Golf practice and rounds as scheduled

## Exercises for Common Golf Problems

### Low Back Pain

**Priority exercises:**
- Dead bugs and bird dogs (core stability)
- Glute bridges and hip thrusts
- Hip flexor stretches
- Thoracic mobility work

**Reduce:** Any exercise that increases pain

### Golfer's Elbow

**Priority exercises:**
- Eccentric wrist exercises
- Forearm stretches
- Shoulder and rotator cuff strengthening

**Reduce:** Gripping exercises, heavy pulls

### Shoulder Issues

**Priority exercises:**
- Face pulls
- External rotation work
- Thoracic mobility (to reduce shoulder demand)
- Serratus exercises (push-up plus)

**Reduce:** Overhead pressing, dips

## Flexibility Routine for Golfers

Do 3-4 times per week, especially off-season.

**Hip Flexors** – 1 minute each side
- Half-kneeling stretch
- Tuck tailbone for deeper stretch

**Hamstrings** – 1 minute each side
- Standing or seated
- Hinge at hips, not back

**Piriformis** – 1 minute each side
- Figure-4 stretch
- Important for hip rotation

**Thoracic Spine** – 2 minutes
- Foam roller extensions
- Thread the needle

**Lats and Shoulders** – 1 minute each side
- Doorway stretch
- Lat stretch at wall

**Chest** – 1 minute
- Doorway stretch, multiple angles

## Equipment for Golf Training

**Essential:**
- Resistance bands
- Medicine ball (6-10 lbs)
- Foam roller

**Helpful:**
- Cable machine or door anchor for bands
- Light dumbbells
- Yoga mat

**Optional:**
- TRX or suspension trainer
- Kettlebells
- Balance board

## The Bottom Line

Golf is an athletic movement that rewards fitness training. A program focused on rotational mobility, core stability, and single-leg strength will improve your swing, add distance, and keep you playing injury-free.

Train 2-3 times per week during season, more in off-season. Warm up before every round. And remember—better physical condition means better golf on the back nine when others are fading.

Your body is your most important piece of golf equipment. Train it well.
