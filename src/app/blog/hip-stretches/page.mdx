# Hip Stretches: Open Your Hips and Move Freely

Tight hips are endemic in modern life. Hours of sitting shorten hip flexors, tighten external rotators, and leave glutes dormant. The result? Limited mobility, back pain, and compromised movement patterns. Regular hip stretching counteracts these effects.

**Key insight:** Hip tightness affects your entire body. Tight hips can cause back pain, knee problems, and limit athletic performance. Opening your hips improves everything.

---

## Understanding Hip Tightness

### What Gets Tight

| Area | Why | Effects |
|------|-----|---------|
| **Hip flexors** | Sitting shortens them | Low back pain, limited extension |
| **Piriformis** | Stress, sitting | Sciatica-like symptoms |
| **External rotators** | Chair position | Limited internal rotation |
| **Glutes** | Underused, sitting | Stiffness, weakness |
| **Adductors** | Narrow stance habits | Limited hip width |
| **TFL/IT band** | Compensation patterns | Knee and hip pain |

### Signs of Tight Hips

- Low back pain
- Difficulty squatting deep
- Knee pain (especially outside)
- Anterior pelvic tilt
- Discomfort sitting cross-legged
- Limited stride when running

## Hip Flexor Stretches

### Half-Kneeling Hip Flexor Stretch

The most essential hip stretch.

**How to do it:**
1. Kneel on one knee, other foot forward
2. Tuck pelvis under (posterior pelvic tilt)
3. Squeeze back glute
4. Lean forward slightly
5. Raise same-side arm overhead for more stretch
6. Hold 30-60 seconds each side

**Key:** Without the pelvic tuck, you'll arch your back instead of stretching the hip flexor.

### Couch Stretch

Intense hip flexor and quad stretch.

**How to do it:**
1. Kneel facing away from wall/couch
2. Place back foot against wall, knee on ground
3. Front foot flat, knee over ankle
4. Tuck pelvis, squeeze glute
5. Work toward upright torso
6. Hold 1-2 minutes each side

### Low Lunge

**How to do it:**
1. Deep lunge position
2. Back knee on ground
3. Sink hips forward and down
4. Keep front knee over ankle
5. Hold 30-60 seconds each side

### Standing Hip Flexor Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Staggered stance
2. Tuck pelvis under
3. Squeeze back glute
4. Lean forward from hips
5. Hold 30 seconds each side

## Glute and Piriformis Stretches

### Figure 4 Stretch (Supine)

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back
2. Cross right ankle over left knee
3. Pull left leg toward chest
4. Feel stretch in right hip/buttock
5. Hold 30-60 seconds each side

### Pigeon Pose

**How to do it:**
1. From hands and knees, bring right knee forward
2. Angle shin toward left hand
3. Extend left leg straight back
4. Lower hips toward ground
5. Fold forward for deeper stretch
6. Hold 1-2 minutes each side

### Seated Piriformis Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Sit in chair
2. Cross one ankle over opposite knee
3. Sit tall and lean forward from hips
4. Feel stretch deep in hip
5. Hold 30-60 seconds each side

### 90/90 Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Sit with front leg at 90° (hip and knee)
2. Back leg also at 90°, out to side
3. Sit tall
4. Front shin stretches external rotators
5. Back hip stretches internal rotators
6. Hold 1-2 minutes each side

### Thread the Needle (Hip Focus)

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back
2. Both knees bent
3. Cross right ankle over left knee
4. Let both legs fall to the left
5. Feel stretch in right hip
6. Hold 30-60 seconds each side

## External Rotator Stretches

### Double Pigeon (Fire Log)

**How to do it:**
1. Sit with legs stacked (shin on shin)
2. Flex both feet
3. Sit tall or fold forward
4. Hold 1-2 minutes each side

### Supine Figure 4 with Rotation

**How to do it:**
1. Standard figure 4 position
2. Let both legs fall toward crossed leg side
3. Increases stretch on top hip
4. Hold 30-45 seconds each side

### Ankle-to-Knee Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Sit with legs extended
2. Place right ankle on left knee
3. Slide left foot toward you
4. Lean forward
5. Hold 30-60 seconds each side

---

## Internal Rotation Stretches

Often neglected but commonly restricted.

### Supine Internal Rotation

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back, knees bent
2. Keep feet wider than hips
3. Let both knees fall inward
4. Feel stretch on outside of hips
5. Hold 30-60 seconds

### Prone Internal Rotation

**How to do it:**
1. Lie face down
2. Bend one knee 90°
3. Let foot fall outward (internally rotates hip)
4. Hold 30-45 seconds each side

### 90/90 with Internal Rotation Focus

**How to do it:**
1. In 90/90 position
2. Focus on back leg
3. Try to lift back knee off ground
4. Actively work internal rotation
5. Hold position or perform repetitions

---

## Adductor (Inner Thigh) Stretches

### Butterfly Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Sit with soles of feet together
2. Let knees fall out
3. Hold ankles
4. Gently press knees down with elbows
5. Hold 30-60 seconds

### Frog Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. On hands and knees
2. Spread knees wide, feet turned out
3. Shift hips back toward heels
4. Keep spine neutral
5. Hold 30-60 seconds

### Wide-Leg Forward Fold

**How to do it:**
1. Sit with legs spread wide
2. Keep back flat
3. Walk hands forward
4. Hold 30-60 seconds

### Side Lunge Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Wide stance
2. Shift weight to one side
3. Bend that knee, keep other leg straight
4. Feel stretch in straight leg's inner thigh
5. Hold 30 seconds each side

## Dynamic Hip Stretches

### Leg Swings (Sagittal)

**How to do it:**
1. Hold support
2. Swing leg forward and backward
3. Let momentum increase range
4. 15-20 swings each leg

### Leg Swings (Frontal)

**How to do it:**
1. Face support
2. Swing leg across body and out to side
3. 15-20 swings each leg

### Hip Circles

**How to do it:**
1. Hands on hips
2. Large circles clockwise
3. Large circles counterclockwise
4. 10-15 each direction

### Walking Hip Opener

**How to do it:**
1. Step forward
2. Pull knee toward chest
3. Open knee out to side (external rotation)
4. Step down and repeat other leg
5. 10-12 each leg

### World's Greatest Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Deep lunge
2. Drop elbow toward instep
3. Rotate, reaching arm to ceiling
4. Step through and repeat
5. 5-8 each side

## Tips for Tight Hips

### Consistency Over Intensity

- 10 minutes daily beats 60 minutes weekly
- Brief stretches throughout day help
- Set reminders to stretch during long sitting

### Breathing

- Deep breaths help muscles relax
- Exhale as you move deeper
- Don't hold breath

### Don't Force It

- Stretch to mild discomfort, not pain
- Flexibility takes time
- Forcing causes tightening, not loosening

### Address the Cause

- Take movement breaks from sitting
- Strengthen glutes (they're probably weak too)
- Vary your positions throughout day

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Hip flexors are priority** — Tight from sitting, affect your back
2. **Don't forget internal rotation** — Often the tightest direction
3. **Glutes need attention too** — Stretch AND strengthen
4. **Pelvic tilt is key** — Essential for effective hip flexor stretch
5. **Consistency wins** — Daily brief sessions beat occasional long ones
6. **Dynamic before, static after** — Match stretch type to timing

**Daily minimum:**
- Hip flexor stretch: 30 sec each side
- Figure 4 or pigeon: 30 sec each side
- 90/90 or butterfly: 30 seconds
- Total: 3-4 minutes

Open hips transform how you move and feel. Build the habit of daily hip stretching, and your body will thank you.
