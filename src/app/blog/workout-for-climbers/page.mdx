# Workout for Climbers: Build Strength for Harder Sends

Climbing is unique—it demands finger strength, pulling power, core tension, and mental focus. Off-the-wall training can accelerate your progress, prevent injuries, and help you send your project. Here's how to train smart.

## What Climbing Demands

### Finger and Grip Strength

The limiting factor for most climbers. Strong fingers hold smaller holds and resist fatigue.

### Pulling Power

Engaging your back, biceps, and shoulders to move your body upward.

### Core Tension

Keeping your body close to the wall, preventing barn-dooring, and controlling swing.

### Shoulder Stability

Protecting your shoulders through a huge range of motion under load.

### Antagonist Balance

Training pushing muscles to balance all that pulling and prevent injury.

### Flexibility

Hip mobility for high steps, shoulder mobility for reaches, general flexibility for movement options.

## Training Philosophy for Climbers

### Climbing Is the Best Training

- Supplemental training supports climbing, doesn't replace it
- Prioritize time on the wall
- Off-wall training fills gaps and prevents injury

### Specificity Matters

- Hangboarding directly trains finger strength
- General fitness helps but isn't enough
- Climbing movements are unique

### Don't Overtrain

- Climbing is already hard on tendons
- Adding heavy training requires recovery
- More isn't always better

### Injury Prevention Is Training

- Antagonist work prevents imbalances
- Shoulder prehab saves your climbing career
- Finger care is non-negotiable

## Finger Strength Training

The biggest bang for your buck off the wall.

### Hangboarding Basics

**When to start:** After 1-2 years of consistent climbing (tendons need time to adapt)

**Beginner Protocol:**
- 10-second hangs on comfortable edge
- 3-5 sets
- Full rest between hangs (3 minutes)
- 2x per week maximum
- Progress by reducing edge size before adding weight

**Intermediate Protocol:**
- 7-second max hangs
- Add weight as needed
- Multiple grip positions
- 2-3x per week

### Grip Positions to Train

- **Half crimp:** Most transferable, safest
- **Open hand:** Good for slopers, easier on pulleys
- **Full crimp:** Train sparingly, highest injury risk
- **Three-finger drag:** Open hand variant
- **Pinch:** Often neglected, use pinch blocks

### Hangboard Safety

- Always warm up thoroughly first
- Never train full crimp to failure
- Stop if you feel finger tweaks
- Progress slowly—tendons adapt slower than muscles

## Pulling Strength

### Pull-Ups (Foundation)

**Standard Pull-Ups**
- Shoulder-width grip
- Full range of motion
- 3 sets × max reps

**Weighted Pull-Ups**
- Add weight when you can do 10+ bodyweight
- 3-5 sets × 3-5 reps
- Focus on strength, not endurance

**Variations:**
- Wide grip (mimics wide moves)
- Narrow grip (compression)
- L-sit pull-ups (adds core)
- Archer pull-ups (unilateral strength)
- One-arm negatives (advanced)

### Rows

**Inverted Rows**
- Adjust angle to modify difficulty
- 3 sets × 10-12 reps

**Dumbbell Rows**
- One arm at a time
- Pull to hip, squeeze back
- 3 sets × 10 each arm

### Lock-Off Strength

**Lock-Off Holds**
- Pull up to 90°, hold
- Build to full lock-off
- 3 holds × 5-10 seconds each position

**Frenchies**
- Pull up, lock at top 5 sec
- Lower to 90°, hold 5 sec
- Lower to 120°, hold 5 sec
- That's one rep

## Core Training for Climbers

Climbing core work emphasizes tension and control, not crunches.

### Front Lever Progressions

**Tucked Front Lever**
- Hang with knees tucked to chest
- Hold body parallel to ground
- 3 sets × 10-20 seconds

**Advanced Tuck → Straddle → Full**
- Progress over months
- Ultimate climbing core exercise

### L-Sit Progressions

**Floor L-Sit**
- Hands on floor or parallettes
- Lift body, legs extended
- 3 sets × 10-20 seconds

**Hanging L-Sit**
- Hang from bar, raise legs to L
- Hold for time or reps

### Functional Core

**Hollow Body Hold**
- Lie on back, arms overhead
- Lift arms, shoulders, legs slightly
- Hold position—mimics body tension on wall
- 3 sets × 20-30 seconds

**Dead Bugs**
- Control and coordination
- 3 sets × 10 each side

**Hanging Knee Raises**
- Control the swing
- Progress to leg raises
- 3 sets × 10-12 reps

## Shoulder Health and Stability

Climbing shoulders take a beating. Protect them.

### External Rotations

**Band External Rotation**
- Elbow at side, rotate forearm out
- 2 sets × 15-20 reps daily

### Face Pulls

- Pull band toward face, elbows high
- External rotation at end
- 3 sets × 15 reps

### Y-T-W Raises

- Lie face down
- Y: Arms overhead, thumbs up
- T: Arms to sides
- W: Elbows bent, squeeze back
- 2 sets × 10 of each

### Scapular Pull-Ups

- Hang with straight arms
- Pull shoulder blades down and back
- Slight lift without bending arms
- 3 sets × 10 reps

## Antagonist Training (Push)

Balance all that pulling to prevent injury.

### Push-Ups

- Standard, wide, narrow
- Builds chest and tricep balance
- 3 sets × 15-20 reps

### Dips

- Bench or parallel bars
- Don't go too deep if shoulders protest
- 3 sets × 10-12 reps

### Overhead Press

- Dumbbells or barbell
- Balance pulling with pushing overhead
- 3 sets × 10-12 reps

### Wrist and Finger Extensions

**Rice Bucket**
- Open and close hands in bucket of rice
- 2-3 minutes

**Reverse Wrist Curls**
- Light weight, high reps
- 2 sets × 20 reps

## Sample Training Programs

### Climbing + Minimal Supplemental (2 days off-wall)

**Day 1: Pulling and Fingers**
- Warm-up: 10 min cardio + shoulder mobility
- Hangboard: 5-6 sets × 10 sec hangs
- Weighted pull-ups: 4 × 5 reps
- Rows: 3 × 10 reps
- Core: Hanging leg raises 3 × 10

**Day 2: Antagonist and Core**
- Push-ups: 3 × 15
- Dips: 3 × 10
- Overhead press: 3 × 10
- Face pulls: 3 × 15
- Hollow body holds: 3 × 30 sec
- Wrist extensions: 2 × 20

### Performance Phase (3 days off-wall)

**Day 1: Max Finger Strength**
- Thorough warm-up
- Hangboard max hangs: 6-8 sets × 7 sec
- Various grip positions
- Light cool-down climbing

**Day 2: Power and Pulling**
- Campus board (if experienced) or explosive pull-ups
- Weighted pull-ups: 5 × 3-5 heavy
- Lock-offs: 3 positions × 5 sec each
- Front lever work: 5 sets

**Day 3: Antagonist and Mobility**
- Push-ups: 3 × 15
- Dips: 3 × 12
- Shoulder press: 3 × 10
- Shoulder prehab circuit
- 20-minute mobility/stretching

### Base Building Phase (Endurance Focus)

More climbing volume, less max strength work:
- ARC training (easy sustained climbing)
- Higher rep pull-up work
- Endurance hangs (lighter, longer)
- Maintain antagonist work

## Flexibility for Climbing

### Hip Mobility (Critical)

**Frog Stretch**
- Knees wide, hips sink toward floor
- 2-3 minutes

**Pigeon Pose**
- Deep hip opener
- 1-2 minutes each side

**Deep Squat Hold**
- Sit in full squat
- 2-3 minutes daily

### Shoulder Mobility

**Sleeper Stretch**
- Internal rotation
- 30 seconds each side

**Doorway Stretch**
- Multiple arm positions
- 30 seconds each

**Wall Slides**
- Back against wall
- Slide arms up and down
- 15 reps

## Injury Prevention

### Finger Care

- Warm up before climbing, always
- Don't climb on tweaky fingers
- Progress hangboarding slowly
- Use open hand grip more
- Take rest days

### Shoulder Care

- Antagonist work weekly
- External rotation work daily
- Don't climb through shoulder pain
- Sleep position matters

### Elbow Care

- Watch for elbow pain (golfer's/tennis elbow)
- Wrist/forearm stretches after climbing
- Antagonist work helps
- Address early, not late

## Periodization for Climbers

### Base Phase (4-8 weeks)

- High volume climbing
- Build aerobic capacity
- General strength work
- Address weaknesses

### Strength Phase (4-6 weeks)

- Max hangs and weighted pull-ups
- Limit bouldering
- Lower volume, higher intensity

### Power Phase (3-4 weeks)

- Campus board (if appropriate)
- Explosive movements
- Project attempts

### Performance/Send Phase (2-4 weeks)

- Reduce training volume
- Fresh for attempts
- Maintain but don't build

### Rest Phase (1-2 weeks)

- Active recovery only
- Let body and mind recover
- Come back hungry

## The Bottom Line

Climbing improvement comes from climbing, but targeted training accelerates progress and prevents the injuries that sideline climbers.

Train your fingers with hangboard work. Build pulling power with weighted pull-ups. Develop core tension with front lever progressions. Protect your shoulders with antagonist and prehab work. Stay flexible.

Train consistently but don't overtrain. Let your fingers recover between sessions. Listen to your body.

The strongest climbers train smart, not just hard. Now go send your project.
