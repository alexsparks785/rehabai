# Sit-to-Stand Exercise: The Most Important Functional Movement

Getting up from a chair is something most people do dozens of times daily without thinking. But this simple movement is actually one of the best predictors of functional independence, fall risk, and longevity. The sit-to-stand exercise builds the strength you need for this essential life skill.

**Key insight:** The ability to rise from a chair without using hands is strongly correlated with independence in older adults. This one exercise can be the difference between aging in place or needing assistance.

## Proper Form

### The Movement

**Standing Up:**
1. Scoot to edge of chair
2. Place feet hip-width apart, slightly back
3. Lean forward from hips (nose over toes)
4. Drive through heels to stand
5. Squeeze glutes at top
6. Stand fully upright

**Sitting Down:**
1. Push hips back (like reaching for chair)
2. Bend knees, lowering with control
3. Keep chest up
4. Touch down gently—don't plop

### Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Problem | Fix |
|---------|---------|-----|
| Knees caving in | Poor hip control | Push knees out |
| Using momentum | Reduces strength benefit | Move slowly and controlled |
| Not leaning forward | Hard to get up | "Nose over toes" |
| Plopping down | Missing eccentric work | Control the descent |
| Pushing off chair | Cheating the exercise | Hands off arms/seat |

## Progressions

### Level 1: Assisted

**With arm rests:**
1. Use arms to help push up
2. Lower slowly
3. Build to less arm use over time
4. Goal: 10 reps with minimal arm use

**With walker or support:**
1. Hold stable support
2. Use upper body assist as needed
3. Progress to lighter touch
4. Goal: Light touch only, then no support

### Level 2: Standard

**No hands:**
1. Cross arms over chest
2. Stand up without using hands
3. Sit down with control
4. Goal: 15-20 reps with good form

### Level 3: Challenging

**Slow tempo:**
1. 3 seconds to stand
2. 3 seconds to sit
3. Increases time under tension
4. Goal: 10-15 reps slow tempo

**Pause at bottom:**
1. Touch chair, pause 1-2 seconds
2. Stand without momentum
3. Removes stretch reflex
4. Goal: 10-15 reps with pause

### Level 4: Advanced

**Single-leg emphasis:**
1. Stand up using mostly one leg
2. Other foot lightly touching for balance
3. Goal: 8-10 reps each leg

**Weighted:**
1. Hold dumbbell or weight at chest
2. Perform standard sit-to-stand
3. Progress weight gradually
4. Goal: Match progressive overload principles

**Lower surface:**
1. Use lower chair or box
2. Requires more strength and mobility
3. Goal: Progress to bench or ottoman height

**Single-leg (full):**
1. Stand up on one leg completely
2. Other leg extended
3. Advanced—requires significant strength
4. Goal: 5-8 reps each leg

## Testing Yourself

### 30-Second Chair Stand Test

**Standard protocol:**
1. Use standard height chair (17 inches)
2. Arms crossed over chest
3. Count complete stands in 30 seconds
4. Must fully stand and fully sit each rep

**Norms by age and gender:**

| Age | Men (reps) | Women (reps) |
|-----|------------|--------------|
| 60-64 | 14-19 | 12-17 |
| 65-69 | 12-18 | 11-16 |
| 70-74 | 12-17 | 10-15 |
| 75-79 | 11-17 | 10-15 |
| 80-84 | 10-15 | 9-14 |
| 85-89 | 8-14 | 8-13 |
| 90-94 | 7-12 | 4-11 |

### 5-Times Sit-to-Stand Test

**Protocol:**
1. Arms crossed over chest
2. Complete 5 full stands as fast as safely possible
3. Time starts on "go," stops when you sit after 5th rep

**Interpretation:**
- **≤11.19 sec:** Normal
- **11.2-13.6 sec:** Mildly impaired
- **13.7-16.7 sec:** Moderately impaired
- **≥16.7 sec:** Significantly impaired

## Sample Programs

### Beginner Program (Daily)

**Week 1-2:**
- 3 sets of 5 reps with arm support
- Rest 60 seconds between sets

**Week 3-4:**
- 3 sets of 8 reps with light arm touch
- Rest 60 seconds between sets

**Week 5-6:**
- 3 sets of 10 reps, no arms
- Rest 60 seconds between sets

### Maintenance Program (3-4x/week)

| Exercise | Sets × Reps |
|----------|-------------|
| Sit-to-stand (no arms) | 3 × 15 |
| Slow tempo (3 sec each way) | 2 × 10 |
| Single-leg emphasis | 2 × 8 each leg |

### Strength Building (3x/week)

| Exercise | Sets × Reps |
|----------|-------------|
| Weighted sit-to-stand | 3 × 10 |
| Pause at bottom | 3 × 10 |
| Lower surface | 2 × 10 |
| Standard (for volume) | 2 × 15 |

### Fall Prevention Focus

| Exercise | Sets × Reps |
|----------|-------------|
| Standard sit-to-stand | 3 × 12 |
| Slow and controlled | 2 × 10 |
| Stand and hold balance (3 sec) | 2 × 10 |
| Single-leg emphasis | 2 × 8 each |

## Troubleshooting

### "I Can't Stand Without Hands"

**Solutions:**
1. Start with arm support
2. Reduce reliance gradually
3. Use higher chair initially
4. Strengthen quads separately
5. Work on leaning forward more

### "My Knees Hurt"

**Solutions:**
1. Check form (knees over toes, not caving)
2. Use higher chair
3. Don't go as low initially
4. Strengthen hips (clamshells, bridges)
5. See professional if persistent

### "I Lose Balance"

**Solutions:**
1. Keep support nearby (not touching)
2. Strengthen ankles (calf raises)
3. Practice separate balance exercises
4. Focus on controlled movement
5. Don't rush

### "It's Too Easy"

**Progressions:**
1. Slow the tempo way down
2. Add pause at bottom
3. Use lower surface
4. Add weight
5. Single-leg variations
