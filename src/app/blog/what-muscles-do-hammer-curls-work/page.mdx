# What Muscles Do Hammer Curls Work? Complete Anatomy Guide

Hammer curls are the secret weapon for building thick, powerful arms. While regular curls target the biceps, the neutral grip of hammer curls shifts emphasis to the brachialis and brachioradialis—muscles that create arm thickness and forearm size.

Let's break down exactly what muscles hammer curls target and why they're essential for complete arm development.

## Primary Muscles Worked

### Brachialis - Maximum Activation

The brachialis is the star of hammer curls:

**Why brachialis is emphasized:**
- Neutral grip removes biceps' mechanical advantage
- Brachialis becomes the primary elbow flexor
- Sits underneath the biceps, pushing it UP

**Activation level:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Why this matters:** The brachialis makes your arms look thicker from all angles. It pushes the biceps up, creating a bigger peak appearance.

### Brachioradialis - Maximum Activation

This large forearm muscle works hard:

**Why brachioradialis is emphasized:**
- Neutral grip is its strongest position
- Major elbow flexor in this position
- Creates forearm size

**Activation level:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Biceps Brachii - High Activation

The biceps still work, but less than regular curls:

**Long Head:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- Works throughout the curl
- Less emphasized than in supinated curls

**Short Head:** ⭐⭐⭐
- Still contributes
- Neutral grip reduces its advantage

## Why Neutral Grip Changes Everything

### In Regular (Supinated) Curls
- Palm faces up
- Biceps in strongest position
- Biceps do most of the work

### In Hammer (Neutral) Curls
- Palm faces inward
- Biceps mechanically disadvantaged
- Brachialis and brachioradialis take over

This is why hammer curls feel different—and why they're essential for complete arm development.

## How to Perform Hammer Curls

### Setup
1. Stand with dumbbells at sides
2. Palms facing your body (neutral grip)
3. Feet shoulder-width apart
4. Core engaged, shoulders back

### Execution
1. **Curl:** Lift dumbbells keeping palms facing each other
2. **Elbows fixed:** Don't let them drift forward
3. **Full contraction:** Bring dumbbells to shoulder height
4. **Squeeze:** Contract at the top
5. **Lower:** Controlled descent to full extension
6. **Repeat:** Maintain neutral grip throughout

### Key Technique Points

- **Neutral grip throughout:** Palms always face each other
- **Elbows pinned:** To your sides, don't swing
- **Full range:** Complete extension to full contraction
- **Control:** No swinging or momentum
- **Both arms:** Together or alternating

## Hammer Curls vs. Regular Curls

| Factor | Hammer Curls | Regular Curls |
|--------|--------------|---------------|
| Primary target | Brachialis, brachioradialis | Biceps |
| Arm thickness | Maximum | Moderate |
| Biceps peak | Moderate (indirect) | Maximum |
| Forearm size | High | Low |
| Grip | Neutral | Supinated |

**Include both** for complete arm development.

## Common Mistakes

### Swinging/Using Momentum
- **Problem:** Body does the work, not arms
- **Fix:** Strict form, reduce weight if needed

### Elbows Drifting Forward
- **Problem:** Reduces arm work, involves shoulders
- **Fix:** Pin elbows to sides throughout

### Partial Range of Motion
- **Problem:** Missing full development
- **Fix:** Full extension to full contraction

### Rotating to Supinated Grip
- **Problem:** Turns into a regular curl
- **Fix:** Keep palms facing each other always

### Going Too Heavy
- **Problem:** Form breaks down
- **Fix:** Weight you can control for 10-12 strict reps

## Hammer Curl Variations

### Standard Hammer Curl
- Both arms together
- Described above
- Most common

### Alternating Hammer Curl
- One arm at a time
- Allows more focus per arm
- Natural walking motion

### Cross-Body Hammer Curl
- Curl across the body toward opposite shoulder
- Slightly different emphasis
- More brachialis focus

### Incline Hammer Curl
- On incline bench
- Arms behind body
- Greater stretch on long head

### Rope Cable Hammer Curl
- Using rope attachment
- Constant tension
- Good for squeeze

### Preacher Hammer Curl
- On preacher bench
- More isolation
- Strict movement

### Seated Hammer Curl
- Sitting down
- Reduces momentum
- Stricter form

## Programming Recommendations

### For Arm Thickness
- 3-4 sets of 10-12 reps
- Include in every arm workout
- Focus on brachialis squeeze

### For Forearm Development
- 3 sets of 12-15 reps
- Part of forearm/grip work
- Hammer curls + wrist work

### In Arm Training
- Include WITH regular curls
- Not instead of them
- Both serve different purposes

### For Complete Arms
- Regular curls: Biceps peak
- Hammer curls: Arm thickness
- Triceps work: 2/3 of arm size
- Include all three

## The Arm Thickness Secret

Many people only do regular curls and wonder why their arms don't look thick.

**The solution:** The brachialis creates arm thickness.
- It's underneath the biceps
- It pushes the biceps UP when developed
- It's visible from the side
- Hammer curls target it directly

If you want arms that look big from every angle—not just when flexed from the front—hammer curls are essential.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Hammer curls target brachialis and brachioradialis** primarily
2. **Neutral grip shifts work** away from biceps
3. **Builds arm THICKNESS**, not just biceps peak
4. **Include WITH regular curls**, not instead
5. **Develops forearms** simultaneously
6. **Essential for complete arm development**

## The Bottom Line

If you want arms that look thick and powerful from every angle, hammer curls are non-negotiable. Regular curls build the biceps peak, but hammer curls build the brachialis that creates overall arm thickness.

Include both in your training. Hammer curls for thickness, regular curls for peak, and plenty of triceps work (which makes up 2/3 of arm size). Your arms will look bigger from every angle.
