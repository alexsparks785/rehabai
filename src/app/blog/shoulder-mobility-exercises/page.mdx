# Shoulder Mobility Exercises: Unlock Full Range of Motion

The shoulder is the most mobile joint in your body—but that mobility is often compromised by modern life. If you can't reach overhead comfortably, have trouble scratching your back, or feel restricted during exercise, shoulder mobility work can transform your movement.

**Key insight:** Shoulder mobility depends on multiple structures working together—the shoulder joint, shoulder blade, thoracic spine, and surrounding muscles. Address all of them for lasting improvement.

---

## Understanding Shoulder Mobility

### Movements to Assess

| Movement | Normal Range | Common Limitation |
|----------|--------------|-------------------|
| **Flexion** (arm overhead) | 180° | Can't reach straight up |
| **Extension** (arm behind) | 50-60° | Trouble reaching back pocket |
| **External rotation** | 90° | Can't reach behind head |
| **Internal rotation** | 70-90° | Can't scratch mid-back |
| **Abduction** (arm to side) | 180° | Limited side raise |

### What Limits Mobility

- **Tight muscles:** Pecs, lats, posterior capsule
- **Weak muscles:** Rotator cuff, lower traps, serratus
- **Thoracic spine:** Poor extension limits overhead reach
- **Shoulder blade:** Scapular dyskinesis affects all movements
- **Joint capsule:** Can become tight or adhered

## Shoulder CARs

The most important shoulder mobility exercise.

### Full Shoulder CARs

**How to do it:**
1. Stand with arm at side
2. Make a fist, create tension in arm
3. Raise arm forward and up (flexion)
4. Continue overhead
5. Rotate to reach behind (extension)
6. Return arm to side via this path
7. Reverse the direction
8. Perform 3-5 circles each direction, each arm

**Key points:**
- Move as slowly as possible
- Explore your full range
- Keep body still—only arm moves
- Create tension throughout

## Overhead Mobility Exercises

### Wall Slides

**How to do it:**
1. Stand with back against wall
2. Arms in "goal post" position (elbows 90°)
3. Keep entire back of arms touching wall
4. Slide arms up the wall
5. Only go as high as you can maintain contact
6. Slide back down
7. Perform 15-20 repetitions

### Floor Angels (Supine)

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back, knees bent
2. Arms in goal post position on floor
3. Slide arms up toward overhead
4. Keep arms touching floor throughout
5. Slide back down
6. Perform 15-20 repetitions

### Lat Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Hold doorframe or sturdy object
2. Step back and hinge at hips
3. Push hips away, feel stretch in lats
4. Hold 30-60 seconds each arm

### Child's Pose with Lat Bias

**How to do it:**
1. Kneel, reach arms forward
2. Sit hips back toward heels
3. Walk hands to one side
4. Feel stretch in opposite lat
5. Hold 30-45 seconds each side

### Prayer Stretch Progression

**How to do it:**
1. Hands on table or bench, step back
2. Push chest down between arms
3. Let head drop through
4. Hold 30-60 seconds

## External Rotation Exercises

Often the most limited movement.

### Doorway External Rotation Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Stand in doorway
2. Elbow bent 90°, forearm on door frame
3. Step forward and turn body away
4. Feel stretch in front of shoulder
5. Hold 30-45 seconds each side

### Sleeper Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on side (shoulder being stretched on bottom)
2. Arm straight out, elbow bent 90°
3. Use top hand to push bottom forearm toward floor
4. Keep shoulder blade flat
5. Hold 30-45 seconds each side

### 90/90 External Rotation

**How to do it:**
1. Stand or sit with arm at 90° abduction
2. Elbow bent 90°
3. Rotate forearm back (external rotation)
4. Go to end range
5. Hold or perform 10-15 reps

### Band External Rotation Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Hold band at shoulder height
2. One arm pulls band, other arm rotates externally
3. Let band assist into stretch
4. Hold 15-20 seconds
5. Release and repeat
6. Perform 5-8 holds each arm

---

## Internal Rotation Exercises

### Cross-Body Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Bring arm across body at shoulder height
2. Use other hand to press arm closer
3. Keep shoulder down
4. Hold 30-45 seconds each arm

### Behind-the-Back Reach

**How to do it:**
1. Reach one arm behind your back
2. Palm facing away from body
3. Use other hand or towel to assist higher
4. Hold 30-45 seconds each side

### Internal Rotation with Towel

**How to do it:**
1. Hold towel behind back
2. One hand over shoulder, one at lower back
3. Use top hand to pull bottom hand up
4. Feel stretch in bottom shoulder
5. Hold 30 seconds, then switch

## Thoracic Spine Mobility

Critical for overhead shoulder movement.

### Foam Roller Extension

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on foam roller across upper back
2. Support head with hands
3. Extend backward over roller
4. Don't arch lower back
5. Move roller to different segments
6. 10 extensions at each position

### Thread the Needle

**How to do it:**
1. On hands and knees
2. Reach one arm under body
3. Let shoulder and head follow
4. Feel rotation in thoracic spine
5. Hold 30 seconds each side

### Open Book

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on side, knees bent 90°
2. Arms extended in front, palms together
3. Rotate top arm open, following with eyes
4. Let chest open to ceiling
5. Return slowly
6. Perform 10-15 reps each side

### Cat-Cow

**How to do it:**
1. On hands and knees
2. **Cat:** Round spine, spread shoulder blades
3. **Cow:** Arch spine, squeeze shoulder blades
4. Move slowly between positions
5. Perform 15-20 repetitions

## Scapular Mobility

### Scapular CARs

**How to do it:**
1. Stand with arm at side
2. Elevate shoulder blade (shrug)
3. Protract (round forward)
4. Depress (pull down)
5. Retract (squeeze back)
6. Complete full circle
7. Reverse direction
8. Perform 5 each direction, each side

### Wall Push-Up Plus

**How to do it:**
1. Hands on wall, arms straight
2. Let shoulder blades come together
3. Push through hands, spreading shoulder blades apart
4. Feel serratus activate around ribs
5. Perform 15-20 repetitions

### Prone Y-T-W Raises

**How to do it:**
1. Lie face down on floor or bench
2. **Y:** Lift arms at 45° angle, thumbs up
3. **T:** Lift arms straight to sides, thumbs up
4. **W:** Elbows bent 90°, squeeze shoulder blades, lift
5. Hold each 3-5 seconds
6. Perform 10-12 of each

### Band Pull-Aparts

**How to do it:**
1. Hold band at shoulder height
2. Arms straight in front
3. Pull band apart by squeezing shoulder blades
4. Control return
5. Perform 20-30 repetitions

1. Doorway pec stretch

## Progress Tracking

### Wall Test

1. Stand with back against wall
2. Try to touch wall with:
   - Back of head
   - Upper back
   - Lower back (small gap OK)
   - Back of hands while arms overhead
3. Track what touches easily vs. what's restricted

### Apley Scratch Test

1. Reach one hand over shoulder, other behind back
2. Try to touch fingers
3. Measure distance between fingertips
4. Track improvement over time

## Key Takeaways

1. **CARs are king** — Do shoulder circles daily for joint health
2. **Address thoracic spine** — Poor upper back mobility limits shoulders
3. **Include scapular work** — Shoulder blade movement affects everything
4. **External rotation needs attention** — Usually the tightest direction
5. **Consistency wins** — 5 minutes daily beats 30 minutes weekly
6. **Move through full range** — Use it or lose it applies to mobility

**Daily minimum:**
- Shoulder CARs: 3 circles each direction
- Wall slides or floor angels: 15 reps
- One thoracic mobility exercise: 1-2 minutes
- Total: 5 minutes

Your shoulders are designed for incredible mobility. Reclaim that range with consistent practice, and everything from daily tasks to athletic performance improves.
