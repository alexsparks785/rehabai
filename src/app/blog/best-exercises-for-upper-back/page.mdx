# Best Exercises for Upper Back: Build Strength and Improve Posture

A strong upper back is the foundation of good posture and shoulder health. Modern life—sitting, screens, driving—weakens these muscles. These exercises rebuild what desk work destroys.

## Upper Back Anatomy

**Trapezius**
- Three parts: upper, middle, lower
- Upper traps: shrug shoulders
- Middle traps: pull shoulder blades together
- Lower traps: pull shoulder blades down and in

**Rhomboids**
- Between spine and shoulder blades
- Retract (squeeze) shoulder blades
- Critical for posture

**Rear Deltoids**
- Back of shoulders
- Often grouped with upper back training
- Important for shoulder balance

**Levator Scapulae**
- Neck to shoulder blade
- Often tight and overworked

## Why Upper Back Strength Matters

**Posture** – Counters forward-slumped position
**Shoulder Health** – Balances pressing movements
**Pain Relief** – Reduces neck and upper back pain
**Aesthetics** – Creates balanced physique
**Performance** – Foundation for pulling movements

## Best Upper Back Exercises

### 1. Face Pull

**The ultimate upper back exercise.**

**How to do it:**
1. Cable or band at face height
2. Grip rope, pull toward face
3. Elbows high, spread rope apart
4. Externally rotate at end—hands by ears
5. Squeeze shoulder blades

**Sets/reps:** 3-4 sets × 15-20 reps

**Why it's best:** Hits rear delts, mid traps, rhomboids, and external rotators in one movement. Do these every upper body session.

### 2. Band Pull-Apart

**Anytime, anywhere upper back work.**

**How to do it:**
1. Hold band at shoulder width, arms extended
2. Pull band apart, bringing to chest
3. Squeeze shoulder blades hard
4. Control return

**Sets/reps:** 3 sets × 15-20 reps

**Why it works:** Simple, effective, can be done daily. Great warm-up or finisher.

### 3. Prone Y-T-W Raises

**Comprehensive upper back activation.**

**How to do it:**
1. Lie face down on bench or floor
2. Y: Arms overhead, thumbs up, lift
3. T: Arms to sides, thumbs up, lift
4. W: Elbows bent, squeeze back, lift
5. Hold each 2-3 seconds

**Sets/reps:** 2-3 sets × 8-10 of each position

**Why it works:** Targets lower traps (Y), mid traps (T), and external rotators (W).

### 4. Barbell Row

**Heavy upper back builder.**

**How to do it:**
1. Hinge at hips, flat back
2. Pull bar to upper abdomen/lower chest
3. Squeeze shoulder blades together
4. Lower with control

**Sets/reps:** 3-4 sets × 8-10 reps

**Why it works:** Allows heavy loading, builds thickness throughout upper back.

**Upper back emphasis:** Pull to chest, keep elbows wide.

### 5. Seated Cable Row (Wide Grip)

**Controlled upper back isolation.**

**How to do it:**
1. Wide grip attachment
2. Pull to upper chest
3. Elbows out and back
4. Squeeze shoulder blades hard
5. Extend arms with control

**Sets/reps:** 3 sets × 12-15 reps

**Why it works:** Wide grip and high pull targets upper back more than lats.

### 6. Shrugs

**Direct upper trap work.**

**How to do it:**
1. Hold weights at sides (dumbbells or barbell)
2. Shrug shoulders straight up toward ears
3. Hold briefly at top
4. Lower with control

**Sets/reps:** 3 sets × 12-15 reps

**Why it works:** Isolates upper traps for size and strength.

**Note:** Most people have tight upper traps from stress—don't overdo these if you have neck tension.

### 7. Reverse Fly

**Rear delt and mid-trap focus.**

**How to do it:**
1. Bent over at hips, flat back
2. Arms hanging, slight elbow bend
3. Raise arms out to sides
4. Squeeze shoulder blades at top
5. Lower with control

**Sets/reps:** 3 sets × 12-15 reps

**Why it works:** Isolates rear delts and rhomboids for posture support.

### 8. Chest-Supported Row

**Upper back without lower back fatigue.**

**How to do it:**
1. Lie face down on incline bench
2. Row dumbbells to sides
3. Squeeze shoulder blades together
4. Control the lowering

**Sets/reps:** 3 sets × 10-12 reps

**Why it works:** Removes lower back from equation, pure upper back work.

### 9. Wall Slide

**Posture and scapular control.**

**How to do it:**
1. Back against wall
2. Arms in "goalpost" position touching wall
3. Slide arms up, maintaining wall contact
4. Slide back down

**Sets/reps:** 2-3 sets × 10-12 reps

**Why it works:** Improves posture, activates lower traps, reveals tightness.

### 10. Prone Cobra

**Full upper back activation.**

**How to do it:**
1. Lie face down, arms at sides
2. Lift chest and arms off floor
3. Rotate thumbs to ceiling
4. Squeeze shoulder blades down and back
5. Hold 10-20 seconds

**Sets/reps:** 3 holds × 15-20 seconds

**Why it works:** Isometric upper back contraction in extension—great for posture.

## Complete Upper Back Workouts

### Quick Posture Fix (10 minutes)

**Do daily or as work break:**

1. Band pull-aparts: 20 reps
2. Wall slides: 10 reps
3. Prone Y-T-W: 8 of each
4. Prone cobra hold: 2 × 15 seconds

### Full Upper Back Session (20 minutes)

**2-3 times per week:**

**Warm-Up (3 minutes)**
- Arm circles
- Band pull-aparts: 15 reps
- Shoulder rolls

**Main Work (17 minutes)**
1. Face pulls: 3 × 15
2. Barbell or cable rows (wide grip): 3 × 10
3. Reverse flys: 3 × 12
4. Prone Y-T-W: 2 × 10 of each
5. Wall slides: 2 × 10
6. Prone cobra: 2 × 15-second holds

### Upper Back Finisher

**Add to any upper body workout:**

1. Face pulls: 2 × 20
2. Band pull-aparts: 2 × 20
3. Prone Y raise: 1 × 15

## Exercises for Specific Goals

### For Posture Improvement
**Prioritize:**
- Face pulls
- Wall slides
- Prone Y-T-W
- Band pull-aparts

**Focus on:** High reps, daily consistency

### For Size/Strength
**Prioritize:**
- Barbell rows
- Shrugs
- Chest-supported rows
- Heavy face pulls

**Focus on:** Progressive overload, 2-3x per week

### For Pain Relief
**Prioritize:**
- Gentle band work
- Prone raises
- Wall slides
- Avoid aggravating movements

**Focus on:** Consistency, light resistance, addressing root cause

### For Athletes
**Prioritize:**
- Face pulls (high volume)
- Rows (various grips)
- Y-T-W raises
- Balance with pressing work

**Focus on:** Injury prevention, shoulder balance

## Programming Upper Back

### Frequency
- Posture work: Daily
- Strength work: 2-3x per week
- Include in every upper body session

### Volume
- High volume tolerated well
- 15-25 sets per week for development
- Face pulls can be done frequently

### Balance
- Match or exceed pressing volume
- For every bench press set, do a row
- More pulling never hurts

## Common Mistakes

### Not Training Upper Back Enough
- Pressing without pulling
- Imbalance leads to injury

### Using Too Much Weight
- Upper back responds to tension and squeeze
- Control matters more than weight

### Neglecting Lower Traps
- All shrugs, no Y raises
- Lower traps are typically weak

### Only Doing Lat Work
- Lat pulldowns don't hit upper back well
- Need rows and face pulls

## The Bottom Line

A strong upper back counters modern posture problems and keeps shoulders healthy. Face pulls and band pull-aparts should be non-negotiable. Include prone Y-T-W raises regularly.

Balance your pressing with equal or greater pulling volume. Train upper back frequently—it can handle it and will reward you with better posture and healthier shoulders.

Your upper back is the foundation of upper body health. Strengthen it consistently and everything else gets better.
