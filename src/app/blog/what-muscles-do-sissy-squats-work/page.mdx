# What Muscles Do Sissy Squats Work? Complete Anatomy Guide

Don't let the name fool you. Sissy squats are one of the most challenging quad exercises you can do, requiring serious strength, balance, and knee health. Named after the Greek myth of Sisyphus, this exercise has been a bodybuilding secret for quad development for decades.

Let's break down exactly what muscles sissy squats target and why they create such an intense quad burn.

## Primary Muscles Worked

### Quadriceps (Maximum Isolation)

Sissy squats are a quad isolation exercise—and they do it better than almost anything else:

**Rectus femoris:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Maximum)
- The only quad muscle that crosses both hip and knee
- Works EXTRA hard because hips stay extended while knees flex
- This is why sissy squats feel so different from regular squats

**Vastus lateralis (outer quad):** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Maximum)
- Major knee extensor
- Takes heavy load throughout the movement

**Vastus medialis (inner quad/VMO):** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Maximum)
- Critical for knee stability
- Works hard especially in the deep position

**Vastus intermedius:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Maximum)
- Deep quad muscle under rectus femoris
- Full activation during knee extension against resistance

**Why the quad activation is special:**
In a regular squat, hip extension (glutes/hamstrings) shares the work. In a sissy squat, hips stay extended throughout—meaning **quads do nearly all the work** of controlling and reversing the movement.

## Secondary Muscles Worked

### Hip Flexors - High Activation

The hip flexors work isometrically to maintain hip extension:
- **Iliopsoas:** Prevents hip flexion throughout
- **Rectus femoris:** Works double-duty (knee extension + hip maintenance)

### Core Muscles - Moderate to High

Your core stabilizes the unusual body position:
- **Rectus abdominis:** Maintains torso angle
- **Obliques:** Prevents rotation
- **Erector spinae:** Keeps spine position

### Tibialis Anterior - High

The front of your shin works hard:
- Maintains ankle position
- Activated during the lean-back
- Often feels fatigued after sissy squats

### Calves - Moderate

The gastrocnemius and soleus work for balance:
- Help control ankle position
- Maintain base of support
- Work isometrically throughout

## What Makes Sissy Squats Unique

### The Biomechanics

In a sissy squat:
1. **Hips stay extended** (no hip hinge)
2. **Knees travel far forward** (extreme knee flexion)
3. **Torso leans back** (counterbalance)
4. **Heels rise** off the ground

This combination creates:
- **Maximum quad stretch** at the bottom
- **Constant tension** throughout the movement
- **Minimal glute/hamstring involvement**
- **Pure knee extension demand**

### The Stretch Factor

Sissy squats place the quads—especially the rectus femoris—under **maximum stretch**. Research suggests stretched-position training may enhance muscle growth.

## Muscle Activation Comparison

| Exercise | Quad Isolation | Rectus Femoris | Glute Involvement |
|----------|---------------|----------------|-------------------|
| Sissy Squats | Maximum | Maximum | Minimal |
| Leg Extensions | Maximum | High | None |
| Hack Squats | High | Moderate | Moderate |
| Front Squats | High | Moderate | High |
| Back Squats | Moderate-High | Moderate | Very High |

Sissy squats provide quad isolation comparable to leg extensions but with **greater range of motion** and **more rectus femoris involvement**.

## How to Perform Sissy Squats

### Assisted Version (Start Here)

**Setup:**
1. Stand next to a pole, rack, or sturdy object
2. Feet shoulder-width apart
3. Hold the support with one hand

**Execution:**
1. Rise onto the balls of your feet
2. Push knees forward while leaning torso back
3. Keep hips extended—don't sit back
4. Lower until deep knee bend (thighs past parallel to ground)
5. Drive through balls of feet to return
6. Squeeze quads hard at the top

### Key Technique Points

- **Hips stay forward:** This is NOT a squat—don't sit back
- **Lean back as you descend:** Torso and thighs form a diagonal line
- **Heels rise:** You'll be on balls of feet throughout
- **Control the negative:** Slow 2-3 second descent
- **Full lockout:** Squeeze quads at top

## Progressive Approach

### Level 1: Wall/Rack Assisted
- Two hands on support
- Focus on learning the movement pattern
- 3 sets of 8-12 reps

### Level 2: Single-Hand Assisted
- One hand on support
- More stability demand
- 3 sets of 10-15 reps

### Level 3: Bodyweight (No Assistance)
- Hands at chest or extended
- Requires good balance and strength
- 3 sets of 8-12 reps

### Level 4: Loaded Sissy Squats
- Hold dumbbell or plate at chest
- Advanced—significant quad strength required
- 3 sets of 8-12 reps

### Level 5: Sissy Squat Machine
- Purpose-built machine locks feet in place
- Allows heaviest loading
- 3 sets of 10-15 reps

## Common Mistakes

### Sitting Back (Hip Hinge)
- **Problem:** Turns it into a regular squat, reduces quad isolation
- **Fix:** Think "knees forward, not hips back"

### Not Rising on Toes
- **Problem:** Limits knee travel and range of motion
- **Fix:** Come up on balls of feet from the start

### Going Too Fast
- **Problem:** Uses momentum, reduces time under tension
- **Fix:** 2-3 seconds down, controlled up

### Excessive Forward Lean
- **Problem:** Torso bends forward instead of back
- **Fix:** Keep chest up, lean torso back as a unit

### Too Much Range Too Soon
- **Problem:** Going too deep before building strength
- **Fix:** Start with partial range, progress depth over time

## The Knee Question

"Are sissy squats bad for your knees?"

This is the most common question about the exercise. Here's the nuanced answer:

### For Healthy Knees:
- Sissy squats are generally safe and beneficial
- They strengthen quad muscles that support the knee
- Progressive loading builds tissue tolerance

### For Knee Issues:
- **Anterior knee pain:** May aggravate—proceed cautiously
- **Patellar tendinopathy:** Often contraindicated initially
- **ACL recovery:** Check with your PT
- **Meniscus issues:** Usually fine with controlled ROM

### The Bottom Line:
If you have healthy knees, sissy squats are not dangerous. If you have knee issues, consult a professional before adding them.

## Who Should Do Sissy Squats?

### Great For:
- Bodybuilders wanting quad development
- Athletes needing knee extension strength
- Anyone wanting to isolate quads without machines
- Home gym training without leg extension machine

### Use Caution If:
- Current patellar tendinopathy
- Anterior knee pain
- Post-knee surgery (until cleared)
- Balance limitations (use assistance)

### Probably Skip If:
- Active knee inflammation
- Recent ACL/PCL injury
- Unable to perform pain-free

## Programming Recommendations

### For Quad Hypertrophy
- 2-3x per week
- 3-4 sets of 10-15 reps
- Focus on slow eccentrics and squeeze at top

### As a Pre-Exhaust
- Before compound leg exercises
- 2 sets of 15-20 reps
- Makes squats/leg press feel much harder

### As a Finisher
- End of leg workout
- 2-3 sets to failure
- Chase the burn

### For General Strength
- 1-2x per week
- 3 sets of 8-12 reps
- Progress to loaded variations

## Equipment Options

### Bodyweight
- Just your body and a support to hold
- Can be done anywhere

### Sissy Squat Bench/Machine
- Locks feet and provides back pad
- Allows loading and eliminates balance demands
- Found in many gyms

### DIY Options
- Smith machine (heels elevated on pad)
- Cable machine behind you
- Resistance band assistance

### Loading Options
- Dumbbell or plate at chest
- Weight vest
- Barbell in front rack (advanced)

## Complementary Exercises

Sissy squats pair well with:

**For Complete Quad Development:**
- Hack squats (heavier loading)
- Leg press (different angle)
- Leg extensions (isolation)

**For Balance:**
- Romanian deadlifts (hamstrings)
- Leg curls (hamstrings)
- Hip thrusts (glutes)

## Key Takeaways

1. **Sissy squats are the ultimate quad isolation exercise** without machines
2. **All four quad muscles** work at maximum due to unique mechanics
3. **Hip extension + knee flexion** = rectus femoris works extra hard
4. **Progress from assisted to loaded** over time
5. **Safe for healthy knees** but use caution with existing issues
6. **Great for home gyms** since they need no equipment

## The Bottom Line

Sissy squats are a challenging, effective, and underutilized quad exercise. The unique combination of hip extension with deep knee flexion creates a quad isolation stimulus that few other exercises can match.

Start with assisted variations, progress gradually, and you'll build quad strength and size that carries over to all your lower body training.

Don't let the name intimidate you—master the sissy squat and your quads will grow.
