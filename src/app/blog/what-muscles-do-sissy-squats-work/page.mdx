## The Sissy Squat: Old-School Quad Builder

Despite its misleading name, the sissy squat is one of the most challenging quad exercises you can perform. This bodyweight movement—where you lean back while keeping knees forward—creates intense quadriceps isolation that machines struggle to match. The name allegedly comes from King Sisyphus of Greek mythology, not any implication about difficulty.

## Primary Muscles Targeted

### Quadriceps (Maximum Isolation)

Sissy squats are primarily a quad exercise, and the isolation is exceptional:

- **Rectus femoris**: Maximally stretched and loaded (this is the key muscle)
- **Vastus lateralis**: High activation throughout
- **Vastus medialis**: Strong contraction at the top
- **Vastus intermedius**: Works throughout the movement

The rectus femoris—the only quad muscle that crosses both the hip and knee—gets an extreme stretch when you lean back while bending the knees. This is what makes sissy squats unique.

### Hip Flexors (Stretched Under Load)

Your hip flexors work isometrically while being lengthened:

- **Iliopsoas**: Stretched throughout the movement
- **Tensor fasciae latae**: Stabilization role

This stretch-under-load makes sissy squats excellent for hip flexor flexibility too.

## Secondary Muscles Engaged

### Core Musculature

Your core works to maintain the backward-leaning position:

- **Rectus abdominis**: Prevents excessive extension
- **Transverse abdominis**: Deep stabilization
- **Obliques**: Lateral stability

### Calves (Isometric)

Your calves stabilize as you rise onto your toes:

- **Gastrocnemius**: Maintains heel-raised position
- **Soleus**: Ankle stability

### Tibialis Anterior

The front of your shin works harder than you'd expect:

- Maintains ankle position
- Balances the calf activation

## Why Sissy Squats Are So Effective

### Rectus Femoris Emphasis

Most quad exercises—squats, leg press, leg extensions—don't fully challenge the rectus femoris because:

- In squats, hip flexion shortens it at one end while knee flexion lengthens the other
- The muscle can't develop full tension

Sissy squats extend the hip while flexing the knee, creating maximum stretch and tension on the rectus femoris. This is why they produce such intense quad burn.

### No Equipment Needed

You can perform sissy squats anywhere with just a sturdy object to hold for balance. This makes them perfect for:

- Home workouts
- Hotel rooms
- Outdoor training
- Gym finishers

### Time Under Tension

The controlled nature of the movement creates exceptional time under tension—a key driver of muscle growth.

## How to Perform Sissy Squats Correctly

### Basic Setup

1. Stand next to a sturdy object (pole, rack, door frame)
2. Hold it lightly for balance (not to pull yourself up)
3. Feet hip-width apart, rise onto balls of feet
4. Keep your body in a straight line from knees to shoulders

### The Movement

1. Bend your knees and lean your torso backward
2. Keep hips extended—don't sit back like a regular squat
3. Lower until you feel an intense quad stretch
4. Your knees will travel far forward (this is correct)
5. Push through the balls of your feet to return
6. Squeeze quads hard at the top

### Key Form Points

- **Straight line**: Knees, hips, and shoulders should stay aligned
- **Heels up**: Stay on balls of feet throughout
- **No hip break**: Don't sit back—lean back
- **Control**: Slow descent, controlled ascent
- **Light grip**: Balance only, don't pull yourself up

## Common Form Errors

- **Breaking at the hips**: This turns it into a partial squat
- **Using arms to pull up**: Defeats the purpose
- **Going too deep too soon**: Build range gradually
- **Flat feet**: Must stay on balls of feet
- **Rushing**: Slow tempo is essential

## Knee Safety Considerations

Sissy squats place significant stress on the knee joint. To perform them safely:

### Build Up Gradually

1. Start with partial range of motion
2. Increase depth over weeks
3. Listen to your knees—stop if there's pain

### Prerequisites

Before attempting sissy squats, you should be able to:

- Perform 20+ bodyweight squats comfortably
- Have no current knee pain or injuries
- Demonstrate good quad and hip flexor flexibility

### Who Should Avoid Them

- Anyone with current knee injuries
- Those with patellar tendinopathy
- People with significant knee arthritis
- Anyone recovering from knee surgery (until cleared)

## Programming Sissy Squats

### For Quad Development

- 12-20 reps per set
- 3-4 sets
- Slow tempo (3 seconds down, 2 seconds up)
- Use as a finisher after compound leg work

### Progression Path

**Level 1: Assisted**
- Two-hand support, partial range

**Level 2: Light Assist**
- One-hand support, increased range

**Level 3: Bodyweight**
- Fingertip balance only, full range

**Level 4: Weighted**
- Hold dumbbell or plate at chest
- Or use a sissy squat bench

### Workout Integration

**After squats:**
- Great finisher for quad pump
- 3 x 15 after main leg work

**Standalone quad focus:**
- Pair with leg curls
- 4 x 12-15

## Sissy Squat Variations

### Sissy Squat Bench

Dedicated benches lock your feet in place, allowing:

- Greater depth
- Weighted versions
- Better stability

### Banded Sissy Squats

Loop a band around a post and your waist:

- Assists the bottom position
- Helps learn the movement

### Weighted Sissy Squats

Once bodyweight is easy:

- Hold a plate at your chest
- Use a weight vest
- Hold a dumbbell

### Smith Machine Sissy Squats

Use the Smith machine bar for balance:

- Set bar at hip height
- Hold lightly while performing the movement

4. Sissy Squats: 3 x 15-20

## The Bottom Line

Sissy squats primarily target your quadriceps with exceptional emphasis on the rectus femoris—the quad muscle that most exercises underwork. They require no equipment and create intense time under tension for muscle growth.

However, they do place significant stress on the knee joint. Build up gradually, ensure you have no existing knee issues, and prioritize form over depth. When performed correctly and progressively, sissy squats are one of the most effective bodyweight quad exercises available.
