# Rowing Exercises: Master the Erg and Build Total Body Fitness

Rowing is the ultimate full-body cardio exercise—working 86% of your muscles while being low-impact on joints. But most people row incorrectly, turning a full-body exercise into an arm and back exercise while setting themselves up for injury.

**Key insight:** Rowing is 60% legs, 20% core, and 20% arms. If your arms are burning first, you're doing it wrong.

---

## Rowing Machine Technique

### The Four Phases

#### 1. The Catch

Starting position at the front of the slide.

**Position:**
- Shins vertical (or close to it)
- Arms straight, reaching forward
- Shoulders in front of hips (slight forward lean)
- Core engaged, back flat (not rounded)
- Heels may lift slightly

**Key:** Don't over-compress. Stop when shins are vertical.

#### 2. The Drive

The power phase—pulling the handle.

**Sequence:** Legs → Core → Arms (in that order!)

**How to do it:**
1. Push through legs first (arms stay straight!)
2. Once legs are nearly extended, lean back slightly (core)
3. Finally, pull arms to lower ribs
4. Legs, back swing, and arm pull flow together—but legs always initiate

**Key:** This is the most common mistake. Don't pull with arms until legs are nearly finished.

#### 3. The Finish

Fully extended position.

**Position:**
- Legs straight but not locked
- Slight lean back (about 1 o'clock)
- Handle at lower ribs/upper abdomen
- Elbows past body, close to sides
- Shoulders down and back

#### 4. The Recovery

Returning to the catch (the "rest" phase).

**Sequence:** Arms → Core → Legs (reverse of drive!)

**How to do it:**
1. Extend arms forward first
2. Rock body forward from hips
3. Bend knees, slide forward once hands pass them
4. Control the return—don't rush

**Key:** Recovery should take longer than the drive (about 2:1 ratio)

## Common Rowing Mistakes

### Technique Errors

| Mistake | Problem | Fix |
|---------|---------|-----|
| Arms pulling first | Loses leg power, strains back | Keep arms straight until legs are 80% extended |
| Pulling too high | Strains shoulders | Handle to lower ribs, not chest |
| Rushing the recovery | No rest, poor positioning | 2:1 recovery to drive ratio |
| Over-compressing | Loses power at the catch | Stop when shins are vertical |
| Hunching back | Strains lower back, loses power | Maintain flat back, core engaged |
| Death grip | Wastes energy, causes forearm pain | Loose grip, hook with fingers |
| Looking down | Rounds spine | Eyes forward, neutral neck |

### Metric Misconceptions

- **High stroke rate ≠ faster:** Power per stroke matters more
- **Pulling harder ≠ better:** Leg drive creates speed
- **Lower splits are built gradually:** Don't sacrifice form for numbers

## Rowing Workouts

### Beginner Workouts

**Technique Focus (Week 1-2):**
- 4 x 5 minutes easy rowing
- Focus on proper sequence
- Rest 2 minutes between pieces
- Rate: 18-22 strokes per minute

**Building Endurance (Week 3-4):**
- 20 minutes steady state
- Conversational pace
- Rate: 20-24 spm
- Consistent split times

### Interval Workouts

**Workout 1: 500m Intervals**
- 4-6 x 500m
- Rest 2 minutes between
- Target consistent pace across all pieces

**Workout 2: Tabata Rows**
- 8 rounds: 20 seconds hard, 10 seconds easy
- Total: 4 minutes
- Maximum effort during work intervals

**Workout 3: Pyramid**
- 1 min hard / 1 min easy
- 2 min hard / 2 min easy
- 3 min hard / 3 min easy
- 2 min hard / 2 min easy
- 1 min hard / 1 min easy

### Endurance Workouts

**Steady State:**
- 30-60 minutes at conversational pace
- Rate: 18-22 spm
- Consistent split throughout

**Progressive Piece:**
- 20 minutes total
- Every 5 minutes, increase pace slightly
- Finish faster than you started

### Test Pieces

**2,000m Test:**
The gold standard rowing benchmark.

**Strategy:**
- First 500m: Establish pace (not too fast)
- Middle 1,000m: Settle into rhythm
- Final 500m: Increase rate and push
- Sprint final 100-200m

**Pacing:** Aim for even or slightly negative splits (getting faster)

## Complementary Strength Exercises

These exercises build the muscles that power your rowing stroke.

### Leg Drive Exercises

#### Leg Press

**Why it helps:** Mimics the leg drive portion of the stroke.

**How to do it:**
1. Feet shoulder-width on platform
2. Lower until knees at 90°
3. Drive through whole foot
4. Don't lock knees at top

**Sets/Reps:** 3x12-15

#### Romanian Deadlift

**Why it helps:** Builds hip hinge power for the body swing.

**How to do it:**
1. Hold barbell or dumbbells
2. Push hips back, lower weights along legs
3. Keep slight knee bend, back flat
4. Drive hips forward to stand

**Sets/Reps:** 3x10-12

#### Single-Leg Deadlift

**Why it helps:** Addresses imbalances between legs.

**How to do it:**
1. Stand on one leg
2. Hinge, reaching toward ground
3. Keep back flat
4. Return to standing

**Sets/Reps:** 3x8-10 each leg

### Core Exercises

#### Plank

**How to do it:**
1. Forearms on ground
2. Body in straight line
3. Hold 30-60 seconds
4. 3 sets

#### Dead Bug

**Why it helps:** Trains core to stabilize while limbs move.

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back, arms up, knees at 90°
2. Press lower back into floor
3. Lower opposite arm and leg
4. 3x10 each side

#### Pallof Press

**Why it helps:** Builds rotational stability for the stroke.

**How to do it:**
1. Stand perpendicular to cable/band
2. Press arms straight out
3. Resist rotation
4. 3x10 each side

#### Seated Good Morning

**Why it helps:** Mimics the body swing portion.

**How to do it:**
1. Sit on bench, bar on back
2. Hinge forward from hips
3. Keep back flat
4. Return to upright

**Sets/Reps:** 3x12

### Pulling Exercises

#### Bent-Over Row

**Why it helps:** Builds the upper back strength for the arm pull.

**How to do it:**
1. Hinge at hips, back flat
2. Pull weight toward lower ribs
3. Squeeze shoulder blades
4. Lower with control

**Sets/Reps:** 3x10-12

#### Single-Arm Dumbbell Row

**How to do it:**
1. One hand and knee on bench
2. Row dumbbell to hip
3. Keep elbow close to body
4. 3x10-12 each side

#### Lat Pulldown

**How to do it:**
1. Grip bar slightly wider than shoulders
2. Pull to upper chest
3. Control the return
4. 3x12

#### Face Pulls

**Why it helps:** Balances the forward-pulling motion.

**How to do it:**
1. Cable at face height
2. Pull toward face, separating hands
3. Squeeze shoulder blades
4. 3x15

## Mobility for Rowers

### Hip Flexor Stretch

Tight hip flexors limit the catch position.

**How to do it:**
1. Half-kneeling position
2. Tuck pelvis under
3. Shift hips forward
4. Hold 30-45 seconds each side

### Hamstring Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Foot on elevated surface
2. Hinge forward, keeping back flat
3. Hold 30 seconds each side

### Thoracic Rotation

**How to do it:**
1. On all fours
2. Hand behind head
3. Rotate elbow toward ground, then toward ceiling
4. 10 each side

### Hip 90/90 Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Sit with front leg at 90°, back leg at 90°
2. Lean forward over front shin
3. Hold 45 seconds each side

### Lat Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Kneel facing wall or pole
2. Arms extended, hands on surface
3. Push chest toward floor
4. Hold 30-45 seconds

## Rowing for Different Goals

### Weight Loss

**Focus:** Longer, moderate-intensity sessions

**Prescription:**
- 30-45 min steady state, 3-4x/week
- Add 1-2 interval sessions
- Total: 150+ minutes per week

### Cardiovascular Fitness

**Focus:** Mix of steady state and intervals

**Prescription:**
- 2 steady state sessions (30-45 min)
- 2 interval sessions
- Progressively increase duration and intensity

### CrossFit/Functional Fitness

**Focus:** Short, high-intensity pieces

**Prescription:**
- Practice technique at moderate pace
- Train 500m-2,000m test pieces
- Include rowing in mixed-modal workouts

### Competitive Rowing

**Focus:** Sport-specific preparation

**Prescription:**
- Periodized training with volume phases
- 2K-specific intervals
- Strength work targeting rowing muscles
- Technical work with coach feedback

## Common Questions

### How hard should I grip?

Loosely. Think of your hands as hooks. A death grip wastes energy and causes forearm fatigue.

### What damper setting should I use?

**Not 10!** Start at 3-5. The damper controls how much air flows in—higher isn't "harder." Find what feels like rowing through water.

### Why does my back hurt?

Usually technique: rounding at the catch, pulling with back before legs, or over-reaching. Video yourself and compare to proper form.

### How do I get a better 2K time?

Build your aerobic base with steady state work, then add race-pace intervals. Technique improvements often yield more than fitness gains.

## The Bottom Line

Rowing is an exceptional full-body exercise that's accessible to nearly everyone. The key is learning proper technique first—legs, then core, then arms—and being patient with progression.

**Priorities:**
1. Master the four-phase technique
2. Build base fitness with steady state work
3. Add intervals gradually
4. Include complementary strength work
5. Don't neglect mobility

The rowing machine rewards efficiency. Row smart, and every stroke counts.
