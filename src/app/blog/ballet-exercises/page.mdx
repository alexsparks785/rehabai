# Ballet Exercises: Build Strength and Grace for Dancers

Ballet demands extreme ranges of motion, precise muscle control, and the strength to make difficult movements appear effortless. Supplementary training builds the physical foundation that allows artistry to flourish while protecting against the injuries that end careers.

**Key insight:** Ballet technique develops specific muscles while often neglecting their antagonists. Balanced strength training prevents the imbalances that lead to injury.

---

## Physical Demands of Ballet

### What Ballet Requires

- **Hip external rotation (turnout):** Fundamental to classical technique
- **Ankle and foot strength:** Relevé, pointe work, jumps
- **Core stability:** Control through all positions
- **Hip flexor strength:** Développé, grand battement
- **Single-leg balance:** Much of ballet is on one leg
- **Jumping power:** Allegro, grand allegro

### Common Ballet Injuries

| Injury | Cause | Prevention Focus |
|--------|-------|------------------|
| Ankle sprains | Relevé, landing | Ankle stability, proprioception |
| Stress fractures | Overuse, pointe work | Load management, bone health |
| Hip impingement | Forced turnout | Hip strength, proper alignment |
| Knee pain | Turnout compensation | Glute strength, alignment |
| Lower back pain | Extension, arabesque | Core strength, hip flexor flexibility |
| Achilles tendinitis | Relevé, jumping | Calf strength and flexibility |

## Turnout Strengthening

True turnout comes from hip external rotation, not forcing the feet.

### Side-Lying Clamshells

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on side, knees bent 45°
2. Keep feet together, lift top knee
3. Don't let hips roll back
4. 3 sets of 20 each side

### Seated External Rotation

**How to do it:**
1. Sit with band around feet
2. Knees bent, feet together
3. Rotate thighs out against resistance
4. 3 sets of 15

### Prone Frog

**How to do it:**
1. Lie face down, knees bent, feet together
2. Squeeze heels together
3. Lift knees slightly off floor
4. Hold 10 seconds, repeat 10 times

### Standing Turnout with Band

**How to do it:**
1. Band around ankles
2. Stand in first position
3. Open to second position against resistance
4. Return with control
5. 3 sets of 12

### Fire Hydrants

**How to do it:**
1. On all fours
2. Lift knee out to side, keeping 90° bend
3. Focus on hip rotation
4. 3 sets of 15 each side

## Foot and Ankle Strength

Critical for pointe work and injury prevention.

### Relevé Holds

**How to do it:**
1. Rise to relevé in parallel or first position
2. Hold 30 seconds
3. Lower with control
4. 3 sets

### Single-Leg Relevé

**How to do it:**
1. Stand on one leg
2. Rise to relevé
3. Lower slowly (4 counts)
4. 3 sets of 12 each leg

### Theraband Foot Exercises

**Pointing:**
1. Band around ball of foot
2. Point against resistance
3. 3 sets of 20 each foot

**Winging:**
1. Band around outside of foot
2. Turn foot inward (sickle direction, but controlled)
3. 3 sets of 15 each foot

### Doming (Intrinsic Foot Strength)

**How to do it:**
1. Stand or sit, feet flat
2. Try to lift arch without curling toes
3. Hold 5 seconds
4. 10-15 reps each foot

### Towel Scrunches

**How to do it:**
1. Place towel on floor
2. Scrunch toward you using toes
3. 3 sets, full towel length

### Marble Pickups

**How to do it:**
1. Pick up marbles with toes
2. Transfer to container
3. 20 marbles each foot

## Core Stability for Dancers

### Plank

**How to do it:**
1. Forearms on floor
2. Body in straight line
3. Hold 30-60 seconds
4. 3 sets

### Side Plank with Leg Lift

**How to do it:**
1. Side plank position
2. Lift top leg toward ceiling
3. Hold 20 seconds each side
4. 3 sets

### Dead Bug

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back, arms up, knees at 90°
2. Lower opposite arm and leg
3. Keep lower back pressed down
4. 3 sets of 12 each side

### Bird Dog

**How to do it:**
1. On all fours
2. Extend opposite arm and leg
3. Hold 3 seconds
4. 3 sets of 10 each side

### Pilates Hundred (Modified)

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back, legs in tabletop
2. Curl head and shoulders up
3. Pump arms for 100 counts
4. Breathe: inhale 5, exhale 5

## Hip Flexor Strength

For développé, grand battement, and extensions.

### Leg Lifts (Supine)

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back
2. Lift one leg toward ceiling (keep it straight)
3. Lower with control
4. 3 sets of 15 each leg

### Seated Leg Lifts

**How to do it:**
1. Sit tall on floor, legs extended
2. Lift one leg keeping it straight
3. Hold 5 seconds
4. 3 sets of 10 each leg

### Standing Développé with Resistance

**How to do it:**
1. Band around ankle, other end anchored low
2. Slowly développé against resistance
3. Control the descent
4. 2 sets of 8 each direction

### Flutter Kicks

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back, hands under hips
2. Lift legs slightly
3. Alternate small kicks
4. 30-60 seconds, 3 sets

## Jumping and Allegro Training

### Calf Raises (Full Range)

**How to do it:**
1. Stand on step, heels hanging off
2. Lower heels below step level
3. Rise to full relevé
4. 3 sets of 15

### Squat Jumps

**How to do it:**
1. Plié in second position
2. Jump straight up
3. Land softly through plié
4. 3 sets of 10

### Single-Leg Hops

**How to do it:**
1. Small hops on one leg
2. Focus on quiet, soft landings
3. 20 hops each leg, 3 sets

### Échappé Jumps

**How to do it:**
1. Start in fifth position
2. Jump to second, land softly
3. Jump back to fifth
4. Focus on turnout and alignment
5. 3 sets of 8

### Box Jumps (Low)

**How to do it:**
1. Jump onto low box
2. Land softly in plié
3. Step down
4. 3 sets of 8

## Flexibility and Mobility

### Hip Flexor Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Half-kneeling position
2. Tuck pelvis, shift forward
3. Hold 30-45 seconds each side

### Frog Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. On all fours, widen knees
2. Turn feet out
3. Rock hips back
4. Hold 60-90 seconds

### Straddle Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Sit with legs wide
2. Walk hands forward
3. Keep back flat
4. Hold 45-60 seconds

### Splits Progression

**Front Splits:**
1. Low lunge, slide back leg back
2. Support with hands
3. Progress gradually over weeks

**Side Splits:**
1. Wide straddle, walk hands forward
2. Support weight as you lower
3. Never force—progress slowly

### Hamstring Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Sit with one leg extended
2. Reach toward foot, back flat
3. Hold 30-45 seconds each leg

## Balance Training

### Single-Leg Balance

**How to do it:**
1. Stand on one leg in parallel
2. Progress to turned out
3. Hold 30-60 seconds each
4. Add arm movements

### Relevé Balance

**How to do it:**
1. Rise to relevé
2. Close eyes
3. Hold as long as possible
4. 3-5 attempts each leg

### Passé Balance Progressions

**How to do it:**
1. Stand in passé (retiré)
2. Hold 30 seconds
3. Rise to relevé in passé
4. Add port de bras

### BOSU Work

**How to do it:**
1. Stand on BOSU in various positions
2. Practice relevé on BOSU
3. Add arm movements for challenge

## Sample Training Programs

### During Intensive Training Periods

**2x per week, 20-25 minutes:**

Focus on injury prevention and balance.

**Session:**
- Clamshells: 2x15 each
- Single-leg relevé: 2x10 each
- Theraband foot work: 5 min
- Plank: 2x30 sec
- Hip flexor stretch: 30 sec each
- Ankle balance: 5 min

### Off-Season Building

**3x per week, 35-40 minutes:**

**Day 1 - Lower Body:**
- Clamshells: 3x20 each
- Fire hydrants: 3x15 each
- Single-leg squats to box: 3x8 each
- Calf raises: 3x15
- Theraband foot series: 10 min

**Day 2 - Core + Balance:**
- Plank: 3x45 sec
- Side plank with leg lift: 3x20 sec each
- Dead bug: 3x12 each
- Pilates Hundred
- Balance progressions: 10 min

**Day 3 - Jumps + Flexibility:**
- Squat jumps: 3x10
- Single-leg hops: 3x20 each
- Échappé jumps: 3x8
- Full flexibility routine: 15 min

## Pointe Work Preparation

### Minimum Requirements

Before beginning pointe work:
- Full relevé held 30+ seconds
- Single-leg relevé with control
- Strong intrinsic foot muscles
- Adequate ankle flexibility
- Teacher approval

### Strengthening for Pointe

**Relevé Progressions:**
1. Two-leg relevé, long holds
2. Single-leg relevé with support
3. Single-leg relevé, no support
4. Échappé relevé

**Foot Strengthening:**
- Doming daily
- Theraband work daily
- Marble pickups
- Towel scrunches

## Injury Prevention Tips

### Training Smart

- **Warm up properly:** Never dance cold
- **Don't force turnout:** Work from the hip, not the knee
- **Build gradually:** Especially for pointe and jumps
- **Rest adequately:** Growth happens during recovery
- **Cross-train:** Balance dance with conditioning

### Warning Signs

Stop and assess if you experience:
- Sharp pain in foot/ankle
- Hip pain with turnout
- Knee pain during plié
- Persistent soreness that doesn't resolve

### When to See a Professional

- Pain lasting more than a few days
- Swelling that persists
- Decreased range of motion
- Pain that changes your technique

## The Bottom Line

Ballet demands extraordinary physicality wrapped in artistry. Supplementary training builds the strength and resilience that allows dancers to pursue their art without breaking down.

**Priority areas:**
1. Hip external rotator strength (turnout from the right muscles)
2. Foot and ankle strength (especially for pointe)
3. Core stability (the center of all movement)
4. Single-leg balance and strength
5. Hip flexor strength (extensions and battements)

Train the body to support the art.
