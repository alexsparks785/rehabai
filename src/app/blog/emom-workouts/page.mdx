# EMOM Workouts: Every Minute on the Minute Training Guide

Looking for a workout format that's time-efficient, challenging, and endlessly adaptable? EMOM—Every Minute on the Minute—might be your new favorite training method. Whether you have 10 minutes or 30, want to build strength or conditioning, EMOMs deliver results with built-in structure and accountability.

## What is an EMOM Workout?

EMOM stands for "Every Minute on the Minute." At the start of each minute, you perform a prescribed number of reps of an exercise. Whatever time remains in that minute is your rest. When the next minute starts, you go again.

### The Basic Structure

**Example: 10-Minute EMOM - 10 Kettlebell Swings**

- 0:00 - Do 10 kettlebell swings (takes ~20 seconds)
- 0:20-1:00 - Rest (~40 seconds)
- 1:00 - Do 10 kettlebell swings
- 1:20-2:00 - Rest
- Continue for 10 minutes (10 rounds total)

**The Catch:** If the exercise takes longer, you get less rest. If you can't finish before the next minute starts, you're behind—and the workout gets even harder.

### Why EMOMs Work

- **Built-in pacing:** The clock keeps you honest
- **Self-regulating rest:** Faster work = more rest
- **Time-efficient:** Know exactly when you'll finish
- **Progressive overload built in:** Add reps or weight over time
- **Versatile:** Works for strength, conditioning, or skill work

## Types of EMOM Workouts

### Single Exercise EMOM

Focus on one movement the entire time:

**Example: 12-Minute Push-Up EMOM**
- Every minute: 10 push-ups
- Total: 120 push-ups in 12 minutes

**Best For:**
- Skill practice
- Building volume on one movement
- Simple, focused sessions

### Alternating EMOM

Switch between two exercises:

**Example: 10-Minute Alternating EMOM**
- Odd minutes (1, 3, 5, 7, 9): 12 goblet squats
- Even minutes (2, 4, 6, 8, 10): 10 push-ups

**Best For:**
- Balancing push/pull or upper/lower
- More variety
- Allowing recovery between similar movements

### Rotating EMOM

Cycle through three or more exercises:

**Example: 12-Minute Rotating EMOM**
- Minute 1: 15 kettlebell swings
- Minute 2: 10 burpees
- Minute 3: 12 dumbbell rows (each arm)
- Minute 4: 15 kettlebell swings
- Continue pattern...

**Best For:**
- Full-body conditioning
- Maintaining intensity with variety
- Longer EMOMs (15-30 minutes)

### Mixed Modal EMOM

Combine strength and conditioning:

**Example: 20-Minute Mixed EMOM**
- Minutes 1-5: 5 heavy deadlifts
- Minutes 6-10: 12 box jumps
- Minutes 11-15: 8 dumbbell presses
- Minutes 16-20: 15 calories on rower

**Best For:**
- Well-rounded fitness
- Competition-style training
- Advanced athletes

## Programming EMOM Workouts

### Choosing Rep Counts

The key is leaving enough rest to recover while still challenging yourself:

| Work Time | Rest Time | Difficulty |
|-----------|-----------|------------|
| 20-25 sec | 35-40 sec | Easy/Recovery |
| 30-35 sec | 25-30 sec | Moderate |
| 40-45 sec | 15-20 sec | Challenging |
| 50+ sec | <10 sec | Very hard |

**Rule of Thumb:** Start with reps that take about 30 seconds, leaving 30 seconds rest. Adjust from there.

### Progression Strategies

**Add Reps:**
- Week 1: 8 reps per minute
- Week 2: 9 reps per minute
- Week 3: 10 reps per minute
- Week 4: Deload, then add weight

**Add Time:**
- Week 1: 10-minute EMOM
- Week 2: 12-minute EMOM
- Week 3: 15-minute EMOM
- Week 4: Back to 10 minutes, more reps

**Add Weight:**
- Week 1: 10 KB swings @ 35 lbs
- Week 2: 10 KB swings @ 40 lbs
- Week 3: 10 KB swings @ 45 lbs
- Week 4: Deload

### Choosing EMOM Duration

| Duration | Best For |
|----------|----------|
| 6-10 min | Finishers, skill work, beginners |
| 12-16 min | Standard conditioning sessions |
| 20-30 min | Longer endurance work, full workouts |
| 30+ min | Advanced, competition prep |

## EMOM Workouts for Different Goals

### For Conditioning

**20-Minute Cardio EMOM:**
- Minute 1: 15 burpees
- Minute 2: 20 mountain climbers
- Minute 3: 12 jumping lunges
- Minute 4: 10 tuck jumps
- Repeat 5 rounds

**Why It Works:** Sustained heart rate elevation with just enough recovery to maintain quality.

### For Strength

**16-Minute Strength EMOM:**
- Odd minutes: 3 heavy back squats (75-80%)
- Even minutes: 5 weighted pull-ups

**Why It Works:** Low reps allow heavy loads. Built-in rest prevents form breakdown.

### For Fat Loss

**15-Minute Metabolic EMOM:**
- Minute 1: 10 dumbbell thrusters
- Minute 2: 15 kettlebell swings
- Minute 3: 12 box jumps

**Why It Works:** High work output, full-body movements, sustained intensity.

### For Skill Practice

**10-Minute Skill EMOM:**
- Every minute: 3 muscle-ups (or attempts)

**Why It Works:** Fresh attempts every minute, accumulated practice without fatigue-induced bad reps.

### For Active Recovery

**12-Minute Recovery EMOM:**
- Minute 1: 30-second plank
- Minute 2: 10 bird dogs (5 each side)
- Minute 3: 8 cat-cow stretches

**Why It Works:** Light movement with ample rest. Promotes blood flow without stress.

## Sample EMOM Workouts

### Beginner: Bodyweight Basics

**12-Minute EMOM (Alternating):**
- Odd minutes: 8 air squats
- Even minutes: 5 push-ups (modify as needed)

**Notes:**
- Should have 30+ seconds rest each minute
- Focus on perfect form
- Scale reps down if needed

### Intermediate: Dumbbell Circuit

**16-Minute EMOM (Rotating):**
- Minute 1: 10 dumbbell goblet squats
- Minute 2: 10 dumbbell push presses
- Minute 3: 10 dumbbell bent-over rows
- Minute 4: 30-second plank
- Repeat 4 rounds

**Notes:**
- Use moderate weight (can do 15+ reps fresh)
- All movements flow with same dumbbells
- Plank minute = active recovery

### Advanced: Barbell Complex

**20-Minute EMOM (Rotating):**
- Minute 1: 5 power cleans (135/95)
- Minute 2: 5 front squats
- Minute 3: 5 push press
- Minute 4: 5 back squats
- Repeat 5 rounds

**Notes:**
- Use weight you can move well when fatigued
- All movements use same barbell
- Requires solid technique

### Finisher: Quick Burn

**6-Minute EMOM:**
- Every minute: 10 burpees

**Notes:**
- Simple but devastating
- 60 burpees in 6 minutes
- Great workout finisher

### Full-Body: 30-Minute Session

**30-Minute EMOM:**
- Minutes 1-10: Alternating
  - Odd: 12 kettlebell swings
  - Even: 8 goblet squats
- Minutes 11-20: Alternating
  - Odd: 10 push-ups
  - Even: 8 dumbbell rows (each arm)
- Minutes 21-30: Alternating
  - Odd: 15 jumping jacks + 5 burpees
  - Even: 30-second hollow hold

**Notes:**
- Complete workout in one format
- Built-in periodization (changes every 10 minutes)
- Mix of strength and conditioning

## EMOM Training Tips

### Start Conservative

Your first EMOM with any exercise should feel easy. Better to have 35 seconds rest than to fall behind by minute 4. You can always add reps next time.

### Watch the Clock

Position yourself where you can clearly see a clock or timer. Phone timers with audio cues work great. You need to know exactly when each minute starts.

### Don't Chase the Clock

If you fall behind (exercise takes longer than a minute), either:
1. Skip a round to get back on track
2. Reduce reps for remaining rounds
3. Accept you'll finish late

Don't sacrifice form to catch up—that's how injuries happen.

### Breathe Intentionally

Use rest time wisely:
- First 10-15 seconds: Recover breathing
- Middle 10-15 seconds: Controlled breaths
- Last 5-10 seconds: Prepare for next round

### Know When to Scale

If you're consistently getting less than 15 seconds rest, the workout is too hard. Scale by:
- Reducing reps
- Reducing weight
- Choosing easier exercise variation
- Shortening total time

### Track Your Workouts

EMOMs are perfect for tracking progress. Record:
- Exercise(s)
- Reps
- Weight
- Total time
- How much rest you had (roughly)

## EMOM Variations

### EMOM + Cap

Set a time cap for completing work. If you finish early, rest.

**Example:** 
- Every minute: 10 calories on rower
- Time cap: 45 seconds
- If you hit 10 cal in 35 seconds, rest 25 seconds
- If you hit 10 cal in 50 seconds, you're over cap (scale down)

### Death by EMOM

Start with low reps, add one rep each minute until failure:

**Death by Burpees:**
- Minute 1: 1 burpee
- Minute 2: 2 burpees
- Minute 3: 3 burpees
- Continue until you can't complete reps in the minute

**Note:** This gets hard fast. Round 15 = 15 burpees in 60 seconds with only seconds to spare.

### EMOM to AMRAP Finisher

EMOM with an AMRAP (As Many Reps As Possible) at the end:

**15-Minute Session:**
- Minutes 1-12: EMOM (10 KB swings per minute)
- Minutes 13-15: AMRAP burpees

### Partner EMOM

You go, I go format:

**20-Minute Partner EMOM:**
- Partner A works on odd minutes
- Partner B works on even minutes
- Each does 12 dumbbell thrusters when it's their turn

## Common EMOM Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Too Many Reps

**The Problem:** No rest, form degrades, can't maintain pace
**The Fix:** Start with reps that give you 25-30 seconds rest

### Mistake 2: Wrong Exercise Selection

**The Problem:** Complex movements that require setup time
**The Fix:** Choose exercises you can start immediately when the minute begins

### Mistake 3: No Progression Plan

**The Problem:** Doing the same EMOM forever, no progress
**The Fix:** Add reps, weight, or time every 1-2 weeks

### Mistake 4: Ignoring Fatigue

**The Problem:** Pushing through when form breaks down
**The Fix:** Reduce reps or end early if quality suffers

### Mistake 5: Only Using EMOMs for Conditioning

**The Problem:** Missing the strength-building potential
**The Fix:** Program heavy, low-rep EMOMs for strength work

## Building an EMOM Program

### Sample Week

**Monday: Strength EMOM**
- 16 minutes, alternating heavy squats and presses

**Tuesday: Conditioning EMOM**
- 20 minutes, rotating through 4 exercises

**Wednesday: Rest or Active Recovery EMOM**
- 12 minutes, light mobility work

**Thursday: Skill EMOM**
- 10 minutes, practicing challenging movement

**Friday: Mixed EMOM**
- 24 minutes, strength and conditioning blocks

**Weekend: Rest or recreational activity**

## The Bottom Line

EMOM workouts are simple, effective, and endlessly adaptable. The built-in structure keeps you accountable, the clock pushes your pace, and the format works for any goal—strength, conditioning, skill practice, or active recovery.

Start simple: pick one exercise, set a timer for 10 minutes, and do 8-10 reps every minute. Notice how the workout self-regulates—work hard, get less rest; pace yourself, recover more. That's the beauty of EMOMs.

As you progress, experiment with alternating exercises, longer durations, heavier weights, and more complex combinations. The EMOM format will grow with you.

Set your timer. When it hits zero, get to work.
