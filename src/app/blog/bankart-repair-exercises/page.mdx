# Bankart Repair Exercises: Rehabilitation After Labrum Surgery

A Bankart repair fixes the torn labrum that results from shoulder dislocation, restoring stability to the joint. Proper rehabilitation is crucial for preventing future dislocations while regaining full shoulder function.

## Understanding Bankart Repair

### What Was Repaired
- **Labrum:** Ring of cartilage around the shoulder socket
- **Bankart lesion:** Tear at front-bottom of labrum
- Occurs when shoulder dislocates anteriorly (forward)
- Repair reattaches labrum to bone with anchors

### Associated Procedures
- Capsular tightening (plication)
- Remplissage (for Hill-Sachs lesion)
- Combined procedures for severe instability

### Success Factors
- Following rehabilitation protocol
- Avoiding re-injury during healing
- Building strength and stability

## The Healing Challenge

**Why rehabilitation is lengthy:**
- Labrum heals slowly
- Repair must become strong before stress
- Range of motion must be protected
- Too much too soon = failure

**Typical timeline:**
- Sling: 4-6 weeks
- Protected motion: 6-12 weeks
- Strengthening: 12+ weeks
- Return to sport: 4-6 months
- Contact sports: 6 months minimum

## Phase 1: Protection (Weeks 0-6)

### Sling Use
- Wear constantly (except for exercises)
- Usually 4-6 weeks
- Sleep in sling

### Precautions (Critical)
**AVOID these positions that stress the repair:**
- External rotation beyond prescribed limit
- Abduction + external rotation (apprehension position)
- Extension past neutral (arm behind body)
- Reaching behind back

### Early Motion (Within Limits)

**Pendulum Exercises**
1. Lean forward, support yourself
2. Let arm hang and swing gently
3. Small circles
4. 10 circles each direction
5. 3-4 times daily

**Passive Forward Elevation** (Supine)
1. Lie on back
2. Good arm lifts surgical arm
3. Usually limited to 90-120°
4. 10-15 repetitions

**Passive External Rotation** (Carefully Limited)
1. Elbow at side, bent 90°
2. Rotate outward gently
3. STOP at surgeon's prescribed limit (often 20-30°)
4. 10-15 repetitions

### Elbow, Wrist, Hand
- Full elbow motion
- Wrist exercises
- Grip strengthening
- Keep joints mobile

### Scapular Exercises

**Scapular Squeezes**
1. Shoulder blades together
2. Hold 5 seconds
3. 15-20 repetitions

## Phase 2: Early Motion (Weeks 6-12)

### Sling Discontinued
- Usually around week 6
- Still avoid risky positions
- Progress motion gradually

### Increasing Range of Motion

**Passive → Active-Assisted → Active**

**Pulley Exercises**
1. Good arm helps lift surgical arm
2. Progress range as allowed
3. 15-20 repetitions

**Wand Exercises**
1. Assisted forward elevation
2. Assisted external rotation (within limits)
3. 15-20 repetitions

**Active ROM** (When Cleared)
1. Forward flexion
2. Abduction
3. External rotation (carefully)
4. 15-20 repetitions each

### External Rotation Progression
- Start limited (30°)
- Progress gradually
- May take until week 10-12 for full range
- Don't force

### Continue Scapular Work
- Squeezes
- Shrugs
- Clocks

## Phase 3: Strengthening (Weeks 12-20)

**Begin when cleared by surgeon.**

### Rotator Cuff Strengthening

**Side-Lying External Rotation**
1. Lie on uninvolved side
2. Elbow at side
3. Light weight or no weight
4. Rotate toward ceiling
5. 2-3 sets of 15

**Standing Band External Rotation**
1. Band at elbow height
2. Elbow at side
3. Rotate outward
4. 2-3 sets of 15

**Standing Band Internal Rotation**
1. Rotate inward against band
2. Strengthens subscapularis
3. 2-3 sets of 15

### Scapular Stabilizers

**Prone Y-T-W**
1. Face down on bench
2. Y: Arms overhead, lift
3. T: Arms out to sides, lift
4. W: Elbows bent, squeeze and lift
5. 2-3 sets of 10 each

**Rows**
1. Light band or weight
2. Pull elbows back
3. Squeeze shoulder blades
4. 2-3 sets of 15

**Face Pulls**
1. Band at face height
2. Pull toward face
3. 2-3 sets of 15

### Important: Avoid Apprehension Position
- Don't stress external rotation at 90° abduction
- Progress this position very gradually
- Often the last to rehabilitate

## Phase 4: Dynamic Stability (Weeks 16-20)

### Rhythmic Stabilization

**Perturbation Training**
1. Arm supported
2. Partner provides gentle pushes
3. Resist without large movements
4. Multiple directions
5. 30-60 second sets

**Ball on Wall**
1. Hand on ball against wall
2. Make circles with ball
3. Challenges stability
4. 30-60 seconds

### Closed Chain Exercises

**Push-Up Progression**
1. Wall push-ups first
2. Progress angle gradually
3. Eventually floor (when ready)
4. 2-3 sets of 10-15

**Plank Holds**
1. Forearm plank
2. Progress to full plank
3. Challenge shoulder stability

### Advanced Rotator Cuff

**External Rotation at 90° Abduction** (Late)
1. Arm out to side, elbow bent
2. Rotate forearm up toward ceiling
3. This is the "apprehension position"
4. Progress very carefully
5. May be last to add

## Phase 5: Sport Preparation (Weeks 20+)

### Plyometric Introduction

**Medicine Ball Throws**
1. Two-handed initially
2. Light weight
3. Chest passes
4. Progress to overhead

**Ball Catches**
1. Two-handed
2. Progress to one-handed
3. Various positions

### Sport-Specific Training

**For Overhead Athletes (Throwing):**
1. Interval throwing program
2. Very gradual progression
3. Start at short distance, low intensity
4. Progress distance, then velocity
5. May take 6-9 months to full throwing

**For Contact Sports:**
1. Non-contact drills first
2. Gradual return to contact
3. Consider bracing initially
4. Usually 6 months minimum

### Return Criteria

**Before full return:**
- Full, pain-free range of motion
- Strength equal to other side
- No apprehension in any position
- Completed sport-specific progression
- Surgeon clearance

## Preventing Re-Dislocation

### Risk Factors for Failure
- Return to activity too soon
- Contact sports
- Young age
- History of multiple dislocations
- Non-compliance with rehab

### Prevention Strategies
- Complete full rehabilitation
- Maintain rotator cuff strength
- Continue exercises long-term
- Proper warm-up before activity
- Consider brace for high-risk sports

## Sample Weekly Schedule (Week 14)

### Day 1
1. Warm-up: ROM exercises, 10 min
2. Band ER/IR: 3×15 each
3. Side-lying ER: 2×15
4. Rows: 2×15
5. Prone Y-T-W: 2×10 each

### Day 2
1. ROM and stretching: 15 min
2. Perturbation training: 5 min
3. Scapular exercises

### Day 3: Rest or Light Activity

### Day 4
1. Warm-up: 10 min
2. All strengthening exercises
3. Wall push-ups: 2×15
4. Ball on wall stability: 3×30 sec

### Day 5
1. Full exercise program
2. Progress resistance if tolerated
3. Begin sport-specific preparation (if ready)

## Managing Concerns

### Stiffness
- Common, especially external rotation
- Maintain ROM exercises
- Physical therapy if needed

### Apprehension
- Feeling like shoulder will dislocate
- Should improve as strength builds
- Report if persistent

### Pain
- Some soreness normal
- Sharp pain or returning symptoms need attention
- Contact surgeon if concerned

## When to Contact Your Surgeon

- Sensation of instability
- Popping or shifting feeling
- Sudden increase in pain
- Loss of previously gained motion
- Any concern about the repair

## Key Takeaways

1. **Protect the repair** — avoid apprehension position early
2. **Progress external rotation carefully** — most critical motion
3. **Sling for 4-6 weeks** — don't skip this phase
4. **Strengthen rotator cuff** — primary dynamic stabilizer
5. **Scapular stability matters** — base for shoulder function
6. **Sport return takes 4-6 months** — minimum for most sports
7. **Contact sports need 6+ months** — don't rush
8. **Maintenance is ongoing** — continue exercises long-term

Bankart repair has excellent success rates when rehabilitation is followed properly. Be patient, respect the healing timeline, and you'll return to activities with a stable shoulder.
