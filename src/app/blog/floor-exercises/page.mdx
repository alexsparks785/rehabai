# Floor Exercises: Complete Workouts Without Standing Up

Floor exercises offer a stable, supportive surface for building strength, improving flexibility, and developing core stability. Whether you have balance concerns, an injury, or simply prefer training on the ground, these exercises provide a comprehensive workout without ever standing up.

## Why Floor Exercises?

### Benefits

- **Stability:** The floor provides full support, reducing fall risk
- **Core focus:** Many floor exercises naturally engage your core
- **Joint-friendly:** Lower impact than standing exercises
- **Accessible:** No equipment needed beyond a mat
- **Effective:** Can build significant strength and flexibility
- **Safe:** Ideal for rehabilitation and those with balance issues

### Who Benefits Most

- People with balance issues or vertigo
- Post-surgery rehabilitation
- Elderly individuals
- Those with lower limb injuries
- Anyone with chronic conditions affecting standing
- Beginners building foundational strength
- Athletes doing supplementary core work

## Supine Exercises (Lying on Your Back)

### Core Exercises

**Dead Bug**
1. Lie on back, arms toward ceiling, knees bent 90°
2. Press lower back into floor
3. Lower opposite arm and leg toward floor
4. Return and switch sides
5. Keep back flat throughout

*Reps: 10 per side, 3 sets*

**Bicycle Crunch**
1. Hands behind head, elbows wide
2. Lift shoulders off floor
3. Bring knee to opposite elbow
4. Extend other leg
5. Alternate in cycling motion

*Reps: 15 per side, 3 sets*

**Reverse Crunch**
1. Knees bent, feet off floor
2. Use abs to curl pelvis toward ribcage
3. Lift hips slightly off floor
4. Lower with control

*Reps: 15-20, 3 sets*

**Leg Lowers**
1. Lie flat, legs straight up toward ceiling
2. Lower legs slowly toward floor
3. Stop before back arches
4. Return to start

*Reps: 10-15, 3 sets*

**Heel Taps**
1. Lie flat, knees bent 90°, feet lifted
2. Tap one heel to floor
3. Return and switch
4. Keep core engaged

*Reps: 15 per side, 3 sets*

### Lower Body Exercises

**Glute Bridge**
1. Knees bent, feet flat, arms at sides
2. Press through heels to lift hips
3. Squeeze glutes at top
4. Lower with control

*Reps: 15-20, 3 sets*

**Single-Leg Glute Bridge**
1. Same as above but one leg extended
2. Press through grounded foot
3. Keep hips level
4. Lower with control

*Reps: 12 per leg, 3 sets*

**Straight Leg Raise**
1. One leg bent (foot flat), one straight
2. Lift straight leg to height of bent knee
3. Lower with control
4. Keep core engaged

*Reps: 15 per leg, 3 sets*

**Clamshell (Hip on Floor)**
1. Lie on back, knees bent, feet together
2. Keep soles together, open knees like a book
3. Close with control

*Reps: 20, 3 sets*

**Frog Pump**
1. Soles of feet together, knees out
2. Press through feet to lift hips
3. Squeeze glutes at top
4. Lower with control

*Reps: 20-25, 3 sets*

### Upper Body (Supine)

**Chest Press (With Weights)**
1. Hold weights at chest level
2. Press up toward ceiling
3. Lower with control

*Reps: 12-15, 3 sets*

**Pullover (With Weight)**
1. Hold weight over chest, arms straight
2. Lower weight overhead toward floor
3. Keep slight bend in elbows
4. Return to start

*Reps: 12-15, 3 sets*

**Floor Press**
1. Hold weights with elbows on floor
2. Press up
3. Lower until elbows touch floor

*Reps: 12-15, 3 sets*

## Prone Exercises (Lying Face Down)

### Back Exercises

**Superman**
1. Lie face down, arms overhead
2. Lift arms, chest, and legs off floor
3. Hold for 2-3 seconds
4. Lower with control

*Reps: 12-15, 3 sets*

**Swimming**
1. Lie face down, arms overhead
2. Lift right arm and left leg
3. Switch to left arm and right leg
4. Continue alternating

*Reps: 15 per side, 3 sets*

**Cobra**
1. Hands under shoulders
2. Press upper body up, keeping hips down
3. Hold for 10-30 seconds
4. Lower

*Reps: 5-10, hold 10-30 seconds each*

**Back Extension**
1. Hands behind head or at sides
2. Lift chest off floor
3. Hold 2-3 seconds
4. Lower

*Reps: 12-15, 3 sets*

**Y-T-W Raises**
1. Lie face down
2. Y: Raise arms at 45° angle, thumbs up
3. T: Raise arms straight to sides, thumbs up
4. W: Elbows bent, raise and rotate thumbs up

*Reps: 10 each position, 2-3 sets*

### Lower Body (Prone)

**Prone Leg Curl**
1. Lie face down, legs straight
2. Bend one knee, bringing heel toward buttock
3. Lower with control
4. Switch legs

*Reps: 15 per leg, 3 sets*

**Prone Hip Extension**
1. Lie face down, forehead on hands
2. Lift one leg straight up
3. Keep hip on floor
4. Lower and switch

*Reps: 12-15 per leg, 3 sets*

## Side-Lying Exercises

### Hip Exercises

**Clamshell**
1. Lie on side, knees bent 45°
2. Keep feet together
3. Lift top knee toward ceiling
4. Lower with control

*Reps: 15-20 per side, 3 sets*

**Side-Lying Leg Raise**
1. Lie on side, legs straight
2. Lift top leg toward ceiling
3. Keep toe pointing forward
4. Lower with control

*Reps: 15-20 per side, 3 sets*

**Inner Thigh Lift**
1. Lie on side, top leg bent in front
2. Lift bottom leg up
3. Lower with control

*Reps: 15-20 per side, 3 sets*

**Side-Lying Circles**
1. Lie on side, top leg lifted slightly
2. Make small circles with the leg
3. 10 forward, 10 backward

*Reps: 10 each direction per side*

### Core (Side-Lying)

**Side Plank (Modified)**
1. Lie on side, prop on elbow
2. Lift hips off floor
3. Keep body in straight line
4. Hold

*Hold: 20-30 seconds per side, 3 sets*

**Side Crunch**
1. Lie on side, legs slightly forward
2. Place hand behind head
3. Crunch obliques, lifting shoulder
4. Lower

*Reps: 15 per side, 3 sets*

## Quadruped Exercises (On All Fours)

### Core and Stability

**Bird Dog**
1. On all fours, spine neutral
2. Extend opposite arm and leg
3. Hold 2-3 seconds
4. Return and switch

*Reps: 10 per side, 3 sets*

**Cat-Cow**
1. On all fours
2. Arch back, look up (cow)
3. Round back, tuck chin (cat)
4. Move slowly between positions

*Reps: 10-15 cycles*

**Fire Hydrant**
1. On all fours
2. Lift one leg out to side, knee bent
3. Keep hips square
4. Lower with control

*Reps: 15 per side, 3 sets*

**Donkey Kick**
1. On all fours
2. Kick one leg back and up, knee bent
3. Squeeze glute at top
4. Lower with control

*Reps: 15 per side, 3 sets*

**Thread the Needle**
1. On all fours
2. Reach one arm under body, rotating torso
3. Let shoulder rest on floor
4. Return and switch

*Reps: 8-10 per side, 2 sets*

## Plank Variations (Floor-Based)

### Front Plank

**Standard Plank**
1. Forearms and toes on floor
2. Body in straight line
3. Hold

*Hold: 20-60 seconds, 3 sets*

**Plank with Knee Tap**
1. Hold plank position
2. Tap one knee to floor
3. Return and switch

*Reps: 10 per side, 3 sets*

**Plank with Hip Dip**
1. Hold plank position
2. Rotate hips to tap one side to floor
3. Return and switch

*Reps: 10 per side, 3 sets*

### Side Plank

**Full Side Plank**
1. Stack feet, prop on one elbow
2. Lift hips, body in straight line
3. Hold

*Hold: 20-30 seconds per side, 3 sets*

**Side Plank with Hip Drop**
1. Hold side plank
2. Lower hips toward floor
3. Lift back to start

*Reps: 10 per side, 3 sets*

## Adding Progression

### Increase Difficulty

- Add ankle weights for leg exercises
- Hold dumbbells for upper body work
- Increase hold times for planks
- Add resistance bands for hip exercises
- Slow down the tempo (more time under tension)
- Add pulses at the hardest point

### Progression Examples

**Glute Bridge Progression:**
1. Two-leg bridge → 
2. Single-leg bridge → 
3. Single-leg bridge with weight on hips

**Plank Progression:**
1. Knee plank → 
2. Full plank → 
3. Plank with arm reach → 
4. Plank with leg lift

**Clamshell Progression:**
1. Bodyweight → 
2. With band around knees → 
3. With heavier band → 
4. With pulse at top

## Tips for Floor Exercise

### Setup

- Use a comfortable mat
- Ensure adequate space
- Keep water nearby
- Warm up before starting

### During Exercise

- Focus on form over reps
- Breathe consistently (exhale on effort)
- Move with control
- Stop if anything hurts

### Getting Up and Down

If getting to/from the floor is challenging:
- Use a sturdy chair for support
- Lower to knees first, then to sitting
- Roll to your side before sitting up
- Consider exercises in bed if floor access is difficult

## Key Takeaways

1. **Floor exercises can be a complete workout**—you can build significant strength without standing
2. **The floor provides stability**—perfect for those with balance issues or injury rehab
3. **Core work is naturally emphasized**—most floor exercises engage your core
4. **Progression is possible**—add weights, bands, or tempo changes to increase difficulty
5. **Mix positions**—supine, prone, side-lying, and quadruped work different muscle groups
6. **Form matters**—the stability of the floor helps you focus on proper technique
7. **Accessible to all levels**—modify exercises easier or harder based on your fitness

Floor exercises prove that effective training doesn't require standing, jumping, or complex equipment. With just your body and a mat, you can build a strong, flexible, and resilient body.
