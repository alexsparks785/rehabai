# What Muscles Do Skull Crushers Work? Complete Anatomy Guide

The skull crusher (lying tricep extension) is one of the most effective exercises for tricep development, especially the long head. This lying movement allows significant loading while targeting all three triceps heads. Here's the complete breakdown.

## Primary Muscles Worked

### Triceps Brachii (All Three Heads)

**Activation Level: Maximum**

All three heads work intensely:

**Long Head:**
- Largest tricep head
- Crosses the shoulder joint
- Maximum stretch when arms are overhead/back
- **Most emphasized in skull crushers**
- Key for overall tricep size

**Lateral Head:**
- Outer horseshoe
- Elbow extension
- Active throughout movement
- Visible when flexed

**Medial Head:**
- Deep head
- Works in all elbow extension
- Stabilizes the movement
- Active throughout

## Secondary Muscles Worked

### Anconeus

**Activation Level: Moderate**

Small elbow muscle:
- Assists elbow extension
- Works with triceps

### Anterior Deltoid (Stabilization)

**Activation Level: Low**

Front shoulder:
- Stabilizes shoulder position
- Keeps arms steady
- Minor contribution

### Core

**Activation Level: Low**

Stabilization:
- Keeps body stable on bench
- Minimal contribution

## Why Skull Crushers Target the Long Head

The long head is unique because it crosses the shoulder joint (attaches to scapula). It's most active when:

1. **Shoulder is flexed** (arms overhead/back)
2. **Combined with elbow extension**

Skull crushers put the arm in this position, maximally stretching and loading the long head.

**Pushdowns** (arms at sides) don't stretch the long head as effectively.

## Skull Crusher Variations

| Variation | Long Head | Total Tricep | Difficulty |
|-----------|-----------|--------------|------------|
| EZ bar skull crusher | Maximum | Very High | Moderate |
| Barbell skull crusher | Maximum | Very High | Moderate |
| Dumbbell skull crusher | Very High | Very High | Harder |
| Cable skull crusher | High | High | Easier |
| Incline skull crusher | Maximum | Very High | Moderate |

### EZ Bar Skull Crusher

Most common version:
- Angled grip reduces wrist strain
- Excellent loading
- Classic choice
- Standard technique

### Barbell Skull Crusher

Straight bar:
- Maximum supination
- Some find harder on wrists
- Heavier possible
- Traditional option

### Dumbbell Skull Crusher

Using dumbbells:
- Independent arms
- Greater ROM possible
- Fixes imbalances
- More stabilization needed

### Cable Skull Crusher

Lying with cable:
- Constant tension
- Different resistance curve
- Good for burnouts
- Lighter weight

### Incline Skull Crusher

On incline bench:
- Even greater long head stretch
- Arms further back
- Very effective
- Advanced variation

## Muscle Activation by Phase

### Starting Position
- Lying on flat bench
- Arms extended toward ceiling
- Grip bar (EZ or straight)
- Elbows pointing up

### Lowering Phase (Eccentric)
- Bend elbows
- Lower weight toward forehead/behind head
- Keep upper arms still
- Feel triceps stretch

### Bottom Position
- Weight near or past forehead
- Maximum tricep (long head) stretch
- Elbows stay pointed up
- Control the weight

### Extension Phase (Concentric)
- Extend elbows
- Push weight back up
- Triceps contract
- Lock out at top

## Where to Lower the Bar

There's debate on where to lower:

| Target | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| To forehead | Traditional "skull crusher" |
| To top of head | More stretch, similar |
| Behind head | Maximum long head stretch |

**Behind the head** (sometimes called "lying tricep extensions") provides more long head stretch but requires more shoulder mobility.

**To forehead** is the classic technique and very effective.

## Common Mistakes

**Elbows flaring out**: Should point at ceiling. Keep them in.

**Moving upper arms**: Should stay fixed. Only forearms move.

**Going too heavy**: Dangerous (it's called SKULL crusher). Control is essential.

**Partial range of motion**: Not getting full stretch or extension. Full ROM.

**Rushing**: Missing the stretch. Controlled movement.

**Bouncing at bottom**: Injury risk. Control the weight.

**Locking out hard**: Elbow stress. Smooth lockout.

**Bar drifting back**: Changes the exercise. Keep over face/forehead.

## How to Maximize Tricep Activation

1. **Keep elbows fixed**: Point at ceiling throughout
2. **Full range of motion**: Deep stretch to full extension
3. **Control the negative**: 2-3 seconds down
4. **Feel the stretch**: Especially long head at bottom
5. **Moderate weight**: Don't sacrifice form
6. **Squeeze at top**: Brief lockout contraction
7. **Behind head option**: For maximum long head
8. **Mind-muscle connection**: Feel triceps working

## Programming Recommendations

**For Tricep Mass:**
- 3-4 sets of 8-12 reps
- Moderate weight
- Full ROM
- 2 times per week

**For Strength:**
- 4 sets of 6-8 reps
- Heavier weight
- Still controlled
- Progressive overload

**In an Arm/Push Workout:**
After compounds:
1. Pressing movements (bench, overhead)
2. **Skull crushers** (long head focus)
3. Pushdowns (lateral/medial emphasis)
4. Bicep work

**Superset Ideas:**
- Skull crushers + close-grip bench
- Skull crushers + pushdowns
- Skull crushers + barbell curls

## Skull Crushers vs Pushdowns

| Factor | Skull Crushers | Pushdowns |
|--------|----------------|-----------|
| Long head | Maximum | Moderate |
| Lateral/medial | High | Very High |
| Stretch | Maximum | Minimal |
| Loading | Moderate-high | Moderate |
| Position | Lying | Standing |

**For complete triceps:** Use both. Skull crushers for long head; pushdowns for lateral/medial.

## Elbow Health Considerations

Skull crushers can stress the elbows:

**Prevention tips:**
- Warm up thoroughly
- Don't go too heavy
- Control the movement
- Don't lock out explosively
- Stop if pain occurs
- Consider EZ bar (easier on joints)

**If elbows are bothered:**
- Reduce weight
- Try cable version
- Adjust bar path
- Consider alternatives temporarily

## The Name

Why "skull crushers"?

The bar path goes toward your skull. If you lose control... well, it's not good. The name is a reminder to:
- Use appropriate weight
- Maintain control
- Use a spotter for heavy sets
- Respect the exercise

## Key Takeaways

- **Primary muscles**: All three triceps heads (maximum), especially long head
- **Secondary**: Anconeus, anterior delts (stabilization)
- **Long head emphasis**: Best lying exercise for the largest tricep head
- **Arm position**: Arms back/overhead stretches long head
- **Keep elbows fixed**: Point at ceiling, don't flare
- **Full ROM**: Deep stretch to full extension
- **Control essential**: It's called skull crushers for a reason
- **Complements pushdowns**: Different tricep head emphasis

Skull crushers are one of the most effective exercises for tricep development, especially the often-undertrained long head. The lying position with arms overhead maximally stretches this largest tricep head. Master the form, use appropriate weight, and include skull crushers for complete tricep development.
