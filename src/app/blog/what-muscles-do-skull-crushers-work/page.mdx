# What Muscles Do Skull Crushers Work? Complete Anatomy Guide

Skull crushers—also called lying triceps extensions—are one of the most effective exercises for building triceps size and strength. The dramatic name comes from the bar path heading toward your forehead, but with proper technique, they're perfectly safe and incredibly effective.

Let's break down exactly what muscles skull crushers target and why they deserve a place in your arm training.

## Primary Muscles Worked

### Triceps Brachii (Maximum Activation)

Skull crushers are a triceps isolation exercise that hits all three heads:

**Long head:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Maximum)
- The largest triceps head
- Crosses the shoulder joint (attaches to scapula)
- Gets extra stretch when arms are overhead or angled back
- THIS is why skull crushers build triceps size so effectively

**Lateral head:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Maximum)
- The "horseshoe" muscle on the outer arm
- Primary elbow extensor
- Works hard throughout the movement

**Medial head:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Very High)
- Deep head, underneath the others
- Stabilizes and assists throughout
- Active across all elbow extension angles

**Why the long head emphasis matters:**
The long head is the biggest triceps muscle, making up about 50% of total triceps mass. Because it crosses the shoulder, it gets stretched when your arms go overhead—exactly what happens in skull crushers. This stretched position creates excellent muscle-building stimulus.

## Secondary Muscles Worked

### Anconeus - High Activation

This small muscle at the back of the elbow:
- Assists with elbow extension
- Helps stabilize the elbow joint
- Works throughout the movement

### Anterior Deltoid - Low to Moderate

The front shoulder helps stabilize:
- Maintains arm position
- Works isometrically
- Activation increases with heavier loads

### Core Muscles - Moderate

Your core stabilizes during the movement:
- **Rectus abdominis:** Prevents excessive arching
- **Erector spinae:** Maintains spine position

## The Anatomy Advantage

### Why Skull Crushers Excel for Triceps

The lying position with arms angled back creates:

1. **Long head stretch:** Arms overhead stretches the long head maximally
2. **Constant tension:** No lockout rest point like pushdowns
3. **Heavy loading:** Lying position allows more weight than standing
4. **Full range:** Deep elbow flexion to full extension

### Comparison to Other Triceps Exercises

| Exercise | Long Head | Lateral Head | Medial Head | Overall |
|----------|-----------|--------------|-------------|---------|
| Skull Crushers | Maximum | Maximum | Very High | Excellent |
| Close-Grip Bench | High | Very High | High | Excellent |
| Overhead Extensions | Maximum | High | High | Very Good |
| Pushdowns | Moderate | Very High | High | Good |
| Dips | High | Very High | High | Excellent |

Skull crushers provide the best long head emphasis of any lying exercise.

## How to Perform Skull Crushers

### Setup
1. Lie flat on a bench
2. Grip EZ bar or barbell shoulder-width or slightly narrower
3. Press weight up, arms vertical
4. Angle arms back slightly toward your head (10-15 degrees)

### Execution
1. **Lower the bar:** Bend elbows, bringing bar toward forehead or just behind head
2. **Keep upper arms fixed:** Only your forearms move
3. **Full stretch:** Lower until you feel triceps stretch
4. **Extend:** Press back up by straightening elbows
5. **Squeeze:** Contract triceps hard at the top

### Key Technique Points

- **Upper arms stay still:** Don't let elbows drift forward and back
- **Slight backward angle:** Arms angled back (not vertical) increases long head stretch
- **Bar path:** To forehead for more lateral head; behind head for more long head
- **Control the negative:** 2-3 seconds down
- **Full lockout:** Squeeze triceps at top

## Bar Path: Forehead vs. Behind Head

### To Forehead (Traditional)
- More lateral head emphasis
- Safer for beginners
- Less shoulder mobility required

### To Behind Head (French Press Style)
- Maximum long head stretch
- Greater muscle-building stimulus
- Requires good shoulder mobility
- Slightly more elbow stress

### Recommendation
Start with forehead path, progress to behind-head as you build strength and mobility.

## Equipment Variations

### EZ Curl Bar (Recommended)
- Angled grip reduces wrist strain
- Most comfortable for most people
- Standard choice for skull crushers

### Straight Barbell
- Works fine if no wrist issues
- Allows closer grip
- Slightly more challenging

### Dumbbells
- Independent arm movement
- Allows neutral or angled grip
- Harder to stabilize

### Cable (Lying)
- Constant tension throughout
- Different resistance curve
- Good variation option

### Single Dumbbell (Two Hands)
- Diamond grip on dumbbell head
- Good for home gyms
- Comfortable wrist position

## Common Mistakes

### Elbows Flaring Wide
- **Problem:** Turns it into a pressing movement, reduces triceps isolation
- **Fix:** Keep elbows pointed at ceiling, shoulder-width apart

### Upper Arms Moving
- **Problem:** Swinging arms forward and back uses momentum
- **Fix:** Lock upper arms in position; only forearms move

### Going Too Heavy
- **Problem:** Form breaks down, stress shifts to joints
- **Fix:** Use weight you can control for 8-12 reps with perfect form

### Bouncing Off Forehead
- **Problem:** Besides being dangerous, it uses momentum
- **Fix:** Pause briefly at the bottom, controlled reversal

### Incomplete Range of Motion
- **Problem:** Partial reps miss the stretched position
- **Fix:** Full stretch at bottom, full lockout at top

## Elbow Health Considerations

Skull crushers can stress the elbows. Here's how to keep them healthy:

### Prevention Strategies
- **Warm up thoroughly:** Light triceps pushdowns first
- **Don't go too heavy:** This is an isolation exercise
- **Control the negative:** No dropping the weight
- **Angle arms back:** Reduces elbow stress vs. vertical

### If You Have Elbow Pain
- Switch to cable variations (less peak stress)
- Use dumbbells with neutral grip
- Try decline bench (changes angle)
- Reduce frequency (1x/week may be enough)

### When to Skip
- Active elbow inflammation
- Sharp pain during movement
- Recent elbow injury (consult PT)

## Programming Recommendations

### For Triceps Hypertrophy
- 2x per week
- 3-4 sets of 8-12 reps
- Focus on stretch and squeeze

### As Main Triceps Exercise
- Perform first in triceps portion
- 3 sets of 8-10 reps
- Progress weight over time

### As Secondary Exercise
- After compound pressing
- 2-3 sets of 10-15 reps
- Focus on pump and feel

### Superset Option
- Pair with biceps curls
- Alternate sets with minimal rest
- Efficient arm training

## Progressive Overload

### Week-to-Week Progression
1. Add 1-2 reps per set when possible
2. When hitting 12+ reps, add 2.5-5 lbs
3. Reset to 8 reps with new weight

### Long-Term Development
- Beginner: 30-50 lbs
- Intermediate: 50-80 lbs
- Advanced: 80-100+ lbs

*These are general ranges—individual variation is normal.*

## Complementary Exercises

Pair skull crushers with:

**For Complete Triceps Development:**
- Close-grip bench press (compound)
- Triceps pushdowns (different angle)
- Overhead triceps extensions (stretched position)

**For Balanced Arms:**
- Barbell curls
- Hammer curls
- Preacher curls

## Key Takeaways

1. **Skull crushers target all three triceps heads** with emphasis on the long head
2. **The stretched position** makes them excellent for muscle growth
3. **Angle arms back slightly** for maximum long head activation
4. **Control is essential**—this is not a power exercise
5. **EZ bar is usually most comfortable** for wrist health
6. **Monitor elbow health** and adjust technique if needed

## The Bottom Line

Skull crushers are one of the best exercises for triceps development, period. The combination of heavy loading potential, full range of motion, and long head stretch makes them highly effective for building arm size.

Use proper technique, progress gradually, and your triceps will grow. Just don't actually crush your skull—the name is dramatic, not a goal.
