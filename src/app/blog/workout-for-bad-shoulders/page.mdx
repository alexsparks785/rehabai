# Workout for Bad Shoulders: Build Strength Without Pain

Shoulder problems are frustrating—they seem to affect everything. Reaching overhead, pushing, pulling, even sleeping can hurt. But with the right approach, you can still train hard, build strength, and often make your shoulders better in the process.

## Understanding Shoulder Problems

The shoulder is the most mobile joint in your body, which makes it vulnerable. Common issues include:

**Rotator Cuff Problems**
- Impingement (pinching when raising arm)
- Tendinitis (inflamed tendons)
- Tears (partial or complete)

**Instability**
- Loose or "clicky" shoulder
- Previous dislocations
- Hypermobility

**Arthritis**
- Osteoarthritis (wear and tear)
- Inflammatory arthritis
- AC joint arthritis

**Frozen Shoulder**
- Severely restricted movement
- Gradual onset and recovery
- Often follows injury or surgery

Each condition has different implications, but general principles apply to training around all of them.

## Movements to Avoid (or Modify)

These common exercises often aggravate shoulder problems:

### Behind-the-Neck Movements
- Behind-neck press
- Behind-neck pulldowns
- **Why:** Extreme external rotation under load stresses the joint

### Wide-Grip Pressing
- Wide-grip bench press
- Wide push-ups
- **Why:** Increased shoulder abduction causes impingement

### Upright Rows
- Barbell upright rows
- High pulls with elbows flared
- **Why:** Internal rotation under load compresses rotator cuff

### Deep Dips
- Parallel bar dips
- Deep ring dips
- **Why:** Shoulder extension under load strains the front of the joint

### Overhead Pressing (Sometimes)
- Strict press
- Push press
- **Depends on your specific issue:** Some shoulders tolerate this, others don't

## Safe Upper Body Exercises

### Chest Training

**Floor Press**
- Limits range of motion to shoulder-safe zone
- Removes stretch at bottom
- Works chest and triceps effectively

**Neutral-Grip Dumbbell Press**
- Palms facing each other
- Keeps shoulders in safer position
- Can adjust angle (flat, incline)

**Close-Grip Push-ups**
- Hands closer than shoulder width
- Elbows stay closer to body
- Easier to control depth

**Cable Flys (Mid-Height)**
- Constant tension, adjustable
- Control the range of motion
- Stop before any pinching

**Svend Press**
- Press plates together in front of chest
- Minimal shoulder stress
- Good chest activation

### Back Training

**Chest-Supported Rows**
- Removes shoulder stabilization demand
- Focuses on lats and mid-back
- Various grip options

**Single-Arm Dumbbell Row**
- Control range of motion
- Keep elbow close to body
- Pause at top for back engagement

**Cable Rows (Neutral Grip)**
- Smooth, adjustable resistance
- Multiple handle options
- Pull to lower chest/upper abdomen

**Face Pulls**
- External rotation strengthens rotator cuff
- Use light weight, high reps
- Crucial for shoulder health

**Lat Pulldowns (In Front, Narrow Grip)**
- Never behind the neck
- Neutral or underhand grip often better
- Control the movement

### Shoulder Training

**Lateral Raises (Modified)**
- Thumbs up or slightly up
- Stop at shoulder height or below
- Light weight, high reps

**Rear Delt Flys**
- Face down on incline bench
- Keep thumbs up
- Strengthens often-weak rear delts

**High Pulls with Elbows Back**
- Different from upright rows
- Lead with elbows going backward
- External rotation focus

**Landmine Press**
- Angled pressing is often tolerated
- Less shoulder strain than overhead
- Great for building pressing strength

**Y-Raises (Prone)**
- Light weight or bodyweight
- Thumbs up position
- Strengthens lower traps

## Rotator Cuff Strengthening

These exercises are therapeutic and should be part of your regular routine:

### External Rotation with Band

1. Elbow at side, bent 90°
2. Rotate forearm outward against band
3. Keep elbow pinned to body
4. 3 sets × 15-20 reps

### Internal Rotation with Band

1. Elbow at side, bent 90°
2. Rotate forearm inward against band
3. Control the movement
4. 3 sets × 15-20 reps

### Prone Y-T-W Raises

1. Lie face down on bench or floor
2. Y: Arms overhead, thumbs up, lift
3. T: Arms out to sides, thumbs up, lift
4. W: Elbows bent, squeeze shoulder blades, lift
5. 2 sets × 10 of each

### Side-Lying External Rotation

1. Lie on side, elbow bent 90°
2. Rotate forearm up toward ceiling
3. Keep elbow at your side
4. 2 sets × 15 each side

### Face Pulls

1. High cable attachment
2. Pull toward face, elbows high
3. Rotate hands outward at end
4. 3 sets × 15-20 reps

## Sample Shoulder-Friendly Workout

### Upper Body Day (Do 2x per week)

**Warm-up (5 minutes)**
- Band pull-aparts: 20 reps
- External rotations: 15 each arm
- Arm circles: 30 seconds each direction
- Cat-cow: 10 cycles

**Main Workout**

1. **Floor Press or Neutral-Grip Dumbbell Press**
   - 3 sets × 10-12 reps
   - Stop at safe depth

2. **Chest-Supported Row**
   - 3 sets × 10-12 reps
   - Neutral or underhand grip

3. **Landmine Press** (or Incline Neutral-Grip Press)
   - 3 sets × 10-12 reps each arm
   - Control throughout

4. **Single-Arm Cable Row**
   - 3 sets × 12 reps each arm
   - Pull to hip, squeeze back

5. **Lateral Raises** (thumbs up)
   - 3 sets × 15 reps
   - Stop at shoulder height

6. **Face Pulls**
   - 3 sets × 15-20 reps
   - Light weight, quality reps

7. **Bicep Curls**
   - 2 sets × 12 reps
   - Usually shoulder-safe

8. **Tricep Pushdowns**
   - 2 sets × 12 reps
   - Keep elbows stable

**Cool-down (5 minutes)**
- Doorway chest stretch: 30 seconds each position
- Cross-body shoulder stretch: 30 seconds each arm
- Sleeper stretch (if tolerated): 30 seconds each side

## Lower Body Training (No Shoulder Limitations)

Bad shoulders shouldn't limit leg training:

**Safe Exercises:**
- Goblet squats (if holding weight is okay)
- Leg press
- Belt squats
- Lunges and split squats
- Romanian deadlifts
- Leg curls and extensions
- Hip thrusts
- Calf raises

**May Need Modification:**
- Back squats (consider safety bar or front squat)
- Deadlifts (grip may be limited)

## Cardio and Conditioning

Most cardio is shoulder-friendly:

**Good Options:**
- Walking and hiking
- Stationary bike
- Elliptical (arms free or light)
- Swimming (depends on stroke and issue)
- Rowing (may need modification)

**May Aggravate:**
- Battle ropes
- Swimming butterfly
- Aggressive rowing

## Tips for Training with Shoulder Issues

### Warm Up Thoroughly

Spend 5-10 minutes on:
- Light rotator cuff work
- Band pull-aparts
- Arm circles and mobility
- Light sets of your first exercise

### Control the Weight

- Slow eccentrics (3 seconds down)
- No bouncing or momentum
- Quality reps over ego lifting

### Find Your Pain-Free Range

- Stop before pain starts
- Partial reps that don't hurt beat full reps that do
- Range of motion may improve over time

### Listen to Your Body

- Sharp pain = stop immediately
- Dull ache = proceed carefully or modify
- Next-day soreness in muscles = good
- Next-day pain in joint = too much

### Progress Gradually

- Don't rush back to heavy weights
- Build strength in rotator cuff first
- Add weight in small increments

## When to See a Professional

Get evaluated if you have:

- Pain that doesn't improve with rest
- Night pain that disrupts sleep
- Weakness when lifting arm
- Clicking, catching, or locking
- History of dislocation
- Numbness or tingling in arm

A physical therapist can identify your specific issue and provide targeted exercises.

## Sample Weekly Schedule

**Monday:** Upper body (shoulder-friendly)

**Tuesday:** Lower body + core

**Wednesday:** Rotator cuff work + cardio

**Thursday:** Upper body (shoulder-friendly)

**Friday:** Lower body + core

**Saturday:** Active recovery—walking, light mobility

**Sunday:** Rest

## The Bottom Line

Shoulder problems don't have to sideline you. With smart exercise selection, proper warm-ups, and attention to rotator cuff strength, you can train hard and often improve your shoulders in the process.

Avoid movements that aggravate your issue, embrace alternatives that work, and be patient. Most shoulder problems respond well to the right training—it just takes time.

Your shoulders carried you this far. Give them what they need to keep going.
