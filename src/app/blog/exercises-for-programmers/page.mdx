# Exercises for Programmers: Combat Coding Posture and RSI

Hours of typing, mouse work, and screen staring create a specific pattern of problems: tight chest and hip flexors, weak back muscles, forward head posture, and hand/wrist strain. This guide provides targeted exercises to keep you coding pain-free for years to come.

## The Programmer's Body Pattern

**What extended coding does:**

**Upper Body:**
- Rounded shoulders from reaching for keyboard
- Forward head posture from leaning toward screen
- Tight chest muscles (pectorals)
- Weak upper back muscles (rhomboids, lower traps)
- Neck strain from monitor position

**Arms and Hands:**
- Repetitive strain in wrists and forearms
- Tight forearm flexors from constant typing
- Carpal tunnel risk from wrist position
- Cubital tunnel risk from elbow position (ulnar nerve)

**Lower Body:**
- Tight hip flexors from sitting
- Weak glutes ("dead butt syndrome")
- Compressed lower back
- Tight hamstrings

## The 2-Minute Desk Reset

**Do this every 30-60 minutes while coding:**

**Chin Tucks (Most Important)**
- Pull chin straight back (make a double chin)
- Hold 5 seconds
- Repeat 5 times
- Reverses forward head posture

**Shoulder Blade Squeeze**
- Pull shoulders back and down
- Squeeze shoulder blades together
- Hold 5 seconds
- Repeat 5 times

**Wrist Circles**
- Rotate wrists 10 times each direction
- Releases forearm tension

**Chest Opener**
- Clasp hands behind back
- Squeeze shoulder blades, lift hands
- Hold 15-20 seconds

**Seated Spinal Twist**
- Cross one leg over the other
- Twist toward crossed leg
- Hold 15 seconds each side

## Hand and Wrist Exercises

**Critical for preventing RSI:**

### Tendon Gliding Sequence
**Do before and after coding sessions:**

1. **Straight position**: Fingers extended, straight
2. **Hook fist**: Bend at middle and end joints, keep knuckles straight
3. **Full fist**: Make a tight fist
4. **Table top**: Bend at knuckles, fingers straight
5. **Straight fist**: Fist with thumb wrapped outside

Move through all 5 positions slowly, 5 times through the sequence.

### Wrist Stretches

**Wrist Flexor Stretch**
- Extend arm, palm up
- Use other hand to pull fingers back toward you
- Hold 30 seconds each side

**Wrist Extensor Stretch**
- Extend arm, palm down
- Use other hand to push fingers down and toward you
- Hold 30 seconds each side

**Prayer Stretch**
- Press palms together in front of chest
- Lower hands while keeping palms together
- Hold 30 seconds

**Reverse Prayer**
- Press backs of hands together
- Raise hands while keeping contact
- Hold 30 seconds

### Grip and Finger Exercises

**Finger Spread**
- Place rubber band around all fingers
- Spread fingers against resistance
- 15 reps

**Finger Taps**
- Touch thumb to each finger quickly
- Forward and backward
- 10 rounds

**Ball Squeeze**
- Squeeze stress ball
- Hold 5 seconds
- 15 reps

### Nerve Gliding

**Median Nerve Glide (Carpal Tunnel Prevention)**
- Start with arm at side, wrist bent, fingers curled
- Extend wrist and fingers while tilting head away
- 10 slow reps each side

**Ulnar Nerve Glide (Cubital Tunnel Prevention)**
- Start with arm at side
- Bend elbow, bring hand toward shoulder, tilt head toward hand
- Straighten arm to side, tilt head away
- 10 slow reps each side

## Neck and Shoulder Routine

**Do morning and evening (10 minutes):**

### Neck Mobility

**Neck Rotations**
- Slowly turn head left, hold 5 seconds
- Turn right, hold 5 seconds
- 5 reps each direction

**Lateral Flexion**
- Ear toward shoulder (don't raise shoulder)
- Hold 30 seconds each side

**Chin to Chest**
- Let head drop forward
- Gently walk fingers up back of head for light pressure
- Hold 30 seconds

### Neck Strengthening

**Chin Tuck with Resistance**
- Place fingers on chin
- Do chin tuck against gentle finger resistance
- Hold 5 seconds
- 10 reps

**Isometric Side Resistance**
- Place hand on side of head
- Press head against hand (don't move head)
- Hold 5 seconds
- 5 reps each side

### Shoulder Exercises

**Wall Angels**
- Back flat against wall
- Arms in "W" position against wall
- Slide up to "Y" and back down
- Keep everything touching wall
- 10 slow reps

**Face Pulls** (with resistance band)
- Band at face height
- Pull toward face, elbows high
- Squeeze shoulder blades at end
- 15 reps

**Band Pull-Aparts**
- Hold band in front at shoulder height
- Pull apart, squeezing shoulder blades
- 15 reps

**Y-T-W Raises** (lying face down or bent over)
- Y: Raise arms overhead in Y shape
- T: Raise arms to sides
- W: Arms at sides, squeeze shoulder blades
- 10 reps each position

## Thoracic Spine Mobility

**Counteract the coding hunch:**

### Foam Roller Thoracic Extension
- Lie on foam roller positioned across upper back
- Support head with hands
- Extend backward over roller
- Move roller up and down spine
- 60-90 seconds

### Thread the Needle
- Start on hands and knees
- Reach right arm under body to left
- Let thoracic spine rotate
- Return and reach to ceiling
- 10 reps each side

### Cat-Cow
- On hands and knees
- Cow: Drop belly, lift head and tailbone
- Cat: Round spine, tuck chin and tailbone
- 10 slow cycles

### Open Book Stretch
- Lie on side, knees bent 90°
- Top arm reaches across, opens toward ceiling
- Follow hand with eyes
- Hold 30 seconds each side

## Hip Flexor and Lower Body

**Essential for sitting all day:**

### Hip Flexor Stretches

**Half-Kneeling Hip Flexor Stretch**
- Kneel on one knee, other foot forward
- Squeeze back glute
- Tuck pelvis (posterior tilt)
- Lean forward slightly
- Hold 60 seconds each side

**Couch Stretch**
- Kneel facing away from couch
- Place back foot up on cushion
- Squeeze back glute, tuck pelvis
- Hold 60-90 seconds each side

### Glute Activation

**Glute Bridges**
- Lie on back, knees bent, feet flat
- Squeeze glutes, lift hips
- Hold 2 seconds at top
- 15 reps

**Clamshells**
- Lie on side, knees bent 90°
- Keep feet together, lift top knee
- Don't let hips roll back
- 15 reps each side

### Standing Desk Movements

**Calf Raises**
- Rise up on toes, lower slowly
- 15 reps every hour

**Weight Shifts**
- Shift weight side to side
- Shift weight front to back

**Mini Squats**
- Small squat, just until thighs engage
- 10 reps

## The Programmer's Workout

**2-3x per week, 25-30 minutes:**

### Posture Correction Focus

**Upper Back Strengthening**
- Rows (band or dumbbell): 3 sets of 12
- Face pulls: 3 sets of 15
- Y-T-W raises: 2 sets of 10 each
- Wall slides: 3 sets of 10

**Core Stability**
- Dead bug: 3 sets of 10 each side
- Bird dog: 3 sets of 10 each side
- Pallof press: 3 sets of 10 each side
- Plank: 3 sets of 30 seconds

**Lower Body**
- Squats: 3 sets of 15
- Romanian deadlifts: 3 sets of 12
- Glute bridges: 3 sets of 15
- Clamshells: 3 sets of 15 each side

**Stretching (end of workout)**
- Chest doorway stretch: 45 sec each side
- Hip flexor stretch: 45 sec each side
- Hamstring stretch: 45 sec each side
- Lat stretch: 45 sec each side

## Ergonomic Setup

**Exercise can't fix a bad setup:**

### Monitor Position
- Top of screen at or slightly below eye level
- 20-26 inches from face
- Direct in front, not to the side

### Keyboard and Mouse
- Elbows at 90° or slightly more
- Wrists neutral (not bent up or down)
- Keyboard at elbow height or slightly below
- Mouse close to keyboard (don't reach)

### Chair Position
- Feet flat on floor (or footrest)
- Knees at 90° or slightly more
- Lower back supported
- Sit back in chair, not perched forward

### Standing Desk Tips
- Stand on anti-fatigue mat
- Alternate sitting and standing
- Don't lock knees
- Same monitor and keyboard rules apply

## Daily Routine Schedule

### Morning (5 minutes)
- Chin tucks: 10 reps
- Thread the needle: 5 each side
- Cat-cow: 10 reps
- Hip flexor stretch: 30 sec each side
- Glute bridges: 10 reps

### Every 30-60 Minutes While Coding
- 2-minute desk reset (see above)
- Stand and move briefly
- Look away from screen (20-20-20 rule)

### Lunch Break (10 minutes)
- Walk outside if possible
- Full neck and shoulder routine
- Hand and wrist exercises

### After Coding Session (10 minutes)
- Tendon gliding sequence
- Wrist stretches
- Thoracic spine mobility
- Hip flexor stretches
- Foam roll upper back if available

### 2-3x Per Week
- Full programmer's workout (30 min)
- Extended stretching (15 min)

## Warning Signs to Watch

### Carpal Tunnel Syndrome
**Symptoms:** Numbness/tingling in thumb, index, middle fingers; weakness in grip; dropping things; night pain

**Prevention:**
- Neutral wrist position
- Regular wrist stretches
- Tendon and nerve gliding exercises
- Take breaks from typing

### Cubital Tunnel Syndrome
**Symptoms:** Numbness/tingling in ring and pinky fingers; elbow pain; weakness

**Prevention:**
- Don't rest elbows on hard surfaces
- Don't keep elbows fully bent for long
- Ulnar nerve gliding exercises

### Thoracic Outlet Syndrome
**Symptoms:** Pain/numbness down arm; cold hands; weakness

**Prevention:**
- Good posture
- Strengthen upper back
- Don't carry heavy bags on shoulders
- Scalene and chest stretches

### When to See a Doctor
- Symptoms persist despite exercise and ergonomic changes
- Numbness or tingling is constant
- Weakness is progressive
- Night pain disrupts sleep
- Symptoms affect work

## Key Takeaways

**The programmer's priority list:**

1. **Breaks**: Every 30-60 minutes, no exceptions
2. **Ergonomics**: Fix your setup before exercise can help fully
3. **Hand/wrist care**: Daily tendon gliding and nerve exercises
4. **Posture muscles**: Strengthen upper back, stretch chest
5. **Hip flexors**: Stretch daily to counter sitting
6. **Full workouts**: 2-3x weekly for overall resilience

**Most important exercises:**
- Chin tucks (do these constantly)
- Tendon gliding sequence (before and after coding)
- Shoulder blade squeezes (throughout day)
- Hip flexor stretches (morning and evening)
- Upper back strengthening (in workouts)

Your code can wait 2 minutes. Your body can't wait forever. Build breaks into your workflow as seriously as you build tests into your code.
