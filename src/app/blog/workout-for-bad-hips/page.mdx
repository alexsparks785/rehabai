# Workout for Bad Hips: Exercises That Strengthen Without Pain

Hip problems affect everything—walking, sitting, sleeping, and especially exercising. But staying active is one of the best things you can do for hip health. The key is choosing the right exercises and knowing how to modify the wrong ones.

## Common Hip Problems

**Hip Arthritis**
- Osteoarthritis (wear and tear)
- Stiffness, especially in the morning
- Pain in groin, thigh, or buttock

**Hip Bursitis**
- Inflammation of the bursa
- Pain on the outside of the hip
- Worse with lying on that side

**Labral Tears**
- Cartilage damage in the socket
- Clicking, catching, or locking
- Deep groin pain

**Hip Flexor Issues**
- Tight or strained hip flexors
- Pain in front of hip
- Common in desk workers

**Femoroacetabular Impingement (FAI)**
- Bone shape causes pinching
- Pain with deep flexion
- Limited range of motion

## Movements to Avoid (or Modify)

### Deep Squats
- Can pinch the hip joint
- **Modify:** Squat to parallel or above
- **Alternative:** Box squats, goblet squats to comfortable depth

### High-Impact Activities
- Running, jumping stress the joint
- **Modify:** Reduce volume, use soft surfaces
- **Alternative:** Swimming, cycling, elliptical

### Deep Lunges
- Can aggravate front of hip
- **Modify:** Shorter stride, don't sink as deep
- **Alternative:** Reverse lunges, step-ups

### Extreme Hip Rotation
- Pigeon pose, figure-4 stretch (sometimes)
- **Modify:** Reduce depth, support with props
- **Alternative:** Supine stretches with control

### Sitting Cross-Legged
- Stresses hip in external rotation
- **Modify:** Use cushions, limit duration
- **Alternative:** Legs extended or one at a time

## Best Exercises for Bad Hips

### Low-Impact Cardio

**Swimming and Water Exercise**
- Water supports body weight
- Reduces joint stress by up to 90%
- Allows pain-free movement
- Best option for severe hip issues

**Cycling**
- Non-weight-bearing
- Maintains hip mobility
- Recumbent bike easiest on hips
- Adjust seat height properly

**Elliptical**
- Low impact, smooth motion
- Keep resistance moderate
- Watch for hip flexor irritation

**Walking**
- Weight-bearing but low impact
- Flat surfaces, cushioned shoes
- Shorter, more frequent walks if needed

### Strengthening Exercises

Strong muscles around the hip protect the joint.

**Glute Bridges**
- Lie on back, knees bent
- Lift hips, squeeze glutes
- Hold 3 seconds at top
- 3 sets × 12-15 reps

**Clamshells**
- Side-lying, knees bent
- Open top knee like a clamshell
- Keep feet together
- 3 sets × 15 each side

**Side-Lying Leg Raises**
- Lie on side, bottom leg bent
- Lift top leg, keep it straight
- Control the lowering
- 3 sets × 12 each side

**Standing Hip Abduction**
- Hold support for balance
- Lift leg out to side
- Keep torso upright
- 3 sets × 12 each side

**Mini Band Walks**
- Band around ankles or above knees
- Step sideways with control
- Keep tension on band
- 2 sets × 15 steps each direction

**Step-Ups**
- Lower step height if needed
- Drive through heel
- Control the descent
- 2 sets × 10 each leg

**Partial Squats**
- Only go as deep as comfortable
- Keep weight in heels
- Use support if needed
- 3 sets × 12 reps

### Hip Mobility Exercises

Maintain range of motion without aggravating the joint.

**Supine Hip Circles**
- Lie on back, one knee to chest
- Circle the knee gently
- 10 circles each direction, each leg

**Knee Drops**
- Lie on back, knees bent
- Let knees fall to one side
- Return to center, go other side
- 10 each direction

**Hip Flexor Stretch (Modified)**
- Half-kneeling, pad under knee
- Tuck tailbone, lean forward gently
- Don't force depth
- 30 seconds each side

**Supine Figure-4 Stretch**
- Lie on back
- Cross ankle over opposite knee
- Pull bottom leg toward you
- 30 seconds each side (pain-free range only)

**Standing Hip Flexion**
- Hold support
- Lift knee toward chest
- Only as high as comfortable
- 10 each leg

## Sample Hip-Friendly Workout

### Full Body Routine (30 minutes)

**Warm-up (5 minutes)**
- Walk in place or recumbent bike
- Gentle hip circles (standing with support)
- Ankle circles and calf raises

**Lower Body (12 minutes)**

1. **Glute Bridges**: 3 sets × 12 reps
2. **Clamshells**: 2 sets × 15 each side
3. **Partial Squats**: 3 sets × 10 reps
4. **Step-Ups** (low step): 2 sets × 8 each leg
5. **Mini Band Walks**: 2 sets × 12 steps each direction

**Upper Body (10 minutes)**

1. **Push-Ups** (wall or floor): 3 sets × 10 reps
2. **Seated Rows with Band**: 3 sets × 12 reps
3. **Overhead Press**: 2 sets × 12 reps
4. **Bicep Curls**: 2 sets × 12 reps

**Core (5 minutes)**

1. **Dead Bugs**: 2 sets × 8 each side
2. **Bird Dogs**: 2 sets × 8 each side
3. **Pallof Press** (or plank if tolerated): 2 sets × 10 each side

**Cool-down (3 minutes)**
- Supine knee drops
- Gentle hip flexor stretch
- Supine figure-4 stretch (pain-free)

## Water Workout for Hips

If land exercise is too painful, try this pool routine:

**Pool Walking**
- Walk across pool forward, backward, sideways
- Water provides resistance without impact
- 10 minutes

**Water Squats**
- In chest-deep water
- Squat down, water supports you
- 3 sets × 15 reps

**Leg Swings**
- Hold pool edge
- Swing leg forward and back
- Then side to side
- 15 each direction, each leg

**Water Cycling**
- On pool noodle or against wall
- Pedal legs like cycling
- 5 minutes

**Hip Circles**
- Standing in water
- Lift knee, make circles
- 10 each direction, each leg

## Tips for Training With Hip Problems

### Warm Up Thoroughly
- 5-10 minutes of gentle movement
- Never start with challenging exercises
- Hip circles, gentle swings, walking

### Find Your Range
- Work within pain-free movement
- Partial range that doesn't hurt beats full range that does
- Range may improve over time

### Strengthen the Glutes
- Weak glutes put more stress on hips
- Prioritize glute bridges, clamshells, hip abduction
- Strong glutes = protected hips

### Don't Sit Too Long
- Prolonged sitting tightens hip flexors
- Take movement breaks every 30 minutes
- Stand or walk when possible

### Ice After Exercise
- If hips feel inflamed post-workout
- 15-20 minutes
- Wrap ice, don't apply directly

### Progress Slowly
- Add repetitions before adding resistance
- Increase gradually (10% rule)
- Listen to pain signals

## When to See a Professional

Get evaluated if you experience:

- Pain that's worsening despite rest
- Night pain that disrupts sleep
- Limping or difficulty walking
- Grinding, clicking, or locking in the joint
- Pain that doesn't respond to modifications
- Symptoms in both hips or radiating down the leg

A physical therapist can identify your specific issue and create a targeted program.

## Sample Weekly Schedule

**Monday:** Hip-friendly full body workout

**Tuesday:** Swimming or water walking (20-30 min)

**Wednesday:** Upper body + core

**Thursday:** Cycling (20-30 min) + hip mobility

**Friday:** Hip-friendly full body workout

**Saturday:** Walking or swimming

**Sunday:** Rest + gentle stretching

## Exercises After Hip Replacement

If you've had a hip replacement, follow your surgeon's specific guidelines. Generally:

**Avoid (often permanently):**
- Deep squats past 90°
- Crossing legs
- Extreme rotation
- High-impact activities

**Focus on:**
- Glute strengthening
- Quad strengthening
- Balance work
- Walking progression

Always clear exercises with your orthopedic surgeon or physical therapist.

## The Bottom Line

Bad hips don't mean no exercise—they mean smarter exercise. Focus on strengthening the muscles around your hip, maintain mobility within comfortable range, and choose low-impact cardio that doesn't aggravate your symptoms.

Many people find that consistent, appropriate exercise actually makes their hips feel better over time. Strong muscles support and protect the joint, while movement keeps it lubricated and mobile.

Start gently, progress slowly, and let your body guide you. Your hips will thank you.
