# Best Exercises for Hip Mobility: Unlock Tight Hips

Tight hips affect everything—your squat depth, your running stride, your lower back, even how you walk. These exercises target all the movements your hips need: flexion, extension, rotation, and abduction.

## Why Hip Mobility Matters

Your hips are designed to move in every direction. When they're tight:
- Squat depth suffers
- Lower back compensates and hurts
- Running and walking become inefficient
- Sitting becomes more uncomfortable
- Athletic performance declines

The good news: hips respond well to consistent mobility work.

## Hip Movement Patterns

Your hips need mobility in multiple directions:

**Flexion** – Knee toward chest
**Extension** – Leg behind you
**External Rotation** – Knee falls outward
**Internal Rotation** – Knee falls inward
**Abduction** – Leg moves away from body
**Adduction** – Leg moves toward body

Complete hip mobility addresses all of these.

## Best Hip Mobility Exercises

### 1. 90/90 Stretch

**The best overall hip opener.**

**How to do it:**
1. Sit on floor
2. Front leg bent 90° in front of you
3. Back leg bent 90° to the side
4. Sit tall, square hips forward
5. Lean forward over front leg for deeper stretch

**Hold:** 1-2 minutes each side

**Why it works:** Simultaneously stretches external rotation (front hip) and internal rotation (back hip).

**Transitions:** Lift and switch between sides for dynamic work.

### 2. Hip Flexor Stretch (Half-Kneeling)

**Addresses the #1 cause of tight hips: sitting.**

**How to do it:**
1. Half-kneeling position, pad under back knee
2. Tuck tailbone (posterior pelvic tilt)
3. Squeeze back glute
4. Lean forward slightly
5. Feel stretch in front of back hip

**Hold:** 30-60 seconds each side

**Why it works:** Directly lengthens hip flexors shortened by sitting.

**Variations:**
- Add overhead reach (intensifies stretch)
- Add lateral lean (hits TFL)
- Add rotation toward front leg

### 3. Pigeon Pose

**Deep external rotation stretch.**

**How to do it:**
1. Front shin angled (more angle = more stretch)
2. Back leg extended straight behind
3. Square hips to front
4. Stay upright or fold forward for depth

**Hold:** 1-2 minutes each side

**Why it works:** Deep stretch for glutes and external rotators.

**Modification:** If too intense, do supine figure-4 stretch instead.

### 4. Deep Squat Hold (Malasana)

**Functional hip opener.**

**How to do it:**
1. Feet slightly wider than hips, toes out
2. Squat as deep as possible
3. Elbows inside knees, hands together
4. Press knees out with elbows
5. Keep chest up, heels down (or elevate heels)

**Hold:** 30-60 seconds, accumulate 3-5 minutes daily

**Why it works:** Opens hips, ankles, and adductors simultaneously. Restores natural resting position.

### 5. Frog Stretch

**Opens adductors and hips.**

**How to do it:**
1. On hands and knees
2. Widen knees as far as comfortable
3. Turn feet out, inside edges on floor
4. Sink hips back and down
5. Rock forward and back gently

**Hold:** 1-2 minutes

**Why it works:** Intense adductor and hip stretch. Progress slowly.

### 6. Couch Stretch

**Intense hip flexor and quad stretch.**

**How to do it:**
1. Face away from couch or wall
2. Back knee on floor, foot up against couch/wall
3. Front foot forward in lunge
4. Stay upright, tuck pelvis
5. Optional: lift torso more upright for intensity

**Hold:** 30-60 seconds each side

**Why it works:** Maximum hip flexor stretch. Essential for desk workers.

### 7. Hip CARs (Controlled Articular Rotations)

**Full range of motion exploration.**

**How to do it:**
1. Stand on one leg, hold support
2. Lift knee to 90°
3. Rotate knee out to side
4. Extend leg behind you
5. Bring back to start in reverse
6. Make largest circles possible

**Reps:** 5 circles each direction, each leg

**Why it works:** Moves hip through full available range, identifies restrictions.

### 8. Supine Figure-4 Stretch

**Gentle external rotation stretch.**

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back
2. Cross one ankle over opposite knee
3. Pull bottom leg toward chest
4. Keep crossed foot flexed

**Hold:** 30-60 seconds each side

**Why it works:** Accessible stretch for piriformis and external rotators. Gentler than pigeon.

### 9. Kneeling Hip Circles

**Dynamic hip opener.**

**How to do it:**
1. On hands and knees
2. Lift one knee off ground
3. Make circles with the knee
4. Forward circles, then reverse
5. Make circles as large as possible

**Reps:** 10 circles each direction, each leg

**Why it works:** Moves hip through rotation in a loaded position.

### 10. Butterfly Stretch

**Adductors and hip opening.**

**How to do it:**
1. Sit, soles of feet together
2. Knees fall out to sides
3. Hold feet, sit tall
4. Gently press knees down or lean forward

**Hold:** 1-2 minutes

**Why it works:** Stretches adductors, opens hips for external rotation.

## Complete Hip Mobility Routine

### Daily Quick Routine (10 minutes)

**Dynamic Warm-Up (2 minutes)**
- Hip circles: 10 each direction
- Leg swings front/back: 10 each leg
- Leg swings side/side: 10 each leg

**Holds (8 minutes)**
- Deep squat hold: 1 minute
- Hip flexor stretch: 45 seconds each side
- 90/90 stretch: 1 minute each side
- Pigeon or figure-4: 45 seconds each side

### Full Mobility Session (25 minutes)

**Movement Prep (5 minutes)**
- Hip CARs: 5 each direction, each leg
- Kneeling hip circles: 10 each direction, each leg
- Deep squat with rocking: 1 minute

**Deep Stretching (18 minutes)**
- 90/90 stretch: 2 minutes each side
- Hip flexor stretch: 1 minute each side
- Couch stretch: 45 seconds each side
- Pigeon pose: 90 seconds each side
- Frog stretch: 90 seconds
- Butterfly stretch: 1 minute

**Integration (2 minutes)**
- Deep squat hold: 1 minute
- Bodyweight squats with focus on depth: 10 reps

## Hip Mobility for Specific Goals

### Better Squat Depth
**Prioritize:**
- Deep squat holds (daily)
- Hip flexor stretches
- Ankle mobility work
- Adductor stretches (frog, butterfly)

### Less Lower Back Pain
**Prioritize:**
- Hip flexor stretches (tight hip flexors tilt pelvis)
- 90/90 stretch
- Glute stretches (pigeon, figure-4)
- Hip CARs

### Better Running
**Prioritize:**
- Hip flexor stretches
- Dynamic hip circles
- Leg swings
- Couch stretch for quad/hip flexor

### Less Hip Pain
**Prioritize:**
- Hip CARs (pain-free range only)
- Gentle stretches to tolerance
- Strengthening alongside mobility
- See a professional if pain persists

## Tips for Better Results

### Consistency Beats Intensity
- 10 minutes daily > 60 minutes weekly
- Hips need frequent input to change
- Build a daily habit

### Don't Force It
- Mild discomfort is okay
- Sharp pain means stop
- Progress takes weeks and months

### Warm Up First
- Muscles stretch better warm
- Move before holding
- After workouts or hot shower is ideal

### Breathe Into Stretches
- Deep breaths help relaxation
- Exhale to deepen stretch
- Don't hold breath

### Combine with Strength
- Mobility without strength creates instability
- Include hip strengthening exercises
- Glute work supports hip health

## Common Mistakes

### Only Stretching Hip Flexors
- Hips need work in all directions
- Include rotation, adduction, abduction
- Balance flexibility with strength

### Stretching Through Pain
- Pain is a signal to stop
- Work to mild discomfort, not beyond
- Forced stretching can cause injury

### Inconsistency
- Missing days slows progress
- Tight hips need regular work
- Make it non-negotiable

### Ignoring Ankles
- Ankle mobility affects hip position
- Especially for squatting
- Include ankle work in your routine

## Sample Weekly Schedule

**Daily:** Quick routine (10 min) OR deep squat accumulation (5 min throughout day)

**2-3x per week:** Full mobility session (25 min)

**Before workouts:** Dynamic hip prep (leg swings, circles)

**After workouts:** Deep stretching holds

## The Bottom Line

Hip mobility comes from consistent, targeted work. Include stretches for hip flexors, external and internal rotation, and adductors. Move your hips through their full range daily.

Ten minutes a day will change your hips in weeks. The deep squat is your friend—spend time there. Combine mobility with strength work for best results.

Your hips are meant to move. Give them the attention they need, and they'll reward you with better movement everywhere.
