# MCL Sprain Exercises: Rehabilitate Your Medial Knee Ligament

The MCL (medial collateral ligament) is one of the most commonly injured knee ligaments. The good news: most MCL injuries heal well without surgery. Proper rehabilitation is key to full recovery.

## Understanding MCL Injuries

The MCL runs along the inner (medial) side of the knee, connecting the femur to the tibia. It prevents the knee from bending inward (valgus stress).

**Injury grades:**
- **Grade I:** Mild sprain, ligament stretched but intact
- **Grade II:** Moderate, partial tear
- **Grade III:** Severe, complete tear

**Common causes:**
- Direct blow to outer knee (football, soccer)
- Twisting injury
- Valgus stress (knee forced inward)
- Often combined with ACL or meniscus injury

**Symptoms:**
- Pain on inner knee
- Swelling (may be minimal)
- Instability with grade II-III
- Difficulty bearing weight initially
- Pain with valgus stress

## Healing Timeline

**Good news:** The MCL has excellent blood supply and heals well.

- **Grade I:** 1-3 weeks
- **Grade II:** 3-6 weeks
- **Grade III:** 6-12 weeks (may need brace)

**Most MCL injuries do NOT require surgery** unless combined with other injuries.

## Phase 1: Protection and Early Motion (Week 1-2)

### PRICE Protocol
- **Protect:** Brace or crutches as needed
- **Rest:** Avoid aggravating activities
- **Ice:** 15-20 minutes, several times daily
- **Compression:** Elastic wrap or sleeve
- **Elevation:** Above heart when possible

### Range of Motion

**Heel Slides**
1. Lie on back
2. Slide heel toward buttock
3. Slide back out
4. 15-20 repetitions
5. Multiple times daily

**Passive Knee Extension**
1. Sit with leg extended
2. Roll towel under ankle (not knee)
3. Let gravity straighten knee
4. Hold 5-10 minutes

**Prone Hang** (Gentle)
1. Lie face down
2. Legs off end of bed
3. Let gravity extend knee
4. 5-10 minutes

### Quad Activation

**Quad Sets**
1. Sit with leg extended
2. Push back of knee into floor
3. Tighten thigh muscle
4. Hold 5-10 seconds
5. 20-30 repetitions

**Straight Leg Raise** (When Comfortable)
1. Lie on back, one knee bent
2. Tighten quad of straight leg
3. Lift leg 6-12 inches
4. 2-3 sets of 15

### Avoid Valgus Stress
- No activities that push knee inward
- Wear hinged brace if prescribed
- Avoid twisting

## Phase 2: Progressive Loading (Weeks 2-4)

### Continued ROM

**Active Knee Flexion**
1. Sit on edge of chair
2. Actively bend and straighten knee
3. Full range as tolerated
4. 20-30 repetitions

**Stationary Bike** (When Tolerated)
1. High seat initially
2. Low resistance
3. Focus on smooth motion
4. 10-15 minutes

### Strengthening

**Mini Squats**
1. Feet shoulder-width
2. Partial squat (pain-free range)
3. Weight evenly distributed
4. 3 sets of 15

**Step-Ups** (Low Step)
1. 4-6 inch step
2. Step up with involved leg
3. Control descent
4. 3 sets of 12

**Leg Press** (Light)
1. Both legs initially
2. Partial range
3. Progress depth and weight
4. 3 sets of 15

**Terminal Knee Extension**
1. Band around knee, anchored behind
2. Start with knee slightly bent
3. Straighten against resistance
4. 3 sets of 15

### Hip Strengthening

**Hip Abduction**
1. Side-lying, top leg straight
2. Lift toward ceiling
3. 3 sets of 15 each side

**Clamshells**
1. Side-lying, knees bent
2. Lift top knee
3. 3 sets of 15 each side

**Glute Bridge**
1. Lie on back, knees bent
2. Lift hips, squeeze glutes
3. 3 sets of 15

## Phase 3: Progressive Strengthening (Weeks 4-8)

### Increased Loading

**Full Squats** (As Tolerated)
1. Full depth if pain-free
2. Proper form
3. 3 sets of 12

**Lunges** (Forward)
1. Step forward into lunge
2. Control the movement
3. 3 sets of 10 each leg

**Single-Leg Press**
1. Progress to single-leg
2. Controlled range
3. 2-3 sets of 12 each

**Romanian Deadlift**
1. Hip hinge movement
2. Soft knees
3. 3 sets of 12

### Single-Leg Exercises

**Single-Leg Squat to Box**
1. Squat onto high box
2. Control descent
3. 2-3 sets of 10 each

**Single-Leg Bridge**
1. One leg extended
2. Bridge on single leg
3. 3 sets of 10 each

**Single-Leg Balance**
1. Stand on involved leg
2. Progress: eyes closed, unstable surface
3. 3×30 seconds

### Hamstring Strengthening

**Hamstring Curls**
1. Machine or band
2. Full range of motion
3. 3 sets of 15

**Nordic Curl** (Modified)
1. Kneel, partner holds ankles
2. Lean forward slowly (eccentric)
3. Use hands to catch
4. 2-3 sets of 6-8

## Phase 4: Agility and Sport Preparation (Weeks 6-12)

### Agility Progression

**Lateral Shuffles**
1. Side-step quickly
2. Low athletic stance
3. 2-3 sets of 20 yards

**Carioca/Grapevine**
1. Side movement with crossover
2. Builds lateral stability
3. 2-3 sets of 20 yards

**Figure-8s**
1. Run figure-8 pattern
2. Start large, progress smaller
3. 5-10 repetitions

**Cutting Drills**
1. Start at 45-degree angles
2. Progress to sharper cuts
3. Increase speed gradually

### Plyometrics

**Box Jumps** (Low)
1. Jump onto low box
2. Step down initially
3. 2-3 sets of 8

**Lateral Hops**
1. Hop side-to-side over line
2. Land softly
3. 2-3 sets of 10 each direction

**Single-Leg Hops** (When Ready)
1. Hop forward on one leg
2. Maintain balance
3. 2-3 sets of 6-8 each

### Sport-Specific Training

**Running Progression:**
1. Walk-jog intervals
2. Continuous jogging
3. Tempo runs
4. Sprints

**Sport-specific:**
1. Kicking (soccer)
2. Skating strides (hockey)
3. Cutting patterns (football, basketball)

## Return to Sport Criteria

**Before full return:**
- Full, pain-free range of motion
- Strength equal to uninvolved side
- No pain or instability with sport-specific movements
- Completed agility and plyometric progressions
- Passed functional testing

**Typical return:**
- Grade I: 1-3 weeks
- Grade II: 4-6 weeks
- Grade III: 8-12 weeks

## Bracing Considerations

### When to Use
- Grade II-III injuries initially
- During sport return (confidence/protection)
- As prescribed by physician

### Types
- Hinged knee brace (most common)
- Allows flexion/extension, limits valgus
- May wean off gradually

### Long-Term
- Many athletes return without brace
- Some continue for high-risk sports
- Individual preference and comfort

## Sample Weekly Program (Week 5)

### Day 1: Quad/Knee Focus
1. Warm-up: Bike 10 min
2. Squats: 3×12
3. Leg press: 3×15
4. Terminal knee extension: 3×15
5. Step-ups: 3×12 each
6. Stretching: 5 min

### Day 2: Hip/Posterior Chain
1. Warm-up: 5 min
2. Romanian deadlift: 3×12
3. Glute bridge: 3×15
4. Hip abduction: 3×15 each
5. Hamstring curls: 3×15
6. Single-leg balance: 3×30 sec

### Day 3: Rest or Light Activity

### Day 4: Single-Leg/Agility
1. Warm-up: 10 min
2. Single-leg squat: 2×10 each
3. Lunges: 3×10 each
4. Lateral shuffles: 3×20 yards
5. Balance exercises: 5 min

### Day 5: Sport-Specific (If Cleared)
1. Full warm-up
2. Sport-specific movements
3. Progressive intensity
4. Cool-down

## Preventing Re-Injury

- Continue strengthening program
- Proper warm-up before activity
- Address any biomechanical issues
- Wear brace if recommended
- Avoid fatigue-related errors

## When to Seek Help

**Contact your doctor if:**
- Severe instability
- Significant swelling that doesn't improve
- Locked knee
- Can't bear weight
- No improvement after 4-6 weeks

**May indicate:**
- Combined ligament injury
- Meniscus tear
- Need for imaging or specialist evaluation

## Key Takeaways

1. **MCL heals well without surgery** — excellent blood supply
2. **Protect early** — brace and controlled motion
3. **Progress through phases** — don't rush return
4. **Strengthen entire leg** — quads, hamstrings, hips
5. **Agility before sport** — prepare for demands
6. **Bracing helps initially** — may wean off later
7. **Return when ready** — meet objective criteria
8. **Most return fully** — prognosis is excellent

MCL injuries, while painful and frustrating, typically heal completely with proper rehabilitation. Stay patient, follow the progression, and you'll return to full activity.
