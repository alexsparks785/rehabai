# Golf Exercises: Build Rotation and Power for Better Drives

The golf swing is one of the most athletically demanding movements in sports—combining speed, coordination, and rotational force through a highly complex kinetic chain. The right exercises can add yards to your drives and keep you playing pain-free.

**Key insight:** The golf swing generates significant rotational forces. Limited hip or thoracic mobility forces compensation through the lower back—the #1 source of golf injuries.

---

## Understanding the Golf Swing Demands

### Physical Requirements

- **Hip mobility:** Internal and external rotation on both sides
- **Thoracic rotation:** Upper back needs ~45° rotation each direction
- **Core stability:** Transfer power from lower to upper body
- **Hip/glute strength:** Generate ground force
- **Scapular stability:** Control the arm swing

### Common Golf Injuries

| Area | Issue | Cause |
|------|-------|-------|
| Lower back | Pain, disc issues | Limited hip/thoracic mobility |
| Lead hip | Impingement | Poor rotation mechanics |
| Trail elbow | Golfer's elbow | Grip/swing pattern issues |
| Lead wrist | Tendinitis | Impact stress |
| Shoulders | Impingement | Poor scapular control |

## Pre-Round Warm-Up (10 Minutes)

Do this before every round. Cold muscles don't rotate well.

### Hip Circles

**How to do it:**
1. Stand on one leg (hold club for balance)
2. Make large circles with the other knee
3. 10 circles forward, 10 backward
4. Switch legs

### 90/90 Hip Switches

**How to do it:**
1. Sit with legs in 90/90 position (both knees at 90°)
2. Rotate hips to switch which leg is in front
3. Keep chest tall throughout
4. Perform 8-10 reps each side

### Thoracic Rotations with Club

**How to do it:**
1. Hold club across shoulders (behind neck)
2. Set up in golf posture
3. Rotate upper body while keeping hips stable
4. Feel stretch through mid-back
5. 10 rotations each direction

### Standing Hip Openers

**How to do it:**
1. Stand in golf stance width
2. Rotate hips as if making a backswing (no arm movement)
3. Feel hip loading on trail side
4. Rotate through to follow-through position
5. 10 slow, controlled reps

### Arm Circles and Shoulder Rolls

**How to do it:**
1. Large arm circles forward and backward (10 each)
2. Shoulder rolls forward and backward (10 each)
3. Cross-body arm swings (10 total)

## Hip Mobility for Golf

Limited hip rotation is the silent killer of golf swings. You need it on both sides.

### Hip 90/90 Stretch

**Why it matters:** Opens both hips simultaneously—internal rotation on one side, external on the other.

**How to do it:**
1. Sit with front leg at 90°, back leg at 90°
2. Lean forward over front shin
3. Keep chest up
4. Hold 30-60 seconds
5. Switch sides

**Golf tip:** The trail hip needs external rotation in the backswing; lead hip needs internal rotation.

### Half-Kneeling Hip Flexor Stretch

**Why it matters:** Tight hip flexors limit hip extension in the follow-through.

**How to do it:**
1. Kneel on one knee, other foot forward
2. Tuck pelvis slightly (flatten lower back)
3. Shift hips forward
4. Add same-side arm reach for more stretch
5. Hold 30-45 seconds each side

### Supine Hip Internal Rotation

**Why it matters:** Lead hip needs internal rotation at impact.

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back, knees bent, feet wide
2. Let knees fall inward one at a time
3. Use hand to gently increase rotation
4. Hold 30 seconds each side

### Seated Hip CARs

**Why it matters:** Controlled articular rotations maintain and improve hip range.

**How to do it:**
1. Sit in chair, one leg hanging free
2. Make slow, controlled circles with knee
3. Move through your full available range
4. 5 circles each direction, each hip

## Thoracic Mobility for Golf

Upper back rotation is where you find "hidden" yards in your swing.

### Open Book Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on side, knees bent 90°
2. Arms extended in front
3. Rotate top arm up and over, following with eyes
4. Keep knees stacked together
5. Hold 5-10 seconds at end range
6. 8-10 reps each side

### Thread the Needle

**How to do it:**
1. Start on all fours
2. Reach one arm under body, rotating torso
3. Let head and shoulder follow the reach
4. Return and reach same arm to ceiling
5. 8-10 reps each side

### Cat-Cow with Rotation

**How to do it:**
1. Start on all fours
2. Do a standard cat-cow motion
3. Add rotation: reach one arm under then up to ceiling
4. Alternate sides
5. 6-8 reps each side

### Quadruped Thoracic Rotation

**How to do it:**
1. Start on all fours
2. Place one hand behind head
3. Rotate elbow down toward opposite arm
4. Rotate up toward ceiling, following with eyes
5. Keep hips and lower back still
6. 10 reps each side

## Core Stability for Golf

The core transfers power from lower body to upper body. Weakness here means lost yards and injury risk.

### Dead Bug

**Why it matters:** Trains anti-extension while moving limbs—similar to maintaining posture during swing.

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back, arms toward ceiling, knees at 90°
2. Press lower back into floor
3. Slowly lower opposite arm and leg
4. Return without back lifting
5. Alternate sides, 8-10 reps each

### Pallof Press

**Why it matters:** Trains anti-rotation—exactly what your core does during the swing.

**How to do it:**
1. Stand perpendicular to cable or band anchor
2. Hold handle at chest
3. Press arms straight out
4. Resist rotation for 3-5 seconds
5. Return with control
6. 8-10 reps each side

### Bird Dog

**How to do it:**
1. Start on all fours
2. Extend opposite arm and leg simultaneously
3. Keep back flat and hips level
4. Hold 2-3 seconds
5. Alternate, 8-10 reps each side

### Side Plank with Rotation

**How to do it:**
1. Set up in side plank on forearm
2. Reach top arm under body (rotate torso)
3. Return and reach arm to ceiling (open up)
4. 8-10 reps each side

## Power and Strength Exercises

Building strength translates to clubhead speed.

### Medicine Ball Rotational Throws

**How to do it:**
1. Stand perpendicular to wall, feet shoulder-width
2. Hold medicine ball at hip
3. Rotate and throw into wall with power
4. Catch and reset
5. 8-10 throws each side

**Progression:** Start with 6-8lb ball, increase as power improves.

### Cable or Band Woodchops

**How to do it:**
1. Set cable high (or anchor band overhead)
2. Stand perpendicular to anchor
3. Pull diagonally down and across body
4. Rotate through hips and torso
5. Control return
6. 10-12 reps each side

### Goblet Squats

**Why it matters:** Hip and leg strength generates ground force.

**How to do it:**
1. Hold weight at chest
2. Squat to depth your hips allow
3. Push through whole foot to stand
4. 10-12 reps

### Single-Leg Romanian Deadlift

**Why it matters:** Hip hinge strength and single-leg stability—critical for weight transfer.

**How to do it:**
1. Stand on one leg, slight knee bend
2. Hinge at hip, reaching opposite hand toward ground
3. Keep back flat
4. Return to standing
5. 8-10 reps each side

### Hip Bridges with Rotation

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back, knees bent
2. Bridge up, squeezing glutes
3. At top, rotate pelvis slightly left, then right
4. Lower with control
5. 10-12 reps

## Glute Activation for Golf

Strong, active glutes stabilize the pelvis and generate power.

### Clamshells

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on side, knees bent 90°
2. Keep feet together, lift top knee
3. Don't let hips roll back
4. 15-20 reps each side

### Lateral Band Walks

**How to do it:**
1. Place band above knees
2. Slight squat position
3. Step sideways, maintaining tension
4. 15-20 steps each direction

### Glute Bridge March

**How to do it:**
1. Bridge up and hold
2. Slowly lift one foot off ground (knee bent)
3. Lower, switch sides
4. Keep hips level throughout
5. 8-10 lifts each side

## Sample Golf Fitness Program

### Off-Season (2-3x per week)

Focus on building mobility and strength.

**Warm-up (5 min):**
- Hip circles
- Thoracic rotations
- Arm circles

**Mobility (10 min):**
- 90/90 stretch: 60s each side
- Open book: 8-10 each side
- Half-kneeling hip flexor: 45s each side

**Strength (20 min):**
- Goblet squat: 3x12
- Single-leg RDL: 3x10 each
- Bird dog: 3x10 each
- Pallof press: 3x10 each
- Lateral band walks: 3x15 each way

**Power (10 min):**
- Medicine ball rotational throws: 3x8 each side
- Cable woodchops: 3x10 each side

### In-Season (1-2x per week)

Maintain mobility and strength without fatiguing for golf.

**Mobility (15 min):**
- Full mobility sequence (focus on hips and thoracic)

**Maintenance (15 min):**
- Dead bug: 2x10 each
- Clamshells: 2x15 each
- Goblet squat: 2x10
- Medicine ball throws: 2x6 each side

### Pre-Round (10 min)

Do this before every round:
- Hip circles
- 90/90 switches
- Thoracic rotations with club
- Standing hip openers
- Practice swings starting at 50% effort

## Common Mistakes

### Training Issues

- **All mobility, no strength:** Flexibility without stability creates injury risk
- **Skipping the warm-up:** Cold muscles don't rotate well
- **Training fatigued muscles before golf:** Do strength training on non-golf days
- **Ignoring the lead hip:** Most golfers only stretch the trail side

### Swing-Related Issues

- **Swaying instead of rotating:** Build hip stability to fix this
- **Lower back doing all the work:** Improve hip and thoracic mobility
- **Early extension (hips thrust toward ball):** Often indicates tight hip flexors

---

## Playing Through Pain

### When to Play

- Mild stiffness that warms up after a few holes
- Old injuries that are stable and managed
- Minor muscle soreness from exercise

### When to Rest

- Sharp pain during swing
- Pain that worsens during round
- Numbness or tingling
- Any acute injury symptoms

### Course Management When Sore

- Warm up more thoroughly
- Start with wedges, work up to driver
- Consider riding instead of walking
- Use wider stance for stability
- Shorten backswing if lower back is tight

---

## The Bottom Line

Golf fitness isn't about becoming a gym rat—it's about having the mobility to make a full turn and the stability to transfer power. A consistent program focusing on hip mobility, thoracic rotation, and core stability will add yards to your drives and years to your golf career.

**Start here:**
1. Do the pre-round warm-up before every round (non-negotiable)
2. Add 2-3 mobility sessions per week
3. Include strength training 1-2x per week in off-season
4. Focus on your limitations (tight hips? Work hips. Stiff upper back? Work thoracic.)

Your body will thank you on the back nine.
