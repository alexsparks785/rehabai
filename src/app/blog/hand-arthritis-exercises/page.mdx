# Hand Arthritis Exercises: Keep Your Fingers Mobile and Strong

Hand arthritis can make everyday tasks—buttoning shirts, opening jars, typing—challenging and painful. But here's the good news: gentle exercise is one of the most effective treatments for maintaining hand function.

**Key insight:** The goal isn't to "cure" arthritis through exercise, but to maintain mobility, reduce stiffness, and keep your hands functional for daily activities.

---

## Understanding Hand Arthritis

### Common Types

| Type | Characteristics | Common Locations |
|------|-----------------|------------------|
| **Osteoarthritis (OA)** | Wear and tear, bony nodules | Thumb base (CMC), fingertips (DIP), middle joints (PIP) |
| **Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA)** | Inflammatory, autoimmune | Knuckles (MCP), wrists, symmetrical |
| **Psoriatic Arthritis** | Associated with psoriasis | Can affect any joint, often asymmetrical |

### The Exercise Paradox

Many people with arthritis avoid using their hands because of pain. But this makes things worse:
- Less movement → more stiffness
- Less use → weaker muscles
- Weaker grip → harder daily tasks
- More compensation → more pain

**The solution:** Gentle, regular movement keeps joints lubricated and muscles strong.

---

## When to Exercise

### Best Times
- **Morning:** Combat overnight stiffness
- **After warming up:** Soak hands in warm water first
- **Before demanding tasks:** Prepare hands for activity
- **During breaks:** Combat computer/phone stiffness

### Heat Before, Ice After
- **Before exercise:** Warm water soak or heating pad (10-15 minutes)
- **After exercise:** Ice if joints feel warm or swollen (10-15 minutes)

### The 2-Hour Rule
If pain increases for more than 2 hours after exercise, you did too much. Scale back next time.

## Range of Motion Exercises

### Finger Tendon Glides

This sequence moves your finger tendons through their full range.

**How to do it:**
1. **Straight:** Start with fingers straight and together
2. **Hook:** Bend just the fingertips and middle joints (like making a hook)
3. **Fist:** Make a full fist
4. **Tabletop:** Bend fingers at knuckles only, keeping fingers straight
5. **Straight fist:** Make a fist with fingertips touching palm at base of fingers

**Repetitions:** Move through all 5 positions, 10 times. Perform 2-3 times daily.

### Finger Spreads

**How to do it:**
1. Place hand flat on table
2. Spread fingers as wide apart as possible
3. Hold 3-5 seconds
4. Bring fingers back together
5. Repeat 10 times

**Variation:** Do this in warm water for easier movement.

### Finger Lifts

**How to do it:**
1. Place hand flat on table, palm down
2. Lift one finger at a time as high as comfortable
3. Hold 2-3 seconds
4. Lower slowly
5. Repeat with each finger, 5-10 times

### Thumb Circles

**How to do it:**
1. Hold hand with fingers straight
2. Move thumb in large circles
3. Make 10 circles clockwise
4. Make 10 circles counterclockwise
5. Repeat with other hand

### Thumb Opposition

**How to do it:**
1. Touch thumb tip to each fingertip, one at a time
2. Make an "O" shape with each touch
3. Go slowly and deliberately
4. Continue back and forth for 10 rounds

**Variation:** Say each finger name as you touch it for cognitive engagement.

### Wrist Circles

**How to do it:**
1. Extend arm with hand relaxed
2. Rotate wrist in large circles
3. Make 10 circles clockwise
4. Make 10 circles counterclockwise
5. Repeat with other wrist

## Strengthening Exercises

### Grip Strengthening

#### Soft Ball Squeeze
**Equipment:** Soft stress ball or tennis ball

**How to do it:**
1. Hold ball in palm
2. Squeeze as firmly as comfortable (not through pain)
3. Hold 3-5 seconds
4. Release slowly
5. Repeat 10-15 times
6. Rest and repeat with other hand

**Progression:** Start with very soft ball, progress to firmer balls over weeks.

#### Putty Exercises
Therapy putty allows varied resistance and multiple exercises:

**Gross grip squeeze:**
- Roll putty into ball
- Squeeze in palm

**Finger press:**
- Press individual fingers into putty

**Pinch and pull:**
- Pinch putty between thumb and fingers
- Pull apart

**Spread:**
- Put putty around fingers
- Spread fingers apart against resistance

### Pinch Strengthening

#### Lateral Pinch (Key Pinch)
**How to do it:**
1. Hold small object (key, coin) between thumb and side of index finger
2. Squeeze firmly
3. Hold 5 seconds
4. Relax
5. Repeat 10 times

#### Tip Pinch
**How to do it:**
1. Touch thumb tip to index fingertip
2. Press together firmly
3. Hold 5 seconds
4. Repeat with each finger
5. Perform 5 repetitions per finger

### Rubber Band Exercises

**Finger extension strengthening:**
1. Place rubber band around all fingertips
2. Spread fingers apart against band resistance
3. Hold 3-5 seconds
4. Relax and repeat
5. Perform 10-15 repetitions

**Progression:** Use multiple bands or thicker bands for more resistance.

### Towel Squeeze

**How to do it:**
1. Roll small towel into cylinder
2. Grip towel and squeeze
3. Hold 5 seconds
4. Relax
5. Repeat 10 times

## Thumb-Specific Exercises

Thumb arthritis (CMC joint) is extremely common and deserves special attention.

### Thumb IP Flexion

**How to do it:**
1. Hold thumb stable at middle joint with other hand
2. Bend just the tip of thumb
3. Straighten
4. Repeat 10-15 times

### Thumb MP Flexion

**How to do it:**
1. Hold thumb stable at base
2. Bend thumb at middle joint only
3. Straighten
4. Repeat 10-15 times

### Thumb Abduction/Adduction

**How to do it:**
1. Place hand flat on table
2. Move thumb away from hand (abduction)
3. Bring thumb back to touch index finger (adduction)
4. Repeat 10-15 times

### Thumb Web Space Stretch

Maintains the space between thumb and index finger.

**How to do it:**
1. Use other hand to gently stretch thumb away from index finger
2. Hold 15-30 seconds
3. Repeat 3-5 times

### Thumb Strengthening (Isometric)

**How to do it:**
1. Place thumb pad against index finger side
2. Press thumb toward index finger
3. Resist with index finger so no movement occurs
4. Hold 5 seconds
5. Repeat 10 times

## Daily Functional Exercises

### Making a Fist

**How to do it:**
1. Start with fingers straight
2. Slowly curl fingers into palm
3. Make a gentle fist (don't squeeze hard)
4. Open hand slowly and stretch fingers wide
5. Repeat 10 times

### "Playing Piano"

**How to do it:**
1. Place fingertips on table as if playing piano
2. Tap each finger alternately
3. Continue for 30-60 seconds
4. Great for dexterity

### Coin Flip

**How to do it:**
1. Place coin flat in palm
2. Use fingers to flip coin over (no help from other hand)
3. Flip back
4. Repeat 10 times

### Button Practice

**How to do it:**
1. Practice buttoning and unbuttoning shirt
2. Start with larger buttons
3. Progress to smaller buttons
4. Builds functional dexterity

## Sample Daily Routine

### Morning Warm-Up (5-10 minutes)

1. Soak hands in warm water (5 minutes)
2. Finger tendon glides (10 repetitions)
3. Finger spreads (10 repetitions)
4. Thumb circles (10 each direction)
5. Make a fist (10 repetitions)
6. Wrist circles (10 each direction)

### Strengthening Routine (3x per week)

1. Soft ball squeeze (15 repetitions each hand)
2. Rubber band finger extension (15 repetitions)
3. Pinch exercises (10 repetitions each type)
4. Putty exercises (5 minutes varied)
5. Towel squeeze (10 repetitions)

### Throughout Day

- Stretch hands every 30-60 minutes during computer work
- Do finger spreads while watching TV
- Practice thumb opposition during breaks
- Use full range of motion in daily tasks

## Activity Modifications

### Protect Your Joints

| Instead of... | Try... |
|--------------|--------|
| Pinching with fingertips | Using whole hand |
| Twisting jars | Using rubber gripper |
| Carrying bags by handles | Forearm carry or cart |
| Gripping pens tightly | Built-up pen grips |
| Repetitive finger motions | Taking frequent breaks |

### Helpful Tools

- Jar openers
- Built-up utensil handles
- Button hooks
- Large grip pens
- Electric can openers
- Lever door handles

## Rheumatoid Arthritis Considerations

If you have RA, exercise during flares requires modifications:

### During Active Flare
- Gentle range of motion only
- No strengthening
- Move each joint through available range 1-2 times daily
- Focus on preventing stiffness without aggravating inflammation

### Between Flares
- Full strengthening program
- Progress as tolerated
- Stop if joint becomes warm or swollen

### Joint Protection Principles
- Respect pain
- Use largest joint possible for tasks
- Avoid sustained gripping
- Alternate hands during repetitive tasks
- Use adaptive equipment

## Aquatic Exercises

Warm water exercise is excellent for hand arthritis.

### In Warm Water
1. Open and close fist (10 times)
2. Finger spreads (10 times)
3. Stir water with fingers
4. Squeeze water out of sponge
5. Pick up marbles from bottom of basin

**Water temperature:** 92-96°F (33-36°C) for comfort.

---

## Paraffin Wax Treatment

Often used before exercise for deeper heat penetration.

**How to use:**
1. Dip hands in warm paraffin wax
2. Let layer dry
3. Dip again (repeat 6-10 times)
4. Wrap in plastic and towel
5. Leave on 15-20 minutes
6. Peel off and exercise

**Note:** Available at physical therapy clinics and for home use.

---

## When to See a Doctor

Seek professional evaluation if you experience:

- Sudden increase in pain or swelling
- Joint warmth or redness
- Numbness or tingling (could indicate carpal tunnel)
- Significant loss of function
- Symptoms not improving with exercise
- New deformity developing

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Move it or lose it** — Regular gentle exercise maintains hand function better than rest
2. **Warm up first** — Heat before exercise reduces stiffness and pain
3. **Balance range of motion and strength** — Both are important
4. **Protect your joints** — Use proper body mechanics and adaptive tools
5. **Consistency beats intensity** — Daily gentle exercise trumps occasional hard workouts
6. **Respect the 2-hour rule** — If pain lasts more than 2 hours after exercise, do less next time

**Sample weekly schedule:**
- Daily: Morning range of motion routine (5-10 minutes)
- 3x weekly: Strengthening exercises
- Throughout day: Stretch breaks during repetitive activities

Hand arthritis is manageable. With consistent gentle exercise and smart joint protection, you can maintain the hand function you need for daily life.
