# Relaxation Exercises: Calm Your Body and Mind

Stress lives in your body. Tension in your shoulders, a clenched jaw, shallow breathing—your body holds onto stress even when your mind wants to relax. These physical relaxation exercises release that tension, calming both body and mind.

## How Stress Affects Your Body

### The Tension Cycle

1. Stress triggers a physical response
2. Muscles tighten, breathing shallows
3. Physical tension signals "danger" to your brain
4. Brain stays in stress mode
5. Cycle continues

### Common Stress Holding Patterns

- **Shoulders:** Rise toward ears, tighten constantly
- **Jaw:** Clench during day, grind at night
- **Neck:** Tightens, contributes to headaches
- **Lower back:** Muscles grip, restrict breathing
- **Hands:** Clench, grip tightly
- **Feet:** Toes curl, arches tighten

### Breaking the Cycle

Physical relaxation exercises interrupt this pattern. By consciously releasing muscle tension, you signal safety to your nervous system, allowing genuine relaxation.

## Progressive Muscle Relaxation (PMR)

The most evidence-based relaxation technique.

### The Process

Systematically tense and release each muscle group, learning the difference between tension and relaxation.

### Full Body PMR (15-20 minutes)

**Find a comfortable position (lying down or seated)**

For each muscle group:
- Tense for 5-7 seconds
- Release suddenly
- Notice the relaxation for 15-20 seconds
- Move to next group

**Sequence:**

1. **Feet:** Curl toes tightly, then release
2. **Calves:** Point toes away (tense calves), then release
3. **Thighs:** Squeeze thighs together, then release
4. **Glutes:** Squeeze buttocks, then release
5. **Abdomen:** Tighten stomach muscles, then release
6. **Chest:** Take deep breath, hold, then release
7. **Hands:** Make tight fists, then release
8. **Forearms:** Bend wrists toward forearms (tense), then release
9. **Upper arms:** Curl arms to tense biceps, then release
10. **Shoulders:** Shrug toward ears, then release
11. **Neck:** Gently press head back against surface, then release
12. **Face:** Scrunch entire face (eyes, mouth), then release
13. **Full body:** Tense everything at once, then release

**End with 2-3 minutes of quiet breathing**

### Quick PMR (5 minutes)

When time is limited:

1. **Lower body:** Tense all leg muscles at once, release
2. **Core:** Tense abs and glutes, release
3. **Upper body:** Tense arms, shoulders, hands, release
4. **Face and neck:** Tense, release
5. **Full body:** Everything at once, release

## Breathing-Based Relaxation

### Diaphragmatic Breathing

1. Lie on back or sit comfortably
2. One hand on chest, one on belly
3. Breathe in slowly through nose
4. Let your belly rise (hand on belly moves)
5. Chest stays relatively still
6. Exhale slowly through mouth
7. Belly falls

*Duration: 5-10 minutes*

### 4-7-8 Breathing

1. Inhale through nose for 4 counts
2. Hold for 7 counts
3. Exhale through mouth for 8 counts
4. Repeat 4 cycles

*This activates the parasympathetic nervous system*

### Box Breathing

1. Inhale for 4 counts
2. Hold for 4 counts
3. Exhale for 4 counts
4. Hold for 4 counts
5. Repeat

*Good for quick calming during stressful moments*

### Extended Exhale

Simply make your exhale longer than your inhale:
- Inhale for 4 counts
- Exhale for 6-8 counts
- This activates relaxation response

## Gentle Stretching for Relaxation

### Neck Release

1. Sit tall
2. Tip right ear toward right shoulder
3. Let gravity provide the stretch
4. Breathe deeply
5. Hold 30-60 seconds
6. Switch sides

### Shoulder Shrug and Release

1. Inhale and shrug shoulders to ears
2. Hold 5 seconds
3. Exhale and drop suddenly
4. Notice the relaxation
5. Repeat 5 times

### Chest Opener

1. Stand or sit
2. Clasp hands behind back
3. Lift and open chest
4. Breathe into the space you create
5. Hold 30-60 seconds

### Child's Pose

1. Kneel, sit back on heels
2. Fold forward, reaching arms ahead
3. Rest forehead on floor or pillow
4. Breathe into your back
5. Hold 1-3 minutes

*One of the most calming positions*

### Supine Twist

1. Lie on back
2. Pull knees to chest
3. Let both knees fall to one side
4. Arms out, head turned opposite
5. Relax completely
6. Hold 1-2 minutes per side

### Legs Up the Wall

1. Sit next to a wall
2. Swing legs up the wall as you lie back
3. Scoot hips close to wall
4. Arms relaxed at sides
5. Breathe and let gravity work

*Hold 5-10 minutes—deeply calming*

### Happy Baby

1. Lie on back
2. Pull knees toward armpits
3. Hold outside of feet
4. Gently rock side to side
5. Hold 1-2 minutes

## Body Scan Meditation

### The Practice

1. Lie down comfortably
2. Close your eyes
3. Start at your feet
4. Notice any sensations without judging
5. Consciously relax that area
6. Move up through your body
7. End at the top of your head

### Body Scan Sequence

- Toes and feet
- Ankles and lower legs
- Knees and upper legs
- Hips and pelvis
- Lower back and abdomen
- Chest and upper back
- Hands and forearms
- Upper arms and shoulders
- Neck and throat
- Face (jaw, cheeks, eyes, forehead)
- Top of head

*Duration: 10-20 minutes*

## Active Relaxation Movements

### Gentle Shaking

1. Stand with feet hip-width apart
2. Begin gently bouncing or shaking
3. Let arms flop loosely
4. Shake out hands, feet, whole body
5. Continue for 1-2 minutes
6. Stop and notice the stillness

*Releases stored physical tension*

### Slow Spinal Wave

1. Stand with feet hip-width apart
2. Tuck chin to chest
3. Slowly roll down, one vertebra at a time
4. Let arms hang, head heavy
5. Pause at bottom
6. Slowly roll back up

*Reps: 5-8, very slowly*

### Cat-Cow Flow

1. On all fours
2. Inhale: arch back, look up (cow)
3. Exhale: round back, tuck chin (cat)
4. Move with breath
5. Make it slow and fluid

*Duration: 2-3 minutes*

### Windmill Arms

1. Stand tall
2. Let arms swing loosely
3. Twist torso gently side to side
4. Arms follow naturally
5. Keep it loose and easy

*Duration: 1-2 minutes*

## Quick Relaxation Techniques

### The 60-Second Reset

When you have only a minute:

1. Three deep breaths
2. Shrug and release shoulders (3 times)
3. Unclench jaw, let tongue drop from roof of mouth
4. One more deep breath

### Tension Scan

Periodically scan your body:
- Are my shoulders by my ears?
- Is my jaw clenched?
- Am I holding my breath?
- Are my hands tight?

Release what you find.

### Sigh It Out

1. Inhale fully
2. Exhale with an audible sigh ("ahhhh")
3. Let your whole body soften
4. Repeat 3-5 times

*Surprisingly effective*

## Evening Relaxation Routine (15 minutes)

### Transition from Day (5 minutes)

1. Gentle shaking: 1 minute
2. Slow spinal waves: 3-4 reps
3. Shoulder shrug and release: 5 times

### Floor Work (7 minutes)

1. Cat-cow flow: 2 minutes
2. Child's pose: 2 minutes
3. Supine twist: 1 minute per side
4. Happy baby: 1 minute

### Final Relaxation (3 minutes)

1. Legs up the wall: 2 minutes
2. Or simply lying flat with diaphragmatic breathing

## Creating a Relaxation Practice

### Environment

- Quiet space when possible
- Comfortable temperature
- Dim lighting if available
- Phone on silent

### Timing

- **Morning:** Short practice (5 minutes) to start day calm
- **Midday:** Brief reset (1-3 minutes) during stress
- **Evening:** Longer practice (15-20 minutes) to unwind

### Consistency

- Daily practice builds skill
- Relaxation becomes easier with practice
- Even 5 minutes helps
- Same time each day helps habit formation

## When Relaxation Is Difficult

### Racing Mind

- Focus on physical sensations, not thoughts
- Use body scan or PMR (gives mind something to do)
- Accept that thoughts will come; return focus to body

### Can't Slow Down

- Start with active relaxation (shaking, movement)
- Then transition to stillness
- Movement may be necessary first

### Physical Pain

- Modify positions as needed
- Focus on areas that feel good
- Avoid positions that increase pain
- Consider seated or standing options

### Falling Asleep

- If resting before bed, that's fine
- If you want to stay awake, sit up slightly
- Keep eyes partially open

## Key Takeaways

1. **Stress lives in your body**—physical release creates mental calm
2. **Progressive muscle relaxation is highly effective**—tense and release systematically
3. **Breathing is your fastest tool**—extended exhale activates relaxation
4. **Gentle stretching promotes calm**—child's pose, twists, legs up the wall
5. **Quick techniques help in the moment**—60-second reset, sighing
6. **Consistency matters**—regular practice makes relaxation easier
7. **Body and mind are connected**—calming one calms the other

You can't think your way out of stress, but you can release your body into relaxation. These physical techniques give you the power to interrupt the stress cycle and create genuine calm.
