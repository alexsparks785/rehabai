# Hip Pain Exercises: Relieve Pain and Restore Function

Hip pain can come from many sources—and the right exercises depend on where and why it hurts. The good news? Most hip pain responds well to targeted exercise, often better than other treatments.

**Key insight:** Hip pain often stems from muscle imbalances, weakness, or tightness rather than structural problems. Addressing these through exercise resolves most cases.

---

## Understanding Hip Pain

### Pain Location Guide

| Location | Common Causes |
|----------|---------------|
| **Front of hip** | Hip flexor strain, labral tear, impingement |
| **Side of hip** | Bursitis, gluteus medius tendinopathy, IT band |
| **Back of hip** | Piriformis syndrome, sciatica, SI joint |
| **Groin** | Adductor strain, labral tear, hip joint arthritis |
| **Deep in joint** | Arthritis, labral tear, impingement |

### Common Conditions

- **Hip bursitis:** Inflammation of bursa on outside of hip
- **Hip flexor strain:** Overuse or acute injury to front of hip
- **Gluteus medius tendinopathy:** Side of hip, often confused with bursitis
- **Piriformis syndrome:** Deep buttock pain, may radiate down leg
- **Hip impingement (FAI):** Bone shapes cause pinching
- **Hip osteoarthritis:** Degeneration of joint cartilage

## General Hip Stretches

### Hip Flexor Stretch

Essential for most hip pain.

**How to do it:**
1. Half-kneeling position
2. Tuck pelvis under (posterior tilt)
3. Squeeze back glute
4. Lean forward slightly
5. Hold 30-60 seconds each side

**Key:** The pelvic tuck is critical—without it, you'll arch your back instead of stretching the hip.

### Figure 4 Stretch

Targets piriformis and external rotators.

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back
2. Cross right ankle over left knee
3. Pull left leg toward chest
4. Feel stretch in right hip/buttock
5. Hold 30-60 seconds each side

### 90/90 Stretch

Targets both hips simultaneously.

**How to do it:**
1. Sit with front leg at 90° (hip and knee)
2. Back leg also at 90°, out to side
3. Sit tall (don't lean back)
4. Hold 1-2 minutes each side

### Adductor Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Wide stance, toes pointed forward
2. Shift weight to one side
3. Bend that knee, keep other leg straight
4. Feel stretch in inner thigh of straight leg
5. Hold 30-45 seconds each side

### Quadriceps Stretch

Affects hip flexion via rectus femoris.

**How to do it:**
1. Stand on one leg (hold support)
2. Pull other heel toward buttock
3. Keep knees together
4. Tuck pelvis under
5. Hold 30-45 seconds each side

## Hip Strengthening Exercises

### Glute Bridge

Foundation for hip strength.

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back, knees bent, feet flat
2. Squeeze glutes, lift hips
3. Create straight line from shoulders to knees
4. Hold 2-3 seconds at top
5. Lower slowly
6. Perform 15-20 reps, 3 sets

### Clamshells

Targets gluteus medius—critical for hip stability.

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on side, knees bent 45°
2. Keep feet together
3. Lift top knee toward ceiling
4. Don't let pelvis roll back
5. Lower slowly
6. Perform 15-20 reps, 3 sets each side

### Side-Lying Hip Abduction

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on side, bottom knee bent
2. Top leg straight
3. Lift top leg toward ceiling
4. Keep toes forward or slightly down
5. Lower slowly
6. Perform 15-20 reps, 3 sets each side

### Fire Hydrant

**How to do it:**
1. On hands and knees
2. Keep knee bent 90°
3. Lift knee out to side
4. Don't shift weight excessively
5. Lower with control
6. Perform 12-15 each side, 2-3 sets

### Standing Hip Abduction

**How to do it:**
1. Stand on one leg, hold support
2. Lift other leg out to side
3. Keep torso upright
4. Lower with control
5. Perform 15 reps each leg, 2-3 sets

### Hip Hinge

**How to do it:**
1. Stand with feet hip-width apart
2. Push hips back, keeping back flat
3. Slight knee bend
4. Feel hamstring stretch
5. Squeeze glutes to return
6. Perform 15-20 reps

## Exercises by Condition

### Hip Bursitis / Greater Trochanteric Pain

**Priority:** Strengthen hip abductors without aggravating

**Key exercises:**
1. Isometric hip abduction (press against wall)
2. Side-lying hip abduction (avoid crossing midline)
3. Glute bridge
4. Clam shells

**Avoid initially:**
- Direct side-lying on painful hip
- IT band stretching (can aggravate)
- Crossing legs

### Hip Flexor Pain

**Priority:** Stretch and gradually strengthen

**Key exercises:**
1. Hip flexor stretch (gentle)
2. Supine hip flexion (lie on back, lift knee)
3. Standing hip marches
4. Glute strengthening (to balance hip flexors)

**Avoid:**
- Deep hip flexion
- Sit-ups and leg raises
- Running until pain-free

### Piriformis Syndrome

**Priority:** Release and stretch piriformis, strengthen glutes

**Key exercises:**
1. Figure 4 stretch
2. Pigeon pose
3. Tennis ball piriformis release
4. Glute bridges
5. Clamshells

### Hip Impingement (FAI)

**Priority:** Strengthen within safe range, avoid pinching positions

**Key exercises:**
1. Glute bridges
2. Clamshells
3. Hip hinge (don't go too deep)
4. Core stability (dead bug, bird dog)

**Avoid:**
- Deep hip flexion (past 90°)
- Internal rotation with flexion
- Deep squats, at least initially

### Hip Osteoarthritis

**Priority:** Maintain mobility, strengthen supporting muscles

**Key exercises:**
1. Gentle range of motion (hip circles, leg swings)
2. Glute bridges
3. Swimming or cycling for cardio
4. Straight leg raises
5. Hip strengthening in pain-free range

**Follow the 2-hour rule:** If pain increases for >2 hours after exercise, you did too much.

## Mobility Exercises

### Hip Circles

**How to do it:**
1. Stand on one leg, hold support
2. Make large circles with other leg
3. 10 circles clockwise
4. 10 circles counterclockwise
5. Repeat other leg

### Hip CARs (Controlled Articular Rotations)

**How to do it:**
1. Stand on one leg, hold support
2. Lift knee high (flexion)
3. Open knee out to side (abduction + external rotation)
4. Extend leg behind (extension)
5. Return to start
6. Reverse direction
7. 5 circles each direction, each hip

### Leg Swings

**Front to Back:**
1. Stand sideways to wall
2. Swing leg forward and backward
3. Let momentum create movement
4. 15-20 swings each leg

**Side to Side:**
1. Face wall
2. Swing leg across body and out to side
3. 15-20 swings each leg

### World's Greatest Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Deep lunge, hands inside front foot
2. Drop elbow toward instep
3. Rotate, reaching arm toward ceiling
4. Straighten front leg for hamstring stretch
5. Return to lunge
6. 5-8 reps each side

## Sample Programs

| Exercise | Sets × Reps |
|----------|-------------|

## Red Flags (See Doctor)

- Pain following trauma or fall
- Severe, sudden-onset pain
- Unable to bear weight
- Visible deformity
- Significant swelling
- Night pain that doesn't improve with position
- Numbness or weakness in leg
- Pain not improving after 6-8 weeks of exercise

---

## Progress Indicators

### Good Signs

- Decreased pain with daily activities
- Improved range of motion
- Stronger hip during exercises
- Better tolerance for walking, stairs
- Reduced morning stiffness

### Timeline Expectations

| Timeframe | Expected Progress |
|-----------|-------------------|
| 2 weeks | Pain management, beginning strength |
| 4-6 weeks | Noticeable improvement in function |
| 8-12 weeks | Significant strength gains |
| 3+ months | Near-normal function for most conditions |

## Key Takeaways

1. **Location matters** — Different pain locations need different approaches
2. **Strength is key** — Weak glutes contribute to most hip pain
3. **Don't over-stretch** — Stretching alone rarely solves hip pain
4. **Include mobility** — Hip CARs and circles maintain joint health
5. **Progress gradually** — Don't push through sharp pain
6. **Address the whole chain** — Core, hips, and legs work together

**Daily minimum:**
- Hip flexor stretch: 30 sec each side
- Glute bridges: 15 reps
- Clamshells: 15 each side
- Hip circles: 10 each direction
- Total: 5-7 minutes

Most hip pain responds well to consistent exercise. Find your specific issue, address it systematically, and stay patient through the recovery process.
