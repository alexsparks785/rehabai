# Hammer Toe Exercises: Straighten and Strengthen Your Toes

Hammer toes—and related conditions like claw toe and mallet toe—cause the smaller toes to bend abnormally. When caught early, exercises can help maintain flexibility and potentially improve alignment. Even in later stages, exercises reduce pain and slow progression.

## Understanding Toe Deformities

### Hammer Toe
- Middle joint (PIP) bends downward
- Tip of toe may point down or be neutral
- Most common in second toe

### Claw Toe
- Middle joint (PIP) AND end joint (DIP) bend downward
- Base joint (MTP) may hyperextend upward
- Toe resembles a claw

### Mallet Toe
- Only end joint (DIP) bends downward
- Tip of toe points down
- Middle joint stays straight

**Common causes:**
- Tight footwear (pointed, narrow, high heels)
- Muscle imbalance (flexors overpower extensors)
- Nerve conditions
- Arthritis
- Trauma
- Genetics

## Stages of Deformity

### Flexible Stage
- Toe can be manually straightened
- Exercises most effective
- Conservative treatment usually successful

### Semi-Rigid Stage
- Toe partially straightens manually
- Some permanent changes
- Exercises still helpful

### Rigid Stage
- Toe cannot be straightened
- Structural changes fixed
- Exercises focus on comfort; surgery may be needed

**Key insight:** The earlier you start exercises, the better the outcome.

## Stretching Exercises

### Passive Toe Extension

**How to do it:**
1. Sit comfortably
2. Grasp the bent toe
3. Gently straighten all joints
4. Hold 30 seconds
5. Repeat 5-10 times each toe

**Do this multiple times daily** for best results.

### Passive Toe Flexion

**How to do it:**
1. Grasp toe
2. Gently bend all joints (make a fist with the toe)
3. Hold 15-30 seconds
4. Maintains flexibility in both directions

### Plantar Fascia and Toe Flexor Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Sit with foot on opposite knee
2. Pull all toes back toward shin
3. Should feel stretch on bottom of foot
4. Hold 30 seconds
5. Repeat 3-5 times

### Towel Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Place towel on floor
2. Put toes on towel, heel on floor
3. Pull towel toward you with hands
4. Stretches the top of the toes and foot
5. Hold 30 seconds

### Bottle Roll Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Sit in chair
2. Place foot on round bottle (frozen water bottle works well)
3. Roll bottle under arch and toes
4. Stretches plantar structures
5. 2-3 minutes each foot

## Strengthening Exercises

### Toe Spreads (Abduction)

**How to do it:**
1. Sit or stand with feet flat
2. Spread all toes apart
3. Hold 5 seconds
4. Relax
5. 15-20 repetitions

### Toe Curls (Flexion Strengthening)

**How to do it:**
1. Sit with feet flat
2. Curl all toes under, gripping the floor
3. Hold 5 seconds
4. Release
5. 15-20 repetitions

### Toe Extensions

**How to do it:**
1. Sit with feet flat
2. Lift all toes off ground while keeping ball of foot down
3. Hold 5 seconds
4. Lower
5. 15-20 repetitions

### Isolated Toe Movements

**Lifting:**
1. Try to lift affected toe(s) independently
2. Hold 5 seconds
3. This retrains the extensor muscles

**Pressing:**
1. Press affected toe into ground while lifting others
2. Strengthens flexors in a functional position

### Towel Scrunches

**How to do it:**
1. Place towel flat on floor
2. Scrunch towel toward you using toes
3. Focus on pulling with all toes evenly
4. 2-3 full scrunches

### Marble Pickups

**How to do it:**
1. Place marbles on floor
2. Pick up with toes, place in cup
3. Use different parts of foot
4. 15-20 pickups

### Resistance Band Exercises

**Toe Flexion with Band:**
1. Loop band around top of toes
2. Hold band ends, provide resistance
3. Curl toes against resistance
4. 15-20 repetitions

**Toe Extension with Band:**
1. Loop band under toes
2. Anchor band with hands
3. Lift toes against resistance
4. 15-20 repetitions

### Paper Crumples

**How to do it:**
1. Place sheet of paper on floor
2. Crumple paper into ball using only toes
3. Then try to flatten it out
4. Works multiple toe muscles

## Intrinsic Muscle Exercises

### Short Foot Exercise

**How to do it:**
1. Sit with feet flat
2. Without curling toes, lift the arch
3. Draw ball of foot toward heel
4. Hold 5-10 seconds
5. 15-20 repetitions

### Toe Yoga

**Big Toe Down, Little Toes Up:**
1. Press big toe into ground
2. Lift small toes
3. Hold 5 seconds

**Little Toes Down, Big Toe Up:**
1. Press small toes into ground
2. Lift big toe
3. Hold 5 seconds

**Alternate between the two** for 15 repetitions.

### Piano Toes

**How to do it:**
1. Lift all toes
2. Lower one at a time, like playing piano
3. Start with big toe, work to pinky
4. Then reverse
5. Builds individual toe control

## Manual Techniques

### Toe Mobilization

**Joint Distraction:**
1. Grasp toe above and below a joint
2. Gently pull apart (traction)
3. Hold 10 seconds
4. Repeat for each joint of affected toe

**Joint Glides:**
1. Stabilize bone on one side of joint
2. Glide bone on other side up/down
3. Small oscillations
4. Maintains joint mobility

### Soft Tissue Massage

**Plantar Foot Massage:**
1. Use thumbs to massage bottom of foot
2. Work from heel to ball of foot
3. Spend extra time on tight areas
4. 5 minutes per foot

**Toe Massage:**
1. Massage each toe individually
2. Work muscles on top and bottom
3. Gentle stretching while massaging

## Splinting and Taping

### Toe Spacers

Silicone separators between toes:
- Stretch tight structures
- Encourage better alignment
- Use during exercises and at rest

### Toe Splints

Devices that hold toes straight:
- Use at night
- May slow progression
- Most helpful in flexible stages

### Buddy Taping

Taping bent toe to adjacent straight toe:
- Provides corrective pull
- Comfortable for daily wear
- Change tape daily

### Crest Pads

Cushions placed under toes:
- Support bent joints
- Reduce pressure on toe tips
- Improve comfort

## Footwear Recommendations

### What to Look For
- Wide, deep toe box
- Low or no heel
- Soft, flexible materials
- Room for toes to move

### What to Avoid
- Narrow, pointed toe boxes
- High heels
- Tight, constricting shoes
- Thin soles

### Consider
- Shoes designed for toe problems
- Depth shoes (extra volume in toe box)
- Custom orthotics if needed

## Exercise Progression

### Week 1-2: Focus on Flexibility
- Passive stretches: 3x daily, all toes
- Towel stretch: 30 sec, 3 times
- Gentle active ROM

### Week 3-4: Add Strengthening
- Continue stretches
- Add toe curls and extensions
- Begin marble pickups and towel scrunches

### Week 5+: Progress and Maintain
- All stretches and strengthening
- Add resistance band exercises
- Include short foot and toe yoga
- Use splints/spacers consistently

## Sample Daily Routine

### Morning (5-7 minutes)
1. Passive toe stretches: 30 sec each toe
2. Plantar fascia stretch: 30 sec
3. Toe spreads: 15 reps
4. Toe curls: 15 reps
5. Toe extensions: 15 reps

### Midday (3-5 minutes)
1. Passive stretches: 30 sec each toe
2. Short foot exercise: 15 reps
3. Wear toe spacers for 30-60 min

### Evening (10 minutes)
1. Bottle roll: 2-3 min each foot
2. Towel scrunches: 2 sets
3. Marble pickups: 15-20
4. Toe yoga: 15 reps each variation
5. Piano toes: 5 complete sequences
6. Passive stretches: 30 sec each toe
7. Apply toe splints for sleeping

## When to Seek Help

### See a Podiatrist If:
- Toe is rigid (can't be straightened)
- Significant pain despite exercises
- Corns or ulcers developing
- Diabetes or circulation problems
- No improvement after 6-8 weeks

### Surgical Options:
- Tendon release
- Tendon transfer
- Joint resection (arthroplasty)
- Fusion (arthrodesis)

**Post-surgical rehabilitation** includes many of the same exercises.

## Special Considerations

### Diabetes
- Check feet daily
- Never force stretches
- Watch for wounds on bent toes
- See podiatrist regularly

### Arthritis
- Gentler stretching
- May need modifications
- Focus on maintaining function

### Neurological Conditions
- Work with healthcare team
- May need custom orthotics
- Focus on protection and function

## Key Takeaways

1. **Start early** — flexible toes respond best to exercise
2. **Stretch multiple times daily** — consistency is key
3. **Strengthen toe extensors** — counteract the flexor tightness
4. **Use spacers and splints** — support alignment between exercises
5. **Wear proper shoes** — wide toe box, low heel
6. **Do towel scrunches and marble pickups** — functional strengthening
7. **Be patient** — changes take weeks to months
8. **Seek help if rigid** — surgery may be needed for fixed deformities

Hammer toes and related conditions are progressive, but early intervention with consistent exercise can maintain flexibility, improve function, and often prevent the need for surgery. Even in later stages, exercises help manage symptoms and slow further progression.
