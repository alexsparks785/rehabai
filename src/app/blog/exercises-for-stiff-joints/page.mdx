# Exercises for Stiff Joints: Restore Mobility and Reduce Pain

Stiff joints make everything harder—getting out of bed, climbing stairs, even turning your head. While it's tempting to avoid movement, gentle exercise is actually one of the best treatments for joint stiffness. The right movements lubricate joints, improve blood flow, and maintain the range of motion you need.

**Key insight:** Joints need movement to stay healthy. Synovial fluid—the lubricant inside your joints—gets distributed through motion. "Motion is lotion."

---

## What Causes Joint Stiffness

### Common Causes

- **Inactivity:** Prolonged sitting or bed rest
- **Morning stiffness:** Fluids settle overnight
- **Arthritis:** Osteoarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis
- **Injury recovery:** Post-surgical or post-injury
- **Aging:** Natural decrease in cartilage and synovial fluid
- **Inflammation:** From overuse or medical conditions

### When to See a Doctor

- Stiffness lasting more than 30-60 minutes (especially in mornings)
- Significant swelling, redness, or warmth
- Joint stiffness after an injury
- Stiffness that progressively worsens
- Fever accompanying joint symptoms

## Gentle Joint Mobility Exercises

### Neck

**Chin Tucks:**
1. Pull chin straight back (make double chin)
2. Hold 5 seconds
3. Release
4. 10 reps

**Neck Rotations:**
1. Slowly turn head left
2. Return to center
3. Slowly turn head right
4. 5-8 each direction

**Ear to Shoulder:**
1. Tilt ear toward shoulder
2. Hold 15-20 seconds
3. Return to center
4. Repeat other side

### Shoulders

**Shoulder Circles:**
1. Roll shoulders forward slowly
2. 10 circles forward
3. 10 circles backward

**Pendulum Swings:**
1. Bend at waist, arm hanging
2. Swing arm gently in small circles
3. 30 seconds each direction, each arm

**Wall Slides:**
1. Back against wall, arms at 90°
2. Slide arms up and down wall
3. 10 reps slowly

### Wrists and Hands

**Wrist Circles:**
1. Make fist, circle wrist slowly
2. 10 circles each direction
3. Each wrist

**Finger Spreads:**
1. Spread fingers wide
2. Make fist
3. Repeat 10 times

**Finger Touches:**
1. Touch thumb to each fingertip
2. 5 cycles each hand

### Spine

**Cat-Cow:**
1. On all fours
2. Arch back (cow), round back (cat)
3. Move slowly through full range
4. 10-15 cycles

**Seated Rotation:**
1. Sit tall
2. Rotate torso to one side
3. Hold 15-20 seconds
4. Repeat other side

**Pelvic Tilts:**
1. Lie on back, knees bent
2. Flatten lower back into floor (posterior tilt)
3. Arch lower back (anterior tilt)
4. 10-15 cycles

### Hips

**Hip Circles:**
1. Stand on one leg (hold support)
2. Circle other leg slowly
3. 5 circles each direction
4. Switch legs

**Knee to Chest:**
1. Lie on back
2. Pull one knee toward chest
3. Hold 20-30 seconds
4. Alternate legs

**Figure-4 Stretch:**
1. Lie on back
2. Cross ankle over opposite knee
3. Pull bottom knee toward chest
4. Hold 30 seconds each side

### Knees

**Heel Slides:**
1. Lie on back, legs extended
2. Slide heel toward buttock, bending knee
3. Slide back out
4. 10 reps each leg

**Seated Knee Extensions:**
1. Sit in chair
2. Slowly straighten one knee
3. Hold 5 seconds
4. Lower slowly
5. 10 reps each leg

**Gentle Squats:**
1. Hold onto chair or counter
2. Squat only to comfortable range
3. Stand back up
4. 10 reps

### Ankles

**Ankle Circles:**
1. Lift foot off ground
2. Circle ankle slowly
3. 10 circles each direction
4. Each ankle

**Ankle Pumps:**
1. Sit or lie with legs extended
2. Point toes, then flex toes up
3. 15-20 reps

**Heel-Toe Rocks:**
1. Standing, rock onto heels
2. Roll onto toes
3. 10-15 reps

## Morning Stiffness Routine (10 minutes)

Do this before getting out of bed and in the first hour of waking.

### In Bed (3 minutes)

**Ankle Pumps:**
- 15-20 reps

**Knee Bends:**
- Slide heels up and down
- 10 each leg

**Hip Circles:**
- Knees bent, circle knees together
- 5 each direction

**Trunk Rotation:**
- Knees bent, drop knees side to side
- 5 each side

### After Getting Up (7 minutes)

**March in Place:**
- 1 minute, gentle

**Shoulder Circles:**
- 10 forward, 10 backward

**Neck Mobility:**
- Chin tucks: 10
- Gentle rotations: 5 each side
- Ear to shoulder: 15 seconds each

**Cat-Cow:**
- 10 cycles

**Gentle Squats:**
- Hold counter, 10 reps

**Hip Circles:**
- Standing, 5 each direction, each leg

**Calf Raises:**
- 10-15 reps

## Joint-by-Joint Programs

### For Stiff Hips

**Daily Routine (10 minutes):**

1. **Hip circles:** 5 each direction, each leg
2. **Knee-to-chest:** 30 seconds each
3. **Figure-4 stretch:** 30 seconds each
4. **Hip flexor stretch:** 30 seconds each
5. **Gentle squats:** 10 reps
6. **Cat-cow:** 10 cycles
7. **Pelvic tilts:** 10 reps

### For Stiff Knees

**Daily Routine (10 minutes):**

1. **Heel slides:** 10 each leg
2. **Seated knee extensions:** 10 each leg
3. **Quad sets:** Tighten quad, hold 5 sec, 10 each
4. **Straight leg raises:** 10 each leg
5. **Gentle squats:** 10 reps (pain-free range)
6. **Calf raises:** 15 reps
7. **Ankle pumps:** 20 reps

### For Stiff Shoulders

**Daily Routine (10 minutes):**

1. **Shoulder circles:** 10 each direction
2. **Pendulum swings:** 30 seconds each arm
3. **Wall slides:** 10 reps
4. **Cross-body stretch:** 20 seconds each
5. **Doorway chest stretch:** 30 seconds each side
6. **Behind back reach:** 20 seconds each
7. **Shoulder shrugs:** 10 reps

### For Stiff Back

**Daily Routine (10 minutes):**

1. **Pelvic tilts:** 10 reps
2. **Cat-cow:** 10-15 cycles
3. **Knees to chest:** 30 seconds
4. **Trunk rotations:** 5 each side
5. **Child's pose:** 30-60 seconds
6. **Gentle standing extension:** 5 reps
7. **Side bends:** 5 each side

## Warm Water Exercises

Warm water reduces joint stiffness and makes movement easier.

### Pool or Bath Exercises

**Walking:**
- Walk in pool (chest-deep water)
- Forward, backward, sideways

**Leg Swings:**
- Hold pool edge
- Swing leg forward and back
- Swing side to side

**Arm Circles:**
- Underwater arm circles
- Less resistance than air

**Squats:**
- Water supports weight
- Squat to comfortable depth

**Gentle Stretching:**
- All stretches are easier in warm water

---

## Heat Before, Ice After

### Heat (Before Exercise)

- **Warm shower or bath:** 10-15 minutes
- **Heating pad:** 15-20 minutes on stiff joints
- **Warm water bottle:** Wrap in towel
- **Purpose:** Increases blood flow, makes joints more pliable

### Ice (After Exercise if Sore)

- **Ice pack:** 15-20 minutes (wrapped in towel)
- **Frozen vegetables:** Work well
- **Purpose:** Reduces any inflammation from activity

## Tips for Managing Stiff Joints

### Daily Habits

- **Move frequently:** Don't stay in one position too long
- **Gentle morning routine:** Start every day with mobility
- **Stay active:** Regular exercise improves long-term stiffness
- **Maintain healthy weight:** Reduces joint stress
- **Stay hydrated:** Joints need water for lubrication

### What to Avoid

- **Prolonged inactivity:** Makes stiffness worse
- **Extreme cold without warming up:** Increases stiffness
- **High-impact exercise without preparation:** Stresses joints
- **Pushing through significant pain:** May cause damage

---

## When Stiffness Indicates a Problem

### Arthritis Warning Signs

- Morning stiffness lasting more than 30 minutes
- Stiffness after sitting that takes time to resolve
- Gradual loss of range of motion
- Cracking or grinding sensations
- Joint swelling

### See a Doctor If

- Stiffness is sudden and severe
- Accompanied by significant swelling
- Follows an injury
- Prevents normal activities
- Getting progressively worse

---

## The Bottom Line

Joint stiffness responds remarkably well to gentle, consistent movement. The key is starting slowly, moving through comfortable ranges, and being consistent daily.

**Daily habits for healthy joints:**
1. Morning mobility routine (10 minutes)
2. Movement breaks throughout day
3. Heat before activity if needed
4. Gentle stretching and mobility work
5. Stay active—motion is lotion

Stiff joints don't have to be permanent. With consistent, gentle movement, most people can significantly improve their mobility and reduce discomfort.
