# What Muscles Do Good Mornings Work? Complete Anatomy Guide

The good morning is a powerful hip hinge exercise that targets the entire posterior chain. With a barbell on your back, you bow forward at the hips and return to standing—building serious hamstring, glute, and lower back strength. Here's the complete breakdown.

## Primary Muscles Worked

### Hamstrings

**Activation Level: Very High**

The hamstrings are heavily targeted:
- Biceps femoris (long and short head)
- Semitendinosus
- Semimembranosus
- Hip extension under significant stretch
- Eccentric loading during descent

### Erector Spinae

**Activation Level: Very High**

The spinal erectors work maximally:
- Maintain spine position
- Resist spinal flexion
- Isometric AND concentric work
- Major lower back builder

### Gluteus Maximus

**Activation Level: High**

The glutes contribute:
- Hip extension to standing
- Work with hamstrings
- Power source at lockout

## Secondary Muscles Worked

### Adductors

**Activation Level: Moderate**

Inner thigh muscles:
- Assist hip extension
- Stabilize the movement

### Core

**Activation Level: High**

Entire midsection:
- Braces to protect spine
- Anti-flexion work
- Essential for safety

### Upper Back (Traps, Rhomboids)

**Activation Level: Moderate**

Support the bar:
- Keep bar in position
- Scapular stability
- Isometric work

## Why Good Mornings Are Effective

### Posterior Chain Powerhouse

Good mornings train:
- Hip extension
- Spinal stability
- Hamstring strength under stretch
- Lower back endurance

### Deadlift and Squat Carryover

Builds strength that transfers to:
- Maintaining back position in deadlifts
- Staying upright in squats
- General hip hinge power
- Injury prevention

### Unique Loading Pattern

Unlike RDLs:
- Bar on back changes leverage
- Higher erector demand
- Different stretch pattern
- Complements other hip hinges

## Good Morning Variations

| Variation | Hamstring | Lower Back | Difficulty |
|-----------|-----------|------------|------------|
| Standard barbell | Very High | Very High | Moderate |
| Seated good morning | High | Very High | Moderate |
| Zercher good morning | High | High | Hard |
| Banded good morning | Moderate | Moderate | Easy |
| Safety bar good morning | Very High | High | Moderate |

### Standard Barbell Good Morning

The classic:
- Bar on upper back (high or low)
- Hip hinge forward
- Most common version
- Foundation movement

### Seated Good Morning

Sitting on bench:
- Eliminates leg drive
- More lower back isolation
- Shorter ROM
- Different feel

### Zercher Good Morning

Bar in elbow crooks:
- Different loading position
- Core intensive
- Unique challenge

### Banded Good Morning

Using resistance band:
- Good for learning
- Warmup option
- Home training
- Lower intensity

## Muscle Activation by Phase

### Starting Position
- Bar on back (like squat position)
- Feet shoulder width
- Slight knee bend (fixed)
- Core braced

### Descent (Eccentric)
- Hinge at hips
- Push hips back
- Torso lowers toward parallel
- Hamstrings stretch under load

### Bottom Position
- Torso near parallel (or as low as form allows)
- Maximum hamstring stretch
- Spine neutral
- Control the position

### Ascent (Concentric)
- Drive hips forward
- Return to standing
- Hamstrings and glutes contract
- Erectors work hard

## Common Mistakes

**Rounding the lower back**: Most dangerous error. Maintain neutral spine.

**Going too heavy**: Form breaks down. Start light, progress slowly.

**Bending knees excessively**: Becomes a squat. Keep slight, fixed bend.

**Hinging too deep**: Past flexibility. Stop when back wants to round.

**Looking up**: Strains neck. Keep head neutral.

**Rushing**: Loss of control. Slow and deliberate.

**Not bracing core**: Back at risk. Brace hard throughout.

**Bar position issues**: Too high on neck. Settle bar properly.

## How to Maximize Posterior Chain Activation

1. **Push hips back**: Think "hips to wall behind"
2. **Maintain neutral spine**: Never round
3. **Control the descent**: 3 seconds down
4. **Feel the hamstring stretch**: Stop at appropriate depth
5. **Squeeze at top**: Full hip extension
6. **Brace hard**: Protect the spine
7. **Slight knee bend**: Fixed, not changing
8. **Start light**: Master form before loading heavy

## Programming Recommendations

**For Posterior Chain Development:**
- 3-4 sets of 8-12 reps
- Moderate weight
- Perfect form
- 1-2 times per week

**For Strength:**
- 3-4 sets of 5-8 reps
- Heavier but controlled
- Focus on form
- Progressive overload

**In a Workout:**
Often after main lifts:
1. Squats or deadlifts (main movement)
2. **Good mornings** (posterior chain accessory)
3. Leg curls or RDLs
4. Core work

**As Squat/Deadlift Accessory:**
Excellent for building:
- Back strength for heavy pulls
- Hip hinge pattern
- Hamstring resilience

## Good Morning vs Romanian Deadlift

| Factor | Good Morning | RDL |
|--------|--------------|-----|
| Bar position | On back | In hands |
| Lower back demand | Higher | High |
| Hamstring focus | Very High | Very High |
| Learning curve | Steeper | Moderate |
| Max loading | Lower | Higher |

**Both excellent hip hinges.** Good mornings have unique spinal loading.

## Safety Considerations

### Start Light
- Learn the movement pattern
- Build strength gradually
- Form is critical
- Ego lifting is dangerous here

### Depth
- Only go as low as spine stays neutral
- Flexibility determines depth
- Quality over depth
- Stop before rounding

### Core Bracing
- Essential throughout
- Big breath before descent
- Maintain throughout rep
- Protect the spine

### When to Avoid
- Active back injury
- Unable to maintain neutral spine
- Without proper instruction

## Who Benefits

**Great for:**
- Powerlifters and strength athletes
- Deadlift improvement
- Squat improvement
- Posterior chain development
- Athletes needing hip hinge strength

**May need alternatives:**
- Those with back issues (try RDLs first)
- Beginners (master RDL first)
- Anyone unable to maintain form

## Key Takeaways

- **Primary muscles**: Hamstrings (very high), erector spinae (very high), glutes (high)
- **Secondary**: Adductors, core, upper back
- **Powerful hip hinge**: Unique bar-on-back loading
- **High lower back demand**: More than RDLs
- **Form critical**: Neutral spine always
- **Start light**: Build up slowly
- **Depth = flexibility**: Don't force range
- **Squat/deadlift accessory**: Great carryover

Good mornings are a powerful but demanding exercise for posterior chain development. The bar-on-back position creates unique spinal loading, building exceptional lower back and hamstring strength. Master the form with light weight, prioritize spine neutrality, and progress gradually for excellent results.
