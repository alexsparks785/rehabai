# How to Fix Tension Headaches: Exercises and Stretches That Work

That band of pressure around your head, the ache at your temples, the tight feeling that won't quit—tension headaches are the most common type of headache, and they're often very treatable without medication. Here's how to address the muscle tension that's causing your pain.

## What Is a Tension Headache?

Tension-type headaches feel like a tight band or pressure around your head. Unlike migraines, they're typically:
- Bilateral (both sides of head)
- Pressing or tightening (not pulsating)
- Mild to moderate intensity
- Not made worse by physical activity
- Without nausea or vomiting

**Common locations:**
- Forehead and temples
- Back of head and neck
- Behind the eyes
- "Band" around the head

## What Causes Tension Headaches?

### Muscle Tension
The primary culprit. Tight muscles in the neck, shoulders, scalp, and jaw refer pain to the head.

**Key muscles involved:**
- Upper trapezius
- Suboccipitals (base of skull)
- Temporalis (temples)
- Sternocleidomastoid (sides of neck)
- Masseter (jaw)
- Scalenes

### Posture
Forward head posture forces neck muscles to work overtime, creating chronic tension. For every inch your head moves forward, it feels 10 pounds heavier to your neck muscles.

### Stress
Mental stress creates physical tension. The neck and shoulders are classic stress-holding areas.

### Eye Strain
Squinting, poor lighting, or uncorrected vision problems cause muscle tension around the eyes and forehead.

### Jaw Clenching
Tension in jaw muscles refers pain to temples and sides of head.

### Dehydration
Can trigger or worsen headaches.

### Sleep Issues
Poor sleep position or inadequate sleep increases muscle tension.

## When to See a Doctor

**Seek immediate care if headache is:**
- The worst headache of your life
- Sudden and severe ("thunderclap")
- Accompanied by fever, stiff neck, confusion
- Following head injury
- With vision changes, weakness, or numbness

**See a doctor if:**
- Headaches are frequent (more than 15 days/month)
- Pattern changes significantly
- Over-the-counter medications don't help
- Headaches interfere with daily life

## Self-Treatment: Immediate Relief

### 1. Apply Heat
Heat relaxes tight muscles.
- Warm towel on neck and shoulders
- Heating pad for 15-20 minutes
- Warm shower directed at neck

### 2. Self-Massage
**Temples (Temporalis):**
- Place fingertips on temples
- Apply gentle circular pressure
- 1-2 minutes

**Base of Skull (Suboccipitals):**
- Find the ridge at the base of your skull
- Press thumbs into the muscles just below
- Hold pressure or make small circles
- 2-3 minutes

**Jaw (Masseter):**
- Find the muscle by clenching briefly
- Relax and massage in circles
- 1-2 minutes per side

**Neck and Shoulders:**
- Squeeze and knead the upper trapezius
- Work from neck toward shoulder
- 2-3 minutes per side

### 3. Cold for Active Pain
If headache is already intense, cold can numb the pain:
- Cold pack on forehead or back of neck
- 10-15 minutes

## Stretches for Tension Headaches

### Neck Stretches

#### 1. Upper Trap Stretch
**How to do it:**
1. Sit tall, hold the edge of your chair with right hand
2. Tilt left ear toward left shoulder
3. Use left hand to gently increase stretch
4. Hold 30-45 seconds per side
5. Repeat 2-3 times

#### 2. Levator Scapulae Stretch
**How to do it:**
1. Turn head 45 degrees to the left
2. Look down toward your left armpit
3. Use left hand to gently pull head down
4. Feel stretch from neck to shoulder blade
5. Hold 30-45 seconds per side

#### 3. Suboccipital Stretch
**How to do it:**
1. Sit tall
2. Tuck chin (double chin) and look down slightly
3. Place hands on back of head
4. Gently pull head forward and down
5. Feel stretch at base of skull
6. Hold 30-45 seconds

#### 4. SCM Stretch
**How to do it:**
1. Sit tall
2. Turn head to look over right shoulder
3. Tilt head back slightly
4. Feel stretch along left side of neck
5. Hold 20-30 seconds per side

#### 5. Neck Rotation
**How to do it:**
1. Slowly turn head to look over left shoulder
2. Hold 20-30 seconds
3. Repeat to the right
4. Do 2-3 reps each side

### Shoulder and Upper Back

#### 6. Shoulder Shrugs with Release
**How to do it:**
1. Inhale and shrug shoulders up toward ears
2. Hold 5 seconds
3. Exhale and completely release
4. Let shoulders drop
5. Repeat 5-10 times

#### 7. Chest Stretch (Doorway)
**How to do it:**
1. Stand in doorway
2. Forearms on frame, elbows at shoulder height
3. Step forward and lean through
4. Feel stretch across chest
5. Hold 30-45 seconds

### Jaw Relaxation

#### 8. Jaw Release
**How to do it:**
1. Place tongue on roof of mouth
2. Slowly open mouth as wide as comfortable
3. Hold 5-10 seconds
4. Close slowly
5. Repeat 5-10 times

#### 9. Jaw Massage with Stretch
**How to do it:**
1. Open mouth slightly
2. Massage masseter muscles (sides of jaw)
3. Slowly open and close while massaging

## Exercises to Prevent Tension Headaches

### Postural Exercises

#### 10. Chin Tucks
**Why:** Corrects forward head posture that causes muscle strain.

**How to do it:**
1. Pull chin straight back (double chin)
2. Keep eyes level
3. Hold 5-10 seconds
4. Repeat 15-20 times
5. Do multiple times daily

#### 11. Chin Tuck Against Wall
**How to do it:**
1. Stand with back against wall
2. Try to press back of head into wall
3. Perform chin tuck while maintaining contact
4. Hold 5-10 seconds
5. 10-15 reps

#### 12. Wall Angels
**How to do it:**
1. Back against wall, feet few inches out
2. Arms in "goal post" position against wall
3. Slide arms up and down, keeping contact
4. 10-15 reps

### Strengthening

#### 13. Deep Neck Flexor Activation
**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back without pillow
2. Gently nod your chin (like saying "yes")
3. Feel the muscles at the front of your neck engage
4. Hold 10 seconds
5. 10 reps

#### 14. Isometric Neck Exercises
**How to do it:**
1. Place palm on forehead
2. Push head into hand (no movement)
3. Hold 5-10 seconds
4. Repeat with hand on each side, then back of head
5. 5-10 reps in each direction

#### 15. Prone Y-T-W
**How to do it:**
1. Lie face down
2. Y: Arms overhead at 45 degrees, lift
3. T: Arms out to sides, lift
4. W: Elbows bent, squeeze shoulder blades, lift
5. 10 reps of each

## Lifestyle Modifications

### Workstation Ergonomics
- Monitor at eye level
- Head not jutting forward toward screen
- Keyboard and mouse at proper height
- Take breaks every 30-60 minutes

### Stress Management
- Regular exercise
- Deep breathing
- Meditation or mindfulness
- Adequate sleep
- Setting boundaries

### Hydration
- Drink water throughout the day
- Watch caffeine intake (too much or too little can trigger headaches)

### Sleep
- Supportive pillow that keeps neck neutral
- Consistent sleep schedule
- 7-9 hours per night

### Eye Care
- Regular eye exams
- Proper lighting for computer work
- Follow the 20-20-20 rule (every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds)

## Your Daily Anti-Headache Routine

### Morning (3 minutes)
- Neck stretches: 30 seconds each direction
- Shoulder shrugs with release: 5 reps
- Chin tucks: 15 reps
- Drink a full glass of water

### Hourly at Desk
- Chin tucks: 5 reps
- Shoulder shrugs: 3 reps
- Brief neck stretches
- Eyes away from screen

### Evening (10 minutes)
- Full stretching routine
- Self-massage: temples, suboccipitals, jaw, traps
- Deep breathing: 3-5 minutes
- Strengthening exercises (3x/week)

## During a Headache

1. **Stop and assess:** What triggered it?
2. **Apply heat** to neck and shoulders
3. **Gentle stretches** (don't force)
4. **Self-massage** of tense areas
5. **Dim lights** if bothering you
6. **Hydrate**
7. **Rest** in a comfortable position
8. **Medication** if needed (don't overuse)

## Timeline for Improvement

- **Acute headache:** May resolve in 30 minutes to a few hours with treatment
- **Weekly habits:** May see reduction in frequency within 2-4 weeks
- **Long-term prevention:** 6-8 weeks of consistent practice for lasting change

## The Bottom Line

Tension headaches are your body telling you something is wrong—usually tight muscles from posture, stress, or both. Treating just the pain (with medication) doesn't address the cause.

The fix involves releasing tight muscles, strengthening weak ones, improving posture, and managing stress. Most people who commit to daily stretching, posture exercises, and lifestyle modifications see significant improvement within a few weeks.

Start with the stretches—they provide the most immediate relief. Add postural exercises to prevent the tension from rebuilding. Address lifestyle factors like workstation setup and stress.

Your headaches are telling you to pay attention to how you hold your body and mind. Listen to them, and they'll have less to say.
