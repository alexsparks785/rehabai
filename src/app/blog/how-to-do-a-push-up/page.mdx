# How to Do a Push-Up: Perfect Your Form and Build Strength

The push-up is the king of bodyweight exercises. It builds your chest, shoulders, triceps, and core—all with zero equipment. But most people do them wrong. Here's how to do push-ups correctly and progress from beginner to advanced.

## The Perfect Push-Up

### Starting Position

**Hand placement:**
- Slightly wider than shoulder-width
- Fingers spread, pointing forward (or slightly out)
- Hands at chest level (not by shoulders or stomach)
- Weight distributed across entire palm

**Body position:**
- Arms fully extended
- Body in straight line from head to heels
- Core engaged (no sagging or piking)
- Feet together or hip-width apart

### The Descent (Lowering)

**Control the movement:**
- Bend elbows to lower body
- Elbows at 45° angle (not flared out at 90°)
- Lower until chest nearly touches floor
- Keep body rigid throughout
- Take 2-3 seconds to lower

### The Bottom Position

**At the lowest point:**
- Chest just above or lightly touching floor
- Upper arms roughly parallel to floor
- Core still engaged
- Don't rest on the floor (maintain tension)

### The Ascent (Pushing Up)

**Drive back up:**
- Push through palms
- Extend arms fully
- Keep body straight
- Don't let hips rise first
- Take 1-2 seconds to push up

### Breathing

**Proper breathing pattern:**
- Inhale on the way down
- Exhale on the way up
- Don't hold your breath

## Common Push-Up Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Sagging Hips

**The problem:** Hips drop toward floor

**Why it's bad:**
- Strains lower back
- Reduces core engagement
- Easier (cheating)

**The fix:**
- Engage core throughout
- Squeeze glutes
- Think "straight line"
- Check in mirror

### Mistake 2: Piking Hips

**The problem:** Butt sticks up in the air

**Why it's bad:**
- Changes the exercise
- Reduces chest work
- Often compensation for weakness

**The fix:**
- Lower hips to straight line
- Engage core
- May need easier variation first

### Mistake 3: Flared Elbows

**The problem:** Elbows point straight out (90° from body)

**Why it's bad:**
- Stresses shoulder joint
- Injury risk
- Less efficient

**The fix:**
- Elbows at 45° angle
- Tuck elbows toward body more
- Think "arrow shape" not "T shape"

### Mistake 4: Partial Range of Motion

**The problem:** Not going low enough

**Why it's bad:**
- Less muscle activation
- Incomplete development
- Cheating yourself

**The fix:**
- Chest to floor (or close)
- Full arm extension at top
- If you can't do full range, use easier variation

### Mistake 5: Head Position

**The problem:** Looking up or letting head drop

**Why it's bad:**
- Strains neck
- Breaks alignment

**The fix:**
- Neutral spine
- Look at floor about 6 inches ahead
- Chin slightly tucked

### Mistake 6: Rushing

**The problem:** Bouncing through reps quickly

**Why it's bad:**
- Less muscle engagement
- Using momentum
- Injury risk

**The fix:**
- Control each rep
- 2-3 seconds down, 1-2 seconds up
- Feel the muscle working

## Push-Up Progressions

### If You Can't Do a Push-Up Yet

**Start here and work your way down:**

**Level 1: Wall Push-Up**
- Hands on wall, feet back
- Lower chest to wall
- Very easy, great for beginners
- Master 3×15 before progressing

**Level 2: Incline Push-Up**
- Hands on bench, table, or stairs
- Higher incline = easier
- Lower incline = harder
- Progress by lowering incline

**Level 3: Knee Push-Up**
- Standard push-up but on knees
- Keep body straight from knees to head
- Full range of motion
- Master 3×12 before floor push-ups

**Level 4: Negative Push-Up**
- Start at top position
- Lower yourself very slowly (5 seconds)
- Use knees to return to top
- Builds strength for full push-up

**Level 5: Full Push-Up**
- Standard floor push-up
- Congratulations!

### Intermediate Progressions

**Once you can do 15+ perfect push-ups:**

**Close-Grip Push-Up:**
- Hands closer together
- More tricep emphasis
- Harder than standard

**Wide Push-Up:**
- Hands wider than shoulders
- More chest emphasis
- Different muscle recruitment

**Diamond Push-Up:**
- Hands form diamond shape
- Very tricep focused
- Challenging

**Decline Push-Up:**
- Feet elevated on bench or step
- More shoulder and upper chest
- Increases difficulty

### Advanced Progressions

**For those who've mastered the basics:**

**Archer Push-Up:**
- Wide stance
- Shift weight to one arm
- Other arm assists but extends out
- Progression toward one-arm

**Pseudo Planche Push-Up:**
- Hands by waist, fingers pointing back or out
- Lean forward significantly
- Very challenging shoulder and core work

**One-Arm Push-Up:**
- Feet wide for balance
- One hand behind back
- The ultimate push-up

**Clapping Push-Up:**
- Explosive push
- Clap at top
- Builds power
- Land with soft elbows

## Push-Up Variations by Muscle Focus

### Chest Emphasis

- Wide push-up
- Decline push-up (upper chest)
- Pause at bottom

### Tricep Emphasis

- Close-grip push-up
- Diamond push-up
- Hands lower (toward stomach)

### Shoulder Emphasis

- Pike push-up
- Decline push-up
- Hands closer to head

### Core Emphasis

- Plank push-up (slow with pause)
- Spider-man push-up (knee to elbow)
- Push-up with rotation

### Push-Up Challenge

**100 Push-Ups Workout:**
- 10 sets of 10 (or however you need to break it up)
- Rest as needed between sets
- Complete all 100
- Great finisher or standalone workout

## Tips for More Push-Ups

### Build Volume Gradually

**Week 1:** 3 sets × current max
**Week 2:** 4 sets × current max
**Week 3:** 3 sets × (current max + 2 reps)
**Continue progressing**

### Grease the Groove

**Throughout the day:**
- Do submaximal sets frequently
- 5 reps every hour
- Accumulates high volume
- Nervous system adaptation

### Strengthen Supporting Muscles

**Core work:**
- Planks, dead bugs, hollow holds
- Core strength = better push-ups

**Tricep work:**
- Dips, tricep extensions
- Often the weak link

### Practice Perfect Form

**Quality over quantity:**
- 10 perfect reps > 20 sloppy reps
- Build proper motor patterns
- Prevents injury, maximizes gains

## The Bottom Line

The push-up is:
- Excellent for chest, shoulders, triceps, core
- Requires zero equipment
- Endlessly progressive
- A skill worth mastering

Key points:
- Straight body line throughout
- Elbows at 45° angle
- Full range of motion
- Control the movement
- Progress through variations

Can't do a push-up yet? Start with wall or incline push-ups. Already banging out 50? Progress to harder variations. The push-up will challenge you at any fitness level—and it's always there when you need it.

Master this one exercise and you'll always have an effective upper body workout available, anywhere, anytime.
