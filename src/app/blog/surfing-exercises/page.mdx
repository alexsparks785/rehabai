# Surfing Exercises: Train for Better Performance in the Water

Surfing looks effortless when done well, but it demands a unique combination of paddling endurance, explosive pop-up power, balance, and rotational strength. What you do on land directly impacts your time in the water.

**Key insight:** Most surfers spend 90% of their water time paddling and only 10% actually riding waves. Training your paddle endurance pays immediate dividends.

---

## Physical Demands of Surfing

### What Surfing Requires

- **Paddle endurance:** Sustained upper body effort
- **Pop-up explosiveness:** Quick transition from prone to standing
- **Balance and proprioception:** Staying on an unstable surface
- **Hip mobility:** Compressed positions, turning
- **Rotational power:** Generating speed on the wave
- **Core stability:** Maintaining position in dynamic conditions

### Common Surfing Issues

| Issue | Cause | Training Focus |
|-------|-------|----------------|
| Early fatigue | Poor paddle endurance | Swim training, pulling exercises |
| Slow pop-up | Weak upper body push | Push-ups, explosive floor work |
| Poor balance | Limited proprioception | Balance board training |
| Limited turns | Tight hips, weak rotation | Hip mobility, rotational power |
| Lower back pain | Repeated extension | Core strength, hip flexor mobility |
| Shoulder pain | Overhead paddle position | Rotator cuff strength, mobility |

## Paddle Power Training

### Swimming

The most specific paddle training.

**Freestyle Focus:**
- 20-30 minutes of pool swimming
- Mix of steady-state and intervals
- Focus on high elbow catch (like paddling)

**If no pool:**
- Open water swimming
- Resistance band paddle simulation

### Lat Pulldowns

**How to do it:**
1. Wide grip on bar
2. Pull to upper chest
3. Control return
4. 3 sets of 12-15

### Bent-Over Rows

**How to do it:**
1. Hinge at hips, flat back
2. Pull weights toward hips
3. Squeeze shoulder blades
4. 3 sets of 12

### Single-Arm Dumbbell Row

**How to do it:**
1. One hand and knee on bench
2. Row dumbbell to hip
3. 3 sets of 10-12 each arm

### Prone Paddle Simulation

**How to do it:**
1. Lie face down on bench or stability ball
2. Hold light dumbbells
3. Alternate paddling motion
4. 3 sets of 30 seconds

### TRX or Ring Rows

**How to do it:**
1. Hang from straps at angle
2. Pull chest to hands
3. Lower with control
4. 3 sets of 12-15

## Pop-Up Training

### Standard Pop-Up Practice

**How to do it:**
1. Lie face down, hands by chest
2. Explosive push-up motion
3. Jump feet forward to standing position
4. Land in surf stance (one foot forward)
5. 3 sets of 8-10

**Key:** Speed matters—drill this until it's automatic.

### Burpee Variations

**Surf Burpees:**
1. Lie face down
2. Pop up to surf stance (not square)
3. Alternate lead foot
4. 3 sets of 10

### Explosive Push-Ups

**How to do it:**
1. Lower into push-up
2. Explode up with enough force for hands to leave ground
3. 3 sets of 8-10

**Progression:** Clap push-ups

### Box Jump to Surf Stance

**How to do it:**
1. Stand facing low box
2. Jump and land in surf stance
3. Stick landing, check position
4. 3 sets of 8

### Medicine Ball Slams

**How to do it:**
1. Raise ball overhead
2. Slam into ground with full power
3. Builds explosive push-up power
4. 3 sets of 10

## Balance Training

### Balance Board Work

**Basic Balance:**
1. Stand on balance board
2. Find equilibrium
3. Hold 30-60 seconds
4. Progress to eyes closed

**Dynamic Balance:**
1. Maintain balance while moving arms
2. Simulate turns on board
3. Practice bottom turn motion

### Single-Leg Balance

**How to do it:**
1. Stand on one leg
2. Hold 30-60 seconds
3. Progress to unstable surface
4. 3 sets each leg

### BOSU Ball Training

**Dome Side Up:**
1. Stand on dome
2. Practice weight shifts
3. Simulate surf movements

**Dome Side Down:**
1. More unstable
2. Advanced balance challenge
3. Squat movements on top

### Single-Leg Squats to Box

**How to do it:**
1. Stand on one leg
2. Squat to touch box behind you
3. Stand back up
4. 3 sets of 8 each leg

### Indo Board Work

If available, this mimics surfboard instability closely.

## Core Stability

### Plank Variations

**Standard Plank:**
- Hold 30-60 seconds, 3 sets

**Side Plank:**
- Hold 30 seconds each side, 3 sets

**Plank on Stability Ball:**
- Forearms on ball
- Hold 30 seconds

### Dead Bug

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back, arms up, knees at 90°
2. Lower opposite arm and leg
3. Keep lower back pressed down
4. 3 sets of 10 each side

### Pallof Press

**How to do it:**
1. Stand perpendicular to cable/band
2. Press arms out, resist rotation
3. 3 sets of 10 each side

### Bird Dog

**How to do it:**
1. On all fours
2. Extend opposite arm and leg
3. Keep back flat
4. 3 sets of 10 each side

### Swiss Ball Rollouts

**How to do it:**
1. Kneel, forearms on ball
2. Roll ball forward, extending body
3. Pull back using core
4. 3 sets of 10

## Rotational Power

### Medicine Ball Rotational Throws

**How to do it:**
1. Stand perpendicular to wall
2. Rotate and throw ball with power
3. Generate from hips
4. 3 sets of 10 each side

### Cable/Band Woodchops

**How to do it:**
1. Cable or band at various heights
2. Rotate diagonally across body
3. Control through full range
4. 3 sets of 10 each direction

### Russian Twists

**How to do it:**
1. Sit with knees bent, feet elevated (or down)
2. Hold weight, rotate side to side
3. 3 sets of 20 total

### Landmine Rotations

**How to do it:**
1. Hold end of barbell at chest
2. Rotate torso, moving bar in arc
3. 3 sets of 10 each side

---

## Hip Mobility

### Deep Squat Hold

**How to do it:**
1. Squat as deep as comfortable
2. Keep heels on ground
3. Hold 60-90 seconds
4. Use support if needed

### 90/90 Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Sit with front leg at 90°, back leg at 90°
2. Lean forward over front shin
3. Hold 45-60 seconds each side

### Pigeon Pose

**How to do it:**
1. Front shin across body
2. Back leg extended
3. Fold forward
4. 60 seconds each side

### Hip Flexor Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Half-kneeling position
2. Tuck pelvis, shift forward
3. Hold 45 seconds each side

**Why it matters:** Tight hip flexors from prone paddling position.

### Frog Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. On all fours, widen knees
2. Rock hips back
3. Hold 60-90 seconds

## Shoulder Health

### Y-T-W Raises

**How to do it:**
1. Lie face down
2. Y: Arms at 45°, thumbs up
3. T: Arms to sides
4. W: Elbows bent, rotate up
5. 10 reps each position

### Face Pulls

**How to do it:**
1. Band at face height
2. Pull toward face, separate hands
3. Squeeze shoulder blades
4. 3 sets of 15

### External Rotation

**How to do it:**
1. Side-lying or with band
2. Elbow at side, rotate out
3. 3 sets of 15 each arm

### Shoulder CARs

**How to do it:**
1. Slow, controlled arm circles
2. Maximum range of motion
3. 5 each direction, each arm

### Doorway Pec Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Arm on doorframe at 90°
2. Step through doorway
3. Hold 30-45 seconds each side

## Sample Training Programs

### Beginner Surfer (2-3 sessions/week)

**Session 1 - Paddle + Push:**
- Lat pulldown: 3x12
- Single-arm row: 3x10 each
- Push-ups: 3x15
- Explosive push-ups: 3x8
- Pop-up practice: 3x8
- Plank: 3x30 sec

**Session 2 - Balance + Core:**
- Balance board: 10 min
- Single-leg balance: 3x30 sec each
- Single-leg squat to box: 3x8 each
- Dead bug: 3x10 each
- Pallof press: 3x10 each
- Russian twists: 3x20

**Session 3 - Mobility + Rotation:**
- Full hip mobility routine: 15 min
- Med ball rotational throws: 3x10 each
- Woodchops: 3x10 each
- Shoulder health circuit
- Stretching: 10 min

### Intermediate Surfer (3-4 sessions/week)

**Day 1 - Paddle Power:**
- Swimming: 20-30 min (or prone paddle simulation)
- Bent-over rows: 3x10
- Single-arm rows: 3x10 each
- Face pulls: 3x15
- Prone paddle simulation: 3x45 sec

**Day 2 - Pop-Up + Power:**
- Surf burpees: 3x10
- Explosive push-ups: 3x10
- Med ball slams: 3x10
- Box jump to stance: 3x8
- Clap push-ups: 3x6
- Core circuit: 15 min

**Day 3 - Balance + Rotation:**
- Balance board: 15 min with movement
- Single-leg squats: 3x8 each
- Rotational throws: 3x10 each
- Cable woodchops: 3x10 each
- Landmine rotations: 3x10 each

**Day 4 - Mobility + Recovery:**
- Swimming (easy): 15 min
- Full hip mobility: 20 min
- Shoulder health circuit
- Foam rolling and stretching

### Pre-Surf Session Warm-Up (10 min)

**On the beach:**
1. Arm circles: 20 each direction
2. Leg swings: 10 each leg
3. Walking lunges with rotation: 10 steps
4. Pop-up practice: 5-8 reps
5. Balance shifts in surf stance: 30 seconds
6. Deep squat hold: 30 seconds

## Surf-Specific Tips

### For Better Paddling

- Strengthen lats and rear delts
- Build endurance with swimming or paddle simulation
- Work shoulder mobility for efficient stroke
- Core stability to reduce energy loss

### For Faster Pop-Ups

- Explosive push strength is key
- Practice the specific movement pattern daily
- Build lower body power for the jump phase
- Flexibility to compress into stance

### For Better Turns

- Rotational power from hips and core
- Hip mobility for compression
- Balance to stay centered
- Practice movement on balance board

### For Longer Sessions

- Cardio endurance (swimming, running)
- Paddle-specific endurance
- Recovery between sessions
- Proper nutrition and hydration

---

## The Bottom Line

Surfing rewards those who prepare. Time spent on paddle strength, pop-up explosiveness, and balance training translates directly to more waves caught and better rides.

**Priority areas:**
1. Paddle endurance (this is 90% of surfing)
2. Pop-up speed and power
3. Balance and proprioception
4. Core stability
5. Hip mobility and rotational power

What you build on land shows up in the water.
