# Best Exercises for Shoulders: Build Strong, Healthy Delts

Strong shoulders aren't just about aesthetics—they're essential for upper body function, injury prevention, and athletic performance. These exercises build all three deltoid heads while keeping your rotator cuff healthy.

## Shoulder Anatomy Basics

Your deltoid has three heads:

**Anterior (Front) Deltoid**
- Raises arm forward
- Works in all pressing movements
- Often overdeveloped relative to other heads

**Lateral (Side) Deltoid**
- Raises arm to the side
- Creates shoulder width
- Needs direct work for development

**Posterior (Rear) Deltoid**
- Raises arm backward
- Often underdeveloped
- Critical for shoulder health and posture

Plus the **rotator cuff**—four small muscles that stabilize the shoulder joint. They're not glamorous, but they keep you lifting.

## The Best Shoulder Exercises

### 1. Overhead Press

**The foundation of shoulder strength.**

**How to do it:**
1. Barbell or dumbbells at shoulder height
2. Brace core, squeeze glutes
3. Press straight overhead
4. Full lockout at top
5. Lower with control

**Sets/reps:** 3-4 sets × 6-10 reps

**Why it's best:** Compound movement that builds overall shoulder strength and size. The standing version also trains core stability.

**Variations:**
- Standing barbell (most demanding)
- Seated dumbbell (more isolation)
- Push press (allows more weight)
- Arnold press (rotation added)

### 2. Lateral Raise

**Builds shoulder width.**

**How to do it:**
1. Dumbbells at sides
2. Slight forward lean
3. Raise arms out to sides until parallel to floor
4. Thumbs neutral or slightly up
5. Control the lowering

**Sets/reps:** 3-4 sets × 12-15 reps

**Why it's best:** Directly targets the lateral delt, which creates the wide-shouldered look.

**Tips:**
- Use lighter weight than ego wants
- Stop at shoulder height
- Lead with elbows, not hands
- Don't swing or use momentum

### 3. Face Pull

**Rear delts and rotator cuff health.**

**How to do it:**
1. Cable or band at face height
2. Rope attachment, overhand grip
3. Pull toward face, elbows high
4. Externally rotate at end—hands end up by ears
5. Squeeze shoulder blades

**Sets/reps:** 3-4 sets × 15-20 reps

**Why it's best:** Trains often-neglected rear delts and external rotators. Essential for shoulder health.

### 4. Dumbbell Shoulder Press

**Unilateral pressing for balanced development.**

**How to do it:**
1. Seated or standing, dumbbells at shoulders
2. Palms forward or neutral
3. Press straight up
4. Full extension, don't bounce
5. Lower to start position

**Sets/reps:** 3 sets × 8-12 reps

**Why it's best:** Dumbbells allow natural movement path, reduce imbalances, and work stabilizers.

### 5. Reverse Fly (Rear Delt Fly)

**Direct rear delt work.**

**How to do it:**
1. Bent over at hips, flat back
2. Dumbbells hanging down
3. Raise arms out to sides
4. Squeeze shoulder blades at top
5. Control the lowering

**Sets/reps:** 3 sets × 12-15 reps

**Why it's best:** Isolates the posterior delt for balanced shoulder development.

**Variations:**
- Seated bent-over
- Cable reverse fly
- Machine reverse fly
- Face-down on incline bench

### 6. Push-Up

**Foundational pressing pattern.**

**How to do it:**
1. Hands slightly wider than shoulders
2. Body in straight line
3. Lower chest to floor
4. Push back up
5. Full range of motion

**Sets/reps:** 3 sets × 10-20 reps

**Why it's best:** Accessible, effective, trains front delts and serratus anterior.

**Variations for more shoulder:**
- Pike push-ups (more vertical angle)
- Decline push-ups (more front delt)
- Hindu push-ups (dynamic)

### 7. Upright Row (Cable or Dumbbells)

**Builds lateral delts and traps.**

**How to do it:**
1. Use cables or dumbbells (not barbell)
2. Pull elbows up and out
3. Stop when elbows reach shoulder height
4. Control the lowering

**Sets/reps:** 3 sets × 10-12 reps

**Why it's best:** Effective for lateral delts when done with proper form.

**Caution:** Some people's shoulders don't tolerate this. If it hurts, skip it.

### 8. External Rotation

**Rotator cuff strength.**

**How to do it:**
1. Band or cable at elbow height
2. Elbow at side, bent 90°
3. Rotate forearm outward
4. Don't let elbow leave your side
5. Control the return

**Sets/reps:** 2-3 sets × 15-20 each arm

**Why it's best:** Directly strengthens external rotators, preventing impingement and injury.

### 9. Y-Raise (Prone)

**Lower trap and shoulder health.**

**How to do it:**
1. Lie face down on bench (or floor)
2. Arms hanging down
3. Raise arms in Y shape, thumbs up
4. Squeeze at top
5. Lower with control

**Sets/reps:** 2-3 sets × 12-15 reps

**Why it's best:** Strengthens lower traps and rear delts, counters forward-shoulder posture.

### 10. Landmine Press

**Shoulder-friendly pressing alternative.**

**How to do it:**
1. Barbell in landmine attachment (or corner)
2. Hold end of bar at shoulder
3. Press up and forward
4. Angle reduces shoulder stress

**Sets/reps:** 3 sets × 10-12 each arm

**Why it's best:** Great for those with shoulder issues who can't overhead press pain-free.

## Complete Shoulder Workout

### Workout A: Strength Focus

**Warm-Up (5 minutes)**
- Band pull-aparts: 20 reps
- External rotations: 15 each arm
- Arm circles: 30 seconds each direction

**Main Workout**

1. **Standing Overhead Press**: 4 × 6-8
2. **Dumbbell Lateral Raise**: 3 × 12
3. **Face Pulls**: 3 × 15
4. **Reverse Fly**: 3 × 12
5. **External Rotation**: 2 × 15 each arm

### Workout B: Hypertrophy Focus

**Warm-Up (5 minutes)**
- Same as above

**Main Workout**

1. **Seated Dumbbell Press**: 4 × 10
2. **Lateral Raise** (drop set on last set): 4 × 12
3. **Reverse Fly**: 3 × 15
4. **Face Pulls**: 3 × 20
5. **Front Raise**: 2 × 12
6. **Prone Y-Raise**: 2 × 15

### Shoulder Finisher (Add to Any Workout)

**Giant Set – 2-3 rounds:**
- Lateral raise: 10 reps
- Front raise: 10 reps
- Reverse fly: 10 reps
- External rotation: 10 reps each arm

No rest between exercises, 90 seconds between rounds.

## Balancing Your Shoulder Training

### Common Imbalances

**Problem:** Front delts overdeveloped, rear delts weak
**Solution:** 2:1 ratio of pulling to pressing, more face pulls and reverse flys

**Problem:** Shoulder pain during pressing
**Solution:** More external rotation work, reduce volume, check form

**Problem:** Poor posture (rounded shoulders)
**Solution:** Face pulls, Y-raises, rows, reduce pressing temporarily

### Exercise Ratio Guideline

For shoulder health, aim for:
- Every pushing exercise: match with a pulling exercise
- Every overhead press: include face pull or reverse fly
- Weekly: equal sets of push and pull

## Shoulder Health Essentials

### Daily Maintenance (2-3 minutes)
- Band pull-aparts: 20 reps
- External rotations: 15 each arm
- Arm circles: 20 each direction

### Before Every Upper Body Workout
- Light rotator cuff activation
- Band work for 2-3 minutes
- Don't skip this

### Warning Signs
- Pain during pressing (not muscle fatigue)
- Clicking or catching
- Night pain
- Weakness raising arm

If you have persistent symptoms, see a professional.

## Exercises to Avoid or Modify

### Behind-Neck Press
**Problem:** Extreme external rotation under load
**Better:** Front military press

### Wide-Grip Upright Row
**Problem:** Internal rotation and impingement risk
**Better:** Cable or dumbbell upright row with moderate grip

### Dips (Deep)
**Problem:** Shoulder extension under load
**Modification:** Don't go below 90° elbow bend

## Programming Shoulders

### Frequency
- Direct shoulder work: 2x per week
- Indirect work from chest/back training counts
- Rotator cuff work: daily or every upper body session

### Volume
- 12-18 sets per week for development
- 8-12 sets for maintenance
- Include all three heads

### Progression
- Add reps before weight on isolation exercises
- Small jumps on pressing (2.5-5 lbs)
- Form matters more than weight on lateral raises

## The Bottom Line

Strong, healthy shoulders require more than just pressing. Build all three deltoid heads—front, side, and rear—while maintaining rotator cuff health.

Overhead press for strength. Lateral raises for width. Face pulls and reverse flys for rear delts and health. External rotations to keep everything working.

Balance your pushing with pulling. Don't neglect the rotator cuff work that seems boring but keeps you lifting.

Train smart, and your shoulders will stay strong and pain-free for years.
