# Exercises for Better Digestion: Move Your Body, Move Your Gut

Physical activity does more than burn calories—it stimulates your digestive system, reduces bloating, and helps food move through your gut more efficiently. The right movements can relieve discomfort and support long-term digestive health.

**Key insight:** Your gut has its own nervous system and muscle contractions (peristalsis) that move food along. Physical movement stimulates these contractions and improves transit time.

---

## How Exercise Helps Digestion

### The Benefits

- **Stimulates peristalsis:** Muscle contractions that move food through gut
- **Reduces bloating:** Movement helps release trapped gas
- **Decreases transit time:** Food moves through faster
- **Reduces stress:** Stress negatively impacts digestion
- **Improves gut bacteria:** Regular exercise supports microbiome diversity
- **Strengthens core:** Supports digestive organs

### Timing Matters

- **Light movement after meals:** Gentle walking aids digestion
- **Intense exercise:** Wait 1-2 hours after eating
- **Morning exercise:** Can stimulate bowel movements
- **Avoid lying down:** After eating, stay upright

---

## Post-Meal Walking

The simplest and most effective digestive aid.

### Why It Works

- Gentle stimulation of digestive tract
- Gravity helps food move down
- Reduces blood sugar spikes
- Prevents post-meal sluggishness

### How to Do It

- Walk for 10-15 minutes after meals
- Easy pace—this isn't exercise for fitness
- Can be indoors or outdoors
- Especially helpful after larger meals

## Gentle Twisting Exercises

Twists massage internal organs and stimulate digestion.

### Seated Twist

**How to do it:**
1. Sit tall in chair or on floor
2. Place right hand on left knee
3. Left hand behind you
4. Rotate torso to the left
5. Hold 30-60 seconds
6. Repeat other side

### Supine Twist

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back, arms out to sides
2. Bend knees, feet on floor
3. Drop both knees to one side
4. Look toward opposite hand
5. Hold 30-60 seconds each side

### Standing Twist

**How to do it:**
1. Feet hip-width apart
2. Arms extended at shoulder height
3. Rotate torso left, then right
4. Arms swing naturally
5. 20 total rotations

## Core and Abdominal Exercises

Strengthening and moving the abdominal area supports digestive organs.

### Knee to Chest

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back
2. Pull one knee toward chest
3. Hold 20-30 seconds
4. Switch legs
5. Then pull both knees together

**Why it works:** Compresses and releases abdominal area, massaging intestines.

### Wind-Relieving Pose (Pavanamuktasana)

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back
2. Pull both knees to chest
3. Hug knees, rock gently side to side
4. Hold 30-60 seconds

**Why it works:** Name says it all—helps release trapped gas.

### Gentle Cat-Cow

**How to do it:**
1. On hands and knees
2. Arch back, drop belly (cow)
3. Round back, tuck chin (cat)
4. Move slowly, 10-15 cycles

**Why it works:** Massages digestive organs, stimulates movement.

### Belly Breathing

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back, knees bent
2. Place hands on belly
3. Breathe deeply into belly
4. Exhale fully, belly contracts
5. 10-15 breaths

**Why it works:** Massages digestive organs from the inside.

## Yoga Sequence for Digestion (15-20 minutes)

### Opening (3 minutes)

**Child's Pose:**
- Kneel, sit back, fold forward
- Arms extended or at sides
- Rest 1-2 minutes
- Deep belly breathing

### Movement (10 minutes)

**Cat-Cow:**
- 10 slow cycles
- Coordinate with breath

**Downward Dog:**
- Hold 30 seconds
- Gentle movement (walking feet)

**Low Lunge with Twist:**
- Step right foot forward
- Left hand on floor, right arm reaches up
- Hold 30 seconds each side

**Seated Forward Fold:**
- Sit with legs extended
- Fold forward gently
- Hold 45-60 seconds

**Seated Twist:**
- Twist to each side
- 30-45 seconds each

**Supine Twist:**
- Both sides
- 30-45 seconds each

### Closing (5 minutes)

**Wind-Relieving Pose:**
- Knees to chest
- Hold 45-60 seconds
- Rock gently

**Happy Baby:**
- Grab outside of feet
- Knees toward armpits
- Hold 45-60 seconds

**Savasana:**
- Lie flat, relax completely
- 2-3 minutes

## Quick Bloating Relief Routine (5 minutes)

For immediate discomfort.

### Sequence

1. **Walking in place:** 1 minute
2. **Knee to chest (each side):** 20 seconds each
3. **Both knees to chest with rock:** 30 seconds
4. **Supine twist (each side):** 20 seconds each
5. **Belly breathing:** 10 deep breaths
6. **Gentle walking:** 1-2 minutes

---

## Morning Routine for Regularity (10 minutes)

Movement first thing can stimulate bowel movements.

### Sequence

1. **Bed stretches:**
   - Knee to chest: 20 seconds each
   - Both knees to chest: 20 seconds
   - Supine twist: 20 seconds each side

2. **Standing movements:**
   - Hip circles: 10 each direction
   - Standing side bends: 10 each side
   - Standing twists: 15 total

3. **Stimulating movements:**
   - Walk in place with high knees: 1 minute
   - Squats (gentle): 10 reps

4. **Drink warm water**

5. **Short walk** if possible

## Abdominal Massage Techniques

Self-massage can directly stimulate the digestive tract.

### Clockwise Circles

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back, knees bent
2. Place hands on belly
3. Make clockwise circles (following colon direction)
4. Gentle to moderate pressure
5. 2-3 minutes

### I-L-U Massage

**How to do it:**
1. Start on left side of abdomen
2. Stroke downward (I)
3. Across from right to left at belly button level, then down (L)
4. Right side up, across, and down left side (U)
5. Repeat sequence 5-10 times

### Point Pressure

**How to do it:**
1. Find tender spots on abdomen
2. Apply gentle pressure
3. Hold 10-20 seconds
4. Release
5. Work around the abdomen clockwise

## Exercise Types and Digestion

### Best for Digestion

**Low-Moderate Intensity:**
- Walking
- Gentle cycling
- Swimming
- Yoga
- Tai chi

### Timing Considerations

**After Eating:**
- Light walking: immediately okay
- Moderate exercise: wait 1 hour
- Intense exercise: wait 2+ hours

**Before Eating:**
- Exercise can stimulate appetite
- Allow time to cool down before meal

### Intense Exercise Caution

- Can slow digestion during exercise
- Blood flow diverts to muscles
- May cause GI distress
- Sensitive individuals should be careful

## Exercises for Specific Issues

### For Bloating

- Walking
- Twists
- Wind-relieving pose
- Belly breathing
- Abdominal massage

### For Constipation

- Morning exercise routine
- Walking
- Squats
- Twists
- Knee to chest positions
- Abdominal massage

### For Acid Reflux

- Gentle walking (not after large meals)
- Avoid exercises lying flat right after eating
- Stay upright
- Don't exercise intensely after eating

### For IBS

- Low-intensity exercise best
- Yoga can help (stress reduction)
- Avoid high-intensity during flares
- Individual variation—find what works

## Daily Habits for Digestive Health

### Movement Throughout Day

- Don't sit for extended periods
- Take walking breaks
- Gentle stretches after meals
- Avoid lying down right after eating

### Eating Habits

- Chew thoroughly
- Don't rush meals
- Stay upright after eating
- Moderate portion sizes

### Stress Management

- Stress impairs digestion
- Exercise helps reduce stress
- Deep breathing calms digestive system
- Mind-gut connection is real

### Hydration

- Adequate water supports digestion
- Don't overhydrate with meals (dilutes digestive enzymes)
- Warm water can stimulate movement

---

## When to See a Doctor

### Red Flags

- Persistent digestive issues despite lifestyle changes
- Blood in stool
- Unexplained weight loss
- Severe pain
- Chronic constipation or diarrhea
- Symptoms that significantly impact quality of life

---

## The Bottom Line

Your digestive system is designed to move—and it works better when your body moves too. Regular gentle exercise, post-meal walks, and targeted movements like twists and knee-to-chest positions can significantly improve digestive comfort and function.

**Daily digestive support:**
1. Walk after meals (10-15 minutes)
2. Morning movement routine
3. Include twisting movements
4. Avoid prolonged sitting
5. Manage stress with movement and breathing

A moving body means a moving gut.
