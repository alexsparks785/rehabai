# Exercises for Firefighters: Build Job-Ready Strength

Firefighting demands everything: the strength to force doors and carry victims, the endurance to climb stairs in full gear, and the ability to perform complex tasks while exhausted and overheated. Training must build functional capacity for real-world demands.

**Key insight:** Cardiovascular events are the leading cause of firefighter line-of-duty deaths. Fitness isn't just about performance—it's about survival.

---

## Physical Demands of Firefighting

### What the Job Requires

- **Loaded carrying:** Victims, hose, equipment (50-100+ lbs)
- **Climbing:** Stairs, ladders while loaded
- **Pulling and dragging:** Hose lines, rescue operations
- **Overhead work:** Ceiling breach, ventilation
- **Grip strength:** Holding tools, hose, during rescue
- **Sustained high-intensity effort:** In heat, under SCBA

### Common Firefighter Injuries

| Injury | Cause | Prevention Focus |
|--------|-------|------------------|
| Back injuries | Lifting, carrying, awkward positions | Core strength, lifting mechanics |
| Knee injuries | Climbing, kneeling, squatting | Leg strength, mobility |
| Shoulder injuries | Overhead work, pulling | Shoulder stability, mobility |
| Cardiac events | Heat stress, exertion | Cardiovascular fitness |
| Heat exhaustion | Exertion in PPE | Conditioning, hydration protocols |

## Functional Strength Exercises

### Deadlifts

**Why it matters:** Foundation for all lifting and dragging movements.

**How to do it:**
1. Feet hip-width, bar over mid-foot
2. Hinge and grip bar
3. Flat back, chest up
4. Drive through legs, extend hips
5. Lower with control
6. 4 sets of 6-8

### Squats

**How to do it:**
1. Bar on upper back or goblet position
2. Squat to depth, chest up
3. Drive through whole foot
4. 4 sets of 8-10

### Farmer's Carries

**Why it matters:** Mimics carrying equipment and victims.

**How to do it:**
1. Hold heavy dumbbells/kettlebells at sides
2. Walk with control, posture upright
3. 3-4 sets of 40-60 yards

**Variation:** Single-arm (suitcase carry) for anti-rotation

### Sled Pushes/Pulls

**Why it matters:** Mimics dragging hose and victims.

**How to do it:**
1. Push sled for distance or time
2. Pull sled backward using rope/handles
3. 4-6 sets of 20-40 yards

### Sandbag Carries

**How to do it:**
1. Bear hug sandbag against chest
2. Walk for distance
3. 3 sets of 40-60 yards

**Variations:** Shoulder carry, front carry

## Climbing and Stair Work

### Loaded Stair Climbing

**Why it matters:** Direct job simulation.

**How to do it:**
1. Wear weighted vest (start 20-30 lbs, build to 50+)
2. Climb stairs at steady pace
3. 5-10 minutes continuous
4. Progress time and weight

### Step-Ups (Weighted)

**How to do it:**
1. Hold weight, step onto high box
2. Drive through top leg
3. Control descent
4. 3 sets of 10 each leg

### Box Jumps

**How to do it:**
1. Jump onto box
2. Land softly, stand fully
3. Step down
4. 3 sets of 8

### Lunges (Walking, Weighted)

**How to do it:**
1. Hold weight, lunge forward
2. Continue walking lunges
3. 3 sets of 20 steps total

---

## Pulling Strength

### Pull-Ups

**How to do it:**
1. Dead hang, pull chin over bar
2. Lower with control
3. 4 sets to near failure

**Progressions:** Weighted pull-ups

### Bent-Over Rows

**How to do it:**
1. Hinge at hips, flat back
2. Pull weight to torso
3. Squeeze shoulder blades
4. 4 sets of 10

### Cable/Band Face Pulls

**How to do it:**
1. Pull to face, separate hands
2. Squeeze shoulder blades
3. 3 sets of 15

### Rope Climbs or Sled Rope Pulls

**How to do it:**
1. Hand-over-hand rope climbing or pulling
2. 3-5 rope climbs or
3. 4-6 sled pulls using rope

---

## Pressing and Overhead Work

### Overhead Press

**Why it matters:** Overhead breaching, ceiling work.

**How to do it:**
1. Bar or dumbbells at shoulder height
2. Press straight overhead
3. Lower with control
4. 4 sets of 8

### Push-Ups

**How to do it:**
1. Full range of motion
2. Chest to floor, full lockout
3. 3 sets of 20 or more

### Dips

**How to do it:**
1. Lower until shoulders at elbow height
2. Press back up
3. 3 sets of 10-15

### Sledgehammer Swings

**Why it matters:** Simulates breaching and forcible entry.

**How to do it:**
1. Swing sledgehammer onto tire
2. Alternate sides
3. 3 sets of 20 total swings

---

## Core Strength

### Plank (Weighted)

**How to do it:**
1. Standard plank position
2. Add weight plate on back
3. Hold 45-60 seconds
4. 3 sets

### Pallof Press

**How to do it:**
1. Stand perpendicular to cable
2. Press out, resist rotation
3. 3 sets of 10 each side

### Deadlifts and Farmer's Carries

Both are excellent core exercises—the core stabilizes during these movements.

### Loaded Carries

**Variations:**
- Farmer's carry (both hands)
- Suitcase carry (one hand)
- Front carry (at chest)
- Overhead carry

All challenge core stability differently.

## Cardiovascular Conditioning

### High-Intensity Interval Training (HIIT)

**Protocol 1: Work/Rest**
- 30 seconds all-out effort
- 30 seconds rest
- 8-12 rounds
- Exercise: Row, bike, run, or battle ropes

**Protocol 2: Tabata**
- 20 seconds work, 10 seconds rest
- 8 rounds
- 4 minutes total

### Loaded Cardio

**Stair climbing in vest:**
- 10-20 minutes
- Simulates fireground conditions

**Ruck walking:**
- 30-45 minutes
- 30-50 lb pack

### Circuit Training

**Example Circuit (4 rounds):**
- Kettlebell swings: 15
- Push-ups: 15
- Goblet squats: 15
- Rows: 15
- Run or row: 200m/250m

Minimal rest between exercises, 2 minutes between rounds.

## Grip Strength

### Dead Hangs

**How to do it:**
1. Hang from bar as long as possible
2. 3-5 attempts
3. Progress to single-arm or weighted

### Farmer's Carries (Heavy)

**How to do it:**
1. Heavy weight, grip challenge
2. 40-60 yards
3. 3-4 sets

### Towel Pull-Ups

**How to do it:**
1. Drape towel over bar
2. Grip towel, perform pull-ups
3. Builds crushing and holding grip

### Plate Pinches

**How to do it:**
1. Pinch weight plates together
2. Hold 20-30 seconds
3. 3-5 sets

## Sample Training Programs

### Minimum Effective (2x per week, 45 minutes)

**Day 1 - Strength:**
- Deadlift: 4x6
- Push-ups: 3x20
- Rows: 3x10
- Farmer's carry: 3x40 yards
- Plank: 3x45 sec

**Day 2 - Conditioning:**
- Loaded stair climbing: 10 min
- Circuit: 4 rounds
  - KB swings: 12
  - Goblet squats: 10
  - Push-ups: 10
  - Row or run: 200m

### Optimal (4x per week)

**Day 1 - Lower Body Strength:**
- Deadlift: 4x6
- Squats: 4x8
- Lunges: 3x10 each
- Calf raises: 3x15
- Core circuit: 10 min

**Day 2 - Upper Body + Carry:**
- Overhead press: 4x8
- Pull-ups: 4 sets
- Rows: 3x10
- Dips: 3x12
- Farmer's carry: 4x50 yards

**Day 3 - Conditioning:**
- Loaded stair climbing: 15 min
- HIIT: 10 rounds (30/30)
- Sledgehammer: 3x20 swings

**Day 4 - Full Body/Functional:**
- Sandbag carry: 3x50 yards
- Sled push/pull: 4x30 yards
- Pull-ups: 3 sets
- Push-ups: 3x20
- Loaded circuit: 3 rounds

## CPAT Preparation

The Candidate Physical Ability Test requires specific preparation.

### CPAT Events

1. Stair climb (weighted)
2. Hose drag
3. Equipment carry
4. Ladder raise and extension
5. Forcible entry
6. Search
7. Rescue (dummy drag)
8. Ceiling breach and pull

### CPAT Training Focus

- **Stair climbing:** Train with vest, build time
- **Dragging:** Sled pulls, rope pulls
- **Carrying:** Farmer's carries, sandbag carries
- **Overhead work:** Overhead press, sledgehammer
- **Endurance:** Complete practice runs of full test

---

## Heat Acclimatization

### Training Considerations

- Train in heat safely when possible (supervised)
- Wear extra layers during conditioning
- Practice working at elevated heart rate
- Stay hydrated—pre-hydrate and replace fluids

### Recovery from Heat Exposure

- Cool down gradually
- Replace electrolytes
- Monitor for heat illness symptoms
- Allow adequate recovery between exposures

---

## Injury Prevention

### Daily Mobility

**Pre-Workout:**
- Leg swings
- Hip circles
- Arm circles
- Cat-cow

**Post-Workout:**
- Hip flexor stretch
- Hamstring stretch
- Shoulder stretches
- Lower back stretches

### Shoulder Health

- Face pulls: 3x15
- External rotation: 3x15 each
- Y-T-W raises: 10 each

### Back Protection

- Core work is primary prevention
- Perfect lifting mechanics
- Don't round back under load

---

## The Bottom Line

Firefighter fitness isn't about looking good—it's about going home. The physical demands of the job require specific preparation: carrying heavy loads, climbing stairs, dragging, and doing it all while exhausted and overheated.

**Priority areas:**
1. Cardiovascular fitness (survival issue)
2. Loaded carrying capacity (farmer's carries, sandbag)
3. Pulling strength (dragging, rescue)
4. Climbing endurance (stairs, ladders)
5. Core stability (protects back during lifting)

Train hard, stay ready, and go home safely.
