# How to Fix Rounded Shoulders: Exercises for Better Posture

Rounded shoulders—when your shoulders roll forward and your upper back curves—affects millions of people. It causes neck pain, shoulder problems, and makes you look less confident. The good news: it's very fixable with the right exercises.

## What Are Rounded Shoulders?

Look at yourself from the side. If your shoulders sit in front of your ears rather than aligned with them, you have rounded shoulders.

**Signs:**
- Shoulders roll forward
- Upper back curves excessively
- Head often juts forward
- Chest appears sunken
- Shoulder blades wing outward

## What Causes Rounded Shoulders?

It's a muscle imbalance pattern:

**Tight muscles (pulling forward):**
- Pectoralis major and minor (chest)
- Front deltoids
- Upper trapezius
- Subscapularis (internal rotator)

**Weak muscles (can't pull back):**
- Middle and lower trapezius
- Rhomboids
- Rear deltoids
- External rotators

**The cause:** Usually desk work, phone use, driving—anything where arms are forward and you're hunched over.

## The Fix: Open the Front, Strengthen the Back

Correcting rounded shoulders requires:
1. Stretching the chest and front shoulders
2. Strengthening the mid/lower traps, rhomboids, and rear delts
3. Practicing proper shoulder position

## Stretches for Rounded Shoulders

### 1. Doorway Chest Stretch

**The most important stretch for rounded shoulders.**

**How to do it:**
1. Stand in doorway
2. Place forearm on door frame, elbow at 90°
3. Step through doorway with one foot
4. Turn body away until you feel chest stretch
5. Hold 30 seconds

**Key:** Do THREE positions:
- Elbow low (below shoulder) – lower chest
- Elbow at shoulder height – middle chest
- Elbow high (above shoulder) – upper chest/pec minor

**Reps:** 30 seconds each position, each side

### 2. Wall Chest Stretch

**Alternative to doorway.**

**How to do it:**
1. Stand perpendicular to wall
2. Place palm on wall, arm straight, slightly behind you
3. Turn body away from wall
4. Feel stretch across chest and front shoulder
5. Hold 30 seconds each side

### 3. Foam Roller Chest Opening

**Passive chest stretch.**

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on foam roller lengthwise (supporting spine)
2. Arms out to sides, palms up
3. Let arms fall toward floor
4. Relax and breathe
5. Hold 1-2 minutes

### 4. Behind-Back Clasp Stretch

**Opens chest and front shoulders.**

**How to do it:**
1. Stand tall
2. Clasp hands behind back
3. Squeeze shoulder blades together
4. Lift arms slightly
5. Hold 30 seconds

### 5. Upper Trap Stretch

**Often tight with rounded shoulders.**

**How to do it:**
1. Sit, hold chair with one hand
2. Tilt ear toward opposite shoulder
3. Use other hand for gentle pressure
4. Hold 30 seconds each side

## Strengthening Exercises for Rounded Shoulders

### 1. Face Pull

**The single best exercise for this problem.**

**How to do it:**
1. Cable or band at face height
2. Pull toward face, elbows high
3. Spread hands apart as you pull
4. Externally rotate—hands finish by ears
5. Squeeze shoulder blades

**Sets/reps:** 3-4 sets × 15-20 reps

**Why it's best:** Hits rear delts, mid/lower traps, rhomboids, and external rotators—everything that's weak.

### 2. Band Pull-Apart

**Simple, effective, do-anywhere.**

**How to do it:**
1. Hold band at shoulder width, arms extended
2. Pull band apart to touch chest
3. Squeeze shoulder blades together
4. Control the return

**Sets/reps:** 3 sets × 15-20 reps

**Tip:** Can do these multiple times daily.

### 3. Prone Y-T-W Raises

**Comprehensive upper back activation.**

**How to do it:**
1. Lie face down on bench or floor
2. Y: Arms overhead, thumbs up, lift and hold 3 seconds
3. T: Arms to sides, thumbs up, lift and hold 3 seconds
4. W: Elbows bent at 90°, squeeze back, lift and hold 3 seconds

**Sets/reps:** 2-3 sets × 8-10 of each position

### 4. Wall Angels

**Teaches proper scapular movement.**

**How to do it:**
1. Stand with back against wall
2. Arms in "goalpost" position, touching wall
3. Slide arms up and down
4. Keep entire arm in contact with wall
5. Move slowly with control

**Sets/reps:** 2-3 sets × 10-12 reps

**Key:** If you can't keep arms on wall, your chest is tight—that's what you're fixing.

### 5. Prone Cobra

**Full upper back contraction.**

**How to do it:**
1. Lie face down, arms at sides
2. Lift chest off ground
3. Rotate arms so thumbs point to ceiling
4. Squeeze shoulder blades down and back
5. Hold 15-20 seconds

**Sets/reps:** 3 holds × 15-20 seconds

### 6. Rowing Movements (Any Variation)

**Build back thickness.**

**How to do it:**
- Any row: cable, dumbbell, barbell, machine
- Focus on squeezing shoulder blades together
- Pull toward lower chest for upper back emphasis

**Sets/reps:** 3-4 sets × 10-12 reps

**Key:** More pulling than pushing in your training overall.

### 7. External Rotation with Band

**Strengthens external rotators.**

**How to do it:**
1. Elbow at side, bent 90°
2. Rotate forearm outward against band
3. Keep elbow pinned to body
4. Control the return

**Sets/reps:** 2-3 sets × 15 each arm

## Complete Rounded Shoulders Correction Routine

### Daily Quick Fix (10 minutes)

**Stretches (5 minutes):**
- Doorway stretch: 30 sec each position, each side
- Upper trap stretch: 30 sec each side

**Strengthening (5 minutes):**
- Band pull-aparts: 20 reps
- Face pulls (or prone Y-T-W): 15 reps (or 8 of each)
- Wall angels: 10 reps

### Full Correction Session (20 minutes)

**Stretching (7 minutes):**
- Doorway chest stretch: 30 sec × 3 positions × both sides
- Foam roller chest opening: 1.5 minutes
- Upper trap stretch: 45 sec each side
- Behind-back clasp: 30 seconds

**Strengthening (11 minutes):**
- Face pulls: 3 × 15
- Band pull-aparts: 3 × 15
- Prone Y-T-W: 2 × 10 of each
- Wall angels: 2 × 12
- Prone cobra: 3 × 15-second holds
- External rotation: 2 × 12 each arm

**Posture Practice (2 minutes):**
- Wall posture check: stand against wall, heels/butt/upper back/head touching
- Practice shoulder blade squeeze and hold
- Walk around maintaining position

## Posture Awareness Throughout the Day

### At Your Desk
- Screen at eye level
- Keyboard and mouse close to body
- Elbows at 90°
- Sit back in chair, don't perch forward

### On Your Phone
- Bring phone to eye level
- Don't crane forward
- Limit phone time

### Standing
- Shoulders back and DOWN (not shrugged up)
- Think "back pocket"—put shoulder blades in back pockets
- Chest open but not puffed

### Movement Breaks
Every 30-60 minutes:
- Stand up
- Squeeze shoulder blades 10 times
- Doorway stretch: 30 seconds
- Roll shoulders

## How Long Until Improvement?

**Week 1-2:** Increased awareness, easier to hold correct position

**Week 3-4:** Stretches feel better, noticeable improvement

**Month 2:** Significant change in resting posture

**Month 3+:** New alignment becomes more automatic

**Commitment:** Daily work for 10 minutes produces results. Occasional long sessions don't.

## Common Mistakes

### Only Stretching
- Won't fix the problem alone
- Must strengthen weak muscles too

### Forcing Shoulders Back Aggressively
- Creates tension, not lasting change
- Think "down and back," not "way back"

### All Chest Work, No Back Work
- Gym work reinforces the imbalance
- Match or exceed push volume with pulls

### Inconsistency
- Random occasional efforts don't work
- Daily practice changes habits

## Balance Your Training

If you train at the gym:
- For every pushing exercise (bench, shoulder press), do at least one pulling exercise (row, face pull)
- Better: 2:1 pulling to pushing ratio
- Include face pulls or band pull-aparts every session

## The Bottom Line

Rounded shoulders come from tight chest muscles and weak upper back muscles. Fix it by stretching the chest (doorway stretches) and strengthening the back (face pulls, band pull-aparts, Y-T-W raises).

Do the daily quick fix routine. Practice awareness throughout the day. Balance your training to include more pulling than pushing.

In weeks, you'll notice improvement. In months, good posture becomes natural. Your neck will thank you. Your shoulders will thank you. And you'll stand taller with more confidence.
