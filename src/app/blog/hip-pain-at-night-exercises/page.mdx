# Hip Pain at Night: Exercises and Sleeping Positions for Relief

Hip pain that gets worse at night or prevents restful sleep is incredibly frustrating. Whether you're a side sleeper with outer hip pain or someone with achy hips regardless of position, understanding the cause and implementing the right strategies can help you sleep better.

## Why Hips Hurt More at Night

### Mechanical Factors

**Side sleeping pressure:**
- Direct compression on the outer hip (greater trochanter)
- Irritates bursa and IT band
- Most common cause of lateral hip pain at night

**Prolonged positioning:**
- Staying in one position for hours
- Reduced blood flow
- Sustained muscle tension

**Mattress issues:**
- Too firm = pressure points
- Too soft = hip sinks, spine misaligns
- Old mattress = inadequate support

### Inflammatory Factors

**Inactivity allows inflammation to build:**
- During the day, movement pumps fluid out
- At night, inflammation accumulates
- Morning stiffness results

**Circadian rhythm:**
- Some inflammatory markers naturally peak at night
- Arthritis pain often worse at night/morning

### Common Conditions Causing Night Hip Pain

**Greater Trochanteric Bursitis:**
- Pain on outer hip
- Worse when lying on that side
- Tender to touch

**Hip Arthritis:**
- Groin or front of hip pain
- Stiffness after sleeping
- May be both sides

**IT Band Syndrome:**
- Outer hip/thigh tightness
- Can be worse with side sleeping
- Often associated with bursitis

**Hip Labral Tear:**
- Deep groin/front hip pain
- Catching or clicking
- Pain with certain positions

**Hip Flexor Issues:**
- Front of hip pain
- Worse after prolonged sitting during the day
- Tight feeling

## Exercises to Reduce Night Hip Pain

### Before Bed Routine (10 minutes)

**Hip Flexor Stretch:**
1. Half-kneeling position (one knee on ground)
2. Tuck your pelvis (posterior tilt)
3. Shift forward slightly until you feel a stretch
4. Keep torso upright

*Hold: 45-60 seconds per side*

**Piriformis Stretch (Figure 4):**
1. Lie on your back
2. Cross one ankle over the opposite knee
3. Gently push the crossed knee away
4. Or pull the bottom leg toward chest

*Hold: 45-60 seconds per side*

**90/90 Hip Stretch:**
1. Sit with front leg bent 90° in front
2. Back leg bent 90° to the side
3. Sit tall and hold
4. Lean slightly forward for deeper stretch

*Hold: 45-60 seconds per side*

**Supine Hip Rotation:**
1. Lie on your back, knees bent, feet flat
2. Let both knees fall to one side
3. Keep shoulders on the floor
4. Feel gentle rotation through hips

*Hold: 30 seconds per side*

**Cat-Cow:**
1. On all fours
2. Arch back, look up (cow)
3. Round back, tuck chin (cat)
4. Move slowly through range

*Reps: 10 cycles*

**Child's Pose with Hip Focus:**
1. Kneel, sit back on heels
2. Spread knees wider than hips
3. Reach arms forward
4. Let hips sink toward floor

*Hold: 60 seconds*

### Strengthening Exercises

**Clamshells:**
1. Lie on non-painful side (or do both sides)
2. Knees bent 45°
3. Keep feet together, lift top knee
4. Don't let pelvis roll back

*Reps: 20 per side, 3 sets*

**Why this helps:** Strengthens gluteus medius, which when weak, contributes to bursitis

**Side-Lying Leg Raise:**
1. Lie on side, legs straight
2. Lift top leg toward ceiling
3. Keep toe pointed forward
4. Lower with control

*Reps: 15-20 per side, 3 sets*

**Glute Bridge:**
1. Lie on back, knees bent
2. Push through heels to lift hips
3. Squeeze glutes at top
4. Lower with control

*Reps: 15-20, 3 sets*

**Isometric Hip Abduction:**
1. Lie on your side
2. Press your top knee into a pillow or the wall (no movement)
3. Hold for 10-15 seconds
4. Relax and repeat

*Reps: 10, 3 sets per side*

**Why this helps:** For bursitis, isometrics often tolerated better than dynamic exercises initially

## Sleep Position Modifications

### For Side Sleepers with Outer Hip Pain

**Don't sleep directly on the painful hip:**
- Switch to the other side
- Or sleep slightly forward (not true side position)

**Pillow between knees is essential:**
- Keeps pelvis neutral
- Reduces IT band tension
- Thick, firm pillow works best

**Consider a pillow under the waist:**
- Fills the gap between ribs and pelvis
- Reduces hip drop and compression

### For Back Sleepers

**Pillow under knees:**
- Reduces hip flexor tension
- Takes pressure off lower back
- May help front-of-hip pain

**Pillow under hips (if needed):**
- Slight elevation can reduce groin pressure

### For Those Who Can't Find Comfort

**Try a recliner position:**
- Either in a recliner or propped up in bed
- Takes pressure off hips
- Useful during flare-ups

### Position Changes

- Try to switch positions during the night
- Use pillows to make position changes easier
- Avoid sleeping in fetal position (hip flexors tighten)

## Mattress Considerations

### What Side Sleepers Need

**Medium to medium-soft often best:**
- Allows hips and shoulders to sink slightly
- Too firm creates pressure points
- Too soft causes spine misalignment

### Mattress Toppers

A 2-3 inch memory foam topper can:
- Reduce pressure points
- Provide contouring for hips
- Be cheaper than a new mattress

### When to Replace

- Mattresses over 7-10 years old lose support
- Visible sagging
- You wake worse than you went to sleep

## Managing Specific Conditions

### Greater Trochanteric Bursitis

**Do:**
- Sleep on the non-painful side
- Use pillow between knees
- Ice for 15-20 minutes before bed
- Strengthen hip abductors (clamshells, leg raises)

**Don't:**
- Lie on the affected side
- Stretch the IT band aggressively (often makes it worse initially)
- Cross legs when sitting

### Hip Arthritis

**Do:**
- Gentle movement before bed
- Anti-inflammatory approaches (ice or heat, as preferred)
- Keep hips moving through gentle range of motion
- Consider NSAIDs with doctor guidance

**Don't:**
- Stay in one position all night
- Avoid all movement (gentle exercise helps)

### IT Band/Tightness

**Do:**
- Foam roll quads, TFL (front/side of hip)—NOT directly on IT band
- Strengthen glutes
- Hip flexor stretching

**Don't:**
- Foam roll directly on IT band (often irritates)
- Stretch IT band aggressively

## Daily Habits That Help Night Pain

### During the Day

- **Avoid prolonged sitting:** Set timer for movement breaks
- **Don't cross legs:** Puts stress on outer hip
- **Stand with even weight:** Don't favor one hip
- **Exercise regularly:** Inactivity worsens hip stiffness

### Evening Routine

- **Light movement after dinner:** Walk, gentle stretching
- **Avoid intense exercise late:** May increase inflammation overnight
- **Warm bath or shower:** Relaxes muscles before bed
- **Stretching routine:** 10 minutes before bed

### Before Bed

- **Ice if inflamed:** 15-20 minutes (for bursitis)
- **Heat if stiff:** 15-20 minutes (for arthritis/stiffness)
- **Set up pillows:** Between knees, under waist if needed
- **Start in best position:** Non-painful side or back

## Complete Night Hip Pain Plan

### Daily (10-15 minutes)

**Morning:**
1. Gentle hip circles in bed before rising
2. Glute bridge: 15 reps
3. Brief walking: 5 minutes

**Evening (before bed):**
1. Hip flexor stretch: 60 seconds per side
2. Figure-4 stretch: 60 seconds per side
3. 90/90 stretch: 45 seconds per side
4. Cat-cow: 10 cycles
5. Supine twist: 30 seconds per side

### 3x Per Week

**Strengthening:**
1. Clamshells: 3x20 per side
2. Side-lying leg raises: 3x15 per side
3. Glute bridges: 3x15
4. Single-leg glute bridge: 2x10 per side
5. Isometric hip abduction: 3x10

### Sleep Setup

- Pillow between knees (always)
- Avoid sleeping on painful hip
- Consider waist support pillow
- Change positions when you wake up

## When to See a Doctor

Seek evaluation if:

- Night pain waking you regularly
- Pain not improving after 2-3 weeks of modifications
- Severe pain that prevents sleep
- Limping or significant weakness
- Groin pain with clicking or catching
- Numbness or tingling in the leg
- Hip pain after a fall or injury

### What They May Recommend

- X-rays or MRI
- Physical therapy
- Anti-inflammatory medications
- Injections (cortisone or other)
- Referral to orthopedist if needed

## Key Takeaways

1. **Side sleepers: pillow between knees is essential**—keeps pelvis aligned
2. **Don't sleep on the painful side**—switch or use three-quarter position
3. **Strengthen your glutes**—weak hip muscles are often the root cause
4. **Stretch hip flexors before bed**—tight flexors contribute to hip pain
5. **Consider your mattress**—too firm is often the problem for side sleepers
6. **Bursitis and IT band issues need specific approaches**—not just stretching
7. **Night pain that wakes you deserves evaluation**—don't ignore persistent symptoms

Nighttime hip pain is frustrating but usually manageable. The combination of better sleep positioning, targeted exercises, and addressing any underlying conditions can dramatically improve your sleep quality.
