# Rock Climbing Exercises: Build Strength for the Wall

Climbing is a unique sport that demands grip strength, pulling power, core tension, and body awareness unlike any other activity. While climbing itself is the best training, targeted exercises accelerate progress and prevent the overuse injuries that plague climbers.

**Key insight:** Climbing creates imbalances—strong pulling muscles, weak pushing muscles; strong forearms, vulnerable fingers. Training should address these imbalances, not just build more pulling strength.

---

## Physical Demands of Climbing

### What Climbing Requires

- **Grip and finger strength:** Holding onto holds
- **Pulling power:** Moving your body upward
- **Core tension:** Keeping body close to wall
- **Hip flexibility:** High steps, flags, drop knees
- **Shoulder stability:** Overhead positions, dynamic moves
- **Antagonist balance:** Pushing muscles to prevent injury

### Common Climbing Injuries

| Injury | Cause | Prevention Focus |
|--------|-------|------------------|
| Finger pulley injuries | Crimping, overloading | Progressive loading, open-hand grips |
| Elbow tendinitis | Repetitive pulling | Antagonist training, gradual volume |
| Shoulder impingement | Overhead positions | Rotator cuff strength, mobility |
| Wrist pain | Compression, awkward positions | Wrist mobility, strengthening |
| Lower back pain | Overhang climbing, core fatigue | Core endurance, hip mobility |

## Finger Strength Training

**Caution:** Finger training is high-risk for injury. Progress slowly and never train fingers when fatigued.

### Hangboard Training

**When to start:** At least 1-2 years of consistent climbing.

**Basic Protocol (Repeaters):**
1. Hang for 7 seconds on medium edge
2. Rest 3 seconds
3. Repeat 6 times = 1 set
4. Rest 3 minutes between sets
5. 3-5 sets total

**Progression:**
- Start with larger edges (20mm+)
- Progress to smaller edges
- Add weight only after mastering bodyweight

### Open-Hand Grip Training

**Why it matters:** Safer than crimping, builds tendon strength.

**How to do it:**
1. Hang on large holds with open grip
2. Don't close fingers (no crimp)
3. 10-second hangs, 5 sets
4. Rest 2-3 minutes between

### Finger Curls

**How to do it:**
1. Hold light dumbbells, arms at sides
2. Open fingers, letting weights roll to fingertips
3. Curl fingers closed
4. 3 sets of 15

**Why:** Builds finger flexor strength with less injury risk.

### Rice Bucket Training

**How to do it:**
1. Fill bucket with rice
2. Open and close hands in rice
3. Rotate wrists
4. Make grabbing motions
5. 2-3 minutes daily

**Benefits:** Builds forearm endurance and rehabs tendons.

## Pulling Strength

### Pull-Ups

The foundational climbing exercise.

**How to do it:**
1. Dead hang, hands shoulder-width
2. Pull until chin over bar
3. Lower with control
4. 3-5 sets of max reps (or 5-8)

**Progressions:**
- Assisted (band or machine)
- Standard pull-ups
- Weighted pull-ups
- One-arm progressions

### Frenchies

Builds lock-off strength at different positions.

**How to do it:**
1. Pull up and lock off at top for 5 seconds
2. Lower slowly, pull up, lock at 90° for 5 seconds
3. Lower slowly, pull up, lock at 120° for 5 seconds
4. That's 1 rep
5. 3-4 reps, 3 sets

### Lock-Offs

**How to do it:**
1. Pull up to top position
2. Hold with arms bent
3. Hold 5-10 seconds
4. Lower with control
5. 3-5 sets

### Typewriters

**How to do it:**
1. Pull up, shift weight to one arm
2. Move horizontally across bar
3. Shift to other arm
4. Return to center, lower
5. 3 sets of 4-6

### Lat Pulldowns

**How to do it:**
1. Wide grip on bar
2. Pull to upper chest
3. Control return
4. 3 sets of 10-12

## Core Training for Climbing

### Front Lever Progressions

**Tuck Front Lever:**
1. Hang from bar
2. Pull knees to chest
3. Rotate until back is parallel to ground
4. Hold 10-20 seconds
5. 3-5 sets

**Progressions:**
- Tuck → Advanced tuck → Single leg → Full front lever

### Hanging Leg Raises

**How to do it:**
1. Hang from bar
2. Raise legs until parallel (or higher)
3. Lower with control
4. 3 sets of 10-15

**Progression:** Straight legs, toes to bar

### Hollow Body Hold

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back
2. Press lower back into floor
3. Lift legs and shoulders slightly
4. Hold 30-60 seconds
5. 3-5 sets

### Dead Bug

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back, arms up, knees at 90°
2. Lower opposite arm and leg
3. Keep lower back pressed down
4. 3 sets of 10 each side

### Plank Variations

**Standard Plank:**
- Hold 30-60 seconds, 3 sets

**Side Plank:**
- Hold 30 seconds each side, 3 sets

**Plank with Hip Dips:**
- Rotate hips side to side while holding plank
- 10 each side

## Antagonist Training

Essential for injury prevention and long-term climbing health.

### Push-Ups

**How to do it:**
1. Standard push-ups with full range
2. 3 sets of 15-20

**Why:** Balances all the pulling in climbing.

### Dips

**How to do it:**
1. Parallel bars or bench
2. Lower until shoulders at elbow height
3. Press back up
4. 3 sets of 10-15

### Shoulder Press

**How to do it:**
1. Press dumbbells or barbell overhead
2. Lower with control
3. 3 sets of 10-12

### Reverse Wrist Curls

**How to do it:**
1. Forearm on thigh, palm down
2. Curl wrist up with weight
3. 3 sets of 15

**Why:** Strengthens wrist extensors to balance flexor dominance.

### External Rotation

**How to do it:**
1. Side-lying or with band
2. Elbow at side, rotate forearm out
3. 3 sets of 15 each arm

## Hip Mobility for Climbing

### Frog Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. On all fours, widen knees
2. Rock hips back toward heels
3. Hold 60-90 seconds

### 90/90 Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Sit with front leg at 90°, back leg at 90°
2. Lean forward over front shin
3. 45-60 seconds each side

### Deep Squat Hold

**How to do it:**
1. Squat as deep as possible
2. Keep heels on ground
3. Use support if needed
4. Hold 60-90 seconds

### Pigeon Pose

**How to do it:**
1. Front shin across body
2. Back leg extended
3. Lean forward
4. 60 seconds each side

### Hip CARs

**How to do it:**
1. On all fours
2. Lift knee out to side
3. Circle hip through full range
4. 5 circles each direction, each hip

---

## Shoulder Health

### Y-T-W Raises

**How to do it:**
1. Lie face down
2. Y: Arms at 45°, thumbs up
3. T: Arms to sides
4. W: Elbows bent, rotate up
5. 10 reps each position

### Face Pulls

**How to do it:**
1. Band or cable at face height
2. Pull toward face, separate hands
3. Squeeze shoulder blades
4. 3 sets of 15

### Shoulder CARs

**How to do it:**
1. Slow, controlled arm circles
2. Maximum range of motion
3. 5 each direction, each arm

### Dead Hangs

**How to do it:**
1. Hang from bar, arms straight
2. Engage shoulders (don't let them ride up to ears)
3. Hold 30-60 seconds
4. 3-5 sets

**Benefits:** Shoulder mobility, decompression, grip endurance.

## Sample Training Programs

### For Beginner Climbers (Climbing 1-2x/week)

**2 additional strength sessions:**

**Session 1:**
- Pull-ups (assisted if needed): 3x max
- Push-ups: 3x15
- Plank: 3x30 sec
- Hip mobility: 10 min

**Session 2:**
- Lat pulldown: 3x12
- Dips: 3x10
- Hanging leg raises: 3x10
- Shoulder press: 3x10
- Dead hangs: 3x30 sec

### For Intermediate Climbers (Climbing 3-4x/week)

**2 strength sessions:**

**Session 1 - Pulling + Core:**
- Pull-ups: 4x6-8
- Lock-offs: 3x5 each position
- Front lever progressions: 3x15 sec
- Hanging leg raises: 3x12
- Dead bug: 3x10 each

**Session 2 - Antagonist + Mobility:**
- Push-ups: 3x20
- Shoulder press: 3x10
- Reverse wrist curls: 3x15
- External rotation: 3x15 each
- Full hip mobility routine: 15 min
- Shoulder CARs: 5 each

**1 finger session (if climbing 2+ years):**
- Hangboard: Basic repeater protocol
- Rice bucket: 3 min
- Forearm stretches

### For Advanced Climbers (Climbing 4-5x/week)

**Periodized approach:**

**Strength Phase (4-6 weeks):**
- Focus on max pull strength
- Weighted pull-ups, heavy lock-offs
- Antagonist volume increases

**Power Phase (4-6 weeks):**
- Campus board (if experienced)
- Dynamic movements
- Reduce volume, increase intensity

**Finger Phase (4-6 weeks):**
- Structured hangboard program
- Careful load management
- Deload before projecting

## Injury Prevention

### Warm-Up Protocol

**Before climbing:**
1. General cardio: 5 min (jumping jacks, jogging)
2. Arm circles, shoulder rolls
3. Wrist circles and stretches
4. Easy traversing: 10-15 min
5. Progressive difficulty

### Warning Signs

Stop climbing if you experience:
- Sharp finger pain
- Elbow pain that persists
- Shoulder clicking or catching
- Wrist pain with movement

### Recovery Practices

- **Rest days:** Essential for tendon adaptation
- **Sleep:** When tendons repair
- **Contrast baths:** For finger recovery
- **Self-massage:** Forearms, shoulders

### The 10% Rule

Don't increase climbing volume or intensity by more than 10% per week. Tendons adapt slower than muscles.

---

## The Bottom Line

Climbing rewards patience and smart training. Building finger strength takes years, not months. Protect your tendons, balance your training, and enjoy the process.

**Priorities:**
1. Antagonist training (injury prevention)
2. Core tension development
3. Pulling strength
4. Hip mobility
5. Finger strength (careful progression)

Climb more, but train smart. The wall will always be there—make sure your body is too.
