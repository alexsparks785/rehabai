# What Muscles Do Lying Tricep Extensions Work? Complete Anatomy Guide

Lying tricep extensions (commonly called skull crushers) are performed lying on a bench, lowering weight toward your forehead or behind your head, then extending back up. This exercise targets all three tricep heads with emphasis on the long head, making it one of the best tricep mass builders.

## Primary Muscle Worked

### Triceps Brachii

**Activation Level: Very High (All Three Heads)**

The triceps are the sole focus:

**Long Head:**
- Largest of the three heads
- Runs along the back/inner arm
- Crosses the shoulder joint
- Most emphasized due to shoulder position
- Key for overall arm mass

**Lateral Head:**
- Outer portion of tricep
- Creates the "horseshoe" shape
- Active throughout movement
- Visible from the side

**Medial Head:**
- Deep, inner portion
- Primary elbow extensor
- Works in all tricep exercises
- Provides stability

**Why skull crushers emphasize the long head:**
The long head crosses the shoulder. When your arms are overhead (like in skull crushers), the long head is stretched and must work through a greater range—increasing its activation.

## Secondary Muscles Worked

### Anconeus

**Activation Level: Moderate**

Small muscle at the elbow:
- Assists elbow extension
- Stabilizes the joint
- Works alongside triceps

### Shoulder Stabilizers

**Activation Level: Low to Moderate**

Various muscles maintain arm position:
- Deltoids stabilize
- Rotator cuff contributes
- Pectorals assist slightly
- Not primary movers

### Core (Isometric)

**Activation Level: Low**

Maintains body position:
- Slight bracing
- Not significant
- Bench provides support

## Why Lying Position Works

The lying position creates:
1. **Shoulder flexion**: Arms overhead stretches long head
2. **Stable base**: Bench supports body
3. **Focused isolation**: Can't cheat with body movement
4. **Safe failure**: Weight goes toward forehead, not on head (when controlled)

## Skull Crushers vs Tricep Extensions: Same Thing?

Yes—"skull crushers" and "lying tricep extensions" refer to the same exercise. Different names:
- Skull crushers (dramatic name, weight goes toward skull)
- Lying tricep extensions (technical name)
- French press (sometimes used)
- Nose breakers (another dramatic option)

## Bar Path Options

| Path | Target Area | Notes |
|------|-------------|-------|
| To forehead | Standard, all heads | Most common |
| Behind head | Long head emphasis | Greater stretch |
| To nose/face | Traditional "skull crusher" | Similar to forehead |

**Behind the head** increases long head stretch but requires more shoulder mobility.

## Muscle Activation by Phase

### Starting Position
- Lying on bench, feet flat
- Arms extended, weight above chest/face
- Elbows pointed toward ceiling
- Grip at shoulder width or narrower

### Lowering Phase (Eccentric)
- Bend elbows to lower weight
- Keep upper arms stationary (or slight drift back)
- Lower toward forehead or behind head
- Control the descent

### Bottom Position
- Weight at forehead level or behind head
- Triceps stretched under load
- Elbows at roughly 90°
- Brief pause

### Extension Phase (Concentric)
- Contract triceps to extend elbows
- Press weight back to starting position
- Keep upper arms stable
- Full extension at top

## Common Mistakes

**Elbows flaring out**: Should point toward ceiling, not sides. Flaring reduces tricep activation.

**Upper arms moving excessively**: Should stay relatively fixed. Too much movement = shoulder exercise.

**Going too heavy**: Leads to poor form and elbow stress. Moderate weight, good form.

**Bouncing at bottom**: Using momentum. Control the weight, pause briefly.

**Incomplete extension**: Not locking out. Full extension for full tricep contraction.

**Bar going to chest**: That's a close-grip bench press. Lower toward head, not chest.

## How to Maximize Tricep Activation

1. **Keep elbows pointed up**: Don't let them flare
2. **Upper arms relatively fixed**: Only forearms move (mostly)
3. **Full range of motion**: Deep stretch, full extension
4. **Control the negative**: 2-3 seconds down
5. **Squeeze at lockout**: Full tricep contraction
6. **Moderate weight**: Form over ego
7. **Try behind-head path**: For more long head stretch

## Equipment Options

| Equipment | Pros | Cons |
|-----------|------|------|
| EZ-bar | Wrist-friendly angle | Standard choice |
| Straight barbell | More tricep stretch | Can stress wrists |
| Dumbbells | Independent arms | Harder to stabilize |
| Cables | Constant tension | Different feel |
| Single dumbbell | Two hands on one DB | Easy to control |

**EZ-bar** is most popular due to the angled grip being easier on wrists.

## Variations

| Variation | Primary Change |
|-----------|----------------|
| EZ-bar skull crusher | Standard, wrist-friendly |
| Straight bar skull crusher | More stretch, wrist stress |
| Dumbbell skull crusher | Independent arms |
| Cable lying extension | Constant tension |
| Decline skull crusher | Different angle |
| Incline skull crusher | More long head stretch |
| Single-arm extension | Unilateral focus |

### Behind-the-Head Extension

Lowering the weight behind your head:
- Greater long head stretch
- Increased range of motion
- Requires shoulder mobility
- Excellent for long head emphasis

### Incline Skull Crusher

On an incline bench:
- Arms more overhead
- Even more long head emphasis
- Greater stretch
- Challenging variation

## Skull Crushers vs Other Tricep Exercises

| Exercise | Long Head | Overall Load | Complexity |
|----------|-----------|--------------|------------|
| Skull crushers | Very High | Moderate-High | Moderate |
| Close-grip bench | Moderate | Very High | Low |
| Pushdowns | Moderate | Moderate | Low |
| Overhead extension | Very High | Moderate | Low |
| Dips | Moderate | High | Moderate |
| Kickbacks | Moderate | Low | Low |

Skull crushers excel at long head development with moderate overall loading.

## Programming Recommendations

**For Tricep Mass:**
- 3-4 sets of 8-12 reps
- Moderate weight, full ROM
- Focus on stretch and squeeze
- 2 times per week

**For Long Head Emphasis:**
- Use behind-head path
- 3 sets of 10-15 reps
- Quality over quantity
- Combine with overhead extensions

**As Secondary Tricep Exercise:**
- After close-grip bench or dips
- 3 sets of 10-12 reps
- Focused isolation work

**Superset Ideas:**
- Skull crushers + close-grip bench (isolation + compound)
- Skull crushers + pushdowns (different angles)
- Skull crushers + diamond push-ups (weighted + bodyweight)

## Elbow Health Considerations

Skull crushers can stress the elbows:

**If you experience elbow pain:**
- Reduce weight
- Try EZ-bar instead of straight bar
- Don't lock out completely
- Use behind-head path (less elbow stress)
- Warm up thoroughly
- Consider alternatives (pushdowns, dips)

**Prevention:**
- Proper warm-up
- Don't ego lift
- Full control
- Balanced tricep training

## Key Takeaways

- **Primary muscle**: Triceps—all three heads, long head emphasis
- **Why long head emphasis**: Overhead arm position stretches the long head
- **Bar path options**: To forehead (standard) or behind head (more stretch)
- **Equipment**: EZ-bar most common (wrist-friendly)
- **Common errors**: Elbows flaring, upper arms moving too much, too heavy
- **Key cues**: Elbows up, control the weight, full ROM
- **Best for**: Tricep mass, long head development

Lying tricep extensions are one of the best exercises for tricep mass, particularly the long head. Master the form, use appropriate weight, and watch your triceps grow.
