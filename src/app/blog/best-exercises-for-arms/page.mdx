# Best Exercises for Arms: Build Bigger Biceps and Triceps

Strong, well-developed arms are both functional and aesthetic. Your arms work in every upper body movement, and direct training builds the size and strength most people want. Here are the exercises that work best.

## Arm Anatomy

### Biceps
- **Biceps brachii:** Two heads (long and short), flexes the elbow and supinates the forearm
- **Brachialis:** Underneath the biceps, contributes to arm size
- **Brachioradialis:** Forearm muscle that assists elbow flexion

### Triceps
- **Three heads:** Long, lateral, and medial
- **Makes up 2/3 of arm size**—don't neglect them
- **Primary function:** Elbow extension

## Best Bicep Exercises

### 1. Barbell Curl

**The classic mass builder.**

**How to do it:**
1. Stand, grip bar shoulder-width, arms at sides
2. Curl bar toward shoulders
3. Keep elbows stationary
4. Lower with control

**Sets/reps:** 3-4 sets × 8-12 reps

**Why it works:** Allows heavy loading, hits both bicep heads, simple and effective.

### 2. Dumbbell Curl

**Unilateral work for balance.**

**How to do it:**
1. Stand or sit, dumbbells at sides
2. Curl up, supinating (rotating) wrist as you lift
3. Squeeze at top
4. Lower with control

**Sets/reps:** 3 sets × 10-12 each arm

**Variations:**
- Alternating curls
- Simultaneous curls
- Seated (stricter form)

### 3. Hammer Curl

**Builds brachialis and forearms.**

**How to do it:**
1. Dumbbells at sides, neutral grip (palms facing each other)
2. Curl keeping neutral grip throughout
3. Don't rotate wrists

**Sets/reps:** 3 sets × 10-12 reps

**Why it works:** Targets brachialis, which pushes the bicep up for more size.

### 4. Incline Dumbbell Curl

**Maximum bicep stretch.**

**How to do it:**
1. Lie back on incline bench (45-60°)
2. Arms hang straight down
3. Curl from fully stretched position
4. Feel the stretch at bottom

**Sets/reps:** 3 sets × 10-12 reps

**Why it works:** Increased stretch at bottom hits the long head, which creates the bicep peak.

### 5. Preacher Curl

**Strict isolation.**

**How to do it:**
1. Arm braced on preacher bench
2. Curl from full extension
3. Squeeze at top
4. Slow eccentric

**Sets/reps:** 3 sets × 10-12 reps

**Why it works:** Eliminates momentum, isolates the bicep completely.

### 6. Cable Curl

**Constant tension throughout.**

**How to do it:**
1. Low cable attachment
2. Curl toward shoulders
3. Keep elbows stationary

**Sets/reps:** 3 sets × 12-15 reps

**Why it works:** Cable provides tension throughout entire range, great for pump.

## Best Tricep Exercises

### 1. Close-Grip Bench Press

**Heavy compound for tricep mass.**

**How to do it:**
1. Grip bar about shoulder-width or slightly narrower
2. Lower bar to lower chest
3. Keep elbows tucked
4. Press up, focusing on tricep extension

**Sets/reps:** 4 sets × 6-10 reps

**Why it works:** Allows heavy loading, builds overall tricep mass and strength.

### 2. Tricep Dip

**Bodyweight tricep builder.**

**How to do it:**
1. Grip parallel bars
2. Keep torso upright (more tricep) or lean forward (more chest)
3. Lower until upper arms are parallel
4. Press back up

**Sets/reps:** 3-4 sets × 8-12 reps

**Why it works:** Compound movement, easily loaded with weight belt.

**Alternative:** Bench dips (feet on floor or elevated)

### 3. Skull Crusher (Lying Tricep Extension)

**Targets long head.**

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on bench, arms extended overhead with EZ bar or dumbbells
2. Lower weight toward forehead by bending elbows
3. Keep upper arms stationary
4. Extend back to start

**Sets/reps:** 3 sets × 10-12 reps

**Why it works:** Great stretch on the long head, which is largest tricep muscle.

### 4. Overhead Tricep Extension

**Maximum stretch on long head.**

**How to do it:**
1. Hold dumbbell or cable overhead
2. Lower behind head by bending elbows
3. Feel deep stretch
4. Extend back up

**Sets/reps:** 3 sets × 10-12 reps

**Why it works:** Long head crosses the shoulder joint—overhead position fully stretches it.

### 5. Tricep Pushdown

**The staple isolation exercise.**

**How to do it:**
1. Cable at high position, bar or rope attachment
2. Elbows pinned to sides
3. Push down to full extension
4. Squeeze triceps hard
5. Control the return

**Sets/reps:** 3-4 sets × 12-15 reps

**Why it works:** Easy to feel the muscle, great for pump and finishing sets.

**Attachments:**
- Straight bar: overall triceps
- V-bar: comfortable grip
- Rope: allows hands to separate for extra squeeze

### 6. Diamond Push-Up

**No equipment required.**

**How to do it:**
1. Hands close together, forming diamond shape
2. Lower chest toward hands
3. Keep elbows close to body
4. Push back up

**Sets/reps:** 3 sets × 10-15 reps

**Why it works:** Excellent tricep activation, can be done anywhere.

## Complete Arm Workout

### Workout A: Size Focus

**Warm-Up**
- Light curls: 15 reps
- Light pushdowns: 15 reps
- Arm circles

**Biceps**
1. **Barbell Curl**: 4 × 8
2. **Incline Dumbbell Curl**: 3 × 10
3. **Hammer Curl**: 3 × 12

**Triceps**
1. **Close-Grip Bench Press**: 4 × 8
2. **Skull Crushers**: 3 × 10
3. **Tricep Pushdown**: 3 × 12

### Workout B: Pump Focus

**Superset workout—pair a bicep and tricep exercise.**

**Superset 1:** 3 rounds
- Dumbbell Curl: 12 reps
- Tricep Pushdown: 12 reps

**Superset 2:** 3 rounds
- Hammer Curl: 12 reps
- Overhead Extension: 12 reps

**Superset 3:** 3 rounds
- Cable Curl: 15 reps
- Rope Pushdown: 15 reps

### Workout C: Strength Focus

**Warm-Up**
- Light movement

**Main Workout**
1. **Close-Grip Bench Press**: 4 × 5-6
2. **Weighted Dips**: 3 × 6-8
3. **Barbell Curl**: 4 × 6-8
4. **Weighted Chin-Ups**: 3 × 6-8

## Training Tips for Arms

### Train Triceps for Arm Size
- Triceps are 2/3 of arm mass
- Don't neglect them for biceps
- Include compound pressing

### Full Range of Motion
- Stretch at the bottom
- Squeeze at the top
- Partial reps = partial results

### Mind-Muscle Connection
- Feel the target muscle working
- Don't just move weight
- Control matters more than weight

### Include Both Compounds and Isolation
- Compounds: close-grip bench, dips, chin-ups
- Isolation: curls, extensions, pushdowns
- You need both for complete development

### Progressive Overload Still Applies
- Add reps before adding weight
- Track your lifts
- Small increments over time

## Common Mistakes

### Too Much Weight, Too Little Form
- Swinging curls
- Using momentum
- Partial range of motion

**Fix:** Drop the weight, feel the muscle.

### Neglecting Triceps
- All curls, no extensions
- Small arms despite lots of training

**Fix:** Equal or more volume on triceps.

### Same Exercises Forever
- Muscles adapt
- Progress stalls

**Fix:** Rotate exercises every 4-8 weeks.

### Overtraining Arms
- Training arms 5x per week
- Not recovering between sessions

**Fix:** 2-3 direct arm sessions per week maximum.

## Programming Arms

### Frequency
- 2-3 times per week
- Can be combined with larger body parts or dedicated arm day

### Volume
- 10-15 sets per week for biceps
- 10-15 sets per week for triceps
- Include indirect work from compound lifts

### Exercise Selection
**Weekly bicep training should include:**
- Straight-bar or dumbbell curl
- Hammer variation
- Stretch-focused movement (incline curls)

**Weekly tricep training should include:**
- Compound pressing
- Overhead extension
- Pushdown variation

## The Bottom Line

Big arms require training both biceps and triceps with full range of motion and progressive overload. Include compound movements like close-grip bench and chin-ups alongside isolation exercises like curls and extensions.

Don't forget that triceps make up most of your arm size. Give them equal or more attention than biceps.

Train arms 2-3 times per week, use proper form over heavy weight, and be patient. Arms respond well to consistency.
