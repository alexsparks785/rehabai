# Shoulder Blade Exercises: Build Scapular Stability

Your shoulder blades are the foundation of all arm movement. When they don't move properly or lack stability, shoulder problems follow. Strong, well-controlled scapular muscles are essential for shoulder health, posture, and upper body performance.

**Key insight:** The shoulder blade must be stable yet mobile—it needs to move smoothly with your arm while providing a solid base for the rotator cuff. Training scapular control transforms shoulder function.

---

## Understanding Scapular Function

### Scapular Movements

| Movement | Action | Key Muscles |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| **Elevation** | Shrugging up | Upper trapezius, levator scapulae |
| **Depression** | Pulling down | Lower trapezius, pectoralis minor |
| **Protraction** | Rounding forward | Serratus anterior |
| **Retraction** | Squeezing back | Rhomboids, middle trapezius |
| **Upward rotation** | Tilting up as arm raises | Serratus, upper/lower trapezius |
| **Downward rotation** | Tilting down | Rhomboids, levator scapulae |

### Why Scapular Stability Matters

- **Shoulder health:** Proper scapular position prevents impingement
- **Posture:** Weak scapular muscles cause rounded shoulders
- **Power transfer:** Stable base for pushing and pulling
- **Injury prevention:** Dyskinesis leads to rotator cuff problems
- **Athletic performance:** Essential for throwing, pressing, pulling

### Signs of Scapular Dysfunction

- Shoulder pain with overhead movements
- Scapular winging (blade sticks out)
- Rounded shoulder posture
- Clicking or snapping around shoulder blade
- Neck and upper back tension

## Basic Scapular Exercises

### Scapular Squeezes

**How to do it:**
1. Sit or stand with arms at sides
2. Squeeze shoulder blades together
3. Don't shrug shoulders up
4. Hold 5 seconds
5. Relax completely
6. Perform 15-20 repetitions

### Scapular Shrugs

**How to do it:**
1. Stand with arms at sides
2. Shrug shoulders toward ears
3. Hold 2-3 seconds
4. Lower slowly
5. Perform 12-15 repetitions

### Scapular Depression

**How to do it:**
1. Sit or stand tall
2. Pull shoulder blades down (opposite of shrug)
3. Think "shoulders away from ears"
4. Hold 5 seconds
5. Perform 10-15 repetitions

### Scapular Protraction/Retraction

**How to do it:**
1. Stand with arms extended in front
2. Push hands forward (protraction)—shoulder blades spread apart
3. Pull hands back (retraction)—shoulder blades squeeze together
4. Move through full range
5. Perform 15-20 repetitions

## Scapular Strengthening

### Wall Slides

**How to do it:**
1. Stand with back against wall
2. Arms in "goal post" position (elbows 90°)
3. Keep back of arms touching wall
4. Slide arms up the wall
5. Only go as high as you can maintain contact
6. Slide back down
7. Perform 15-20 repetitions

**Key:** If arms come off wall, you've gone too high.

### Scapular Wall Push-Up

**How to do it:**
1. Stand facing wall, hands at shoulder height
2. Let shoulder blades come together (retract)
3. Push hard into wall, spreading shoulder blades apart (protract)
4. Feel serratus anterior work around ribs
5. Perform 15-20 repetitions

### Push-Up Plus

**How to do it:**
1. Regular push-up position (or from knees)
2. At top of push-up, push extra ("plus")
3. Spread shoulder blades apart maximally
4. Let shoulder blades come back together
5. Perform full push-up, repeat plus at top
6. Perform 10-15 repetitions

### Face Pulls

**How to do it:**
1. Attach band at face height
2. Grip band with both hands
3. Pull toward face, elbows high
4. At end, rotate hands outward
5. Squeeze shoulder blades together
6. Control return
7. Perform 15-20 repetitions

### Band Pull-Aparts

**How to do it:**
1. Hold band at shoulder height, arms extended
2. Pull band apart by squeezing shoulder blades
3. Keep arms straight
4. Control return to start
5. Perform 20-30 repetitions

### Prone Y-T-W Raises

**Y Raise:**
1. Lie face down
2. Arms at 45° angle (Y shape)
3. Thumbs up
4. Lift arms, squeezing shoulder blades
5. Hold 3 seconds
6. Perform 10-12 repetitions

**T Raise:**
1. Arms straight out to sides
2. Thumbs up
3. Lift arms, squeezing shoulder blades together
4. Perform 10-12 repetitions

**W Raise:**
1. Elbows bent 90°, upper arms at shoulder level
2. Squeeze shoulder blades together
3. Rotate forearms up toward ceiling
4. Perform 10-12 repetitions

### Low Rows (Scapular Focus)

**How to do it:**
1. Cable or band at chest height
2. Pull elbows back and down
3. Lead with shoulder blades—squeeze first
4. Feel lower traps engage
5. Perform 12-15 repetitions

## Advanced Exercises

### Scapular Pull-Ups

**How to do it:**
1. Hang from bar, arms straight
2. Pull shoulder blades down and together (no elbow bend)
3. Body rises slightly
4. Control the release
5. Perform 10-15 repetitions

### Serratus Punch

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back, arm pointing toward ceiling
2. Hold light weight
3. Push hand toward ceiling (shoulder blade lifts off floor)
4. Lower shoulder blade back down
5. Perform 12-15 repetitions each arm

### Bear Position Scapular Push-Ups

**How to do it:**
1. On hands and knees, lift knees 1 inch off ground
2. Let shoulder blades pinch together
3. Push into hands, spreading shoulder blades
4. Maintain flat back and hovering knees
5. Perform 10-15 repetitions

### Landmine Press

**How to do it:**
1. Press barbell from shoulder with one arm
2. At top, reach forward (protract shoulder blade)
3. Control return
4. Perform 10-12 repetitions each arm

## Scapular CARs

Controlled Articular Rotations for full scapular health.

**How to do it:**
1. Stand with arm at side
2. Elevate shoulder blade (shrug up)
3. Protract (round forward)
4. Depress (pull down)
5. Retract (squeeze back)
6. Complete full circle smoothly
7. Reverse direction
8. Perform 5 circles each direction, each side

**Key:** Isolate scapula—don't move the arm itself.

## Condition-Specific Exercises

### For Scapular Winging

**Priority exercises:**
1. Wall push-up plus
2. Push-up plus (progress from knees)
3. Serratus punch
4. Bear position push-ups
5. Face pulls

### For Rounded Shoulders

**Priority exercises:**
1. Band pull-aparts
2. Face pulls
3. Prone Y-T-W
4. Wall slides
5. Scapular squeezes

### For Shoulder Impingement

**Priority exercises:**
1. Scapular wall push-ups
2. Prone Y and T raises
3. Low rows with squeeze
4. Wall slides
5. Side-lying external rotation

## Common Mistakes

### 1. Shrugging During Exercises

**Problem:** Upper traps dominate, lower traps don't engage
**Fix:** Cue "shoulders down" before and during exercises

### 2. Neglecting Serratus Anterior

**Problem:** Scapular winging, poor protraction
**Fix:** Include push-up plus, serratus punch, bear position work

### 3. Only Doing Retraction

**Problem:** Imbalanced training, tight posterior muscles
**Fix:** Include protraction work (push-up plus, serratus exercises)

### 4. Moving Arm Instead of Scapula

**Problem:** Arm muscles compensate for weak scapular control
**Fix:** Isolate scapular movement, especially in CARs

### 5. Going Too Heavy

**Problem:** Poor scapular control, compensation patterns
**Fix:** Light weight, focus on muscle engagement

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Scapular control is foundational** — Affects all shoulder function
2. **Balance protraction and retraction** — Both matter
3. **Lower trap activation is key** — Often weak, causes problems
4. **Serratus anterior prevents winging** — Push-up plus is essential
5. **CARs maintain mobility** — Do them daily
6. **Light weight, quality movement** — Feel the muscles work

**Daily minimum:**
- Scapular CARs: 5 circles each direction
- Band pull-aparts: 20 reps
- Wall slides or push-up plus: 10 reps
- Total: 3-4 minutes

Strong, well-controlled shoulder blades are the foundation of shoulder health. Build this foundation, and everything from pressing to pulling improves.
