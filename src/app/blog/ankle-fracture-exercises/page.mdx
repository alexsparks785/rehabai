# Ankle Fracture Exercises: Recovery After Broken Ankle

An ankle fracture requires careful rehabilitation to restore mobility, strength, and function. Whether treated in a cast or with surgery, proper exercises are essential for returning to normal activities.

## Understanding Ankle Fractures

Ankle fractures can involve one or more bones:
- **Lateral malleolus:** Outer ankle (fibula) — most common
- **Medial malleolus:** Inner ankle (tibia)
- **Posterior malleolus:** Back of tibia
- **Bimalleolar:** Two bones
- **Trimalleolar:** Three areas

**Treatment depends on:**
- Stability of the fracture
- Displacement
- Number of bones involved
- Ligament involvement

**Cast/boot:** Stable, non-displaced fractures
**Surgery:** Unstable, displaced, or multiple fractures

## Healing Timeline

- **Bone healing:** 6-12 weeks typically
- **Protected weight-bearing:** 6-8 weeks
- **Return to normal activities:** 3-6 months
- **Full recovery:** 6-12 months

**Follow YOUR surgeon's or physician's specific protocol.**

## Phase 1: Non-Weight-Bearing (Weeks 0-6)

### Exercises While Casted/Booted

**Toe Movements**
1. Wiggle toes frequently
2. Curl and spread toes
3. Maintains circulation and mobility
4. Multiple times daily

**Ankle Pumps** (If Boot Allows)
1. Point toes down (plantarflexion)
2. Pull toes up (dorsiflexion)
3. Within boot or after removal for exercises
4. 20-30 repetitions

**Hip and Knee Exercises**
1. Straight leg raises
2. Hip abduction (side-lying)
3. Quad sets
4. Maintains leg strength

**Upper Body/Core**
1. Maintain fitness where possible
2. Seated exercises
3. Core work (modified)

### Swelling Management
- Elevate above heart when possible
- Ankle pumps promote circulation
- Ice if accessible and recommended
- Compression when appropriate

## Phase 2: Protected Weight-Bearing (Weeks 6-8)

### Transition Period
- Walking boot typically
- Crutches with partial weight
- Progress as directed by physician

### Range of Motion

**Active Ankle ROM**
1. Sit with foot off floor
2. Point and flex ankle
3. Circles in both directions
4. In and out movements
5. 15-20 repetitions each

**Alphabet Exercise**
1. Trace letters A-Z with big toe
2. Uses all ankle movements
3. 1-2 times through

**Calf Stretches** (Gentle)
1. Towel around foot
2. Pull foot toward shin
3. Hold 30 seconds
4. Gentle, no pain

### Early Strengthening

**Isometric Exercises**
1. Push foot against immovable object
2. All directions (up, down, in, out)
3. Hold 5-10 seconds
4. 10 repetitions each direction

**Toe Raises** (Seated)
1. Sit with feet flat
2. Lift toes off floor
3. 20-30 repetitions

**Heel Raises** (Seated)
1. Lift heels off floor
2. 20-30 repetitions

## Phase 3: Full Weight-Bearing (Weeks 8-12)

### Progressive ROM

**Standing Calf Stretch**
1. Wall stretch, back knee straight
2. Hold 30 seconds
3. Repeat 3 times

**Soleus Stretch**
1. Same position, bend back knee
2. Hold 30 seconds
3. Repeat 3 times

**Ankle Mobilization**
1. Knee-to-wall stretch
2. Measure progress
3. Work toward equal sides

### Strengthening Progression

**Heel Raises** (Standing)
1. Both feet initially
2. Rise onto toes
3. Lower slowly
4. 3 sets of 15
5. Progress to single leg

**Resistance Band Exercises**
1. Band around forefoot
2. Plantarflexion (point)
3. Dorsiflexion (pull up)
4. Inversion (turn in)
5. Eversion (turn out)
6. 3 sets of 15 each direction

**Step-Ups**
1. Low step (4-6 inches)
2. Progress height
3. 3 sets of 12 each side

**Mini Squats**
1. Partial squat
2. Equal weight both legs
3. 3 sets of 15

### Gait Training

**Walking Focus**
1. Heel-toe pattern
2. Equal step length
3. Push through toes
4. Progress distance gradually

## Phase 4: Progressive Strengthening (Weeks 12-16)

### Advanced Calf Work

**Single-Leg Heel Raises**
1. On flat ground first
2. Progress to step (eccentric)
3. 3 sets of 12-15

**Eccentric Heel Drops**
1. Rise on both feet
2. Lower on one foot (off step)
3. 3 sets of 15

### Functional Exercises

**Lunges**
1. Forward and reverse
2. Control and balance
3. 3 sets of 10 each

**Single-Leg Squats**
1. Partial depth
2. Progress as able
3. 2-3 sets of 10

**Step-Downs**
1. Stand on step
2. Lower opposite foot toward floor
3. Control with stance leg
4. 3 sets of 12 each

### Balance Training

**Single-Leg Balance**
1. Stand on injured leg
2. Progress: eyes closed, foam pad
3. 3×30 seconds

**Balance Board**
1. Start with bilateral stance
2. Progress to single leg
3. Add reaches and movements

## Phase 5: Return to Activities (Weeks 16+)

### Agility Drills

**Lateral Shuffles**
1. Side-step quickly
2. Stay low
3. 2-3 sets of 20 yards

**Carioca**
1. Crossover side movement
2. 2-3 sets of 20 yards

**Figure-8s**
1. Walk, then jog
2. Progress to running
3. Start large, progress smaller

### Plyometrics (When Ready)

**Box Jumps** (Low)
1. Jump onto low box
2. Step down initially
3. 2-3 sets of 8

**Lateral Hops**
1. Side-to-side over line
2. Land softly
3. 2-3 sets of 10 each direction

**Single-Leg Hops**
1. Forward, lateral, rotational
2. Build progressively
3. 2-3 sets of 6-8 each

### Running Progression

1. Walk-jog intervals
2. Continuous jogging
3. Tempo runs
4. Sprints
5. Direction changes

## Sample Weekly Program (Week 10)

### Day 1
1. Warm-up: Bike or walking 10 min
2. Standing calf stretch: 3×30 sec
3. Band exercises (4-way): 3×15 each
4. Heel raises (bilateral): 3×15
5. Step-ups: 3×12 each
6. Single-leg balance: 3×30 sec

### Day 2
1. Warm-up: 5 min
2. Squats: 3×12
3. Lunges: 3×10 each
4. Step-downs: 3×12 each
5. Balance board: 5 min

### Day 3: Rest or Light Walking

### Day 4
1. Warm-up: 10 min
2. Single-leg heel raises: 2×12 each
3. Band exercises: 3×15 each
4. Single-leg squat: 2×10 each
5. Balance challenges: 10 min

### Day 5
1. Walking progression: 20-30 min
2. Light stretching
3. Ice if needed

## Managing Swelling

### Common and Normal
Swelling may persist for months after ankle fracture.

### Management
- Elevate when resting
- Compression sock
- Ice after activity
- Ankle pumps frequently
- Movement helps

### When to Be Concerned
- Sudden increase in swelling
- Redness and warmth
- Severe pain
- Contact your physician

## Hardware Considerations

### If You Have Plates/Screws
- Usually stay permanently
- Removal if symptomatic (rare)
- May feel hardware sometimes
- Usually not problematic

### Scar Management
1. Massage after healed (4-6 weeks)
2. Mobilize in all directions
3. Prevents adhesions

## Return to Sport Criteria

**Before full return:**
- Full, pain-free ROM
- Strength >90% of other side
- Single-leg hop test near equal
- Completed agility progression
- Sport-specific movements pain-free
- Physician clearance

## Key Takeaways

1. **Follow weight-bearing restrictions** — don't rush
2. **Keep toes moving** — even while casted
3. **ROM is priority early** — prevent stiffness
4. **Progress through phases** — systematic approach
5. **Balance training is essential** — prevents future sprains
6. **Swelling is normal** — may last months
7. **Full recovery takes time** — 6-12 months typical
8. **Return when ready** — meet objective criteria

Ankle fracture recovery requires patience, but with consistent rehabilitation, most people return to full activity. Follow your medical team's guidance and progress systematically through each phase.
