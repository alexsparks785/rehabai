# Best Exercises for Hamstrings: Build Stronger, More Flexible Legs

Your hamstrings are crucial for running, jumping, and protecting your knees. Yet they're often underdeveloped compared to quadriceps. These exercises build the hamstring strength and flexibility you need.

## Understanding Hamstrings

Three muscles make up the hamstrings:

**Biceps Femoris** – Outer hamstring (long and short heads)
**Semitendinosus** – Inner hamstring
**Semimembranosus** – Inner hamstring, deepest

These muscles:
- Flex the knee (curl leg)
- Extend the hip (straighten from bent position)
- Cross both hip and knee joints (except short head)

Training both functions—knee flexion and hip extension—is essential for complete development.

## Why Hamstring Strength Matters

**Injury Prevention** – Weak hamstrings are prone to strains
**Knee Health** – Balance quads to protect ACL
**Athletic Performance** – Power for running and jumping
**Posture** – Balance anterior pelvic tilt
**Aesthetics** – Complete leg development

## Best Hamstring Exercises

### 1. Romanian Deadlift (RDL)

**The king of hamstring exercises.**

**How to do it:**
1. Hold barbell or dumbbells in front of thighs
2. Hinge at hips, pushing them back
3. Keep back flat, slight knee bend
4. Lower until you feel deep hamstring stretch
5. Drive hips forward to stand

**Sets/reps:** 3-4 sets × 8-10 reps

**Why it's best:** Loads hamstrings through full stretch under tension. Builds both strength and flexibility.

### 2. Nordic Curl

**The ultimate hamstring strengthener.**

**How to do it:**
1. Kneel, ankles secured (under pad or held by partner)
2. Keep body straight from knees to head
3. Lower yourself forward as slowly as possible
4. Catch yourself at bottom, push back up
5. Resist gravity as long as you can

**Sets/reps:** 3 sets × 4-8 reps (or negatives only)

**Why it's best:** Eccentric loading builds hamstring strength like nothing else. Dramatically reduces injury risk.

**Progression:** Start with just negatives (lowering only). Build to full reps over months.

### 3. Lying Leg Curl

**Direct hamstring isolation.**

**How to do it:**
1. Lie face down on machine
2. Pad behind ankles
3. Curl heels toward glutes
4. Squeeze at top
5. Lower with control

**Sets/reps:** 3 sets × 10-12 reps

**Why it's best:** Isolates knee flexion function of hamstrings.

### 4. Seated Leg Curl

**Emphasizes stretch position.**

**How to do it:**
1. Sit in machine, pad behind ankles
2. Curl heels under seat
3. Squeeze at full contraction
4. Extend with control

**Sets/reps:** 3 sets × 10-12 reps

**Why it's best:** Hips flexed position emphasizes stretch. Complements lying curl.

### 5. Stiff-Leg Deadlift

**Maximum hamstring stretch.**

**How to do it:**
1. Minimal knee bend throughout (not locked)
2. Hinge deeply at hips
3. Lower as far as flexibility allows
4. Feel intense hamstring stretch
5. Drive hips forward to stand

**Sets/reps:** 3 sets × 8-10 reps

**Why it's best:** Greater stretch than RDL, maximum lengthened position work.

### 6. Single-Leg Romanian Deadlift

**Unilateral hip hinge.**

**How to do it:**
1. Stand on one leg
2. Hinge at hip, reaching toward ground
3. Back leg extends behind for balance
4. Keep back flat throughout
5. Return to standing

**Sets/reps:** 3 sets × 8-10 each leg

**Why it's best:** Addresses imbalances, challenges stability, functional movement.

### 7. Glute-Ham Raise

**Gold standard posterior chain exercise.**

**How to do it:**
1. Position in GHD with knees on pad
2. Start with torso parallel to ground
3. Lower body by extending at knees
4. Pull yourself back up by curling
5. Control throughout

**Sets/reps:** 3 sets × 6-10 reps

**Why it's best:** Combines hip extension and knee flexion—hits hamstrings completely.

**No GHD?** Nordic curls are similar.

### 8. Good Morning

**Barbell hip hinge.**

**How to do it:**
1. Bar on upper back (like squat position)
2. Hinge at hips, pushing them back
3. Slight knee bend
4. Lower until torso approaches parallel
5. Drive hips to stand

**Sets/reps:** 3 sets × 8-10 reps

**Why it's best:** Loads hamstrings and back through hip hinge pattern.

**Caution:** Start light, master form before adding weight.

### 9. Slider/Swiss Ball Leg Curl

**Bodyweight hamstring challenge.**

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back, heels on sliders or ball
2. Lift hips into bridge position
3. Curl heels toward glutes
4. Extend back out
5. Keep hips elevated throughout

**Sets/reps:** 3 sets × 10-12 reps

**Why it's best:** Equipment-free hamstring curl. Challenging and effective.

### 10. Kettlebell Swing

**Power and conditioning.**

**How to do it:**
1. Hinge at hips, kettlebell between legs
2. Drive hips forward explosively
3. Let momentum swing kettlebell to shoulder height
4. Control the return, hinge again

**Sets/reps:** 3 sets × 15-20 reps

**Why it's best:** Trains hamstrings with power and endurance. Great conditioning.

## Complete Hamstring Workouts

### Workout A: Strength Focus

**Warm-Up (3 minutes)**
- Leg swings: 10 each direction
- Bodyweight RDLs: 10 reps
- Glute bridges: 10 reps

**Main Workout**
1. Romanian Deadlift: 4 × 8
2. Lying Leg Curl: 3 × 10
3. Nordic Curl Negatives: 3 × 5
4. Single-Leg RDL: 2 × 8 each

### Workout B: Balanced Development

**Warm-Up (3 minutes)**
- Same as above

**Main Workout**
1. Stiff-Leg Deadlift: 3 × 10
2. Seated Leg Curl: 3 × 12
3. Glute-Ham Raise (or Nordic): 3 × 6
4. Kettlebell Swings: 3 × 15
5. Slider Leg Curls: 2 × 12

### Bodyweight Only

**No equipment needed:**
1. Single-Leg RDL (bodyweight): 3 × 10 each
2. Nordic Curl Negatives: 3 × 5
3. Slider/Towel Leg Curls: 3 × 10
4. Glute Bridges (single-leg): 3 × 12 each

## Hamstring Training Tips

### Train Both Functions
- Hip extension: RDLs, good mornings, swings
- Knee flexion: curls, Nordics, GHR
- You need both for complete development

### Include Eccentric Work
- Nordic curls build resilient hamstrings
- Slow negatives on RDLs help
- Eccentric strength prevents strains

### Don't Neglect Single-Leg Work
- Addresses imbalances
- More sport-specific
- Include regularly

### Balance with Quad Work
- Don't ignore quads for hamstrings
- Balanced development is key
- Aim for roughly equal strength

### Stretch After Training
- Hamstrings tighten from training
- Post-workout stretching helps
- Maintain flexibility

## Common Mistakes

### All Curls, No Hip Hinges
- Misses hip extension function
- RDLs are essential, not optional

### Rounding Back on RDLs
- Shifts load to lower back
- Keep spine neutral throughout
- Reduce weight if back rounds

### Ignoring Eccentric Training
- Nordic curls prevent injuries
- Eccentric strength is crucial
- Add them even if they're hard

### Training Hamstrings After Quads Only
- Hamstrings fatigued from quad work
- Give them dedicated attention
- Or train them first occasionally

## Hamstring Flexibility

Tight hamstrings? Add these:

**Standing Hamstring Stretch**
- Foot on elevated surface
- Hinge at hips toward foot
- Hold 30-60 seconds

**Lying Hamstring Stretch**
- On back, raise one leg
- Pull toward chest with strap
- Keep knee straight

**RDLs themselves** build flexibility under load.

## Programming Hamstrings

### Frequency
- 2x per week minimum
- Can be part of leg day or separate

### Volume
- 10-15 sets per week for development
- Mix hip extension and knee flexion

### Exercise Order
- Heavy compounds first (RDLs)
- Isolation second (curls)
- Nordic work can go anywhere

## The Bottom Line

Strong hamstrings require both hip extension work (RDLs, good mornings) and knee flexion work (curls, Nordics). Don't skip either.

Include Nordic curls or negatives regularly—they're the best injury prevention tool for hamstrings. Train hamstrings twice per week with intention.

Your hamstrings are half your upper leg. Train them like it.
