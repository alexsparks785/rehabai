# Best Exercises You Should Be Doing: The Most Effective Movements

With thousands of exercises to choose from, it's easy to overcomplicate training. But the truth is, a handful of movements deliver the vast majority of results. Here are the exercises that belong in virtually every program.

## What Makes an Exercise "Best"?

The most effective exercises share these qualities:

**Compound movements**: Work multiple joints and muscles simultaneously.

**Functional**: Transfer to real-world activities and sports.

**Scalable**: Can be progressed or regressed for any fitness level.

**Efficient**: Maximum results for time invested.

**Safe**: Can be performed long-term without excessive injury risk.

## The Essential Lower Body Exercises

### 1. Squat (Any Variation)

**Why it's essential**: The squat is the king of lower body exercises, training quads, glutes, hamstrings, and core simultaneously.

**Muscles worked**: Quadriceps, glutes, hamstrings, core, lower back

**Best variations**:
- Goblet squat (beginner-friendly)
- Back squat (maximum loading)
- Front squat (quad emphasis, core challenge)
- Bulgarian split squat (unilateral, mobility)

**How to progress**: Bodyweight → goblet → barbell back squat → heavier loads

### 2. Hip Hinge (Deadlift/RDL)

**Why it's essential**: Trains the entire posterior chain—the muscles most people neglect and most need for posture, power, and injury prevention.

**Muscles worked**: Hamstrings, glutes, lower back, upper back, grip

**Best variations**:
- Romanian deadlift (hamstring focus)
- Conventional deadlift (full body)
- Trap bar deadlift (easier on lower back)
- Single-leg RDL (balance, unilateral)

**How to progress**: Bodyweight hinge → light RDL → conventional deadlift → heavier loads

### 3. Lunge

**Why it's essential**: Unilateral training addresses imbalances, improves balance, and has excellent athletic carryover.

**Muscles worked**: Quads, glutes, hamstrings, core (stabilization)

**Best variations**:
- Reverse lunge (knee-friendly)
- Walking lunge (dynamic)
- Bulgarian split squat (intensity)
- Lateral lunge (frontal plane)

### 4. Hip Thrust

**Why it's essential**: The most effective exercise for glute development, with research showing higher glute activation than squats or deadlifts.

**Muscles worked**: Glutes (primary), hamstrings

**How to do it**: Upper back on bench, drive hips up, squeeze glutes at top.

## The Essential Upper Body Exercises

### 5. Push-Up (Or Bench Press)

**Why it's essential**: The foundational horizontal pushing movement. Builds chest, shoulders, and triceps while requiring core stability.

**Muscles worked**: Chest, anterior deltoids, triceps, core

**Best variations**:
- Push-up (bodyweight, scalable)
- Dumbbell bench press (unilateral, shoulder-friendly)
- Barbell bench press (maximum loading)
- Incline press (upper chest emphasis)

**How to progress**: Incline push-up → standard push-up → decline → weighted

### 6. Row (Any Variation)

**Why it's essential**: Balances all the pushing most people do, builds back thickness, improves posture, and protects shoulders.

**Muscles worked**: Lats, rhomboids, rear deltoids, biceps, lower back

**Best variations**:
- Dumbbell row (unilateral, accessible)
- Barbell row (heavy loading)
- Cable row (constant tension)
- Inverted row (bodyweight option)

**Key point**: Most people need more pulling than pushing. Prioritize rows.

### 7. Vertical Pull (Pull-Up/Lat Pulldown)

**Why it's essential**: Builds back width, creates the V-taper appearance, and develops functional pulling strength.

**Muscles worked**: Lats, biceps, rear deltoids, core

**Best variations**:
- Lat pulldown (accessible, adjustable)
- Pull-up (bodyweight mastery)
- Chin-up (more bicep involvement)
- Assisted pull-up (progression tool)

**Goal**: Work toward unassisted pull-ups if you can't do them yet.

### 8. Overhead Press

**Why it's essential**: Builds shoulder strength and size, requires total body stability, and has excellent functional carryover.

**Muscles worked**: Deltoids (all heads), triceps, upper chest, core

**Best variations**:
- Dumbbell press (unilateral, shoulder-friendly)
- Barbell press (maximum loading)
- Push press (power development)
- Arnold press (full range of motion)

## The Essential Core Exercises

### 9. Plank (And Variations)

**Why it's essential**: Builds anti-extension strength—the core's primary function in real life and athletics.

**Muscles worked**: Rectus abdominis, obliques, transverse abdominis, lower back

**Best variations**:
- Standard plank (foundation)
- Side plank (obliques, lateral stability)
- Plank with shoulder tap (anti-rotation)
- Dead bug (dynamic stability)

### 10. Loaded Carry

**Why it's essential**: Perhaps the most functional core exercise. Builds grip, core stability, and total body coordination.

**Muscles worked**: Core (all), grip, traps, full body stabilization

**Best variations**:
- Farmer's walk (bilateral)
- Suitcase carry (unilateral, anti-lateral flexion)
- Overhead carry (shoulder stability)
- Goblet carry (front-loaded challenge)

**How to do it**: Pick up heavy weights, walk with perfect posture.

## Honorable Mentions

These exercises are excellent additions depending on your goals:

### For Glutes
- **Cable kickback**: Isolation for glute detail
- **Clamshell**: Glute medius activation

### For Arms
- **Bicep curl**: Direct bicep work
- **Tricep pushdown**: Direct tricep work

### For Shoulders
- **Lateral raise**: Side delt development
- **Face pull**: Rear delt and rotator cuff health

### For Core
- **Pallof press**: Anti-rotation strength
- **Ab wheel rollout**: Advanced anti-extension

### For Conditioning
- **Kettlebell swing**: Power and cardio combined
- **Burpee**: Full body conditioning

## Common Questions

### "Do I need isolation exercises?"

Compound exercises cover most needs. Add isolation work:
- For lagging body parts
- For aesthetics (arms, shoulders)
- Once you've mastered the basics
- If you have extra time

### "What about abs?"

Squats, deadlifts, overhead presses, and carries all train your core. Direct ab work is supplementary, not essential.

### "How many exercises do I need?"

For a complete workout: 4-6 exercises is plenty. The exercises above cover all major movement patterns.

### "Can I build a good physique with just these?"

Absolutely. These exercises have built countless impressive physiques. Fancy exercises are unnecessary.

## The Bottom Line

The best exercises are:

1. **Squat** (quad, glute, full lower body)
2. **Hip hinge** (posterior chain)
3. **Lunge** (unilateral lower body)
4. **Hip thrust** (glute focused)
5. **Push-up/Bench** (horizontal push)
6. **Row** (horizontal pull)
7. **Pull-up/Pulldown** (vertical pull)
8. **Overhead press** (vertical push)
9. **Plank** (core stability)
10. **Loaded carry** (functional core)

Master these movements. Progress them over time. Everything else is supplementary.

Simplicity works. Complexity is often just distraction.
