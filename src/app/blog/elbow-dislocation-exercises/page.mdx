# Elbow Dislocation Exercises: Recovery and Rehabilitation

An elbow dislocation is a serious injury requiring careful rehabilitation. The right exercises restore motion, rebuild strength, and prevent long-term complications like chronic instability or stiffness.

## Understanding Elbow Dislocation

Elbow dislocation occurs when the bones of the forearm (ulna and radius) are displaced from the upper arm bone (humerus). It's the second most common major joint dislocation after the shoulder.

**Types:**
- **Simple dislocation:** No fractures involved
- **Complex dislocation:** Associated fractures (more serious)

**Common causes:**
- Falling on outstretched hand
- Sports injuries
- Motor vehicle accidents
- Direct trauma

**Associated injuries:**
- Ligament tears (medial and lateral collateral)
- Fractures (radial head, coronoid, olecranon)
- Nerve injuries (ulnar nerve most common)
- Vascular injuries (rare but serious)

## Treatment Overview

After reduction (putting the joint back in place):

**Simple dislocations:**
- Brief immobilization (7-10 days typically)
- Early protected motion
- Progressive rehabilitation

**Complex dislocations:**
- May require surgery
- Longer immobilization
- Modified rehabilitation

**Critical principle:** Early motion (when cleared) prevents stiffness, which is the most common complication.

## Phase 1: Protected Motion (Days 7-21)

### When to Start

**Begin exercises only after:**
- Joint is reduced and stable
- Your doctor clears you for movement
- Any splint/brace allows motion

### Elbow Flexion/Extension

**How to do it:**
1. Support arm on table or pillow
2. Slowly bend elbow as far as comfortable
3. Slowly straighten (don't force full extension)
4. 10-15 repetitions
5. 4-6 times daily

**Focus:** Achieve functional range (30-130 degrees of flexion) first.

### Forearm Rotation

**How to do it:**
1. Elbow at side, bent 90 degrees
2. Slowly rotate palm up (supination)
3. Slowly rotate palm down (pronation)
4. 10-15 repetitions
5. 4-6 times daily

### Wrist and Finger Exercises

Keep hand mobile to prevent stiffness.

**Wrist circles:** 10 each direction
**Fist making:** 15 repetitions
**Finger spreads:** 15 repetitions

### Shoulder Mobility

**Pendulum exercises:**
1. Lean forward
2. Let arm hang and swing gently
3. Small circles
4. 10 each direction

**Shoulder shrugs and rolls:** 10 repetitions each

### What to Avoid
- Forcing through pain
- Valgus stress (pushing elbow inward)
- Heavy lifting or gripping
- Weight-bearing on arm

## Phase 2: Progressive Mobility (Weeks 3-6)

### Active-Assisted Range of Motion

Use your other hand to help achieve more range.

**Assisted Elbow Flexion**
1. Use good hand to help bend elbow further
2. Gentle sustained pressure
3. Hold 20-30 seconds at end range
4. 5-10 repetitions

**Assisted Elbow Extension**
1. Place arm on table, forearm off edge
2. Let gravity assist straightening
3. Or gently press with other hand
4. Hold 20-30 seconds
5. 5-10 repetitions

### Stretching Exercises

**Wrist Flexor Stretch**
1. Extend arm, palm up
2. Pull fingers back
3. Hold 30 seconds
4. Repeat 3 times

**Wrist Extensor Stretch**
1. Extend arm, palm down
2. Press wrist down
3. Hold 30 seconds
4. Repeat 3 times

**Triceps Stretch**
1. Arm overhead, bend elbow
2. Use other hand to press elbow back
3. Hold 30 seconds

**Biceps Stretch**
1. Arm behind, palm on wall
2. Rotate body away
3. Hold 30 seconds

### Progressive Forearm Rotation

**With Resistance:**
1. Hold light rod or hammer
2. Rotate fully both directions
3. 15-20 repetitions
4. Weight of rod provides gentle resistance

### Heat Before Exercise

Warming the joint improves mobility:
- Warm shower or bath
- Heating pad for 10-15 minutes
- Warm water soaks

## Phase 3: Strengthening (Weeks 6-12)

### Isometric Exercises

No joint movement—builds strength safely.

**Isometric Elbow Flexion**
1. Elbow bent 90 degrees
2. Place other hand on wrist
3. Try to bend against resistance
4. Hold 5-10 seconds
5. 10 repetitions

**Isometric Elbow Extension**
1. Same position
2. Try to straighten against resistance
3. Hold 5-10 seconds
4. 10 repetitions

**Isometric Forearm Rotation**
1. Forearm neutral, elbow at side
2. Partner or other hand resists rotation
3. Hold 5-10 seconds
4. Both directions

### Dynamic Strengthening

**Biceps Curls**
1. Light weight (2-5 lbs to start)
2. Full range of motion
3. 2-3 sets of 15

**Triceps Extensions**
1. Light weight
2. Overhead or kickback style
3. 2-3 sets of 15

**Forearm Curls**
1. Wrist curls palm up
2. Reverse curls palm down
3. 2-3 sets of 15 each

**Hammer Curls**
1. Neutral grip (thumbs up)
2. Curl to shoulder
3. 2-3 sets of 15

### Pronation/Supination Strengthening

**With Resistance Band:**
1. Band anchored, elbow at side
2. Rotate forearm against resistance
3. Both directions
4. 2-3 sets of 15

**With Weighted Rod:**
1. Hold rod at one end
2. Rotate forearm
3. 2-3 sets of 15

### Grip Strengthening

**Ball Squeeze**
1. Tennis ball or stress ball
2. Firm squeeze, hold 5 seconds
3. 15-20 repetitions

**Hand Gripper**
1. Light to moderate resistance
2. 2-3 sets of 10-15

**Putty Exercises**
1. Various grips and pinches
2. 3-5 minutes

## Phase 4: Functional Strengthening (Weeks 10-16)

### Closed Chain Exercises

Weight-bearing through the arm.

**Wall Push-Ups**
1. Hands on wall, shoulder width
2. Lean in, then push back
3. 2-3 sets of 15

**Counter Push-Ups**
1. Hands on counter
2. Lower chest toward counter
3. Push back up
4. 2-3 sets of 15

**Quadruped Position**
1. On hands and knees
2. Hold position, rock gently
3. Progress to lifting opposite arm/leg

**Push-Up Progression**
1. Knee push-ups
2. Full push-ups (when strong enough)

### Stability Exercises

**Ball on Wall Circles**
1. Hand on ball against wall
2. Make small circles
3. 10-15 each direction

**Perturbation Training**
1. Arm supported on ball or unstable surface
2. Resist gentle pushes
3. 30-60 seconds

### Sport-Specific Preparation

**Throwing progression:**
1. Easy catches
2. Light tosses
3. Gradual distance and force
4. Full throwing (when cleared)

**Racquet sports:**
1. Shadow swings
2. Light hitting
3. Gradual intensity

## Preventing Complications

### Stiffness (Most Common)

**Prevention:**
- Start motion early (when cleared)
- Perform exercises frequently
- Use heat before stretching
- Be consistent

**Management if stiff:**
- Increase stretching frequency
- Consider physical therapy
- Static progressive splinting (if severe)

### Instability

**Signs:**
- Feeling of elbow slipping
- Pain with certain movements
- Giving way

**Prevention:**
- Strengthen stabilizing muscles
- Avoid valgus stress
- Follow activity restrictions

### Heterotopic Ossification

Abnormal bone formation in soft tissues (more common with head injury).

**Prevention:**
- Early motion
- Avoid forced manipulation
- Follow medical guidance

## Sample Daily Program

### Week 2-3 (Protected Motion)
**4-6 times daily:**
1. Elbow flexion/extension: 15 reps
2. Forearm rotation: 15 reps
3. Wrist/finger exercises: 15 reps
4. Ice after: 15 min

### Week 4-6 (Progressive Mobility)
**3 times daily:**
1. Active-assisted ROM: 10 reps with holds
2. Stretches: 30 sec each
3. Begin light isometrics
4. Heat before, ice after

### Week 7-10 (Strengthening)
**2 times daily:**
1. ROM and stretches: 10 min
2. Isometric exercises: 10 reps each
3. Light resistance: 2x15
4. Grip exercises: 2-3 min

### Week 11+ (Functional)
**Once daily (+ gym sessions):**
1. Stretches: 5 min
2. Strengthening: 3-4 exercises, 2-3 sets
3. Closed chain exercises
4. Sport-specific as appropriate

## Return to Activity Criteria

**Before full return:**
- Full or near-full range of motion
- 80%+ strength compared to other side
- No pain with activity
- Good stability
- Doctor clearance

**Return to sports:**
- Gradual progression
- May need brace initially
- Full contact last

## Working with Healthcare Providers

### Physical/Occupational Therapist
Especially helpful for:
- Joint mobilization
- Custom splinting
- Supervised exercise progression
- Modalities (heat, ultrasound)

### Surgeon Follow-Up
- Usually at 2 weeks, 6 weeks, 3 months, 6 months
- X-rays to confirm joint position
- Clearance for activity progression

## Key Takeaways

1. **Early motion prevents stiffness** — start when cleared
2. **Frequent exercise is key** — multiple times daily initially
3. **Progress gradually** — don't rush strengthening
4. **Avoid valgus stress** — protects healing ligaments
5. **Heat before exercise** — improves mobility
6. **Strengthen stabilizers** — prevents chronic instability
7. **Be patient** — full recovery takes 3-6 months
8. **Seek help if not progressing** — therapy can make a big difference

Elbow dislocations can fully recover with proper rehabilitation. Follow your medical team's guidance, stay consistent with exercises, and progress systematically back to your normal activities.
