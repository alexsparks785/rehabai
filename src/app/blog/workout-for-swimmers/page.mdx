# Workout for Swimmers: Dryland Training for Speed and Power

What you do outside the pool matters as much as your time in it. Dryland training builds the strength, power, and resilience that makes you faster in the water and keeps your shoulders healthy for years of swimming.

## Why Swimmers Need Dryland Training

### More Power Per Stroke

Stronger muscles generate more force against the water. Studies show dryland training improves:
- Sprint speed by 2-5%
- Start and turn power
- Stroke rate maintenance when fatigued

### Injury Prevention

Swimming is repetitive—thousands of shoulder rotations weekly. Dryland strengthens:
- Rotator cuff muscles
- Scapular stabilizers
- Core stability
- Muscle imbalances from stroke patterns

### Better Endurance

Muscular endurance built on land transfers to the pool:
- Maintain stroke quality longer
- Strong finish in races
- Faster recovery between sets

## Key Areas for Swimmers

### Shoulders and Upper Back

The engine of swimming. Focus on:
- Rotator cuff strength
- Scapular stability
- Lat power
- Balanced development (pulling and pushing)

### Core

Connects upper and lower body for streamlined position:
- Rotation control
- Streamline stability
- Body position maintenance

### Hips and Legs

For kicks, starts, and turns:
- Hip flexor strength
- Ankle flexibility
- Explosive power

### Flexibility

Swimming requires extreme ranges:
- Shoulder mobility
- Ankle plantar flexion
- Thoracic extension

## Essential Exercises for Swimmers

### Shoulder Health and Strength

**External Rotation with Band**
1. Elbow at side, bent 90°
2. Rotate forearm outward
3. Slow and controlled
4. 3 sets × 15 each arm

*Why:* Balances the internal rotation dominance from swimming.

**Face Pulls**
1. High cable or band attachment
2. Pull toward face, elbows high
3. Squeeze shoulder blades
4. 3 sets × 15 reps

**Y-T-W Raises**
1. Lie face down on bench or floor
2. Y: Arms overhead, thumbs up, lift
3. T: Arms out to sides, lift
4. W: Elbows bent, squeeze and lift
5. 2 sets × 10 of each

**Scapular Push-Ups**
1. Plank position
2. Pinch shoulder blades together, then spread apart
3. Arms stay straight throughout
4. 2 sets × 15 reps

### Back and Lat Exercises

**Pull-Ups / Lat Pulldowns**
1. Shoulder-width or slightly wider grip
2. Pull chest to bar / bar to chest
3. Control the lowering phase
4. 3 sets × 8-12 reps

**Single-Arm Dumbbell Row**
1. Knee and hand on bench
2. Pull dumbbell to hip
3. Squeeze lat at top
4. 3 sets × 10 each arm

**Straight-Arm Pulldowns**
1. High cable attachment
2. Arms straight, pull down to thighs
3. Mimics catch phase of stroke
4. 3 sets × 12 reps

### Core Exercises

**Flutter Kicks**
1. Lie on back, hands under hips
2. Small, quick leg kicks
3. Keep lower back pressed down
4. 3 sets × 30 seconds

*Why:* Directly mimics kicking position and strengthens hip flexors.

**Streamline Holds**
1. Lie on stomach, arms extended overhead
2. Hands stacked, biceps by ears
3. Lift arms and legs slightly
4. Hold 20-30 seconds × 3 sets

**Plank Variations**
1. Standard plank: 30-45 seconds
2. Side plank: 20-30 seconds each side
3. Plank with arm reach: 8 each side

**Dead Bugs**
1. On back, arms up, legs 90°
2. Lower opposite arm and leg
3. Maintain neutral spine
4. 3 sets × 10 each side

**Medicine Ball Rotations**
1. Seated, feet off ground
2. Rotate ball side to side
3. Control the movement
4. 2 sets × 20 total

### Lower Body Exercises

**Jump Squats**
1. Squat down, explode up
2. Land softly with bent knees
3. Builds start and turn power
4. 3 sets × 8 reps

**Single-Leg Romanian Deadlift**
1. Balance on one leg
2. Hinge at hip, reach toward ground
3. Build hip stability
4. 3 sets × 8 each side

**Calf Raises**
1. Single or double leg
2. Full range of motion
3. Important for push-offs
4. 3 sets × 15 reps

**Ankle Mobility Work**
1. Kneeling ankle stretch
2. Point and flex with band
3. Essential for kick efficiency

### Power Exercises

**Medicine Ball Slams**
1. Lift ball overhead
2. Slam into ground with force
3. Full body power
4. 3 sets × 8 reps

**Box Jumps**
1. Jump onto box, step down
2. Focus on explosive hip extension
3. 3 sets × 6 reps

**Explosive Push-Ups**
1. Push up explosively (hands leave ground)
2. Land softly, repeat
3. 3 sets × 6-8 reps

## Sample Dryland Workouts

### Pre-Swim Activation (10 minutes)

Do before practice to activate muscles and prevent injury:

**Band Work (3 minutes)**
- External rotations: 15 each arm
- Pull-aparts: 15 reps
- Face pulls: 15 reps

**Dynamic Mobility (4 minutes)**
- Arm circles: 15 each direction
- Leg swings: 10 each leg, each direction
- Hip circles: 10 each direction
- Ankle circles: 10 each direction

**Core Activation (3 minutes)**
- Plank: 30 seconds
- Dead bugs: 8 each side
- Streamline hold: 20 seconds

### Full Dryland Session (45 minutes)

Do 2-3 times per week.

**Warm-Up (5 minutes)**
- Light cardio (jumping jacks, jogging)
- Dynamic stretches

**Shoulder Health Circuit (8 minutes)**
- External rotations: 2 × 15 each
- Y-T-W raises: 2 × 10 of each
- Face pulls: 2 × 15
- Scapular push-ups: 2 × 12

**Strength Circuit – 2 rounds (18 minutes)**
1. Pull-ups or lat pulldowns: 10 reps
2. Push-ups: 12 reps
3. Single-arm rows: 10 each side
4. Goblet squats: 12 reps
5. Single-leg RDL: 8 each side
6. Straight-arm pulldowns: 12 reps

Rest 30 seconds between exercises, 1 minute between rounds.

**Core Circuit (8 minutes)**
1. Flutter kicks: 30 seconds
2. Plank: 45 seconds
3. Medicine ball rotations: 20 reps
4. Streamline holds: 20 seconds
5. Side plank: 20 seconds each side

2 rounds, minimal rest.

**Cool-Down and Stretch (6 minutes)**
- Doorway chest stretch: 30 seconds each position
- Lat stretch: 30 seconds each side
- Hip flexor stretch: 30 seconds each side
- Shoulder stretches: 30 seconds each

### Power Session (30 minutes)

Once per week during competition phase:

**Warm-Up (5 minutes)**
- Dynamic movement
- Activation exercises

**Power Circuit – 3 rounds (20 minutes)**
1. Box jumps: 6 reps
2. Medicine ball slams: 8 reps
3. Explosive push-ups: 6 reps
4. Jump squats: 8 reps
5. Medicine ball rotational throws: 6 each side

Rest 45 seconds between exercises, 2 minutes between rounds.

**Cool-Down (5 minutes)**
- Light stretching

## Flexibility Routine for Swimmers

Flexibility directly impacts stroke efficiency. Do 3-4 times weekly.

### Shoulder Mobility

**Doorway Stretch**
- Low, middle, and high positions
- 30 seconds each

**Sleeper Stretch**
- Lie on side, pin shoulder, rotate arm down
- 30 seconds each side

**Wall Slides**
- Back against wall, slide arms up and down
- 15 reps

### Thoracic Spine

**Foam Roller Extensions**
- Roller at upper back
- Extend over roller
- 10 extensions

**Thread the Needle**
- On hands and knees, reach arm under body
- 8 each side

### Ankle Mobility

**Kneeling Ankle Stretch**
- Knee over toes, push forward
- 30 seconds each side

**Band Plantar Flexion**
- Point toes against band resistance
- 15 each side

### Hip Mobility

**90/90 Stretch**
- Front and back leg at 90°
- 30 seconds each position

**Hip Flexor Stretch**
- Half-kneeling, tuck tailbone
- 30 seconds each side

## Preventing Swimmer's Shoulder

Shoulder injuries are the most common swimming injury. Prevent them by:

### Strengthening External Rotators
- Band external rotations daily
- Y-T-W raises 3× per week
- Face pulls in every dryland session

### Balancing Push and Pull
- For every pushing exercise, do 2 pulling exercises
- Swimmers already pull thousands of times per practice

### Maintaining Shoulder Mobility
- Daily stretching
- Don't skip warm-up
- Address tightness before it becomes pain

### Managing Volume
- Gradual increases in yardage
- Recovery between hard sessions
- Listen to warning signs

## Training by Season

### Off-Season
- Build strength foundation
- Higher volume dryland (3-4 sessions/week)
- Focus on correcting imbalances

### Pre-Season
- Add power work
- Maintain strength
- 2-3 sessions/week

### Competition Season
- Maintenance only
- 1-2 short sessions/week
- Focus on activation and mobility

## The Bottom Line

Dryland training makes you faster and keeps you healthy. Focus on shoulder health, core stability, and power development. Don't neglect flexibility—it's as important as strength.

Start with 2 sessions per week, always including shoulder prehab work. Build consistency before intensity. Your time in the water will improve because of your work on land.

Strong swimmers are built on deck as much as in the pool.
