# How to Fix Elbow Pain: Exercises for Common Elbow Problems

Elbow pain can make everyday tasks—from typing to opening jars to shaking hands—surprisingly difficult. The good news: most elbow pain responds well to targeted exercises. The key is identifying which type you have and addressing it appropriately.

## Types of Elbow Pain

### Lateral Epicondylitis (Tennis Elbow)
**Location:** Outside of elbow
**Cause:** Overuse of wrist extensor muscles
**Common culprits:** Typing, mouse use, gripping, backhand tennis, using tools
**Symptoms:** Pain on outer elbow, weak grip, pain when lifting with palm down

### Medial Epicondylitis (Golfer's Elbow)
**Location:** Inside of elbow
**Cause:** Overuse of wrist flexor muscles
**Common culprits:** Gripping, throwing, golf swing, weight training
**Symptoms:** Pain on inner elbow, pain when flexing wrist, weak grip

### Cubital Tunnel Syndrome
**Location:** Inside of elbow (ulnar nerve)
**Cause:** Compression or stretching of ulnar nerve
**Common culprits:** Leaning on elbow, keeping elbow bent (phone, sleeping)
**Symptoms:** Numbness in ring/pinky fingers, elbow pain, weak grip

### Triceps Tendinitis
**Location:** Back of elbow
**Cause:** Overuse of triceps
**Common culprits:** Pushing exercises, throwing
**Symptoms:** Pain at back of elbow, especially when straightening arm against resistance

### Bursitis
**Location:** Tip of elbow (olecranon)
**Cause:** Pressure, trauma, or inflammation
**Symptoms:** Swelling at elbow tip, pain when leaning on elbow

## Identifying Your Elbow Pain

### Quick Tests

**For Tennis Elbow:**
1. Extend your arm, palm down
2. Try to lift your hand against resistance
3. Pain on outer elbow = likely tennis elbow

**For Golfer's Elbow:**
1. Extend your arm, palm up
2. Flex your wrist against resistance
3. Pain on inner elbow = likely golfer's elbow

**For Cubital Tunnel:**
1. Tap the inside of your elbow (funny bone area)
2. Tingling into pinky/ring finger = possible cubital tunnel
3. Symptoms worse with elbow bent for long periods

## Exercises for Tennis Elbow (Outer Elbow Pain)

### 1. Eccentric Wrist Extension
**Why:** The gold standard for tennis elbow treatment. Eccentric loading promotes tendon healing.

**How to do it:**
1. Rest forearm on table, wrist hanging off edge, palm down
2. Hold a light weight (1-2 lbs to start)
3. Use your other hand to lift the weight up (wrist extension)
4. Slowly lower the weight over 3-5 seconds (the injured arm does this alone)
5. Use your good hand to lift again
6. Do 3 sets of 15, twice daily

**Progression:** Gradually increase weight as tolerated over weeks.

### 2. Wrist Extensor Stretch
**How to do it:**
1. Extend your arm in front of you, palm down
2. Use your other hand to gently push your fingers down
3. Keep elbow straight
4. Hold 30-45 seconds
5. Do 3-4 times daily

### 3. Finger Extension with Rubber Band
**How to do it:**
1. Place a rubber band around all fingers and thumb
2. Spread fingers apart against the resistance
3. Hold briefly, return
4. Do 15-20 reps

### 4. Grip Strengthening (Once Pain Improves)
**How to do it:**
1. Squeeze a soft ball or therapy putty
2. Hold 5 seconds
3. Do 15-20 reps
4. Progress to firmer resistance

## Exercises for Golfer's Elbow (Inner Elbow Pain)

### 5. Eccentric Wrist Flexion
**How to do it:**
1. Rest forearm on table, wrist hanging off edge, palm UP
2. Hold a light weight
3. Use other hand to lift wrist up (flexion)
4. Slowly lower over 3-5 seconds with the injured arm
5. Do 3 sets of 15, twice daily

### 6. Wrist Flexor Stretch
**How to do it:**
1. Extend arm in front, palm up
2. Use other hand to pull fingers down (toward floor)
3. Keep elbow straight
4. Hold 30-45 seconds
5. Do 3-4 times daily

### 7. Forearm Pronation/Supination
**How to do it:**
1. Hold a hammer or weighted object at the end of the handle
2. Elbow bent at 90 degrees at your side
3. Rotate forearm so palm faces up, then down
4. Do 15-20 reps
5. Increase lever length for more challenge

## Exercises for Cubital Tunnel Syndrome

### 8. Ulnar Nerve Glide
**How to do it:**
1. Make an "OK" sign with your affected hand
2. Place the OK circle over your eye
3. Extend elbow out to the side (like you're looking through a telescope)
4. Slowly bend and straighten elbow
5. Movement should be smooth and pain-free
6. Do 10-15 reps, 3 times daily

### 9. Elbow Straightening Awareness
**Throughout the day:**
- Avoid keeping elbow bent for long periods
- Take breaks from phone holding
- Don't lean on your elbow
- Keep elbow straighter when sleeping (consider a towel wrap)

### 10. Median Nerve Glide
**How to do it:**
1. Arm out to side, palm up
2. Extend wrist (fingers up) while tilting head toward arm
3. Flex wrist (fingers down) while tilting head away
4. Smooth back-and-forth motion
5. Do 10-15 reps

## General Elbow Exercises

### 11. Wrist Circles
**How to do it:**
1. Make a fist
2. Circle your wrist slowly
3. 10 circles each direction
4. Do several times daily

### 12. Forearm Strengthening (Hammer Rotations)
**How to do it:**
1. Hold a hammer at the end of the handle
2. Keep elbow at your side, bent 90 degrees
3. Rotate handle left and right (like turning a key)
4. Control the movement
5. Do 15-20 reps each direction

### 13. Towel Wringing
**How to do it:**
1. Hold a rolled towel with both hands
2. Wring it like you're squeezing out water
3. Reverse direction
4. Do 10-15 wrings each direction

### 14. Ball Squeeze (Varied Positions)
**How to do it:**
1. Squeeze a stress ball with elbow at different angles
2. Straight, 45 degrees, 90 degrees
3. Hold each squeeze 5 seconds
4. 10 reps at each position

## Treatment Strategies

### Reduce Aggravating Activities
Identify what triggers your pain:
- Modify grip technique
- Use larger handles on tools
- Take frequent breaks
- Alternate hands when possible

### Bracing
- **Tennis elbow strap:** Worn below the elbow, takes stress off the tendon
- **Wrist brace:** May help if wrist motion aggravates symptoms
- **Night splint for cubital tunnel:** Prevents elbow bending during sleep

### Ice vs. Heat
- **Acute pain (first few weeks):** Ice 15-20 minutes after activities
- **Chronic pain:** Heat before activity to warm up, ice after
- **Both can help:** See what works for you

### Massage
- Self-massage the forearm muscles (not the painful tendon itself)
- Use a massage ball or your fingers
- Work the muscle belly, not the attachment

## Your Daily Routine

### Morning (3 minutes)
- Wrist circles: 10 each direction
- Appropriate stretch for your condition: 30 seconds
- Nerve glide if cubital tunnel: 10 reps

### Before Aggravating Activities
- Warm-up stretches
- Light forearm massage
- Consider bracing

### After Activities
- Ice if inflamed
- Stretching
- Eccentric exercises (main treatment)

### Evening (5-7 minutes)
- Eccentric exercise routine: 3x15
- Full stretching
- Self-massage if helpful

## Timeline for Recovery

**Tennis/Golfer's Elbow:**
- Mild cases: 4-6 weeks
- Moderate cases: 6-12 weeks
- Chronic cases: 3-6 months

**Cubital Tunnel:**
- If addressed early: 4-8 weeks
- If chronic: May take longer or need medical intervention

**Key point:** Tendon injuries heal slowly. Be patient and consistent.

## When to See a Doctor

Seek evaluation if:
- Pain is severe or worsening despite self-care
- Significant weakness in grip or arm
- Symptoms persist beyond 6-8 weeks of consistent treatment
- Numbness or tingling is constant
- Visible swelling or deformity
- Pain following a specific injury/trauma
- Symptoms affect both arms

### Medical Treatments
- Physical therapy
- Corticosteroid injection (temporary relief, limited use)
- PRP injection (some evidence for tennis elbow)
- Shockwave therapy
- Surgery (last resort for chronic cases)

## Preventing Elbow Pain

### Workstation Ergonomics
- Keyboard and mouse at proper height
- Forearms supported
- Neutral wrist position
- Regular breaks

### Sports/Activity
- Proper technique (tennis, golf, lifting)
- Appropriate equipment (grip size, racquet weight)
- Gradual progression in training
- Adequate rest between sessions

### General
- Maintain forearm strength
- Regular stretching
- Address problems early (don't push through)
- Vary repetitive tasks

## The Bottom Line

Elbow pain usually comes from overworked tendons that haven't had time to recover. The fix is counterintuitive: instead of complete rest, you need controlled loading through eccentric exercises to stimulate healing.

Identify whether you have outer elbow (tennis elbow), inner elbow (golfer's elbow), or nerve-related (cubital tunnel) pain. Then apply the appropriate exercises consistently.

Eccentric exercises are your main treatment—they work, but they take time. Expect 6-12 weeks for significant improvement. Stretching, activity modification, and bracing support the process.

Be patient with tendons. They heal slowly. But with consistent work, most elbow pain resolves without medical intervention.
