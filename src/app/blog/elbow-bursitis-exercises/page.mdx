# Elbow Bursitis Exercises: Manage Olecranon Swelling and Pain

Elbow bursitis—swelling of the fluid-filled sac at the tip of the elbow—can be uncomfortable and limiting. While the primary treatment focuses on reducing inflammation, the right exercises maintain mobility and prevent stiffness.

## Understanding Elbow Bursitis

The olecranon bursa is a small, fluid-filled sac that cushions the bony tip of the elbow (olecranon). When irritated, it fills with excess fluid and swells.

**Also called:**
- Olecranon bursitis
- Student's elbow
- Miner's elbow
- Draftsman's elbow

**Common causes:**
- Prolonged leaning on elbow
- Direct trauma (fall, blow)
- Repetitive motion
- Infection (septic bursitis)
- Gout or rheumatoid arthritis

**Symptoms:**
- Swelling at elbow tip (can be golf ball-sized)
- Pain with direct pressure
- Limited motion (due to swelling)
- Warmth and redness (especially if infected)

## Important: Rule Out Infection

**Septic bursitis** (infected bursa) requires medical treatment, not just exercises.

**Seek medical attention if:**
- Significant warmth or redness
- Fever or chills
- Rapidly worsening swelling
- Breaking skin over bursa
- Feeling generally unwell

Infected bursitis typically needs antibiotics and sometimes drainage.

## Acute Phase Management

### RICE Protocol
- **Rest:** Avoid pressure on elbow
- **Ice:** 15-20 minutes, several times daily
- **Compression:** Elastic wrap (gentle, don't restrict circulation)
- **Elevation:** Keep elbow above heart when possible

### Avoid Aggravating Factors
- Don't lean on elbows
- Avoid direct pressure
- Use elbow pads if needed
- Modify work/study positions

### Gentle Range of Motion

Even during acute phase, maintain mobility.

**Elbow Bends**
1. Slowly bend and straighten elbow
2. Move through comfortable range
3. 10-15 repetitions
4. Several times daily

**Forearm Rotation**
1. Elbow at side, bent 90 degrees
2. Rotate palm up, then palm down
3. 10-15 repetitions

## Range of Motion Exercises

### Full Elbow Flexion/Extension

**How to do it:**
1. Let arm hang at side
2. Bend elbow fully (hand toward shoulder)
3. Straighten elbow fully
4. 15-20 repetitions
5. Move smoothly through full range

### Assisted Elbow Extension

If stiffness limits straightening:
1. Support arm on table
2. Use other hand to gently press arm straight
3. Hold 15-30 seconds
4. Repeat 5-10 times

### Assisted Elbow Flexion

If bending is limited:
1. Use other hand to assist bending
2. Gently increase range
3. Hold 15-30 seconds
4. Repeat 5-10 times

### Forearm Pronation/Supination

**How to do it:**
1. Elbow at side, bent 90 degrees
2. Rotate palm fully up (supination)
3. Rotate palm fully down (pronation)
4. 15-20 repetitions

## Stretching Exercises

### Wrist Flexor Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Extend arm, palm up
2. Use other hand to pull fingers back
3. Feel stretch in forearm and inner elbow
4. Hold 30 seconds
5. Repeat 3 times

### Wrist Extensor Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Extend arm, palm down
2. Use other hand to push wrist down
3. Feel stretch in outer forearm
4. Hold 30 seconds
5. Repeat 3 times

### Triceps Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Raise arm overhead
2. Bend elbow, hand reaches toward opposite shoulder
3. Use other hand to gently press elbow back
4. Hold 30 seconds
5. Repeat each side

### Biceps Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Stand facing wall, arm extended behind you
2. Palm against wall, fingers pointing down
3. Rotate body away from arm
4. Feel stretch in biceps and front of shoulder
5. Hold 30 seconds each side

## Strengthening Exercises

Once acute inflammation subsides, strengthen surrounding muscles.

### Isometric Exercises

**Isometric Elbow Flexion**
1. Elbow bent 90 degrees
2. Place other hand on wrist
3. Try to bend elbow while resisting
4. Hold 5-10 seconds
5. 10 repetitions

**Isometric Elbow Extension**
1. Same position
2. Place other hand under wrist
3. Try to straighten while resisting
4. Hold 5-10 seconds
5. 10 repetitions

### Biceps Curls

**How to do it:**
1. Hold light weight (2-5 lbs)
2. Start with arm straight
3. Curl weight toward shoulder
4. Lower slowly
5. 2-3 sets of 15

### Triceps Extensions

**How to do it:**
1. Hold weight overhead with both hands
2. Lower behind head by bending elbows
3. Extend back to start
4. 2-3 sets of 15

**Alternative - Triceps Kickback:**
1. Lean forward, elbow at side
2. Extend forearm backward
3. 2-3 sets of 15 each arm

### Forearm Strengthening

**Wrist Curls**
1. Forearm on table, palm up
2. Curl wrist up with light weight
3. 2-3 sets of 15

**Reverse Wrist Curls**
1. Forearm on table, palm down
2. Extend wrist up
3. 2-3 sets of 15

### Grip Strengthening

**Ball Squeeze**
1. Squeeze stress ball or tennis ball
2. Hold 5 seconds
3. 15-20 repetitions

## Functional Exercises

### Push-Up Progression

Only when bursa inflammation resolved:

**Level 1:** Wall push-ups
**Level 2:** Counter push-ups
**Level 3:** Knee push-ups
**Level 4:** Full push-ups

**Use padding** under elbows if needed.

### Elbow Plank (Modified)

**How to do it:**
1. Start on forearms and knees
2. Maintain straight line from head to knees
3. Hold 15-30 seconds
4. Progress to full plank

**Note:** Use padded surface under elbows.

## Prevention Strategies

### Avoid Direct Pressure
- Don't lean on elbows at desk
- Use armrests with padding
- Reposition frequently

### Use Elbow Pads
- Wear during activities that risk impact
- Use for prolonged positioning
- Consider while sleeping if needed

### Ergonomic Modifications
- Adjust desk and chair height
- Use forearms for support, not elbows
- Take frequent breaks

### Padding and Protection
- Foam pads on hard surfaces
- Gel pads for prolonged work
- Protective gear for sports

## Sample Daily Program

### Acute Phase (Days 1-7)
- Ice: 15-20 min, 3-4 times daily
- Gentle ROM: 10 reps, 3 times daily
- Avoid direct pressure
- Compression wrap if tolerated

### Subacute Phase (Weeks 2-4)
**Morning:**
1. ROM exercises: 15 reps each
2. Stretches: 30 sec each
3. Isometrics: 10 reps each

**Evening:**
1. ROM exercises: 15 reps
2. Stretches: 30 sec each
3. Ice if swollen

### Recovery Phase (Week 4+)
**3-4 times per week:**
1. Stretches: 5 minutes
2. Biceps curls: 2x15
3. Triceps extensions: 2x15
4. Forearm exercises: 2x15 each
5. Grip work: 2-3 minutes

## When to Seek Medical Help

**Consult a doctor if:**
- Swelling doesn't improve after 2 weeks
- Signs of infection (warmth, redness, fever)
- Recurrent episodes
- Significant pain
- Unable to move elbow

**Treatment options:**
- Aspiration (draining fluid)
- Corticosteroid injection
- Antibiotics (if infected)
- Surgery (for chronic cases)

## Chronic/Recurrent Bursitis

If bursitis keeps coming back:
- Identify and eliminate causes
- Use elbow pads consistently
- Strengthen surrounding muscles
- Consider medical evaluation
- Surgery may be needed for persistent cases

## Key Takeaways

1. **Avoid direct pressure** — the most important prevention
2. **Ice regularly** — reduces inflammation
3. **Maintain ROM** — prevent stiffness
4. **Strengthen when inflammation subsides** — supports the joint
5. **Use elbow pads** — protect during activities
6. **Watch for infection signs** — needs medical treatment
7. **Modify ergonomics** — address root causes
8. **Be patient** — may take weeks to fully resolve

Elbow bursitis usually responds well to conservative management. Protect the elbow, reduce inflammation, maintain mobility, and address the underlying cause to prevent recurrence.
