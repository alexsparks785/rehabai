# Pickleball Exercises: Stay on the Court and Play Your Best

Pickleball is America's fastest-growing sport—and with that growth comes a surge in pickleball-related injuries. The quick lateral movements, overhead shots, and repetitive arm motions create specific demands that targeted training can address.

**Key insight:** Most pickleball injuries happen to players over 50 who are new to racket sports. Building baseline strength and mobility before playing reduces injury risk dramatically.

---

## Physical Demands of Pickleball

### What the Sport Requires

- **Lateral quickness:** Side-to-side movement at the net
- **Shoulder stability:** Overhead shots and serves
- **Wrist/forearm strength:** Paddle control and dinking
- **Hip mobility:** Low ready position, quick pivots
- **Balance and reaction time:** Fast exchanges at the kitchen
- **Cardiovascular endurance:** Extended rallies

### Common Pickleball Injuries

| Injury | Cause | Prevention Focus |
|--------|-------|------------------|
| Shoulder strain | Overhead shots, poor mechanics | Rotator cuff strength, mobility |
| Pickleball elbow | Repetitive wrist/forearm stress | Forearm strengthening, technique |
| Knee pain | Lateral movement, pivoting | Quad/hip strength, proper footwork |
| Ankle sprains | Quick direction changes | Balance work, ankle stability |
| Lower back pain | Rotation, bent-over position | Core stability, hip mobility |
| Achilles tendinitis | Explosive movement, tight calves | Calf strengthening and stretching |

## Pre-Play Warm-Up (8-10 Minutes)

Do this before every session to reduce injury risk.

### Dynamic Movement

**Arm Circles**
- 10 forward, 10 backward
- Progress from small to large

**Leg Swings**
- 10 forward/back each leg
- 10 side-to-side each leg

**Walking Lunges with Rotation**
- 10 total steps
- Rotate toward front leg

**Lateral Shuffles**
- 20 feet each direction
- Stay low, don't cross feet

**High Knees**
- 20 steps in place
- Pump arms

### Shoulder Activation

**Band Pull-Aparts**
- 15 reps
- Squeeze shoulder blades

**External Rotation with Band**
- 10 each arm
- Elbow at side, rotate out

### Sport-Specific

**Shadow Swings**
- 10 forehand drives
- 10 backhand drives
- 10 overhead swings

**Ready Position Holds**
- 3 x 10 seconds in athletic stance
- Weight on balls of feet

## Shoulder Health Exercises

Critical for overhead shots and long-term play.

### External Rotation (Side-Lying)

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on side, upper arm along body
2. Elbow bent 90°, forearm across belly
3. Rotate forearm toward ceiling
4. Lower with control
5. 3 sets of 12-15 each side

### Y-T-W Raises

**How to do it:**
1. Lie face down on bench or floor
2. Y: Raise arms at 45° angle, thumbs up
3. T: Raise arms straight out to sides
4. W: Elbows bent, rotate and lift like goal posts
5. 10 reps each position

### Face Pulls

**How to do it:**
1. Band or cable at face height
2. Pull toward face, separating hands
3. End with hands by ears, elbows high
4. 3 sets of 15

### Shoulder CARs (Controlled Articular Rotations)

**How to do it:**
1. Stand tall, make fist
2. Slowly raise arm forward, then overhead
3. Rotate out and back, then down
4. Maximum range, controlled speed
5. 5 circles each direction, each arm

### Sleeper Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on side, bottom arm at 90° in front
2. Use top hand to press forearm toward floor
3. Gentle pressure—don't force
4. Hold 30 seconds each side

## Lateral Movement Training

### Lateral Band Walks

**How to do it:**
1. Band above knees
2. Athletic stance, slight squat
3. Step sideways, maintaining tension
4. 15-20 steps each direction

### Lateral Lunges

**How to do it:**
1. Step wide to one side
2. Bend stepping knee, keep other leg straight
3. Push back to center
4. 10-12 each side

### Carioca (Grapevine)

**How to do it:**
1. Move laterally, alternating feet in front and behind
2. Rotate hips through full range
3. 20 feet each direction

### Lateral Bounds

**How to do it:**
1. Stand on one leg
2. Bound sideways to other leg
3. Stick landing for 2 seconds
4. 8-10 each direction

### Defensive Slides

**How to do it:**
1. Athletic stance, low position
2. Slide 5 steps left, 5 steps right
3. Stay low throughout
4. 30 seconds on, 30 off, 3-4 sets

## Core Stability for Pickleball

### Pallof Press

**Why it matters:** Builds rotational stability for controlled shots.

**How to do it:**
1. Stand perpendicular to band/cable
2. Hold at chest, press arms out
3. Resist rotation
4. 3 sets of 10 each side

### Bird Dog

**How to do it:**
1. On all fours
2. Extend opposite arm and leg
3. Keep back flat, hips level
4. 3 sets of 10 each side

### Dead Bug

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back, arms up, knees at 90°
2. Press lower back into floor
3. Lower opposite arm and leg
4. 3 sets of 10 each side

### Side Plank

**How to do it:**
1. Forearm on ground, feet stacked
2. Lift hips, maintain straight line
3. Hold 30 seconds each side
4. 3 sets

### Medicine Ball Rotational Throws

**How to do it:**
1. Stand perpendicular to wall
2. Rotate and throw ball into wall
3. Catch and repeat
4. 10 throws each side

## Forearm and Wrist Strength

Prevents "pickleball elbow" and improves paddle control.

### Wrist Curls

**How to do it:**
1. Forearm on thigh, palm up, weight in hand
2. Curl wrist up, lower slowly
3. 15-20 reps each arm

### Reverse Wrist Curls

**How to do it:**
1. Forearm on thigh, palm down
2. Extend wrist up against resistance
3. 15-20 reps each arm

### Radial/Ulnar Deviation

**How to do it:**
1. Hold weight at end (like hammer)
2. Move wrist side to side
3. 15 each direction, each arm

### Grip Strengthening

**How to do it:**
1. Squeeze tennis ball or grip trainer
2. Hold 5 seconds, release
3. 15-20 reps each hand

### Forearm Pronation/Supination

**How to do it:**
1. Hold weight, elbow at side
2. Rotate forearm palm up, then palm down
3. 15 each direction

---

## Lower Body Strength

### Goblet Squats

**How to do it:**
1. Hold weight at chest
2. Squat to comfortable depth
3. Drive through whole foot
4. 3 sets of 12-15

### Single-Leg Romanian Deadlift

**How to do it:**
1. Stand on one leg
2. Hinge at hip, reach toward ground
3. Keep back flat
4. 3 sets of 10 each leg

### Step-Ups

**How to do it:**
1. Step onto box or bench
2. Drive through top leg
3. Control descent
4. 3 sets of 10 each leg

### Calf Raises

**How to do it:**
1. Rise onto balls of feet
2. Lower slowly below starting height
3. 3 sets of 15-20

**Progression:** Single-leg calf raises

---

## Balance Exercises

### Single-Leg Balance

**How to do it:**
1. Stand on one leg
2. Hold 30-60 seconds
3. Progress to eyes closed
4. 3 sets each leg

### Single-Leg Balance with Paddle Movements

**How to do it:**
1. Stand on one leg holding paddle
2. Make various stroke motions
3. Maintain balance throughout
4. 30 seconds each leg

### Balance Board or BOSU

**How to do it:**
1. Stand on unstable surface
2. Maintain balance
3. Progress to eyes closed or movement

## Post-Play Recovery

### Key Stretches

**Shoulder Cross-Body Stretch**
- Pull arm across body
- Hold 30 seconds each side

**Wrist Flexor Stretch**
- Arm extended, palm up, pull fingers back
- Hold 30 seconds each

**Wrist Extensor Stretch**
- Arm extended, palm down, pull hand down
- Hold 30 seconds each

**Hip Flexor Stretch**
- Half-kneeling position
- Hold 30-45 seconds each

**Calf Stretch**
- Wall stretch, straight and bent knee versions
- Hold 30 seconds each

### Foam Rolling

- Forearms: 60 seconds each
- Quads: 60 seconds
- Calves: 60 seconds each
- Upper back: 60 seconds

## Sample Weekly Program

### For Recreational Players (2-3x strength/week)

**Day 1 - Upper Body Focus:**
- Shoulder warm-up: 5 min
- Y-T-W raises: 2x10 each
- Face pulls: 3x15
- Wrist curls/reverse curls: 2x15 each
- Pallof press: 3x10 each side
- Forearm stretches

**Day 2 - Lower Body Focus:**
- Dynamic warm-up: 5 min
- Goblet squats: 3x12
- Lateral lunges: 3x10 each
- Single-leg RDL: 3x10 each
- Lateral band walks: 3x15 each way
- Calf raises: 3x15
- Hip stretches

**Day 3 - Movement and Balance:**
- Agility ladder or lateral shuffles: 10 min
- Balance work: 10 min
- Core circuit: 10 min
- Full body stretch: 10 min

### Pre-Game Day

Light movement only—no heavy strength work within 24 hours of play.

## Injury Prevention Tips

### Playing Smart

- **Warm up every time:** Non-negotiable, even for "just hitting"
- **Stay hydrated:** Cramps lead to injuries
- **Know your limits:** Fatigue causes most injuries
- **Proper footwear:** Court shoes with lateral support
- **Rest days matter:** Don't play through pain

### Warning Signs

Stop and assess if you experience:
- Sharp shoulder pain during serves/overheads
- Elbow pain that persists after play
- Knee pain when pivoting
- Ankle instability or repeated rolling

### When to See a Professional

- Pain lasting more than 2 weeks
- Swelling that doesn't resolve
- Decreased range of motion
- Pain that changes your movement pattern

---

## The Bottom Line

Pickleball is accessible and fun, but the quick movements and repetitive strokes take a toll—especially if you're new to racket sports or returning to athletics after time off. A little prevention goes a long way.

**Priority areas:**
1. Shoulder mobility and rotator cuff strength
2. Lateral movement training
3. Forearm/wrist strengthening
4. Core stability
5. Balance work

Train off the court so you can keep playing on it.
