# Hip Mobility Exercises: Unlock Tight Hips and Move Better

Tight hips are an epidemic. Hours of sitting, lack of varied movement, and underuse of our hip's full range create stiffness that affects everything from your squat depth to your lower back comfort.

**Key insight:** Your hips are designed to move in every direction—flexion, extension, rotation, abduction, adduction. Modern life uses maybe 10% of that range. Reclaiming your hip mobility transforms how you move and feel.

---

## Understanding Hip Mobility

### What Your Hips Should Do

| Movement | Full Range | Common Limitation |
|----------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Flexion** (knee to chest) | 120°+ | Tight hip flexors, poor control |
| **Extension** (leg behind you) | 20-30° | Sitting shortens hip flexors |
| **External rotation** | 45°+ | Often limited from sitting |
| **Internal rotation** | 35°+ | Usually the tightest direction |
| **Abduction** (leg out to side) | 45°+ | Tight adductors |
| **Adduction** (leg across body) | 25°+ | Tight hip abductors |

### Why Hip Mobility Matters

- **Deep squats:** Need hip flexion and external rotation
- **Running:** Needs hip extension
- **Sitting comfortably:** Needs external rotation
- **Low back health:** Tight hips transfer stress to spine
- **Athletic performance:** Power comes from hip mobility + strength

### Mobility vs. Flexibility

**Flexibility** = How far joint can passively move (stretching)
**Mobility** = How far joint can actively move under control

You need both. Flexibility without control creates instability. This guide builds both.

## Hip Assessment

### 90/90 Test

Sit on floor with one leg in front (knee and hip both at 90°), one leg to side (knee and hip both at 90°).

**Results:**
- Can you sit upright easily? → Good baseline
- Leaning back or falling over? → Limited rotation
- Pain? → May need evaluation

### Deep Squat Test

Squat as low as possible with feet shoulder-width, toes slightly out.

**Results:**
- Heels stay down, torso upright → Good hip mobility
- Heels raise → Ankle mobility limitation
- Excessive forward lean → Hip flexion limitation
- Knees cave → Hip rotation limitation

### Thomas Test (Hip Flexor Tightness)

1. Lie on back at edge of table/bed
2. Pull one knee to chest
3. Let other leg hang

**Results:**
- Thigh rests at or below table level → Normal
- Thigh stays above table → Tight hip flexors
- Knee straightens → Tight rectus femoris

## Hip Flexion Exercises

### Knee to Chest Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back
2. Pull one knee toward chest
3. Keep other leg flat (don't let it rise)
4. Hold 30-60 seconds
5. Repeat other side

### Deep Squat Hold

**How to do it:**
1. Squat as deep as comfortable
2. Feet flat (elevate heels if needed initially)
3. Push elbows against inner knees
4. Keep chest up
5. Hold 30-60 seconds, accumulate 2-3 minutes daily

### Frog Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Start on hands and knees
2. Spread knees wide, feet turned out
3. Shift hips back toward heels
4. Keep spine neutral
5. Hold 30-60 seconds

## Hip Extension Exercises

### Half-Kneeling Hip Flexor Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Kneel on one knee, other foot forward
2. Tuck pelvis under (posterior pelvic tilt)
3. Squeeze glute of back leg
4. Shift weight forward slightly
5. Feel stretch in front of back hip
6. Hold 30-60 seconds each side

**Key:** The pelvic tuck is essential. Without it, you'll just arch your back.

### Couch Stretch

Intense hip flexor and quad stretch.

**How to do it:**
1. Kneel facing away from wall/couch
2. Place back foot against wall, knee on ground
3. Front foot flat, knee over ankle
4. Squeeze glute, tuck pelvis
5. Work toward upright torso
6. Hold 1-2 minutes each side

### Prone Hip Extension

Active mobility.

**How to do it:**
1. Lie face down
2. Squeeze one glute
3. Lift that leg straight back
4. Don't arch lower back
5. Hold 5 seconds
6. Perform 10-15 repetitions each leg

## Hip Rotation Exercises

### 90/90 Position

The foundation position for hip rotation work.

**How to do it:**
1. Sit with front leg at 90/90 (hip and knee both bent 90°)
2. Back leg also at 90/90, out to side
3. Front shin should be roughly parallel to your body
4. Work on sitting tall, not leaning back
5. Hold 30-60 seconds, then switch sides

### 90/90 Switches

**How to do it:**
1. Start in 90/90 position
2. Without using hands, rotate both legs to opposite side
3. Move slowly and with control
4. Continue switching back and forth
5. Perform 10-15 switches

**Progression:** Keep feet off ground while switching.

### Supine Figure 4 Stretch

External rotation stretch.

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back, knees bent
2. Place one ankle on opposite knee
3. Pull bottom leg toward chest
4. Feel stretch in outside of top hip
5. Hold 30-60 seconds each side

### Internal Rotation Stretch

Often the most limited direction.

**How to do it:**
1. Sit with legs in front, knees bent
2. Keep feet wide, let knees fall inward
3. Press knees toward floor
4. Feel stretch in outside of hips
5. Hold 30-60 seconds

## Hip CARs (Controlled Articular Rotations)

The single best hip mobility exercise.

### Standing Hip CARs

**How to do it:**
1. Stand on one leg, hold support
2. Lift knee high (flexion)
3. Rotate knee out to side (external rotation + abduction)
4. Extend leg behind you (extension)
5. Sweep leg around to front (internal rotation)
6. Complete full circle
7. Reverse direction
8. Perform 3-5 circles each direction, each hip

**Key points:**
- Move as slowly as possible
- Keep standing hip still—only working hip moves
- Explore full range in every direction
- Don't compensate with spine or pelvis

### Quadruped Hip CARs

Easier version with more support.

**How to do it:**
1. Start on hands and knees
2. Lift one knee off ground
3. Make large circles with knee
4. Keep spine still
5. Perform 5-10 circles each direction, each hip

## Dynamic Hip Mobility

### Leg Swings

**Forward/Backward:**
1. Hold support, stand on one leg
2. Swing other leg forward and back
3. Keep leg relatively straight
4. Let momentum create movement
5. Perform 15-20 swings each leg

**Side to Side:**
1. Face support, hold with both hands
2. Swing leg across body and out to side
3. Let hip open and close
4. Perform 15-20 swings each leg

### World's Greatest Stretch

Hits multiple hip positions in one flow.

**How to do it:**
1. Step into deep lunge
2. Drop inside elbow toward instep
3. Rotate same arm up toward ceiling
4. Straighten front leg, fold over it
5. Return to lunge and repeat
6. Perform 5-8 per side

### Walking Spiderman

**How to do it:**
1. Step into deep lunge
2. Place both hands inside front foot
3. Drop hips, feel hip flexor stretch on back leg
4. Step other foot forward into lunge
5. Continue walking forward
6. Perform 10-12 total lunges

---

## Strength for Mobility

Mobility without strength is unstable. Build control at end ranges.

### Hip Flexion PAILs/RAILs

**How to do it:**
1. Pull knee toward chest (stretch)
2. Hold 2 minutes
3. PAIL: Press knee away from chest (isometric, 10-20 sec)
4. RAIL: Pull knee closer (active, 10-20 sec)
5. Repeat contract/relax 2-3 times

### 90/90 Lifts

**How to do it:**
1. Sit in 90/90 position
2. Without using hands, lift front knee off ground
3. Hold 5-10 seconds
4. Lower and lift back knee
5. Perform 8-10 lifts each position

### Active Pigeon

**How to do it:**
1. Set up in pigeon stretch
2. Instead of relaxing, actively pull front knee toward chest
3. Hold contraction 5 seconds
4. Relax deeper into stretch
5. Repeat 5-8 times

## Sample Routines

### Daily Maintenance (10 minutes)

| Exercise | Duration/Reps |
|----------|---------------|

| Deep squat hold | 2 minutes |
| 90/90 position | 1 minute each side |
| Half-kneeling hip flexor stretch | 1 minute each side |

## Hip Mobility for Specific Goals

### For Deeper Squats
- Prioritize hip flexion and external rotation
- Deep squat holds daily
- Ankle mobility matters too
- Goblet squat "pry" technique

### For Running
- Focus on hip extension (hip flexor stretches)
- Single-leg stability
- Dynamic movements over static holds

### For Sitting Comfort
- External rotation (90/90, figure 4)
- Hip flexor lengthening
- Regular movement breaks

### For Lower Back Pain
- All directions important
- Emphasize extension if sitting a lot
- Build strength at end ranges
- Don't force through pain

## Common Mistakes

### 1. Forcing Range
**Problem:** Pushing too hard, too fast
**Fix:** Gentle, consistent effort over time

### 2. Ignoring Internal Rotation
**Problem:** Only working external rotation
**Fix:** Include internal rotation work—it's usually tighter

### 3. Compensating with Spine
**Problem:** Moving lower back instead of hip
**Fix:** Brace core, isolate hip movement

### 4. Stretching Without Strengthening
**Problem:** Gaining flexibility you can't control
**Fix:** Include PAILs/RAILs and active end-range work

### 5. Inconsistency
**Problem:** Occasional long sessions vs. daily short sessions
**Fix:** 10 minutes daily beats 1 hour weekly

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **CARs are king** — Do hip circles daily for full range maintenance
2. **Internal rotation is key** — Usually the most limited, most important
3. **Strength at end range matters** — Flexibility without control is injury risk
4. **Be patient** — Hip mobility takes months, not days
5. **Move throughout the day** — Sitting is the enemy of hip mobility
6. **Address all directions** — Flexion, extension, rotation, abduction

**Weekly volume suggestion:**
- Daily: 5-10 minutes hip mobility
- 2-3x/week: Longer 15-20 minute sessions

Your hips have incredible potential for movement. Reclaiming that range of motion transforms your squat, your running, your comfort, and your overall movement quality.
