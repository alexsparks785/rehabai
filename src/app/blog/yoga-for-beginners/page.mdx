# Yoga for Beginners: Start Your Practice with Confidence

Yoga isn't about touching your toes or pretzel-like poses. It's about showing up, breathing, and meeting your body where it is today. Every expert was once a beginner—and the poses you struggle with now will teach you the most.

**Key insight:** Yoga is a practice, not a performance. There's no "good" or "bad" at yoga—only where you are right now.

---

## What You Need to Start

### Equipment

- **Yoga mat:** Any basic mat works to start
- **Comfortable clothing:** Stretchy, nothing too loose
- **Optional props:** Blocks, strap, blanket (pillows and towels work too)

### Mindset

- Let go of expectations
- Listen to your body over the instructor
- Breathing matters more than how a pose looks
- Consistency beats intensity

---

## Fundamental Breathing

### Ujjayi Breath (Ocean Breath)

The foundation of yoga breathing.

**How to do it:**
1. Inhale through nose
2. Exhale through nose, slightly constricting back of throat
3. Create a soft "ocean wave" sound
4. Keep breath slow and controlled

**Practice:** 5-10 breaths before starting poses

### When to Breathe

- **Inhale:** When opening, extending, or lifting
- **Exhale:** When folding, twisting, or deepening
- **Always:** Keep breathing—never hold your breath

## Foundational Poses

### Mountain Pose (Tadasana)

The foundation of all standing poses.

**How to do it:**
1. Stand with feet hip-width apart (or together)
2. Distribute weight evenly across both feet
3. Stack joints: ankles, knees, hips, shoulders
4. Arms at sides, palms forward
5. Lift through crown of head
6. Engage core gently

**Hold:** 5-10 breaths

### Child's Pose (Balasana)

Your rest position—return here whenever needed.

**How to do it:**
1. Kneel on mat, big toes touching
2. Sit back on heels
3. Fold forward, forehead to mat
4. Arms extended forward or alongside body
5. Let belly rest on thighs

**Hold:** As long as needed

**Modification:** Widen knees for belly space

### Downward Facing Dog (Adho Mukha Svanasana)

The most recognized yoga pose.

**How to do it:**
1. Start on all fours
2. Tuck toes, lift hips toward ceiling
3. Straighten legs (slight bend is fine)
4. Press hands into mat, spread fingers
5. Let head hang between arms
6. Press heels toward floor (they don't need to touch)

**Hold:** 5-10 breaths

**Tip:** Bent knees are completely fine—focus on lengthening spine

### Cat-Cow (Marjaryasana-Bitilasana)

Spinal warm-up and breath coordination.

**How to do it:**
1. Start on all fours, wrists under shoulders, knees under hips
2. **Cow (inhale):** Drop belly, lift chest and tailbone, look up
3. **Cat (exhale):** Round spine, tuck chin and tailbone
4. Flow between with breath

**Repeat:** 5-10 cycles

### Warrior I (Virabhadrasana I)

Builds strength and opens hips.

**How to do it:**
1. From standing, step one foot back 3-4 feet
2. Back foot angled 45°, front foot forward
3. Bend front knee over ankle (not past toes)
4. Square hips toward front
5. Raise arms overhead, palms facing
6. Look up or forward

**Hold:** 5 breaths each side

### Warrior II (Virabhadrasana II)

Strengthens legs and opens hips.

**How to do it:**
1. From Warrior I, open hips to side
2. Extend arms parallel to floor
3. Front knee stays bent over ankle
4. Gaze over front fingertips
5. Back leg straight, foot flat

**Hold:** 5 breaths each side

### Triangle Pose (Trikonasana)

Side body stretch and leg strength.

**How to do it:**
1. From Warrior II, straighten front leg
2. Reach forward, then lower front hand to shin or block
3. Top arm extends toward ceiling
4. Keep chest open, not collapsed forward
5. Gaze up, forward, or down (neck comfort)

**Hold:** 5 breaths each side

### Tree Pose (Vrksasana)

Balance and focus.

**How to do it:**
1. Stand on one leg
2. Place other foot on inner thigh or calf (avoid knee)
3. Press foot and leg together
4. Hands at heart or overhead
5. Find a focal point (drishti)

**Hold:** 5-10 breaths each side

**Tip:** Use a wall for support at first

### Cobra Pose (Bhujangasana)

Gentle backbend, strengthens back.

**How to do it:**
1. Lie face down, hands under shoulders
2. Press tops of feet into mat
3. Inhale, lift chest using back muscles
4. Keep elbows slightly bent, close to body
5. Shoulders away from ears

**Hold:** 5 breaths

**Key:** Lift with back muscles, don't push heavily with hands

### Bridge Pose (Setu Bandhasana)

Backbend that strengthens glutes and opens chest.

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back, knees bent, feet flat
2. Arms at sides, palms down
3. Press feet down, lift hips
4. Walk shoulders under, interlace fingers (optional)
5. Keep knees parallel, not splaying

**Hold:** 5-10 breaths

### Seated Forward Fold (Paschimottanasana)

Hamstring stretch and calming pose.

**How to do it:**
1. Sit with legs extended
2. Inhale, lift arms and lengthen spine
3. Exhale, hinge at hips, reaching toward feet
4. Keep spine long—don't round to reach further
5. Hold wherever you feel stretch

**Hold:** 5-10 breaths

**Tip:** Bend knees if hamstrings are tight

### Corpse Pose (Savasana)

Final relaxation—never skip this.

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back, legs extended, arms at sides
2. Palms face up
3. Let feet fall open naturally
4. Close eyes
5. Release all effort
6. Simply breathe and be

**Hold:** 5-10 minutes

## Beginner Sequence (20 Minutes)

### Warm-Up (5 minutes)

1. **Seated breathing:** 10 breaths
2. **Cat-Cow:** 8 cycles
3. **Child's Pose:** 5 breaths

### Standing Poses (8 minutes)

1. **Mountain Pose:** 5 breaths
2. **Forward Fold:** 5 breaths (bend knees generously)
3. **Halfway Lift:** inhale, exhale fold
4. **Downward Dog:** 5 breaths
5. **Warrior I (right):** 5 breaths
6. **Warrior II (right):** 5 breaths
7. **Triangle (right):** 5 breaths
8. **Return to Down Dog:** 5 breaths
9. Repeat left side
10. **Tree Pose:** 5 breaths each side

### Floor Poses (4 minutes)

1. **Cobra:** 3 breaths, 2 times
2. **Child's Pose:** 5 breaths
3. **Bridge:** 5 breaths, 2 times
4. **Seated Forward Fold:** 10 breaths

### Closing (3 minutes)

1. **Supine Twist:** 5 breaths each side
2. **Knees to Chest:** rock gently
3. **Savasana:** 2-3 minutes minimum

## Common Mistakes

### Alignment Issues

- **Holding breath:** Keep breathing through every pose
- **Forcing depth:** Back off if you feel strain
- **Comparing to others:** Your practice is yours
- **Skipping modifications:** Props and modifications are tools, not cheating
- **Rushing Savasana:** The integration time matters

### Physical Mistakes

- **Hyperextending joints:** Keep slight bend in elbows and knees
- **Collapsing in poses:** Engage muscles even in stretches
- **Ignoring pain:** Discomfort is okay, pain is not
- **Forgetting the breath:** It's called yoga "practice" not yoga "perfect"

---

## When to Modify

### Use a Block

- Hand doesn't reach floor comfortably
- Triangle or Half Moon poses
- Seated poses where hips are tight

### Use a Strap

- Can't reach feet in forward folds
- Shoulder stretches
- Bound poses

### Bend Your Knees

- Tight hamstrings in forward folds
- Downward Dog feels too intense
- Any time you need to protect lower back

### Take Child's Pose

- You're tired
- You need a break
- You feel overwhelmed
- Anytime, for any reason

## Building a Practice

### Frequency

- **Ideal:** 3-5 times per week
- **Minimum:** 1-2 times per week for progress
- **Something beats nothing:** Even 10 minutes counts

### Progression

**Weeks 1-2:**
- Focus on learning poses
- Don't worry about holding long
- Use modifications freely

**Weeks 3-4:**
- Begin linking poses with breath
- Hold poses slightly longer
- Reduce reliance on modifications where comfortable

**Month 2+:**
- Flow more smoothly
- Add new poses
- Explore different styles (vinyasa, yin, etc.)

### When to Practice

- **Morning:** Energizing, sets tone for day
- **Evening:** Calming, aids sleep
- **Anytime:** Better than skipping

## Different Yoga Styles

### Hatha

Slow-paced, holding poses. Great for beginners.

### Vinyasa

Flowing movement with breath. More athletic.

### Yin

Long-held passive stretches. Deep relaxation.

### Restorative

Fully supported poses. Maximum relaxation.

### Power/Ashtanga

Intense, athletic practice. Build up to these.

**Recommendation:** Start with Hatha or slow Vinyasa classes labeled "beginner."

---

## The Bottom Line

Yoga is about the journey, not the destination. You don't need to be flexible to start—you become flexible through practice. Show up, breathe, move, and let go of judgment.

**Start here:**
1. Learn the foundational poses above
2. Practice the 20-minute sequence 2-3 times per week
3. Always include Savasana
4. Use props without hesitation
5. Be patient with yourself

The only bad yoga practice is the one you didn't do.
