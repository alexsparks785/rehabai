# AMRAP Workouts: As Many Rounds As Possible Training Guide

Want a workout format that's simple to understand, infinitely scalable, and guaranteed to push your limits? AMRAP—As Many Rounds (or Reps) As Possible—delivers all of that in a fixed time window. Set a timer, pick your exercises, and see how much work you can do.

## What is an AMRAP Workout?

AMRAP stands for "As Many Rounds As Possible" (or sometimes "As Many Reps As Possible"). You're given a set time limit and a list of exercises with prescribed reps. Your job: complete as many full rounds as you can before time runs out.

### The Basic Structure

**Example: 15-Minute AMRAP**
- 10 push-ups
- 15 air squats
- 20 sit-ups

Start the clock. Do 10 push-ups, 15 air squats, 20 sit-ups. That's one round. Immediately start round two. Keep going until 15 minutes is up. Your score is total rounds plus any extra reps.

**Sample Score:** 6 rounds + 10 push-ups + 8 air squats = 6+28 (or written as 6 rounds + 28 reps)

### Why AMRAPs Work

- **Self-pacing:** You control intensity based on your fitness
- **Measurable progress:** Easy to track and beat your score
- **Time-efficient:** Know exactly when you'll finish
- **Scalable:** Same workout works for beginners and advanced
- **Mental challenge:** Pushing through when you want to stop

## AMRAP vs. Other Workout Formats

### AMRAP vs. EMOM

| AMRAP | EMOM |
|-------|------|
| Continuous work, self-paced rest | Structured rest each minute |
| Score = total rounds/reps | Score = completion (pass/fail) |
| You control the pace | Clock controls the pace |
| Can slow down when tired | Must maintain pace or fail |

### AMRAP vs. For Time

| AMRAP | For Time |
|-------|----------|
| Fixed time, variable work | Fixed work, variable time |
| Always finish "on time" | May take longer than expected |
| Score = rounds completed | Score = time to finish |
| Good for any fitness level | Can be discouraging if slow |

### AMRAP vs. Intervals

| AMRAP | Intervals |
|-------|-----------|
| Continuous with self-selected rest | Prescribed work/rest periods |
| Full round before resting | Rest between each interval |
| Workout is one long effort | Multiple distinct efforts |

## Programming AMRAP Workouts

### Choosing Time Domains

| Duration | Character | Best For |
|----------|-----------|----------|
| 5-7 min | Sprint, high intensity | Power, quick conditioning |
| 8-12 min | Middle ground | Balanced conditioning |
| 15-20 min | Sustained effort | Endurance, pacing practice |
| 20-30 min | Long grind | Mental toughness, aerobic base |
| 30+ min | Ultra endurance | Advanced, competition prep |

### Selecting Exercises

**Good AMRAP exercises:**
- Simple, familiar movements
- Minimal setup/transition time
- Scalable to all levels
- Can be done safely when fatigued

**Examples:**
- Push-ups, pull-ups, dips
- Squats, lunges, box jumps
- Kettlebell swings, cleans, snatches
- Burpees, mountain climbers
- Rows, runs, bike

**Less ideal:**
- Complex Olympic lifts
- Heavy maximal lifts
- Movements requiring equipment changes
- Highly technical skills

### Rep Schemes That Work

**Classic Triplet:** 3 exercises, round numbers
- 10-15-20 (ascending)
- 21-15-9 (descending)
- 10-10-10 (equal)

**Couplet:** 2 exercises, push/pull or upper/lower
- 10 pull-ups, 20 push-ups
- 15 thrusters, 15 burpees

**Chipper Style:** Higher reps, fewer rounds expected
- 20-30-40-50 reps through exercises
- May only complete 1-2 rounds

**Single Movement:** Simple but brutal
- AMRAP 7: Burpees
- Your score = total burpees

## AMRAP Workouts for Different Goals

### For Conditioning

**12-Minute AMRAP:**
- 10 burpees
- 20 jumping lunges
- 30 mountain climbers

**Why It Works:** High heart rate, full-body movements, continuous effort builds cardiovascular capacity.

### For Strength-Endurance

**15-Minute AMRAP:**
- 5 dumbbell deadlifts (heavy)
- 5 dumbbell hang cleans
- 5 dumbbell push presses
- 5 dumbbell front squats

**Why It Works:** Moderate reps with load, full-body strength under fatigue.

### For Fat Loss

**20-Minute AMRAP:**
- 15 kettlebell swings
- 10 goblet squats
- 5 burpees

**Why It Works:** High caloric burn, metabolic demand, sustained work output.

### For Beginners

**10-Minute AMRAP:**
- 5 push-ups (or modified)
- 10 air squats
- 15 jumping jacks

**Why It Works:** Simple movements, manageable reps, builds confidence and work capacity.

### For Mental Toughness

**30-Minute AMRAP:**
- 400m run (or 500m row)
- 15 thrusters (moderate weight)
- 15 pull-ups (or ring rows)

**Why It Works:** Long duration forces pacing, mental game becomes crucial.

## Sample AMRAP Workouts

### Beginner: Bodyweight Basics

**8-Minute AMRAP:**
- 5 push-ups
- 10 sit-ups
- 15 air squats

**Target:** 5-7 rounds
**Scaling:** Knee push-ups, reduce reps

### Intermediate: Dumbbell Blast

**12-Minute AMRAP:**
- 8 dumbbell thrusters
- 12 dumbbell rows (total)
- 16 walking lunges

**Target:** 5-6 rounds
**Weight:** Moderate (can do 15+ thrusters unbroken when fresh)

### Advanced: Heavy Grind

**15-Minute AMRAP:**
- 3 power cleans (185/125)
- 6 front squats
- 9 bar-facing burpees

**Target:** 6-8 rounds
**Note:** Weight should be challenging but sustainable

### Bodyweight Only

**20-Minute AMRAP:**
- 5 pull-ups
- 10 push-ups
- 15 air squats

**Note:** This is "Cindy" - a classic CrossFit benchmark. Target: 15-20+ rounds for fit individuals.

### Cardio Focus

**15-Minute AMRAP:**
- 200m run
- 10 burpees
- 20 double-unders (or 40 singles)

**Target:** 5-7 rounds depending on running speed

### Full-Body Kettlebell

**16-Minute AMRAP:**
- 10 kettlebell swings
- 8 goblet squats
- 6 kettlebell push presses (3 each arm)
- 4 Turkish get-ups (2 each side)

**Target:** 4-6 rounds (get-ups slow you down)

### Partner AMRAP

**20-Minute AMRAP (You Go, I Go):**
- Partner A: 10 calories row
- Partner B: 10 calories row
- Continue alternating

**Score:** Total calories or rounds

**Variation:** Split the work
- Partner A: 5 pull-ups
- Partner B: 10 push-ups
- Together: 15 squats
- Both must complete before moving on

## AMRAP Pacing Strategies

### Start Conservative

The biggest mistake: going too hard in round one. You have the whole workout ahead. Start at 70-80% effort, find a rhythm, then push harder in the final minutes if you have gas left.

### Break Before You Have To

Don't push to failure on any movement. If you know you can do 15 push-ups unbroken but the workout calls for 10, consider doing 5-5 or taking a breath at 7. Staying ahead of fatigue maintains pace.

### Partition Wisely

**Example: 15 Thrusters**
- All 15 unbroken: Fast but may require long rest after
- 8-7 split: Brief rest, maintain steady pace
- 5-5-5 split: Very manageable, consistent

The right partition depends on fitness level and workout length. Longer AMRAPs reward conservative partitioning.

### Transitions Matter

Time between exercises adds up. Minimize transition time:
- Set up equipment before starting
- Plan your movement order
- Don't walk away from your station
- Take rest in position, not wandering

### Negative Split

Try to make each round faster than (or equal to) the previous. This requires starting conservatively and building. It's psychologically powerful—you finish strong instead of fading.

## Scaling AMRAPs

### Reduce Reps

Can't do 10 pull-ups per round? Do 5. The structure stays the same, intensity adjusts to your level.

### Modify Movements

| Rx Movement | Scaled Option |
|-------------|---------------|
| Pull-ups | Ring rows, jumping pull-ups |
| Push-ups | Knee push-ups, incline push-ups |
| Pistols | Air squats, assisted pistols |
| Double-unders | Single-unders (2:1 or 3:1) |
| Toes-to-bar | Knee raises, sit-ups |

### Reduce Weight

Using dumbbells or barbells? Drop the weight until you can maintain movement quality throughout.

### Shorten Duration

A 20-minute AMRAP too daunting? Do 10 or 12 minutes. Build up over time.

### Add Rest Intervals

Not a pure AMRAP, but helpful for beginners:
- Work for 2 minutes
- Rest for 30 seconds
- Repeat for total time
- Score = total work completed

## Tracking AMRAP Progress

### Record Your Score

Every AMRAP should be logged:
- Workout description
- Your score (rounds + reps)
- Weight used
- How you felt
- Any scaling

### Retest Regularly

Repeat the same AMRAP every 4-8 weeks. Improvement might look like:
- More rounds completed
- Same rounds with heavier weight
- Same rounds with less scaling
- Same rounds but feeling easier

### Benchmark AMRAPs

Some classic AMRAPs to test regularly:

**Cindy (20 min):**
- 5 pull-ups, 10 push-ups, 15 squats

**Mary (20 min):**
- 5 handstand push-ups, 10 pistols, 15 pull-ups

**Chelsea (30 min - EMOM style):**
- 5 pull-ups, 10 push-ups, 15 squats per minute

**Open Workout 12.1:**
- 7-minute AMRAP: Burpees

## Common AMRAP Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Starting Too Fast

**The Problem:** Burning out in round 2-3, barely moving by halfway
**The Fix:** Start at 70% effort, negative split if possible

### Mistake 2: Resting Too Long

**The Problem:** Taking 30-second breaks between movements
**The Fix:** Keep moving. Short breaks (3-5 breaths) in position, not wandering

### Mistake 3: Sloppy Reps

**The Problem:** Sacrificing form to get more rounds
**The Fix:** Quality reps only. A no-rep is wasted time and injury risk

### Mistake 4: Not Scaling Appropriately

**The Problem:** Choosing Rx when you can barely complete movements
**The Fix:** Scale to maintain intensity. 6 rounds scaled > 2 rounds Rx

### Mistake 5: No Strategy

**The Problem:** Just going until you can't
**The Fix:** Plan your pace, partition sets, know your transitions

## Building an AMRAP Program

### Weekly Structure

**Monday:** Short, intense AMRAP (7-10 min)
**Tuesday:** Strength work (not AMRAP)
**Wednesday:** Medium AMRAP (12-15 min)
**Thursday:** Active recovery
**Friday:** Long AMRAP (20+ min) or mixed workout
**Weekend:** Rest or recreational activity

### Monthly Progression

**Week 1:** Establish baseline scores on 2-3 AMRAPs
**Week 2:** Similar workouts, focus on pacing
**Week 3:** Push intensity, try to beat Week 1 scores
**Week 4:** Deload - shorter AMRAPs, lower intensity

### Combining With Other Training

AMRAPs work well as:
- **Finishers:** 5-7 min AMRAP after strength work
- **Standalone conditioning:** 15-20 min AMRAP days
- **Testing:** Benchmark AMRAPs monthly
- **Active recovery:** Easy 10-min AMRAP with light movements

## The Bottom Line

AMRAP workouts are beautifully simple: set a timer, do the work, count your rounds. But that simplicity hides depth. Learning to pace, push through discomfort, and strategize your effort takes time and practice.

Start with shorter AMRAPs and simple movements. Master pacing before adding complexity. Track your scores and retest regularly—watching those numbers climb is incredibly motivating.

The clock is your opponent and your accountability partner. When it starts, there's nowhere to hide. That's what makes AMRAPs so effective. Set your timer, take a breath, and get to work.

Your score is waiting.
