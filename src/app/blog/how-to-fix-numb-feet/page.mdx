# How to Fix Numb Feet: Causes, Exercises, and Solutions

Numb feet can range from an occasional annoyance (your foot "fell asleep") to a persistent problem that affects your balance and quality of life. Understanding the cause is key to fixing it. Here's what you need to know.

## Why Do Feet Go Numb?

### Temporary Causes

**Positional Compression**
Sitting cross-legged, sitting on your foot, or any position that compresses nerves or blood vessels. The "foot fell asleep" feeling.

**Tight Footwear**
Shoes that are too tight, especially around the toe box or instep, can compress nerves.

**Cold Exposure**
Cold reduces blood flow and can cause temporary numbness.

### Nerve-Related Causes

**Peripheral Neuropathy**
Nerve damage affecting the feet. Most commonly caused by diabetes, but also by B12 deficiency, alcohol use, medications, or other conditions.

**Sciatica**
Compression of the sciatic nerve in the lower back or buttock can cause numbness that radiates into the foot.

**Tarsal Tunnel Syndrome**
Compression of the tibial nerve at the inside of the ankle—similar to carpal tunnel but in the foot.

**Peroneal Nerve Compression**
Compression of the nerve on the outer side of the knee, causing numbness on top of the foot.

**Morton's Neuroma**
Thickening of tissue around a nerve between the toes, causing numbness and pain in the ball of the foot.

### Circulation-Related Causes

**Peripheral Artery Disease (PAD)**
Narrowing of arteries reduces blood flow to the feet. More common in smokers and diabetics.

**Sitting Too Long**
Prolonged sitting reduces circulation to the feet and legs.

**Raynaud's Phenomenon**
Blood vessel spasms in response to cold or stress, causing numbness and color changes.

### Other Causes

- Diabetes (both nerve and circulation effects)
- Multiple sclerosis
- Vitamin deficiencies (B12, B6)
- Thyroid disorders
- Medication side effects
- Chronic alcohol use

## When to See a Doctor

**Seek medical attention if:**
- Numbness is persistent or worsening
- Numbness started suddenly without clear cause
- You have diabetes
- You have weakness along with numbness
- Numbness spreads or affects other areas
- You have wounds on your feet that don't heal
- Numbness is accompanied by back pain
- You have color changes in your feet
- Numbness affects your balance or walking

## Exercises and Strategies by Cause

### For Positional Numbness (Feet Falling Asleep)

#### 1. Change Position Frequently
- Don't sit with legs crossed for long periods
- Avoid sitting on your feet
- Stand up and move every 30-60 minutes

#### 2. Ankle Pumps
**Why:** Gets blood flowing immediately.

**How to do it:**
1. Flex your foot (toes toward shin)
2. Point your foot (toes down)
3. Pump back and forth quickly
4. Do 20-30 pumps to restore circulation

#### 3. Toe Wiggles
**How to do it:**
1. Spread your toes apart
2. Squeeze them together
3. Wiggle them individually
4. Repeat for 30-60 seconds

#### 4. Calf Raises
**How to do it:**
1. Stand up
2. Rise onto your toes
3. Lower slowly
4. Do 15-20 reps to pump blood back

### For Sciatica-Related Foot Numbness

#### 5. Sciatic Nerve Glide
**How to do it:**
1. Sit in a chair
2. Slouch your upper body while straightening your affected leg
3. Point your toes
4. Sit up tall while bending your knee and flexing your foot
5. Move smoothly back and forth
6. Do 10-15 reps, 2-3 times daily

#### 6. Piriformis Stretch
**Why:** The piriformis can compress the sciatic nerve.

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on your back
2. Cross affected ankle over opposite knee
3. Pull the uncrossed leg toward your chest
4. Feel stretch in buttock
5. Hold 45-60 seconds

#### 7. Press-Up (McKenzie Extension)
**Why:** Can help with disc-related sciatica.

**How to do it:**
1. Lie face down
2. Place hands under shoulders
3. Press up, keeping hips on floor
4. Let lower back sag
5. Hold briefly, lower down
6. Do 10 reps, multiple times daily

**Note:** Stop if this increases leg/foot symptoms.

### For Tarsal Tunnel Syndrome

#### 8. Tibial Nerve Glide
**How to do it:**
1. Sit with affected leg extended
2. Point your foot and toes, turn foot inward
3. Bend knee and flex foot/toes
4. Move smoothly between positions
5. Do 10-15 reps, 2-3 times daily

#### 9. Ankle Eversion Stretch
**How to do it:**
1. Sit with ankle crossed over opposite knee
2. Grasp your foot and gently turn sole upward
3. Hold 30 seconds
4. Repeat 3 times

#### 10. Calf Stretch
**Why:** Tight calves can contribute to tarsal tunnel.

**How to do it:**
1. Stand facing a wall
2. Step back with one foot, heel down
3. Lean forward until you feel the stretch
4. Hold 30-45 seconds per side

### For Morton's Neuroma

#### 11. Toe Spacing
**How to do it:**
- Wear toe spacers for 15-30 minutes daily
- Use shoes with a wide toe box
- Avoid high heels and narrow shoes

#### 12. Marble Pickups
**Why:** Strengthens toe muscles and promotes mobility.

**How to do it:**
1. Place marbles on the floor
2. Pick them up with your toes
3. Transfer to a cup
4. Spend 3-5 minutes practicing

#### 13. Metatarsal Massage
**How to do it:**
1. Roll a golf ball under the ball of your foot
2. Apply moderate pressure
3. Focus on the area between the metatarsal heads
4. Spend 2-3 minutes per foot

### For Poor Circulation

#### 14. Leg Elevation
**How to do it:**
1. Lie down and elevate feet above heart level
2. Use pillows under calves
3. Hold for 10-15 minutes
4. Do several times daily

#### 15. Walking
**Why:** The best exercise for circulation.

**How to do it:**
- Walk for 20-30 minutes daily
- Even short walks help
- Build up gradually if deconditioned

#### 16. Heel-Toe Walking
**How to do it:**
1. Walk normally, emphasizing the heel-to-toe roll
2. This pumps blood through the foot with each step
3. Include in your daily walking

#### 17. Foot Massage
**How to do it:**
1. Massage from toes toward heel
2. Squeeze and knead the foot
3. Push blood toward the heart
4. Spend 2-3 minutes per foot

### General Nerve Health Exercises

#### 18. Balance Exercises
**Why:** Important if numbness affects proprioception.

**Single-Leg Stand:**
1. Stand near a wall for support
2. Lift one foot
3. Hold as long as possible
4. Work up to 60 seconds per leg

**Tandem Stand:**
1. Place one foot directly in front of the other (heel to toe)
2. Balance in this position
3. Hold 30 seconds, switch which foot is forward

#### 19. Ankle Circles
**How to do it:**
1. Lift your foot off the ground
2. Rotate ankle in full circles
3. 10 circles each direction
4. Do both feet

## Lifestyle Modifications

### Footwear
- Wear shoes with a wide toe box
- Avoid high heels and pointed shoes
- Ensure proper fit—not too tight
- Consider custom orthotics if you have structural issues
- Replace worn-out shoes

### Sitting Habits
- Don't cross legs for extended periods
- Use a footrest to reduce pressure on backs of thighs
- Stand and move every 30-60 minutes
- Avoid sitting on hard edges that compress nerves

### Circulation Support
- Stay active—regular exercise improves circulation
- Quit smoking (major factor in PAD)
- Manage blood sugar if diabetic
- Stay hydrated
- Consider compression socks if recommended

### Temperature
- Keep feet warm in cold weather
- Wear warm socks
- Avoid prolonged cold exposure
- Warm water soaks (if not diabetic or have sensation loss)

### Nutrition
- Adequate B12 (especially for vegetarians/vegans)
- Balanced diet for overall nerve health
- Limit alcohol
- Manage blood sugar

## When Exercises Aren't Enough

If numbness persists despite self-care:

**Medical evaluation may include:**
- Physical examination
- Nerve conduction studies
- Blood tests (B12, glucose, thyroid)
- Imaging (for spinal causes)
- Vascular studies (for circulation issues)

**Treatment options:**
- Physical therapy
- Medications for neuropathic pain
- Treatment of underlying conditions (diabetes, B12 deficiency)
- Orthotics or shoe modifications
- Injections (for neuromas or tarsal tunnel)
- Surgery (in severe cases)

## Your Daily Routine for Numb Feet

### Morning
- Ankle circles and toe wiggles: 1 minute
- Calf stretches: 30 seconds each
- Brief walking: 5-10 minutes

### Throughout the Day
- Position changes every 30-60 minutes
- Ankle pumps when sitting for long periods
- Walking breaks

### Evening
- Foot massage: 2-3 minutes per foot
- Nerve glides (if nerve-related): 10-15 reps
- Leg elevation: 10-15 minutes
- Warm water soak (if appropriate)

## The Bottom Line

Numb feet usually have a fixable cause—whether it's positional habits, tight shoes, or a nerve issue that responds to exercises and lifestyle changes.

Start by identifying the pattern: When does numbness occur? Where exactly? What makes it better or worse? This points you toward the cause.

For temporary numbness from position or sitting, simple movement and circulation exercises provide quick relief. For nerve-related causes, consistent nerve glides and addressing the compression point are key.

If numbness is persistent, progressive, or accompanied by other symptoms, get it evaluated. Conditions like diabetes, B12 deficiency, and circulation problems are treatable—but they need proper diagnosis.

Your feet have a lot of nerves for a reason—they need to feel the ground to keep you balanced and safe. Take numbness seriously, identify the cause, and take action.
