# Carpal Tunnel Exercises: Relieve Wrist and Hand Symptoms

Carpal tunnel syndrome affects millions of people, causing numbness, tingling, and weakness in the hand. While severe cases may need surgery, many people find significant relief through targeted exercises.

**Key insight:** Nerve gliding exercises help the median nerve move smoothly through the carpal tunnel. Combined with stretching and ergonomic changes, exercises can reduce or eliminate symptoms for many people.

---

## Understanding Carpal Tunnel Syndrome

### What's Happening

The carpal tunnel is a narrow passage in your wrist formed by bones and ligament. The median nerve and flexor tendons pass through this space. When the tunnel contents are compressed or swollen, the nerve gets irritated.

### Symptoms

- Numbness/tingling in thumb, index, middle, and half of ring finger
- Symptoms often worse at night
- Weakness in hand grip
- Dropping objects
- Pain radiating up forearm
- "Shaking out" hands for relief

### Common Causes

- Repetitive wrist motions
- Prolonged wrist flexion or extension
- Pregnancy (swelling)
- Diabetes
- Thyroid disorders
- Rheumatoid arthritis
- Computer/phone use

## Nerve Gliding Exercises

These help the median nerve slide freely through the tunnel.

### Median Nerve Glide (6-Position Sequence)

**Position 1 - Fist:**
- Make a fist, wrist neutral
- Hold 3-5 seconds

**Position 2 - Straight Fingers:**
- Straighten fingers, wrist neutral
- Hold 3-5 seconds

**Position 3 - Wrist Extension:**
- Fingers straight, bend wrist back
- Hold 3-5 seconds

**Position 4 - Thumb Extension:**
- Add extending thumb away from palm
- Hold 3-5 seconds

**Position 5 - Forearm Supination:**
- Turn palm toward ceiling
- Hold 3-5 seconds

**Position 6 - Gentle Thumb Stretch:**
- Use other hand to gently stretch thumb back
- Hold 3-5 seconds

**Protocol:** Move through all 6 positions, 5-10 complete sequences, 2-3 times daily

### Simple Median Nerve Glide

**How to do it:**
1. Start with arm at side, elbow bent, wrist neutral
2. Extend wrist (fingers toward ceiling)
3. Straighten elbow while keeping wrist extended
4. Gently tilt head away from stretched arm
5. Return to start
6. Perform 10-15 repetitions, 2-3 times daily

**Key:** Movement should be smooth and controlled. Stop if symptoms worsen.

### Wrist Flexion/Extension Glide

**How to do it:**
1. Arm straight in front, palm up
2. Make a fist
3. Bend wrist down (flexion)
4. Open fingers and extend wrist back
5. Flow between positions smoothly
6. Perform 10-15 repetitions

## Tendon Gliding Exercises

Help flexor tendons move smoothly through the tunnel.

### Finger Tendon Glides

Move through these five positions:

**1. Straight:** All fingers extended
**2. Hook:** Bend at middle and end joints only
**3. Fist:** Full fist
**4. Tabletop:** Bend only at knuckles, fingers straight
**5. Straight Fist:** Fingers curled, tips touch palm at base

**Protocol:** Hold each position 3-5 seconds, perform 10 complete sequences, 2-3 times daily

### Thumb Tendon Glide

**How to do it:**
1. Start with thumb extended
2. Touch thumb to base of pinky finger
3. Return to extended position
4. Touch thumb to tip of each finger
5. Return to extended position
6. Perform 10 complete sequences

## Stretches

### Wrist Flexor Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Extend arm in front, palm up
2. Use other hand to pull fingers back toward you
3. Keep elbow straight
4. Feel stretch in inner forearm
5. Hold 20-30 seconds
6. Repeat 3-4 times each arm

### Wrist Extensor Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Extend arm in front, palm down
2. Use other hand to pull fingers down
3. Keep elbow straight
4. Feel stretch in outer forearm
5. Hold 20-30 seconds
6. Repeat 3-4 times each arm

### Prayer Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Press palms together in front of chest
2. Lower hands while keeping palms pressed
3. Feel stretch in wrists and forearms
4. Hold 20-30 seconds
5. Repeat 3-4 times

### Reverse Prayer Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Press backs of hands together
2. Fingers pointing down
3. Raise hands while keeping contact
4. Feel stretch in wrists
5. Hold 20-30 seconds
6. Repeat 3-4 times

## Strengthening Exercises

Build strength to support the wrist.

### Wrist Curls

**How to do it:**
1. Rest forearm on table, palm up, wrist over edge
2. Hold light weight (1-3 lbs)
3. Curl wrist up
4. Lower slowly
5. Perform 12-15 reps, 2 sets

### Reverse Wrist Curls

**How to do it:**
1. Rest forearm on table, palm down, wrist over edge
2. Hold light weight
3. Raise back of hand toward ceiling
4. Lower slowly
5. Perform 12-15 reps, 2 sets

### Grip Strengthening

**How to do it:**
1. Squeeze soft ball or stress ball
2. Hold 5 seconds
3. Release slowly
4. Perform 10-15 reps

**Note:** Skip if gripping worsens symptoms.

### Finger Spread with Band

**How to do it:**
1. Place rubber band around all fingertips
2. Spread fingers apart against resistance
3. Hold 3-5 seconds
4. Release
5. Perform 15-20 reps

---

## Wrist Mobilization

### Wrist Circles

**How to do it:**
1. Extend arm, make loose fist
2. Slowly circle wrist clockwise
3. Then counterclockwise
4. 10 circles each direction
5. Perform 2-3 times daily

### Wrist Flexion/Extension

**How to do it:**
1. Forearm supported, hand over edge
2. Move wrist through full range
3. Flex (palm toward floor) and extend (palm toward ceiling)
4. Move slowly through full range
5. Perform 15-20 reps

## Sample Daily Routine

### Morning (5 minutes)

| Exercise | Duration/Reps |
|----------|---------------|
| Wrist circles | 10 each direction |
| Median nerve glide (6-position) | 5 sequences |
| Wrist flexor stretch | 2 × 20 sec each arm |
| Wrist extensor stretch | 2 × 20 sec each arm |

### Work Breaks (Every 1-2 Hours)

| Exercise | Duration/Reps |
|----------|---------------|
| Simple median nerve glide | 10 reps |
| Finger tendon glides | 5 sequences |
| Prayer stretch | 20 seconds |
| Shake hands gently | 10 seconds |

### Evening (5 minutes)

| Exercise | Duration/Reps |
|----------|---------------|
| Median nerve glide (6-position) | 5-10 sequences |
| Tendon glides | 10 sequences |
| All stretches | 2 × 20 sec each |
| Strengthening (if tolerated) | 2 × 12 reps |

## Night Management

### Night Splinting

Many people benefit from wearing a wrist splint at night:
- Keeps wrist in neutral position
- Prevents prolonged flexion during sleep
- Reduces morning symptoms

**Wear for:** 6-8 weeks initially, then as needed

### Sleep Position

- Avoid sleeping on hands
- Keep wrists straight, not tucked
- Pillow positioning to support arms

---

## Workstation Ergonomics

### Keyboard/Mouse Position

- Wrists in neutral position (not bent up or down)
- Keyboard at elbow height
- Mouse close to keyboard
- Use wrist rest for support during pauses, not while typing

### Take Breaks

- Micro-breaks every 20-30 minutes
- Longer breaks every 1-2 hours
- Use break time for exercises

### Phone Use

- Hold phone up rather than looking down
- Use speakerphone or headset
- Alternate hands
- Voice-to-text when possible

---

## What to Avoid

### Aggravating Activities

- Prolonged gripping
- Repetitive wrist flexion/extension
- Vibrating tools
- Sleeping on hands
- Cold exposure without protection

### During Exercise

- Don't push through increased numbness/tingling
- Avoid sustained stretches over 30 seconds
- Don't force nerve glides—gentle is better
- Stop if symptoms worsen

---

## Progress Indicators

### Positive Signs

- Reduced nighttime symptoms
- Less frequent daytime numbness
- Improved grip strength
- Longer time before symptoms start
- Less need to shake hands

### Time to See Doctor

- No improvement after 4-6 weeks of exercises
- Symptoms worsening despite exercise
- Constant numbness (not intermittent)
- Visible muscle wasting at thumb base
- Significant weakness
- Unable to do daily activities

---

## Conservative vs. Surgical Treatment

### Exercises May Be Enough If:

- Symptoms are mild to moderate
- Intermittent, not constant
- No significant muscle wasting
- Symptoms present less than 6 months
- Positive response to splinting

### Surgery May Be Needed If:

- Severe, constant symptoms
- Muscle wasting present
- No improvement with conservative treatment
- EMG shows significant nerve damage
- Symptoms for extended period without treatment

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Nerve glides are essential** — Help median nerve move freely
2. **Consistency matters** — Multiple brief sessions daily
3. **Night splinting helps** — Prevents flexion during sleep
4. **Address ergonomics** — Reduce aggravating factors
5. **Gentle is better** — Don't force movements
6. **Know when to seek help** — Constant symptoms need evaluation

**Daily formula:**
- Morning: 5 minutes nerve glides + stretches
- Throughout day: Brief exercise breaks
- Evening: Complete routine
- Night: Splint if helpful

Many cases of carpal tunnel syndrome respond well to exercises combined with ergonomic modifications and night splinting. Start the exercises, make your environment better, and track your progress.
