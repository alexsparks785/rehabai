Carpal tunnel syndrome affects millions of people, especially those who spend hours typing, using a mouse, or performing repetitive hand movements. While severe cases may require medical treatment, many people find significant relief through targeted exercises and ergonomic changes.

This guide covers exercises that can help relieve symptoms and prevent carpal tunnel from developing or worsening.

## Understanding Carpal Tunnel Syndrome

### What's Happening
The carpal tunnel is a narrow passageway in your wrist formed by bones and a ligament. Through this tunnel runs the median nerve (which provides sensation to your thumb, index, middle, and half of your ring finger) along with nine tendons.

When the tunnel becomes compressed—from swelling, inflammation, or anatomical factors—the median nerve gets squeezed, causing symptoms.

### Common Symptoms
- Numbness or tingling in thumb, index, middle, and ring fingers
- Pain in wrist, hand, or forearm
- Weakness in grip strength
- Symptoms often worse at night
- Dropping objects more frequently
- "Shaking out" hands for relief

### Risk Factors
- Repetitive hand/wrist movements
- Prolonged wrist flexion or extension
- Pregnancy (hormonal swelling)
- Diabetes, thyroid disorders
- Inflammatory conditions (rheumatoid arthritis)
- Obesity
- Wrist fracture or injury history

## Important Note

These exercises can help with mild to moderate symptoms and prevention. However, if you have:
- Persistent numbness
- Significant weakness
- Muscle wasting at the base of thumb
- Symptoms lasting more than 2 weeks without improvement

...please see a healthcare provider. You may need additional treatment including splinting, injections, or surgery.

## Stretching Exercises

### Wrist Flexor Stretch
1. Extend your arm in front, palm up
2. Use other hand to gently pull fingers down and back
3. Keep elbow straight
4. Feel stretch in inner forearm
5. Hold 20-30 seconds, repeat 3 times each side

### Wrist Extensor Stretch
1. Extend arm in front, palm down
2. Use other hand to gently push hand down
3. Keep elbow straight
4. Feel stretch in outer forearm
5. Hold 20-30 seconds, repeat 3 times each side

### Prayer Stretch
1. Place palms together in front of chest, fingers pointing up
2. Keeping palms together, slowly lower hands toward waist
3. Stop when you feel a stretch in your wrists
4. Hold 20-30 seconds, repeat 2-3 times

### Reverse Prayer Stretch
1. Place backs of hands together, fingers pointing down
2. Raise hands toward chest level
3. Feel stretch in back of wrists
4. Hold 15-20 seconds, repeat 2-3 times

### Finger Spread Stretch
1. Place rubber band around all five fingers
2. Spread fingers apart against resistance
3. Hold 5 seconds
4. Repeat 15-20 times

## Nerve Gliding Exercises

Nerve glides help the median nerve move more freely through the carpal tunnel. Do these gently—they shouldn't cause pain.

### Basic Median Nerve Glide
Perform as one flowing sequence:

1. **Start:** Make a fist, wrist neutral
2. **Position 2:** Straighten fingers, thumb tucked in
3. **Position 3:** Extend wrist back, fingers and thumb still straight
4. **Position 4:** Turn palm up, extend thumb away from hand
5. **Position 5:** Use other hand to gently pull thumb back

Move slowly through each position, holding 3-5 seconds. Repeat sequence 5-10 times.

### Simple Nerve Glide
1. Start with arm at side, elbow bent, wrist flexed, fingers in fist
2. Gradually straighten elbow while extending wrist and opening fingers
3. End with arm straight, wrist extended, fingers spread
4. Reverse the motion
5. Repeat 10-15 times

### Neck and Shoulder Component
The median nerve originates in the neck. Including neck movement can enhance the glide:

1. Start in position 5 of basic glide (arm extended, palm up)
2. Tilt head away from the extended arm
3. Then tilt head toward the extended arm
4. Feel gentle tension change in arm
5. Repeat 5-8 times

**Important:** Nerve glides should create a gentle stretching sensation, not pain or tingling. If symptoms worsen, stop and consult a healthcare provider.

## Tendon Gliding Exercises

These help the tendons that pass through the carpal tunnel move smoothly.

### Tendon Glide Sequence
Move through these positions, holding each 5 seconds:

1. **Straight:** Fingers and thumb extended straight
2. **Hook:** Bend fingers at middle and end joints only (like a hook)
3. **Fist:** Make a full fist
4. **Table top:** Bend fingers at knuckles only, keeping rest straight
5. **Straight fist:** Bend at all joints but keep fingers touching palm

Repeat sequence 10 times, 2-3 times per day.

## Strengthening Exercises

### Grip Strengthening
1. Squeeze a soft stress ball or therapy putty
2. Hold 5 seconds
3. Repeat 10-15 times
4. Don't overdo it—this should be moderate effort

### Wrist Curls
1. Forearm resting on thigh or table, wrist hanging off edge
2. Palm up, hold light weight (1-3 lbs) or water bottle
3. Curl wrist up
4. Lower slowly
5. 2 sets of 12-15 reps

### Reverse Wrist Curls
1. Same position, palm down
2. Extend wrist up against gravity
3. Lower slowly
4. 2 sets of 12-15 reps

### Finger Extensions
1. Place rubber band around all fingers
2. Spread fingers apart
3. Hold 3-5 seconds
4. Repeat 15-20 times

### Thumb Opposition
1. Touch thumb tip to each finger tip
2. Make an "O" shape with each touch
3. 2 sets of 10 (touching all four fingers = 1 rep)

## Self-Massage Techniques

### Forearm Massage
1. Place forearm on table, palm up
2. Use opposite hand to apply pressure to inner forearm
3. Move from elbow toward wrist
4. Focus on tight or tender spots
5. 1-2 minutes each arm

### Wrist Massage
1. Use thumb to apply circular pressure around wrist
2. Focus on both sides of wrist and palm
3. Gentle pressure—shouldn't cause pain
4. 1-2 minutes each wrist

### Thenar Massage
1. The thenar is the fleshy area at base of thumb
2. Use opposite thumb to massage in circles
3. Apply moderate pressure
4. 30-60 seconds each hand

## Daily Exercise Routine

### Morning Routine (5 minutes)
1. Wrist circles: 10 each direction
2. Wrist flexor stretch: 20 seconds each
3. Wrist extensor stretch: 20 seconds each
4. Tendon glides: 5 repetitions
5. Median nerve glide: 5 repetitions

### Work Breaks (Every 30-60 minutes, 1-2 minutes)
1. Shake out hands
2. Make fists, then spread fingers wide: 10 times
3. Prayer stretch: 15 seconds
4. Wrist circles: 5 each direction
5. Quick nerve glide: 3-5 repetitions

### Evening Routine (5 minutes)
1. All stretches from morning routine
2. Self-massage: 1-2 minutes per arm
3. Tendon glides: 10 repetitions
4. Nerve glides: 10 repetitions
5. Optional: ice wrists for 10 minutes if symptomatic

## Ergonomic Recommendations

Exercises alone won't solve carpal tunnel if you're constantly irritating the nerve. Address your workspace:

### Keyboard and Mouse
- **Wrist position:** Keep wrists neutral (straight), not bent up or down
- **Keyboard height:** Elbows at 90 degrees or slightly open
- **Mouse:** Keep close to keyboard, at same height
- **Light touch:** Don't pound keys or grip mouse tightly

### Wrist Rests
- Use during pauses, not while typing
- Wrists should float while actively typing
- Rest the heel of hand, not the wrist itself

### Take Breaks
- Every 20-30 minutes: Quick hand/wrist break
- Every hour: Get up and move
- Use reminder apps or timers

### Night Splinting
- Wearing a wrist splint at night keeps wrist neutral
- Prevents sleeping with wrists flexed (common cause of night symptoms)
- OTC splints available at pharmacies
- Wear for 4-6 weeks to see if it helps

## What to Avoid

### Wrist Positions
- Prolonged flexion (wrist bent down)
- Prolonged extension (wrist bent up)
- Pressure directly on the carpal tunnel (base of palm)

### Activities That May Aggravate
- Gripping tools tightly for long periods
- Repetitive forceful motions
- Vibrating tools (without breaks)
- Sleeping on hands

### Exercise Cautions
- Don't stretch into pain
- Nerve glides shouldn't cause tingling
- Stop any exercise that worsens symptoms
- Avoid heavy grip work if symptomatic

## When to Seek Medical Help

**See a healthcare provider if:**
- Symptoms persist more than 2-3 weeks despite self-care
- Numbness is constant (not just intermittent)
- Grip weakness affects daily activities
- You notice muscle wasting at thumb base
- Symptoms are getting progressively worse

**Treatment options beyond exercises:**
- Corticosteroid injections
- Physical therapy
- Surgery (carpal tunnel release) for severe cases

## Prevention Tips

Even if you don't currently have symptoms:

**Regular stretching:** 2-3 brief sessions daily
**Neutral wrist position:** Set up workspace properly
**Frequent breaks:** Don't type for hours without pause
**Vary activities:** Alternate between tasks
**Strengthen gradually:** Build wrist and forearm strength
**Manage underlying conditions:** Control diabetes, thyroid issues

## The Bottom Line

Carpal tunnel syndrome often responds well to conservative treatment, especially when caught early. A combination of exercises, ergonomic changes, and activity modification can provide significant relief.

**Key takeaways:**
- Start with gentle stretches and nerve glides
- Take frequent breaks during repetitive work
- Set up your workspace for neutral wrist position
- Consider night splinting for night symptoms
- Be consistent—daily exercises work better than occasional sessions
- Seek medical help if symptoms persist or worsen

Your wrists and hands are essential tools. Taking a few minutes daily to care for them can prevent months of pain and dysfunction.
