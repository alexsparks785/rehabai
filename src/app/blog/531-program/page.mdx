# 5/3/1 Program: Jim Wendler's Proven Strength Building System

Want to get stronger without burning out? The 5/3/1 program, created by powerlifter Jim Wendler, has helped thousands build lasting strength through submaximal training and patient progression. No grinding, no missed reps—just consistent progress over months and years.

## What is 5/3/1?

5/3/1 is a strength training program built around four main lifts—squat, bench press, deadlift, and overhead press—using a specific percentage-based progression over 3-4 week cycles.

### Core Philosophy

**"Start light, progress slowly."**

Wendler's approach rejects ego lifting:
- Train with submaximal weights (not grinding)
- Add weight slowly but consistently
- Never miss reps
- Think long-term (months and years, not weeks)

### The Training Max Concept

5/3/1 uses a "Training Max" (TM)—typically 85-90% of your true 1RM. All percentages are calculated from this TM, not your actual max. This builds in a buffer for sustainable progress.

**Example:**
- True 1RM Squat: 315 lbs
- Training Max (90%): 285 lbs
- All percentages based on 285, not 315

## The Basic 5/3/1 Structure

### The 4-Week Cycle

**Week 1 (5s Week):**
- Set 1: 65% × 5
- Set 2: 75% × 5
- Set 3: 85% × 5+ (AMRAP)

**Week 2 (3s Week):**
- Set 1: 70% × 3
- Set 2: 80% × 3
- Set 3: 90% × 3+ (AMRAP)

**Week 3 (5/3/1 Week):**
- Set 1: 75% × 5
- Set 2: 85% × 3
- Set 3: 95% × 1+ (AMRAP)

**Week 4 (Deload):**
- Set 1: 40% × 5
- Set 2: 50% × 5
- Set 3: 60% × 5

### The "+" Sets (AMRAP)

The final set of each main lift is "As Many Reps As Possible" while maintaining good form. This is where you push—but never to failure with bad form.

**Guidelines:**
- Stop with 1-2 reps left in the tank
- Never grind ugly reps
- Record your reps (this tracks progress)

### After Each Cycle

Add weight to your Training Max:
- **Upper body lifts:** +5 lbs
- **Lower body lifts:** +10 lbs

Then start the next cycle with new percentages.

## Setting Up 5/3/1

### Step 1: Find Your Training Max

**Option A:** Test actual 1RM, take 85-90%
**Option B:** Use a rep max calculator from a recent set
**Option C:** Start conservatively (err on the light side)

**Formula for estimated 1RM:**
Weight × Reps × 0.0333 + Weight = Estimated 1RM

Then take 85-90% for Training Max.

**Example:**
- Bench Press: 185 lbs × 8 reps
- Estimated 1RM: 185 × 8 × 0.0333 + 185 = 234 lbs
- Training Max (90%): 210 lbs

### Step 2: Calculate Your Percentages

**Week 1 weights (Training Max: 210 lbs):**
- 65% = 137 lbs → round to 135
- 75% = 158 lbs → round to 160
- 85% = 179 lbs → round to 180

### Step 3: Choose Your Template

5/3/1 has many variations. Start with the basics:

**4-Day Template:**
- Monday: Squat
- Tuesday: Bench Press
- Thursday: Deadlift
- Friday: Overhead Press

## 5/3/1 Templates

### Original 5/3/1

One main lift per day + assistance work:

**Day 1 - Squat:**
- Squat: 5/3/1 sets
- Assistance: Leg work, core

**Day 2 - Bench:**
- Bench: 5/3/1 sets
- Assistance: Pushing, pulling

**Day 3 - Deadlift:**
- Deadlift: 5/3/1 sets
- Assistance: Hip hinge, core

**Day 4 - Press:**
- Overhead Press: 5/3/1 sets
- Assistance: Pushing, pulling

### Boring But Big (BBB)

The most popular 5/3/1 template. After main lift, do 5×10 of the same lift at 50-60%:

**Squat Day:**
- Squat: 5/3/1 (3 working sets)
- Squat: 5 × 10 @ 50-60% TM
- Assistance: Leg curls, ab work

**Bench Day:**
- Bench: 5/3/1 (3 working sets)
- Bench: 5 × 10 @ 50-60% TM
- Assistance: Rows, curls

**Why BBB Works:**
- High volume drives hypertrophy
- Same movement pattern reinforced
- Simple to program and execute

### First Set Last (FSL)

After main work, repeat the first set for additional volume:

**Options:**
- FSL 5×5: First set weight × 5 sets of 5 reps
- FSL 3×5: First set weight × 3 sets of 5 reps
- FSL AMRAP: First set weight × 1 AMRAP set

**Example (Week 1):**
- Main work: 65% × 5, 75% × 5, 85% × 5+
- FSL: 65% × 5, 5, 5, 5, 5

### 5/3/1 for Beginners

Full body approach, 3 days per week:

**Day 1:**
- Squat: 5/3/1
- Bench: 5/3/1
- Assistance

**Day 2:**
- Deadlift: 5/3/1
- Press: 5/3/1
- Assistance

**Day 3:**
- Squat: 5/3/1
- Bench: 5/3/1
- Assistance

**Progression:** Add weight weekly (not monthly) since beginners recover faster.

### Building the Monolith

A challenging 6-week program with prescribed assistance:

**Highlights:**
- 3 days per week
- High squat frequency
- Specific rep schemes (widowmaker sets)
- Conditioning requirements
- Nutrition requirements (1.5 lbs ground beef + dozen eggs daily)

**For:** Intermediate+ lifters seeking a challenge.

## Assistance Work

Wendler categorizes assistance into three types:

### Push Exercises
- Dips
- Push-ups
- Dumbbell press
- Tricep extensions

### Pull Exercises
- Rows (any variation)
- Pull-ups/chin-ups
- Face pulls
- Curls

### Single Leg/Core
- Lunges
- Bulgarian split squats
- Leg raises
- Ab wheel
- Planks

### Assistance Guidelines

**Simplest Approach (50-100 reps each):**
- 50-100 reps of push
- 50-100 reps of pull
- 50-100 reps of single leg/core

**Example:**
- Dips: 5 × 15 = 75 reps (push)
- Chin-ups: 5 × 10 = 50 reps (pull)
- Hanging leg raises: 5 × 15 = 75 reps (core)

### Keep Assistance Simple

Wendler emphasizes: **assistance should assist, not dominate.** Don't overthink it. Pick basic movements, do the reps, move on.

## Progression Over Time

### The Long Game

5/3/1 adds 5-10 lbs per cycle. Over a year:

**Upper Body:**
- 13 cycles × 5 lbs = 65 lbs added to TM

**Lower Body:**
- 13 cycles × 10 lbs = 130 lbs added to TM

These are Training Max increases. Your actual strength gains will be even higher as your TM catches up to your real max.

### When Progress Stalls

If you can't hit the minimum reps (5, 3, or 1) on the + set:

1. **Reset Training Max:** Take 3 cycles back (reduce TM by 15-30 lbs)
2. **Work back up:** You'll break through the plateau
3. **Evaluate recovery:** Sleep, nutrition, stress

### The 7th Week Protocol

Every 2 cycles, test your Training Max:
- Week 7: TM × 1 rep (should be easy)
- If hard or missed: TM is too high, reset

## Conditioning in 5/3/1

### Easy Conditioning (2-3x/week)

- Light cycling
- Swimming

### Hard Conditioning (1-2x/week)
- Prowler/sled work

### Why Conditioning Matters

- Improves work capacity
- Faster recovery between sets
- Better body composition
- General health

## Common 5/3/1 Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Starting Too Heavy

**The Problem:** Ego lifting, setting TM at actual max
**The Fix:** TM should be 85-90% of true max. When in doubt, go lighter.

### Mistake 2: Skipping the Deload

**The Problem:** "I feel fine, I don't need it"
**The Fix:** Deloads are part of the program. They allow supercompensation.

### Mistake 3: Too Much Assistance

**The Problem:** Treating assistance like a bodybuilding split
**The Fix:** 50-100 reps each category. Keep it simple.

### Mistake 4: Chasing Rep PRs on AMRAP

**The Problem:** Grinding ugly reps to beat records
**The Fix:** Stop with 1-2 clean reps left. Quality over quantity.

### Mistake 5: Adding Weight Too Fast

**The Problem:** Doubling the recommended progression
**The Fix:** Trust the slow progression. 5/10 lbs per cycle works.

## Who Should Use 5/3/1?

### Perfect For:

- **Intermediate lifters:** Past linear progression
- **Long-term thinkers:** Willing to progress slowly
- **Busy adults:** Flexible scheduling, sustainable
- **Older lifters:** Submaximal training is joint-friendly
- **Athletes:** Leaves room for sport practice

### May Not Suit:

- **True beginners:** Can progress faster with simpler programs
- **Competitive powerlifters:** May need more specificity
- **Those wanting fast results:** Requires patience

## Tips for Success

### 1. Track Everything

Log your workouts, especially AMRAP reps. These track progress better than Training Max alone.

### 2. Don't Modify Immediately

Run the basic program for 2-3 cycles before changing anything. Trust the process.

### 3. Prioritize Recovery

5/3/1's submaximal approach only works if you actually recover. Sleep and nutrition matter.

### 4. Add Conditioning

It's not optional. Even easy conditioning improves everything.

### 5. Be Patient

This is a long-term program. Think years, not weeks. The gains compound over time.

## The Bottom Line

5/3/1 isn't sexy or complicated—and that's exactly why it works. Submaximal training, slow progression, and consistent effort add up to remarkable strength over time.

The program respects your body and your recovery. You're never grinding, never missing reps, never burning out. Just steady progress, cycle after cycle, month after month.

Start with a conservative Training Max. Follow the percentages. Push the AMRAP sets (but not to failure). Add weight after each cycle. Do your conditioning. Trust the process.

In a year, you'll be dramatically stronger. In five years, you'll be a different lifter entirely. That's the power of patient, consistent training.

5/3/1. Start light. Progress slow. Get strong.
