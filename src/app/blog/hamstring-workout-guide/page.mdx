# Hamstring Workout Guide: Build Strong, Flexible Hamstrings

Your hamstrings are chronically undertrained and overstretched. They sit while you work, get ignored at the gym, and then get blamed when your back hurts. Here's how to give them the attention they deserve.

## Hamstring Anatomy

### The Four Muscles

The "hamstrings" are actually four separate muscles:

**Biceps Femoris (Long Head):**
- Outer hamstring
- Crosses both hip and knee
- Hip extension and knee flexion
- Most commonly injured

**Biceps Femoris (Short Head):**
- Only crosses the knee
- Knee flexion only
- Doesn't contribute to hip extension

**Semitendinosus:**
- Inner hamstring
- Crosses both joints
- Hip extension and knee flexion
- Also assists internal rotation

**Semimembranosus:**
- Deepest inner hamstring
- Crosses both joints
- Same functions as semitendinosus

### Hamstring Functions

**Hip Extension:**
- Working with glutes
- Standing up from bent position
- Running, jumping
- Deadlift, good morning movements

**Knee Flexion:**
- Bending the knee
- Bringing heel to butt
- Leg curl movements

**Knee Stability:**
- Protect ACL from excessive force
- Stabilize knee during landing
- Critical for athletic movements

### The Dual-Joint Problem

Because most hamstring muscles cross two joints:
- When hip extends, hamstrings shorten at hip but lengthen at knee
- When knee flexes, hamstrings shorten at knee but lengthen at hip
- Need to train BOTH functions for complete development

## Best Hamstring Exercises

### Hip Extension Exercises (Hip-Dominant)

**Romanian Deadlift (RDL):**
- Primary hamstring builder
- Hinge at hips, slight knee bend
- Feel stretch in hamstrings
- Weight stays close to body
- Drive hips forward to stand

**Stiff-Leg Deadlift:**
- Similar to RDL, straighter legs
- Longer hamstring stretch
- More range of motion
- Lighter weight than RDL

**Good Morning:**
- Bar on back like squat
- Hinge forward at hips
- Stop when parallel to floor
- Stand by driving hips forward

**Cable Pull-Through:**
- Great for learning hip hinge
- Constant tension
- Face away from cable
- Hinge and stand up

**Single-Leg RDL:**
- Unilateral hip hinge
- Excellent for balance and stability
- Addresses strength imbalances
- Challenging coordination

**45° Back Extension:**
- Face down on hyperextension bench
- Hinge at hips, not lower back
- Drive up with hamstrings and glutes
- Can add weight for progression

### Knee Flexion Exercises (Knee-Dominant)

**Lying Leg Curl:**
- Classic hamstring isolation
- Machine-based, easy to load
- Full range of motion
- Control eccentric portion

**Seated Leg Curl:**
- Hamstrings in lengthened position
- Different strength curve than lying
- Excellent complement to lying curl
- Both should be in your program

**Nordic Curl:**
- Bodyweight eccentric exercise
- Extremely challenging
- Superior for hamstring strength and injury prevention
- Progress slowly—very demanding

**Stability Ball Leg Curl:**
- Feet on ball, hips up
- Curl ball toward you
- Great home exercise
- Requires coordination

**Sliding Leg Curl:**
- Feet on sliders or towel
- Bridge up, slide feet out and in
- Eccentric emphasis
- Challenging bodyweight option

### Hybrid Exercises

**Glute-Ham Raise:**
- On GHD machine
- Combines hip extension and knee flexion
- One of the best hamstring exercises
- Difficult—build up gradually

**Romanian Deadlift to Curl:**
- RDL at bottom, curl at top
- Works both functions in one rep
- Advanced combination
- Lighter weight, more control

## Sample Hamstring Workouts

### Beginner Hamstring Workout (20 minutes)

Learn the movement patterns:

| Exercise | Sets | Reps | Rest |
|----------|------|------|------|
| Romanian Deadlift | 3 | 10-12 | 90s |
| Lying Leg Curl | 3 | 12-15 | 60s |
| Glute Bridge | 2 | 15 | 45s |
| Stability Ball Curl | 2 | 10 | 45s |

**Notes:**
- Focus on feeling hamstrings stretch and contract
- Light weight, perfect form
- Master RDL before going heavy

### Intermediate Hamstring Workout (30 minutes)

Increased volume and intensity:

| Exercise | Sets | Reps | Rest |
|----------|------|------|------|
| Romanian Deadlift | 4 | 8-10 | 90s |
| Seated Leg Curl | 3 | 10-12 | 60s |
| Single-Leg RDL | 3 | 10 each | 60s |
| Lying Leg Curl | 3 | 12-15 | 45s |
| 45° Back Extension | 2 | 15 | 45s |

**Notes:**
- Progressive overload on RDL
- Mix of hip and knee dominant
- Single-leg work for balance

### Advanced Hamstring Workout (40 minutes)

High volume, challenging exercises:

| Exercise | Sets | Reps | Rest |
|----------|------|------|------|
| Romanian Deadlift | 4 | 6-8 | 2min |
| Nordic Curl (eccentric) | 4 | 4-6 | 90s |
| Good Morning | 3 | 10-12 | 90s |
| Glute-Ham Raise | 3 | 8-10 | 60s |
| Lying Leg Curl | 3 | 12-15 | 45s |
| Single-Leg RDL | 2 | 10 each | 60s |

**Notes:**
- Heavy RDL for strength
- Nordic curls for injury prevention
- High total volume

### Home Hamstring Workout (Minimal Equipment)

| Exercise | Sets | Reps | Rest |
|----------|------|------|------|
| Single-Leg RDL | 3 | 12 each | 60s |
| Sliding Leg Curl | 3 | 8-10 | 60s |
| Stability Ball Curl | 3 | 12 | 45s |
| Single-Leg Bridge | 3 | 12 each | 45s |
| Good Morning (bodyweight) | 2 | 15 | 45s |

Add dumbbells or resistance bands for progression.

## Training Principles for Hamstrings

### Train Both Functions

Critical for complete development:
- Hip extension: RDLs, good mornings, back extensions
- Knee flexion: leg curls (lying, seated, Nordic)
- Don't skip either category
- Include both in every hamstring workout

### Eccentric Emphasis

Hamstrings are prone to injury during eccentric (lengthening) phase:
- Control the lowering portion of every rep
- Nordic curls emphasize eccentric strength
- 3-4 second lowering on leg curls
- Eccentric training prevents injuries

### Progressive Overload

Build strength systematically:
- Track your RDL numbers
- Add weight when reps feel controlled
- Small increases (5-10 lbs)
- Strength protects against injury

### Training Frequency

Hamstrings recover moderately fast:
- 2x per week for most people
- 48-72 hours between sessions
- Can be trained more frequently with lower volume
- Hit both functions each session

### Mind-Muscle Connection

Feel the hamstrings working:
- Concentrate on the stretch during RDLs
- Squeeze hard at peak curl contraction
- Touch hamstrings to confirm activation
- Don't let other muscles take over

## Common Hamstring Training Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Only Doing Leg Curls

**The problem:** Ignoring hip extension exercises

**The fix:**
- RDLs should be your primary hamstring exercise
- Hip extension is the more important function
- Leg curls are accessory, not primary
- Train both movement patterns

### Mistake 2: Using Too Much Weight on RDLs

**The problem:** Rounding lower back, losing hamstring tension

**The fix:**
- Keep back flat throughout
- Weight you can control with good form
- Feel stretch in hamstrings, not lower back
- Film yourself to check

### Mistake 3: Rushing the Eccentric

**The problem:** Dropping the weight, missing eccentric benefits

**The fix:**
- 3-4 second lowering on curls
- Control the descent on RDLs
- Eccentric strength prevents injury
- Slower = better for hamstrings

### Mistake 4: Neglecting Single-Leg Work

**The problem:** Imbalances between legs

**The fix:**
- Include single-leg RDL weekly
- Single-leg curls occasionally
- Address any strength differences
- Prevents compensation patterns

### Mistake 5: Insufficient Range of Motion

**The problem:** Partial reps, missing the stretch

**The fix:**
- Full stretch at bottom of RDL
- Complete knee extension on curls
- Full range builds complete muscle
- Don't limit yourself

## Hamstring Injury Prevention

### Why Hamstrings Get Injured

Common in running and sprinting sports:
- Rapid lengthening under load
- Often weak relative to quads
- Poor eccentric strength
- Tight hip flexors limit function

### Prevention Strategies

**Eccentric Strength:**
- Nordic curls are the gold standard
- Control lowering on all hamstring exercises
- Build strength through full range
- Research shows significant injury reduction

**Adequate Warm-Up:**
- Dynamic stretching before activity
- Activation exercises
- Gradual intensity increase
- Never sprint cold

**Quad-to-Hamstring Balance:**
- Hamstrings should be ~60% of quad strength
- If quads are much stronger, injury risk increases
- Balance pushing and pulling leg exercises
- Don't skip hamstring work

**Flexibility Maintenance:**
- Moderate static stretching after training
- Address hip flexor tightness
- Don't overstretch—strength matters more
- Flexible AND strong

## Hamstrings and Performance

### Athletic Benefits

Strong hamstrings improve:
- Sprint speed (hip extension power)
- Deceleration (eccentric strength)
- Jump performance
- Change of direction
- Kick power in sports

### ACL Protection

Hamstrings protect the knee:
- Counter the pull of the quadriceps
- Reduce ACL strain during landing
- Especially important for women (higher ACL injury rates)
- Strong hamstrings = safer knees

## The Bottom Line

Building strong hamstrings requires:
- Hip extension exercises (RDLs first, good mornings, back extensions)
- Knee flexion exercises (leg curls, Nordic curls)
- Eccentric emphasis for injury prevention
- Progressive overload with good form
- Consistency—2x per week minimum

The Romanian deadlift is your primary hamstring builder. Master it, load it progressively, and supplement with leg curls and Nordic work. Your hamstrings will get stronger, your lower back will feel better, and your injury risk will decrease.

Don't be the person who only trains the muscles they can see. Strong hamstrings are the foundation of a resilient, powerful lower body.
