# Exercises for Constipation: Get Things Moving Naturally

Physical activity is one of the most effective natural remedies for constipation. Movement stimulates the muscles of the intestines, increases blood flow to the digestive tract, and helps move stool through the colon. If you're feeling stuck, getting moving might be the answer.

**Key insight:** Exercise stimulates peristalsis—the wave-like muscle contractions that move food and waste through your digestive tract. A sedentary lifestyle is a major contributor to constipation.

---

## How Exercise Helps Constipation

### The Mechanisms

- **Stimulates peristalsis:** Muscle contractions in intestines
- **Increases blood flow:** To digestive organs
- **Strengthens abdominal muscles:** Supports bowel movements
- **Reduces stress:** Stress contributes to constipation
- **Decreases transit time:** Food moves through faster
- **Massages internal organs:** Through twisting and bending

### Best Timing

- **Morning exercise:** Often stimulates bowel movement
- **After meals:** Gentle movement aids digestion
- **Regularly:** Consistent exercise prevents constipation
- **Not during urgency:** If you need to go, go first

## Quick Relief Routine (10 minutes)

For immediate constipation relief.

### Warm-Up (2 minutes)

**Walking in Place:**
- March with high knees
- Pump arms
- 1-2 minutes

**Hip Circles:**
- Hands on hips
- Large circles
- 10 each direction

### Targeted Movements (6 minutes)

**Knee to Chest:**
1. Lie on back
2. Pull right knee to chest
3. Hold 20-30 seconds
4. Switch legs
5. Pull both knees to chest, rock gently

**Wind-Relieving Pose:**
1. Lie on back
2. Hug both knees to chest
3. Rock side to side
4. Hold 30-60 seconds

**Supine Twist:**
1. Lie on back, arms out
2. Drop both knees to right
3. Look left
4. Hold 30 seconds
5. Switch sides

**Cat-Cow:**
1. On hands and knees
2. Arch back (cow), round back (cat)
3. 10 slow cycles

**Deep Squat:**
1. Feet wider than hips
2. Squat as deep as comfortable
3. Hold 30-60 seconds (hold support if needed)

### Finish (2 minutes)

**Belly Breathing:**
1. Lie on back
2. Deep breaths into belly
3. 10 breaths

**Gentle Walking:**
- 1-2 minutes

## Yoga Poses for Constipation

### Wind-Relieving Pose (Pavanamuktasana)

**Why it works:** Compresses abdomen, stimulates colon

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back
2. Pull knees to chest
3. Hug knees, rock gently
4. Hold 1-2 minutes

### Knees to Chest Variations

**Single Knee:**
1. Pull right knee to chest
2. Keep left leg extended
3. Hold 30-60 seconds
4. Switch

**Both Knees with Rock:**
1. Pull both knees in
2. Rock gently side to side
3. Massage lower back

### Supine Twist (Supta Matsyendrasana)

**Why it works:** Massages intestines, stimulates peristalsis

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back
2. Pull right knee to chest
3. Drop knee to left side
4. Extend right arm, look right
5. Hold 1-2 minutes each side

### Seated Forward Fold (Paschimottanasana)

**Why it works:** Compresses abdomen

**How to do it:**
1. Sit with legs extended
2. Fold forward from hips
3. Reach toward feet
4. Hold 1-2 minutes

### Child's Pose (Balasana)

**Why it works:** Gentle compression, relaxation

**How to do it:**
1. Kneel, sit back on heels
2. Fold forward, arms extended
3. Knees together or apart
4. Hold 1-2 minutes

### Deep Squat (Malasana)

**Why it works:** Opens hips, aligns colon for elimination

**How to do it:**
1. Stand with feet wider than hips
2. Squat deep, elbows inside knees
3. Hands together at chest
4. Hold 1-2 minutes (use support if needed)

### Cat-Cow (Marjaryasana-Bitilasana)

**Why it works:** Massages digestive organs

**How to do it:**
1. On hands and knees
2. Inhale, arch back, look up (cow)
3. Exhale, round back, tuck chin (cat)
4. 15-20 slow cycles

## Abdominal Exercises

Strengthening and moving abdominal muscles supports bowel function.

### Belly Breathing

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back, knees bent
2. Place hands on belly
3. Inhale deeply—belly rises
4. Exhale fully—belly contracts
5. 15-20 breaths

### Abdominal Massage

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back, knees bent
2. Place hands on lower right abdomen
3. Make clockwise circles (following colon path)
4. Gentle to moderate pressure
5. 2-3 minutes

### Pelvic Tilts

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back, knees bent
2. Flatten lower back into floor
3. Tilt pelvis, squeeze abs
4. Release
5. 15-20 reps

### Leg Cycling

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back
2. Lift legs, bend knees
3. Cycle legs as if pedaling
4. 30-60 seconds
5. Reverse direction

### Seated Stomach Squeeze

**How to do it:**
1. Sit on toilet (natural position)
2. Lean forward slightly
3. Place hands on stomach
4. Gently press and release
5. 5-10 times

## Walking for Constipation

### Why Walking Works

- Most accessible exercise
- Stimulates intestinal contractions
- Increases heart rate and circulation
- Natural body movement aids digestion

### How to Use Walking

**Morning Walk:**
- 15-20 minutes before breakfast
- Often stimulates bowel movement
- Warm water before helps

**After Meals:**
- 10-15 minutes after meals
- Aids digestion and transit

**Daily Habit:**
- Aim for 30 minutes total
- Can break into shorter walks

## Morning Routine for Regularity (15 minutes)

Establish this routine to promote regular bowel movements.

### In Bed (3 minutes)

**Before Getting Up:**
1. Knees to chest: 30 seconds each, then both
2. Supine twist: 30 seconds each side
3. Deep belly breaths: 10 breaths

### Standing (5 minutes)

**After Getting Up:**
1. Drink warm water (8-16 oz)
2. Hip circles: 10 each direction
3. Side bends: 10 each side
4. Standing twists: 15 total
5. Deep squat hold: 30-60 seconds

### Movement (5-7 minutes)

1. Walk in place with high knees: 2 minutes
2. Gentle squats: 10 reps
3. Walking: 3-5 minutes

### Then

- Allow time for bathroom
- Don't rush or force
- Respond to urges promptly

## Cardio for Constipation

Regular cardio exercise helps prevent constipation.

### Best Options

**Walking:**
- 30 minutes daily
- Accessible and effective

**Jogging/Running:**
- Often stimulates bowels
- Impact helps movement

**Swimming:**
- Gentle on body
- Uses core muscles

**Cycling:**
- Engages abdominal muscles
- Regular movement

**Dancing:**
- Fun and effective
- Uses twisting motions

### Guidelines

- Aim for 30 minutes most days
- Moderate intensity
- Consistency is key
- Morning exercise often most effective

---

## Lifestyle Factors

### Support Your Exercise

**Hydration:**
- Drink plenty of water
- Warm water in morning especially helpful

**Fiber:**
- Adequate fiber with fluid
- Increase gradually

**Routine:**
- Regular meal times
- Regular exercise times
- Regular bathroom times

**Don't Ignore Urges:**
- When you feel the need, go
- Delaying leads to harder stool

**Toilet Posture:**
- Squatting position is ideal
- Use stool under feet to raise knees
- Lean forward slightly

---

## When Constipation Is Severe

### Gentle Approaches

If very constipated:
- Start with gentle walking
- Knee to chest poses
- Avoid intense exercise initially
- Focus on relaxation

### Seek Medical Help If

- Constipation lasts more than 2 weeks
- Blood in stool
- Severe abdominal pain
- Vomiting with constipation
- Sudden change in bowel habits
- Unintentional weight loss

---

## Exercise for Different Situations

### Travel Constipation

- Walk as much as possible
- Do yoga poses in hotel
- Stay hydrated
- Stick to routine as much as possible

### Pregnancy Constipation

- Gentle walking
- Modified yoga poses
- Avoid lying flat on back after first trimester
- Check with healthcare provider

### Medication-Related Constipation

- Exercise can help counteract
- May need additional interventions
- Talk to doctor about options

### Chronic Constipation

- Regular exercise routine essential
- May need combined approaches
- Work with healthcare provider
- Pelvic floor therapy may help

---

## The Bottom Line

Movement is medicine for constipation. Your intestines are muscles that respond to physical activity. Regular exercise—especially walking, yoga twists, and abdominal work—can prevent and relieve constipation naturally.

**Daily constipation prevention:**
1. Morning routine with movement
2. Walk 30 minutes daily
3. Include twisting exercises
4. Stay hydrated
5. Respond to urges promptly
6. Maintain regular schedule

When things aren't moving, get your body moving first. Often, that's all it takes to get things going again.
