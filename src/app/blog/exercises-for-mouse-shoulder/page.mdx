# Exercises for Mouse Shoulder: Relief for Computer Users

"Mouse shoulder" describes the pain and tension that develops in your dominant shoulder from hours of computer mouse use. The constant small movements, static positioning, and reaching create a predictable pattern of tightness and weakness. Here's how to fix it.

## Understanding Mouse Shoulder

### What Causes It

**The position problem:**
- Arm extended forward to reach mouse
- Shoulder slightly raised
- Sustained muscle contraction for hours
- Minimal movement variety

**Muscles affected:**
- Upper trapezius (chronically tight)
- Levator scapulae (tight from raised shoulder)
- Rotator cuff (fatigued from static holding)
- Pectorals (shortened)
- Rhomboids (weakened, overstretched)

### Symptoms

- Aching in shoulder and upper back
- Pain between shoulder blades
- Neck tension on mouse side
- Trigger points in upper trap
- Worse at end of workday
- May radiate into arm

## Immediate Relief

### Quick Desk Stretches

**Upper Trap Stretch**
- Sit tall, hold chair seat with right hand
- Tilt left ear toward left shoulder
- Gentle pressure with left hand on head
- Hold 30 seconds
- Switch sides

**Levator Scapulae Stretch**
- Turn head 45° to the left
- Look down toward armpit
- Gentle pressure on back of head
- Hold 30 seconds each side

**Doorway Chest Stretch**
- Forearm on door frame, elbow at shoulder height
- Step through, turn body away
- Hold 30 seconds each side
- Stretches tight pecs

**Cross-Body Shoulder Stretch**
- Bring right arm across body
- Use left hand to press above elbow
- Feel stretch in back of shoulder
- Hold 30 seconds each side

### Self-Massage

**Upper Trap Release**
- Find tender spot in upper trap (top of shoulder)
- Apply pressure with opposite hand
- Hold 30-60 seconds
- Or use lacrosse ball against wall

**Levator Scapulae Release**
- Ball between wall and area where neck meets shoulder blade
- Roll around, find trigger points
- Hold pressure on tender spots
- 60 seconds each side

## Strengthening Exercises

### Fix the Imbalance

**The pattern:** Front muscles tight, back muscles weak

**The fix:** Stretch the front, strengthen the back

### Upper Back Strengthening

**Band Pull-Aparts**
- Hold band at shoulder height
- Pull apart, squeezing shoulder blades
- Control return
- 15-20 reps
- Do several times daily

**Face Pulls**
- Band or cable at face height
- Pull toward face, elbows high
- Squeeze shoulder blades
- 15 reps

**Y-T-W Raises**
- Bend forward or lie face down
- Y: Arms overhead at angle, thumbs up
- T: Arms to sides, thumbs up
- W: Elbows bent, squeeze back
- 10 reps each position

**Rows**
- Band attached to door or dumbbell
- Pull toward body, squeeze shoulder blade
- Control return
- 12-15 reps each arm

**Wall Angels**
- Back flat against wall
- Arms in W position against wall
- Slide up to Y, back down
- Keep everything touching wall
- 10 slow reps

### Rotator Cuff Strengthening

**External Rotation**
- Elbow at side, bent 90°
- Band attached or hold light weight
- Rotate forearm outward
- 15 reps each arm

**Side-Lying External Rotation**
- Lie on non-mouse side
- Light weight in top hand
- Elbow at side, rotate weight up
- 12-15 reps

### Scapular Stability

**Scapular Squeezes**
- Squeeze shoulder blades together
- Hold 5 seconds
- Release
- 15 reps

**Prone Scapular Retraction**
- Lie face down, arms at sides
- Lift arms, squeezing shoulder blades
- Hold 3 seconds
- 12 reps

## Daily Routine

### Morning (5 minutes)

- Neck stretches: 30 sec each direction
- Shoulder rolls: 10 forward, 10 backward
- Chest stretch: 30 sec each side
- Band pull-aparts: 15 reps
- Arm circles: 10 each direction

### Every Hour at Desk

- Shoulder rolls: 10
- Shoulder blade squeezes: 10
- Neck stretch: 15 sec each side
- Stand and move: 1 minute

### End of Workday (10 minutes)

**Stretching:**
- Upper trap stretch: 45 sec each
- Levator scapulae stretch: 45 sec each
- Doorway chest stretch: 45 sec each
- Cross-body stretch: 30 sec each

**Self-massage:**
- Upper trap release: 60 sec each
- Between shoulder blades: 60 sec

**Strengthening:**
- Band pull-aparts: 20 reps
- External rotation: 12 each
- Wall angels: 10 reps

## Ergonomic Fixes

### Mouse Position

**Problems:**
- Mouse too far away (reaching)
- Mouse too high (shoulder raised)
- Mouse not in line with keyboard

**Solutions:**
- Mouse close to body (elbow at 90°)
- Mouse at same height as keyboard
- Wrist neutral, not bent
- Consider mouse pad with wrist rest

### Alternative Mice

**Options to reduce strain:**
- Vertical mouse (more natural forearm position)
- Trackball (fingers move, not arm)
- Trackpad (vary movement type)
- Switch hands periodically
- Use keyboard shortcuts more

### Desk Setup

- Keyboard and mouse at elbow height
- Elbows at 90° or slightly more
- Shoulders relaxed, not raised
- Monitor at eye level
- Armrests that support forearms

## The 20-20-20 Plus Rule

**Every 20 minutes:**
- Look 20 feet away for 20 seconds (for eyes)
- **Plus:** Roll shoulders, squeeze shoulder blades

**Every hour:**
- Stand up
- Brief stretch or movement break
- Reset posture

## Progress Expectations

**Week 1-2:**
- More awareness of posture
- Some relief from stretching
- May feel new muscles working

**Week 3-4:**
- Noticeable reduction in end-of-day pain
- Better posture awareness
- Stretches and exercises becoming habit

**Month 2+:**
- Significant improvement
- Prevention mindset
- New baseline of shoulder health

## Key Takeaways

**The mouse shoulder formula:**
- **Stretch:** Upper trap, levator scap, chest
- **Strengthen:** Upper back, rotator cuff, scapular muscles
- **Move:** Every hour, vary positions
- **Setup:** Ergonomics matter

**Most important exercises:**
- Band pull-aparts (do daily)
- Upper trap stretch (multiple times daily)
- External rotation (rotator cuff health)
- Wall angels (scapular mobility)

**Daily minimums:**
- Hourly movement breaks
- End-of-day stretching
- Regular strengthening (3x/week minimum)
- Proper ergonomics always

Your shoulder pain is a signal that something needs to change. The exercises fix the symptom; the ergonomics and movement habits fix the cause. Address both for lasting relief.
