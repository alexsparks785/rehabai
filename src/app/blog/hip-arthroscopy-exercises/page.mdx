# Hip Arthroscopy Exercises: Post-Surgical Rehabilitation Guide

Hip arthroscopy has become increasingly common for treating labral tears, FAI (femoroacetabular impingement), and other hip conditions. While the surgery itself is minimally invasive, proper rehabilitation is crucial for optimal outcomes.

**Key insight:** Recovery typically takes 4-6 months, with full return to high-level sports requiring 6-12 months. Rushing rehabilitation increases the risk of re-injury and poor outcomes.

---

## Understanding Your Surgery

### Common Procedures

Different procedures have different recovery timelines:

| Procedure | Description | Timeline Impact |
|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Labral debridement** | Trimming damaged labral tissue | Faster recovery (3-4 months) |
| **Labral repair** | Suturing labrum back to bone | Longer protection (4-6 months) |
| **FAI correction** | Reshaping bone (cam/pincer) | Standard timeline |
| **Microfracture** | Stimulating cartilage regrowth | Extended protection (6+ months) |

**Critical:** Follow your surgeon's specific protocol. These general guidelines may need modification based on your procedure.

### What Was Protected/Repaired

Understanding surgical restrictions:
- **Labral repair:** Limit hip flexion beyond 90° for 4-6 weeks
- **Capsular repair:** Restrict external rotation initially
- **Microfracture:** Extended period of protected weight-bearing

## Phase 1: Protection (Weeks 0-2)

### Goals
- Protect surgical repair
- Control pain and swelling
- Prevent blood clots
- Maintain gentle mobility

### Exercises

#### Ankle Pumps
Essential for circulation and preventing blood clots.

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on your back with leg extended
2. Pump ankle up (toes toward you) and down (toes pointing away)
3. Perform 20-30 repetitions every hour while awake

#### Quad Sets
Maintain quad activation without hip movement.

**How to do it:**
1. Lie with leg straight
2. Tighten thigh muscle, pressing back of knee toward surface
3. Hold 5-10 seconds
4. Relax completely
5. Perform 10-20 repetitions, 3-4 times daily

#### Gluteal Squeezes
Activate glutes safely.

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on your back
2. Squeeze buttock muscles together
3. Hold 5-10 seconds
4. Relax
5. Perform 10-20 repetitions, 3-4 times daily

#### Heel Slides (if allowed)
Gentle hip flexion within permitted range.

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on your back
2. Slowly slide heel toward buttock
3. Only go to prescribed angle (often 90° max)
4. Slide back to start
5. Perform 10-15 repetitions, 2-3 times daily

**Precaution:** Your surgeon may restrict this initially. Confirm your specific hip flexion limits.

#### Supine Hip Abduction (Log Roll)
Gentle abductor activation.

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on your back with legs straight
2. Roll entire leg outward like a log
3. Roll back to center
4. Perform 10-15 repetitions, 2-3 times daily

### Phase 1 Precautions
- Use crutches as directed (typically 2-4 weeks)
- Follow weight-bearing restrictions
- No active hip flexion against gravity
- Avoid external rotation past neutral
- Ice 20 minutes, 3-4 times daily
- Sleep with pillow between knees

## Phase 2: Early Motion (Weeks 2-6)

### Goals
- Restore passive and active-assisted range of motion
- Begin weight-bearing progression
- Start gentle strengthening
- Normalize gait pattern

### Exercises

#### Passive Hip Flexion (Supine)
Gradually increase hip flexion.

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on your back
2. Use your hands or a strap to lift knee toward chest
3. Progress range as permitted (week by week)
4. Hold gently at end range for 10-15 seconds
5. Perform 10 repetitions, 2-3 times daily

#### Supine Hip Internal/External Rotation
Restore rotational motion.

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back with knee bent, foot flat
2. Let knee fall inward (external rotation of hip)
3. Return to center
4. Let knee fall outward (internal rotation of hip)
5. Keep pelvis stable
6. Perform 10-15 repetitions each direction

**Note:** External rotation may be restricted if capsule was repaired.

#### Prone Lying (Hip Extension Stretch)
Combat hip flexor tightness from sitting.

**How to do it:**
1. Lie face down with pillow under hips if needed
2. Simply hold this position to stretch hip flexors
3. Start with 5-10 minutes, progress to 20-30 minutes
4. Perform 1-2 times daily

#### Seated Marching
Early hip flexor strengthening.

**How to do it:**
1. Sit in chair with good posture
2. Slowly lift knee toward ceiling
3. Only lift within pain-free range
4. Lower slowly
5. Perform 10-15 repetitions each leg

#### Standing Weight Shifts
Progress to single-leg weight bearing.

**How to do it:**
1. Stand with hands on counter for balance
2. Shift weight side to side
3. Progress to lifting opposite foot slightly off ground
4. Hold 5-10 seconds
5. Perform 10 repetitions each side

#### Stationary Bike (When Cleared)
Usually allowed around week 3-4.

**How to do it:**
1. Raise seat high to limit hip flexion
2. Use minimal resistance
3. Pedal smoothly
4. Start with 5-10 minutes
5. Progress duration before adding resistance

### Gait Training
- Wean from crutches per surgeon's protocol (usually weeks 3-4)
- Focus on smooth heel-to-toe pattern
- Avoid hip hiking or Trendelenburg gait
- Use mirror for visual feedback

## Phase 3: Progressive Strengthening (Weeks 6-12)

### Goals
- Build hip strength
- Improve single-leg stability
- Restore full range of motion
- Progress functional activities

### Exercises

#### Prone Hip Extension
Strengthen glutes without hip flexion.

**How to do it:**
1. Lie face down
2. Squeeze glute and lift straight leg off surface
3. Keep knee straight, don't arch low back
4. Hold 2-3 seconds
5. Lower slowly
6. Perform 15-20 repetitions, 2-3 sets

#### Side-Lying Hip Abduction
Target gluteus medius.

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on uninvolved side
2. Keep body straight (not jackknifed)
3. Lift top leg toward ceiling (about 45°)
4. Keep toes pointed forward or slightly down
5. Lower slowly
6. Perform 15-20 repetitions, 2-3 sets

#### Clamshells (When Cleared)
Typically allowed once external rotation restrictions are lifted.

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on side with knees bent at 45°
2. Keep feet together
3. Lift top knee toward ceiling
4. Don't let pelvis roll backward
5. Lower slowly
6. Perform 15-20 repetitions, 2-3 sets

#### Standing Hip Flexion
Active hip flexion against gravity.

**How to do it:**
1. Stand holding support
2. Lift knee toward chest
3. Keep standing leg straight
4. Lower with control
5. Perform 15-20 repetitions, 2-3 sets

#### Mini Squats
Begin loading through the hip.

**How to do it:**
1. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart
2. Squat down 45-60 degrees
3. Keep weight through heels
4. Don't let knees cave inward
5. Stand back up
6. Perform 15-20 repetitions, 2-3 sets

#### Bridging
Progress glute strengthening.

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back with knees bent
2. Squeeze glutes and lift hips off surface
3. Create straight line from shoulders to knees
4. Hold 3-5 seconds
5. Lower slowly
6. Perform 15-20 repetitions, 2-3 sets

**Progression:** Single-leg bridge when double-leg becomes easy.

#### Step-Ups
Functional strengthening.

**How to do it:**
1. Start with low step (4-6 inches)
2. Place surgical leg on step
3. Press through heel to stand up
4. Don't push off with back leg
5. Lower slowly
6. Perform 10-15 repetitions, 2-3 sets

**Progression:** Increase step height as strength improves.

#### Single-Leg Balance
Improve proprioception.

**How to do it:**
1. Stand on surgical leg
2. Hold for 30-60 seconds
3. Keep hip level (no dropping)
4. Progress by closing eyes, unstable surface, or adding movements

## Phase 4: Advanced Strengthening (Weeks 12-20)

### Goals
- Build sport-specific strength
- Progress to higher-level activities
- Prepare for return to running
- Develop power and endurance

### Exercises

#### Single-Leg Romanian Deadlift
Hip hinge pattern with single-leg stability.

**How to do it:**
1. Stand on surgical leg
2. Hinge at hip, reaching opposite leg back
3. Keep back flat, slight knee bend
4. Feel stretch in standing leg hamstring
5. Return to standing
6. Perform 10-12 repetitions, 2-3 sets

#### Lateral Band Walks
Strengthen hip abductors under load.

**How to do it:**
1. Place resistance band around ankles or above knees
2. Assume athletic stance (knees slightly bent)
3. Step sideways maintaining tension on band
4. Don't let knees cave inward
5. Perform 15-20 steps each direction, 2-3 sets

#### Bulgarian Split Squat
Advanced single-leg strengthening.

**How to do it:**
1. Place rear foot on bench behind you
2. Lower down into lunge position
3. Keep front knee over ankle
4. Drive through front heel to stand
5. Perform 10-12 repetitions each leg, 2-3 sets

#### Hip Airplane
Rotational control in single-leg stance.

**How to do it:**
1. Stand on surgical leg, hinge forward slightly
2. Rotate trunk and pelvis toward standing leg
3. Then rotate away (opposite direction)
4. Maintain balance throughout
5. Perform 8-10 rotations each direction, 2 sets

#### Single-Leg Squat (Pistol Progression)
Ultimate single-leg strength.

**How to do it:**
1. Stand on one leg
2. Squat down on single leg
3. Control descent (don't collapse)
4. Stand back up
5. Start with box squat, progress to full depth
6. Perform 8-10 repetitions, 2-3 sets

## Phase 5: Return to Sport (Weeks 20+)

### Goals
- Sport-specific training
- Plyometric preparation
- Running progression
- Confidence building

### Running Progression

**Week 1:**
- Walk 5 min, jog 1 min × 5
- Every other day

**Week 2:**
- Walk 4 min, jog 2 min × 5
- Every other day

**Week 3:**
- Walk 3 min, jog 3 min × 5
- Every other day

**Week 4:**
- Walk 2 min, jog 4 min × 5
- Every other day

**Weeks 5-8:**
- Continue increasing jog intervals
- Progress to continuous running
- Add distance before speed

### Plyometric Progression

**Level 1: (Weeks 16-18)**
- Double-leg hops in place
- Squat jumps with soft landing
- Box step-ups with hop

**Level 2: (Weeks 18-20)**
- Forward/backward hops
- Lateral hops
- Box jumps (low height)

**Level 3: (Weeks 20+)**
- Single-leg hops
- Bounding
- Sport-specific cutting

### Return to Sport Criteria

Before returning to full sport participation:

| Test | Minimum Score |
|------|---------------|
| Single-leg squat symmetry | &gt;90% |
| Hop tests (4 tests) | &gt;90% |
| Pain-free with all activities | Yes |
| Full range of motion | Yes |
| Isokinetic strength testing | &gt;85% |
| Sport-specific drills | Pain-free |

## Special Considerations

### Labral Repair Patients
- Stricter hip flexion limits (often &lt;90° for 4-6 weeks)
- Longer time to full loading
- May need hip flexion restrictor brace

### Microfracture Patients
- Extended protected weight bearing (6-8 weeks)
- Continuous passive motion (CPM) machine often prescribed
- Longer overall recovery (9-12 months)

### FAI Patients
- Watch for impingement positions during rehab
- May have more flexibility once bone is reshaped
- Still need to build strength and control

### Bilateral Surgery
- Staged surgeries recommended (one hip at a time)
- First hip should be well-rehabilitated before second surgery
- Allow 4-6 months between procedures

---

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

### Persistent Hip Flexor Tightness
- Spend more time in prone position
- Add Thomas stretch when cleared
- Avoid sitting for prolonged periods

### Glute Weakness
- Focus on activation before strengthening
- Use external cues (don't let hip drop)
- Consider electrical stimulation

### Clicking/Catching
- May be normal soft tissue healing
- Report to surgeon if painful
- Usually resolves by 6 months

### Anterior Hip Pain
- May indicate inflammation
- Ice after exercise
- Don't push through sharp pain

## Key Takeaways

1. **Follow your surgeon's protocol** — These are general guidelines; your surgery may require modifications
2. **Don't rush** — Labral repairs and microfracture need time to heal
3. **Build foundation first** — Gluteal weakness is common; address it thoroughly
4. **Progress systematically** — Meet criteria before advancing phases
5. **Return to sport is a process** — 6-12 months is typical for high-level athletes

**Timeline summary:**
- Phase 1 (Weeks 0-2): Protection
- Phase 2 (Weeks 2-6): Early motion
- Phase 3 (Weeks 6-12): Progressive strengthening
- Phase 4 (Weeks 12-20): Advanced strengthening
- Phase 5 (Weeks 20+): Return to sport

Hip arthroscopy recovery requires patience and consistent effort. Work closely with your surgeon and physical therapist to ensure the best possible outcome.
