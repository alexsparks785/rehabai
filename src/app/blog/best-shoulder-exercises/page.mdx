# Best Shoulder Exercises for All Three Heads

Well-developed shoulders create width, improve posture, and give your physique a powerful, athletic look. But complete shoulder development requires training all three heads—not just pressing. Here's how to build rounded, strong shoulders from every angle.

## Shoulder Anatomy Basics

### The Three Deltoid Heads

**Anterior (Front) Deltoid**:
- Location: Front of shoulder
- Function: Shoulder flexion, horizontal adduction
- Trained by: Pressing, front raises, chest pressing

**Lateral (Side) Deltoid**:
- Location: Side of shoulder
- Function: Shoulder abduction (raising arm to side)
- Trained by: Lateral raises, upright rows
- Creates shoulder width and "capped" look

**Posterior (Rear) Deltoid**:
- Location: Back of shoulder
- Function: Shoulder extension, external rotation
- Trained by: Reverse flyes, face pulls, rows
- Often neglected but critical for balanced development

### Training Implication

Most pressing movements hit front delts heavily. Side and rear delts need direct work for complete development.

## The Best Shoulder Exercises

### Pressing Movements (Compound)

#### 1. Overhead Press (Barbell)

**The Foundation of Shoulder Strength**

**Why It's Essential**:
- Best overall shoulder mass builder
- Heavy loading possible
- Builds pressing strength
- Trains core stability

**Proper Form**:
1. Bar at collarbone, just outside shoulders
2. Brace core, squeeze glutes
3. Press bar straight up, moving head back slightly
4. Lock out overhead, bar over mid-foot
5. Lower with control

**Sets/Reps**: 3-4 sets of 5-8 reps for strength/mass

**Tip**: Avoid excessive back arch—stay tight and controlled.

#### 2. Dumbbell Shoulder Press

**Excellent Alternative to Barbell**

**Why It's Effective**:
- Greater range of motion
- Each arm works independently
- Easier on shoulders for many people
- Standing or seated options

**Proper Form**:
1. Dumbbells at shoulder height, palms forward
2. Press up, bringing dumbbells together at top
3. Lower with control to shoulder height
4. Don't bounce at bottom

**Sets/Reps**: 3-4 sets of 8-12 reps

**Variations**:
- Seated: More isolated, can go heavier
- Arnold press: Added rotation, more complete delt activation

#### 3. Arnold Press

**Unique Rotational Movement**

**Why It's Useful**:
- Full range of motion with rotation
- Hits all three heads somewhat
- Time under tension
- Variety from standard pressing

**Proper Form**:
1. Start with dumbbells at shoulder, palms facing you
2. Press up while rotating palms forward
3. End with palms facing away at top
4. Reverse motion on the way down

**Sets/Reps**: 3 sets of 10-12 reps

### Lateral (Side) Delt Exercises

#### 4. Lateral Raise

**The Essential Side Delt Builder**

**Why It's Essential**:
- Directly targets lateral deltoid
- Creates shoulder width
- Simple but highly effective
- Multiple variations available

**Proper Form**:
1. Stand with dumbbells at sides
2. Slight bend in elbows (maintain throughout)
3. Raise arms to sides until parallel with floor
4. Lead with elbows, not hands
5. Lower with control

**Sets/Reps**: 3-4 sets of 12-20 reps

**Key Points**:
- Light weight with control beats heavy and sloppy
- Think "pour water out of a pitcher" at top (slight external rotation)
- Keep shoulders down—don't shrug

#### 5. Cable Lateral Raise

**Constant Tension Version**

**Why It's Useful**:
- Tension throughout entire range (cable advantage)
- Easier to isolate side delt
- Less momentum possible
- Excellent mind-muscle connection

**Proper Form**:
1. Stand sideways to low cable
2. Pull handle across body and up to side
3. Pause at top, squeeze
4. Control the descent

**Sets/Reps**: 3-4 sets of 12-15 reps each arm

#### 6. Machine Lateral Raise

**Easy Isolation**

**Why It's Useful**:
- Fixed path, easy to learn
- Consistent resistance curve
- Good for higher reps/burnout
- Removes stabilization requirement

**Sets/Reps**: 3 sets of 15-20 reps

### Rear Delt Exercises

#### 7. Face Pulls

**The Most Important Exercise You're Probably Not Doing**

**Why They're Essential**:
- Targets rear delts and external rotators
- Improves posture
- Balances pressing dominance
- Shoulder health and longevity

**Proper Form**:
1. Set cable at face height with rope
2. Pull toward face, separating hands
3. Externally rotate shoulders (thumbs point back)
4. Squeeze rear delts and upper back
5. Control return

**Sets/Reps**: 3-4 sets of 15-25 reps

**Note**: Do these every upper body day. Non-negotiable.

#### 8. Reverse Pec Deck / Rear Delt Fly Machine

**Easy Rear Delt Isolation**

**Why It's Useful**:
- Simple to execute
- Isolates rear delts effectively
- Good for high reps
- Constant tension

**Proper Form**:
1. Sit facing the pad
2. Arms extended, slight elbow bend
3. Squeeze shoulder blades, pull handles back
4. Pause at contraction
5. Control return

**Sets/Reps**: 3-4 sets of 15-20 reps

#### 9. Bent-Over Dumbbell Reverse Fly

**Classic Rear Delt Movement**

**Why It's Effective**:
- No machine required
- Good stretch and contraction
- Can adjust torso angle
- Unilateral option available

**Proper Form**:
1. Hinge forward, torso nearly parallel to floor
2. Dumbbells hanging below, slight elbow bend
3. Raise arms out to sides
4. Squeeze rear delts at top
5. Lower with control

**Sets/Reps**: 3-4 sets of 12-15 reps

**Tip**: Don't use momentum. Light weight, strict form.

### Front Delt Exercises

#### 10. Front Raise

**Direct Front Delt Work**

**When to Use**: Only if front delts are lagging (usually they're not—pressing hits them hard).

**Proper Form**:
1. Dumbbells in front of thighs
2. Raise one or both arms to eye level
3. Palms down or neutral
4. Lower with control

**Sets/Reps**: 2-3 sets of 12-15 reps (if needed)

**Note**: Most people get enough front delt work from pressing. Front raises are often unnecessary.

### Compound Movements That Hit Shoulders

#### 11. Upright Row

**Controversial but Effective**

**Why It's Useful**:
- Hits lateral delts and traps
- Compound pulling movement
- Can load heavier than raises

**Form Consideration**: Wide grip with elbows staying above hands is generally safer than narrow grip. Don't pull higher than chest level. If it hurts your shoulders, skip it.

**Sets/Reps**: 3 sets of 10-12 reps (if tolerated)

## Building Complete Shoulder Workouts

### Sample Workout A (Balanced Development)

1. **Overhead Press**: 4×6
2. **Lateral Raise**: 4×15
3. **Face Pulls**: 4×15
4. **Rear Delt Fly**: 3×15
5. **Dumbbell Shoulder Press**: 3×10

### Sample Workout B (Width Focus)

1. **Dumbbell Shoulder Press**: 4×8
2. **Cable Lateral Raise**: 4×12 each
3. **Machine Lateral Raise**: 3×15
4. **Face Pulls**: 4×15
5. **Rear Delt Fly**: 3×15

### Sample Workout C (Minimalist)

1. **Overhead Press**: 4×6
2. **Lateral Raise**: 4×15
3. **Face Pulls**: 4×20

### Shoulder Training Within Push Day

**Push Day Example**:
1. Bench Press: 4×6
2. Overhead Press: 3×8
3. Incline Dumbbell Press: 3×10
4. Lateral Raise: 3×15
5. Tricep work
6. Face Pulls: 3×15 (or do on pull day)

## Training Tips for Shoulder Development

### Volume and Frequency

**Train shoulders 2× per week** (directly or indirectly)

**Recommended Weekly Volume**:
- Front delts: 0-6 sets direct (get hit by pressing)
- Side delts: 8-16 sets direct
- Rear delts: 8-16 sets direct

### Side Delt Priority

**Side delts create the "wide shoulders" look**. They need dedicated volume because:
- Pressing barely hits them
- They respond well to higher reps
- Most people undertrain them

### Rear Delt Priority

**Rear delts are almost always underdeveloped** because:
- Not visible in the mirror
- Not trained by pressing
- Often forgotten

Include rear delt work every upper body session. Your posture and shoulder health will thank you.

### Common Mistakes

1. **Neglecting lateral raises**: Pressing alone doesn't build side delts
2. **Going too heavy on raises**: Light weight, strict form is better
3. **Forgetting rear delts**: Critical for balance and posture
4. **Too much front delt work**: Usually over-trained by pressing
5. **Using momentum**: Swinging defeats the purpose

### Shoulder Health Considerations

- Warm up thoroughly before pressing
- Include face pulls for external rotator health
- Balance pressing with pulling
- If something hurts, modify or skip it
- Don't sacrifice form for weight

## Exercise Selection by Deltoid Head

### Front Delts

- Overhead press (primary)
- Front raise (usually unnecessary)
- Incline pressing (also hits)

### Side Delts

- Lateral raises (essential)
- Cable lateral raises
- Machine lateral raises
- Wide-grip upright rows

### Rear Delts

- Face pulls (essential)
- Reverse pec deck
- Bent-over rear delt fly
- Cable rear delt fly
- Rows (secondary contribution)

## The Bottom Line

For complete, rounded shoulders:

**Must-Have Exercises**:
1. Overhead press (any variation)
2. Lateral raises (side delt width)
3. Face pulls or rear delt flyes (rear delt and posture)

**Training Principles**:
- Press for overall mass
- Raises for width (side delts need direct work)
- Face pulls for rear delts and health (never skip)
- Higher reps for isolation work (12-20)
- Lower reps for pressing (5-10)

Build your shoulder routine around these foundational movements, ensure you're hitting all three heads, and develop the boulder shoulders that transform your physique.
