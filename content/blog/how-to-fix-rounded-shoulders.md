# How to Fix Rounded Shoulders: Complete Exercise Guide

Rounded shoulders—that forward, hunched position—is epidemic in our screen-based world. Hours of typing, scrolling, and sitting have pulled our shoulders forward, creating neck pain, shoulder problems, and that characteristic slouched appearance.

This guide provides the exact protocol to fix rounded shoulders through targeted stretching and strengthening.

## What Are Rounded Shoulders?

In ideal posture, your shoulders sit back with the shoulder blades flat against your rib cage. In rounded shoulder posture:

- Shoulders roll forward and internally rotate
- Shoulder blades spread apart (protract)
- Upper back rounds (increased thoracic kyphosis)
- Head often juts forward
- Chest appears concave

**The visual:** When standing relaxed, the backs of your hands should face sideways. If your palms face backward, your shoulders are internally rotated and rounded.

## The Muscle Imbalance Pattern

Rounded shoulders follow a predictable pattern called "upper crossed syndrome":

### Tight/Overactive Muscles (Pull Shoulders Forward)

1. **Pectoralis major and minor** — shortened from forward posture
2. **Upper trapezius** — overworking to compensate
3. **Levator scapulae** — chronically elevated
4. **Latissimus dorsi** — internal rotation contribution
5. **Subscapularis** — internal rotation dominance

### Weak/Lengthened Muscles (Can't Pull Shoulders Back)

1. **Lower trapezius** — critical weak link, can't depress scapula
2. **Middle trapezius** — can't retract scapula
3. **Rhomboids** — overstretched, not truly tight
4. **Serratus anterior** — can't control scapula on rib cage
5. **External rotators (infraspinatus, teres minor)** — overpowered

**The paradox:** The muscles that hurt (between shoulder blades) are usually WEAK and OVERSTRETCHED, not tight. Stretching them more makes the problem worse.

## Self-Assessment: Do You Have Rounded Shoulders?

### The Wall Test

1. Stand with back against wall, heels 6 inches away
2. Press lower back and upper back against wall
3. Can the backs of your shoulders touch the wall easily?
4. Can you touch your head to the wall without straining?

**Results:**
- Everything touches easily = normal posture
- Shoulders don't reach wall = rounded shoulders
- Head can't touch = forward head (often accompanies)

### The Hand Position Test

1. Stand relaxed, arms at sides
2. Look down at your hands
3. Which direction do your palms face?

**Results:**
- Palms face thighs (thumbs forward) = normal
- Palms face backward = internal rotation/rounded shoulders

### Mirror Assessment

1. Stand sideways in mirror
2. Drop a plumb line from your ear
3. It should pass through the center of your shoulder

**Results:**
- Ear, shoulder, hip aligned = normal
- Shoulder forward of ear = rounded shoulders

## The Complete Rounded Shoulder Correction Protocol

### Phase 1: Release and Stretch (Do Daily)

#### Pectoralis Stretches

**Doorway Pec Stretch**
1. Stand in doorway, forearm on frame
2. Elbow at shoulder height (90°)
3. Step through doorway, rotate body away
4. Feel stretch across chest
5. Hold 60-90 seconds each side
6. Repeat with arm higher and lower to get all fibers

*Key point:* Don't just lean—rotate your torso away from the arm. This creates true stretch.

**Corner Stretch (Both Sides)**
1. Stand facing corner
2. Forearms on each wall, elbows at shoulder height
3. Lean into corner
4. Hold 60-90 seconds
5. Adjust arm height to target different fibers

#### Pectoralis Minor Release

**Ball Release Against Wall**
1. Place lacrosse ball between chest and wall
2. Position below collarbone, toward shoulder
3. Lean in with comfortable pressure
4. Hold tender spots 60-90 seconds
5. Small movements to work the area

*This is often the key muscle.* Tight pec minor tips the scapula forward—you can't fix rounded shoulders without addressing it.

#### Lat Stretch

**Lat Stretch at Wall/Door**
1. Grab doorframe or pole at shoulder height
2. Step back, hinge at hips
3. Let arm straighten, body lower
4. Feel stretch along side/under arm
5. Hold 60-90 seconds each side

#### Upper Trap and Levator Release

**Upper Trap Stretch**
1. Sit or stand tall
2. Tip ear toward shoulder
3. Gently pull head with same-side hand
4. Hold 30-60 seconds each side

**Levator Scapulae Stretch**
1. Rotate head 45° (look into armpit)
2. Tuck chin and look down
3. Gentle pressure with hand on back of head
4. Hold 30-60 seconds each side

### Phase 2: Activate and Strengthen (Do 3-4x/Week)

#### Lower Trapezius (The Most Important Muscle)

**Prone Y Raise**
1. Lie face down, arms overhead in Y position
2. Thumbs pointing up
3. Lift arms off floor by squeezing lower traps
4. Hold 2-3 seconds at top
5. Lower with control
6. 3 sets of 15

*Key point:* Don't use momentum. The lift should come from your lower back, not your arms pulling.

**Wall Slide**
1. Stand with back against wall
2. Arms at 90° (goal posts position)
3. KEEP contact with wall as you slide arms up
4. Slide back down
5. 3 sets of 15

*Key point:* If you can't keep arms on wall, you're too tight. Start with less range of motion.

**Face Pull with External Rotation**
1. Cable or band at face height
2. Pull toward face, elbows high
3. At the end, rotate hands back (external rotation)
4. Squeeze shoulder blades together
5. 3 sets of 15-20

*This exercise is gold.* It trains retraction AND external rotation simultaneously.

#### Middle Trapezius and Rhomboids

**Prone T Raise**
1. Lie face down, arms out to sides (T position)
2. Thumbs up
3. Lift arms by squeezing middle back
4. Hold 2-3 seconds
5. 3 sets of 15

**Seated Row with Squeeze**
1. Row with cable or band
2. At end range, squeeze shoulder blades together
3. Hold squeeze for 2 seconds
4. 3 sets of 12-15

*Key point:* The squeeze at the end is the important part. Don't just row—retract and hold.

#### Serratus Anterior

**Push-Up Plus**
1. Regular push-up position
2. At the top, push further (protract shoulder blades)
3. Then allow shoulder blades to come together
4. Repeat the "plus" movement
5. 3 sets of 15

**Serratus Punches**
1. Lie on back, arm straight up holding weight
2. Punch ceiling by protracting scapula
3. Lower scapula back
4. Keep arm straight throughout
5. 3 sets of 15 each side

#### External Rotators

**Side-Lying External Rotation**
1. Lie on side, elbow bent 90° at waist
2. Rotate forearm up toward ceiling
3. Keep elbow at side throughout
4. 3 sets of 15 each side

**Band Pull-Apart**
1. Hold band in front, arms straight
2. Pull band apart by squeezing shoulder blades
3. Control return
4. 3 sets of 20-30

*Do these daily.* Band pull-aparts are the easiest exercise to do frequently and create significant change.

### Phase 3: Integrate and Maintain

#### Postural Awareness

**Hourly Check-Ins**
Set reminder to check shoulder position:
- Roll shoulders back and down
- Gently squeeze shoulder blades
- This is your new "home base"
- Don't hold tightly—just find the position

**The Reset**
Multiple times daily:
1. Take a breath
2. Roll shoulders up, back, and down
3. Gently engage lower traps
4. Return to activity with awareness

#### The 2:1 Rule

For every pushing exercise, do TWO pulling exercises:
- 1 set bench press → 2 sets rows
- 1 set push-ups → 2 sets face pulls
- 1 set overhead press → 2 sets pull-aparts

**Most people have this backwards.** They push twice as much as they pull, reinforcing the problem.

#### Workstation Ergonomics

- Monitor at eye level
- Keyboard close (elbows at 90°, shoulders relaxed)
- Chair supports upright posture
- Regular breaks to move

## The Daily Protocol Summary

### Morning (5 minutes)
- Doorway pec stretch: 60 sec each side
- Band pull-aparts: 30 reps
- Wall slides: 15 reps

### Throughout Day
- Hourly posture resets
- Regular standing/moving breaks
- Band pull-aparts when possible

### Evening/Workout (15-20 minutes, 3-4x/week)
- Full stretching routine
- Complete strengthening protocol
- Extra focus on lower traps and face pulls

## Common Mistakes

### 1. Stretching the Upper Back

The muscles between your shoulder blades are already OVERSTRETCHED. Stretching them more makes rounded shoulders worse. Strengthen them instead.

### 2. Only Stretching Chest

Stretching alone won't fix the problem. You MUST strengthen the back muscles, or the shoulders have nothing to pull them back.

### 3. Ignoring Pec Minor

Pec major gets all the attention, but pec minor is often the key. It tips the scapula forward. Must release it specifically.

### 4. Forgetting External Rotation

Internal rotation dominance is part of the pattern. Include external rotation exercises, not just retraction.

### 5. Too Much Upper Trap Work (Shrugs)

Upper traps are already overactive. More shrugs don't help. Focus on LOWER traps instead.

### 6. Expecting Quick Results

This pattern develops over years. Expect 8-12 weeks minimum for noticeable change. It takes consistent daily effort.

## Progression Timeline

**Weeks 1-2:** Focus on mobility and activation
- Chest stretching (daily)
- Band pull-aparts (daily)
- Learning to engage lower traps

**Weeks 3-6:** Add strengthening
- Full strengthening protocol
- Begin postural awareness
- 2:1 pulling ratio

**Weeks 7-12:** Integration
- Progress exercises
- Movement pattern correction
- Habitual posture change

**Ongoing:** Maintenance
- Continue strengthening 2-3x/week
- Daily band pull-aparts
- Permanent postural awareness

## Related Issues

**Forward Head Posture:** Often accompanies rounded shoulders. Address both together.

**Upper Crossed Syndrome:** The full pattern includes rounded shoulders + forward head + muscle imbalances. Same treatment approach.

**Shoulder Pain:** Rounded shoulders contribute to impingement. Fixing posture often helps shoulder problems.

## When to Seek Help

Consider professional evaluation if:
- Significant shoulder pain or injury
- Symptoms not improving after 6-8 weeks
- Numbness or tingling in arms
- Difficulty performing exercises correctly

## The Bottom Line

Fixing rounded shoulders requires:

**Stretch:**
- Pectoralis major and minor (the key)
- Lats
- Upper traps and levator scapulae

**Strengthen:**
- Lower trapezius (most important)
- Middle trapezius and rhomboids
- Serratus anterior
- External rotators

**Practice:**
- 2:1 pulling-to-pushing ratio
- Postural awareness throughout day
- Consistency over months

**Remember:** The muscles that hurt (between shoulder blades) are the victims, not the criminals. Stretch the front, strengthen the back.
