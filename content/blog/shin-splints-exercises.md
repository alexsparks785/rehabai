# Shin Splints Exercises: Relieve Pain and Prevent Recurrence

Shin splints — that nagging pain along the inner shin — sideline countless runners and athletes. But with the right exercises and management, you can recover fully and prevent them from returning.

This guide covers exercises that address shin splints at their source.

## Understanding Shin Splints

### What Are Shin Splints?

Medial tibial stress syndrome (MTSS) — the medical term for shin splints — is pain along the inner edge of the shinbone (tibia). It's caused by stress on the shin bone and surrounding tissues.

### Common Causes

- **Too much, too soon** — Rapid increase in running volume or intensity
- **Poor footwear** — Worn out or inappropriate shoes
- **Hard surfaces** — Running on concrete
- **Weak lower leg muscles** — Particularly tibialis posterior
- **Overpronation** — Flat feet or excessive inward roll
- **Tight calves** — Overworking the shin muscles
- **Poor running form**

### Symptoms

- Pain along inner shin during or after activity
- Tenderness along the shinbone
- Mild swelling
- Pain that starts during exercise and may ease with rest
- Pain when pressing on affected area

### When to See a Doctor

- Pain at rest (possible stress fracture)
- Severe swelling
- Pain that worsens despite rest
- Numbness or weakness
- Visible deformity

## Strengthening Exercises

### Toe Raises (Tibialis Anterior)

Strengthens the muscle along the front of the shin.

1. Stand with back against wall
2. Feet about a foot from wall
3. Lift toes while keeping heels on ground
4. Hold 2 seconds
5. Lower slowly
6. Repeat 15-20 times
7. Progress to single-leg

### Heel Walks

1. Lift toes off ground, walk on heels only
2. Walk 20-30 steps
3. Keep toes pulled up throughout
4. Rest, repeat 3 times

### Calf Raises (Eccentric Focus)

Strengthens the calf while protecting the shin.

1. Stand on step, heels hanging off
2. Rise up on both feet
3. Shift weight to one foot
4. Lower slowly (3-4 seconds) on single leg
5. Rise up on both feet again
6. Repeat 12-15 each leg

### Towel Scrunches

Strengthens foot intrinsic muscles.

1. Sit with foot flat on towel
2. Scrunch towel toward you using toes
3. Spread towel back out
4. Repeat until fatigued
5. Do both feet

### Marble Pickup

1. Place marbles on floor
2. Pick up each marble with toes
3. Place in bowl
4. Do both feet
5. 2-3 minutes per foot

### Ankle Inversions (with band)

Strengthens tibialis posterior — often weak in shin splints.

1. Loop band around foot
2. Anchor band to side
3. Turn foot inward against resistance
4. Control return
5. 15-20 reps each foot

### Single-Leg Balance

1. Stand on one leg
2. Hold 30-60 seconds
3. Progress to unstable surface
4. Add reaching movements

## Stretching Exercises

### Calf Stretch (Gastrocnemius)

Tight calves contribute to shin splints.

1. Wall stretch position
2. Back leg straight, heel down
3. Lean forward
4. Hold 30-45 seconds
5. Repeat each leg

### Calf Stretch (Soleus)

1. Same position as above
2. Bend back knee while keeping heel down
3. Feel stretch lower in calf
4. Hold 30-45 seconds

### Kneeling Shin Stretch

1. Kneel on soft surface
2. Point toes, tops of feet flat on floor
3. Sit back on heels
4. Feel stretch in shin/top of foot
5. Hold 30 seconds

### Standing Shin Stretch

1. Stand, point one foot behind you
2. Tops of toes on ground
3. Press ankle toward ground
4. Feel stretch on front of shin
5. Hold 30 seconds each leg

### Foam Rolling Calves

1. Sit on floor, foam roller under calves
2. Roll from ankle to below knee
3. Rotate leg to hit different angles
4. 1-2 minutes per leg

## Mobility Exercises

### Ankle Circles

1. Lift foot off ground
2. Circle ankle in both directions
3. 10-15 circles each direction
4. Do both feet

### Ankle Alphabet

1. Lift foot off ground
2. Draw each letter of alphabet with big toe
3. Full range of motion
4. Do both feet

### Calf Raises (Full Range)

1. Stand on step, heels hanging off
2. Lower heels below step level
3. Rise up on toes
4. Full range up and down
5. 15-20 reps

## Complete Shin Splints Programs

### Acute Phase (Active Shin Splints)

When still experiencing pain.

**Daily:**

1. **Ice** — 15-20 minutes after activity
2. **Calf stretch (gastroc)** — 30 seconds x 3 each
3. **Calf stretch (soleus)** — 30 seconds x 3 each
4. **Ankle circles** — 10 each direction
5. **Toe raises** — 15 reps x 2 sets
6. **Foam roll calves** — 1-2 minutes each

**Activity modification:**
- Reduce or stop running temporarily
- Cross-train with low-impact (swimming, cycling)
- Avoid hills and hard surfaces

### Recovery Phase (Pain Decreasing)

**Daily stretching:**
1. Calf stretch (gastroc) — 45 seconds x 2 each
2. Calf stretch (soleus) — 45 seconds x 2 each
3. Shin stretch — 30 seconds x 2 each

**Strengthening (daily or every other day):**
1. Toe raises — 20 reps x 3 sets
2. Heel walks — 30 steps x 3
3. Eccentric calf raises — 12 reps x 3 each
4. Ankle inversions (band) — 15 reps x 2 each
5. Single-leg balance — 30 seconds x 3 each

**Gradual return to running:**
- Start with walk/run intervals
- Increase running time gradually (10% rule)
- Run on soft surfaces initially

### Prevention Phase (Ongoing Maintenance)

**Before runs:**
1. Ankle circles — 10 each direction
2. Calf raises — 15 reps
3. Toe raises — 15 reps
4. Heel walks — 20 steps

**2-3x per week (strength):**
1. Eccentric calf raises — 3x15 each leg
2. Toe raises — 3x20
3. Single-leg balance — 3x45 seconds each
4. Towel scrunches — 2 minutes each foot

**After runs:**
1. Calf stretches — 30-45 seconds each
2. Shin stretch — 30 seconds each
3. Foam roll calves — 2 minutes

## Prevention Strategies

### Training

- **10% rule:** Increase mileage by no more than 10% per week
- **Vary surfaces:** Mix soft and hard surfaces
- **Rest days:** Don't run every day
- **Cross-train:** Swimming, cycling, elliptical

### Footwear

- **Replace shoes regularly** (every 300-500 miles)
- **Proper fit:** Get fitted at running store
- **Consider orthotics** if you overpronate

### Running Form

- **Increase cadence:** Shorter, quicker steps
- **Land softly:** Reduce impact
- **Avoid overstriding:** Feet land under hips

### Gradual Progression

Most shin splints come from doing too much too soon. Be patient with progression.

## Return to Running Protocol

### Week 1-2
- Walk 30 minutes without pain
- If pain-free, begin walk/run intervals
- 4 minutes walking, 1 minute jogging
- 20-30 minutes total

### Week 3-4
- Increase running intervals
- 3 minutes walking, 2 minutes jogging
- If any pain, back off

### Week 5-6
- More running, less walking
- 1 minute walking, 4 minutes jogging
- Stay on soft surfaces

### Week 7+
- Gradual return to full running
- Follow 10% rule for mileage increases
- Continue strengthening exercises

## Key Takeaways

1. **Strengthen the lower leg** — Toe raises, heel walks, ankle inversions
2. **Stretch tight calves** — Often contributing factor
3. **Eccentric calf training** is highly effective
4. **Progress gradually** — 10% rule for running increases
5. **Rest when needed** — Don't run through pain
6. **Check your shoes** — Replace worn footwear
7. **Prevention is ongoing** — Continue exercises even when pain-free

Shin splints are frustrating but very treatable. Address the underlying weakness, modify training appropriately, and be patient with recovery. Most runners can return to full activity without recurrence when they address the root causes.
