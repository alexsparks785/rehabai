# Shoulder Workout at Home Without Weights: Build Capped Delts

Building impressive shoulders at home is challenging but absolutely achievable. Your shoulders are involved in every pressing and raising motion—and with the right exercises, you can develop all three heads without any equipment.

## Understanding Your Shoulders

**Three deltoid heads:**
- **Anterior (front):** Easiest to train—pressing movements
- **Lateral (side):** Creates width—raising movements
- **Posterior (rear):** Often neglected—pulling/reverse movements

A complete shoulder workout hits all three.

---

## Front Delt Exercises

### 1. Pike Push-Up
**Targets:** Front delts, shoulders overall

1. Start in push-up position
2. Walk feet toward hands, hips high
3. Body forms an inverted V
4. Lower head toward floor
5. Push back up
6. 10-15 reps

**The king of bodyweight shoulder exercises.**

### 2. Decline Pike Push-Up
**Targets:** Front delts (more intense)

1. Feet elevated on chair or bed
2. Hips high, body in inverted V
3. Lower head toward floor
4. Push back up
5. 8-12 reps

### 3. Wall Handstand Push-Up
**Targets:** Front delts, overall shoulder (advanced)

1. Kick up to handstand against wall
2. Lower head toward floor
3. Push back up
4. 5-10 reps (or partials)

**Progress to this from pike push-ups.**

### 4. Hindu Push-Up
**Targets:** Front delts, chest, flexibility

1. Start in downward dog position
2. Swoop down (chest near floor)
3. Push forward and up into upward dog
4. Return to start via reverse path
5. 10-12 reps

## Lateral Delt Exercises

### 1. Plank to Downward Dog
**Targets:** Lateral delts, shoulder stability

1. Start in plank position
2. Push hips up and back into downward dog
3. Return to plank
4. 15-20 reps

### 2. Side Plank with Arm Raise
**Targets:** Lateral delts, core

1. Side plank position
2. Raise top arm toward ceiling
3. Lower with control
4. 10-12 reps each side

### 3. Arm Circles
**Targets:** Lateral delt endurance

1. Arms straight out to sides
2. Small circles: 30 seconds forward, 30 seconds back
3. Large circles: 30 seconds forward, 30 seconds back
4. Arms will burn!

### 4. Wall Walks
**Targets:** Entire shoulder complex

1. Start in push-up position, feet against wall
2. Walk feet up wall while walking hands back
3. Get as vertical as comfortable
4. Walk back down
5. 5-8 reps

### 5. Lateral Raises (Water Bottles/Books)
**Targets:** Lateral delts

1. Hold water bottles or books at sides
2. Raise arms out to sides, to shoulder height
3. Lower with control
4. 12-15 reps

---

## Rear Delt Exercises

### 1. Prone Y-T-W Raises
**Targets:** Rear delts, lower traps

1. Lie face down
2. **Y:** Arms at 45° overhead, lift (15 reps)
3. **T:** Arms straight out, lift (15 reps)
4. **W:** Elbows bent, squeeze back, lift (15 reps)

**Essential for rear delts and posture!**

### 2. Reverse Snow Angels
**Targets:** Rear delts, rhomboids

1. Lie face down, arms at sides
2. Lift arms slightly off floor
3. Sweep arms overhead and back
4. Keep arms off floor entire time
5. 12-15 reps

### 3. Prone I-Y-T
**Targets:** Rear delts, rotator cuff

1. Lie face down
2. **I:** Arms overhead, thumbs up, lift
3. **Y:** Arms at 45°, lift
4. **T:** Arms out, lift
5. 10 reps each position

### 4. Band/Towel Pull-Apart (If Available)
**Targets:** Rear delts

1. Hold band or stretched towel in front
2. Pull hands apart by squeezing rear delts
3. Return with control
4. 15-20 reps

### 5. Inverted Row (Wide Grip, High Pull)
**Targets:** Rear delts, upper back

1. Under a table, wide grip
2. Pull elbows out, toward armpits
3. Squeeze shoulder blades
4. 10-15 reps

## Progression Path

### Pike Push-Up Progression
1. Incline pike (hands elevated)
2. Standard pike
3. Decline pike (feet elevated)
4. Wall handstand push-up (partial range)
5. Full handstand push-up

### Lateral Delt Progression
1. Arm circles (no weight)
2. Lateral raises (light household items)
3. Side plank arm raises
4. Wall walks

### Rear Delt Progression
1. Prone raises (no weight)
2. Prone raises (slow tempo)
3. Prone raises (paused at top)
4. Inverted rows (wide grip)

---

## Making Exercises Harder

### For Pike Push-Ups
- Elevate feet higher
- Closer hand position
- Pause at bottom
- Slow tempo

### For Arm Circles
- Hold something (books, bottles)
- Longer duration
- Combine with isometric holds

### For Prone Raises
- Add tempo (slow lift)
- Add pause at top
- Hold light objects
- Elevate starting position

---

## Common Mistakes

### Pike Push-Ups
- Not pushing hips high enough
- Too much arm bend in the pike position
- Elbows flaring out excessively

### Arm Circles
- Making circles too big too fast
- Not maintaining tension
- Letting arms drop below shoulder level

### Prone Raises
- Using momentum to swing arms
- Not squeezing at the top
- Lifting too high (hyperextending back)

## Key Takeaway

Building shoulders at home requires pike push-ups as your foundation for front delts, creative lateral work like arm circles and wall walks for width, and prone raises for rear delts. Progress toward handstand push-ups over time. Train shoulders 2x per week, focus on all three heads, and don't neglect your rear delts—they're crucial for shoulder health and that 3D look. No weights needed.
