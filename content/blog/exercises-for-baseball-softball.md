# Exercises for Baseball and Softball: Build Power and Prevent Arm Injuries

Baseball and softball demand explosive rotational power, arm durability, and quick lateral movement. Whether you're pitching, hitting, or fielding, the right training program builds the athleticism you need while protecting against the repetitive stress of throwing.

**Reading time:** 10 minutes

## The Physical Demands

Baseball and softball require:
- **Rotational power** for hitting and throwing
- **Arm strength and durability** for throwing volume
- **Hip mobility and strength** for rotation and fielding
- **Core stability** for power transfer
- **Lateral quickness** for fielding
- **Shoulder stability** for arm health

## Common Injuries

Understanding injury patterns guides smart training:
- **Shoulder impingement and rotator cuff issues** - from throwing
- **Elbow injuries (UCL strains)** - especially in pitchers
- **Hip impingement** - from rotation
- **Oblique strains** - from hitting/throwing
- **Hamstring strains** - from sprinting

## Arm Care Exercises

Essential for anyone who throws.

### External Rotation (Side-Lying)

1. Lie on non-throwing side
2. Throwing arm: elbow at side, bent 90°
3. Hold light weight (1-3 lbs)
4. Rotate forearm toward ceiling
5. Lower slowly

**Sets/reps:** 2-3 sets of 15

### External Rotation (90/90 Position)

1. Arm out to side, elbow bent 90° (goal post)
2. Band or cable resistance
3. Rotate forearm up toward ceiling
4. Control the return

**Sets/reps:** 2-3 sets of 12-15

### Internal Rotation with Band

1. Elbow at side, bent 90°
2. Rotate forearm across body
3. Keep elbow pinned
4. Control return

**Sets/reps:** 2-3 sets of 15

### Prone Y-T-W-L

Scapular stability:

1. Lie face down on bench
2. **Y:** Arms 45° overhead, thumbs up, lift
3. **T:** Arms out, lift
4. **W:** Elbows bent 90°, squeeze and lift
5. **L:** Arms at sides, external rotate

**Sets/reps:** 2 sets of 10 each position

### Face Pulls

1. Band at face height
2. Pull toward face, elbows high
3. External rotate at end (hands by ears)
4. Squeeze shoulder blades

**Sets/reps:** 3 sets of 15-20

### Serratus Punches

1. Lie on back or stand with band
2. Arms extended toward ceiling
3. Push hands further (protract shoulder blades)
4. Return with control

**Sets/reps:** 3 sets of 15

### Sleeper Stretch

Internal rotation flexibility:

1. Lie on throwing side
2. Throwing arm in front, elbow bent 90°
3. Use other hand to push forearm toward floor
4. Gentle stretch only
5. Hold 30 seconds

### Doorway Stretch

Pec flexibility:

1. Forearm on doorframe, elbow at shoulder height
2. Step through doorway
3. Feel chest stretch
4. Hold 30 seconds each side

## Elbow Care

### Wrist Flexor Curls

1. Forearm on thigh, palm up
2. Curl weight toward ceiling
3. Lower slowly
4. 3 × 15

### Wrist Extensor Curls

1. Forearm on thigh, palm down
2. Curl weight toward ceiling
3. Lower slowly
4. 3 × 15

### Pronation/Supination

1. Hold light dumbbell by end
2. Rotate forearm (palm up to palm down)
3. Controlled movement
4. 2 × 15 each direction

### Band Wrist Flexion/Extension

1. Band around hand
2. Flex and extend wrist against resistance
3. 2 × 15 each direction

## Rotational Power

### Medicine Ball Rotational Throws

**Parallel Stance:**
1. Stand sideways to wall
2. Ball at hip
3. Rotate explosively, throw at wall
4. Catch and repeat

**Sets/reps:** 3 × 10 each side

**Step and Throw:**
1. Start facing away from wall
2. Step toward wall as you rotate
3. More hip involvement

**Sets/reps:** 3 × 8 each side

### Cable or Band Chops

**High to Low:**
1. Anchor high, pull diagonally down
2. Rotate through core
3. Control return

**Low to High:**
1. Anchor low, pull diagonally up
2. Rotate explosively
3. Control return

**Sets/reps:** 3 × 10 each direction, each side

### Rotational Pallof Press

1. Band anchored to side
2. Press out, then rotate toward anchor
3. Resist rotation, return
4. Challenge anti-rotation

**Sets/reps:** 3 × 8 each side

## Hip Exercises

### Hip 90/90 Stretch

1. Front leg 90° in front
2. Back leg 90° to side
3. Sit tall, lean forward
4. Switch sides (windshield wiper)

**Sets/reps:** 60 seconds each side, or 10 transitions

### Hip Flexor Stretch (Half-Kneeling)

1. Half-kneeling position
2. Tuck pelvis under
3. Lean forward gently
4. Hold 30-60 seconds each side

### Lateral Lunges

1. Wide step to side
2. Bend stepping leg
3. Other leg straight
4. Push back to center

**Sets/reps:** 3 × 10 each side

### Single-Leg Romanian Deadlift

1. Stand on one leg
2. Hinge forward, back leg rises
3. Keep back flat
4. Return to standing

**Sets/reps:** 3 × 8-10 each leg

### Hip Thrusts

1. Upper back on bench
2. Drive through heels
3. Squeeze glutes at top
4. Lower with control

**Sets/reps:** 3 × 12

### Clamshells with Band

1. Lie on side, band above knees
2. Feet together, lift top knee
3. Don't rotate hips
4. Lower with control

**Sets/reps:** 3 × 15 each side

## Lower Body Power

### Box Jumps

1. Face box
2. Quick countermovement
3. Jump and land softly
4. Step down, reset

**Sets/reps:** 3-4 × 5-6

### Lateral Bounds

1. Stand on right leg
2. Bound to left leg
3. Stick landing
4. Bound back

**Sets/reps:** 3 × 8 each direction

### Rotational Jump

1. Start facing one direction
2. Jump and rotate 90° in air
3. Land facing perpendicular
4. Alternate directions

**Sets/reps:** 3 × 6 each direction

### Split Squat Jumps

1. Lunge position
2. Jump and switch legs
3. Land in opposite lunge
4. Continuous movement

**Sets/reps:** 3 × 10 total

## Core Stability

### Dead Bug

1. Lie on back, arms up, knees 90°
2. Lower opposite arm and leg
3. Keep lower back pressed down
4. Alternate sides

**Sets/reps:** 3 × 10 each side

### Pallof Press

1. Band anchored to side
2. Hold at chest
3. Press arms out
4. Resist rotation

**Sets/reps:** 3 × 10 each side

### Plank with Shoulder Tap

1. Push-up position
2. Tap left shoulder with right hand
3. Don't rotate hips
4. Alternate

**Sets/reps:** 3 × 10 each side

### Side Plank

1. Elbow and feet (or knees)
2. Straight line
3. Don't let hips drop

**Sets/reps:** 3 × 30 seconds each side

## Sample Weekly Program

### Day 1: Lower Body Power + Arm Care
- Box jumps: 3 × 6
- Squats: 4 × 6
- Romanian deadlifts: 3 × 10
- Lateral bounds: 3 × 8 each
- Arm care circuit: 15 minutes

### Day 2: Upper Body + Rotation
- Rows: 3 × 10
- Push-ups: 3 × 15
- Face pulls: 3 × 15
- Med ball rotational throws: 3 × 10 each
- Cable chops: 3 × 10 each
- Core circuit: 10 minutes

### Day 3: Practice/Recovery
- Sport practice
- Light arm care
- Stretching and mobility

### Day 4: Lower Body Strength + Hip Mobility
- Bulgarian split squats: 3 × 10 each
- Hip thrusts: 3 × 12
- Lateral lunges: 3 × 10 each
- Single-leg RDL: 3 × 8 each
- Hip 90/90 stretch: 2 minutes
- Hip flexor stretch: 1 minute each

### Day 5: Upper Body + Arm Care
- Pull-ups or pulldowns: 3 × 10
- Dumbbell press: 3 × 10
- Prone Y-T-W: 2 × 10 each
- Full arm care routine
- Rotational core work

## Pre-Game Routine

**Arm Care (10-15 minutes before throwing):**
1. Band external/internal rotation: 10 each
2. Band pull-aparts: 15
3. Arm circles (small to large): 20
4. Sleeper stretch: 20 seconds each
5. Doorway stretch: 20 seconds each

**Dynamic Warm-Up:**
1. Jog/shuffle: 2 minutes
2. Leg swings: 10 each direction
3. Walking lunges with rotation: 10 each
4. High knees: 20 steps
5. Lateral shuffles: 20 each direction
6. Light throwing progression

## Throwing Progression After Time Off

If returning from break:

**Day 1:** 30 throws at 60 feet, easy
**Day 2:** Rest
**Day 3:** 40 throws at 75 feet
**Day 4:** Rest
**Day 5:** 50 throws at 90 feet
**Continue:** Gradually increase distance and intensity

Never go from zero to max effort.

## Position-Specific Considerations

### Pitchers:
- More arm care volume
- Focus on hip mobility
- Manage throwing counts carefully
- Extra rotator cuff work

### Catchers:
- Extra hip mobility (squat position)
- Knee strengthening
- Quick feet drills
- Throwing mechanics from squat

### Infielders/Outfielders:
- Lateral movement focus
- First-step quickness
- Sprint work
- Reactive agility drills

## When to See a Professional

Seek evaluation for:
- Shoulder pain during or after throwing
- Elbow pain, especially inner elbow
- Numbness or tingling in arm or hand
- Loss of velocity or accuracy
- Any sharp or sudden pain

## Key Takeaways

1. **Arm care is non-negotiable** - do it every day you throw
2. **Build rotational power** - med balls and cable work
3. **Hip mobility matters** - restricted hips stress the arm
4. **External rotation is key** - balance the throwing motion
5. **Train legs for power** - rotation starts from the ground
6. **Progress throwing gradually** - never go zero to max

Your arm health determines your career length. The time you invest in arm care and proper training pays dividends in durability and performance.
