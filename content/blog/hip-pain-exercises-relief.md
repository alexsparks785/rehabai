# Hip Pain Exercises: Relief and Mobility

Hip pain limits everything—walking, sitting, sleeping. Whether it's from tightness, weakness, or overuse, the right exercises can help. Here's your guide to hip relief.

## Understanding Hip Pain

### Common Causes
- **Hip flexor tightness**: From prolonged sitting
- **Bursitis**: Inflamed bursa (outer hip)
- **Labral issues**: Cartilage problems
- **Arthritis**: Joint degeneration
- **Muscle strains**: Overuse or injury
- **Impingement**: Bone contact during movement

### Where Does It Hurt?
- **Front of hip**: Hip flexors, labrum
- **Outside of hip**: Bursitis, IT band
- **Groin**: Adductors, hip flexors
- **Deep in hip**: Joint issues, labrum
- **Back of hip**: Piriformis, glutes

### When to See a Doctor
- Severe pain limiting walking
- Pain after fall or injury
- Visible deformity
- Numbness or tingling
- Pain with fever
- No improvement after 2-3 weeks

## Phase 1: Pain Relief

Gentle movements when hip is painful.

### Knee-to-Chest
1. Lie on back
2. Pull one knee toward chest
3. Hold 20-30 seconds
4. Keep other leg flat or bent
5. **Do**: 3 times each side

### Supine Hip Circles
1. Lie on back, knee bent
2. Draw small circles with knee
3. 10 circles each direction
4. Both legs

### Pelvic Tilts
1. Lie on back, knees bent
2. Flatten lower back to floor
3. Then arch slightly
4. Rock pelvis gently
5. **Do**: 15-20 reps

### Gentle Hip Flexion/Extension
1. Lie on back, one leg bent
2. Slide straight leg away, then back
3. Keep movement pain-free
4. **Do**: 10-15 each leg

### Supported Hip Rotation
1. Sit in chair
2. Cross ankle over knee
3. Gently press knee down
4. Hold 20 seconds
5. Very gentle pressure

## Phase 2: Stretching

Increase hip flexibility as pain allows.

### Hip Flexor Stretch
1. Half-kneeling position
2. Tuck pelvis under
3. Squeeze back glute
4. Lean forward slightly
5. Hold 60-90 seconds each side

### Pigeon Pose
1. Front leg bent in front
2. Back leg extended
3. Keep hips square
4. Fold forward for deeper stretch
5. Hold 60-90 seconds each side

### Figure-4 Stretch
1. Lie on back
2. Cross ankle over opposite knee
3. Pull bottom knee toward chest
4. Hold 45-60 seconds each side

### Adductor Stretch
1. Sit with soles of feet together
2. Let knees fall toward floor
3. Lean forward with straight back
4. Hold 45-60 seconds

### 90/90 Stretch
1. Both legs at 90° angles
2. Front shin parallel to body
3. Back shin perpendicular
4. Sit tall, lean into front hip
5. Hold 45-60 seconds each side

### Lying Hip Rotation
1. Lie on back, knees bent
2. Let both knees fall to one side
3. Keep shoulders down
4. Hold 30 seconds each side

## Phase 3: Strengthening

Build strength to support the hip.

### Glute Bridge
1. Lie on back, knees bent
2. Drive through heels, lift hips
3. Squeeze glutes at top
4. **Do**: 3 sets of 15

### Clamshell
1. Lie on side, knees bent
2. Open top knee like clamshell
3. Keep feet together
4. **Do**: 3 sets of 15 each side

### Side-Lying Hip Abduction
1. Lie on side, legs straight
2. Lift top leg toward ceiling
3. Keep hips stacked
4. **Do**: 3 sets of 15 each side

### Standing Hip Flexion
1. Stand tall, hold support
2. Lift knee toward chest
3. Hold 2 seconds
4. **Do**: 3 sets of 12 each leg

### Standing Hip Extension
1. Stand tall, hold support
2. Kick leg straight back
3. Squeeze glute
4. Don't lean forward
5. **Do**: 3 sets of 12 each leg

### Hip Circles (Standing)
1. Stand on one leg
2. Draw circles with other knee
3. 10 circles each direction
4. Each leg

### Monster Walk
1. Band around ankles
2. Quarter squat position
3. Walk forward with wide steps
4. **Do**: 3 sets of 20 steps

### Single-Leg Glute Bridge
1. Bridge position, one leg extended
2. Lift on single leg
3. Keep hips level
4. **Do**: 3 sets of 10 each side

## Phase 4: Mobility and Integration

### Deep Squat Hold
1. Squat as deep as comfortable
2. Use support if needed
3. Hold and explore position
4. Shift weight side to side
5. 1-2 minutes

### Hip CAR (Controlled Articular Rotation)
1. Stand on one leg
2. Lift knee, open out to side
3. Extend leg back
4. Circle back to start
5. Full controlled rotation
6. 5 each direction, each leg

### Walking Lunges
1. Step forward into lunge
2. Lower back knee toward floor
3. Drive through front heel
4. Alternate legs
5. **Do**: 3 sets of 10 each leg

### Lateral Lunge
1. Step wide to one side
2. Sit into that hip
3. Other leg stays straight
4. Push back to start
5. **Do**: 3 sets of 10 each side

## Daily Hip Routine

### Morning (5 minutes)
1. Knee-to-chest: 30 sec each
2. Hip circles: 10 each direction
3. Pelvic tilts: 15 reps
4. Glute bridge: 15 reps

### After Sitting (Every 2 hours)
1. Standing hip flexor stretch: 20 sec each
2. Standing hip circles: 5 each direction
3. Glute squeeze: 10 reps (squeeze and hold)

### Evening (10 minutes)
1. Hip flexor stretch: 60 sec each
2. Figure-4 stretch: 45 sec each
3. 90/90 stretch: 45 sec each
4. Pigeon pose: 60 sec each
5. Clamshell: 15 each side
6. Glute bridge: 15 reps

## For Specific Conditions

### Hip Flexor Tightness
Focus on:
- Hip flexor stretches (multiple times daily)
- Glute strengthening
- Reducing sitting time
- Posterior pelvic tilt exercises

### Bursitis (Outer Hip)
Focus on:
- Glute medius strengthening
- Avoid lying on affected side
- IT band work
- Reduce aggravating activities

### Hip Impingement
- Avoid deep flexion initially
- Strengthen hip stabilizers
- Work with professional for specific guidance
- Don't force into painful positions

### Arthritis
- Keep moving (gentle movement helps)
- Low-impact exercises
- Water-based exercise
- Maintain strength without aggravation

## Exercises to Avoid

### When Hip Is Painful
- Deep squats
- Heavy hip hinge movements
- High-impact activities
- Stretches that cause sharp pain

### Modify These
- Squats: Reduce depth
- Lunges: Shorter stride
- Running: Walk or pool instead
- Any exercise causing pain

## Common Mistakes

### Pushing Through Pain
Pain is information. Back off and modify.

### Only Stretching
Strength is equally important. Build hip stability.

### Ignoring Glutes
Weak glutes create hip problems. Strengthen them.

### Too Much Too Soon
Progress gradually. Hips are complex joints.

### Sitting After Stretching
Movement after stretching helps. Don't return to sitting immediately.

## Recovery Timeline

### Week 1-2
- Gentle movement and stretching
- Reduce aggravating activities
- Phase 1-2 exercises

### Week 3-4
- Progress to strengthening
- Continue mobility work
- Gradual return to activities

### Week 5+
- Full exercise program
- Sport-specific return
- Maintenance routine

---

Hip pain responds well to the right combination of stretching and strengthening. Be patient, stay consistent, and address the root cause—often weak glutes and tight flexors.

Start with the daily routine. Your hips carry you through life—take care of them.
