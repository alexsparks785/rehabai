# Exercise Modifications: Adapt Workouts for Any Level

Can't do a push-up? Make it easier. Regular squats too easy? Make them harder. Here's how to modify any exercise for your current level.

## Why Modifications Matter

### Benefits
- Train at appropriate intensity
- Avoid injury from exercises too advanced
- Continue progressing when basics get easy
- Work around limitations or injuries
- Include everyone in any workout

### The Goal
Find the version of an exercise that challenges you appropriately—not too easy, not too hard.

## Universal Modification Principles

### To Make Easier
- Reduce range of motion
- Use assistance (bands, supports)
- Change angle (incline push-ups)
- Reduce speed
- Bilateral instead of unilateral
- Remove impact

### To Make Harder
- Increase range of motion
- Add resistance
- Change angle (decline push-ups)
- Slow down tempo
- Unilateral instead of bilateral
- Add instability

## Push-Up Modifications

### Easier
1. **Wall push-up**: Hands on wall, body angled
2. **Incline push-up**: Hands on bench/counter
3. **Knee push-up**: Knees on floor
4. **Negative only**: Lower slowly, use knees to push up

### Standard
Full push-up with proper form.

### Harder
1. **Decline push-up**: Feet elevated
2. **Diamond push-up**: Hands together
3. **Archer push-up**: Wide, lower to one side
4. **One-arm push-up**: Ultimate difficulty
5. **Weighted**: Vest or plate on back

## Squat Modifications

### Easier
1. **Chair squat**: Sit to chair, stand up
2. **Assisted squat**: Hold TRX or doorframe
3. **Partial squat**: Don't go as deep
4. **Wall squat**: Back against wall, slide down

### Standard
Bodyweight squat to parallel or below.

### Harder
1. **Goblet squat**: Hold weight at chest
2. **Jump squat**: Add explosive jump
3. **Pause squat**: Pause at bottom
4. **Single-leg squat**: Pistol or assisted pistol
5. **Barbell squat**: Back or front loaded

## Lunge Modifications

### Easier
1. **Stationary lunge**: No stepping, just up/down
2. **Assisted lunge**: Hold support
3. **Reverse lunge**: Step back (easier balance)
4. **Partial range**: Don't go as deep

### Standard
Forward or reverse lunge, full range.

### Harder
1. **Walking lunge**: Continuous forward motion
2. **Weighted lunge**: Dumbbells or barbell
3. **Deficit lunge**: Front foot elevated
4. **Jump lunge**: Explosive switch
5. **Bulgarian split squat**: Rear foot elevated

## Plank Modifications

### Easier
1. **Wall plank**: Hands on wall
2. **Incline plank**: Hands on bench
3. **Knee plank**: Knees on floor
4. **Shorter holds**: 10-15 seconds

### Standard
Full plank on forearms and toes, 30-60 seconds.

### Harder
1. **Extended plank**: Arms farther forward
2. **Weighted plank**: Plate on back
3. **Single-arm plank**: One arm raised
4. **Single-leg plank**: One leg raised
5. **Plank shoulder taps**: Touch opposite shoulder

## Pull-Up Modifications

### Easier
1. **Dead hang**: Just hang from bar
2. **Scapular pulls**: Engage shoulder blades only
3. **Band-assisted**: Resistance band under knees
4. **Machine-assisted**: Counterweight machine
5. **Negative only**: Jump up, lower slowly
6. **Inverted row**: Horizontal pulling

### Standard
Full pull-up from dead hang to chin over bar.

### Harder
1. **Weighted pull-up**: Belt or vest
2. **Archer pull-up**: Wide, pull to one side
3. **L-sit pull-up**: Legs extended forward
4. **Muscle-up**: Pull over bar
5. **One-arm pull-up**: Ultimate

## Burpee Modifications

### Easier
1. **Step-back burpee**: Step feet back instead of jump
2. **No push-up**: Skip push-up portion
3. **No jump**: Step up instead of jump
4. **Incline burpee**: Hands on bench

### Standard
Full burpee: squat, jump back, push-up, jump forward, jump up.

### Harder
1. **Burpee pull-up**: Add pull-up at top
2. **Burpee box jump**: Jump onto box
3. **Double burpee**: Two push-ups
4. **Weighted burpee**: Hold dumbbells

## Glute Bridge Modifications

### Easier
1. **Partial bridge**: Don't lift as high
2. **Feet closer**: Less range of motion
3. **Hold wall**: For stability

### Standard
Full glute bridge with squeeze at top.

### Harder
1. **Single-leg bridge**: One leg extended
2. **Marching bridge**: Alternate lifting feet
3. **Banded bridge**: Band above knees
4. **Elevated bridge**: Feet on bench
5. **Hip thrust**: Upper back on bench

## Row Modifications

### Easier
1. **High incline row**: More upright angle
2. **Lighter weight**: Reduce load
3. **Machine row**: Supported movement

### Standard
Dumbbell or barbell row with proper form.

### Harder
1. **Heavier load**: Progressive overload
2. **Pause reps**: Hold at contraction
3. **Single-arm row**: One arm at a time
4. **Deficit row**: Greater range of motion

## Modification Quick Guide

| Exercise | Easier | Harder |
|----------|--------|--------|
| Push-up | Wall/Incline/Knee | Decline/Diamond/One-arm |
| Squat | Chair/Assisted | Weighted/Single-leg |
| Lunge | Stationary/Assisted | Bulgarian/Jump |
| Plank | Knee/Incline | Weighted/Single-limb |
| Pull-up | Band/Negative | Weighted/L-sit |
| Burpee | Step/No jump | Pull-up/Box jump |
| Bridge | Partial | Single-leg/Hip thrust |

## How to Progress

### Step 1: Master Current Level
Complete all reps with good form.

### Step 2: Add Volume
Increase reps or sets at current level.

### Step 3: Advance to Next Level
Move to harder modification when current is easy.

### Step 4: Reduce Volume Initially
Fewer reps of harder exercise, then build.

### Example Progression
1. Knee push-ups: 3 × 15 (master)
2. Knee push-ups: 4 × 15 (volume)
3. Full push-ups: 3 × 5 (advance, reduce volume)
4. Full push-ups: 3 × 10 (build back up)
5. Continue pattern

## Modifying for Injuries

### Principle
Find the version that doesn't cause pain.

### Common Adjustments
- **Knee pain**: Reduce squat depth, avoid impact
- **Shoulder pain**: Reduce range, change angle
- **Back pain**: Avoid loaded flexion, brace core
- **Wrist pain**: Use fists or handles

### When to Stop
Sharp pain = stop the exercise. Consult professional if pain persists.

---

Every exercise has easier and harder versions. Meet yourself where you are, challenge yourself appropriately, and progress over time.

The best exercise is the one you can do with good form at your current level. Start there and advance.
