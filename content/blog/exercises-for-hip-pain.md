# Exercises for Hip Pain: Relieve Discomfort and Build Stronger Hips

Hip pain can make walking, sitting, and sleeping difficult. Whether from arthritis, bursitis, or muscle tightness, the right exercises can provide significant relief and prevent future problems.

## Understanding Hip Pain

### Common Causes

**Hip Osteoarthritis**
- Wear and tear of cartilage
- Groin pain, stiffness
- Worse with activity, better with rest
- Common over age 50

**Hip Bursitis (Trochanteric)**
- Inflammation of bursa on outer hip
- Pain on side of hip
- Worse lying on that side
- Tender to touch

**Hip Flexor Strain/Tightness**
- Front of hip pain
- Common from prolonged sitting
- Pain with hip flexion or stretching

**Labral Tear**
- Damage to cartilage ring
- Clicking, catching, or locking
- Deep groin pain
- Often needs medical evaluation

**SI Joint Dysfunction**
- Low back/buttock pain
- Can refer to hip
- Often one-sided

**Piriformis Syndrome**
- Deep buttock pain
- Can cause sciatica-like symptoms
- Worse with sitting

### When to See a Doctor

- Severe pain after injury
- Unable to bear weight
- Visible deformity
- Fever with hip pain
- Numbness or weakness in leg
- No improvement after 2-3 weeks

## Phase 1: Pain Relief

When hip pain is significant:

### Gentle Movement

**Knee-to-Chest**
- Lie on back
- Pull one knee toward chest
- Hold 20-30 seconds
- Alternate sides

**Supine Hip Rotation**
- Lie on back, knees bent
- Let both knees fall to one side
- Hold 20-30 seconds
- Alternate sides

**Pelvic Tilts**
- Lie on back, knees bent
- Flatten lower back into floor
- Hold 5 seconds
- 10-15 reps

**Gentle Hip Circles**
- Standing with support
- Small circles with leg
- 10 each direction per leg

### Positions of Relief

**90/90 Position**
- Lie on back
- Legs on chair (hips and knees at 90°)
- Relaxes hip flexors

**Side-Lying with Pillow**
- Lie on non-painful side
- Pillow between knees
- Aligns pelvis

## Phase 2: Mobility and Flexibility

### Hip Flexor Stretches

**Half-Kneeling Hip Flexor Stretch**
- One knee down, one foot forward
- Tuck pelvis (flatten lower back)
- Lean forward slightly
- Hold 60-90 seconds each side

**Supine Hip Flexor Stretch**
- Lie on edge of bed/table
- Pull one knee to chest
- Let other leg hang off edge
- Hold 60 seconds each side

**Couch Stretch**
- Back knee on ground, foot against wall
- Front foot forward
- Stay upright
- Hold 60-90 seconds each side

### Glute and Piriformis Stretches

**Pigeon Pose**
- Front shin across body
- Back leg extended
- Fold forward over front leg
- Hold 60-90 seconds each side

**Figure 4 Stretch**
- Lie on back
- Ankle over opposite knee
- Pull bottom leg toward chest
- Hold 45-60 seconds each side

**Seated Piriformis Stretch**
- Sit in chair
- Ankle over opposite knee
- Lean forward
- Hold 45-60 seconds each side

### Adductor Stretches

**Butterfly Stretch**
- Soles of feet together
- Knees out to sides
- Gently press knees down
- Hold 45-60 seconds

**Side-Lying Adductor Stretch**
- Lie on back near wall
- Legs up wall, let them fall apart
- Hold 60-90 seconds

**Frog Stretch**
- On all fours, knees wide
- Lower hips toward floor
- Hold 60 seconds

### Hip Rotation Mobility

**90/90 Hip Stretch**
- Sit with both legs at 90°
- Front leg: external rotation
- Back leg: internal rotation
- Lean over front leg, then switch

**Hip CARs (Controlled Articular Rotations)**
- Standing with support
- Lift knee, circle hip through full range
- Slow and controlled
- 5 each direction per hip

## Phase 3: Strengthening

### Glute Strengthening

**Glute Bridge**
- Lie on back, knees bent
- Drive through heels, lift hips
- Squeeze glutes at top
- 3 x 15

**Single-Leg Glute Bridge**
- One leg extended or crossed
- Drive through planted foot
- 3 x 12 each side

**Hip Thrust**
- Shoulders on bench
- Drive through heels
- Full hip extension
- 3 x 12

**Clamshells**
- Side-lying, knees bent
- Open top knee, feet together
- Don't rotate pelvis
- 3 x 15 each side

**Lateral Band Walks**
- Band above knees
- Athletic stance
- Step sideways, maintain tension
- 3 x 15 each direction

### Hip Abductor Strengthening

**Side-Lying Hip Abduction**
- Lie on side, legs straight
- Lift top leg toward ceiling
- Don't rotate hip forward
- 3 x 15 each side

**Standing Hip Abduction**
- Hold support
- Lift leg out to side
- Keep torso upright
- 3 x 15 each side

**Fire Hydrants**
- On all fours
- Lift knee out to side
- Keep back flat
- 3 x 12 each side

### Hip Flexor Strengthening

**Standing Hip Flexion**
- Hold support
- Lift knee toward chest
- Control the movement
- 3 x 12 each side

**Seated Knee Raise**
- Sit in chair
- Lift knee toward ceiling
- 3 x 12 each side

**Dead Bug**
- Lie on back, arms up, legs in tabletop
- Lower opposite arm and leg
- 3 x 10 each side

### Functional Strengthening

**Squats (Pain-Free Range)**
- Start with partial depth
- Progress as tolerated
- 3 x 12

**Step-Ups**
- Moderate height
- Drive through lead leg
- 3 x 10 each leg

**Lunges (Modified)**
- Shorter stride if needed
- Reverse lunges often easier
- 3 x 10 each leg

**Single-Leg Romanian Deadlift**
- Balance and posterior chain
- 3 x 10 each leg

## Condition-Specific Approaches

### Hip Osteoarthritis

**Focus on:**
- Maintaining movement (use it or lose it)
- Low-impact exercise
- Strengthening without excessive loading
- Range of motion preservation

**Best exercises:**
- Swimming, water aerobics
- Cycling (adjust seat height)
- Gentle stretching daily
- Glute bridges, clamshells
- Partial squats, step-ups

### Hip Bursitis

**Focus on:**
- Reducing irritation
- Strengthening hip abductors
- Avoiding sleeping on painful side
- IT band/TFL flexibility

**Best exercises:**
- Clamshells (key exercise)
- Side-lying hip abduction
- Lateral band walks
- IT band stretching
- Avoid deep squats initially

### Tight Hip Flexors

**Focus on:**
- Stretching hip flexors (multiple times daily)
- Glute activation
- Breaking up prolonged sitting
- Core stability

**Best exercises:**
- Hip flexor stretches (all variations)
- Glute bridges
- Dead bugs
- Standing breaks every 30-60 minutes

### Piriformis Syndrome

**Focus on:**
- Piriformis stretching
- Glute strengthening
- Hip rotation mobility
- Avoiding prolonged sitting

**Best exercises:**
- Figure 4 stretch
- Pigeon pose
- Clamshells
- Hip CARs

## Sample Programs

### Acute Pain (Daily, 15 min)

1. Knee-to-chest: 5 each side, 20 sec holds
2. Gentle hip circles: 10 each direction per leg
3. Pelvic tilts: 10 reps
4. Supine hip rotation: 20 sec each side
5. Figure 4 stretch: 30 sec each side
6. 90/90 rest position: 5 minutes

### Recovery Phase (Daily, 20-25 min)

1. Hip flexor stretch: 60 sec each side
2. Pigeon pose: 45 sec each side
3. Figure 4 stretch: 45 sec each side
4. Glute bridges: 3 x 15
5. Clamshells: 3 x 15 each side
6. Side-lying hip abduction: 3 x 12 each
7. Dead bugs: 3 x 10 each side

### Maintenance Phase (3-4x weekly, 25-30 min)

1. Hip CARs: 5 each direction per hip
2. Hip flexor stretch: 45 sec each
3. 90/90 stretch: 45 sec each position
4. Hip thrust: 3 x 12
5. Lateral band walks: 3 x 15 each direction
6. Single-leg glute bridge: 3 x 12 each
7. Step-ups: 3 x 10 each leg
8. Single-leg RDL: 3 x 10 each leg

## Daily Hip Maintenance

**Morning (5 min):**
- Hip circles: 10 each direction
- Hip flexor stretch: 30 sec each
- Glute bridges: 10 reps

**Throughout day (if sitting):**
- Stand every 30-60 minutes
- Hip flexor stretch when standing
- Brief walk

**Evening (10 min):**
- Full stretching routine
- Foam rolling if helpful

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Hip flexor tightness is epidemic** — stretch daily, multiple times
- **Glutes support the hip** — strengthen them consistently
- **Movement is medicine** — keep the hip moving
- **Address the cause** — sitting, weakness, imbalances
- **Progress gradually** — stability before strength
- **Consistency matters** — daily movement beats occasional intense sessions

Most hip pain improves significantly with targeted exercise. Build strength around the hip, maintain mobility, and address the underlying causes. If pain persists or worsens, seek professional evaluation.
