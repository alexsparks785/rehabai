# Exercises for Ankle Mobility: Improve Range of Motion

Ankle mobility affects everything from squatting depth to running mechanics to everyday walking. Limited ankle movement causes compensations that lead to knee, hip, and back issues. Here's how to improve it.

## Why Ankle Mobility Matters

### For Exercise

- **Squatting:** Limited ankle mobility forces forward lean or heel rise
- **Running:** Affects stride mechanics
- **Jumping:** Impacts landing and takeoff
- **Lunging:** Restricts depth and form

### For Daily Life

- Walking on uneven surfaces
- Climbing stairs
- Getting up from low positions
- Balance and stability

### Common Restrictions

**Dorsiflexion** (toes toward shin): Most commonly limited, affects squatting

**Plantarflexion** (pointing toes): Less commonly limited

---

## Testing Your Ankle Mobility

### Wall Test (Dorsiflexion)

1. Face wall in lunge position
2. Front foot 4-5 inches from wall
3. Keep heel down, drive knee toward wall
4. Can your knee touch wall without heel lifting?

**Normal:** Knee reaches wall with heel down
**Limited:** Heel lifts before knee reaches wall

**Measure:** Distance from wall when heel starts lifting

### Squat Test

1. Attempt deep squat
2. Observe:
   - Do heels rise?
   - Does torso fall forward excessively?
   - Can you stay upright?

## Mobility Exercises

### Ankle Circles

Basic mobility maintenance.

1. Seated or lying
2. Circle ankle clockwise
3. Then counterclockwise
4. 15-20 each direction
5. Move through maximum range

### Ankle Alphabet

1. "Write" alphabet in air with big toe
2. Full range of motion
3. Both ankles

### Banded Ankle Mobilization

Most effective for improving dorsiflexion.

1. Loop resistance band around ankle
2. Anchor band behind you (low)
3. Step forward into half-kneeling lunge
4. Drive knee forward over toes
5. Band pulls ankle joint back, creating space
6. 10-15 reps, hold end position 2-3 seconds
7. 2-3 sets each ankle

### Wall Ankle Mobilization

1. Face wall in lunge position
2. Front foot close to wall
3. Drive knee forward over toes
4. Keep heel down as long as possible
5. 10-15 reps each side
6. Try to get closer to wall over time

### Elevated Ankle Mobilization

1. Front foot on small elevation (plate, book)
2. Lunge position
3. Drive knee forward
4. Elevation allows greater range
5. 10-15 reps each side

## Stretching Exercises

### Calf Stretch (Gastrocnemius)

Tight calves limit dorsiflexion.

1. Wall stretch, back leg straight
2. Lean in, keep heel down
3. Hold 45-60 seconds
4. 2-3 times each side

### Calf Stretch (Soleus)

1. Same position
2. Bend back knee
3. Keep heel down
4. Feel stretch lower in calf
5. Hold 45-60 seconds

### Standing Calf Stretch (Edge of Step)

1. Stand on step, heels hanging off
2. Lower heels below step level
3. Hold 45-60 seconds

### Downward Dog

1. Start in plank position
2. Push hips up and back
3. Press heels toward floor
4. Hold 30-60 seconds

### Deep Squat Hold

If you can get into position:
1. Squat as deep as possible
2. Hold onto support if needed
3. Work on keeping heels down
4. Hold 30-60 seconds, accumulate 2-3 minutes daily

## Strengthening Exercises

Mobility without strength is incomplete.

### Calf Raises

1. Stand on edge of step
2. Rise up on toes
3. Lower heels below step (full range)
4. 3 sets of 15

**Single-leg progression:** More effective

### Tibialis Raises

Strengthens front of lower leg.

1. Stand with back against wall
2. Feet about 12 inches from wall
3. Lift toes toward shins
4. Lower slowly
5. 3 sets of 15-20

### Heel Walks

1. Walk on heels, toes up
2. 30-40 steps
3. Strengthens tibialis

### Single-Leg Balance

1. Stand on one foot
2. Barefoot if safe
3. 30-60 seconds each
4. Progress to unstable surfaces

## Complete Ankle Mobility Routine

### Daily Routine (10 minutes)

1. Ankle circles: 15 each direction per ankle
2. Banded ankle mobilization: 2 x 15 each
3. Wall ankle mobilization: 2 x 10 each
4. Calf stretch (straight): 45 sec each
5. Calf stretch (bent): 45 sec each
6. Deep squat hold: 30-60 seconds

### Pre-Workout (5 minutes)

Before squatting or leg work:

1. Ankle circles: 10 each direction
2. Banded ankle mobilization: 15 each
3. Calf stretch: 30 seconds each
4. Deep squat with pause: 30 seconds

### Comprehensive Session (20 minutes)

**2-3 times per week:**

**Mobility (8 min):**
1. Ankle circles: 15 each direction
2. Ankle alphabet: both feet
3. Banded mobilization: 3 x 15 each
4. Wall mobilization: 2 x 15 each
5. Elevated mobilization: 2 x 10 each

**Stretching (6 min):**
1. Calf stretch (straight): 60 sec each
2. Calf stretch (bent): 60 sec each
3. Step stretch: 45 sec
4. Downward dog: 45 sec

**Strengthening (6 min):**
1. Calf raises: 3 x 15
2. Tibialis raises: 2 x 15
3. Heel walks: 30 steps
4. Single-leg balance: 30 sec each

---

## Progressions

### Week 1-2

- Daily mobility and stretching
- Focus on technique
- Note baseline measurements

### Week 3-4

- Add banded mobilization
- Increase stretch hold times
- Begin strengthening

### Week 5-8

- Increase resistance/difficulty
- Longer deep squat holds
- Test improvements

### Ongoing

- Maintenance 2-3 times weekly
- Pre-workout routine
- Address any regressions

## Integration with Training

### Squatting

Temporarily elevate heels (plate or squat shoes) while building mobility. Work toward flat-foot squats as mobility improves.

### Running

Good ankle mobility:
- Reduces Achilles strain
- Improves running economy
- Reduces injury risk

### Jumping/Landing

Adequate dorsiflexion allows:
- Better force absorption
- Reduced knee stress
- Improved performance

---

## Factors Limiting Ankle Mobility

### Soft Tissue

- Tight calves
- Tight Achilles tendon
- Tight joint capsule

**Solution:** Stretching and mobilization

### Joint Structure

- Previous injury
- Bone spurs
- Joint shape

**Solution:** May have natural limits; focus on what's controllable

### Footwear

- Stiff, supportive shoes reduce natural movement
- High heels shorten calves over time

**Solution:** Spend time barefoot; wear flat shoes when possible

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Dorsiflexion is key** — most commonly limited
- **Banded mobilization works** — most effective single exercise
- **Stretch your calves** — both straight and bent knee
- **Be consistent** — mobility improves gradually
- **Test and retest** — track progress with wall test
- **Strengthen too** — mobility without strength is incomplete

Better ankle mobility means better squats, healthier knees, and improved athletic performance. It's worth the investment of 10-15 minutes daily.
