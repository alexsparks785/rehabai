# Neck Strain Exercises: Recovery for Pulled Neck Muscle

A neck strain—often called a "crick in the neck" or "wry neck"—can make every head turn painful. Whether you woke up with sudden neck pain, strained it during exercise, or developed it from poor posture, the right exercises can speed recovery and prevent recurrence.

## Understanding Neck Strains

A neck strain involves injury to muscles or tendons in the cervical region. Common muscles affected include the trapezius, levator scapulae, scalenes, and sternocleidomastoid.

**Common causes:**
- Sleeping in awkward position
- Sudden movement or jarring
- Poor posture (phone, computer)
- Carrying heavy bags
- Stress and tension
- Exercise with poor form

**Types of neck strain:**
- **Muscle strain:** Overstretched or torn muscle fibers
- **Torticollis (wry neck):** Muscle spasm causing head to tilt
- **Tension neck:** Chronic strain from posture and stress

**Symptoms:**
- Pain on one or both sides of neck
- Stiffness and reduced range of motion
- Pain with head movement
- Muscle tightness or spasm
- Headaches (often at base of skull)
- Shoulder pain

## Phase 1: Acute Management (Days 1-3)

### Heat vs. Ice

**Heat is usually better for neck strain:**
- Relaxes tight muscles
- Increases blood flow
- 15-20 minutes, several times daily
- Warm shower helps

**Ice:** Use if significant inflammation or acute injury (first 24-48 hours).

### Gentle Movement

**Don't immobilize:** Gentle movement within pain limits speeds recovery.

**Chin tucks:**
1. Sit or stand with good posture
2. Pull chin straight back (make double chin)
3. Hold 5 seconds
4. 10-15 repetitions
5. Several times daily

**Gentle rotation:**
1. Slowly turn head to look over shoulder
2. Only go as far as comfortable
3. Hold 5 seconds
4. Return to center
5. Repeat other side
6. 5-10 each direction

**Gentle side bending:**
1. Tilt ear toward shoulder
2. Don't lift shoulder
3. Go to comfortable range only
4. Hold 5 seconds
5. 5-10 each side

### Positioning

**Sleep position:**
- Use supportive pillow (neck aligned with spine)
- Side-lying or back sleeping
- Avoid stomach sleeping
- Roll up towel under neck for support

**During day:**
- Avoid holding phone with shoulder
- Take breaks from screens
- Support neck while reclining

## Phase 2: Mobility Restoration (Days 3-14)

### Progressive Range of Motion

**Full rotation:**
1. Turn head fully to one side
2. Hold 5-10 seconds
3. Return and repeat other side
4. 10 repetitions each side

**Full side bending:**
1. Tilt ear toward shoulder
2. Full comfortable range
3. Hold 5-10 seconds
4. 10 each side

**Flexion/extension:**
1. Look down (chin toward chest)
2. Look up (carefully)
3. 10 repetitions

### Stretching

**Upper trapezius stretch:**
1. Sit, hold chair seat with one hand
2. Tilt head to opposite side
3. Gently add pressure with other hand
4. Hold 20-30 seconds
5. 2-3 each side

**Levator scapulae stretch:**
1. Sit, hold chair seat
2. Turn head 45 degrees away
3. Look down toward armpit
4. Add gentle pressure
5. Hold 20-30 seconds each side

**Scalene stretch:**
1. Sit, hold chair seat
2. Tilt head to opposite side
3. Rotate head slightly upward
4. Hold 20-30 seconds each side

**SCM stretch:**
1. Tilt head back slightly
2. Rotate away from side being stretched
3. Feel stretch at front/side of neck
4. Hold 20-30 seconds each side

### Self-Massage

**Tennis ball release:**
1. Lie on back, ball under upper trapezius
2. Apply gentle pressure
3. Hold on tender spots 30-60 seconds
4. Roll to find different spots

**Fingertip massage:**
1. Locate tight, tender muscles
2. Apply sustained pressure
3. Use circular friction
4. 30-60 seconds per spot

## Phase 3: Strengthening (Week 2+)

### Deep Neck Flexor Strengthening

**Chin tuck with hold:**
1. Lie on back, small towel under neck
2. Tuck chin gently
3. Hold 10-15 seconds
4. 10-15 repetitions

**Chin tuck with head lift:**
1. Lie on back
2. Tuck chin first
3. Lift head 1 inch off floor
4. Hold 5-10 seconds
5. 10 repetitions

### Isometric Neck Strengthening

**All directions:**

**Flexion:**
1. Hand on forehead
2. Press head into hand (don't move)
3. Hold 5-10 seconds
4. 10 repetitions

**Extension:**
1. Hands clasped behind head
2. Press head back into hands
3. Hold 5-10 seconds
4. 10 repetitions

**Side bending:**
1. Hand on side of head
2. Press into hand
3. Hold 5-10 seconds
4. 10 each side

**Rotation:**
1. Hand on side of face
2. Try to turn against resistance
3. Hold 5-10 seconds
4. 10 each side

### Scapular Strengthening

Strong scapular muscles support the neck:

**Scapular squeezes:**
1. Squeeze shoulder blades together
2. Hold 5 seconds
3. 15-20 repetitions

**Prone Y-T-W:**
1. Lie face down
2. Arms in Y, T, W positions
3. Lift and hold 3-5 seconds
4. 10 each position

**Rows:**
1. Band or weights
2. Pull elbows back
3. Squeeze shoulder blades
4. 15 repetitions

## Postural Correction

### Workstation Ergonomics

- Monitor at eye level
- Keyboard allowing relaxed shoulders
- Feet flat on floor
- Take breaks every 30-45 minutes

### Phone Use

- Hold phone at eye level
- Use headset or speaker
- Avoid cradling phone between ear and shoulder
- Limit prolonged use

### Posture Exercises

**Wall angels:**
1. Back against wall
2. Arms in goalpost position
3. Slide up and down
4. Keep contact with wall
5. 10-15 repetitions

**Chin tucks throughout day:**
- At red lights
- During commercials
- When checking phone
- Set hourly reminders

## Sample Daily Routine

### Morning (10 minutes)

1. Heat application: 10-15 minutes
2. Gentle ROM all directions: 10 reps each
3. Chin tucks: 15 reps
4. Upper trap stretch: 30 seconds each side

### Midday (5 minutes)

1. ROM exercises: 5 reps each direction
2. Chin tucks: 10 reps
3. Posture reset
4. Shoulder rolls: 10 backward

### Evening (15 minutes)

1. Heat if needed
2. All stretches: 30 seconds each
3. Self-massage: 2-3 minutes
4. Isometrics: 10 reps each direction
5. Prone Y-T-W: 10 each

## When to Seek Medical Attention

See a doctor if:
- Pain radiates into arm
- Numbness or weakness in arm/hand
- Severe pain not improving
- Symptoms after trauma
- Fever with neck pain
- Difficulty swallowing or breathing
- Symptoms don't improve in 1-2 weeks

## Preventing Recurrence

1. **Maintain flexibility** - Daily neck stretches
2. **Strengthen neck muscles** - Regular exercises
3. **Fix your posture** - Ergonomic setup
4. **Manage stress** - Stress causes neck tension
5. **Sleep well** - Good pillow, good position
6. **Take breaks** - From screens and repetitive tasks
7. **Stay active** - General fitness helps

## The Bottom Line

Neck strain recovery responds well to movement:

1. **Keep moving** - Gentle motion within pain limits
2. **Use heat** - Relaxes tight muscles
3. **Stretch tight muscles** - Upper traps, levator, scalenes
4. **Strengthen weak muscles** - Deep neck flexors, scapular muscles
5. **Fix your posture** - Root cause for many

Most neck strains resolve within 1-2 weeks with appropriate care. If you're getting frequent neck pain, address the underlying causes: posture, stress, workstation setup, and sleep position. Your neck will thank you.
