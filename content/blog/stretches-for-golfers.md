Golf demands more from your body than it appears. The swing requires rotation through your hips and spine, stability in your core, and mobility in your shoulders. When these areas are tight, your swing suffers and injury risk increases.

These stretches target the key areas golfers need: hip rotation, thoracic mobility, shoulder flexibility, and hamstring length. Use them before your round to prepare your body and after to recover.

## Why Golfers Need to Stretch

**The golf swing is asymmetrical.** You rotate powerfully in one direction, creating muscle imbalances over time.

**Modern life creates tightness.** Sitting shortens hip flexors and tightens the thoracic spine—both critical for the golf swing.

**Injury prevention.** Lower back, shoulder, and elbow injuries are common in golf. Flexible muscles reduce strain.

**Better performance.** More rotation equals more clubhead speed. Mobility directly impacts power and consistency.

## The Best Stretches for Golfers

### 1. Thoracic Spine Rotation (Seated)

**What it targets:** Mid-back rotation—essential for a full backswing

**How to do it:**
1. Sit in a chair or on a bench, feet flat on the floor
2. Cross your arms over your chest
3. Keeping your hips facing forward, rotate your torso to the right
4. Go as far as comfortable, looking over your right shoulder
5. Hold 20-30 seconds
6. Rotate to the left
7. Repeat 3 times each direction

### 2. Open Books (Lying Thoracic Rotation)

**What it targets:** Thoracic spine mobility, chest opening

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on your right side with knees bent at 90 degrees
2. Extend both arms in front of you, palms together
3. Keeping your knees stacked, rotate your left arm up and over, opening your chest toward the ceiling
4. Try to touch your left hand to the floor behind you
5. Let your head follow your hand
6. Hold 30 seconds
7. Return and repeat 5 times
8. Switch sides

### 3. Hip Flexor Stretch

**What it targets:** Hip flexors—tight hip flexors limit hip rotation and force your lower back to compensate

**How to do it:**
1. Kneel on your right knee, left foot flat in front
2. Keep your torso upright
3. Push your hips forward
4. Squeeze your right glute for a deeper stretch
5. Hold 30-60 seconds
6. Switch sides

### 4. 90-90 Hip Stretch

**What it targets:** Hip internal and external rotation—both critical for the golf swing

**How to do it:**
1. Sit with your front leg bent 90 degrees in front of you (shin parallel to your body)
2. Your back leg is bent 90 degrees to your side
3. Sit tall and lean slightly forward over your front leg
4. Feel the stretch in your front hip
5. Hold 30-60 seconds
6. Switch legs (rotate your whole body to reverse the position)

### 5. Figure-4 Stretch

**What it targets:** Piriformis, glutes, hip rotators

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on your back with knees bent
2. Cross your right ankle over your left knee
3. Reach through and clasp behind your left thigh
4. Pull your thigh toward your chest
5. Feel the stretch in your right hip and glute
6. Hold 30-60 seconds
7. Switch sides

### 6. Standing Side Bend

**What it targets:** Obliques, lats, spine lateral flexion

**How to do it:**
1. Stand with feet hip-width apart
2. Hold a golf club overhead with both hands, arms straight
3. Lean to your right, creating a C-shape with your body
4. Feel the stretch along your left side
5. Hold 20-30 seconds
6. Switch sides

### 7. Shoulder Cross-Body Stretch

**What it targets:** Posterior shoulder, rotator cuff

**How to do it:**
1. Bring your right arm across your body at shoulder height
2. Use your left hand to pull your right arm closer to your chest
3. Keep your right shoulder down
4. Feel the stretch in the back of your shoulder
5. Hold 30 seconds
6. Switch arms

### 8. Doorway Chest Stretch

**What it targets:** Chest, front shoulders—opens the chest for a full backswing

**How to do it:**
1. Stand in a doorway
2. Place your forearms on the door frame, elbows at shoulder height
3. Step one foot forward through the doorway
4. Lean forward until you feel a stretch in your chest
5. Hold 30-60 seconds

### 9. Lat Stretch

**What it targets:** Lats—important for overhead arm position and rotation

**How to do it:**
1. Stand next to a wall or doorframe
2. Reach your right arm overhead and grab the frame
3. Push your right hip away from the wall
4. Lean away to increase the stretch
5. Feel the stretch along your right side and under your arm
6. Hold 30 seconds
7. Switch sides

### 10. Standing Hamstring Stretch

**What it targets:** Hamstrings—tight hamstrings limit hip hinge and posture at address

**How to do it:**
1. Place your right heel on a low step or raised surface
2. Keep your leg straight
3. Hinge forward from your hips with a flat back
4. Feel the stretch in the back of your thigh
5. Hold 30-60 seconds
6. Switch legs

### 11. Calf Stretch

**What it targets:** Calves—affect balance and stability during the swing

**How to do it:**
1. Stand facing a wall
2. Step your right foot back, keeping it straight
3. Press your heel into the ground
4. Lean toward the wall
5. Hold 30 seconds
6. Switch legs

### 12. Wrist Flexor and Extensor Stretches

**What it targets:** Forearms—prevent golfer's elbow and tennis elbow

**Flexor stretch:**
1. Extend your arm, palm up
2. Use your other hand to gently pull fingers toward the floor
3. Hold 30 seconds each arm

**Extensor stretch:**
1. Extend your arm, palm down
2. Use your other hand to gently pull hand toward the floor
3. Hold 30 seconds each arm

### 13. Trunk Rotation with Club

**What it targets:** Full trunk rotation—mimics the golf swing

**How to do it:**
1. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart
2. Hold a club across your shoulders behind your neck
3. Rotate your torso to the right as far as comfortable
4. Hold 5 seconds
5. Rotate to the left
6. Repeat 10 times each direction

## Pre-Round Warm-Up Routine (10 minutes)

Do this before you tee off:

1. **Trunk rotation with club:** 10 reps each direction
2. **Standing side bends with club:** 5 each side, holding 10 seconds
3. **Hip circles:** 10 each direction, each leg
4. **Leg swings front-to-back:** 10 each leg
5. **Leg swings side-to-side:** 10 each leg
6. **Arm circles:** 10 forward, 10 backward
7. **Doorway chest stretch:** 30 seconds
8. **Open books:** 5 reps each side
9. **Practice swings:** Start at 50%, build to full speed

## Post-Round Recovery Routine (15 minutes)

Do this after your round to prevent stiffness:

1. **Thoracic rotation (seated):** 30 seconds each side
2. **Open books:** 5 reps each side, holding 30 seconds
3. **Hip flexor stretch:** 60 seconds each side
4. **Figure-4 stretch:** 60 seconds each side
5. **Standing hamstring stretch:** 45 seconds each leg
6. **Cross-body shoulder stretch:** 30 seconds each arm
7. **Doorway chest stretch:** 60 seconds
8. **Wrist stretches:** 30 seconds each direction, each arm
9. **Standing side bend:** 30 seconds each side

## Off-Course Flexibility Routine (20 minutes)

Do this 2-3 times per week for long-term flexibility:

1. **Cat-cow:** 15 cycles
2. **Open books:** 10 reps each side
3. **Thread the needle:** 45 seconds each side
4. **Hip flexor stretch:** 90 seconds each side
5. **90-90 hip stretch:** 60 seconds each position
6. **Figure-4 stretch:** 60 seconds each side
7. **Hamstring stretch (supine):** 60 seconds each leg
8. **Lat stretch:** 45 seconds each side
9. **Doorway chest stretch:** 90 seconds (vary arm positions)
10. **Seated spinal twist:** 45 seconds each side

## Common Golf Injuries and Stretches That Help

**Lower back pain:**
- Hip flexor stretch
- Thoracic rotation
- Hamstring stretch
- Figure-4 stretch

**Golfer's elbow (medial epicondylitis):**
- Wrist flexor stretch
- Forearm massage

**Shoulder pain:**
- Cross-body stretch
- Doorway chest stretch
- Lat stretch

**Hip pain:**
- 90-90 stretch
- Hip flexor stretch
- Figure-4 stretch

## Tips for Golfer Flexibility

**Stretch consistently, not just before golf.** Regular flexibility work produces better results than occasional stretching.

**Focus on rotation.** The golf swing is rotational. Prioritize thoracic and hip rotation stretches.

**Address your weak side.** Golf creates imbalances. Stretch both sides, but give extra attention to tighter areas.

**Don't skip the warm-up.** Cold muscles don't stretch well. Light activity before stretching improves results.

**Strength matters too.** Flexible but weak muscles don't protect your joints. Include strength training.

## The Bottom Line

Golf performance depends on mobility through your hips, spine, and shoulders. These stretches:

1. **Improve thoracic rotation** for a bigger backswing
2. **Open your hips** for better power transfer
3. **Loosen your shoulders** for a full, pain-free swing
4. **Prevent common golf injuries** through balanced flexibility

Make stretching as much a part of your golf routine as your pre-shot routine. Your swing—and your body—will thank you.
