# Partner Exercises: Workouts You Can Do Together

Working out with a partner makes exercise more fun, keeps you accountable, and opens up exercise possibilities you can't do alone. Whether it's a friend, spouse, or gym buddy, these partner exercises will challenge you both.

## Benefits of Partner Workouts

### Why Train Together?

- **Accountability:** Harder to skip when someone's waiting
- **Motivation:** Push each other harder
- **Fun:** Exercise becomes social
- **Safety:** Built-in spotter
- **Variety:** New exercise options
- **Bonding:** Shared accomplishment

### Making It Work

- Similar fitness levels help (or modify exercises)
- Communicate clearly
- Be encouraging, not competitive (unless you both want that)
- Take turns leading
- Make it regular

## Partner Warm-Up

### Mirror Exercise

1. Face each other
2. One person leads movements
3. Other mirrors exactly
4. Switch leaders every 30 seconds
5. 2-3 minutes total

### Partner Circles

1. Stand back to back
2. Pass medicine ball (or any object) around both bodies
3. 10 circles each direction
4. Warms up core and coordination

### High-Five Jacks

1. Face each other
2. Do jumping jacks together
3. High-five at top of each rep
4. 20-30 reps

## Upper Body Partner Exercises

### Partner Push-Up High-Fives

1. Both in push-up position, facing each other
2. Do push-up together
3. At top, high-five opposite hands
4. Repeat with other hands
5. 10-15 reps

### Wheelbarrow Push-Ups

1. Partner A in push-up position
2. Partner B holds A's ankles
3. A does push-ups while B walks forward
4. 10 reps, then switch

### Partner Plank

1. Both in plank position, facing each other
2. Try to tap partner's hand while staying stable
3. Alternate hands
4. 30-60 seconds

### Resistance Band Rows

1. Sit facing each other, legs extended
2. Hold band between you
3. One rows while other provides resistance
4. 15 reps each, then switch

### Partner Tricep Dips

1. Back to back, hands on each other's shoulders
2. Both lower into dip
3. Push back up together
4. 10-12 reps

## Lower Body Partner Exercises

### Partner Wall Sit

1. Back to back, both in wall sit position
2. Use each other's backs as the wall
3. Hold as long as possible
4. Support each other

### Squat Facing

1. Face each other, hold hands
2. Squat down together
3. Use each other for balance
4. 15-20 reps

### Partner Lunges

1. Face each other, hold hands
2. Both lunge back (opposite legs)
3. Return and switch
4. 10 each leg

### Leg Press Partner

1. Partner A lies on back, feet up
2. Partner B places hands on A's feet
3. A presses B up and lowers
4. B provides resistance
5. 10-15 reps, then switch

### Squat Jump High-Five

1. Both squat
2. Jump up together
3. High-five at peak
4. Land in squat
5. 10-15 reps

## Core Partner Exercises

### Sit-Up Pass

1. Both on backs, feet interlocked
2. Both sit up
3. Pass medicine ball at top
4. Lower
5. 15-20 reps

### Plank Claps

1. Both in high plank, facing each other
2. Clap right hands
3. Clap left hands
4. Keep alternating
5. 20 total claps

### Russian Twist Pass

1. Sit facing each other, knees bent
2. Lean back slightly
3. Pass ball/weight with twist
4. 20 passes total

### Leg Throw-Downs

1. Partner A lies on back
2. Partner B stands at A's head
3. A grabs B's ankles, lifts legs
4. B pushes legs down
5. A controls and lifts again
6. 10-15 reps, then switch

### Partner Dead Bug

1. Both on backs, heads toward each other
2. Arms extended, touching hands
3. Both do dead bug while pressing hands together
4. 10 each side

## Cardio Partner Exercises

### Partner Sprints

1. Sprint to partner and back
2. Tag and they go
3. Continue alternating
4. 5-10 sprints each

### Burpee Leapfrog

1. Partner A does burpee
2. Partner B jumps over A (while A is down)
3. B does burpee
4. A jumps over B
5. Continue moving forward
6. 10 total

### Jump Rope Together

1. Long rope, one person at each end
2. Third person jumps (or both hold one end)
3. Or individual ropes, synchronized
4. 1-2 minutes

### Partner Bear Crawl Race

1. Both start in bear crawl position
2. Race to finish line
3. Or: Race down, bear crawl back
4. 3-5 races

## Resistance Exercises

### Band Partner Row

1. Face each other, both hold band
2. One pulls (row) while other resists
3. 10 reps, then switch roles
4. Great for back and biceps

### Band Chest Press

1. Back to back
2. One pushes forward against band resistance
3. Other provides resistance
4. 10 reps, switch

### Partner Pallof Press

1. One holds band at chest
2. Other holds other end, provides resistance
3. Presser extends arms, resists rotation
4. 10 each side, switch roles

### Human Resistance Curl

1. Partner A holds arm out, fist down
2. Partner B curls against A's resistance
3. A provides steady resistance throughout
4. 10 reps each arm, switch

## Partner Stretching

### Seated Hamstring Stretch

1. Sit facing, legs extended, soles touching
2. Hold hands
3. One leans forward, other leans back
4. Hold 30 seconds, then switch

### Partner Chest Opener

1. Stand, one behind the other
2. Front person clasps hands behind back
3. Back person gently lifts arms up
4. Hold 30 seconds, switch

### Quad Stretch Assist

1. One person on belly
2. Partner gently pushes heel toward buttock
3. Hold 30 seconds each leg
4. Switch roles

### Back-to-Back Twist

1. Sit back to back
2. Both twist in same direction
3. Hold each other's hands/knees
4. Hold 20-30 seconds, switch direction

## Sample Partner Workouts

### Quick Partner Workout (15 minutes)

1. Mirror warm-up: 2 min
2. Partner push-up high-fives: 10 reps
3. Partner wall sit: 45 sec
4. Sit-up pass: 15 reps
5. Squat facing: 15 reps
6. Plank claps: 20 claps
7. Partner lunges: 10 each leg
8. Stretching: 3 min

### Full Partner Session (30 minutes)

**Warm-Up (5 min):**
1. Mirror exercise: 2 min
2. High-five jacks: 30
3. Partner circles: 10 each direction

**Strength Circuit (20 min, 2 rounds):**
1. Partner push-ups: 12 reps
2. Partner wall sit: 45 sec
3. Sit-up pass: 15 reps
4. Squat jump high-fives: 10 reps
5. Russian twist pass: 20 passes
6. Partner lunges: 10 each leg
7. Plank challenge: 45 sec
8. Rest 1 min between rounds

**Cool-Down (5 min):**
1. Walking together: 2 min
2. Partner stretches: 3 min

### Partner HIIT (20 minutes)

**30 sec work, 15 sec transition:**

Round 1:
1. Partner A: Burpees / Partner B: Plank hold
2. Switch
3. Both: Squat jump high-fives
4. Partner A: Mountain climbers / Partner B: Wall sit
5. Switch
6. Both: Partner push-ups

Rest 1 min, repeat 2-3 rounds total.

## Tips for Success

### Communication

- Count together
- Call out when you need a break
- Encourage without pushing too hard
- Give feedback on form

### Matching Abilities

- Modify for different fitness levels
- One can do harder variation
- Focus on effort, not comparison

### Safety

- Clear space of obstacles
- Agree on signals for "stop"
- Start lighter than you think
- Spot each other when needed

## The Bottom Line

Partner workouts offer:

1. **Accountability** (show up for each other)
2. **Fun** (exercise becomes social)
3. **New exercises** (impossible alone)
4. **Motivation** (push each other)
5. **Bonding** (shared achievement)

Find a partner, pick some exercises, and make fitness a shared experience. You'll both be stronger for it—in more ways than one.
