# Bone Spur Exercises: Manage Pain and Maintain Function

Bone spurs (osteophytes) are bony projections that develop along joint edges, usually where bones meet. While you can't exercise away a bone spur, the right exercises can reduce pain, improve mobility, strengthen supporting muscles, and help you stay active despite having bone spurs.

## Understanding Bone Spurs

**What they are:**
- Bony growths along joint margins
- Body's attempt to increase surface area
- Response to stress, arthritis, or injury
- Often form where tendons/ligaments attach

**Common locations:**
- Spine (cervical and lumbar)
- Heels (calcaneal spurs)
- Shoulders
- Hips
- Knees

**Causes:**
- Osteoarthritis (most common)
- Repetitive stress
- Aging
- Injury
- Plantar fasciitis (heel spurs)
- Poor posture (spine)

**Important fact:** Many bone spurs cause no symptoms and are found incidentally. Pain often comes from inflammation of surrounding soft tissues, not the spur itself.

## General Principles

1. **Keep moving** — Inactivity makes joints stiffer
2. **Low impact** — Reduce stress on affected joints
3. **Strengthen supporting muscles** — Takes load off joints
4. **Maintain flexibility** — Prevent further stiffening
5. **Listen to your body** — Avoid exercises that cause sharp pain

## Heel/Foot Bone Spur Exercises

Heel spurs often accompany plantar fasciitis.

### Plantar Fascia Stretch
1. Sit, cross affected foot over knee
2. Pull toes back toward shin
3. Feel stretch along arch
4. Hold 30 seconds
5. Do before getting out of bed

### Calf Stretch
1. Face wall, hands on wall
2. Step affected foot back
3. Keep heel down, knee straight
4. Lean forward
5. Hold 30 seconds each leg

### Towel Stretch
1. Sit with legs extended
2. Loop towel around ball of foot
3. Pull toward you
4. Hold 30 seconds

### Towel Scrunches
1. Place towel flat under foot
2. Scrunch toward you with toes
3. 2-3 towel lengths
4. Strengthens arch muscles

### Marble Pickups
1. Pick up marbles with toes
2. Transfer to cup
3. 20 marbles each foot

### Rolling
1. Roll frozen water bottle under arch
2. Or golf ball (more intense)
3. 2-3 minutes
4. Combines stretch and ice

## Spine Bone Spur Exercises

### Neck (Cervical Spine)

**Chin Tucks**
1. Sit tall
2. Draw chin back (double chin)
3. Hold 5 seconds
4. 15 repetitions

**Gentle Neck Range of Motion**
1. Turn head side to side
2. Tilt ear to shoulder
3. Look up and down
4. Move slowly, don't force
5. 5-10 each direction

**Isometric Neck Strengthening**
1. Place hand on forehead
2. Press head into hand (no movement)
3. Hold 5 seconds
4. Repeat each direction
5. 10 each direction

### Lower Back (Lumbar Spine)

**Cat-Cow**
1. On hands and knees
2. Arch back up (cat)
3. Drop belly down (cow)
4. Move slowly
5. 10-15 repetitions

**Knee to Chest**
1. Lie on back
2. Pull one knee toward chest
3. Hold 30 seconds
4. Repeat other side
5. Then both together

**Pelvic Tilts**
1. Lie on back, knees bent
2. Flatten lower back to floor
3. Then arch slightly
4. 15 repetitions

**Partial Curl-Up**
1. Lie on back, knees bent
2. Place hands behind head
3. Lift shoulders slightly (not full sit-up)
4. Hold 3 seconds
5. 10 repetitions

**Bridge**
1. Lie on back, knees bent
2. Lift hips
3. Hold 5 seconds
4. Lower with control
5. 15 repetitions

## Shoulder Bone Spur Exercises

### Pendulum Exercise
1. Lean forward, support with good arm
2. Let affected arm hang
3. Swing gently: circles, forward/back, side to side
4. 1-2 minutes
5. Very gentle—uses gravity

### Shoulder Shrugs
1. Raise shoulders toward ears
2. Hold 5 seconds
3. Release
4. 15 repetitions

### Wall Slides
1. Back against wall
2. Arms in W position
3. Slide up to Y position
4. Keep arms touching wall
5. 2 sets of 10

### External Rotation with Band
1. Elbow at side, bent 90°
2. Band attached to side
3. Rotate forearm outward
4. Control return
5. 3 sets of 12

### Rows
1. Band or light weight
2. Pull toward body
3. Squeeze shoulder blades
4. 3 sets of 12

**Avoid:** Overhead movements that cause impingement pain

## Hip Bone Spur Exercises

### Gentle Hip Circles
1. Lie on back
2. Bring one knee toward chest
3. Make small circles with knee
4. 10 each direction

### Supine Hip Flexor Stretch
1. Lie on edge of bed
2. Pull one knee to chest
3. Let other leg hang off edge
4. Hold 30 seconds each

### Clamshells
1. Side-lying, knees bent
2. Keep feet together
3. Lift top knee
4. 3 sets of 15 each side

### Glute Bridges
1. Lie on back, knees bent
2. Lift hips
3. Don't overextend
4. 3 sets of 12

### Pool Walking
- Excellent for hip bone spurs
- Reduces joint loading
- Allows movement without pain

## Knee Bone Spur Exercises

### Quad Sets
1. Sit with leg extended
2. Tighten thigh, push knee down
3. Hold 5-10 seconds
4. 3 sets of 15

### Straight Leg Raises
1. Lie on back
2. Keep leg straight
3. Lift 6-8 inches
4. Lower with control
5. 3 sets of 12 each leg

### Heel Slides
1. Lie on back
2. Slide heel toward buttock
3. Go to comfortable range
4. 20 repetitions

### Step-Ups (Low Step)
1. 4-6 inch step
2. Step up with control
3. Don't let knee cave in
4. 3 sets of 10 each leg

### Swimming/Water Walking
- Reduces joint stress
- Excellent for knee spurs
- Maintains fitness

## General Strengthening

Strong muscles support joints and reduce stress on areas with bone spurs.

### Core Work
- Planks
- Dead bugs
- Bird dogs

### Hip Strengthening
- Clamshells
- Side-lying leg raises
- Bridges

### Overall Conditioning
- Walking
- Swimming
- Cycling (if tolerated)
- Elliptical

## Low-Impact Cardio Options

- Swimming
- Water aerobics
- Cycling (adjust for comfort)
- Elliptical
- Walking
- Rowing (if tolerated)

**Avoid or modify:**
- Running (high impact)
- Jumping
- High-impact aerobics

## Pain Management Around Exercise

### Before Exercise
- Gentle warm-up (5 minutes walking)
- Heat to affected area (may help)
- Medication if recommended by doctor

### During Exercise
- Stop if sharp pain
- Some discomfort may be normal
- Don't push through significant pain

### After Exercise
- Ice for 15-20 minutes if inflamed
- Gentle stretching
- Rest if needed

## Daily Routine Example

### Morning (10 minutes)
1. Gentle range of motion (3 min)
2. Light stretching (4 min)
3. Low-level strengthening (3 min)

### Midday
- Movement break
- Gentle stretches

### Evening (15 minutes)
1. Strengthening exercises (8 min)
2. Stretching (5 min)
3. Ice if needed (15 min)

## When to Seek Help

See a doctor if you have:
- Severe pain limiting function
- Numbness or weakness
- Loss of bladder/bowel control (spine)
- Significant reduction in range of motion
- Pain not improving with conservative care

**Treatment options:**
- Physical therapy
- Anti-inflammatory medications
- Cortisone injections
- Surgery (rare, for severe cases)

## What to Expect

**Exercise CAN:**
- Reduce pain
- Improve function
- Strengthen supporting muscles
- Maintain mobility
- Slow progression of arthritis

**Exercise CANNOT:**
- Remove bone spurs
- Reverse joint damage
- Cure underlying arthritis

**Important:** Many people with bone spurs live pain-free by maintaining muscle strength, flexibility, and appropriate activity levels. The spur itself isn't always the problem—inflammation and muscle weakness often are.

Focus on what you CAN do, stay active within your limits, and work with healthcare providers if symptoms don't improve.
