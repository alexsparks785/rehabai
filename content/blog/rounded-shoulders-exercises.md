# Rounded Shoulders Exercises: Fix Forward Shoulder Posture

Rounded shoulders—that hunched, forward posture—is one of the most common postural problems today. Hours of computer work, phone use, and driving create this pattern. The good news: it's very correctable with the right exercises.

## Understanding Rounded Shoulders

### What Causes Them

**Tight Muscles (Front):**
- Pectoralis major and minor
- Anterior deltoid
- Subscapularis

**Weak Muscles (Back):**
- Rhomboids
- Middle and lower trapezius
- Posterior deltoid
- Serratus anterior

**Contributing Factors:**
- Prolonged sitting
- Computer and phone use
- Driving
- Carrying bags on shoulders
- Sleeping position
- Weak core

### The Muscle Imbalance

Rounded shoulders involve:
- Tight chest pulling shoulders forward
- Weak upper back unable to pull back
- Scapulae (shoulder blades) winging or protracted
- Forward head posture often accompanies

### Signs of Rounded Shoulders

- Shoulders visible in front of ears (side view)
- Palms face backward when standing
- Upper back curves excessively
- Difficulty reaching behind back
- Neck and upper back pain
- Shoulder impingement symptoms

## Phase 1: Release and Stretch

### Doorway Chest Stretch

**Basic Version:**
1. Stand in doorway
2. Forearms on door frame, elbows at shoulder height
3. Step one foot forward
4. Lean through doorway
5. Feel stretch across chest
6. Hold 30-60 seconds

**Variations:**
- Elbows higher (more pec minor)
- Elbows lower (more pec major)
- One arm at a time (deeper stretch)

### Corner Stretch

1. Face corner of room
2. Forearms on each wall
3. Lean into corner
4. Hold 30-60 seconds

### Floor Chest Stretch

1. Lie face down, one arm out to side (90° from body)
2. Roll toward that arm
3. Feel deep stretch in chest
4. Hold 30-45 seconds each side

### Foam Roller Chest Opener

1. Lie lengthwise on foam roller (head to hips supported)
2. Arms out to sides, elbows bent (goalpost)
3. Let arms fall toward floor
4. Relax and breathe
5. Hold 1-2 minutes

### Pec Minor Release

Using ball against wall:
1. Place tennis/lacrosse ball below collarbone
2. Lean into wall
3. Find tender spots
4. Hold 60-90 seconds

### Lat Stretch

Tight lats contribute to rounded shoulders:

1. Kneel near wall or sturdy object
2. Reach arms forward, hands on surface
3. Sit hips back
4. Let chest drop toward floor
5. Hold 30-45 seconds

## Phase 2: Mobility

### Thoracic Extension

1. Foam roller across upper back
2. Support head with hands
3. Extend over roller
4. Move roller to different levels
5. 10-15 extensions at each spot

### Thread the Needle

1. Hands and knees position
2. Reach one arm under body
3. Rotate torso, shoulder toward floor
4. Return and reach up
5. 10 reps each side

### Wall Slides

1. Back against wall
2. Arms in goalpost position
3. Slide arms up, keeping contact with wall
4. Slide back down
5. 10-15 reps

**Note:** Most people can't keep arms on wall at first—that's normal.

### Shoulder Circles

1. Arms at sides
2. Circle shoulders backward
3. Large, slow circles
4. 15-20 circles

### Cat-Cow

1. Hands and knees
2. Round back, tuck chin (cat)
3. Arch back, lift chest (cow)
4. 10-15 cycles

## Phase 3: Strengthening

### Band Pull-Apart

Essential for rounded shoulders:

1. Hold band in front, arms extended
2. Pull band apart to sides
3. Squeeze shoulder blades together
4. Return slowly
5. 15-20 reps, 3 sets

### Face Pulls

1. Band or cable at face height
2. Pull toward face, elbows high
3. Hands finish near ears
4. Squeeze shoulder blades
5. 15-20 reps, 3 sets

### Prone Y-T-W

**Y Raise:**
1. Lie face down
2. Arms overhead in Y position
3. Lift arms (thumbs up)
4. 12-15 reps

**T Raise:**
1. Arms out to sides
2. Lift with thumbs up
3. 12-15 reps

**W Raise:**
1. Arms bent, elbows at sides
2. Lift elbows, squeeze shoulder blades
3. 12-15 reps

### Rows (Any Variation)

**Band Rows:**
1. Band at chest height
2. Pull toward body
3. Squeeze shoulder blades at end
4. 15 reps, 3 sets

**Dumbbell Rows:**
1. Bent over, one hand on bench
2. Row weight to hip
3. Squeeze at top
4. 12 reps each side, 3 sets

### External Rotation

1. Elbow at side, bent 90°
2. Band or weight in hand
3. Rotate forearm outward
4. Keep elbow pinned
5. 15 reps each arm, 3 sets

### Scapular Retraction

1. Sit or stand tall
2. Squeeze shoulder blades together
3. Hold 5 seconds
4. Release
5. 15-20 reps

### Reverse Fly

1. Bent over or lying face down
2. Arms hanging, slight bend
3. Lift arms to sides
4. Squeeze shoulder blades
5. 12-15 reps, 3 sets

### Wall Angels

1. Back flat against wall
2. Arms in goalpost position, touching wall
3. Slide arms up and down
4. Keep contact with wall throughout
5. 10-15 reps, 3 sets

### Push-Up Plus

Strengthens serratus anterior:

1. Push-up position (or on knees)
2. At top, push further (round upper back)
3. Protract shoulder blades
4. Return to normal push-up top
5. 10-15 reps, 3 sets

## Phase 4: Postural Integration

### Chin Tuck

Often accompanies rounded shoulders:

1. Pull chin straight back
2. Make double chin
3. Hold 5 seconds
4. 15-20 reps throughout day

### Brugger's Relief Position

Reset posture throughout day:

1. Sit at edge of chair
2. Feet apart, toes out
3. Arms at sides, palms forward
4. Lift chest, squeeze shoulder blades
5. Tuck chin slightly
6. Hold 20-30 seconds
7. Repeat hourly

### Standing Posture Check

Practice correct alignment:

1. Stand against wall
2. Heels, buttocks, shoulder blades, head touch wall
3. Chin tucked
4. Hold 30-60 seconds
5. Step away and maintain

## Sample Programs

### Daily Quick Fix (5 minutes)

1. Doorway stretch: 30 sec
2. Chin tucks: 10 reps
3. Band pull-aparts: 15 reps
4. Shoulder circles: 15 backward
5. Brugger's position: 20 sec

### Full Correction Session (15-20 minutes)

**Release/Stretch (5 min):**
1. Foam roller chest opener: 1 min
2. Doorway stretch: 45 sec each angle
3. Floor chest stretch: 30 sec each
4. Lat stretch: 30 sec

**Mobility (3 min):**
1. Thoracic extension: 10 reps
2. Thread the needle: 8 each side
3. Cat-cow: 10 cycles

**Strengthening (8 min):**
1. Band pull-aparts: 3×15
2. Face pulls: 3×15
3. Y-T-W: 2×10 each
4. Wall angels: 3×10
5. External rotation: 2×12 each

**Posture (2 min):**
1. Chin tucks: 15 reps
2. Wall standing: 1 min

### Workday Routine

**Every Hour:**
- Brugger's position: 20 sec
- Chin tucks: 5 reps
- Shoulder rolls: 10 backward

**2-3x During Workday:**
- Doorway stretch: 30 sec
- Band pull-aparts: 15 reps

**Before/After Work:**
- Full 15-minute routine

## Lifestyle Modifications

### Workstation

- Monitor at eye level
- Keyboard at elbow height
- Chair with back support
- Avoid reaching forward

### Phone/Tablet

- Bring device to eye level
- Use stands when possible
- Limit time looking down

### Sleeping

- Avoid stomach sleeping
- Side sleepers: pillow not too high
- Back sleepers: thin pillow

### Carrying

- Use backpack instead of shoulder bag
- Carry weight evenly
- Don't carry on same shoulder constantly

## Progress Timeline

**Week 1-2:**
- Stretches feel tight
- Difficulty with wall angels
- Building awareness

**Week 2-4:**
- Stretches becoming easier
- Exercises less challenging
- Catching poor posture

**Week 4-8:**
- Visible improvement in posture
- Exercises feel stronger
- Better posture becoming natural

**Week 8-12:**
- Significant correction
- Good posture is default
- Maintenance phase

## The Bottom Line

Fixing rounded shoulders requires:

1. **Stretch the front** (chest, anterior shoulder)
2. **Mobilize the thoracic spine**
3. **Strengthen the back** (rhomboids, traps, rotators)
4. **Practice good posture** (awareness + breaks)
5. **Be consistent** (daily attention)

It took years to develop rounded shoulders—give yourself weeks to months to correct them. But with consistent work, proper posture can become your new normal.
