# Core Workout at Home: 10-Minute Ab Routine No Equipment

A strong core isn't just about visible abs—it's about stability, posture, and protecting your back. This 10-minute routine works your entire core: rectus abdominis, obliques, transverse abdominis, and lower back.

## What Makes a Good Core Workout

**A complete core routine includes:**
- Anti-extension (resisting back arch): Planks, dead bugs
- Anti-rotation (resisting twisting): Pallof variations, bird-dogs
- Anti-lateral flexion (resisting side bend): Side planks
- Flexion (crunching): Crunches, leg raises
- Rotation (controlled twisting): Bicycles, Russian twists

**This routine covers all of them.**

---

## The 10-Minute Core Routine

### Exercise 1: Dead Bug (60 seconds)
**Targets:** Deep core, anti-extension

1. Lie on back, arms straight up, knees at 90°
2. Lower opposite arm and leg toward floor
3. Keep lower back pressed into ground
4. Return, switch sides
5. Continue for 60 seconds

**Key:** If your back arches off the floor, you've gone too far.

---

### Exercise 2: Plank (45 seconds)
**Targets:** Full core, anti-extension

1. Forearms and toes on ground
2. Body in straight line from head to heels
3. Squeeze glutes, brace core
4. Don't let hips sag or pike up
5. Hold 45 seconds

**Too easy?** Try plank shoulder taps instead.

---

### Exercise 3: Bicycle Crunches (60 seconds)
**Targets:** Rectus abdominis, obliques, rotation

1. Lie on back, hands behind head
2. Bring opposite elbow to opposite knee
3. Extend other leg straight
4. Alternate sides continuously
5. 60 seconds

**Key:** Rotate your torso, don't just move your elbow.

---

### Exercise 4: Side Plank - Right (30 seconds)
**Targets:** Obliques, lateral stability

1. On right forearm, feet stacked
2. Lift hips, body in straight line
3. Hold 30 seconds
4. Don't let hips sag

**Easier:** Knees on ground instead of feet.

---

### Exercise 5: Side Plank - Left (30 seconds)
**Targets:** Obliques, lateral stability

1. Same as above, left side
2. Hold 30 seconds

---

### Exercise 6: Bird-Dog (60 seconds)
**Targets:** Deep core, anti-rotation, lower back

1. On hands and knees
2. Extend opposite arm and leg
3. Keep hips level—don't rotate
4. Hold 2-3 seconds
5. Alternate sides for 60 seconds

**Key:** The goal is stability, not height.

---

### Exercise 7: Leg Raises (45 seconds)
**Targets:** Lower abs

1. Lie on back, legs straight
2. Hands under lower back for support
3. Lift legs toward ceiling
4. Lower slowly, stopping before back arches
5. 45 seconds

**Easier:** Bend knees slightly.

---

### Exercise 8: Mountain Climbers (45 seconds)
**Targets:** Core, cardio, hip flexors

1. Plank position
2. Drive one knee toward chest
3. Quickly switch legs
4. Keep hips stable, don't bounce
5. 45 seconds

**Keep your core tight—this shouldn't just be leg movement.**

---

### Exercise 9: Reverse Crunch (45 seconds)
**Targets:** Lower abs

1. Lie on back, knees bent 90°
2. Lift hips off floor, bringing knees toward chest
3. Lower with control
4. 45 seconds

**Key:** The lift comes from your lower abs curling up, not momentum.

---

### Exercise 10: Plank Hold (60 seconds)
**Targets:** Full core, endurance

1. Back to forearm plank
2. Focus on maintaining perfect form
3. Hold 60 seconds to finish strong

## Harder Variations

### Make Planks Harder
- Shoulder taps
- Plank jacks
- Plank to push-up
- Single-arm or single-leg plank

### Make Crunches Harder
- Slower tempo
- Add a hold at peak contraction
- Weighted (hold something)

### Make Leg Raises Harder
- Straight legs
- Slower descent
- Hanging leg raises (if you have a bar)

### Make Side Planks Harder
- Hip dips
- Reach under and rotate
- Lift top leg

## Common Mistakes

### Lower Back Coming Off Floor
- Means you're going beyond your core strength
- Reduce range of motion
- Focus on keeping back pressed down

### Holding Breath
- Breathe throughout
- Exhale on exertion
- Holding breath raises blood pressure

### Rushing Through Exercises
- Slow and controlled is more effective
- Feel each muscle working
- Quality over quantity

### Only Doing Crunches
- Core needs variety
- Include anti-rotation and stability work
- Train all planes of movement

---

## Key Takeaway

A strong core requires more than crunches—you need planks, rotational work, and anti-rotation exercises. This 10-minute routine covers everything in a quick, efficient session. Do it 3-4 times per week, focus on quality over speed, and don't let your lower back arch during exercises. Core strength improves posture, reduces back pain, and transfers to everything else you do.
