# Back Pain When Sitting Exercises: Relief for Desk Workers

If sitting causes your back to ache, you're not alone. Prolonged sitting is one of the most common causes of back pain, putting more pressure on your spine than standing. These exercises will help relieve pain, strengthen weak muscles, and make sitting more tolerable.

## Why Sitting Hurts Your Back

**What happens when you sit:**
- Disc pressure increases 40% compared to standing
- Hip flexors shorten
- Glutes become inactive
- Core muscles disengage
- Spine often rounds forward
- Back muscles fatigue from holding posture

**Common patterns:**
- Pain starts after 20-30 minutes of sitting
- Worse at end of day
- Relief when standing or walking
- Stiffness when first standing up

## At-Desk Exercises

Do these without leaving your chair:

### Seated Pelvic Tilts
1. Sit tall on edge of chair
2. Arch lower back (anterior tilt)
3. Then round lower back (posterior tilt)
4. Rock between positions
5. 15-20 times
6. Do every hour

### Seated Cat-Cow
1. Sit tall, hands on knees
2. Round back, tuck chin (cat)
3. Arch back, lift chest (cow)
4. Flow smoothly between
5. 10 repetitions

### Seated Spinal Twist
1. Sit tall
2. Turn torso to one side
3. Hold chair back for leverage
4. Hold 20 seconds each side
5. Repeat 2-3 times

### Seated Figure 4 Stretch
1. Cross ankle over opposite knee
2. Sit tall
3. Lean forward slightly
4. Feel stretch in hip
5. Hold 30 seconds each side

### Chin Tucks
1. Sit tall
2. Draw chin back (double chin)
3. Keep eyes level
4. Hold 5 seconds
5. 15 repetitions
6. Reduces neck strain that affects back

### Seated Glute Squeeze
1. Sit tall
2. Squeeze glutes together
3. Hold 5 seconds
4. Relax
5. 15 repetitions
6. Wakes up inactive glutes

### Seated March
1. Sit tall
2. Lift one knee, then other
3. Alternate for 30 seconds
4. Engages core and hip flexors

## Standing Breaks (Every 30-60 Minutes)

### Standing Back Extension
1. Stand, place hands on lower back
2. Gently lean backward
3. Hold 5 seconds
4. Repeat 5 times
5. Counteracts sitting flexion

### Standing Hip Flexor Stretch
1. Step forward into lunge stance
2. Tuck pelvis under
3. Shift weight forward
4. Feel stretch in back hip
5. Hold 30 seconds each side

### Standing Cat-Cow
1. Stand, hands on thighs
2. Round back, tuck chin
3. Arch back, lift chest
4. 10 repetitions

### Wall Angels
1. Back against wall
2. Arms in W position
3. Slide to Y position
4. Keep arms touching wall
5. 10 repetitions

### Hip Circles
1. Stand, hands on hips
2. Circle hips clockwise
3. Then counterclockwise
4. 10 each direction

## Core Strengthening

Weak core fails to support spine during sitting:

### Dead Bug
1. Lie on back
2. Arms to ceiling, knees bent 90°
3. Lower opposite arm and leg
4. Keep lower back pressed to floor
5. 3 sets of 10 each side

### Bird Dog
1. On hands and knees
2. Extend opposite arm and leg
3. Keep spine neutral
4. Hold 5 seconds
5. 3 sets of 10 each side

### Plank
1. Forearm plank
2. Body in straight line
3. Don't let back sag
4. Hold 30-60 seconds
5. 3 sets

### Side Plank
1. On forearm, stacked feet
2. Keep body straight
3. Hold 20-30 seconds each side
4. 3 sets

### Glute Bridge
1. Lie on back, knees bent
2. Lift hips, squeeze glutes
3. Hold 5 seconds
4. 3 sets of 15

## Hip Flexor Stretching

Tight hip flexors pull on lower back:

### Half-Kneeling Hip Flexor Stretch
1. Kneel on one knee
2. Tuck pelvis under
3. Lean forward slightly
4. Hold 30-60 seconds each side
5. Do 2-3 times daily

### Couch Stretch
1. Face away from couch
2. Place knee on floor, shin against couch
3. Front foot forward
4. Stay upright
5. Hold 1-2 minutes each side

### Supine Hip Flexor Stretch
1. Lie on edge of bed
2. Pull one knee to chest
3. Let other leg hang off
4. Hold 30-60 seconds each side

## Glute Strengthening

Weak glutes shift load to lower back:

### Clamshells
1. Side-lying, knees bent
2. Lift top knee
3. Keep feet together
4. 3 sets of 15 each side

### Fire Hydrants
1. On hands and knees
2. Lift knee out to side
3. 3 sets of 12 each side

### Hip Thrusts
1. Upper back on bench/couch
2. Feet flat on floor
3. Drive hips up
4. Squeeze glutes
5. 3 sets of 12

### Single Leg Glute Bridge
1. Lie on back
2. One leg extended
3. Bridge on single leg
4. 3 sets of 10 each leg

## Thoracic Mobility

Stiff upper back forces lower back to compensate:

### Thoracic Extension on Foam Roller
1. Roller perpendicular to spine
2. Support head with hands
3. Extend back over roller
4. Move to different levels
5. 2-3 minutes

### Thread the Needle
1. On hands and knees
2. Reach one arm under body
3. Rotate and lower shoulder
4. Hold 30 seconds each side

### Open Book
1. Side-lying, knees bent
2. Arms extended in front
3. Open top arm to other side
4. Follow with eyes
5. Hold 30 seconds each side

## Workstation Setup

Proper setup reduces strain:

### Chair
- Feet flat on floor
- Knees at or below hip level
- Lumbar support (or roll)
- Arm rests at elbow height

### Desk/Monitor
- Screen at eye level
- Arm's length away
- Keyboard at elbow height

### Habits
- Stand every 30-60 minutes
- Walk while on phone
- Consider sit-stand desk
- Move throughout day

## Daily Routine

### Morning (10 minutes)
1. Cat-cow (2 min)
2. Hip flexor stretches (3 min)
3. Glute bridges (2 min)
4. Thoracic mobility (3 min)

### At Work (throughout day)
- Movement break every 30-60 minutes
- Seated exercises at desk
- Standing stretches on breaks

### Evening (15 minutes)
1. Full stretching routine (5 min)
2. Core strengthening (5 min)
3. Hip/glute work (5 min)

## Sample Weekly Plan

**Daily:**
- Hourly movement breaks
- Morning and evening routines

**3x per week:**
- Full core strengthening workout
- Hip and glute work

**Weekend:**
- Active recovery (walking, swimming)
- Extended stretching

## Progress Timeline

**Week 1:** Pain awareness, establishing routine
**Week 2-4:** Reduced end-of-day pain
**Month 2:** Significantly better sitting tolerance
**Ongoing:** Maintain with consistent exercise

## When to Seek Help

See a healthcare provider if:
- Pain radiates down legs
- Numbness or tingling
- Weakness
- Bowel/bladder changes
- Pain severe or worsening
- No improvement after 4-6 weeks

## What to Expect

**Exercise CAN:**
- Reduce sitting-related pain
- Build supporting muscles
- Improve posture
- Increase sitting tolerance

**Keys to success:**
- Regular movement breaks (most important)
- Consistent strengthening
- Good workstation setup
- Overall activity level matters

The best posture is your next posture. No position is good for hours on end—regular movement is the real solution to sitting-related back pain.
