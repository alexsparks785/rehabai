A racing mind isn't the only thing keeping you awake. Physical tension from the day accumulates in your muscles, making it hard to relax. A gentle stretching routine before bed releases this tension, signals your body it's time to sleep, and helps you drift off faster.

These stretches are specifically designed for bedtime—gentle, calming, and focused on the areas where we hold the most tension: neck, shoulders, back, and hips.

## Why Stretching Helps You Sleep

**Releases physical tension.** Tight muscles are uncomfortable and keep your nervous system alert.

**Activates the parasympathetic nervous system.** Slow, gentle stretching triggers your "rest and digest" response.

**Reduces cortisol.** Stretching can lower stress hormones that interfere with sleep.

**Creates a bedtime ritual.** Consistent pre-sleep routines signal your brain that it's time for rest.

**Improves circulation.** Better blood flow helps regulate body temperature for sleep.

## Guidelines for Bedtime Stretching

**Keep it gentle.** This isn't the time for intense stretching. Use 50-70% of your maximum stretch.

**Breathe slowly and deeply.** Inhale for 4 counts, exhale for 6-8 counts.

**Hold longer.** 60-90 second holds allow muscles to fully relax.

**Dim the lights.** Create a sleep-friendly environment while you stretch.

**Avoid stimulation.** No phones, no TV, no bright screens.

**Do it consistently.** A nightly routine trains your body to expect sleep.

## Bedtime Stretches for Better Sleep

### 1. Neck Rolls

**What it releases:** Neck tension from the day

**How to do it:**
1. Sit comfortably or lie on your back
2. Drop your right ear toward your right shoulder
3. Slowly roll your chin down toward your chest
4. Continue to your left shoulder
5. Don't roll your head backward—just side to front to side
6. Repeat 5 times in each direction
7. Move very slowly, breathing deeply

### 2. Upper Trapezius Stretch

**What it releases:** Shoulder and neck tension

**How to do it:**
1. Sit comfortably
2. Tilt your head to the right, ear toward shoulder
3. Let gravity create the stretch (no hand pressure)
4. Let your left shoulder drop down and relax
5. Breathe deeply and hold 60-90 seconds
6. Switch sides

### 3. Seated Forward Fold

**What it releases:** Lower back, hamstrings, calming effect

**How to do it:**
1. Sit with legs extended in front of you
2. Inhale and lengthen your spine
3. Exhale and fold forward from your hips
4. Let your hands rest wherever they reach—thighs, shins, or feet
5. Let your head hang heavy
6. Breathe deeply and relax into the stretch
7. Hold 60-90 seconds

### 4. Butterfly Stretch

**What it releases:** Hips, inner thighs, groin

**How to do it:**
1. Sit with soles of your feet together, knees falling open
2. Hold your feet or ankles
3. Sit tall, then gently fold forward
4. Let your head and arms hang relaxed
5. Don't force your knees down—let gravity work
6. Breathe deeply and hold 60-90 seconds

### 5. Supine Twist

**What it releases:** Lower back, hips, promotes digestion

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on your back
2. Bend both knees and bring them toward your chest
3. Let both knees fall to the right
4. Extend your arms out in a T position
5. Turn your head to the left
6. Relax completely and breathe
7. Hold 60-90 seconds
8. Switch sides

### 6. Knee-to-Chest Stretch

**What it releases:** Lower back, glutes

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on your back
2. Bring both knees toward your chest
3. Wrap your arms around your legs
4. Gently rock side to side if it feels good
5. Breathe deeply into your lower back
6. Hold 60-90 seconds

### 7. Child's Pose

**What it releases:** Lower back, hips, shoulders, calms the mind

**How to do it:**
1. Kneel and sit back toward your heels
2. Fold forward, resting your forehead on the floor (or a pillow)
3. Extend your arms forward or rest them by your sides
4. Breathe deeply, letting your belly expand against your thighs
5. Hold 90 seconds to 2 minutes

### 8. Reclined Figure-4

**What it releases:** Hips, glutes, piriformis

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on your back with knees bent
2. Cross your right ankle over your left knee
3. Stay here for a gentle stretch, or reach through and clasp behind your left thigh
4. Gently pull your left thigh toward you
5. Relax your head and shoulders
6. Hold 60-90 seconds
7. Switch sides

### 9. Legs Up the Wall

**What it does:** Promotes circulation, calms the nervous system, relieves tired legs

**How to do it:**
1. Sit with your right side against a wall
2. Swing your legs up the wall as you lower your back to the floor
3. Scoot your hips close to the wall (or a few inches away if that's more comfortable)
4. Rest your arms by your sides, palms up
5. Close your eyes and breathe slowly
6. Hold 3-5 minutes

**This is the ultimate pre-sleep stretch.** It's deeply calming and can be done in bed with your legs up the headboard.

### 10. Supine Spinal Twist

**What it releases:** Entire spine, promotes relaxation

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on your back
2. Extend your arms in a T position
3. Bring your right knee toward your chest
4. Let your right knee cross over your body to the left
5. Turn your head to the right
6. Let gravity do the work—don't force
7. Breathe deeply and hold 90 seconds
8. Switch sides

### 11. Happy Baby Pose

**What it releases:** Inner hips, groin, lower back

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on your back
2. Bend your knees toward your armpits
3. Grab the outsides of your feet
4. Gently pull your knees toward the floor beside your ribs
5. Keep your lower back on the floor
6. Rock gently side to side if it feels good
7. Hold 60-90 seconds

### 12. Corpse Pose (Savasana)

**What it does:** Final relaxation before sleep

**How to do it:**
1. Lie flat on your back
2. Let your legs fall open naturally
3. Rest your arms by your sides, palms up
4. Close your eyes
5. Breathe slowly and deeply
6. Mentally scan your body and release any remaining tension
7. Stay here 3-5 minutes or until you're ready to sleep

## Complete Bedtime Stretching Routine (15-20 minutes)

1. **Neck rolls:** 5 each direction
2. **Upper trap stretch:** Both sides, 60 seconds each
3. **Child's pose:** 90 seconds
4. **Cat-cow (optional):** 10 gentle cycles
5. **Knee-to-chest:** 60 seconds
6. **Supine twist:** Both sides, 60 seconds each
7. **Reclined figure-4:** Both sides, 60 seconds each
8. **Happy baby:** 60 seconds
9. **Legs up the wall:** 3-5 minutes
10. **Corpse pose:** Until ready to sleep

## Quick 5-Minute Routine

When time is short:
1. **Neck rolls:** 3 each direction
2. **Child's pose:** 60 seconds
3. **Supine twist:** Both sides, 45 seconds each
4. **Knee-to-chest:** 45 seconds
5. **Legs up the wall or corpse pose:** 2 minutes

## Tips for Better Sleep Stretching

**Time it right.** Do your stretching 30-60 minutes before you want to be asleep.

**Create the environment.** Dim lights, cool room, no screens.

**Use deep breathing.** Slow exhales activate relaxation responses.

**Add calming music or silence.** Avoid stimulating content.

**Keep it consistent.** Same routine at the same time builds a sleep habit.

**Stay warm.** Stretch in comfortable, warm clothing. Cold muscles don't relax well.

**Don't overdo it.** Intense stretching can be stimulating. Keep it gentle.

## The Bottom Line

A bedtime stretching routine is one of the simplest ways to improve your sleep. These gentle stretches:

1. **Release physical tension** accumulated during the day
2. **Calm your nervous system** and prepare your body for rest
3. **Create a consistent ritual** that signals sleep time
4. **Improve circulation** for better temperature regulation
5. **Quiet your mind** by focusing on your body and breath

Make bedtime stretching a non-negotiable part of your evening routine, and you'll fall asleep faster, sleep deeper, and wake up feeling better.
