# Best Stretches for Office Workers: Combat Desk Pain

Eight hours at a desk does predictable things to your body: tight hip flexors, rounded shoulders, forward head, stiff upper back, weak glutes. The same pattern, millions of people.

These stretches specifically target what desk work does to you. Do them at your desk during the day and at home for deeper release.

## The Desk Worker's Problem Areas

**Hip Flexors:** Shortened from sitting, pull on lower back
**Chest/Shoulders:** Tight from reaching forward, roll shoulders in
**Upper Back:** Stiff from hunching, loses mobility
**Neck:** Strained from forward head posture
**Glutes:** Weakened and tight from being sat on
**Hamstrings:** Feel tight but are actually lengthened

## At-Desk Stretches (No Floor Required)

Do these every 1-2 hours. Each takes under a minute.

### Seated Hip Flexor Stretch
- Sit on edge of chair, scoot one leg back
- Keep that foot flat, lean forward slightly
- Feel stretch in front of back hip
- Hold 30-45 seconds each side

### Seated Figure-4
- Cross one ankle over opposite knee
- Sit tall, hinge forward from hips
- Feel stretch in outer hip/glute
- Hold 30-45 seconds each side

### Seated Spinal Twist
- Sit tall, cross right leg over left
- Twist upper body to right, use left hand on outside of right knee
- Keep sitting tall, don't round
- Hold 20-30 seconds each side

### Chest Doorway Stretch
- Forearm on door frame, elbow at shoulder height
- Step through door until chest stretches
- Hold 30-45 seconds each side

### Upper Trap Stretch
- Sit, hold chair with right hand
- Tilt head left, gently rotate to look at floor
- Optional: light pressure with left hand
- Hold 30-45 seconds each side

### Chin Tucks
- Sit tall, draw chin straight back (double chin)
- Hold 5 seconds, repeat 10-15 times
- Counteracts forward head posture

### Neck Rotations
- Slowly turn head fully left, hold 5 seconds
- Return center, turn right
- 5 each direction

### Shoulder Rolls
- Big circles: up, back, down, forward
- 10 forward, 10 backward
- Release upper trap tension

### Wrist Stretches
- Extend arm, palm up, pull fingers back with other hand
- Then palm down, pull fingers toward you
- Hold 15-20 seconds each position, each hand

### Standing Back Extension
- Stand, hands on lower back
- Gently lean backward
- Hold 2-3 seconds, repeat 5-10 times
- Do after prolonged sitting

## At-Home Deep Stretches (Floor Routine)

Do this routine after work for deeper release. Takes 15-20 minutes.

### Hip Flexor Stretch (Half Kneeling)
- Kneel on one knee (pad under knee)
- Other foot forward, 90 degrees
- Tuck pelvis under (flatten lower back)
- Lean forward slightly
- Hold 60-90 seconds each side
- **The most important stretch for desk workers**

### Couch Stretch (Intense Hip Flexor)
- Kneel facing away from couch
- Put back foot on couch cushion
- Front foot forward in lunge
- Work toward upright torso
- Hold 60-90 seconds each side

### Supine Figure-4
- Lie on back, cross ankle over opposite knee
- Pull bottom leg toward chest
- Relax shoulders and head
- Hold 60-90 seconds each side

### Supine Twist
- Lie on back, knees bent
- Drop both knees to one side
- Keep shoulders on floor
- Hold 60 seconds each side

### Cat-Cow
- All fours, alternate arching and rounding
- Move slowly through full range
- 15-20 cycles

### Thread the Needle
- All fours, reach one arm under body
- Let upper back rotate, shoulder toward floor
- Hold 30 seconds, alternate
- 5 each side

### Child's Pose
- Sit back on heels, reach arms forward
- Let entire back release
- Hold 60-90 seconds

### Chest Stretch (Floor)
- Lie on foam roller lengthwise (along spine)
- Arms out to sides, palms up
- Let arms fall toward floor
- Hold 60-90 seconds

### Thoracic Extension
- Foam roller across mid-back
- Support head with hands
- Extend over roller (open chest to ceiling)
- Move roller up and down spine

### Hamstring Stretch
- Lie on back, one leg up
- Use strap or towel around foot
- Keep knee as straight as comfortable
- Hold 60 seconds each side

### Calf Stretch
- Wall stretch, back leg straight, heel down
- Hold 45 seconds each side
- Repeat with knee slightly bent

## Quick Routines

### 2-Minute Desk Break
1. Chin tucks x 10
2. Shoulder rolls x 10 each way
3. Seated twist 15 sec each
4. Neck stretches 15 sec each side

### 5-Minute Reset
1. Standing back extension x 10
2. Doorway chest stretch 30 sec each
3. Upper trap stretch 30 sec each
4. Seated hip flexor 30 sec each
5. Seated figure-4 30 sec each

### 10-Minute Lunch Break
1. Walk 2 minutes first
2. All at-desk stretches (30 sec each position)
3. Standing back extension
4. March in place 30 seconds

### 15-Minute Evening Routine
1. Cat-cow x 15
2. Hip flexor stretch 60 sec each
3. Figure-4 stretch 60 sec each
4. Thread the needle 30 sec each
5. Supine twist 45 sec each
6. Chest stretch 60 sec
7. Child's pose 60 sec

## Strengthening to Complement Stretching

Stretching alone isn't enough. Strengthen what's weak:

**For Forward Head:** Chin tucks with resistance, deep neck flexor strengthening

**For Rounded Shoulders:** Face pulls, band pull-aparts, rows, Y-T-W raises

**For Weak Core:** Dead bugs, planks, bird dogs

**For Weak Glutes:** Glute bridges, clamshells, hip hinges

Do strengthening 3x per week for lasting change.

## Posture Reminders

**Monitor:** Top at eye level, arm's length away

**Keyboard:** Elbows at 90 degrees, shoulders relaxed

**Chair:** Feet flat, lumbar support, sitting tall

**Phone:** At chest level, not lap

**Breaks:** Stand every 30-45 minutes minimum

**Movement:** Walk or stretch every hour

## Making It a Habit

**Tie to existing triggers:**
- Do chin tucks every time you check email
- Stretch when you get coffee
- Do shoulder rolls after meetings
- Full routine immediately after work, before sitting on couch

**Use reminders:**
- Set phone alarm every hour
- Desktop app reminders
- Sticky note on monitor

**Track it:**
- Check off each day
- Notice how you feel after consistent practice

## The Bottom Line

Desk work creates predictable problems. These stretches specifically counter what sitting does to your body:

- **Hip flexors:** Lengthen what sitting shortens
- **Chest/shoulders:** Open what sitting closes
- **Upper back:** Mobilize what sitting stiffens
- **Neck:** Reset what screens push forward

The key is consistency—a little every day beats a lot once a week. Even 2 minutes every hour makes a measurable difference.

Your desk job doesn't have to wreck your body. Fight back with movement.
