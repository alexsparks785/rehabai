# How to Do a Plank: Perfect Your Form and Build Core Strength

The plank is one of the most effective core exercises ever invented—when done correctly. Unfortunately, most people do planks with poor form, limiting results and risking injury. Here's how to master this foundational exercise.

**Reading time:** 7 minutes

## Why Planks Work

The plank builds:
- **Core stability** - the ability to resist movement under load
- **Deep core strength** - transverse abdominis, the "corset" muscle
- **Back protection** - supports the spine during all activities
- **Total body tension** - integrates upper and lower body
- **Posture** - reinforces proper alignment

Unlike crunches, planks train your core the way it actually functions—as a stabilizer, not a mover.

## The Basic Forearm Plank

### Setup

1. Start face down on the floor
2. Place forearms on ground, elbows under shoulders
3. Clasp hands together or keep forearms parallel
4. Tuck toes under, ready to lift

### The Position

1. Lift your body off the ground
2. Form a straight line from head to heels
3. Engage your core—imagine bracing for a punch
4. Squeeze your glutes
5. Keep your neck neutral (look at the floor)

### Common Mistakes

**Hips Too High (Pike Position)**
- Creates a mountain shape
- Reduces core engagement
- Fix: Lower hips until body is straight

**Hips Sagging**
- Most common error
- Strains lower back
- Fix: Squeeze glutes, tilt pelvis slightly under

**Head Dropping or Lifting**
- Strains neck
- Fix: Keep ears in line with shoulders, look at floor

**Holding Breath**
- Increases blood pressure
- Reduces endurance
- Fix: Breathe normally throughout

**Elbows Too Far Forward**
- Increases shoulder strain
- Fix: Keep elbows directly under shoulders

## Plank Progressions

### Level 1: Knee Plank

If full plank is too difficult:

1. Forearms on ground, knees on ground
2. Straight line from head to knees
3. Same core engagement
4. Build to 30-60 seconds

### Level 2: Standard Forearm Plank

1. Full plank on forearms and toes
2. Straight line head to heels
3. Build to 60 seconds

### Level 3: High Plank (Straight Arm)

1. Push-up position, arms straight
2. Hands under shoulders
3. Greater challenge for shoulders
4. Build to 60 seconds

### Level 4: Plank with Leg Lift

1. Standard plank position
2. Lift one leg 6 inches off ground
3. Hold 5 seconds
4. Switch legs
5. 5-10 lifts each side

### Level 5: Plank with Arm Reach

1. Standard plank position
2. Extend one arm forward
3. Hold 5 seconds
4. Switch arms
5. Maintain hip stability

### Level 6: Body Saw

1. Forearm plank position
2. Rock body forward (shoulders past elbows)
3. Rock back (shoulders behind elbows)
4. Control movement, maintain alignment
5. 10-15 reps

## Side Plank

Targets obliques and lateral stability.

### Basic Side Plank

1. Lie on side, elbow under shoulder
2. Stack or stagger feet
3. Lift hips off ground
4. Straight line from head to feet
5. Hold 20-30 seconds each side

### Side Plank on Knees

Easier variation:

1. Same position, bottom knee on ground
2. Straight line from head to knee
3. Good starting point

### Side Plank with Hip Dip

Advanced variation:

1. Side plank position
2. Lower hip toward ground
3. Lift back up
4. 10-12 reps each side

### Side Plank with Rotation

1. Side plank position
2. Reach top arm under body
3. Rotate back, arm toward ceiling
4. 8-10 reps each side

## How Long Should You Hold a Plank?

### Quality Over Duration

A 20-second plank with perfect form beats a 2-minute plank with sagging hips.

### Recommended Durations

- **Beginner:** 10-30 seconds
- **Intermediate:** 30-60 seconds
- **Advanced:** 60-90 seconds

### Sets

- 3-4 sets is plenty
- Rest 30-60 seconds between sets
- Stop when form breaks down

### The Truth About Long Planks

Holding a plank for several minutes isn't necessary:
- After 60-90 seconds, benefits diminish
- Better to progress to harder variations
- Focus on quality and tension, not time

## Plank Variations for Different Goals

### For Beginners
- Knee plank
- Wall plank (hands on wall, body at angle)
- Incline plank (hands on bench)

### For Core Stability
- Standard forearm plank
- Dead bug (related movement)
- Bird dog (related movement)

### For Obliques
- Side plank
- Side plank with hip dip
- Side plank with rotation

### For Athletes
- Body saw
- Plank with shoulder tap
- Plank with leg lift
- Plank on unstable surface

### For Anti-Rotation
- Plank with single arm reach
- Plank with resistance band pull
- Pallof press (standing plank alternative)

## Common Questions

### Why does my lower back hurt during planks?

Usually from:
1. Hips sagging (most common)
2. Not engaging glutes
3. Core too weak—start with easier variation

Fix: Squeeze glutes, tuck pelvis slightly, use knee plank if needed.

### Why do my shoulders hurt?

Usually from:
1. Elbows too far forward
2. Shoulder blades not engaged
3. Holding too long

Fix: Elbows under shoulders, pull shoulder blades down and back.

### How often should I do planks?

- Can be done daily for short durations
- Or 3-4 times per week with other core work
- Part of a complete core routine, not the only exercise

### Are planks better than crunches?

For most purposes, yes:
- Planks train core stability (how it actually functions)
- Lower back strain
- More functional carryover
- But both can be part of a complete program

## Sample Plank Workouts

### Beginner Core Circuit

1. Knee Plank: 3 × 20-30 seconds
2. Dead Bug: 3 × 8 each side
3. Bird Dog: 3 × 8 each side
4. Glute Bridge: 3 × 12

### Intermediate Plank Challenge

1. Forearm Plank: 3 × 45 seconds
2. Side Plank: 3 × 30 seconds each side
3. Plank with Leg Lift: 3 × 8 each leg
4. Body Saw: 3 × 10

### Advanced Core Session

1. High Plank: 2 × 45 seconds
2. Forearm Plank with Shoulder Tap: 3 × 10 each side
3. Side Plank with Hip Dip: 3 × 10 each side
4. Body Saw: 3 × 12
5. Plank with Arm Reach: 3 × 8 each side

## Key Technique Cues

Remember these during every plank:

1. **Straight line** - head to heels (or head to knees)
2. **Squeeze glutes** - protects lower back
3. **Brace core** - like preparing for a punch
4. **Breathe** - don't hold your breath
5. **Elbows under shoulders** - protects joints
6. **Neutral neck** - look at floor between hands

## Key Takeaways

1. **Form over time** - perfect 20 seconds beats sloppy 2 minutes
2. **Progress the variation** - not just the duration
3. **Squeeze glutes** - prevents lower back sagging
4. **Breathe normally** - don't hold breath
5. **Include side planks** - complete core training
6. **Stop when form fails** - that's your real limit

The plank is simple but not easy. Master the basics, progress thoughtfully, and this single exercise can transform your core strength.
