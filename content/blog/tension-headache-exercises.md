# Tension Headache Exercises: Natural Relief for Head and Neck Pain

Tension headaches—that dull, pressing pain that wraps around your head like a tight band—are the most common type of headache. While medications can help, exercises that target the muscles involved often provide lasting relief without side effects.

## Understanding Tension Headaches

### What Causes Them

Tension headaches typically involve tight, overworked muscles in the:

- **Suboccipitals** (base of skull)
- **Upper trapezius** (top of shoulders)
- **Levator scapulae** (neck to shoulder blade)
- **Sternocleidomastoid** (front/side of neck)
- **Temporalis** (sides of head/temples)
- **Masseter** (jaw muscles)

These muscles become tense from:
- Poor posture (especially forward head)
- Stress and anxiety
- Eye strain
- Jaw clenching
- Prolonged sitting
- Sleeping position

### Signs of Tension Headache

- Dull, aching pain (not throbbing)
- Pressure or tightness around forehead or back of head
- Tenderness in scalp, neck, and shoulders
- Often both sides of head
- Not worsened by physical activity
- No nausea or light sensitivity (unlike migraines)

## Immediate Relief Exercises

When a headache hits, try these first.

### Suboccipital Release

The suboccipitals at the skull base are often the main culprits.

**Finger Pressure:**
1. Place fingertips at base of skull
2. Find the bony ridge and muscles just below
3. Apply steady pressure (not painful)
4. Hold 60-90 seconds
5. Small nodding movements while pressing

**Tennis Ball Release:**
1. Lie on back, tennis ball under skull base
2. Let head relax into ball
3. Slowly turn head side to side
4. Find tender spots and hold
5. 2-3 minutes

### Neck Stretches

**Upper Trapezius:**
1. Drop ear toward shoulder
2. Gently assist with same-side hand
3. Keep opposite shoulder down
4. Hold 30-45 seconds each side

**Levator Scapulae:**
1. Turn head 45° to one side
2. Look down toward armpit
3. Gently pull head with hand
4. Hold 30-45 seconds each side

**SCM Stretch:**
1. Turn head to one side
2. Tilt chin up slightly
3. Feel stretch in front of neck
4. Hold 30 seconds each side

### Chin Tucks

Relieves tension from forward head posture.

1. Sit or stand tall
2. Pull chin straight back (not down)
3. Create a "double chin"
4. Hold 5-10 seconds
5. Repeat 10-15 times

### Temple Massage

1. Place fingertips on temples
2. Apply gentle circular pressure
3. 10-15 circles in each direction
4. Then move to different spots around temples

### Jaw Release

If you clench your jaw:

1. Place tongue on roof of mouth
2. Open mouth while keeping tongue in place
3. This relaxes the jaw muscles
4. Hold 10 seconds, repeat 5 times

## Preventive Exercise Routine

Do these daily to reduce headache frequency.

### Neck Mobility

**Neck Rotations:**
1. Slowly turn head left
2. Hold 2 seconds
3. Turn right
4. 10 repetitions each direction

**Neck Side Bends:**
1. Drop ear toward shoulder
2. Hold 2 seconds
3. Repeat other side
4. 10 repetitions each side

**Neck Flexion/Extension:**
1. Drop chin to chest
2. Hold 2 seconds
3. Look up gently
4. 10 repetitions

### Shoulder Rolls

Releases upper trapezius tension.

1. Roll shoulders backward in large circles
2. 10-15 rolls
3. Then forward 10-15 rolls

### Shoulder Blade Squeezes

Counteracts rounded shoulder posture.

1. Sit or stand tall
2. Squeeze shoulder blades together
3. Hold 5 seconds
4. Release
5. 10-15 repetitions

### Cat-Cow

Mobilizes the entire spine.

1. Hands and knees position
2. Arch back, drop head (cat)
3. Sag back, lift head (cow)
4. 10 slow cycles

### Deep Breathing

Stress contributes to tension headaches.

**Diaphragmatic Breathing:**
1. Place hand on belly
2. Breathe in through nose, belly rises
3. Slow exhale through mouth
4. 10 deep breaths

## Strengthening Exercises

Weak muscles fatigue and tense up. Strengthen to prevent.

### Deep Neck Flexor Strengthening

These muscles support proper head position.

**Chin Tuck with Hold:**
1. Lie on back without pillow
2. Tuck chin (flatten neck to floor)
3. Hold 10 seconds
4. 10-15 repetitions

**Progression:**
1. Same position, chin tucked
2. Lift head 1 inch off floor
3. Hold 5 seconds, maintaining tuck
4. Work up to 10 reps

### Upper Back Strengthening

Weak upper back leads to forward head posture.

**Prone Y-T-W:**
1. Lie face down
2. Y: Arms overhead, lift with thumbs up
3. T: Arms to sides, lift
4. W: Arms bent, elbows up, squeeze shoulder blades
5. Hold each 5 seconds, 10 reps each

**Face Pulls (with band):**
1. Band at face height
2. Pull toward face, elbows high
3. Squeeze shoulder blades
4. 15-20 reps

### Wall Angels

Restores shoulder and upper back mobility.

1. Back flat against wall
2. Arms in "goalpost" position
3. Slide arms up, keeping contact with wall
4. Lower and repeat
5. 10-15 repetitions

## Posture Correction

Poor posture is a primary cause of tension headaches.

### Workstation Setup

- Screen at eye level
- 20-26 inches from face
- Keyboard at elbow height
- Chair supports lower back
- Feet flat on floor

### Posture Cues

- Ears over shoulders
- Shoulders over hips
- Chin level (not jutting forward)
- Relax shoulders down

### Movement Breaks

Every 30-60 minutes:
1. Stand up
2. Chin tucks: 5 reps
3. Shoulder rolls: 10 each direction
4. Quick neck stretches: 15 seconds each
5. Deep breaths: 5 breaths

## Jaw Exercises

Jaw tension contributes to many tension headaches.

### Jaw Relaxation

**Resting Position:**
- Lips together, teeth apart
- Tongue rests on roof of mouth
- Jaw muscles relaxed

Practice this awareness throughout the day.

### Jaw Stretches

**Controlled Opening:**
1. Place tongue on roof of mouth
2. Slowly open mouth
3. Only go as far as comfortable
4. 10 repetitions

**Resisted Opening:**
1. Place fist under chin
2. Open mouth against gentle resistance
3. Hold 5 seconds
4. 10 repetitions

**Lateral Jaw Movement:**
1. Move jaw side to side slowly
2. Stay within comfortable range
3. 10 repetitions each direction

## Sample Routines

### Morning Prevention (5 minutes)

1. Neck mobility: All directions, 5 each
2. Shoulder rolls: 10 each direction
3. Chin tucks: 10 reps
4. Upper trap stretch: 30 sec each
5. Deep breathing: 5 breaths

### Workday Routine (Every 1-2 hours, 2 minutes)

1. Chin tucks: 5 reps
2. Shoulder rolls: 10 backward
3. Neck rotation: 5 each way
4. Shoulder blade squeeze: 5 reps
5. 3 deep breaths

### Evening Relaxation (10 minutes)

1. Suboccipital release: 2 minutes
2. All neck stretches: 45 sec each
3. Temple massage: 1 minute
4. Jaw relaxation: 1 minute
5. Cat-cow: 10 cycles
6. Prone Y-T-W: 10 each
7. Deep breathing: 10 breaths

### Acute Headache Relief (5-10 minutes)

1. Suboccipital release (tennis ball): 3 minutes
2. Upper trap stretch: 60 sec each
3. Levator stretch: 60 sec each
4. Temple massage: 2 minutes
5. Chin tucks: 15 reps
6. Deep breathing: 10 breaths

## Other Helpful Strategies

### Heat Application

- Warm compress on neck/shoulders
- 15-20 minutes
- Relaxes tight muscles

### Hydration

Dehydration contributes to headaches. Drink water consistently throughout day.

### Eye Strain Prevention

- Follow 20-20-20 rule: Every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds
- Adjust screen brightness
- Consider blue light glasses

### Sleep Position

- Avoid stomach sleeping
- Use supportive pillow
- Keep neck neutral

### Stress Management

- Regular exercise
- Adequate sleep
- Mindfulness or meditation
- Time management

## When to See a Doctor

Seek medical evaluation if:

- Severe sudden headache ("worst headache of life")
- Headaches with fever, stiff neck, confusion
- Headaches after head injury
- New headache pattern after age 50
- Headaches that wake you from sleep
- Progressively worsening headaches
- Headaches not relieved by over-the-counter medications
- Neurological symptoms (vision changes, weakness, numbness)

## Progress Expectations

**Week 1-2:**
- Learning exercises
- Some immediate relief with acute routine

**Week 2-4:**
- Reduced headache frequency
- Better posture awareness

**Week 4-8:**
- Significantly fewer headaches
- Muscle strength improving

**Long-term:**
- Maintenance with daily routine
- Quick intervention at first sign of tension
- Rare headaches

## The Bottom Line

Tension headaches respond well to:

1. **Release tight muscles** (suboccipitals, traps, jaw)
2. **Stretch regularly** (neck, shoulders)
3. **Strengthen weak muscles** (deep neck flexors, upper back)
4. **Improve posture** (workstation setup, movement breaks)
5. **Manage stress** (breathing, lifestyle)

Most people see significant improvement within 2-4 weeks of consistent exercise. The key is addressing the root cause—muscle tension and poor posture—rather than just treating symptoms.
