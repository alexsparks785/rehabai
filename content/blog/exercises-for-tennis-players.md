# Exercises for Tennis Players: Improve Your Game and Prevent Injury

Tennis demands explosive power, quick direction changes, shoulder stability, and endurance—all while repeating the same motions thousands of times. The right training program improves performance and keeps you injury-free.

## Physical Demands of Tennis

### What Tennis Requires

- **Rotational power:** For serves and groundstrokes
- **Shoulder stability:** Repetitive overhead motions
- **Core strength:** Transfers power from legs to racket
- **Lateral agility:** Quick side-to-side movement
- **Leg power:** Explosive starts and stops
- **Wrist/forearm strength:** Racket control
- **Endurance:** Matches can last hours

### Common Tennis Injuries

- Tennis elbow (lateral epicondylitis)
- Shoulder impingement/rotator cuff
- Lower back pain
- Wrist injuries
- Knee issues
- Ankle sprains

Training addresses both performance and injury prevention.

## Shoulder Exercises

### External Rotation

Essential for rotator cuff health.

**Side-Lying:**
1. Lie on side, elbow at 90°
2. Rotate forearm toward ceiling
3. Keep elbow pinned to side
4. 15 reps each arm, 2-3 sets

**Standing with Band:**
1. Band at elbow height
2. Elbow at side, bent 90°
3. Rotate forearm outward
4. 15 reps each arm

### Internal Rotation

**With Band:**
1. Band anchored outside
2. Pull across body, rotating inward
3. Keep elbow at side
4. 15 reps each arm

### Y-T-W Raises

Scapular stability for serving.

1. Lie face down or stand bent over
2. Y position: Arms overhead, thumbs up
3. T position: Arms to sides
4. W position: Elbows bent, squeeze shoulder blades
5. 10-12 each position

### Face Pulls

1. Band at face height
2. Pull toward face, elbows high
3. Squeeze shoulder blades
4. 15-20 reps, 3 sets

### Scapular Push-Ups

1. Plank position
2. Lower by letting shoulder blades come together
3. Push back up, spreading shoulder blades
4. 12-15 reps

## Core and Rotation

### Medicine Ball Rotational Throws

**Side Throw:**
1. Stand sideways to wall
2. Rotate and throw med ball into wall
3. Catch and repeat
4. 10-12 each side

**Overhead Throw (Serve Simulation):**
1. Stand facing away from wall
2. Overhead throw with rotation
3. 10-12 reps

### Pallof Press

Anti-rotation for core stability.

1. Band at chest height, stand sideways
2. Press arms straight out
3. Resist rotation
4. Hold 5-10 seconds
5. 10 reps each side

### Cable/Band Chops

**High to Low (Forehand Simulation):**
1. Start high on one side
2. Pull diagonally across body
3. Rotate through core
4. 12-15 each side

**Low to High (One-Handed Backhand):**
1. Start low on one side
2. Pull diagonally up and across
3. 12-15 each side

### Russian Twists

1. Seated, lean back slightly
2. Rotate side to side
3. Can hold weight
4. 20 total (10 each side)

### Dead Bug with Rotation

1. On back, knees up
2. Extend opposite arm and leg
3. Add slight rotation
4. 10 each side

### Plank Rotations

1. Start in plank
2. Rotate to side plank
3. Return to plank
4. Rotate other side
5. 10 total

## Leg Power and Agility

### Split Squats

1. One foot forward, one back
2. Lower until both knees at 90°
3. Drive up explosively
4. 10-12 each leg

### Lateral Lunges

1. Wide stance
2. Shift weight to one side
3. Push back to center
4. 12 each side

### Lateral Bounds

1. Stand on one leg
2. Jump sideways to other leg
3. Stick landing, balance
4. 10 each direction

### Box Jumps

1. Face box
2. Jump up, land softly
3. Step down
4. 10-12 reps

### Single-Leg Romanian Deadlift

1. Stand on one leg
2. Hinge at hip
3. Return to standing
4. 10 each leg

### Calf Raises

For push-off power:
1. Single-leg calf raises
2. 15-20 each leg
3. 3 sets

## Forearm and Wrist

### Wrist Curls

**Flexion:**
1. Forearm on table, palm up
2. Curl wrist up
3. 15 reps

**Extension:**
1. Palm down
2. Raise back of hand
3. 15 reps

### Wrist Rotation

1. Hold hammer or weighted bar at end
2. Rotate forearm
3. 15 each direction

### Grip Strengthening

1. Squeeze tennis ball or grip trainer
2. 3 sets of 15-20

### Rubber Band Extension

1. Rubber band around fingers
2. Spread against resistance
3. 20 reps
4. Balances grip work

## Agility and Footwork

### Ladder Drills

If you have a ladder, or imagine one:

1. **Quick feet:** Both feet in each square
2. **Lateral shuffle:** Move sideways
3. **In-out:** Feet in, feet out
4. **Icky shuffle:** Complex pattern

### Split-Step Practice

1. Small jump, land in athletic stance
2. React and move in direction
3. 10-15 reps

### Cone Drills

**5-10-5 Shuttle:**
1. Sprint 5 yards, touch
2. Sprint 10 yards other direction, touch
3. Sprint 5 yards back

**Star Drill:**
1. Cones in star pattern
2. Sprint to each cone and back to center
3. Mix up directions

### Shadow Tennis

1. Practice footwork without ball
2. Imagine points
3. Work on split-steps and recovery
4. 3-5 minutes

## Sample Programs

### Pre-Season (4-6 weeks before)

**3x Per Week, 45 minutes:**

*Day 1 (Power):*
1. Med ball rotational throws: 3×10 each
2. Box jumps: 3×10
3. Split squats: 3×12 each
4. Lateral bounds: 3×10 each

*Day 2 (Stability):*
1. External rotation: 3×15 each
2. Y-T-W: 2×12 each
3. Pallof press: 3×10 each
4. Single-leg RDL: 3×10 each
5. Plank: 3×45 sec

*Day 3 (Endurance/Agility):*
1. Ladder drills: 10 min
2. Cone drills: 10 min
3. Split-step practice: 5 min
4. Shadow tennis: 5 min

### In-Season Maintenance

**2x Per Week, 30 minutes:**

*Day 1:*
1. External rotation: 2×12 each
2. Med ball throws: 2×10 each
3. Pallof press: 2×10 each
4. Lateral lunges: 2×10 each
5. Calf raises: 2×15

*Day 2:*
1. Face pulls: 2×15
2. Cable chops: 2×10 each
3. Single-leg work: 2×10 each
4. Agility drills: 5-10 min

### Injury Prevention Focus

**Daily (10 minutes):**
1. External rotation: 15 each
2. Wrist curls (both): 15 each
3. Rubber band extensions: 20
4. Shoulder stretches: 30 sec each

## Pre-Match Warm-Up

### Dynamic Warm-Up (10 minutes)

1. Jogging: 2 min
2. High knees: 30 sec
3. Butt kicks: 30 sec
4. Lateral shuffle: 1 min
5. Arm circles: 30 sec
6. Torso rotations: 30 sec
7. Leg swings: 10 each
8. Shadow strokes: 2 min
9. Light groundstrokes: 3-5 min

## Post-Match Recovery

### Stretching (10 minutes)

1. Shoulder stretch (cross-body): 30 sec each
2. Tricep stretch: 30 sec each
3. Forearm stretches: 30 sec each
4. Hip flexor stretch: 45 sec each
5. Quad stretch: 30 sec each
6. Calf stretch: 30 sec each
7. Supine twist: 45 sec each side

### Foam Rolling

1. Upper back: 1 min
2. Lats: 30 sec each
3. Quads: 1 min
4. IT band: 30 sec each
5. Calves: 30 sec each

## The Bottom Line

Tennis-specific training requires:

1. **Shoulder stability** (rotator cuff, scapular work)
2. **Rotational power** (med ball, chops)
3. **Core strength** (Pallof press, planks)
4. **Leg power** (split squats, bounds)
5. **Agility** (ladder, cone drills)
6. **Forearm balance** (flexors AND extensors)
7. **Recovery** (stretching, foam rolling)

Train like a tennis player, and you'll play like one—with power, agility, and durability to last through long matches and long seasons.
