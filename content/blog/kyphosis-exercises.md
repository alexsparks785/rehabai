# Kyphosis Exercises: Correct Rounded Upper Back Posture

That hunched, rounded upper back that makes you look older and feel stiff? That's kyphosis—an excessive forward curvature of the thoracic spine. Whether it's from years of desk work, phone use, or aging, the right exercises can significantly improve your posture and reduce associated pain.

## Understanding Kyphosis

The thoracic spine (upper and mid-back) naturally has a slight forward curve. Kyphosis occurs when this curve becomes excessive, creating a hunched appearance.

### Types of Kyphosis

**Postural kyphosis:** Most common, caused by poor posture habits. Fully correctable with exercise.

**Scheuermann's kyphosis:** Structural, develops during adolescence. Exercise helps but may not fully correct.

**Age-related kyphosis:** Often involves osteoporosis and vertebral changes. Exercise can prevent worsening and improve function.

### Associated Problems

- Neck pain and headaches
- Upper back pain and stiffness
- Shoulder problems
- Reduced lung capacity
- Balance issues
- Fatigue

### The Muscle Imbalance

**Tight/shortened:**
- Pectoralis major and minor
- Upper abdominals
- Anterior shoulder muscles
- Neck flexors

**Weak/lengthened:**
- Thoracic extensors
- Lower and middle trapezius
- Rhomboids
- Deep neck flexors

Correcting kyphosis requires stretching the tight muscles and strengthening the weak ones.

## Phase 1: Mobility and Release

Start by improving thoracic mobility and releasing tight structures.

### Foam Roller Thoracic Extension

The most effective mobility exercise for kyphosis:

1. Lie on a foam roller placed horizontally across your upper back
2. Support your head with your hands
3. Let your upper back extend over the roller
4. Move the roller to different segments (upper, middle)
5. Spend 30-60 seconds at each level
6. Total: 2-3 minutes

**Key:** Keep your ribs down—don't just arch at the lower back.

### Thoracic Extension Over Towel Roll

Gentler alternative:
1. Roll a towel into a cylinder
2. Lie on your back with the towel across your upper back
3. Arms out to sides or overhead
4. Breathe deeply and relax
5. Hold 2-3 minutes

### Cat-Cow Focus on Extension

1. Start on hands and knees
2. Let your belly drop, lifting your head and tailbone (cow)
3. Focus on moving through the UPPER back, not just lower
4. Hold the extended position 5 seconds
5. Round back (cat) gently
6. 10-15 repetitions, emphasizing the extension

### Thread the Needle

Thoracic rotation:
1. Start on hands and knees
2. Thread one arm under your body
3. Let your shoulder lower toward the floor
4. Rotate as far as comfortable
5. Hold 30 seconds
6. Return and repeat other side
7. 3 repetitions each side

### Open Book

1. Lie on your side, knees bent
2. Arms extended in front of you, palms together
3. Rotate your top arm open, following it with your eyes
4. Let your chest open toward the ceiling
5. Keep knees stacked together
6. Hold 30 seconds
7. 3 repetitions each side

### Pec Stretch (Doorway)

1. Stand in a doorway
2. Place forearms on the door frame, elbows at 90 degrees
3. Step one foot through the doorway
4. Lean forward until you feel chest stretch
5. Hold 30-45 seconds
6. Repeat with elbows higher (targets different pec fibers)

### Corner Stretch

1. Face a corner
2. Place forearms on each wall
3. Lean into the corner
4. Feel stretch across chest
5. Hold 45-60 seconds
6. Repeat 2-3 times

## Phase 2: Strengthen the Upper Back

Build strength in the muscles that extend and stabilize the thoracic spine.

### Prone Y-T-W-L Raises

Excellent for mid/lower traps and rhomboids:

**Y's:**
1. Lie face down, arms at sides
2. Raise arms at 45 degrees, thumbs up
3. Squeeze shoulder blades together
4. Hold 2-3 seconds
5. Lower slowly
6. 10-15 reps

**T's:**
1. Same position
2. Raise arms directly to sides
3. 10-15 reps

**W's:**
1. Same position, elbows bent
2. Rotate arms up, squeezing shoulder blades
3. 10-15 reps

**L's:**
1. Arms at 90 degrees from body, elbows bent 90 degrees
2. Rotate forearms toward ceiling
3. 10-15 reps

### Face Pulls

1. Attach band at face height
2. Grip with both hands
3. Pull toward face, separating hands
4. Elbows high, squeeze shoulder blades
5. 3 sets of 15

### Band Pull-Aparts

1. Hold band at shoulder height, arms straight
2. Pull band apart by squeezing shoulder blades
3. Keep arms at shoulder height
4. 3 sets of 15-20

### Prone Cobra

1. Lie face down, arms at sides
2. Lift chest and arms off floor
3. Rotate palms outward, squeeze shoulder blades
4. Hold 10-20 seconds
5. 3-5 repetitions

### Superman (Modified)

1. Lie face down
2. Lift just your chest and arms
3. Keep neck neutral (look at floor)
4. Hold 5-10 seconds
5. 10 repetitions

### Seated Cable Row (or Band Row)

1. Sit tall with good posture
2. Pull band/cable toward lower chest
3. Squeeze shoulder blades together at end
4. Control the return
5. 3 sets of 12-15

## Phase 3: Core and Postural Integration

Build the endurance to maintain good posture throughout the day.

### Wall Angels

1. Stand with back against wall
2. Press head, upper back, hips, and calves into wall
3. Arms at 90 degrees (goalpost position)
4. Slide arms up while maintaining all contact points
5. Slide back down
6. 2-3 sets of 10

**If your head doesn't touch the wall easily, this shows how much correction you need.**

### Wall Lean Thoracic Extension

1. Stand facing a wall, arm's length away
2. Place hands on wall at shoulder height
3. Let your chest drop toward the wall
4. Squeeze shoulder blades together
5. Hold 20-30 seconds
6. Repeat 3-5 times

### Chin Tucks (Against Wall)

1. Stand with back against wall
2. Try to press back of head into wall
3. Tuck chin (double chin)
4. Hold 5-10 seconds
5. 15 repetitions

### Quadruped Thoracic Extension

1. Start on hands and knees
2. Place one hand behind your head
3. Rotate that elbow toward the ceiling
4. Open your chest
5. Return and repeat
6. 10 reps each side

### Plank with Scapular Protraction/Retraction

1. Hold a plank position
2. Without bending elbows, push upper back toward ceiling
3. Let shoulder blades separate
4. Then squeeze shoulder blades together
5. Alternate: 10 reps

### Standing Thoracic Extension

1. Clasp hands behind your head
2. Lift elbows and look up
3. Gently extend upper back (not lower back)
4. Hold 5-10 seconds
5. 10 repetitions

## Daily Program

### Morning Routine (15 minutes)

1. Foam roller thoracic extension: 2-3 minutes
2. Cat-cow with extension focus: 10 reps
3. Thread the needle: 3 each side
4. Doorway pec stretch: 45 seconds each position
5. Prone Y-T-W: 10 reps each
6. Wall angels: 10 reps
7. Chin tucks: 15 reps

### Throughout the Day

**Hourly:**
- Posture check and reset
- Chin tuck: 5 reps
- Scapular squeeze: 5 reps, hold 5 seconds each

**During breaks:**
- Doorway stretch: 30 seconds
- Standing thoracic extension: 5 reps

### Evening Routine (10 minutes)

1. Open book: 3 each side
2. Thoracic extension over towel: 2 minutes
3. Face pulls or band pull-aparts: 15 reps
4. Prone cobra: 3 × 15 seconds
5. Corner stretch: 45 seconds

## Ergonomic Adjustments

### Computer Setup

- **Monitor:** Eye level or slightly above
- **Chair:** Supports natural spine curves
- **Keyboard:** Elbows at 90 degrees
- **Consider:** Standing desk or sit-stand setup

### Phone Use

- Raise phone to eye level
- Don't drop head forward
- Take frequent breaks

### Sleeping

- **Side sleepers:** Pillow that keeps spine neutral
- **Back sleepers:** Not too many pillows (keeps head forward)
- **Consider:** Small rolled towel under thoracic spine for 5-10 minutes before sleep

### Driving

- Adjust seat for good posture
- Use lumbar support
- Check that head isn't jutting forward

## Special Considerations

### Osteoporosis-Related Kyphosis

If kyphosis involves vertebral compression fractures:
- Be gentler with all exercises
- Avoid loaded flexion completely
- Focus on extension and posture
- Work with a physical therapist

### Scheuermann's Kyphosis

Structural changes limit how much correction is possible:
- Exercise prevents worsening
- Focus on pain reduction and function
- May benefit from bracing in adolescents
- Maintain realistic expectations

### Elderly Adults

- Start with gentler exercises
- Use chair support as needed
- Focus heavily on balance (kyphosis affects balance)
- Progress slowly

## Expected Timeline

- **Weeks 1-2:** Improved awareness, slightly easier to stand tall
- **Weeks 3-4:** Noticeable improvement in mobility
- **Weeks 6-8:** Visible postural improvement
- **Weeks 12+:** Significant correction for postural kyphosis
- **Ongoing:** Maintenance required—posture reverts without continued effort

**Note:** Structural kyphosis improves less than postural kyphosis, but exercise still helps with pain and function.

## When to Seek Help

See a healthcare provider if:

- Kyphosis is progressing rapidly
- You experience significant pain
- Numbness or tingling in arms
- Breathing difficulties
- Kyphosis appeared suddenly
- Balance problems or falls

A physical therapist can assess your specific situation and provide hands-on treatment.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Thoracic mobility first**—foam roller extension is foundational
2. **Stretch what's tight** (pecs, anterior shoulders)
3. **Strengthen what's weak** (mid/lower traps, rhomboids, extensors)
4. **Wall angels** reveal and correct your posture
5. **Frequent breaks** matter more than long sessions
6. **Fix your workspace**—ergonomics are essential
7. **Be patient**—posture changes over months, not days

Kyphosis develops over years, so correcting it takes time. But with consistent effort—stretching tight muscles, strengthening weak ones, and building postural awareness—significant improvement is possible. Stand tall, open your chest, and breathe deeply.
