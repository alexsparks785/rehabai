# Exercises for COPD: Breathe Better, Move Better

Living with COPD (Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease) doesn't mean giving up on exercise—quite the opposite. Regular physical activity is one of the most effective ways to manage COPD, reduce breathlessness, and maintain independence. Here's how to exercise safely with lung disease.

**Reading time:** 9 minutes

## How Exercise Helps COPD

Exercise benefits people with COPD by:
- **Improving muscle efficiency** - muscles need less oxygen for same work
- **Reducing breathlessness** - body adapts to handle exertion
- **Building endurance** - daily activities become easier
- **Strengthening breathing muscles** - diaphragm and intercostals
- **Improving quality of life** - more energy, better mood
- **Reducing hospitalizations** - fewer exacerbations

Many people with COPD avoid exercise because it makes them breathless. But avoiding activity makes COPD worse over time. The right exercise program breaks this cycle.

## Before You Start

### Medical Clearance
Talk to your doctor before starting, especially if you have:
- Severe COPD
- Need for supplemental oxygen
- Heart problems
- Recent hospitalization

### Understand Your Oxygen Needs
- Your doctor may prescribe oxygen during exercise
- Know your target oxygen saturation (usually >88-90%)
- Learn to use a pulse oximeter if recommended

### Know When to Stop
Stop exercise and rest if you experience:
- Severe shortness of breath
- Oxygen saturation drops below 88%
- Dizziness or confusion
- Chest pain
- Unusually rapid heart rate

## Breathing Techniques

Master these before starting exercise.

### Pursed Lip Breathing

Use during and after exercise:

1. Inhale slowly through nose (2 counts)
2. Purse lips like blowing a candle
3. Exhale slowly through pursed lips (4 counts)
4. Exhale should be twice as long as inhale

**Practice:** 5-10 minutes, several times daily

### Diaphragmatic Breathing

Strengthens your breathing muscle:

1. Sit or lie down comfortably
2. Place one hand on chest, one on belly
3. Breathe in through nose—belly should rise
4. Chest should stay relatively still
5. Exhale through pursed lips—belly falls
6. Practice 5-10 minutes daily

### Coordinated Breathing

Sync breath with movement:

1. Inhale during easier part of movement
2. Exhale during harder part
3. Never hold your breath
4. Use pursed lip breathing on exertion

## Aerobic Exercise

### Walking

Start here—it's the best exercise for COPD:

**Interval Approach:**
1. Walk at comfortable pace 1-2 minutes
2. Walk slightly faster 30-60 seconds
3. Return to comfortable pace
4. Repeat for total of 10-20 minutes
5. Rest as needed

**Progression:**
- Week 1-2: 10 minutes total, rest as needed
- Week 3-4: 15 minutes, longer intervals
- Week 5-6: 20 minutes
- Week 7+: 20-30 minutes

### Stationary Cycling

Low-impact alternative:

1. Start with 5-10 minutes
2. Very low resistance initially
3. Pedal at comfortable pace
4. Use pursed lip breathing
5. Progress duration before intensity

### Arm Ergometer (Arm Bike)

Important because arm activities often cause breathlessness:

1. Start with 5 minutes
2. Very light resistance
3. Coordinate breathing with movement
4. Build up gradually

### Swimming Pool Walking

Warm water, supported exercise:

1. Walk in chest-deep water
2. Water supports body weight
3. Warm, humid air may be easier to breathe
4. Start with 10-15 minutes

## Strength Training

Building muscle reduces demand on lungs.

### Seated Leg Extensions

1. Sit in chair with back supported
2. Extend one leg straight
3. Hold 2-3 seconds
4. Lower slowly
5. 10-12 reps each leg
6. Exhale while extending

### Wall Push-Ups

1. Hands on wall, shoulder height
2. Inhale as you lean in
3. Exhale as you push back
4. 8-12 reps

### Seated Rows

1. Resistance band around feet or anchored
2. Exhale as you pull handles toward waist
3. Inhale as you release
4. 10-12 reps

### Seated Arm Raises

1. Sit tall, light weights or no weights
2. Exhale as you raise arms to shoulder height
3. Inhale as you lower
4. 10-12 reps

### Chair Squats

1. Stand in front of chair
2. Inhale as you sit down slowly
3. Exhale as you stand up
4. Use armrests if needed
5. 8-10 reps

### Bicep Curls

1. Light weights or cans
2. Exhale as you curl up
3. Inhale as you lower
4. 12-15 reps

## Breathing Muscle Training

### Inspiratory Muscle Training (IMT)

Using a breathing trainer device:

1. Inhale against resistance
2. Strengthens diaphragm
3. 10-15 minutes daily
4. Talk to your doctor about appropriate device

### Active Breathing Exercises

**Segmental Breathing:**
1. Place hands on lower ribs
2. Breathe into that area specifically
3. Feel ribs expand sideways
4. 5-10 breaths

**Sustained Maximal Inspiration:**
1. Inhale as deeply as possible
2. Hold 3 seconds
3. Exhale slowly
4. 5-10 reps

## Flexibility and Stretching

Tight muscles make breathing harder.

### Chest Stretch

Opens breathing muscles:

1. Stand in doorway
2. Arms on doorframe at shoulder height
3. Step through gently
4. Feel chest open
5. Hold 30 seconds

### Side Stretch

Stretches breathing muscles:

1. Stand or sit tall
2. Raise right arm overhead
3. Lean gently to left
4. Breathe into stretched side
5. Hold 20-30 seconds each side

### Neck Stretch

Releases accessory breathing muscles:

1. Drop ear toward shoulder
2. Hold 20-30 seconds
3. Switch sides
4. Don't lift shoulder

## Managing Breathlessness During Exercise

### Pacing
- Go slowly
- Take breaks before you need them
- Alternate hard and easy activities
- Don't rush

### Positions for Recovery
**Leaning Forward:**
1. Sit in chair, lean forward
2. Elbows on knees
3. Relaxes shoulders, opens airways

**Standing with Support:**
1. Lean against wall
2. Or place hands on counter
3. Shoulders relaxed

### The Talk Test
- If you can't speak a short sentence, slow down
- If you can have a full conversation, you can push a bit harder
- Aim for "somewhat breathless but able to talk"

## Pulmonary Rehabilitation

If available, pulmonary rehab is highly recommended:

### What It Includes:
- Supervised exercise training
- Education about COPD
- Breathing techniques
- Nutritional counseling
- Psychological support

### Benefits:
- Improves exercise capacity by 15-25%
- Reduces hospitalization
- Improves quality of life
- Provides structured program

Ask your doctor about pulmonary rehabilitation.

## Environmental Considerations

### Avoid Exercising:
- In very cold air (can trigger bronchospasm)
- In very hot, humid conditions
- On high pollution days
- During pollen season if allergic

### Best Conditions:
- Moderate temperature
- Indoor controlled environment
- When symptoms are stable

## Using Supplemental Oxygen

If prescribed oxygen during exercise:

1. Use as directed by your doctor
2. Start oxygen before exercise
3. Continue during and after until recovered
4. May need higher flow rate during activity
5. Portable systems allow outdoor exercise

## Key Takeaways

1. **Exercise improves COPD** - muscles become more efficient
2. **Master breathing techniques** - pursed lip and diaphragmatic
3. **Coordinate breath with movement** - exhale on exertion
4. **Start low, go slow** - progress gradually
5. **Include all types** - aerobic, strength, and flexibility
6. **Rest when needed** - recovery positions help
7. **Consider pulmonary rehab** - supervised programs are very effective

Exercise may feel hard at first, but it's one of the best things you can do for COPD. Your body will adapt, activities will become easier, and your quality of life will improve.
