# Exercises for Text Neck: Fix Forward Head Posture

Your head weighs about 10-12 pounds in neutral position. Tilt it forward 45 degrees to look at your phone, and that effective weight increases to nearly 50 pounds on your neck. Do that for hours daily, and you get "text neck"—a modern epidemic.

The good news: text neck is reversible with the right exercises and habit changes.

## What Is Text Neck?

Text neck is forward head posture caused by repeatedly looking down at devices. Over time, this creates:

- **Tight muscles:** Suboccipitals, upper traps, levator scapulae, pectorals, SCM
- **Weak muscles:** Deep neck flexors, lower traps, rhomboids
- **Stiff joints:** Cervical and upper thoracic spine
- **Pain:** Neck, upper back, shoulders, headaches

## Signs You Have Text Neck

- Head sits forward of shoulders when viewed from side
- Rounded upper back
- Shoulders rolled forward
- Neck pain, especially after device use
- Tension headaches
- Upper back ache between shoulder blades
- Stiffness turning your head

## The Fix: Four Components

### Part 1: Stretch Tight Muscles

**Upper Trap Stretch**
- Sit tall, hold chair with right hand
- Tilt head left, gently rotate to look at floor
- Light pressure with left hand
- Hold 45-60 seconds each side
- **Do:** 2-3x daily

**Levator Scapulae Stretch**
- Same setup as upper trap
- Turn head 45° toward opposite armpit
- Look down toward that armpit
- Hold 45-60 seconds each side

**Suboccipital Stretch**
- Sit tall, tuck chin (double chin)
- Place fingers at base of skull
- Gently nod head forward
- Feel stretch at skull base
- Hold 30-45 seconds

**Chest/Pec Stretch**
- Doorway stretch: forearm on frame
- Step through until chest stretches
- Do at multiple arm angles (low, middle, high)
- Hold 45-60 seconds each position

**SCM Stretch**
- Turn head 45° to one side
- Tilt head back slightly
- Feel stretch on opposite front of neck
- Hold 30 seconds each side
- **Gentle:** This area is sensitive

### Part 2: Strengthen Weak Muscles

**Chin Tucks (Foundation Exercise)**
- Sit or stand tall
- Draw chin straight back (make double chin)
- Don't tilt head up or down
- Hold 5 seconds
- 15-20 reps, multiple times daily
- **Why:** Directly trains deep neck flexors and resets head position

**Chin Tucks with Resistance**
- Place two fingers on chin
- Push chin back against finger resistance
- Hold 5 seconds
- 15 reps, 3 sets
- **Why:** Adds strength component

**Chin Tuck with Lift**
- Lie face down, forehead on rolled towel
- Tuck chin, then lift head 1 inch off floor
- Keep chin tucked throughout
- Hold 5-10 seconds
- 10 reps, 3 sets
- **Why:** Strengthens deep neck flexors against gravity

**Prone Y-T-W Raises**
- Lie face down
- Y: Arms overhead at 45°, lift and squeeze
- T: Arms straight to sides, lift and squeeze
- W: Elbows bent, external rotation
- 10 each position, 2 sets

**Face Pulls**
- Band at face height
- Pull toward face, elbows high and wide
- Squeeze shoulder blades
- 15 reps, 3 sets

**Band Pull-Aparts**
- Band at chest height, arms straight
- Pull apart, squeeze shoulder blades
- 20 reps, 3 sets

### Part 3: Mobilize Stiff Joints

**Cervical Rotations**
- Sit tall, slowly turn head fully left
- Hold 5 seconds, return to center
- Turn right, hold 5 seconds
- 10 each direction
- **Key:** Keep chin level, don't let it drift up

**Cervical Side Bends**
- Sit tall, tilt ear toward shoulder
- Keep shoulders down and level
- Hold 5 seconds each side
- 10 each direction

**Thoracic Extension Over Roller**
- Foam roller across mid-back
- Support head with hands
- Extend backward over roller
- Move roller up and down spine
- 2-3 minutes

**Thread the Needle**
- All fours, reach one arm under body
- Rotate upper back, shoulder toward floor
- Hold 20-30 seconds each side
- 5 each side

**Cat-Cow with Neck**
- Standard cat-cow
- Add neck: look up during cow, tuck chin during cat
- 15 slow cycles

### Part 4: Retrain Position

**Wall Posture Reset**
- Stand with heels 2" from wall
- Back of head, upper back, butt touch wall
- Tuck chin slightly
- Hold 60 seconds
- **Why:** Teaches brain correct alignment

**Mirror Check**
- Stand sideways to mirror
- Check: Is your ear over your shoulder?
- Correct position if not
- Do frequently throughout day

**Phone Position Awareness**
- Bring phone up to eye level instead of looking down
- Use stands and holders
- Take breaks every 15-20 minutes

## Daily Routine for Text Neck

**Morning (5 minutes):**
- Chin tucks x 15
- Upper trap stretch 30 sec each
- Cat-cow with neck x 10
- Wall posture reset 30 sec

**Every Hour at Phone/Computer (1 minute):**
- Chin tucks x 10
- Neck rotations x 5 each
- Shoulder rolls x 10

**Evening (15 minutes):**
- Full stretching routine (Part 1)
- Strengthening exercises (Part 2)
- Thoracic mobility (Part 3)

## Phone and Device Habits

**Raise the device:** Eye level, not lap level

**Support your arms:** Elbows on table or armrests

**Take breaks:** Every 15-20 minutes, look up and move

**Use voice features:** Dictation, voice commands reduce looking down

**Limit duration:** Set timers for phone use

**Proper workstation:** Monitor at eye level, keyboard at elbow height

## Timeline for Improvement

**Week 1-2:** Exercises feel awkward, awareness increases

**Week 2-4:** Neck feels less tight, catching yourself in forward posture

**Week 4-8:** Visible improvement in head position

**Week 8-12:** Significant change for most people

**Ongoing:** Maintenance exercises and continued habit awareness

## Common Mistakes

**Only stretching:** You need to strengthen the weak muscles too

**Forgetting chin tucks:** This is the single most important exercise

**Not changing device habits:** Exercises can't overcome 8 hours of forward head position

**Expecting quick results:** Postural patterns take time to reverse

**Overstretching neck:** Be gentle—neck muscles are small and sensitive

## Red Flags

See a healthcare provider if:

- **Numbness or tingling** in arms or hands
- **Weakness** in arms or grip
- **Severe headaches** especially with neck movement
- **Dizziness** with neck movement
- **Pain that doesn't improve** after 4-6 weeks
- **Pain radiating down arm**

## The Bottom Line

Text neck is a modern problem with a straightforward solution:

1. **Stretch** what's tight (upper traps, chest, suboccipitals)
2. **Strengthen** what's weak (deep neck flexors, lower traps)
3. **Mobilize** stiff joints (cervical and thoracic spine)
4. **Change habits** (raise devices, take breaks)

The chin tuck is your most important exercise—do it throughout the day. Combined with the other exercises and device habit changes, most people see significant improvement within 8-12 weeks.

Your neck wasn't designed to hold your head forward all day. Give it the help it needs.
