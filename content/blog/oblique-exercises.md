# Oblique Exercises: Build Strong, Functional Side Abs

Your obliques—the muscles running along the sides of your torso—are far more than aesthetic "side abs." They're essential for rotation, anti-rotation, lateral flexion, and core stability. Building strong obliques improves athletic performance, protects your spine, and yes, creates that tapered waistline.

## Understanding the Obliques

There are two oblique muscles on each side of your torso:

**External obliques:**
- Larger, more superficial
- Run diagonally downward from ribs to pelvis
- Primary rotators of the trunk
- Active when rotating to opposite side

**Internal obliques:**
- Deeper, beneath external obliques
- Run diagonally upward
- Assist rotation and lateral flexion
- Active when rotating to same side

**Primary functions:**
- Trunk rotation (twisting)
- Anti-rotation (resisting rotation)
- Lateral flexion (side bending)
- Spinal stabilization
- Assist with breathing (forced exhalation)
- Compress abdominal contents

**Why they matter:**
- Essential for rotational sports (golf, tennis, throwing)
- Protect spine during twisting movements
- Transfer force between upper and lower body
- Support the spine under load
- Complete the core stabilization system

## Beginner Exercises

### Dead Bug (Anti-Rotation Focus)

1. Lie on back, arms toward ceiling
2. Hips and knees at 90 degrees
3. Lower opposite arm and leg
4. Keep lower back pressed to floor
5. Obliques work to prevent rotation
6. 10-12 repetitions each side

### Side Plank (Modified)

1. Lie on side, elbow under shoulder
2. Knees bent at 90 degrees
3. Lift hips off ground
4. Hold position
5. 20-30 seconds each side

### Bicycle Crunch

1. Lie on back, hands behind head
2. Lift shoulders off ground
3. Bring opposite elbow to knee
4. Extend other leg
5. Alternate sides
6. 15-20 repetitions each side

### Standing Side Bend

1. Stand with feet hip-width
2. One dumbbell at side
3. Bend toward weighted side
4. Return using obliques
5. 15 repetitions each side

**Note:** Keep the movement controlled—no momentum.

### Russian Twist (Bodyweight)

1. Sit with knees bent, feet elevated or on floor
2. Lean back slightly
3. Rotate torso side to side
4. Keep chest lifted
5. 15-20 repetitions each side

### Pallof Press (Anti-Rotation)

The foundational anti-rotation exercise:

1. Cable or band at chest height
2. Stand sideways to anchor
3. Hold handle at chest
4. Press arms straight forward
5. Resist rotation
6. Hold 3-5 seconds
7. 10 repetitions each side

## Intermediate Exercises

### Side Plank (Full)

1. Lie on side, elbow under shoulder
2. Stack feet or stagger them
3. Lift hips, body in straight line
4. 30-45 seconds each side

### Side Plank with Hip Dip

1. Full side plank position
2. Lower hip toward floor
3. Lift back to straight line
4. 12-15 repetitions each side

### Cable Woodchop (High to Low)

1. Cable at high position
2. Stand sideways to machine
3. Pull cable diagonally across body (high to low)
4. Rotate through core
5. 12 repetitions each side

### Cable Woodchop (Low to High)

1. Cable at low position
2. Stand sideways
3. Pull cable diagonally up and across
4. Rotation through core
5. 12 repetitions each side

### Hanging Knee Raise with Twist

1. Hang from bar
2. Raise knees toward chest
3. Add rotation, bringing knees to one side
4. Alternate sides
5. 10-12 repetitions each side

### Copenhagen Plank Rotation

1. Copenhagen plank position
2. Reach top arm under body
3. Rotate and return
4. 8-10 repetitions each side

### Landmine Rotation

1. Barbell in landmine attachment
2. Hold end of barbell at chest
3. Rotate bar side to side
4. Keep hips relatively stable
5. 12 repetitions each side

## Advanced Exercises

### Side Plank with Rotation

1. Side plank position
2. Reach top arm under body (threading)
3. Rotate and open back up
4. 10-12 repetitions each side

### Pallof Press with Rotation

1. Standard Pallof position
2. Press out
3. Rotate away from anchor, then back
4. Return to start
5. 8-10 repetitions each side

### Hanging Windshield Wiper

1. Hang from bar, legs raised straight
2. Rotate legs side to side (like windshield wipers)
3. Control the movement
4. 8-10 repetitions each side

### Medicine Ball Rotational Throw

1. Stand sideways to wall
2. Medicine ball at hip
3. Rotate and throw ball into wall
4. Catch and repeat
5. 10-12 throws each side

### Turkish Get-Up

1. Lie on back with weight pressed up
2. Stand up through a series of movements
3. Obliques stabilize throughout
4. 3-5 repetitions each side

### Offset Loaded Carry

1. Heavy weight in one hand
2. Walk with tall posture
3. Obliques resist lateral flexion
4. 30-40 yards each side

### Dragon Flag

1. Lie on bench, hold behind head
2. Raise body up (only shoulders touch)
3. Lower with control
4. Advanced movement requiring significant strength

## Anti-Rotation Training

Anti-rotation is a critical oblique function—resisting rotation rather than creating it:

**Why it matters:**
- Protects spine during movement
- Essential for most real-world activities
- Foundation for safe rotation
- Often more important than active rotation

**Key anti-rotation exercises:**
1. Pallof press (all variations)
2. Single-arm carries
3. Dead bug
4. Single-arm rows and presses
5. Off-set loaded exercises

## Sample Programs

### Beginner Core (Weeks 1-4)

**3x per week:**
1. Dead bug: 3 × 10 each side
2. Side plank (modified): 3 × 20 seconds each side
3. Pallof press: 3 × 10 each side
4. Bicycle crunch: 2 × 15 each side
5. Russian twist: 2 × 15 each side

### Intermediate Development (Weeks 5-8)

**3x per week:**
1. Side plank (full): 3 × 30 seconds each side
2. Cable woodchop (high to low): 3 × 12 each side
3. Pallof press with rotation: 3 × 10 each side
4. Side plank with hip dip: 2 × 12 each side
5. Hanging knee raise with twist: 2 × 10 each side

### Advanced Power (Weeks 9+)

**2-3x per week:**
1. Medicine ball rotational throw: 3 × 10 each side
2. Side plank with rotation: 3 × 10 each side
3. Landmine rotation: 3 × 12 each side
4. Hanging windshield wiper: 2 × 8 each side
5. Turkish get-up: 2 × 3 each side

### Athlete/Rotational Sport Focus

**3x per week:**
1. Cable woodchop (both directions): 3 × 10 each side
2. Med ball throws: 3 × 10 each side
3. Pallof press variations: 2 × 10 each side
4. Side plank: 2 × 30 seconds each side
5. Landmine rotation: 2 × 12 each side

## Integration with Full Core Training

Obliques are part of the complete core system:

**Complete core session:**
1. Anti-extension: Dead bug, plank
2. Anti-rotation: Pallof press
3. Anti-lateral flexion: Side plank, carries
4. Rotation: Woodchops, throws
5. Hip flexion: Leg raises

**The principle:** Train all core functions, not just rotation.

## Common Mistakes

### Only Doing Side Bends

Side bends isolate one function. Include rotation and anti-rotation for complete development.

### Using Momentum

Momentum reduces oblique activation. Slow, controlled movement builds strength.

### Neglecting Anti-Rotation

Creating rotation is impressive, but resisting rotation is often more functional and protective.

### Going Too Heavy on Rotation

Rotational exercises don't need heavy weight. Power comes from speed and control, not load.

### Ignoring One Side

Many people have asymmetrical oblique strength. Train both sides equally (or give extra attention to the weak side).

## When to Seek Help

See a professional if:

- Pain during rotation or side bending
- Sharp pain in side of abdomen
- Significant asymmetry
- Difficulty stabilizing during exercises
- History of abdominal surgery
- Hernia or suspected hernia

## The Bottom Line

Your obliques are essential for rotational power, spinal stability, and core function—not just for aesthetics. The keys to building strong obliques:

1. **Include all functions** - Rotation, anti-rotation, lateral flexion, anti-lateral flexion
2. **Prioritize anti-rotation** - It's often more important than active rotation
3. **Control the movement** - Quality over speed or load
4. **Train both sides equally** - Address asymmetries
5. **Progress to power** - Once strength is built, add speed with throws
6. **Integrate with full core** - Obliques are part of the system
7. **Be consistent** - 3x per week for best results

Strong obliques make every twist, turn, and rotational movement safer and more powerful. Start with Pallof press and side plank, then progress from there.
