# Post-Rotator Cuff Surgery Exercises: Complete Recovery Guide

Rotator cuff repair fixes the tear, but rehabilitation rebuilds the shoulder. Full recovery takes 4-6 months of careful progression, protecting the repair while restoring motion and strength. Here's your complete guide to rotator cuff surgery rehabilitation.

## Understanding Rotator Cuff Recovery

The rotator cuff is four muscles that stabilize and move the shoulder. After surgical repair, the tendon-to-bone healing takes 12-16 weeks, and you must protect this repair while gradually restoring function.

**Healing timeline:**
- Weeks 0-6: Initial healing, protection phase
- Weeks 6-12: Tissue strengthening, motion restoration
- Weeks 12-16: Healing maturing, gentle strengthening
- Months 4-6: Progressive strengthening
- Months 6-12: Full recovery and activity return

**Repair size matters:**
- Small tears: May progress faster
- Large/massive tears: Slower, more protection needed
- Follow YOUR surgeon's protocol

## Phase 1: Protection (Weeks 0-6)

### Goals
- Protect the repair
- Control pain and swelling
- Prevent stiffness (within limits)
- Maintain elbow, wrist, hand function

### Sling Use

- Wear sling as directed (typically 4-6 weeks)
- Remove only for exercises and bathing
- Sleep in sling or with pillow support

### What to Avoid

- Active shoulder movement
- Lifting anything with operated arm
- Pushing or pulling
- Reaching behind back
- Sudden movements

### Passive Range of Motion (PROM)

Someone else moves your arm, or you use your other hand:

**Pendulum exercises:**
1. Lean forward, support with good arm
2. Let operated arm hang
3. Make small circles using body movement (not shoulder muscles)
4. 10 circles each direction
5. 2-3 times daily

**Table slides:**
1. Sit at table, forearm on towel
2. Use body to slide arm forward
3. Don't use shoulder muscles
4. 10 repetitions

**Supine flexion (assisted):**
1. Lie on back
2. Use good arm to lift operated arm overhead
3. Stay within surgeon's limits
4. 10 repetitions
5. 2-3 times daily

**Supine external rotation (assisted):**
1. Lie on back, elbow at side, bent 90 degrees
2. Hold stick with both hands
3. Use good arm to rotate operated arm outward
4. Stay within surgeon's limits (often 30-45 degrees initially)
5. 10 repetitions

### Active Hand, Wrist, Elbow

Keep lower arm active:

**Ball squeezes:**
- Squeeze soft ball
- 10-20 repetitions, several times daily

**Wrist exercises:**
- Wrist curls (no weight)
- Wrist rotations
- Grip exercises

**Elbow:**
- Bend and straighten elbow
- Keep arm supported

### Ice and Pain Management

- Ice 20 minutes, several times daily
- Take medications as prescribed
- Keep shoulder elevated when resting

## Phase 2: Early Motion (Weeks 6-12)

### Goals
- Restore full passive range of motion
- Begin active-assisted motion
- Continue protecting repair

### Active-Assisted Range of Motion (AAROM)

You start helping with movement:

**Supine flexion (self-assisted):**
1. Lie on back
2. Good arm helps lift operated arm
3. Operated arm provides some effort
4. Progress to active as able
5. 15-20 repetitions

**Pulley exercises:**
1. Attach pulley over door
2. Hold handles with both hands
3. Good arm pulls down, operated arm goes up
4. Control throughout
5. 10-15 repetitions

**Wall climbing:**
1. Face wall
2. Walk fingers up wall
3. Only as high as comfortable
4. Don't force
5. 10-15 repetitions

**External rotation progression:**
1. Continue supine ER with stick
2. Progress to sitting/standing
3. Gradually increase range
4. 10-15 repetitions

### Scapular Exercises

**Scapular squeezes:**
1. Sit or stand with good posture
2. Squeeze shoulder blades together
3. Hold 5 seconds
4. 15-20 repetitions

**Scapular clocks:**
1. Arm supported on table
2. Move shoulder blade: up, down, forward, back
3. 10 repetitions each direction

### Active Range of Motion (AROM)

When cleared (usually week 8-10):

**Flexion:**
1. Raise arm forward (no weight)
2. Use muscles but don't force
3. Progress range gradually

**Abduction:**
1. Raise arm to side
2. Thumb up
3. Progress gradually

**External rotation:**
1. Elbow at side, bent 90 degrees
2. Rotate forearm outward
3. No resistance yet

## Phase 3: Strengthening Preparation (Weeks 12-16)

### Goals
- Full active range of motion
- Begin gentle strengthening
- Improve neuromuscular control

### Isometric Strengthening

No movement, just muscle contraction:

**Isometric flexion:**
1. Stand facing wall
2. Push into wall gently (10-20% effort)
3. Hold 5-10 seconds
4. 10-15 repetitions

**Isometric abduction:**
1. Stand sideways to wall
2. Push elbow into wall
3. Hold 5-10 seconds
4. 10-15 repetitions

**Isometric external rotation:**
1. Elbow at side, bent 90 degrees
2. Push outward against wall or doorframe
3. Hold 5-10 seconds
4. 10-15 repetitions

**Isometric internal rotation:**
1. Same position
2. Push inward against doorframe
3. Hold 5-10 seconds
4. 10-15 repetitions

### Light Resistance Band

When cleared (usually week 14-16):

**External rotation:**
1. Elbow at side, band attached to door
2. Rotate arm outward against light resistance
3. 15 repetitions
4. Very light resistance initially

**Internal rotation:**
1. Same setup, opposite direction
2. Rotate arm inward
3. 15 repetitions

**Rowing:**
1. Band attached at waist height
2. Pull elbow back
3. Squeeze shoulder blade
4. 15 repetitions

## Phase 4: Progressive Strengthening (Months 4-6)

### Goals
- Build rotator cuff strength
- Restore scapular stability
- Progress toward functional activities

### Rotator Cuff Strengthening

**Side-lying external rotation:**
1. Lie on unaffected side
2. Light weight (1-3 lbs)
3. Elbow at side, rotate forearm up
4. 15 repetitions, 2-3 sets

**Prone horizontal abduction:**
1. Lie face down, arm hanging
2. Lift arm to side, thumb up
3. 15 repetitions, 2-3 sets

**Prone Y-T-W:**
1. Lie face down
2. Y: arms overhead, lift
3. T: arms to sides, lift
4. W: elbows bent, squeeze shoulder blades
5. 10 each position, 2-3 sets

### Scapular Strengthening

**Rows:**
1. Band or cable
2. Pull elbows back
3. 15 repetitions, 2-3 sets

**Face pulls:**
1. Band at face height
2. Pull toward face, elbows high
3. 15 repetitions, 2-3 sets

**Serratus punches:**
1. Lie on back, arm straight up
2. Punch toward ceiling (scapula lifts off ground)
3. 15 repetitions

### Deltoid Strengthening

**Flexion:**
1. Raise arm forward with light weight
2. 15 repetitions, 2-3 sets

**Abduction:**
1. Raise arm to side with light weight
2. Stop at shoulder level
3. 15 repetitions, 2-3 sets

### Progression Principles

- Increase reps before weight
- Progress weight in small increments (1-2 lbs)
- No pain with exercises
- Maintain good form

## Phase 5: Return to Activity (Months 6+)

### Goals
- Full strength restoration
- Return to work and recreational activities
- Sport-specific training if applicable

### Advanced Strengthening

- Progress to heavier weights
- Add overhead exercises (when cleared)
- Incorporate functional movements

### Return to Activities

**General timeline (varies by tear size and repair):**
- Light desk work: 2-4 weeks
- Driving: 6-8 weeks
- Light lifting: 12-16 weeks
- Gym workouts: 4-6 months
- Swimming: 4-6 months
- Golf/tennis: 6-9 months
- Contact sports: 9-12 months

**Always get surgeon clearance for specific activities.**

## Sample Daily Routines

### Phase 1 (Weeks 0-6)

**3-4 times daily:**
- Pendulums: 10 circles each direction
- Passive flexion: 10 reps
- Passive external rotation: 10 reps
- Ball squeezes: 20 reps
- Elbow bends: 15 reps
- Ice: 20 minutes

### Phase 2 (Weeks 6-12)

**2-3 times daily:**
- Pulley exercises: 15 reps
- Wall climbing: 15 reps
- Active-assisted motions: all directions
- Scapular squeezes: 20 reps
- Begin AROM when cleared

### Phase 3-4 (Weeks 12-24)

**Daily/Every other day:**
- Stretching: Full ROM maintenance
- Isometrics: All directions (phase 3)
- Resistance exercises: Progress per protocol
- Scapular strengthening: 2-3 exercises, 2-3 sets

### Phase 5 (6+ months)

**3-4x weekly:**
- Full shoulder workout
- Progressive resistance
- Sport/activity-specific training

## Common Mistakes

1. **Progressing too fast** - Healing takes 12-16 weeks
2. **Skipping passive motion** - Leads to stiffness
3. **Using shoulder muscles in Phase 1** - Can compromise repair
4. **Neglecting scapular exercises** - Essential for shoulder function
5. **Not wearing sling** - Repair needs protection

## When to Contact Your Surgeon

- Sudden increase in pain
- New weakness
- Feeling of "giving way"
- Signs of infection (fever, drainage, redness)
- No progress with rehab

## The Bottom Line

Rotator cuff rehabilitation requires patience:

1. **Protect the repair** - Healing takes 12-16 weeks
2. **Restore motion first** - Before strengthening
3. **Progress gradually** - Each phase builds on the last
4. **Do the scapular work** - It's as important as rotator cuff exercises
5. **Be patient** - Full recovery takes 6-12 months

The repair is only as good as the rehabilitation. Your surgeon fixed the tear—now you need to rebuild the shoulder. Follow the protocol, do the exercises, and you'll return to full function.
