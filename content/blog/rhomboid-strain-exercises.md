# Rhomboid Strain Exercises: Recovery Guide for Upper Back Muscle Pain

The rhomboids are muscles between your spine and shoulder blades that pull your scapulae together and back. A rhomboid strain causes pain between the shoulder blades that worsens with rowing movements, squeezing your shoulders back, or even deep breathing. With proper care, most rhomboid strains heal within weeks.

## Understanding Rhomboid Strains

### Anatomy

**Rhomboid Minor:**
- Upper portion, from C7-T1 to scapula

**Rhomboid Major:**
- Larger, lower portion, from T2-T5 to scapula

**Function:**
- Scapular retraction (pulling shoulder blades together)
- Scapular elevation
- Downward rotation of scapula
- Stabilizing scapula during arm movements

### How Rhomboid Strains Happen

**Common causes:**
- Repetitive rowing or pulling motions
- Sudden forceful movements (throwing, catching)
- Prolonged poor posture (rounded shoulders)
- Heavy lifting with rounded upper back
- Carrying heavy loads
- Sports: rowing, swimming, tennis, golf
- Overuse from desk work and stress

### Symptoms

- Pain between shoulder blade and spine
- Pain with squeezing shoulder blades together
- Discomfort with deep breathing
- Pain when reaching across body
- Muscle spasm or knots
- Pain with rowing or pulling movements
- Tenderness to touch in the area

### Strain Grades

- **Grade I:** Mild strain, 1-2 weeks recovery
- **Grade II:** Moderate strain, partial tear, 3-6 weeks recovery
- **Grade III:** Severe strain/complete tear, 6-12+ weeks recovery

## Phase 1: Acute Phase (Days 1-7)

### Goals:
- Reduce pain and inflammation
- Allow initial healing
- Prevent muscle spasm

### Initial Management

**Ice:** 15-20 minutes every 2-3 hours for first 48-72 hours
**Heat:** May help after 72 hours, especially for muscle spasm
**Rest:** Avoid aggravating movements

### 1. Gentle Shoulder Blade Glides

**How to do it:**
1. Sit or stand with arms relaxed
2. Gently slide shoulder blades toward spine
3. Then let them glide forward and apart
4. Very small, gentle movements
5. 10-15 repetitions
6. Should not increase pain

### 2. Neck Rotations (Gentle)

Reduces tension in connected muscles.

**How to do it:**
1. Sit comfortably
2. Slowly turn head side to side
3. Move through pain-free range only
4. 10 rotations each direction

### 3. Shoulder Circles

**How to do it:**
1. Let arms hang relaxed
2. Circle shoulders forward 10 times
3. Circle shoulders backward 10 times
4. Keep movements small and controlled

### 4. Diaphragmatic Breathing

Reduces muscle tension and pain.

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on back, knees bent
2. Place hand on belly
3. Breathe into belly, letting it rise
4. Exhale slowly
5. 5 minutes, several times daily

### 5. Heat Application (After 72 Hours)

**How to do it:**
- Apply heating pad to area for 15-20 minutes
- Helps relax muscle spasm
- Can use before stretching

### Positions to Avoid:
- Rowing or pulling exercises
- Overhead lifting
- Heavy carrying
- Prolonged rounded posture
- Sleeping on stomach

## Phase 2: Early Recovery (Weeks 1-3)

### Goals:
- Restore range of motion
- Begin gentle stretching
- Light muscle activation

### Stretches for Rhomboids

### 6. Cross-Body Arm Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Bring one arm across chest
2. Use other hand to pull it closer
3. Let shoulder blade slide forward
4. Feel stretch between shoulder blade and spine
5. Hold 30 seconds each side
6. 3 repetitions

### 7. Seated Rotation Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. Sit in chair, cross arms on chest
2. Rotate upper body to one side
3. Feel stretch in upper back
4. Hold 20-30 seconds each side
5. 3 repetitions

### 8. Cat-Cow Stretch

**How to do it:**
1. On hands and knees
2. Round back, tuck chin—feel stretch between shoulder blades
3. Arch back, lift head
4. Move slowly
5. 15-20 repetitions

### 9. Thread the Needle

**How to do it:**
1. On hands and knees
2. Thread one arm under body
3. Lower shoulder toward floor
4. Rotate thoracic spine
5. Hold 30 seconds each side
6. 3 repetitions

### 10. Child's Pose with Reach

**How to do it:**
1. Kneel and sit back toward heels
2. Reach arms forward
3. Walk hands to one side
4. Feel stretch on opposite side of upper back
5. Hold 30 seconds each side

### Gentle Activation

### 11. Isometric Scapular Retraction

**How to do it:**
1. Stand with back against wall
2. Bend elbows 90°, arms against wall
3. Gently press elbows into wall
4. Hold 5 seconds at 30-50% effort
5. 10 repetitions
6. Should not cause sharp pain

### 12. Prone Arm Lifts (No Weight)

**How to do it:**
1. Lie face down, forehead on towel
2. Arms at sides
3. Lift arms slightly, squeeze shoulder blades
4. Hold 3-5 seconds
5. 10-15 repetitions

## Phase 3: Strengthening (Weeks 3-6)

### Goals:
- Rebuild rhomboid strength
- Restore scapular stability
- Balance with opposing muscles

### 13. Prone Y-T-I Raises

**Y Position:**
1. Lie face down, arms in Y overhead
2. Lift arms off floor, thumbs up
3. Squeeze shoulder blades
4. Hold 3 seconds
5. 10-15 repetitions

**T Position:**
1. Arms straight out to sides
2. Thumbs up
3. Lift and squeeze shoulder blades
4. Hold 3 seconds
5. 10-15 repetitions

**I Position:**
1. Arms straight at sides
2. Lift toward ceiling
3. Squeeze shoulder blades
4. Hold 3 seconds
5. 10-15 repetitions

### 14. Band Pull-Aparts

Excellent rhomboid exercise.

**How to do it:**
1. Hold resistance band at chest height
2. Arms straight in front
3. Pull band apart, bringing hands to sides
4. Focus on squeezing shoulder blades
5. Control return
6. 15-20 repetitions
7. Progress band resistance

### 15. Face Pulls

**How to do it:**
1. Cable or band at face height
2. Pull toward face, elbows high
3. At end, externally rotate—hands to ears
4. Squeeze shoulder blades
5. Control return
6. 15-20 repetitions

### 16. Seated Row (Light)

**How to do it:**
1. Sit at cable row machine
2. Pull handles toward lower ribs
3. Focus on scapular retraction
4. Hold contraction 2 seconds
5. Control return
6. 12-15 repetitions
7. Start at 50% normal weight

### 17. Single-Arm Band Row

**How to do it:**
1. Anchor band at chest height
2. Pull toward hip, rotating torso slightly
3. Squeeze shoulder blade
4. 12-15 repetitions each side

### 18. Wall Angels

**How to do it:**
1. Stand with back against wall
2. Arms in "goalpost" position, touching wall
3. Slide arms up and down wall
4. Keep contact with wall throughout
5. 15 repetitions

### 19. Scapular Push-Ups

Strengthens serratus anterior—balances with rhomboids.

**How to do it:**
1. Push-up position (or on knees)
2. Keep arms straight
3. Let shoulder blades pinch together (sag)
4. Push through hands to spread shoulder blades
5. 15-20 repetitions

## Phase 4: Return to Activity (Weeks 6+)

### Goals:
- Full strength and endurance
- Return to normal training
- Prevention of re-injury

### 20. Bent-Over Rows

**How to do it:**
1. Hinge at hips, flat back
2. Row weight toward lower ribs
3. Squeeze shoulder blades at top
4. Lower with control
5. 10-12 repetitions
6. Start at 50% normal weight, progress

### 21. Single-Arm Dumbbell Row

**How to do it:**
1. Support on bench
2. Row dumbbell toward hip
3. Focus on scapular movement
4. 10-12 repetitions each side

### 22. Inverted Rows

**How to do it:**
1. Set bar at hip height
2. Hang underneath, heels on floor
3. Pull chest to bar
4. Lower with control
5. 10-15 repetitions
6. Adjust angle to modify difficulty

### 23. Pull-Ups/Lat Pulldowns

**How to do it:**
1. Focus on initiating with scapular depression and retraction
2. Full range of motion
3. Control the descent
4. Start light if using pulldowns

## Daily Stretching Routine

### Morning (3 minutes):
1. Cat-cow: 10 repetitions
2. Shoulder circles: 10 each direction
3. Cross-body stretch: 20 sec each side

### Work Breaks (2 minutes, every 2 hours):
1. Shoulder blade squeezes: 10 reps
2. Neck rotations: 10 each direction
3. Cross-body stretch: 15 sec each side

### Evening (5 minutes):
1. Thread the needle: 30 sec each side
2. Cat-cow: 15 repetitions
3. Cross-body stretch: 30 sec each side
4. Child's pose with reach: 30 sec each side
5. Foam rolling if desired

## Posture Correction

Poor posture is a major contributor to rhomboid strain. Address it:

### Desk Setup:
- Monitor at eye level
- Keyboard at elbow height
- Chair supporting lumbar curve
- Shoulders back, not rounded forward
- Regular breaks to move

### Standing:
- Weight even on both feet
- Shoulders back, not rounded
- Ears over shoulders
- Imagine string pulling head up

### Exercises for Posture:
- Chin tucks
- Wall angels
- Doorway pec stretch
- Lower trap strengthening

## Common Mistakes

1. **Returning to rows too quickly** — Gradual progression essential
2. **Only stretching, not strengthening** — Both are needed
3. **Ignoring posture** — Root cause often goes unaddressed
4. **Neglecting opposing muscles** — Pecs and serratus need attention too
5. **Training through pain** — Delays healing significantly

## When Rhomboid Pain Isn't a Strain

Pain between shoulder blades can have other causes:
- **Thoracic spine dysfunction** — Joint or disc issues
- **Cervical radiculopathy** — Nerve from neck
- **Scapular dyskinesis** — Poor shoulder blade movement pattern
- **Trigger points** — Myofascial pain
- **Visceral referred pain** — Gallbladder, heart (rare but serious)

**See a doctor if:**
- Pain persists beyond 4-6 weeks
- Numbness or tingling in arms
- Pain with breathing (could be cardiac)
- Severe or worsening symptoms
- Pain spreading to arm or chest

## Recovery Timeline

**Grade I:**
- Light activity: 1-2 weeks
- Full training: 2-3 weeks

**Grade II:**
- Light activity: 2-3 weeks
- Full training: 4-6 weeks

**Grade III:**
- Medical evaluation needed
- 6-12+ weeks recovery
- May need imaging

## Prevention Strategies

1. **Warm up** before rowing/pulling exercises
2. **Strengthen progressively** — Don't spike volume suddenly
3. **Balance training** — Don't neglect pushing movements
4. **Address posture** — Daily awareness and correction
5. **Manage desk ergonomics** — Set up workspace properly
6. **Regular stretching** — Especially after prolonged sitting
7. **Don't ignore early warning signs** — Tightness and fatigue

## Key Takeaways

1. **Rest initially, then move** — Early gentle movement aids healing
2. **Stretch and strengthen** — Both are essential for recovery
3. **Fix your posture** — Often the root cause
4. **Progress rows gradually** — Don't rush back to heavy pulling
5. **Balance with pushing work** — And serratus anterior strengthening
6. **Prevention is ongoing** — Maintain flexibility and strength

Rhomboid strains can be frustrating because they affect so many daily movements, but they respond well to conservative care. Follow the phases, be patient with progression, and address the underlying causes to prevent recurrence.
