# Exercises for Police Officers: Tactical Fitness Training

Law enforcement demands physical readiness for unpredictable situations. You might sit in a vehicle for hours, then sprint after a suspect, engage in a physical altercation, and drag someone to safety—all within minutes. This guide builds the tactical fitness required for the job.

## Physical Demands of Law Enforcement

### Job-Specific Requirements

**Strength needs:**
- Controlling and restraining subjects
- Defensive tactics and ground fighting
- Lifting and carrying equipment
- Dragging victims or injured officers
- Breaching and forcing entry

**Speed and power:**
- Foot pursuits (sprinting, direction changes)
- Explosive movements for defensive tactics
- Getting up quickly from ground positions
- React-and-move scenarios

**Endurance:**
- Extended physical confrontations
- Foot pursuits lasting 30+ seconds
- Maintaining composure during stress
- Recovery between high-intensity efforts

**Mobility:**
- Getting in/out of vehicle repeatedly
- Ground fighting positions
- Weapon transitions and drawing
- Moving through obstacles

### The Duty Belt Factor

Your duty belt adds 15-25 pounds of equipment, affecting posture, lower back stress, and movement mechanics. Training must account for this load.

## Foundation Strength Training

### Lower Body Power and Strength

**Trap Bar Deadlift**
Builds overall strength with less lower back stress—ideal for officers with belt-related back issues.

- Sets: 4 x 5 reps
- Focus: Explosive drive off floor
- Why: Foundation for all lower body strength

**Box Jump (Step Down)**
Develops explosive leg power for sprinting and jumping over obstacles.

- Sets: 4 x 5 reps
- Height: 20-30 inches based on ability
- Step down between reps to reduce joint stress
- Why: Explosive power for pursuits

**Bulgarian Split Squat**
Single-leg strength for running, direction changes, and imbalance correction.

- Sets: 3 x 8 each leg
- Progression: Add dumbbells or barbell
- Why: Unilateral strength prevents injury

**Hip Thrust**
Builds glute power for sprinting and explosive hip drive in ground fighting.

- Sets: 3 x 10-12
- Focus: Full hip extension, squeeze at top
- Why: Sprint power and hip drive

### Upper Body Strength

**Bench Press**
Pushing strength for defensive tactics and controlling subjects.

- Sets: 4 x 6-8 reps
- Include close-grip variation for tricep emphasis
- Why: Push strength for control techniques

**Weighted Pull-Ups/Chin-Ups**
Pulling strength for climbing, defensive tactics, and dragging.

- Sets: 4 x 6-8 reps
- Progression: Add weight as strength improves
- Why: Climbing fences, controlling subjects

**Dumbbell Row**
Unilateral pulling strength and grip endurance.

- Sets: 3 x 10 each arm
- Focus: Full stretch to full contraction
- Why: Pulling strength with grip development

**Push Press**
Overhead power for defensive tactics and lifting overhead.

- Sets: 3 x 6-8 reps
- Focus: Leg drive into explosive press
- Why: Full-body power transfer

### Grip and Forearm Development

Strong grip is critical for weapon retention, controlling subjects, and handcuffing.

**Farmer's Carries**
- Sets: 4 x 40-50 yards
- Weight: As heavy as maintainable with good posture
- Why: Grip endurance and core stability

**Dead Hangs**
- Sets: 3 x max time
- Target: Build to 60+ seconds
- Why: Grip strength and shoulder health

**Wrist Curls and Reverse Curls**
- Sets: 2 x 15-20 each
- Why: Balanced forearm development

## Combat and Defensive Tactics Training

### Ground Fighting Preparation

**Turkish Get-Up**
The single best exercise for getting up from ground positions while maintaining awareness.

- Sets: 3 x 3 each side
- Focus: Controlled, precise movement
- Progression: Add weight slowly
- Why: Getting up safely during confrontation

**Hip Escapes (Shrimping)**
Fundamental defensive tactics movement for ground fighting.

- Sets: 3 x 10 each direction
- Why: Escaping from bottom position

**Bridge and Roll**
Defensive tactic for escaping mount position.

- Sets: 3 x 10 each direction
- Focus: Explosive hip drive
- Why: Essential ground defense

**Bear Crawl**
Low movement for tactical situations and ground fighting transitions.

- Sets: 3 x 30 yards
- Variations: Forward, backward, lateral
- Why: Ground mobility and full-body conditioning

### Grappling-Specific Strength

**Cable/Band Anti-Rotation Press (Pallof)**
Resisting rotation during confrontations.

- Sets: 3 x 10 each side
- Why: Core stability during grappling

**Sandbag Drills**
Simulates controlling an uncooperative subject.

*Sandbag Clean and Press:*
- Sets: 3 x 8
- Why: Lifting and controlling irregular loads

*Sandbag Drag:*
- Sets: 4 x 30 yards
- Why: Dragging victims or subjects

**Medicine Ball Throws**
Develops rotational power for defensive tactics.

- Rotational throws: 3 x 8 each side
- Overhead slams: 3 x 10
- Why: Explosive power for control techniques

## Speed and Agility Training

### Sprint Development

**10-Yard Burst Sprints**
Most foot pursuits involve short, explosive efforts.

- Sets: 8-10 x 10 yards
- Rest: 30-45 sec between sprints
- Focus: Explosive first step
- Why: Initial pursuit acceleration

**30-40 Yard Sprints**
Builds speed maintenance for longer pursuits.

- Sets: 6 x 30-40 yards
- Rest: 60 sec between sprints
- Why: Sustained sprint capacity

### Agility and Direction Change

**5-10-5 Shuttle (Pro Agility)**
Rapid direction changes mimicking pursuit scenarios.

- Sets: 6-8 reps
- Focus: Low center of gravity, quick feet
- Rest: 45 sec between reps

**Cone Drills**
- T-Drill: 4-6 reps
- Box Drill: 4-6 reps (forward, lateral, backward)
- Random React: Partner points direction, you go

**Backpedal to Sprint**
Common pattern when observing then pursuing.

- Sets: 6 reps
- Distance: 10 yards backpedal, turn, 20 yards sprint
- Why: Realistic pursuit pattern

### Obstacle Navigation

**Box Step-Over**
Simulates clearing obstacles during pursuit.

- Sets: 3 x 10 each direction
- Focus: Quick, controlled movement

**Hurdle Hops**
Jumping over obstacles in pursuit.

- Sets: 4 x 5 hurdles
- Height: Start low (6-12 inches)

**Wall Climb Simulation**
- Muscle-up progressions or
- Jump to pull-up bar and muscle over
- Why: Fence climbing preparation

## Cardiovascular Conditioning

### High-Intensity Intervals

**Pursuit Simulation**
Mimics the metabolic demands of a foot chase.

*Workout:*
- 30 sec all-out sprint
- 30 sec jog
- Repeat 8-10 times
- Total: 8-10 minutes

**Assault Bike Sprints**
Full-body conditioning without impact stress.

- 20 sec max effort
- 40 sec easy spin
- Repeat 10 times
- Why: Builds recovery capacity

### Lactic Tolerance (The Burn Training)

Physical confrontations often last 30-90 seconds of sustained high-intensity effort. Build tolerance for this.

*Workout:*
- Burpees x 30 seconds
- Rest 30 seconds
- Push-ups x 30 seconds
- Rest 30 seconds
- Squats x 30 seconds
- Rest 30 seconds
- Repeat 4 rounds

### Zone 2 Aerobic Base

Build foundation for recovery between high-intensity efforts.

- Running: 30-45 min at conversational pace
- Rucking: 45-60 min with 20-30 lb pack
- Cycling/Swimming: 45-60 min moderate effort
- Frequency: 2-3x/week

## Shift-Specific Considerations

### Managing Seated Posture

Hours in a patrol vehicle creates:
- Hip flexor tightness
- Glute inhibition
- Lower back compression
- Thoracic spine stiffness

**In-Car Movement (Every 1-2 Hours):**
- Seated cat-cow (arch and round back)
- Neck rotations (slow, controlled)
- Shoulder shrugs and rolls
- Ankle circles

**When Out of Vehicle:**
- Standing hip flexor stretch (30 sec each)
- Walking/pacing when writing reports
- Squat down fully a few times
- Arm circles and shoulder stretches

### Duty Belt Compensation

**Strengthen what the belt stresses:**
- Core anti-rotation (Pallof press)
- Hip flexors (active stretching)
- Lower back (bird dogs, deadlifts)
- Hip stabilizers (clamshells, lateral band walks)

**Pre-shift routine (5 min):**
1. Hip flexor stretches
2. Cat-cow x 10
3. Bodyweight squats x 10
4. Glute bridges x 10
5. Band pull-aparts x 15

## Weekly Training Program

### Week Structure (5-Day Split)

| Day | Focus | Duration |
|-----|-------|----------|
| Monday | Lower Body Strength | 50 min |
| Tuesday | Upper Body + Combat Prep | 50 min |
| Wednesday | Speed/Agility | 40 min |
| Thursday | Full Body Power + Conditioning | 45 min |
| Friday | Active Recovery | 30 min |
| Saturday | Zone 2 Cardio | 45-60 min |
| Sunday | Rest |  |

### Day 1: Lower Body Strength

*Warm-up (8 min):*
- Bike 3 min
- Hip circles, leg swings
- Bodyweight squats x 10
- Glute bridges x 10

*Main Work:*
1. Trap Bar Deadlift: 4 x 5
2. Bulgarian Split Squat: 3 x 8 each
3. Hip Thrust: 3 x 10
4. Box Jump (step down): 4 x 5
5. Farmer's Carry: 3 x 40 yards

*Core:*
- Pallof Press: 3 x 10 each
- Dead Bug: 3 x 10 each

### Day 2: Upper Body + Combat Prep

*Warm-up (8 min):*
- Row 3 min
- Arm circles, band pull-aparts
- Push-ups x 10

*Main Work:*
1. Bench Press: 4 x 6
2. Weighted Pull-Ups: 4 x 6
3. Push Press: 3 x 8
4. Dumbbell Row: 3 x 10 each

*Combat Prep:*
- Turkish Get-Up: 3 x 3 each side
- Medicine Ball Rotational Throws: 3 x 8 each
- Hip Escapes: 2 x 10 each

### Day 3: Speed and Agility

*Dynamic Warm-up (10 min):*
- Jog 400m
- High knees, butt kicks, karaokes
- Leg swings, arm circles

*Sprint Work:*
1. 10-yard bursts: 8 reps (30 sec rest)
2. 30-yard sprints: 4 reps (60 sec rest)

*Agility:*
3. 5-10-5 shuttle: 6 reps (45 sec rest)
4. Backpedal to sprint: 4 reps

*Finish:*
5. Bear crawl: 3 x 20 yards
6. Lateral shuffle: 3 x 30 yards

### Day 4: Full Body Power + Conditioning

*Warm-up (5 min):*
- Jump rope 2 min
- Dynamic movement prep

*Power Circuit (3 rounds):*
- Box jumps x 5
- Medicine ball slams x 8
- Kettlebell swings x 10
- Rest 90 sec

*Conditioning (Pursuit Simulation):*
- 30 sec sprint / 30 sec jog x 8

*Finish:*
- Sandbag drag: 3 x 30 yards

### Day 5: Active Recovery

*Mobility Flow (30 min):*
- Foam roll entire body (10 min)
- Hip opener sequence (90/90, pigeon, frog)
- Thoracic spine work (extensions, rotations)
- Shoulder mobility (CARS, stretch)
- Light walking or swimming (15 min)

## Physical Ability Test Preparation

### Common Police Academy Test Elements

**Running:**
- 1.5 mile run (target: under 12 min)
- 300-meter sprint (target: under 60 sec)

**Strength endurance:**
- Push-ups (target: 40+ in 1 min)
- Sit-ups (target: 40+ in 1 min)

**Agility:**
- Obstacle course completion
- Dummy drag (165 lbs, 30+ feet)

### Test-Specific Preparation

**1.5 Mile Run:**
- Build aerobic base with Zone 2 running
- Include tempo runs (1 mile at goal pace)
- Practice full test distance weekly

**300-Meter Sprint:**
- Build with 150-200m repeats
- Practice full distance at race pace
- Focus on pacing (don't go out too fast)

**Push-ups/Sit-ups:**
- Practice 3x/week
- Greasing the groove (multiple sets throughout day)
- Build to 50+ in practice to hit 40+ in test

**Dummy Drag:**
- Sled drags, backward pulling
- Heavy sandbag carries
- Deadlift strength development

## Injury Prevention

### Common Law Enforcement Injuries

1. **Lower back pain** - From duty belt and seated posture
2. **Knee injuries** - From pursuit and takedown movements
3. **Shoulder injuries** - From defensive tactics
4. **Ankle sprains** - From running on uneven surfaces

### Prevention Protocols

**Back protection:**
- Daily hip flexor stretching
- Core anti-rotation training
- Proper lifting mechanics
- Regular decompression (hanging)

**Knee protection:**
- Single-leg strength training
- Don't neglect hamstrings
- Landing mechanics practice
- Build up sprint volume gradually

**Shoulder protection:**
- Rotator cuff prehab (YTWs, band work)
- Maintain full mobility
- Balance push with pull volume
- Include face pulls in every upper workout

**Ankle protection:**
- Single-leg balance work
- Ankle mobility drills
- Proper footwear
- Terrain awareness during pursuit

## Mental Conditioning

### Stress Inoculation Through Training

High-intensity training prepares your body and mind for stress:

- Train when fatigued occasionally
- Practice skills under physical stress
- Include cognitive tasks during conditioning (counting, problem-solving)
- Simulate time pressure in workouts

### Breathing for Performance

**Box Breathing (Recovery):**
- 4 seconds inhale
- 4 seconds hold
- 4 seconds exhale
- 4 seconds hold
- Use between high-intensity efforts

**Combat/Tactical Breathing:**
- Use when heart rate is elevated
- Signals parasympathetic system
- Helps maintain composure during confrontation

## Summary

Law enforcement fitness requires:

1. **Functional strength** - Deadlift, squat, press, pull
2. **Explosive power** - Box jumps, throws, kettlebell work
3. **Sprint and agility** - Short bursts and direction changes
4. **Combat preparation** - Get-ups, ground mobility, grappling strength
5. **Cardiovascular capacity** - HIIT for pursuits, Zone 2 for recovery
6. **Mobility maintenance** - Counter seated posture and belt effects

Train consistently on your days off, use active recovery on shift days, and prioritize movements that transfer directly to your job requirements. Your physical preparedness could save your life or the life of a citizen you're sworn to protect.

Stay fit, stay safe, stay ready.
