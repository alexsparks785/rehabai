# Squat Form Guide: Master the Perfect Squat

The squat is one of the most fundamental human movements. Mastering proper form builds strength safely and transfers to everything from athletic performance to daily life.

## Why Squat Form Matters

**Benefits of good form:**
- Maximum muscle activation
- Joint-safe movement
- Prevents injury
- Builds proper movement patterns
- Allows progressive loading

**Consequences of poor form:**
- Knee, hip, or back pain
- Limited depth and muscle engagement
- Increased injury risk
- Strength plateaus

---

## The Basic Squat Movement

### Setup

1. **Feet:** Shoulder-width or slightly wider
2. **Toes:** Pointed slightly outward (15-30°)
3. **Weight:** Distributed across entire foot
4. **Posture:** Chest up, core braced

### The Descent

1. **Initiate:** Push hips back slightly
2. **Knees:** Track over toes as you descend
3. **Torso:** Stay relatively upright
4. **Depth:** Crease of hip below top of knee (parallel or below)
5. **Pace:** Controlled, 2-3 seconds down

### The Ascent

1. **Drive:** Push through full foot
2. **Knees:** Continue tracking over toes
3. **Hips and shoulders:** Rise at same rate
4. **Lockout:** Stand fully upright, squeeze glutes

## Key Form Cues

### "Sit Back and Down"

- Think of sitting into a chair
- Hips go back as knees bend
- Not just straight down

### "Spread the Floor"

- Push feet outward (without moving them)
- Activates glutes
- Prevents knee collapse

### "Chest Up"

- Maintain proud chest position
- Prevents excessive forward lean
- Keeps spine neutral

### "Knees Out"

- Knees track over toes
- Don't let them cave inward
- Same direction as toes pointing

### "Brace Your Core"

- Deep breath into belly
- Tighten abs like expecting a punch
- Maintain throughout lift

## Common Squat Mistakes

### Knee Cave (Valgus)

**The problem:** Knees collapse inward

**Causes:** Weak glutes, tight adductors, poor motor control

**Fixes:**
- "Spread the floor" cue
- Banded squats for feedback
- Glute strengthening
- Lower the weight

### Excessive Forward Lean

**The problem:** Torso tilts too far forward

**Causes:** Weak quads, tight ankles, poor mobility

**Fixes:**
- "Chest up" cue
- Ankle mobility work
- Front squats to train upright position
- Elevate heels temporarily

### Heels Rising

**The problem:** Heels lift off floor

**Causes:** Limited ankle dorsiflexion

**Fixes:**
- Ankle mobility exercises
- Calf stretching
- Squat shoes (elevated heel)
- Plates under heels temporarily

### Butt Wink

**The problem:** Lower back rounds at bottom

**Causes:** Hip mobility limitations, going too deep for current mobility

**Fixes:**
- Stop just before rounding occurs
- Hip mobility work
- Core bracing practice
- Gradually increase depth

### Good Morning Squat

**The problem:** Hips rise faster than shoulders (turns into hip hinge)

**Causes:** Weak quads, motor pattern issue

**Fixes:**
- Slow eccentrics
- Pause squats
- Front squats
- "Chest up" cue

### Half Squats

**The problem:** Not reaching proper depth

**Causes:** Ego, mobility limitations, habit

**Fixes:**
- Use less weight
- Box squats to target depth
- Mobility work
- Video yourself

## Squat Variations

### Goblet Squat

**Best for:** Learning proper form, beginners

- Hold weight at chest
- Forces upright posture
- Great squat teacher

### Bodyweight Squat

**Best for:** Building pattern, high reps, beginners

- No equipment needed
- Focus purely on form
- Can do anywhere

### Back Squat (High Bar)

**Best for:** Quad development, overall strength

- Bar on upper traps
- More upright torso
- Most common squat style

### Back Squat (Low Bar)

**Best for:** Maximum weight, powerlifting

- Bar on rear deltoids
- More forward lean
- Greater hip involvement

### Front Squat

**Best for:** Quad focus, core strength, fixing forward lean

- Bar on front deltoids
- Very upright torso
- Excellent for form development

### Box Squat

**Best for:** Learning depth, building hip power

- Sit to box at parallel
- Pause briefly
- Teaches proper depth

### Split Squat/Bulgarian Split Squat

**Best for:** Single-leg strength, balance, fixing imbalances

- One foot forward, one back
- Addresses side-to-side differences

## Squat Depth

### What Is "Proper" Depth?

**Parallel:** Hip crease at same level as top of knee
**Below parallel:** Hip crease below knee (ATG = "ass to grass")

### How Deep Should You Go?

**Minimum:** Parallel (hip crease at knee level)
**Ideal:** As deep as you can with good form
**Limit:** When form breaks down (butt wink, etc.)

### Depth Progression

1. Go only as deep as form allows
2. Work on mobility
3. Gradually increase depth
4. Never sacrifice form for depth

## Mobility for Better Squats

### Ankle Mobility

**Why it matters:** Limited dorsiflexion causes heel rise and forward lean

**Exercises:**
- Banded ankle mobilization
- Calf stretches
- Wall ankle mobilizations

### Hip Mobility

**Why it matters:** Needed for depth without butt wink

**Exercises:**
- 90/90 hip stretch
- Deep squat holds
- Pigeon pose
- Hip CARs

### Thoracic Mobility

**Why it matters:** Allows upright posture

**Exercises:**
- Thoracic extensions on foam roller
- Cat-cow
- Thread the needle

## Programming Squats

### Beginners

**Focus:** Form, form, form

- Bodyweight and goblet squats
- 3 sets of 10-15 reps
- 2-3 times per week
- Video and analyze form

### Intermediate

**Focus:** Building strength

- Progress to barbell squats
- 3-5 sets of 5-10 reps
- 2 times per week
- Progressive overload

### Advanced

**Focus:** Performance goals

- Periodized programming
- Variations for weaknesses
- 2-3 times per week
- Mix of rep ranges

## Warm-Up for Squatting

### General (3-5 min)

- Light cardio
- Leg swings
- Hip circles

### Specific (5 min)

1. Goblet squat hold: 30 seconds
2. Bodyweight squats: 10 reps
3. Banded glute activation: 15 reps
4. Ankle mobilization: 10 each side

### Warm-Up Sets

- Empty bar: 10 reps
- 50% working weight: 5 reps
- 70% working weight: 3 reps
- 90% working weight: 1-2 reps
- Working sets

## Fixing Your Squat

### Step 1: Video Yourself

- Front and side angles
- Watch for mistakes listed above
- Compare to good examples

### Step 2: Identify Limitations

- Mobility issues?
- Strength weaknesses?
- Motor pattern problems?

### Step 3: Address Root Causes

- Mobility work for restrictions
- Accessory exercises for weaknesses
- Cues and practice for patterns

### Step 4: Progress Gradually

- Don't rush adding weight
- Form before load
- Patience wins

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Depth matters** — at least parallel, more if form allows
- **Knees track toes** — no caving inward
- **Core stays braced** — throughout entire lift
- **Chest stays up** — avoid excessive forward lean
- **Mobility supports form** — address limitations
- **Progress with good form** — don't sacrifice technique for weight

The squat is a skill that takes time to master. Be patient, focus on form, and the strength will follow.
