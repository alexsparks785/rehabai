# Wrist Exercises and Stretches: Relieve Pain and Build Strength

Your wrists handle constant stress from typing, phones, exercise, and daily tasks. Weak or tight wrists lead to pain, reduced grip strength, and injury risk. Here's how to keep your wrists healthy and strong.

## Why Wrist Health Matters

Your wrists:
- Control hand function for everything you do
- Bear load during push-ups, yoga, and lifting
- Absorb stress from typing and mouse use
- Connect arm strength to hand grip
- Are vulnerable to repetitive strain

## Common Wrist Problems

- **Carpal tunnel syndrome**: Nerve compression
- **Tendinitis**: Tendon inflammation
- **De Quervain's**: Thumb-side tendon pain
- **Wrist strain**: Muscle/ligament overload
- **Ganglion cyst**: Fluid-filled bump
- **General stiffness**: Lack of mobility

## Wrist Stretches

### 1. Prayer Stretch (Wrist Flexor Stretch)

1. Press palms together in front of chest
2. Lower hands toward waist, keeping palms together
3. Feel stretch in forearms/wrists
4. Hold 30 seconds
5. Repeat 2-3 times

### 2. Reverse Prayer (Wrist Extensor Stretch)

1. Press backs of hands together
2. Point fingers down
3. Lower hands toward waist
4. Hold 30 seconds
5. Repeat 2-3 times

### 3. Wrist Flexor Stretch

1. Extend arm in front, palm up
2. Use other hand to bend wrist down
3. Keep elbow straight
4. Hold 30 seconds each arm

### 4. Wrist Extensor Stretch

1. Extend arm in front, palm down
2. Use other hand to bend wrist down
3. Keep elbow straight
4. Hold 30 seconds each arm

### 5. Wrist Circles

1. Extend arms in front
2. Make fists
3. Circle wrists slowly
4. 10 circles each direction

### 6. Finger Spread and Squeeze

1. Spread fingers wide apart
2. Hold 5 seconds
3. Make tight fist
4. Hold 5 seconds
5. Repeat 10 times

### 7. Thumb Stretch

1. Make a fist with thumb inside
2. Gently bend wrist toward pinky side
3. Feel stretch at base of thumb
4. Hold 30 seconds each hand

### 8. Wall Wrist Stretch

1. Face wall, place palm flat on wall, fingers down
2. Straighten elbow
3. Gently turn away from wall
4. Feel stretch in forearm
5. Hold 30 seconds each arm

## Wrist Strengthening Exercises

### 9. Wrist Curls (Flexion)

1. Rest forearm on table, wrist over edge
2. Palm up, hold light weight
3. Curl wrist up
4. Lower slowly
5. Do 2-3 sets of 15

### 10. Reverse Wrist Curls (Extension)

1. Rest forearm on table, wrist over edge
2. Palm down, hold light weight
3. Raise wrist up
4. Lower slowly
5. Do 2-3 sets of 15

### 11. Wrist Radial Deviation

1. Hold weight or hammer at end of handle
2. Forearm on table, thumb up
3. Raise weight by bending wrist up
4. Lower slowly
5. Do 2 sets of 12 each arm

### 12. Wrist Ulnar Deviation

1. Same position as above
2. Lower weight by bending wrist down
3. Raise slowly
4. Do 2 sets of 12 each arm

### 13. Pronation/Supination

1. Hold weight or hammer at end
2. Elbow at side, bent 90 degrees
3. Rotate forearm palm up, then palm down
4. Do 2 sets of 15 each arm

### 14. Grip Strengthening

**Stress ball squeezes:**
1. Squeeze stress ball or tennis ball
2. Hold 5 seconds
3. Release
4. Do 15-20 reps each hand

**Gripper exercises:**
1. Close gripper fully
2. Hold 3 seconds
3. Release slowly
4. Do 2 sets of 10-15

### 15. Finger Extensions with Band

1. Loop rubber band around all fingers
2. Spread fingers against resistance
3. Do 2 sets of 15-20

### 16. Rice Bucket Exercises

Fill bucket with rice:
1. Insert hand into rice
2. Open and close fist
3. Rotate wrist
4. Grab and release handfuls
5. Spend 2-3 minutes

### 17. Plate Pinches

1. Pinch weight plate between fingers and thumb
2. Hold as long as possible
3. Build up to 30+ seconds
4. Do 2-3 sets each hand

## Desk Worker Routine

For those who type or use a mouse all day.

### Every Hour (1 minute)

1. Wrist circles: 10 each direction
2. Finger spread and squeeze: 5 reps
3. Prayer stretch: 15 seconds
4. Shake hands out

### Daily Routine (5 minutes)

**Stretches:**
- Prayer stretch: 30 sec
- Reverse prayer: 30 sec
- Wrist flexor stretch: 30 sec each
- Wrist extensor stretch: 30 sec each
- Thumb stretch: 30 sec each

**Strengthening:**
- Stress ball squeezes: 15 each hand
- Finger extensions: 15 reps

## Athlete/Lifter Routine

For those who bear weight on wrists.

### Pre-Workout (3 minutes)

1. Wrist circles: 10 each direction
2. Wrist flexor stretch: 30 sec each
3. Wrist extensor stretch: 30 sec each
4. Wall wrist stretch: 30 sec each
5. Light wrist rotations

### Strength Work (2-3x/week)

- Wrist curls: 3x15
- Reverse wrist curls: 3x15
- Pronation/supination: 2x15
- Grip work: 2-3 sets

## Wrist Mobility for Yoga and Push-Ups

### Wrist Warm-Up Sequence

1. **On all fours, fingers forward**: Rock forward and back
2. **Fingers pointing toward knees**: Rock gently
3. **Fingers pointing out to sides**: Rock gently
4. **Backs of hands on floor**: Rock gently
5. Spend 30 seconds in each position

### Building Wrist Tolerance

If push-ups or yoga hurt your wrists:

1. Start on fists instead of flat palms
2. Use push-up handles
3. Work on wrist mobility and strength separately
4. Gradually increase weight-bearing time
5. Progress slowly over weeks

## Self-Massage for Wrists

### Forearm Flexor Massage

1. Palm up, locate meaty part of inner forearm
2. Press with thumb from elbow toward wrist
3. Pause on tender spots
4. Spend 1-2 minutes each arm

### Forearm Extensor Massage

1. Palm down, locate muscles on top of forearm
2. Press with fingers or opposite thumb
3. Work from elbow toward wrist
4. Spend 1-2 minutes each arm

### Wrist Joint Mobilization

1. Grasp wrist with opposite hand
2. Gently rotate, flex, extend
3. Apply light traction (pull gently)
4. Move through all ranges

## Ergonomic Tips

### Keyboard Position

- Wrists neutral or slightly extended
- Keyboard at elbow height
- Consider ergonomic keyboard

### Mouse Use

- Keep wrist neutral
- Move from shoulder, not wrist
- Consider vertical mouse

### Phone Use

- Avoid thumb-heavy texting
- Use voice-to-text
- Take frequent breaks

## When to See a Doctor

Seek evaluation if:

- Numbness or tingling in fingers
- Weakness in grip
- Pain that wakes you at night
- Swelling that doesn't resolve
- Pain after trauma
- Symptoms lasting more than 2 weeks despite rest

## Prevention Tips

1. **Take breaks**: Every 30-60 minutes when typing
2. **Stretch daily**: Even when pain-free
3. **Strengthen regularly**: 2-3x/week
4. **Maintain neutral wrist**: During computer work
5. **Warm up**: Before wrist-loading activities
6. **Progress gradually**: Don't suddenly increase load

## The Bottom Line

Healthy wrists require:

1. **Regular stretching**: Daily, especially for desk workers
2. **Strength work**: 2-3x/week
3. **Breaks and ergonomics**: During repetitive tasks
4. **Warm-ups**: Before loading activities
5. **Attention to pain**: Don't ignore warning signs

Start with the desk worker routine if you type frequently. Add strengthening as wrists allow. Prevention is much easier than recovery.
