# Best Exercises for Posture: Fix Your Alignment

Poor posture is epidemic. Hours of sitting, phone use, and computer work leave most people with rounded shoulders, forward head, and a hunched upper back. The good news: posture is fixable with the right exercises.

This guide covers the best exercises to improve your posture.

## Common Postural Problems

### Upper Crossed Syndrome

**What it looks like:**
- Rounded shoulders
- Forward head
- Hunched upper back

**What's tight:**
- Chest muscles
- Upper trapezius
- Neck muscles

**What's weak:**
- Lower trapezius
- Rhomboids
- Deep neck flexors

### Lower Crossed Syndrome

**What it looks like:**
- Anterior pelvic tilt (belly pooches forward)
- Excessive lower back arch
- Tight hip flexors

**What's tight:**
- Hip flexors
- Lower back muscles

**What's weak:**
- Abs
- Glutes

### Forward Head Posture

**What it looks like:**
- Head juts forward
- Ears in front of shoulders

**Effects:**
- Neck pain
- Headaches
- Shoulder tension

## The Fix: Stretch + Strengthen

### The Principle

**Stretch what's tight:**
- Chest
- Hip flexors
- Upper traps

**Strengthen what's weak:**
- Upper back
- Core
- Glutes

Balance restores good posture.

## Upper Body Posture Exercises

### Stretches

#### Doorway Chest Stretch

**Purpose:** Open tight chest

**Execution:**
1. Forearm on door frame, elbow at shoulder height
2. Step through doorway
3. Feel stretch across chest
4. Hold 30-60 seconds each side

#### Upper Trap Stretch

**Purpose:** Release tight upper traps

**Execution:**
1. Tilt ear toward shoulder
2. Gently add pressure with hand
3. Don't rotate head
4. Hold 30 seconds each side

#### Chin Tuck

**Purpose:** Correct forward head posture

**Execution:**
1. Sit or stand tall
2. Pull chin straight back (make double chin)
3. Hold 5-10 seconds
4. Repeat 10-15 times

#### Thoracic Extension (Foam Roller)

**Purpose:** Reverse upper back rounding

**Execution:**
1. Foam roller under upper back
2. Hands behind head
3. Extend back over roller
4. Move to different segments
5. 1-2 minutes total

### Strengthening

#### Face Pulls

**Purpose:** Strengthen rear delts, rhomboids, external rotators

**Execution:**
1. Cable or band at face height
2. Pull toward face
3. Spread hands apart, rotate thumbs back
4. Squeeze shoulder blades

**Sets/Reps:** 3x15-20

#### Band Pull-Aparts

**Purpose:** Strengthen mid-back

**Execution:**
1. Hold band at chest height
2. Arms straight in front
3. Pull band apart
4. Squeeze shoulder blades
5. Control return

**Sets/Reps:** 3x15-20 (can do daily)

#### Prone Y Raises

**Purpose:** Strengthen lower traps

**Execution:**
1. Lie face down on bench or floor
2. Arms in Y position
3. Raise arms, thumbs up
4. Squeeze at top

**Sets/Reps:** 3x12-15 with light weight

#### Prone T Raises

**Purpose:** Strengthen mid-traps, rhomboids

**Execution:**
1. Lie face down
2. Arms out to sides (T position)
3. Raise arms, squeeze shoulder blades
4. Lower with control

**Sets/Reps:** 3x12-15

#### Wall Slides

**Purpose:** Scapular control, posture awareness

**Execution:**
1. Back against wall
2. Arms in "goal post" position
3. Slide arms up and down wall
4. Keep entire arm touching wall

**Sets/Reps:** 2x10-15

#### Rows (All Variations)

**Purpose:** Overall upper back strength

**Execution:**
- Pull with elbows, squeeze shoulder blades
- Include in regular training
- Multiple times per week

## Lower Body Posture Exercises

### Stretches

#### Hip Flexor Stretch

**Purpose:** Release tight hip flexors

**Execution:**
1. Half-kneeling position
2. Squeeze glute of back leg
3. Push hips slightly forward
4. Don't arch lower back
5. Hold 30-60 seconds each side

#### Cat-Cow Stretch

**Purpose:** Mobilize spine

**Execution:**
1. On hands and knees
2. Round back up (cat)
3. Drop belly, look up (cow)
4. Flow between positions

**Reps:** 10-15 cycles

### Strengthening

#### Glute Bridges

**Purpose:** Strengthen glutes, oppose tight hip flexors

**Execution:**
1. Lie on back, knees bent
2. Drive hips up, squeeze glutes
3. Don't hyperextend lower back
4. Lower with control

**Sets/Reps:** 3x15-20

#### Dead Bug

**Purpose:** Core stability, lower back protection

**Execution:**
1. Lie on back, arms up, knees at 90°
2. Lower opposite arm and leg
3. Keep lower back pressed to floor
4. Return and switch

**Sets/Reps:** 3x10 each side

#### Bird Dog

**Purpose:** Core and back stability

**Execution:**
1. On hands and knees
2. Extend opposite arm and leg
3. Keep spine neutral
4. Hold 3-5 seconds, switch

**Sets/Reps:** 3x10 each side

#### Plank

**Purpose:** Overall core stability

**Execution:**
- Forearms and toes on ground
- Body in straight line
- Squeeze glutes, brace core
- Hold 20-60 seconds

**Sets:** 3 sets

## Daily Posture Routine

### Morning (5 minutes)

1. Chin tucks: 10 reps
2. Doorway stretch: 30 sec each side
3. Cat-cow: 10 cycles
4. Band pull-aparts: 15 reps (or towel stretch)

### During Work (Every 1-2 Hours)

1. Stand up and move
2. Chin tucks: 5 reps
3. Shoulder rolls: 10 each direction
4. Wall angels: 5 reps (if space available)

### Evening (10 minutes)

1. Foam roller thoracic extension: 2 min
2. Hip flexor stretch: 45 sec each side
3. Doorway stretch: 45 sec each side
4. Prone Y-T raises: 10 each
5. Glute bridge: 15 reps
6. Dead bug: 10 each side

## Posture Throughout the Day

### At the Desk

- Screen at eye level
- Keyboard at elbow height
- Feet flat on floor
- Back supported
- Take breaks every 30-60 minutes

### Standing

- Weight on both feet
- Shoulders back (not forced)
- Ears over shoulders
- Engage core slightly

### Phone Use

- Bring phone to eye level
- Don't hunch over
- Limit time

### Sleeping

- Avoid stomach sleeping
- Side sleepers: pillow between knees
- Back sleepers: pillow under knees

## Training Adjustments for Better Posture

### More of These

- Rows (all variations)
- Face pulls
- Rear delt exercises
- External rotation work
- Pull-ups/chin-ups

### Less of These (Temporarily)

- Excessive bench pressing
- Front raises
- Heavy overhead pressing
- Anything that tightens chest/front delts

### Pull to Push Ratio

**For posture correction:**
- Do 2-3 pulling exercises for every 1 pushing
- Example: Bench press → Rows, Face pulls, Rear flies

## Timeline for Improvement

**Week 1-2:**
- Building habit
- Increased awareness
- May feel "worse" (noticing poor posture)

**Week 3-4:**
- Stretches feel easier
- Can hold good posture longer
- Starting to remember to self-correct

**Week 5-8:**
- Noticeable improvement
- Good posture feels more natural
- Others may comment

**Week 9-12+:**
- Significant change
- Good posture becomes default
- Maintenance phase

**Full correction:** 3-6 months of consistent effort

## Common Mistakes

### Only Stretching

**Problem:** Flexibility without strength to hold position
**Fix:** Include strengthening exercises

### Forcing Good Posture

**Problem:** Exhausting, unsustainable
**Fix:** Build strength so good posture is comfortable

### Inconsistency

**Problem:** Occasional effort doesn't create change
**Fix:** Daily practice, even if brief

### Ignoring Root Cause

**Problem:** Exercises but same bad habits
**Fix:** Modify desk setup, phone habits, daily positions

## Conclusion

Good posture comes from balanced muscles. Stretch what's tight, strengthen what's weak, and practice awareness throughout the day.

**Key Takeaways:**
- Stretch chest, hip flexors, upper traps
- Strengthen upper back, core, glutes
- Do face pulls and band pull-aparts regularly
- Set up your workspace properly
- Move and break up sitting time
- Consistency over intensity
- Results take 3-6 months

Your posture can improve at any age. Start today and stand taller tomorrow.
