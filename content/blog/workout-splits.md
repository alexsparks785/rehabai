# Workout Splits: How to Structure Your Training Week

How you divide your training across the week matters. The right split fits your schedule, recovery capacity, and goals. The wrong one leads to poor results or burnout. Here's how to choose and implement the best training split for you.

## What Is a Workout Split?

A workout split is how you organize exercises across your training week. It determines:
- Which muscles you train together
- How often you train each muscle
- How many days per week you lift

Different splits have different advantages. None is universally "best"—context determines the winner.

## The Major Splits

### Full Body (3x/week)

Train all major muscle groups every session.

**Sample schedule:**
- Monday: Full body
- Tuesday: Off
- Wednesday: Full body
- Thursday: Off
- Friday: Full body
- Weekend: Off

**Sample workout:**
- Squat: 3x8
- Bench Press: 3x8
- Row: 3x8
- Romanian Deadlift: 3x10
- Overhead Press: 3x10
- Bicep Curl: 2x12
- Tricep Pushdown: 2x12

**Pros:**
- High frequency (3x/week per muscle)
- Efficient—only 3 gym days needed
- Great for beginners
- Good for strength focus
- Natural recovery built in

**Cons:**
- Long sessions (60-90 minutes)
- Hard to fit much volume per muscle
- Fatigue accumulates through workout
- Less specialization possible

**Best for:** Beginners, those with 3 days to train, strength focus, older trainees

### Upper/Lower (4x/week)

Alternate upper body and lower body days.

**Sample schedule:**
- Monday: Upper
- Tuesday: Lower
- Wednesday: Off
- Thursday: Upper
- Friday: Lower
- Weekend: Off

**Sample upper day:**
- Bench Press: 4x6-8
- Row: 4x8
- Overhead Press: 3x8-10
- Pulldown: 3x10
- Bicep Curl: 3x10-12
- Tricep Pushdown: 3x10-12

**Sample lower day:**
- Squat: 4x6-8
- Romanian Deadlift: 4x8
- Leg Press: 3x10-12
- Leg Curl: 3x10-12
- Calf Raise: 4x15

**Pros:**
- Good frequency (2x/week per muscle)
- Balanced approach
- Manageable session length
- Good volume per muscle
- Flexible scheduling

**Cons:**
- Requires 4 days minimum
- Lower body days can be brutal
- Less direct arm work typically

**Best for:** Intermediate lifters, those with 4 days to train, balanced development

### Push/Pull/Legs (PPL) (3-6x/week)

Divide by movement pattern: pushing, pulling, legs.

**Sample schedule (6 days):**
- Monday: Push
- Tuesday: Pull
- Wednesday: Legs
- Thursday: Push
- Friday: Pull
- Saturday: Legs
- Sunday: Off

**Sample schedule (3 days):**
- Monday: Push
- Wednesday: Pull
- Friday: Legs

**Sample push day:**
- Bench Press: 4x6-8
- Incline Dumbbell Press: 3x10
- Dumbbell Fly: 3x12
- Overhead Press: 3x8-10
- Lateral Raise: 3x15
- Tricep Pushdown: 3x12
- Overhead Tricep Extension: 3x12

**Sample pull day:**
- Deadlift: 4x5
- Pulldown: 4x8-10
- Row: 4x10
- Face Pull: 3x15
- Bicep Curl: 3x10-12
- Hammer Curl: 3x10-12

**Sample leg day:**
- Squat: 4x6-8
- Romanian Deadlift: 3x8-10
- Leg Press: 3x10-12
- Leg Curl: 3x10-12
- Leg Extension: 3x12-15
- Calf Raise: 4x15-20

**Pros:**
- High volume per session
- Logical groupings (no muscle trained fatigued from previous exercise)
- Flexible frequency (run once or twice per week)
- Popular and well-established

**Cons:**
- Can be 6 days if run twice
- 3-day version has low frequency
- Leg days are demanding
- Requires good recovery

**Best for:** Intermediate to advanced, those prioritizing hypertrophy, 6-day trainees

### Bro Split (5-6x/week)

One muscle group per day.

**Sample schedule:**
- Monday: Chest
- Tuesday: Back
- Wednesday: Shoulders
- Thursday: Legs
- Friday: Arms
- Weekend: Off

**Sample chest day:**
- Bench Press: 4x6-8
- Incline Press: 4x8-10
- Dumbbell Fly: 3x12
- Cable Crossover: 3x12-15
- Push-Ups: 3x failure

**Pros:**
- High volume per muscle per session
- Great pump
- Simple to understand
- Allows focus and specialization

**Cons:**
- Low frequency (1x/week per muscle)
- Research shows lower frequency = less growth
- Requires 5-6 gym days
- Wasted potential for most natural lifters

**Best for:** Advanced bodybuilders, enhanced athletes, those recovering from high volume

### Other Splits

**Push/Pull (4-6x/week)**
Just two days alternated. Legs included in push (quad focus) or pull (hamstring focus) or both.

**Arnold Split (6x/week)**
- Day 1: Chest + Back
- Day 2: Shoulders + Arms
- Day 3: Legs
- Repeat

**Upper/Lower/Push/Pull (5x/week)**
Hybrid approach hitting muscles with varying frequency.

## How to Choose Your Split

### Based on Days Available

| Days Available | Best Splits |
|---------------|-------------|
| 2 days | Full body |
| 3 days | Full body, PPL (1x through) |
| 4 days | Upper/Lower, PPL + 1 |
| 5 days | Upper/Lower + extra day, PPL + 2 |
| 6 days | PPL (2x through), Arnold split |

### Based on Experience

**Beginner (0-1 years):**
Full body, 3x/week. High frequency, lower volume per session, focus on learning movements.

**Intermediate (1-3 years):**
Upper/Lower or PPL. More volume needed, can handle more frequency, still need recovery.

**Advanced (3+ years):**
Any split based on preference and goals. May need higher volume, specialization, or creative approaches.

### Based on Goals

**Strength:**
Full body or Upper/Lower. Frequent practice of main lifts, manageable fatigue.

**Hypertrophy:**
PPL or Upper/Lower. Higher volume, good frequency, enough recovery.

**General Fitness:**
Full body. Hit everything, efficient, 3 days is sustainable.

**Sport Performance:**
Full body or Upper/Lower. Keep gym work supplementary, save energy for sport.

### Based on Recovery

**Good recovery (young, low stress, good sleep):**
Can handle higher frequency and volume. PPL 6x works.

**Moderate recovery:**
Upper/Lower 4x or Full body 3x. Balance stimulus and rest.

**Poor recovery (older, high stress, poor sleep):**
Full body 2-3x or Upper/Lower 3-4x. Less volume, more rest.

## Frequency vs. Volume Trade-offs

### Higher Frequency (Full Body)
- More sessions per muscle per week
- Less volume per session
- Research-supported for strength and size
- Requires restraint per session

### Lower Frequency (Bro Split)
- More volume per muscle per session
- Fewer sessions per muscle per week
- Popular but likely suboptimal for naturals
- Great pump but not necessarily better results

### The Sweet Spot
Most research suggests **2x per week per muscle** is ideal for most people. This aligns with:
- Upper/Lower 4x
- PPL 6x
- Full body 3x (technically 3x frequency, which is also fine)

## Making Any Split Work

### Progressive Overload
More important than the split itself. Track weights, aim to improve.

### Appropriate Volume
10-20 sets per muscle per week for most intermediates. Split this across sessions.

### Recovery Management
Sleep, nutrition, and stress management determine what you can handle.

### Consistency
The best split is one you'll actually follow. A "suboptimal" split done consistently beats an "optimal" split done inconsistently.

## Common Mistakes

### Copying Advanced Lifters
Elite bodybuilders often use bro splits. They also use steroids, which change recovery dynamics. Natural lifters usually do better with higher frequency.

### Overcomplicating
Fancy splits aren't better. Simple, consistent training beats complex, inconsistent training.

### Ignoring Recovery
Adding days without adding recovery leads to overtraining. More isn't always better.

### No Progression Plan
The split is the structure, but progression is the driver. Have a plan to add weight or reps over time.

### Frequent Changes
Switching splits constantly prevents adaptation. Stick with a split for at least 8-12 weeks before evaluating.

## Sample Weekly Programs

### Beginner Full Body (3x/week)

**Every session:**
- Squat variation: 3x8
- Horizontal push: 3x8
- Horizontal pull: 3x8
- Hip hinge: 3x10
- Vertical push or pull: 2x10
- Core: 2 sets

### Intermediate Upper/Lower (4x/week)

**Upper A:**
- Bench Press: 4x6
- Row: 4x8
- Overhead Press: 3x8
- Pulldown: 3x10
- Bicep Curl: 3x12
- Tricep Pushdown: 3x12

**Lower A:**
- Squat: 4x6
- Romanian Deadlift: 4x8
- Leg Press: 3x10
- Leg Curl: 3x10
- Calf Raise: 4x15

**Upper B:**
- Overhead Press: 4x6
- Pulldown: 4x8
- Incline Press: 3x10
- Row: 3x10
- Hammer Curl: 3x12
- Overhead Extension: 3x12

**Lower B:**
- Deadlift: 4x5
- Front Squat: 3x8
- Leg Extension: 3x12
- Leg Curl: 3x12
- Calf Raise: 4x15

### Advanced PPL (6x/week)

Run Push/Pull/Legs twice per week with slight variations between sessions (heavier/lighter, different exercises, etc.)

## Final Recommendation

**If unsure, start here:**

- **Beginner:** Full body 3x/week
- **Intermediate:** Upper/Lower 4x/week
- **Advanced with time:** PPL 6x/week
- **Advanced with less time:** Upper/Lower 4x or Full body 3x

Then adjust based on:
- How you feel
- Progress you're making
- Schedule constraints
- Personal preference

The split matters, but execution matters more. Pick something reasonable and do it well.
